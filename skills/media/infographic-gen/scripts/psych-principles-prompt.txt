# Stop Using Psychological Principles at Random
## Here Is the Exact Sequence That Converts

Most marketers sprinkle persuasion principles everywhere hoping something sticks. Research says the opposite: sequencing matters as much as selection. Wrong principle at the wrong moment destroys trust.

TIER 1: FOUNDATION (Deploy First -- Build the Relationship)

1. LIKING -- The prospect must feel connection first. The brain filters out persuasion from untrusted sources. No liking = scarcity reads as manipulation.

2. RECIPROCITY -- Give genuine value (not disguised pitches). Creates biological obligation to return the favor. A free PDF creates a reader, not a buyer -- you still need the rest of the stack.

TIER 2: VALIDATION (Deploy Second -- Reduce Risk)

3. SOCIAL PROOF -- Others like them already made this bet and survived. Weak generic reviews are worse than no reviews at all.

4. AUTHORITY -- "Does it actually work?" Expert credibility must be recognized by YOUR specific audience, not just anyone.

5. COMMITMENT & CONSISTENCY -- Small commitments (quiz, survey, checklist) activate the consistency drive. People pay money to stay consistent with their self-declared identity.

TIER 3: CONVERSION (Deploy Last -- Force the Decision)

6. ANCHORING -- Price perception is relative. You choose which reference point gets planted first.

7. DECOY EFFECT -- A strategic middle option makes the premium tier look reasonable. The decoy isn't designed to sell -- it makes something else sell.

8. SCARCITY -- Real deadlines only. Fake countdown timers destroy credibility permanently.

9. LOSS AVERSION -- Losing hurts 2x more than gaining feels good. "You're leaving $400 on the table" beats "You could earn $400 more."

10. FREQUENCY ILLUSION -- After first brand contact, retargeting makes you feel omnipresent. Structure your media buy to activate it.

WHY THIS SEQUENCE WORKS: The limbic system (emotional safety) must clear a stimulus before the prefrontal cortex (rational evaluation) engages. Skipping emotional clearance = clicks but zero conversions.

TOP MISTAKES: Leading with scarcity to cold traffic. Using authority before liking. Stacking all principles at once (cognitive overload, not persuasion).
