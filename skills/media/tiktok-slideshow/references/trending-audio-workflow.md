# Trending Audio Matching Workflow

The TikTok-specific distribution multiplier most operators skip. The platform weights audio fit as a ranking signal, and content paired with audio currently in the algorithmic push window distributes faster than the same content with non-trending audio.

This dynamic is unique to TikTok at this scale. Instagram has audio support for Reels but does not weight audio fit as a primary distribution signal. YouTube Shorts has audio support but the algorithm prioritizes other signals more heavily. TikTok's audio integration is what makes trending audio matching a meaningful distribution multiplier on this specific platform.

5 to 10 minutes per production batch. Worth it every time.

## The 4-step workflow

Run this at the start of every TikTok production batch. Not as a one-time setup. Trending audio cycles through the push window weekly.

### Step 1: Identify trending tracks in the niche

Open TikTok's discovery surface for trending sounds. Filter for the target content category. Look at:

- Tracks marked as trending in the past 3 to 7 days
- Tracks being used by the top performing creators in the niche
- Tracks where the play count is climbing fast (algorithmic push window indicator)

Pull a shortlist of 8 to 12 candidate tracks per batch. You will narrow this down in the next step.

### Step 2: Filter for emotional register match

Trending audio does not always match the content. Forcing a mismatched audio onto content produces worse performance than no audio matching at all. The viewer reads the mismatch in the first second and bounces.

For each candidate track, identify the emotional register:

- Reflective and slow (works for: Realization, Personal Story with introspective angle)
- Driving and energetic (works for: Routine Breakdown, Personal Story with achievement angle)
- Playful and ironic (works for: Controversial Opinion, Ranked List with humor)
- Tense and dramatic (works for: Personal Story with stakes, Realization with diagnosis angle)
- Aspirational and warm (works for: Personal Story with transformation, Routine Breakdown with lifestyle angle)
- Sharp and confrontational (works for: Controversial Opinion at peak intensity)

Match each slideshow in the batch to a track whose register fits. If the slideshow is a controversial opinion attacking conventional wisdom, do not pair it with reflective acoustic music. If the slideshow is a soft realization about why something hurt, do not pair it with driving EDM.

### Step 3: Assign before scheduling

Each slideshow in the batch gets paired with audio that is:

1. Currently trending (in the algorithmic push window this week)
2. Emotionally matched to the slideshow's content register

Assign all batch slideshows in the same session. Track the audio assignment in the slideshow handoff (see SKILL.md output format).

### Step 4: Monitor and rotate

Trending audio cycles fast. Audio that was trending last week may not be trending this week. The audio matching workflow runs every production batch, not as a one-time setup.

Each batch:

- Re-check the trending list (do not assume previous batch's tracks are still trending)
- Drop any track that has fallen out of the push window
- Add 2 to 3 new candidates from the current week's trending surface

Some tracks stick around in the push window for 2 to 3 weeks. Most cycle out within 7 to 10 days. Do not get attached to a track. The platform's algorithm has already moved on by the time the operator notices.

## Time investment vs distribution lift

The audio research takes 5 to 10 minutes per production batch.

This is operationally cheap intelligence work that pays back in algorithmic distribution that operators skipping the audio matching step never access. The lift compounds across high production volumes.

Slideshows with currently-trending audio in the right emotional register distribute meaningfully faster than slideshows with random audio or no audio matching. Operators who skip this step are leaving TikTok-specific distribution multiplication on the table. The lift exists. The workflow to capture it takes minutes per batch. There is no reason to skip it.

## How to phrase the audio note in the handoff

When the skill outputs a finished slideshow, the audio note should include:

- Track name and artist
- Brief register match note ("driving energetic, fits the achievement frame in slide 1")
- Trending status as of generation date

Example audio note:

> **Audio:** "Original sound by [creator]" (driving build-up energy, currently trending in tech/AI niche as of [date]). Register matches the "I built 5 bots in 30 days" achievement framing.

The user can then verify the track is still trending before scheduling. If 24 to 48 hours have passed between skill output and posting, re-check the trending status.

## Common failure modes

### Forcing a mismatch

The track is trending. The slideshow's emotional register is opposite. The operator pairs them anyway because "trending is what matters". The viewer reads the dissonance and bounces. Trending audio is necessary, not sufficient. Match register or use silence.

### Stale audio

The operator pulled trending audio at the start of the month and reused it for 4 weeks. By week 3 it is no longer in the push window. The slideshows still post but distribute as if they had no audio matching. Re-check every batch.

### Mismatch between slide pacing and audio pacing

Some tracks have a beat drop or a tonal shift at a specific point. If the slideshow's emotional turn (the "reveal" slide in a Ranked List, the "realization" beat in a Realization) does not align with the audio's emotional turn, the slideshow feels off-tempo. When picking a track with a beat drop, place the slideshow's emotional turn on the slide that hits the drop.

### Skipping audio because the slideshow "does not need music"

Even on slideshows that feel quiet (Realization in particular), audio still matters for distribution. Use a quiet ambient or reflective track. Silence is treated by the algorithm as no audio assigned, which loses the audio fit signal entirely.

## How this connects to the production rhythm

Audio matching is the third step of the production workflow, after format pick and script-plus-image generation. It is also the cheapest step. If a batch is running tight on time, every other step compresses before audio matching does. The lift is too high for the cost.
