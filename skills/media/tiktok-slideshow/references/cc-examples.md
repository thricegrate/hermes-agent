# CC-Specific Worked Examples

How the 5 slideshow formats apply to Cyber Corsairs content angles. Use these as worked examples when the generic templates need to be filled with the actual CC voice, audience, and product context.

## CC audience snapshot

Per `private project notes/MEMORY.md` and `private project coaching/`:

- **Age band**: 35-50+ Americans (mostly non-business-owner, want to learn AI)
- **Goals**: 33% want to make money, plus learning AI and automating life
- **Blocks**: 50% feel overwhelmed and do not know where to start
- **Buy signal**: 75% have buy signal, 40% want free trial first, 25% free-only
- **What they engage with**: free demos of working tools, build-in-public stories of failure-into-iteration, contrarian takes on AI hype

## CC content angles to slot into formats

Five primary angles for CC slideshows, each mapped to the format that fits best.

### Angle 1: the product skill demos (build-in-public)

Format fit: **Personal Story** or **Routine Breakdown**

Setup: a specific skill in the the product got built. The slideshow tells the story of why it got built and what it does.

Personal Story flavor:
- Slide 1 hook: "I was spending 3 hours a day on [thing]. Then I built this skill."
- Slide 2-5: discovery, mechanism, the moment it clicked, the proof
- Slide 6 CTA: "Comment SKILL and I will send you the demo"

Routine Breakdown flavor:
- Slide 1: "The 5-step workflow my the product runs while I sleep"
- Slides 2-6: each step in the automation
- Slide 7 CTA: "Comment the product and I will show you the setup"

### Angle 2: Learn-AI app launches

Format fit: **Personal Story** or **Realization**

Setup: a new free demo app got launched (Cursiv-style, app-iteration strategy). The slideshow drives traffic to the demo.

Personal Story flavor:
- Slide 1 hook: "I built an AI app in 3 days that teaches you [skill]. Here is what happened."
- Slide 2-5: why I built it, what surprised me, what users did with it, what I would change
- Slide 6 CTA: "Free trial in bio"

Realization flavor:
- Slide 1 hook: "I just realized why most people give up on learning AI."
- Slides 2-5: the actual block, why the standard advice fails, what works instead, evidence
- Slide 6 CTA: "I built a free 5-minute demo that fixes this. Comment APP."

### Angle 3: Coaching pivot story arc

Format fit: **Personal Story** with stakes, or **Realization**

Setup: the coaching app pivot story (62K opens to 50 clicks, audience misread, the iteration). This is real and specific, which makes it a strong build-in-public artifact.

Personal Story flavor:
- Slide 1 hook: "I sent 62,000 emails. 50 people clicked. Here is what I learned."
- Slide 2-5: what I assumed, what they actually wanted, the misread, the rebuild
- Slide 6 CTA: "Following the rebuild for free? Bio link."

Realization flavor:
- Slide 1 hook: "I just realized I was building for the wrong audience."
- Slide 2-5: what the survey said, what they actually meant, the gap, the new direction
- Slide 6 CTA: closing reflection or "Comment LEARN and I will share what I am building now"

### Angle 4: AI hype contrarian takes

Format fit: **Controversial Opinion**

Setup: most AI content sounds the same. CC has a credible position to argue something the niche disagrees with. The position has to be defensible across 5 slides.

Sample positions that fit CC's voice:
- "Most AI skills are useless. Here is why."
- "ChatGPT is not the right tool for what you are trying to do."
- "Building one big AI app is the wrong move. Here is what actually works."
- "Learning AI in 30 days is a lie. Here is the real timeline."

Each position needs: specific evidence, real data points, and a closing slide that lands the conviction. CC voice means short sentences, blunt phrasing, no hedging.

### Angle 5: Tool ranked lists

Format fit: **Ranked List**

Setup: rank tools the audience already knows about, with at least one surprise in the order.

Sample lists:
- "Top 5 AI tools for solo founders. Number 1 is free."
- "I tested 7 AI writing apps. They are not all the same."
- "5 the product skills that replaced $200 of paid software."

Pattern: the audience expects ChatGPT or Claude at #1. Either confirm with specific reasoning, or place a less-famous tool at #1 with strong evidence. Both work. Either way the swipe-through completes.

## Sample slot-fills

### Personal Story sample (filled, the product skill demo)

```
SLIDE 1 (HOOK): "I built an AI bot in 4 hours that writes my newsletter for me. 215K subscribers
have not noticed."

SLIDE 2: "Background: I write 4 newsletters a week. It used to take 2 hours a piece. The bot
now does the first draft."

SLIDE 3: "The bot is not magic. It reads my voice memo for the topic, pulls from my notes, and
drafts in my voice. I edit the draft. Total time: 25 minutes per issue instead of 2 hours."

SLIDE 4: "The trick was not better prompts. It was loading my voice as a reference and giving
the bot 5 example issues to learn from."

SLIDE 5: "The output sounds like me because the bot was trained on me. Most people skip this
step and wonder why their AI content sounds generic."

SLIDE 6 (CTA): "Comment NEWSLETTER and I will send you the bot setup."

AUDIO: Driving energetic track in the tech/AI niche. Register matches the achievement frame.

POSTING HINT: Tuesday or Thursday 10am-12pm ET (CC audience peak).
```

### Controversial Opinion sample (filled, AI hype take)

```
SLIDE 1 (HOOK): "Most AI skills you see online are useless."

SLIDE 2: "I have tested over 100 in the last year. Maybe 5 actually saved me time. The rest were

```

## Voice notes specific to CC

When slot-filling these formats with CC content, keep:

- Short sentences. One thought per line.
- No em-dashes (project rule).
- Simple words. "use" not "leverage", "set up" not "implement".
- Self-deprecating where authentic ("Background: I write 4 newsletters a week. It used to take 2 hours a piece.").
- Specific numbers (215K, 4 hours, 50 clicks, 62,000 emails). The audience trusts numbers more than adjectives.
- The Duryodhana Principle when relevant (knowing vs doing gap).
- "I just..." not "I leveraged..." (conversational, not business-speak).
- Founder voice (English is third language, blunt, dry humor).

When CC content references the the product, frame it as "the AI system that runs my business" not "AI Operating System". Save the the product positioning for product-specific slideshows where the term is the product name.

## When NOT to use CC examples

If the user is producing slideshows for a different product, niche, or persona, do not pull from these examples directly. Use the generic templates in `templates/` and slot-fill from the user's persona, product, and angle.

These examples exist for the case where the slideshow IS for CC content. They show the voice, the specificity, and the angle work that turns a generic format into a CC-native slideshow.
