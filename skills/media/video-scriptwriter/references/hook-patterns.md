# Hook Patterns for Video Ads

Four hook patterns designed specifically for direct-response video ads. Each pattern includes the psychology behind it, a script template, visual direction for the opening shot, text overlay approach, and an example.

The hook is the first 3-8 seconds. It determines whether the viewer watches or scrolls. Every hook must accomplish two things: (1) create a reason to keep watching, and (2) signal what the ad is about so the right people self-select.

---

## 1. Contrarian Warning

**Psychology:** Challenge a belief the viewer holds. This creates cognitive dissonance: they need to resolve the gap between what they believed and what you just claimed. That tension keeps them watching. The preempt ("I know that sounds crazy") prevents the dismissal reflex by acknowledging the claim is surprising.

**Script template:**
> "If you are still [common practice everyone does], you are [negative consequence they did not expect]. I know that sounds crazy, but [transition to credential or explanation]."

**Variations:**
- "Stop [common advice]. It is actually [opposite truth]."
- "The [common practice] everyone recommends? It is the reason your [metric] is [bad result]."
- "[Common belief] is a myth. Here is what actually works."

**Visual direction:**

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Setting** | Environment that signals expertise before words are spoken. Home office with books/whiteboards, clean studio, professional space. The background does the credentialing work |
| **Hero** | Medium shot, direct eye contact. Confident, slightly provocative energy. Leans forward or gestures on the key word. Not angry, not aggressive: think "friend who knows something you do not" |
| **Text Overlay** | Stagger the contrarian claim **word by word**. Each word appears in sync with the speaker, building the full phrase. This is critical for muted viewers: the word-by-word reveal creates the same tension as hearing it. Use bold, high-contrast text |
| **Camera** | Static or very subtle push-in (1-2% zoom over 5 seconds). No pans, no movement. The claim needs the viewer's full attention |

**Example:**
> "If you are still posting every day on Instagram, you are actually killing your reach. I know that sounds crazy, but after growing 400+ accounts, here is what I found."
>
> Text overlay: "If" ... "you're" ... "still" ... "posting" ... "every" ... "day" ... (staggered, bold white on dark)

**Best paired with:** Contrarian Expert framework (Act 1: Provoke)

## 2. Problem Exposer

**Psychology:** Name the hidden reason behind a problem the viewer already knows they have. The viewer is aware of the symptom but not the cause. When you reveal the cause, you demonstrate expertise and create a knowledge gap: they need to watch to learn the solution.

**Script template:**
> "The real reason your [thing] is not [desired result]? It has nothing to do with [what they think the problem is]. It is actually [hidden cause]."

**Variations:**
- "You are doing everything right with [activity] except this one thing."
- "There is a reason [common approach] stopped working for you."
- "Nobody talks about this, but [hidden factor] is why [bad result]."

**Visual direction:**

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Setting** | Can be casual or professional. The reveal works in any environment. A "lean-in" feel: slightly closer framing signals sharing a secret |
| **Hero** | Medium-tight shot. Conspiratorial energy, like sharing insider knowledge. Slight lean forward, lowered voice pitch on the reveal |
| **Text Overlay** | Display the "hidden cause" as a bold callout when revealed. Use a reveal animation: blur-to-sharp, or appear from behind an element. The hidden cause should visually "pop" |
| **Camera** | Start slightly wider, then cut to tighter on the reveal. The visual tightening mirrors the "zeroing in" on the real cause |

**Example:**
> "The real reason your ads are not converting? It has nothing to do with your targeting. It is your first three seconds."
>
> Text overlay: "It's your first 3 seconds" (appears sharp after blur effect)

**Best paired with:** Contrarian Expert (when the contrarian angle is about a hidden mechanism) or Story Bridge (when the problem is personal/relatable)

## 3. Result Reversal

**Psychology:** Show a surprising or counterintuitive result, then make the viewer wonder how it was achieved. The result itself is the hook: it creates a "how did they do that?" impulse. Works especially well when the result is visual (before/after, dashboard, product demo).

**Script template:**
> "[Specific impressive result] in [surprisingly short time / with surprisingly little effort]. And I did not even [expected requirement]. Here is how."

**Variations:**
- "I just [achieved X] without [Y that everyone thinks is required]."
- "[Number] [result] in [timeframe]. No [expected requirement]. No [common prerequisite]. Just [simple method]."
- "Watch this: [show result]. Now let me show you how."

**Visual direction:**

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Setting** | The result IS the setting. Screen recording of a dashboard, before/after split screen, product in action. For talking head: hold up the phone/laptop showing the result |
| **Hero** | If on camera: genuine excitement, not manufactured hype. If voiceover: the visual of the result is the hero |
| **Text Overlay** | Display the result metric large and prominent: "$47K in 30 days" or "Before / After" with the specific numbers. This is the most important text in the entire ad |
| **Camera** | Screen capture with highlight/zoom on the key metric, or split-screen with clear before/after labels |

**Example:**
> [Screen recording showing a Shopify dashboard with $47K in sales]
> "Forty-seven thousand dollars in the last 30 days from one product. No paid ads. No influencers. Let me show you the exact system."
>
> Text overlay: "$47,382 in 30 days" (large, accent color, top of frame)

**Best paired with:** Demo Proof framework (Act 1: Result First) or Contrarian Expert (when the result contradicts expectations)

## 4. Identity Mirror

**Psychology:** Call out the exact person and their exact situation so precisely that they feel like the ad was made for them. This works through recognition: when someone describes your situation better than you could, you trust them to understand your solution too. The specificity is what converts; vague call-outs get ignored.

**Script template:**
> "This is for [specific person] who [specific situation] and has been [specific frustrating experience]. If that is you, keep watching."

**Variations:**
- "If you are a [role] who has been [struggle] for [time period], this is going to change everything."
- "You are a [specific person]. You have tried [thing 1], [thing 2], and [thing 3]. None of it worked. Here is why."
- "I made this for [specific person] who [specific situation] and is tired of [specific frustration]."

**Visual direction:**

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Setting** | Match the viewer's world. If targeting freelancers: coffee shop or messy desk. If targeting parents: living room. If targeting founders: late-night office. The setting says "I am one of you" |
| **Hero** | Tight shot, warm energy. Speaking to one specific person, not a crowd. Genuine empathy, not performance |
| **Text Overlay** | Display the identity call-out: "For [specific person] who [specific situation]". Let it sit on screen for the full hook duration so scrolling viewers catch it |
| **Camera** | Tight to medium, static. Intimate framing. No movement that creates distance |

**Example:**
> "This is for the freelance designer who is charging $2K per project, working 60-hour weeks, and still cannot figure out how to hit $10K months. If that is you, keep watching."
>
> Text overlay: "For freelance designers stuck at $2K/project" (displayed across bottom third, held for 5 seconds)

**Best paired with:** Story Bridge framework (Act 1: Mirror) or Contrarian Expert (when combined with a problem exposer in Act 1)

## Choosing Your Hook

| Your situation | Best hook pattern |
|---------------|-------------------|
| You have a contrarian insight that challenges conventional wisdom | **Contrarian Warning** |
| The viewer's real problem is different from what they think | **Problem Exposer** |
| You can show an impressive, visual result immediately | **Result Reversal** |
| Your audience is a very specific niche with a shared struggle | **Identity Mirror** |
| You are not sure | Start with **Contrarian Warning** (highest engagement) or **Identity Mirror** (highest relevance) |

## Combining Hooks with Frameworks

| Hook Pattern | Default Framework | Also works with |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Contrarian Warning | Contrarian Expert | Story Bridge |
| Problem Exposer | Contrarian Expert | Story Bridge |
| Result Reversal | Demo Proof | Contrarian Expert |
| Identity Mirror | Story Bridge | Contrarian Expert |

Any hook can technically pair with any framework. The defaults above represent the most natural fits, but test alternatives.

---

## Hook Testing

Always provide at least two hook variations per blueprint. The hook is the highest-leverage element to A/B test:

- **Same framework, different hooks:** Test Contrarian Warning vs Identity Mirror on the same ad structure
- **Same hook, different specifics:** Test two different contrarian claims or two different identity call-outs
- **Same words, different visuals:** Test the same hook script with different backgrounds, framing, or text overlay styles

Test hooks before testing anything else in the ad. A better hook improves every metric downstream.
