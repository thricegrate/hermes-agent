# Production Style Guide

Complete visual and audio direction for video ad blueprints. This guide covers every production decision: pacing, visual style, text overlays, backgrounds, talent direction, camera work, sound design, and editing. Apply these guidelines when filling in the six elements for each take.

---

## Visual Pacing

**Core rule: new visual element every 5-10 seconds. Never let a single static frame last more than 10 seconds.**

A "visual element" is anything that changes what the viewer sees:
- Cut to a different angle or shot
- Text overlay appearing or changing
- B-roll footage cutting in
- Visual aid (diagram, annotation, screenshot) appearing
- Zoom or camera movement shift
- Split-screen or picture-in-picture change

### Pacing by Act

| Act Function | Pacing | Refresh Rate |
|-------------|--------|--------------|
| Hook (first 5-8 seconds) | Moderate | One change (text overlay stagger) |
| Credential | Fast | New element every 3-5 seconds |
| Education / Walkthrough | Medium-fast | New element every 5-8 seconds |
| Testimonials | Fast | New clip every 3-8 seconds |
| Offer / CTA | Moderate | Let the words breathe, fewer cuts |

### What Counts as "Static"

A shot is static if nothing changes for the viewer. Talking head with no text, no cuts, no visual aids, and no camera movement = static. Even a slight zoom or a text overlay appearing breaks the static feeling.

## Hybrid Production Style

The best-performing video ads combine two production qualities within the same ad:

### Polished Expert Segments (Acts 1-3 in Contrarian Expert)
- Clean, well-lit environment
- Consistent color grade
- Professional audio (lapel mic or shotgun)
- Smooth camera work (static or subtle movement)
- Branded text overlays with consistent font and color

### Raw UGC Testimonials (Act 4 in Contrarian Expert)
- Phone-quality video, natural lighting
- Each testimonial in a different authentic environment
- Slightly shaky camera is fine (selfie-style)
- Audio may have ambient noise (signals authenticity)
- No branded overlays on testimonial clips (just the result stat)

**Why hybrid works:** The polished segments build trust in the expert. The raw testimonials build trust in the results. The contrast between the two signals "this person is professional, AND real people are getting results." If everything is polished, it feels too corporate. If everything is raw, it feels too amateur.

## Text Overlay Strategy

**Assume sound-off.** 85% of social media video is watched without sound initially. A viewer scrolling with sound off should understand the core message from text overlays alone.

### What Gets a Text Overlay

| Content Type | Overlay Required? | Style |
|-------------|------------------|-------|
| Contrarian claim / hook | Yes, always | Word-by-word stagger, bold |
| Key insight or reframe | Yes, always | Static phrase, 5-7 words max |
| Specific result / number | Yes, always | Large, accent color |
| Credential points | Yes | Lower-third or bullet list |
| CTA / offer | Yes, always | With arrow or visual indicator |
| Transitional dialogue | No | Only if it carries meaning |
| Emotional / empathy lines | Optional | Can enhance, not required |

### Text Overlay Styles

**Word-by-word stagger (hooks only):**
- Each word appears in sync with the speaker
- Words build left to right or stack vertically
- Creates the same tension as hearing the claim build
- Use for the opening 3-5 seconds only

**Static phrase (education and reframes):**
- Full phrase appears at once, stays for 2-4 seconds
- Positioned consistently (top center or bottom third)
- Bold, high-contrast, readable at phone size
- 5-7 words maximum per overlay

**Result callout (testimonials and proof):**
- Large text, accent color (yellow, green, or brand highlight)
- Positioned prominently (center or top third)
- Shows the specific number or metric
- Stays on screen for the duration of that testimonial clip

**CTA overlay (final act):**
- Clear action text: "Comment WORKSHOP" or "Link in bio"
- Arrow, hand pointing, or visual indicator directing attention
- Stays on screen for the full CTA section (5+ seconds)

### Text Overlay Technical Specs

| Platform | Safe Zone | Max Font Size | Recommended Placement |
|----------|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Instagram Reels (9:16) | Avoid top 15% and bottom 20% | 48px | Center third of frame |
| TikTok (9:16) | Avoid top 10% and bottom 25% | 48px | Center to upper-center |
| Facebook Feed (1:1 / 4:5) | Avoid bottom 10% | 36px | Center to upper |
| YouTube Shorts (9:16) | Avoid bottom 20% | 48px | Center third |
| LinkedIn (1:1 / 16:9) | Full frame usable | 36px | Lower third or center |

## Background and Setting Direction

The background communicates before a single word is spoken. Choose settings that signal what the viewer needs to feel in each act.

### Setting by Signal

| Signal Needed | Setting Examples |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Expertise** | Home office with bookshelf/whiteboard, clean studio, professional workspace |
| **Relatability** | Kitchen table, living room couch, car (dashboard cam), walking outside |
| **Success/proof** | Results on screen, product in use, modern office, team environment |
| **Intimacy** | Tight framing anywhere, minimal background, soft lighting |
| **Energy** | Bright, well-lit space, outdoor with natural light, co-working space |

### Setting by Framework Act

**Contrarian Expert:**
- Acts 1-2 (Provoke, Credential): Expertise setting. Environment does the credentialing before words begin
- Act 3 (Empathize + Educate): Shift to intimacy for empathy phase, then educational setting (whiteboard, screen) for the teach
- Act 4 (Prove): Each testimonial in their own authentic environment. Diversity of settings signals diversity of results
- Act 5 (Offer): Return to Act 1 setting. Visual bookend creates completion

**Story Bridge:**
- Act 1 (Mirror): Relatability setting. Match the viewer's world
- Act 2 (Journey): Movement (walking shot, different locations) to signal a journey
- Act 3 (Transformation): Success/proof setting. Visual evidence of the outcome
- Act 4 (Bridge): Back to intimate/relatable. Full circle

**Demo Proof:**
- Act 1 (Result First): Screen recording or product shot. The product is the setting
- Act 2 (Context): Brief talking head, relatable setting
- Act 3 (Walkthrough): Screen recording or hands-on demo
- Act 4 (Stack + CTA): Return to talking head or clean graphic

## Hero and Talent Direction

"Hero" refers to whoever is on camera: the expert, the creator, the spokesperson, or UGC actors.

### Framing by Act Function

| Act Function | Framing | Why |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Hook | Medium shot (waist up) | Establishes presence without being too intimate too fast |
| Credential | Medium to medium-tight | Authority needs some space, but tighter than hook |
| Empathy | Tight (chest up) | Intimacy. The viewer should feel like a 1-on-1 conversation |
| Education | Medium or PIP | Room for visual aids. Talking head moves to corner or alternates |
| Testimonial | Selfie-style tight | Raw, authentic, personal |
| CTA | Medium, direct to camera | Confident, grounded, eye contact |

### Energy Direction

| Act Function | Energy | Body Language |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Hook / Provoke | Confident, slightly provocative | Lean forward on key words, controlled gestures |
| Credential | Matter-of-fact | Casual hand gestures, relaxed shoulders |
| Empathy | Warm, understanding | Softer eyes, slower pace, slight head tilt |
| Education | Engaged, clear | Purposeful gestures, points to visual aids |
| Testimonial | Genuine, natural | No direction; let people be themselves |
| CTA | Calm confidence | Direct eye contact, steady, inviting not pushy |

### Wardrobe Guidelines

- **Expert segments:** Clean, simple. Solid colors that contrast with the background. No logos, no distracting patterns
- **Testimonials:** Whatever they actually wear. Authenticity matters more than aesthetics
- **General rule:** The viewer should focus on words and visuals, not clothing. If you notice the wardrobe, it is too loud

## Visual Aids

Visual aids are layered graphics used during educational or explanatory segments. They transform abstract concepts into visual understanding.

### Types of Visual Aids

| Type | When to Use | How to Execute |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Diagram** | Explaining a process or system | Simple shapes, arrows, labels. Animate elements appearing in sequence |
| **Before/After** | Showing transformation | Split screen or sequential. Label clearly |
| **Annotation** | Highlighting part of a screen or image | Arrows, circles, highlights on the key element |
| **Number callout** | Emphasizing a metric or result | Large text, accent color, appears with emphasis sound |
| **List build** | Stacking points or features | Items appear one at a time as they are mentioned |
| **Comparison** | Showing old way vs new way | Two columns, X marks on old way, check marks on new |

### Visual Aid Rules

1. **One idea per aid.** Do not cram multiple concepts into one graphic
2. **Animate the build.** Elements should appear as they are discussed, not all at once
3. **3-second minimum.** Each visual aid stays on screen long enough to process
4. **Label everything.** Do not assume the viewer understands unlabeled graphics
5. **Simple over clever.** Clean shapes, clear text, high contrast. No 3D effects, no shadows, no decorative elements

## Sound Design

Sound design supports the visual story without calling attention to itself.

### Music

- **Low background track:** Subtle, not driving. The viewer should not notice it consciously, but its absence would feel wrong
- **Genre:** Lo-fi, ambient electronic, or minimal acoustic depending on brand. No lyrics
- **Dynamic:** Slightly louder during credential and proof sections, quieter during empathy and CTA
- **Volume:** 15-20% of dialogue volume. If you have to lower dialogue to hear music, the music is too loud

### Transition Sounds

- **Light whoosh** between major act changes
- **Subtle pop or click** when text overlays appear
- **Brief riser** before revealing a result or key insight
- Keep all transition sounds under 0.5 seconds

### Silence

- Strategic silence for 0.5-1 second before a key claim or result can be more powerful than any sound
- The contrast between sound and silence creates emphasis
- Use sparingly: 1-2 silence moments per ad maximum

## Camera Direction

### Shot Types

| Shot | Framing | Use For |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Wide** | Full body or environment | Establishing shots, showing setting context |
| **Medium** | Waist to head | Primary talking head, authority, presentations |
| **Medium-tight** | Chest to head | Credentials, conversational moments |
| **Tight** | Shoulders to head | Emotion, intimacy, CTAs |
| **Extreme tight** | Face only | Key emotional moments (use sparingly) |
| **PIP (Picture-in-Picture)** | Talking head in corner | During screen recordings, walkthroughs |

### Camera Movement

| Movement | Effect | Use For |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Static** | Stable, authoritative | Hooks, CTAs, credential stacks |
| **Subtle push-in** (1-2% zoom over 5s) | Draws viewer in | Building to a key reveal or insight |
| **Handheld / slight movement** | Authentic, raw | Testimonials, casual segments |
| **Pan** | Reveals context | Showing environment, before/after spaces |

### Angle Changes

- Use at least 2 different angles/framings in the expert sections to create visual variety
- Cut between angles on natural speech breaks, not mid-sentence
- The A-angle (primary) and B-angle (secondary) should be noticeably different: change both the framing and the horizontal position

## Editing Direction

### Cut Rhythm

| Section | Cuts Per 10 Seconds | Notes |
|---------|---------------------|-------|
| Hook | 1-2 | Few cuts. Let the claim land |
| Credential | 2-3 | Faster. Stack points quickly |
| Education | 2-4 | Match to visual aids appearing |
| Testimonials | 3-5 | Fast montage. Each clip is tight |
| CTA | 1 | Slow down. Let the offer breathe |

### Transition Types

- **Hard cut:** Default for 90% of transitions. Clean, fast, no effect
- **Jump cut:** Within the same shot, removing pauses/filler. Fine for talking head
- **Morph cut:** Smooth face-match between angles. Use in expert sections
- **Flash / brief white frame:** Between major act changes. 2-3 frames of white creates a subtle beat
- **No fades, dissolves, or wipes.** These read as dated. Hard cuts only

### Color Grading

- **Expert segments:** Clean, slightly warm, well-exposed. Professional but not cold
- **Testimonials:** Minimal grading. Let the raw quality show. Light contrast adjustment only
- **Consistency within acts:** All expert segments should match. All testimonials can vary (they are different people/environments)
