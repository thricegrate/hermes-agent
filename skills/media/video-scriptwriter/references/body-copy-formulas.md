# Body Copy Formulas

Body copy is the supporting text for video ads: the caption, the ad description, and the backbone of the spoken script. Good body copy works independently of the video. A viewer who reads the caption without watching should still understand the offer and feel compelled to act.

Three formulas below, each with a template, a filled example, and tone notes.

---

## 1. Problem Stack (Primary)

The most direct formula. Calls out the viewer, names their failed attempts, reframes the problem, and introduces the fix. Reads like a helpful post, not an ad.

### Structure

| Line | Purpose | Length |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Line 1** | Call out the exact person with the exact problem | 1 sentence |
| **Lines 2-3** | Name everything they have tried that has not worked | 2-3 short sentences |
| **Lines 4-5** | Reframe why those solutions failed | 1-2 sentences |
| **Line 6** | Introduce the fix with a low-friction CTA | 1 sentence |

### Template

```
[Identity] who [specific problem]:

You have tried [solution 1]. You have tried [solution 2].
Maybe you even [solution 3 that feels like overkill].

The problem is not [what they think]. The problem is [reframe].

[Product/method] fixes this by [mechanism in one phrase].

[CTA: comment/click/link] to [what they get].
```

### Example: Online Course for Freelance Designers

```
Freelance designers stuck under $5K/month:

You have tried raising your rates. You have tried niching down.
You have posted on Twitter, cold-emailed agencies, built a portfolio site.

None of it worked because pricing is not the problem.
The problem is you are selling deliverables instead of outcomes.

The Outcome Positioning Workshop shows you how to reframe
every proposal so clients pay for results, not hours.

Comment "OUTCOME" and I will send you the free workshop link.
```

### Tone Notes

- Reads like a friend diagnosing your problem, not a marketer pitching
- No hype words: no "revolutionary," "game-changing," "incredible"
- Name real solutions they have tried. The more specific, the more the viewer feels understood
- The reframe is the key moment: it has to be genuinely insightful, not just a clever turn of phrase
- CTA is conversational: "comment X" or "click the link" feels lighter than "buy now" or "enroll today"

### When to Use

Best for: Contrarian Expert and Story Bridge frameworks. Any offer where the viewer has already tried to solve the problem themselves.

## 2. Bridge Formula

Paints the current state, paints the desired state, then positions the product as the bridge between them. Works by making the gap feel both painful and closeable.

### Structure

| Section | Purpose | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Current State** | Describe where they are right now (with emotional weight) | 2 sentences |
| **Desired State** | Describe where they want to be (specific, tangible) | 2 sentences |
| **The Bridge** | Position the product as the connection between the two | 1-2 sentences |
| **CTA** | Low-friction action to cross the bridge | 1 sentence |

### Template

```
Right now you [current painful situation].
[Emotional consequence of staying here].

Imagine instead: [desired state with specific detail].
[What life looks like when the problem is solved].

[Product] is the bridge.
[One sentence on what it does or includes].

[CTA] to get started.
```

### Example: Meal Planning App

```
Right now you spend 45 minutes every Sunday staring at recipes,
then end up ordering takeout by Wednesday anyway.

Imagine instead: a full week of meals planned in 3 minutes.
Grocery list auto-generated. No decision fatigue. No food waste.

Prep Plan does this for you. It learns what you like
and builds your week in one tap.

Download free for your first 2 weeks. Link in bio.
```

### Tone Notes

- The current state should sting. Be specific about the pain, not vague
- The desired state should feel achievable, not aspirational fantasy
- "The bridge" metaphor works because it implies a journey that is short and supported
- Keep the product description to one mechanism, not a feature list
- CTA should feel like a natural next step, not a sales push

### When to Use

Best for: Story Bridge framework. Products with a clear before/after. Offers where the viewer knows what they want but does not know how to get there.

## 3. Story Stack

A micro-narrative in body copy form. Tells a compressed version of a transformation story: the struggle, the discovery, the result. The reader inserts themselves into the story.

### Structure

| Beat | Purpose | Length |
|------|---------|--------|
| **The Struggle** | Set up the relatable problem through a character | 2 sentences |
| **The Discovery** | Introduce the turning point | 1-2 sentences |
| **The Result** | Show the specific outcome with numbers | 1-2 sentences |
| **The Offer** | Make the same path available to the reader | 1 sentence |
| **CTA** | How to start | 1 sentence |

### Template

```
[Time period] ago, [character] was [struggling with specific problem].
[Emotional detail about how it felt].

Then [character] [discovered/tried/found] [product or method].
[One specific thing that changed].

[Time period] later: [specific measurable result].
[Second result or lifestyle change].

Everything [character] used is inside [product].

[CTA] to get the same [method/system/tool].
```

### Example: Email Marketing Course

```
6 months ago, Sarah was sending newsletters to 2,000 subscribers
and getting 12 clicks per email. She was ready to quit.

Then she learned the EEC method: Engage, Educate, CTA.
She rewrote one email using the framework and got 340 clicks.

6 months later: 18,000 subscribers, $4K/month from email alone.
She sends 3 emails a week and it takes her 2 hours total.

Everything Sarah used is inside the Email Growth Playbook.

Comment "EMAIL" and I will send you the free first module.
```

### Tone Notes

- The character can be "I," a client, or a composite. Use whatever is most relatable
- Specific numbers are mandatory. "Grew my email list" is weak. "Went from 2K to 18K subscribers" is proof
- The discovery beat should name one specific thing, not "everything changed"
- The result should include at least one number and one lifestyle detail
- Keep the story compressed: 5-7 lines total. This is body copy, not a blog post

### When to Use

Best for: Story Bridge and Contrarian Expert frameworks. Any offer where a transformation story exists. Especially effective when the character matches the target viewer's demographics.

## Choosing Your Formula

| Situation | Best formula |
|-----------|-------------|
| Viewer has tried to solve the problem before | **Problem Stack** |
| Clear before/after states exist | **Bridge Formula** |
| You have a compelling transformation story | **Story Stack** |
| You are not sure | **Problem Stack** (most versatile) |

## Formula + Framework Pairings

| Formula | Primary Framework | Also works with |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Problem Stack | Contrarian Expert | Story Bridge, Demo Proof |
| Bridge Formula | Story Bridge | Demo Proof |
| Story Stack | Story Bridge | Contrarian Expert |

---

## Body Copy Rules (All Formulas)

1. **Under 7 lines.** Social media captions get truncated. Front-load the most important information
2. **No em dashes.** Use colons, semicolons, commas, or periods instead
3. **One idea per line.** Short sentences. White space between sections
4. **Specific over general.** Numbers, timeframes, and named methods beat adjectives
5. **CTA uses a trigger word.** "Comment WORKSHOP" or "Comment PLAN" gives the viewer a simple action and helps you track response
6. **Test the caption alone.** Cover the video. If the caption alone does not make someone curious, rewrite it
