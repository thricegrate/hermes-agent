# UGC Cold Traffic Script Architecture (6 Sections)

A persuasion sequence specifically designed for AI-generated UGC targeting cold audiences via paid social or organic reels. Each section performs a specific psychological function that depends on the sections before it having done their job correctly.

**Key difference from other frameworks:** This architecture starts with persona identification, not a hook. Cold traffic needs to feel SEEN before it will listen. The Persona Signal replaces the traditional pattern-interrupt opening because strangers who feel identified are more receptive than strangers who are merely surprised.

**Requires:** A completed 5-Layer Persona Depth Profile (from `niche-finder`). Without it, the architecture produces generic scripts that don't convert.

---

## The 6 Sections

### Section 1: The Persona Signal (seconds 0-5)

**Purpose:** Make the exact right person feel directly addressed before any claim is made.

**Formula:** [Specific situation description] + [Specific detail that signals you understand the nuance of that situation]

**Example:**
> "If you had a baby in the last 18 months and you've been doing everything right -- eating clean, staying active, drinking your water -- and nothing is moving"

This has 3 specificity layers: the life stage, the timeframe, and the specific attempts made. Each layer narrows the audience but deepens the identification response.

**Uses:** Persona Depth Layers 1-2 (Demographic Shell + Psychographic Core)

**The test:** Read the first sentence to someone who does NOT match the target persona. If they immediately know the content is not for them, the persona signal is working correctly.

**What to avoid:**
- Generic openers ("Hey guys," "Are you struggling with...")
- Hook-style pattern interrupts (save those for other frameworks)
- Addressing a demographic instead of a situation

---

### Section 2: Pain Acknowledgment (seconds 5-15)

**Purpose:** Convert the identification signal into emotional resonance by describing the lived experience with enough specificity that the viewer feels understood rather than categorized.

**Must contain (minimum):**
1. One specific functional pain point named in the persona's own language
2. One specific emotional pain point that goes deeper than the functional problem
3. One specific failed attempt the persona has made, and the frustration that followed

**Example:**
> "You've tried the calorie counting, you've tried the workout programs your friend recommended, and honestly at this point you're just tired. Not tired of trying -- tired of feeling like your body is working against you."

**Uses:** Persona Depth Layer 3 (Pain Architecture -- exact language from review mining)

**What the pain section is NOT:**
- A list of problems
- A clinical description of challenges
- A vague acknowledgment that things are hard
- Paraphrased marketer language

It IS a specific, emotionally authentic description of what it feels like to be this person on a specific difficult day.

---

### Section 3: The Mechanism Bridge (seconds 15-25)

**Purpose:** Simultaneously validates the viewer's experience of failure (not their fault) AND introduces the category of solution the product belongs to without sounding promotional.

**Formula:** [Explanation of the root cause that failed alternatives weren't addressing] + [Introduction of the approach that addresses the actual root cause]

**Example:**
> "What I didn't understand was that the standard approaches all address the symptom -- the weight itself -- while completely ignoring the hormonal environment that was actively working against everything I was trying to do. Once I understood that, everything I was doing wrong made sense."

**Uses:** Persona Depth Layer 4 (Failed Alternatives Map -- the pattern across failures)

**Critical details:**
- Validates the failed attempts (not the viewer's fault, the approach was wrong)
- Introduces the unique mechanism WITHOUT naming the product yet
- Must feel like a natural continuation of the pain section, NOT a pivot to selling
- The viewer should nod along, not lean back

**What to avoid:**
- Naming the product in this section
- Marketing claims ("the most powerful," "revolutionary")
- Making the viewer feel stupid for trying what they tried

---

### Section 4: The Solution Introduction (seconds 25-35)

**Purpose:** Introduce the product through the lens of the unique mechanism. The viewer should understand what the product does differently before being told what it is.

**The product is introduced as the answer to the question the mechanism bridge raised.** Not as a product placement interrupting a story.

**Example:**
> "That's when I found [Product] -- it's specifically designed around [the mechanism from Section 3], so instead of fighting your body, you're actually working with it for the first time."

**What to avoid in this section:**
- Feature lists
- Superlative claims ("the best," "the most powerful")
- Price mentions
- Urgency language ("limited time," "act now")
- Anything that activates the advertising skepticism response

**Keep it brief.** The mechanism bridge already did the heavy lifting. This section just names what they've been waiting to hear about.

---

### Section 5: The Transformation Description (seconds 35-48)

**Purpose:** Paint the after state with enough emotional detail that the viewer can genuinely imagine themselves experiencing it. This converts emotional resonance into purchase intent.

**Must contain (minimum):**
1. A specific, measurable outcome
2. At least 1 specific moment or experience that illustrates the transformation (not just describes it)
3. At least 1 secondary transformation beyond the primary goal

**Example:**
> "I'm 22 pounds down in 10 weeks and I feel like myself again. Not just the number -- the energy. I'm actually present with my kids instead of running on empty. And I wore a dress last week that I hadn't put on since before I was pregnant."

That's 3 transformation layers: the functional outcome (22 lbs), the energy transformation (present with kids), and the specific moment (the dress).

**Uses:** Persona Depth Layer 5 (Transformation Vision -- specific moments + secondary transformations)

**What to avoid:**
- Generic outcomes ("feel great," "changed my life")
- Only functional results without emotional depth
- Claims that sound too good to be true

---

### Section 6: The Call to Action (seconds 48-55)

**Purpose:** End the conversation naturally, like one person telling another about something that genuinely helped them.

**The pressure-free recommendation format outperforms urgency-driven CTAs on cold organic traffic** because it doesn't activate advertising skepticism at the moment the viewer is closest to a purchase decision.

**Example:**
> "The link is below if you want to see what I used. I would have found this a lot earlier if someone had told me about it."

**What to avoid:**
- Urgency language ("limited time," "don't miss out," "act now")
- Directive tone ("click the link," "buy now," "sign up today")
- Discount mentions (signals desperation on cold traffic)
- Multiple CTAs

**If routing to quiz funnel:** "There's a quick quiz in the link below that'll tell you which [product/approach] is right for your specific situation." This frames the quiz as personalized help, not a lead capture mechanism.

## Timing Tables by Duration

### 15-20 Second Version (Abbreviated)
```
[0-5s]   Persona Signal (abbreviated to 1 sentence)
[5-12s]  Mechanism + Solution (combined, core insight only)
[12-18s] CTA
```
Contains only: identification, the core mechanism statement, and the recommendation. No pain section, no full transformation. Use for retargeting audiences who've already seen the full version.

### 30-35 Second Version (Standard)
```
[0-5s]   Persona Signal
[5-12s]  Pain Acknowledgment (abbreviated: 1 functional + 1 emotional, no failed attempt)
[12-20s] Mechanism Bridge (abbreviated: root cause only, no alternative validation)
[20-28s] Solution + Transformation (combined: product name + 1 measurable outcome)
[28-33s] CTA
```

### 45-55 Second Version (Full)
```
[0-5s]   Persona Signal
[5-15s]  Pain Acknowledgment (full: functional + emotional + failed attempt)
[15-25s] Mechanism Bridge (full: why alternatives failed + root cause approach)
[25-35s] Solution Introduction
[35-48s] Transformation Description (full: measurable + moment + secondary)
[48-55s] CTA
```

**Pacing:** 2.5-3 words per second. 55s = 138-165 words total.

## Variation Production

From 1 validated 45-55s script, produce these variations:

1. **5 hook variations:** Swap Section 1 (Persona Signal) with 5 different UGC cold-traffic hook categories (see `video-hook` references) while keeping Sections 2-6 identical
2. **3 length variations:** 15-20s, 30-35s, and 45-55s versions of the same concept
3. **2 delivery register variations:** High energy (enthusiasm in transformation section) vs calm authority (understated confidence throughout)

That's 30 variations from 1 validated script (5 hooks x 3 lengths x 2 registers).
