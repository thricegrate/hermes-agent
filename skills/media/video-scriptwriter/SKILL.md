# Video Scriptwriter

Write production-ready video ad scripts with full scene-by-scene breakdowns and optional take-by-take visual direction. This skill combines quick scripting (dialogue + B-roll + timing) with strategic framework-driven blueprints (setting, hero, dialogue, text overlay, camera, edit notes for every take). Choose the depth you need.

For YouTube long-form scripts (8-20 minutes), route to `youtube-producer`.

For direct-response ad scripts (paid Meta/TikTok ads, VSLs, podcast clips, skits, authority spots, POV creator stories), see the **Direct Response Ad Script System** section below for the complete pipeline (hook engineering → format selection → script generation → review). The strategic frameworks (A-E) above remain valid for direct-response work but the Direct Response system adds format-specific structures, hook scoring, and a script reviewer that catches voice and structural issues before production.

## Before You Start

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If private product-marketing context notes exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered.

Gather this context (ask if not provided):

### 1. Offer
- What are you selling? (Course, coaching, template, tool, community, service)
- Price point and purchase friction level
- What transformation or outcome does it deliver?
- Landing page URL (if available)

### 2. Target Viewer
- Who is the ideal viewer? (Role, situation, experience level)
- What is their #1 pain point right now?
- What have they already tried that has not worked?

### 3. Proof Assets
- Personal results to share? (Numbers, timeline, before/after)
- Testimonials available? Video or text?
- Case studies, screenshots, or data points?

### 4. Creator Context
- Who is on camera? (Creator, spokesperson, UGC actors)
- What credentials or authority can be stacked?
- Available shooting setup: studio, home office, outdoor, screen recording?

### 5. Format
- Target duration: 15s, 30s, 45s, 60s, or 90s?
- Primary platform: Meta, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn?
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical), 1:1 (square), 16:9 (landscape)?
- Depth needed: quick script or full blueprint with visual direction?

## Emotional Hook Rule (MANDATORY)

Every script must open with a line that triggers a gut-level emotion in the first 1-3 seconds. Before writing, pick your target emotion: WOW, WTF, OMG, LOL, UGH, WHOA, NAH, YEP, OOF, HMM, DAMN, SMH. The first line of dialogue should express or trigger that emotion. In talking-head scripts, the actor CAN say the abbreviation literally ("WTF, this AI just..." or "OMG you guys..."). See `skills/humanizer/SKILL.md` for the full list.

## The Three Frameworks

Choose the framework that matches your offer type and available assets. Each uses a different persuasion strategy but all produce the same output format. For detailed act-by-act breakdowns, timing, and examples, see `references/frameworks.md`.

### A. Contrarian Expert (5 Acts)

Open with a claim that challenges conventional wisdom, then back it with authority, education, proof, and a proportional offer.

**Acts:** Provoke, Credential, Empathize + Educate, Prove, Offer
**Best for:** Expert-led offers, courses, coaching, consulting, info products
**Requires:** Creator with credentials, contrarian angle, testimonials

### B. Story Bridge (4 Acts)

Mirror the viewer's situation, walk them through a discovery journey, show the transformation, and bridge them to the same path.

**Acts:** Mirror, Journey, Transformation, Bridge
**Best for:** Personal brands, community offers, relatable products, lifestyle offers
**Requires:** A compelling personal or customer story with clear before/after

### C. Demo Proof (4 Acts)

Lead with the end result, give brief context, walk through the product step by step, then stack value and close.

**Acts:** Result First, Context, Walkthrough, Stack + CTA
**Best for:** Tools, software, templates, physical products, anything demonstrable
**Requires:** Screen recording or hands-on footage capability

### D. UGC Cold Traffic (6 Sections)

Built specifically for AI-generated UGC targeting cold audiences via paid social or organic reels. Differs from the other 3 frameworks by starting with persona identification instead of a hook, because cold traffic needs to feel SEEN before it will listen.

**Sections:** Persona Signal, Pain Acknowledgment, Mechanism Bridge, Solution Introduction, Transformation Description, CTA
**Best for:** AI avatar UGC, paid social ads targeting cold audiences, organic reels for product promotion
**Requires:** A completed 5-Layer Persona Depth Profile (from `niche-finder` Step 4b). Without it, the script will be generic.

**Full architecture, timing tables, variation production, and quality checklists:** See `references/ugc-6-section-architecture.md`
**Fill-in-the-blank template:** See `templates/ugc-6-section-template.md`

### E. Sheridan Observer (Scene-Driven)

Drop the viewer into a scene. Show, don't tell. Raise a question, don't preach. Based on Taylor Sheridan's screenwriting craft (Sicario, 1883, Landman).

**Beats:** The Window (scene drop) > The Surrogate (one person's eyes) > The Gap (something doesn't add up) > The Reveal (behavior shows truth) > The Cost (what was lost) > The Question (open ending)
**Best for:** Personal brand content, build-in-public, story-first content where entertainment IS the product, organic video where you want viewers to sit with a feeling rather than click a link
**Requires:** A real story with a cost and no neat answer. If you have a clear lesson, use Story Bridge instead.

**Key Sheridan principles for video:**
- Observer, not judge. Don't tell the viewer what to think.
- Simple premise, complex character moment. One situation, all the depth in the person.
- Let silence do work. Pauses after statements. The gap between what's said and what's meant.
- End with a question, not a CTA. The question IS the hook for the next video.

**Full framework reference:** `skills/storyteller/references/sheridan-framework.md`

### Framework Selection Guide

| Offer Type | Recommended | Why |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| Online course / coaching | Contrarian Expert | Authority + education builds trust |
| Community / membership | Story Bridge | Belonging requires emotional connection |
| SaaS / tool / template | Demo Proof | Seeing is believing |
| Service / agency | Contrarian Expert | Challenge status quo, prove expertise |
| Physical product | Demo Proof | Show it in action |
| Build-in-public / personal brand | Sheridan Observer | Scene-driven, no preaching, raises questions |
| Personal brand | Story Bridge | Your story IS the product |
| UGC for cold social traffic | UGC Cold Traffic | Persona identification before pitch builds trust with strangers |

### Hook Psychology: 3 Drivers of Attention

1. **Curiosity** (61% of top-performing titles): create an information gap
2. **Desire** (46%): sell the outcome
3. **Fear** (40%): threat avoidance

Stack multiple drivers for exponential impact. Ethical boundary: Zone 2 of the Ethical Clickbait spectrum (most extreme truthful version). The Comment Section Test: would viewers feel misled after watching?

For the direct-response 5-lever framework (Curiosity gap, Fear/loss aversion, Contrarian, Social proof, Identity protection) with scoring rubric, see [references/hook-engineering.md](references/hook-engineering.md). The 5 levers are tactical extensions of the 3 Drivers above, scoped specifically for direct-response ad hook generation in batches.

## Direct Response Ad Script System

A complete pipeline for direct-response ad scripts: hook engineering, format selection, script generation, and review. Use this when producing paid Meta/TikTok ads, VSLs, podcast clips, skits, authority spots, or POV creator stories.

The strategic frameworks (Contrarian Expert, Story Bridge, Demo Proof, UGC Cold Traffic, Sheridan Observer) above are concept-level. The Direct Response system below is execution-level. They run together: pick a strategic framework for the concept, then pick a direct-response format for the execution.

### Voice rules (mandatory for all direct-response scripts)

- No em dashes
- No "I'm obsessed", "game-changing", "transformational"
- No "It's not X, it's Y" structures (every variant)
- No "Most guys / most women / most people" as line openers
- No two-fragment contrast (matched short fragments separated by a period)
- No three-word stacked closers
- The mechanism enters before the product
- In long-form formats, product enters at 75-85% of runtime
- Every line sounds natural aloud
- If a line takes longer than 4 seconds to say, shorten it

Full system prompt: [references/direct-response-voice-system.md](references/direct-response-voice-system.md).

### The 4 direct-response format families

Each family fits different positioning, audiences, and runtime budgets.

#### 1. Two-actor formats (45-60 sec or 30-45 sec)

The conversation feels overheard, not performed. Information unfolds through dialogue.

- **Podcast clip (Host + Expert)**: mechanism-heavy offers, authority positioning, 45-60 sec
- **Skit (Skeptic + Believer)**: objection handling, fast-scrolling environments, 30-45 sec

Reference: [references/two-actor-formats.md](references/two-actor-formats.md). Templates: [templates/two-actor-scripts.md](templates/two-actor-scripts.md).

#### 2. Authority-led formats (30-45 sec or 25-40 sec)

Authority must feel earned, not performed. Real authority is calm and specific, not hyped.

- **Pharmacist / Doctor in-studio**: credibility-heavy categories, deeper mechanisms, 30-45 sec
- **Street interview (authority reveal)**: curiosity, retention, social proof, 25-40 sec

Reference: [references/authority-led-formats.md](references/authority-led-formats.md). Templates: [templates/authority-led-scripts.md](templates/authority-led-scripts.md).

#### 3. Long-form formats (3-4 min or 30-90 sec)

Long-form earns the right to sell. Product enters at 75-85% of runtime.

- **Long-form yapper / VSL**: deep mechanism, identity transformation, 3-4 min
- **POV personal story**: relatability, creator-led, 30-90 sec

Reference: [references/long-form-formats.md](references/long-form-formats.md). Templates: [templates/long-form-scripts.md](templates/long-form-scripts.md).

### Hook engineering (5 levers + 4-dim scoring)

Generate 10 hook variations across 5 psychological levers, score them across 4 dimensions, pick the top 3 to produce.

The 5 levers:
1. Curiosity gap
2. Fear / loss aversion
3. Contrarian
4. Social proof
5. Identity protection

The 4 scoring dimensions (each 1-10, total /40):
- Stop-power
- Specificity
- Lever clarity
- Natural cadence

Reference: [references/hook-engineering.md](references/hook-engineering.md). Template: [templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md](templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md).

Mandatory before production. Checks voice violations, structural weaknesses, and pacing. Outputs production-ready: YES/NO with specific line-level fixes.

### Anti-AI script tells

10 patterns specific to scripts (different from prose AI tells covered by `humanizer`):

1. The voiceover cadence
2. The therapist reframe
3. The stage-direction adjective
4. The symptom list that ends in a crescendo
5. The fake specificity trap
6. The mechanism-reveal that sounds like a press release
7. The CTA that breaks character
8. The emotional beat with no object
9. The "imagine if" hypothetical
10. The wisdom-bestowing closer

Plus the read-aloud test and the 60-second pre-production checklist.

### The direct-response workflow

The full pipeline runs in this order:

1. Define inputs (persona, awareness stage, insight, format)
2. Generate 10 hooks across 5 levers ([templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md](templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md))
3. Score hooks, pick top 3
4. Pick the format family for the strongest hook
5. Generate the full script using the format-specific template
6. Run the script reviewer ([templates/script-reviewer.md](templates/script-reviewer.md))
7. Apply fixes
8. Re-run reviewer if 5+ fixes were made
9. Send to production (creator brief, voice generation, video generation per `ugc-production` if at scale)

When a script wins, run the hook variation generator (Prompt 10 in [templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md](templates/hook-generator-and-scorer.md)) to spin 10 alternate hooks that flow into the existing body. For the broader UGC component-level Winner Variation Pipeline that turns 1 winning reel into 1,000+ tested combinations, see `skills/ugc-production/templates/variation-from-winner-reel.md`.

## The Creator Video Framework (MANDATORY for scripts 60s+)

Every video script 60 seconds or longer MUST follow this 5-act structure:

### Act 1: HOOK (first 3-10 seconds)
- Pattern interrupt or big promise in the FIRST sentence
- Tease the most valuable thing ("...and at the end I'll show you the one thing that 10x'd my results")
- Create an open loop the viewer MUST close by watching

### Act 2: BRAG (10-30 seconds)
- Establish credibility through achievements
- Show proof: numbers, screenshots, results
- Factual, not arrogant. Let the numbers speak.

### Act 3: VALUE BOMB (30%-60% of total runtime)
- Deliver the MAJORITY of promised value early
- Give away 50%+ of the good stuff right away
- Be genuinely useful. Do not hold back.

### Act 4: INTEGRATION (15-30 seconds, naturally woven in)
- CTA, sponsor read, or product mention that flows FROM the content
- Must feel like a natural part of the script, never a jarring interruption

### Act 5: PAYOFF + CLOSE (remaining runtime)
- Deliver the teased "most valuable thing" from the hook
- Use the South Park "but/therefore" principle (never "and then")
- Leave the viewer feeling they got MORE than promised

### Framework Quick Reference
```
[0-5%]   HOOK: Big promise + open loop + tease the payoff
[5-15%]  BRAG: Credentials + proof + "why listen to me"
[15-60%] VALUE BOMB: Deliver 50%+ of promised value generously
[60-70%] INTEGRATION: Natural CTA/sponsor woven into content
[70-95%] PAYOFF: Deliver the teased thing + emotional rollercoaster
[95-100%] CLOSE: Final CTA + callback to hook
```

### Advanced Retention Techniques

Layer these ON TOP of the 5-act framework:

1. **Triple Hook Stack:** 3 hooks in first 15 seconds: situation + action + payoff tease
2. **Oscillation Pattern:** Every "up" followed by "down." 5+ emotional state changes per minute
3. **Near-Miss Pattern:** 2-3 almost-wins before the real win. Frustration compounds.
4. **Vulnerability Before Victory:** Lowest point RIGHT BEFORE breakthrough
5. **Expectation Subversion:** Promise one ending, deliver something deeper
6. **Ticking Clock:** Deadlines, countdowns, urgency
7. **Escalating Stakes:** Each act raises what's at risk
8. **Tension-Release-Tension Cycle:** Breather moments between intensity peaks
9. **Show-Don't-Tell Brag:** Let the story prove competence
10. **The Reframe Close:** Reframe what the story was really about

Full analysis: `private project content/storytelling-analysis-car-theft-video.md` and `private project content/storytelling-analysis-100hrs-ai-video.md`

## Script Structure by Duration

### 15-Second Script (Stories/Reels)
```
[0-2s]  HOOK - Pattern interrupt
[2-8s]  VALUE - One key insight
[8-13s] CTA - Clear next step
[13-15s] END CARD
```

### 25-30 Second Script (Standard)
```
[0-3s]   HOOK - Pattern interrupt
[3-10s]  PROBLEM - Agitate pain point
[10-20s] SOLUTION - Present offer
[20-27s] PROOF + CTA - Social proof + action
[27-30s] END CARD
```

### 45-60 Second Script (Long-form)
```
[0-5s]   HOOK - Strong opening
[5-15s]  PROBLEM - Deep agitation
[15-30s] SOLUTION - Explain transformation
[30-45s] PROOF - Testimonials/results
[45-55s] CTA - Clear call to action
[55-60s] END CARD
```

### 60-Second Framework Timing

| Framework | Act 1 | Act 2 | Act 3 | Act 4 | Act 5 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Contrarian Expert | 0:00-0:05 | 0:05-0:10 | 0:10-0:30 | 0:30-0:45 | 0:45-0:60 |
| Story Bridge | 0:00-0:08 | 0:08-0:25 | 0:25-0:45 | 0:45-0:60 | - |
| Demo Proof | 0:00-0:05 | 0:05-0:15 | 0:15-0:45 | 0:45-0:60 | - |

### 90-Second Framework Timing

| Framework | Act 1 | Act 2 | Act 3 | Act 4 | Act 5 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Contrarian Expert | 0:00-0:08 | 0:08-0:15 | 0:15-0:45 | 0:45-1:10 | 1:10-1:30 |
| Story Bridge | 0:00-0:10 | 0:10-0:35 | 0:35-1:05 | 1:05-1:30 | - |
| Demo Proof | 0:00-0:08 | 0:08-0:20 | 0:20-1:05 | 1:05-1:30 | - |

**Pacing:** 2.5-3 words per second for natural delivery. 60s = 150-180 words. 90s = 225-270 words.

**Compression rule:** When cutting from 90s to 60s, shorten Act 3 first. Never compress Act 1 (hook) or the final act (offer/CTA).

## Workflow

### 1. Receive Brief
From `marketing-ads`, `creative-director`, or direct request: ad concept, funnel stage, target duration, messaging, landing page, brand voice.

### 2. Select Framework + Hook
Choose framework using the selection guide. Select a hook pattern from `references/frameworks.md`. Each framework has a default hook pattern, but any hook works with any framework.

### 3. Write Script
- Start with hook (most important)
- Build emotional arc
- Time out each section
- For full blueprints: specify all 6 elements per take (setting, hero, dialogue, text overlay, camera, edit note)
- For quick scripts: dialogue + B-roll suggestions + on-screen text

### 4. Write Body Copy (if full blueprint)
Write supporting copy for the ad caption/description. Use a body copy formula from `references/frameworks.md`.

### 5. Review & Polish
- Read aloud for timing
- Check word count vs duration
- Ensure CTA is clear
- Verify landing page alignment

### 6. Add Production Notes
- Thumbnail concepts
- A/B test variations
- Equipment suggestions
- Editing notes

## Hook Formulas

**Pattern Interrupt:** "If you're still [wrong behavior], you're [consequence]."
**Curiosity Gap:** "I [achieved result] and here's what nobody tells you..."
**Controversial Take:** "Stop [common advice]. Here's what actually works."
**Identity Call-Out:** "This is for [specific person] who [specific situation]..."
**Question Hook:** "Why do [surprising thing] when you could [better alternative]?"
**Result Lead:** "[Company] went from [before] to [after]. Here's how."

---

## On-Screen Text Guidelines

1. **Keep it short**: 3-5 words max per text element
2. **Reinforce, don't repeat**: Add to dialogue, don't duplicate
3. **Use hierarchy**: Important words bigger/bolder
4. **Time it right**: Appear with dialogue, not before
5. **Position consistently**: Same area throughout video

---

## Sponsored Content Integration

Integrate sponsorships INTO the content narrative. A jarring sponsor interruption creates a retention crater.

**Bad:** "This video is sponsored by [Brand]. They make great products."
**Good:** "We needed to solve [problem relevant to the video] and [Brand] was the tool that did it."

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Hook grabs attention in first 3 seconds
- [ ] Emotional arc is clear
- [ ] Dialogue sounds natural (read aloud)
- [ ] Word count matches duration (2.5-3 words/second)
- [ ] CTA is specific and clear
- [ ] On-screen text reinforces (not duplicates)
- [ ] Sound-off ready: text overlays for every key claim
- [ ] Visual refresh every 5-10 seconds (no static frame >10s)
- [ ] B-roll suggestions are actionable
- [ ] Thumbnail concepts included
- [ ] A/B variations provided (at least hook and CTA variants)
- [ ] No em dashes

## Ad Preview Images

The first frame IS the preview on Meta/TikTok. Design it like a thumbnail:
- 3 elements max, single focal point
- High contrast, bold colors
- Face with strong emotion (+35-50% CTR)
- Must be understood in 0.5 seconds
- Test in dark mode (80% of users)

## Video Learning Science

For scripts aimed at education, courses, tutorials, or any content where the viewer needs to LEARN something (not just buy):

**See [references/video-learning-science.md](references/video-learning-science.md)** for:
- Mayer's 5 Multimedia Principles (Signalling, Coherence, Redundancy, Spatial/Temporal Contiguity)
- The 6-minute rule (keep under 6 min; screencasts under 3 min)
- 12 lecture styles spectrum (board-centric to speaker-centric) with when-to-use
- Dual Channel Theory (pictorial + auditory = 50% retention vs 10% read-only)
- Narration guidelines (conversational tone, faster pace, procedural framing)
- Video analytics framework (heat maps, abandon rates, attention scores)

**Key rule from learning science:** Do NOT show on-screen text while narrating the same words (Redundancy Principle). Audio + visuals = good. Audio + visuals + identical text = hurts comprehension.
