# Cole's Complete Product Creation Checklist (Low-Ticket Launchpad)

> Source: Nicolas Cole, Low-Ticket Launchpad course
> Module: 8 - Complete Product Creation
> Context: End-to-end 8-step checklist covering the entire product creation process from niche selection to launch. Use as a pre-launch preparation framework before entering the 5-Phase Launch approach. Each step references detailed frameworks in other Cole references.

## Overview

This is the master checklist that ties together everything from Cole's Low-Ticket Launchpad. Each step below summarizes the framework. For deep-dive details, follow the cross-references to the dedicated Cole reference files in each skill.

Use this checklist **before** entering our 5-Phase Launch approach (Internal -> Alpha -> Beta -> Early Access -> Full). Steps 1-4 validate the idea. Step 5 locks the offer. Steps 6-7 build the product. Step 8 ships it.

## Step 1: Niche Down

**Goal:** Go from broad industry to a specific person with a specific problem.

- **Mega-Category** (Industry) -> **Niche** (Sub-category) -> **Niche-Within-Niche** (Specific person)
- Example: "Wellness" -> "Sleep Wellness" -> "Sleep Wellness For Startup Founders"

**6 Niching Methods:**

1. **By Industry:** Pick a sub-category within the mega-category. "Healthcare" -> "Physical Therapy."
2. **By Demographic:** Add a specific demographic. "Physical Therapy" -> "Physical Therapy For Remote Workers."
3. **By Physical Location:** Add a geography. "Physical Therapy" -> "Physical Therapists In Chicago."
4. **By Digital Platform:** Add a platform. "Physical Therapy" -> "Physical Therapists On LinkedIn."
5. **By Price:** Add a price range or socioeconomic status. "Physical Therapy" -> "Physical Therapy For Single Mothers On Food Stamps."
6. **By Distribution:** Add a delivery method. "Physical Therapy" -> "Physical Therapy On Zoom."

## Step 2: List 10 Biggest Problems

**Goal:** Identify the most painful, urgent, and expensive problems for your niche person.

- Make a list of 10 problems for your specific person in your specific niche.
- Each problem must be **painful** (they feel it daily).
- Each problem must be **urgent** (they want it solved now, not someday).
- Each problem must be **valuable/expensive** -- it costs the person real TIME or MONEY (upside they're missing, or downside they're suffering).

> **Cross-reference:** `skills/presell-validator/references/cole-idea-validation.md` for problem identification and validation frameworks.

---

## Step 3: Map 10 Most Desirable Outcomes

**Goal:** Pair each problem with its outcome, then rank to find the best product opportunity.

- Take your list of 10 Biggest Problems.
- Next to each one, write the **Outcome** that happens if that problem gets solved.
- Stack-rank the list by: most painful problem + most valuable outcome.
- Pick the best one. That's your product.

> **Cross-reference:** `skills/presell-validator/references/cole-idea-validation.md` for outcome mapping and ranking.

## Step 4: Stress-Test Your Product Idea

**Goal:** Run your chosen Problem + Outcome through a 5-point validation filter.

1. **Solved for yourself?** You've personally gone through this problem and come out the other side.
2. **Genuinely care about solving?** It should bother you that this problem exists in the world.
3. **Objectively measurable?** You can "prove" you've solved it with numbers, screenshots, or results.
4. **Requires implementation (not just "an answer")?** Solving it takes more than a Google search. Some kind of doing is involved.
5. **Correlated to money/time/status?** The problem is connected to MAKING MONEY, NOT LOSING MONEY, SAVING A TON OF TIME, or UNLOCKING STATUS.

If it doesn't pass all 5, go back to Step 3 and pick a different Problem + Outcome pair.

> **Cross-reference:** `skills/presell-validator/references/cole-idea-validation.md` for the full stress-test framework and examples.

## Step 5: Complete Offer Creation Checklist

**Goal:** Lock down exactly what you're selling, for whom, and why it's worth the price.

Fill out all 10 points:

1. **Specific Problem** -- one problem, stated clearly.
2. **Solved For A Specific Person** -- who exactly is this for?
3. **Solved In A Specific Way** -- what's your method/approach?
4. **Achieving An Objective Outcome** -- what measurable result do they get?
5. **In A Specific Time Frame** -- how long until they see results?
6. **Price-Anchored To Upside 5x-10x** -- the value of the outcome is 5-10x the price. (A $47 product should unlock $235-$470 in value.)
7. **Price-Anchored To Downside 5x-10x** -- the cost of NOT solving this problem is 5-10x the price.
8. **Named Something Tangible** -- the product name sounds like a real thing, not a vague concept. (Playbook, Toolkit, System, Blueprint, etc.)
9. **Positioned In A Niche Where You Have Experience** -- you've done this, not just read about it.
10. **And Proof It Works** -- screenshots, testimonials, case studies, your own results.

This checklist also saves time later when creating marketing materials (sales pages, emails, ads) because every positioning question is already answered.

## Step 6: Outline Digital Product Curriculum

**Goal:** Turn your offer into a structured, deliverable product.

Follow these 5 steps:

1. **Start With The End (Objective) Goal** -- what is the ONE measurable outcome the buyer achieves after completing the product?
2. **Make A Bulleted List Of Action Steps** -- what does the buyer need to DO to achieve that goal? List every step.
3. **Name Each Module Something Tangible** -- each module name should sound like a deliverable. ("The 5-Minute Sleep Audit" not "Module 1: Introduction.")
4. **Pair Each Module With A Tangible Asset/Template** -- every module includes something the buyer can use immediately (checklist, template, swipe file, calculator, worksheet).
5. **List 3-7 Main Points/Sub-Steps Per Module** -- each module contains 3-7 sub-steps that achieve that module's larger action step. All modules ladder up to the end goal.

> **Cross-reference:** `skills/product-architect/references/cole-curriculum-framework.md` for detailed curriculum outlining frameworks and module structure.

## Step 7: Create The Perfect Course Module

**Goal:** Make each module comprehensive using a 7-section structure.

For every module in your curriculum, include as many of these sections as possible:

1. **Reasons Why (Benefits)** -- why this module matters. What changes for the buyer after completing it.
2. **Mistakes To Avoid (Problems)** -- common pitfalls. What most people get wrong at this step.
3. **Steps How To (Action Steps)** -- the actual process. Step-by-step instructions.
4. **Commonly Asked Questions (Objections)** -- preempt doubts. Answer the "but what about..." questions.
5. **Walkthrough Example (Proof)** -- show it working. Real example, screenshot, case study.
6. **Bonus Asset (Template)** -- a tangible takeaway. Checklist, swipe file, spreadsheet, prompt pack.
7. **Assignment (Homework)** -- what the buyer does next. Force implementation, not just consumption.

> **Cross-reference:** `skills/product-architect/references/cole-curriculum-framework.md` for the Perfect Course Module framework in detail.

## Step 8: Design, Record, Launch

**Goal:** Ship it.

Three things left:

1. **Get your digital product designed** -- slides, workbook layouts, template formatting.
2. **Record video (or audio) for each module** -- walk through the content, show examples.
3. **Put it up for sale** -- landing page, payment processing, delivery.

This is where Cole's product creation process ends and our launch-strategy phases begin.

## Integration: Pre-Launch to Launch Handoff

Use Steps 1-7 of this checklist to prepare the product. Then enter the 5-Phase Launch approach:

1. **Internal Launch** -- test with team/inner circle
2. **Alpha Launch** -- small group, collect feedback
3. **Beta Launch** -- wider group, refine positioning
4. **Early Access Launch** -- list segment, build social proof
5. **Full Launch** -- public, all channels, full scarcity stack

The product must be solid (Steps 1-7 complete) before Phase 1. Step 8 happens across Phases 1-5.
