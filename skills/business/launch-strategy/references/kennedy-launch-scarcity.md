# Kennedy Launch Scarcity & Offer Stack

Dan Kennedy's scarcity mechanics and offer stack frameworks applied to product launches. These layer on top of the existing Five-Phase Launch Approach and ORB Framework.

---

## 5 Scarcity Types Mapped to Launch Phases

Scarcity isn't something you bolt on at the end. You engineer it into every phase of the launch from day one.

### Type 1: Deadline (Specific Date and Time)

The most fundamental scarcity lever. "Enrollment closes Friday, March 21 at 11:59 PM EST." Not "limited time." Not "ending soon." A specific date and time.

**Launch phase mapping:**
- **Phase 3 (Beta):** "Beta pricing available until [date]. After that, full price."
- **Phase 4 (Early Access):** "Early access pricing expires [date]."
- **Phase 5 (Full Launch):** "Launch week pricing ends [date]."

### Type 2: Limited Quantity (Must Be Real)

Cap the number of spots, units, or licenses. The cap must be genuine. If you extend it, your audience learns your limits are fake and every future launch suffers.

**Launch phase mapping:**
- **Phase 2 (Alpha):** "Testing with 10 users." (Naturally limited)
- **Phase 3 (Beta):** "50 beta spots available."
- **Phase 4 (Early Access):** "First 100 customers get founding member status."
- **Phase 5 (Full Launch):** "Limited to 200 in the first cohort."

### Type 3: Disappearing Bonuses

Bonuses that are only available during a specific window. After the window closes, the core product remains but the bonuses vanish.

**Launch phase mapping:**
- **Phase 3 (Beta):** "Beta testers get lifetime access to all future updates." (Goes away after beta)
- **Phase 4 (Early Access):** "Early access buyers get the 1-on-1 onboarding call." (Not available at full launch)
- **Phase 5 (Full Launch):** "Launch week bonus: the Implementation Sprint Kit."

### Type 4: Rising Price

The price goes up at specific milestones. Early buyers get rewarded. Late buyers pay more.

**Launch phase mapping:**
- **Phase 2-3:** Free or heavily discounted (beta testing price)
- **Phase 4:** Early access price (50-70% of full price)
- **Phase 5, Week 1:** Launch price (80-90% of full price)
- **Phase 5, Post-launch:** Full price

**Example timeline:**
```
Beta:           Free (10 spots)
Early Access:   $97 (first 50 buyers)
Launch Week:    $147 (everyone else)
Post-Launch:    $197 (ongoing)
```

### Type 5: Social Scarcity ("Not Everyone Qualifies")

Kennedy's take-away selling applied to launches. Making the product harder to get increases desire. Application required. Criteria published. Not everyone gets in.

**Launch phase mapping:**
- **Phase 2 (Alpha):** "We're hand-picking 10 testers who meet [criteria]."
- **Phase 3 (Beta):** "Apply for beta access. We review every application."
- **Phase 4 (Early Access):** "Early access is limited to [specific audience segment]."
- **Phase 5 (Full Launch):** "Enrollment requires a brief application." (For high-ticket)

## The Scarcity Escalation Sequence

This is the email/communication sequence that deploys scarcity across the launch. Each message increases the urgency.

### Full Offer (Launch Day)

Present the complete offer. All bonuses. Full stack. Deadline clearly stated. This is the "everything you get" email.

**Subject line pattern:** "It's here: [Product Name] is live"

### Reminder (3 Days Before Deadline)

Light touch. Reference a specific bonus or benefit they'll miss. Don't repeat the full offer.

**Subject line pattern:** "Quick reminder about [specific bonus]"

### 24-Hour Warning

Urgency increases. Mention the specific things that disappear tomorrow. List what they lose by waiting.

**Subject line pattern:** "24 hours left: [Product] closes tomorrow"

### Last Chance (Deadline Day)

Final push. Morning email: "Today is the last day." Evening email (if appropriate): "A few hours left."

**Subject line pattern:** "Final hours: [deadline detail]"

### SECOND NOTICE (Post-Deadline, Kennedy Specific)

Kennedy's direct mail technique adapted for email. 2-3 days after the deadline, send a "second notice" formatted like an official reminder. This catches people who procrastinated and creates a second surge.

**Subject line:** "SECOND NOTICE: [Product Name]"

**Body structure:**
```
This is a second notice regarding [Product Name].

Our records indicate you visited the page but didn't complete
your enrollment before the deadline.

Due to [legitimate reason: a few spots opened up / we extended
the bonus window for 48 hours / we're opening 10 additional seats],
you have until [new short deadline] to take advantage of [specific benefit].

After [deadline], this offer is permanently closed.

[CTA]
```

**Why it works:** The formal "second notice" framing creates pattern interrupt. It feels different from a regular marketing email, which gets attention. And it gives a legitimate reason for the extension, which maintains credibility.

## Irresistible Offer Stack as Launch Checklist

Kennedy's 7-component offer stack mapped to launch preparation. Every component must be ready before launch day.

### Pre-Launch Offer Prep

- [ ] **Core product defined in one sentence.** If you can't describe it in one sentence, you're not ready to launch.
- [ ] **Price justified through value build.** Calculate the 5-10x ROI story. Write it out.
- [ ] **2-3 bonuses designed.** Each bonus addresses a specific objection to buying. Not random extras.
- [ ] **Deadline set.** Specific date and time. Communicated clearly in every launch asset.
- [ ] **Fast-action incentive ready.** What do the first 50 buyers get that nobody else does?
- [ ] **Late-response penalty defined.** What goes away after the deadline? Price increase? Bonus removal?
- [ ] **Guarantee written.** Type selected (pure, conditional, performance, or anti). Copy finalized.
- [ ] **Thud factor created.** Visual mockups, component counts, "look at everything you get" graphics.

### Launch Day Offer Checklist

- [ ] Sales page live with complete offer stack
- [ ] Launch email includes full value build and deadline
- [ ] All bonuses accessible immediately upon purchase
- [ ] Guarantee prominently displayed on sales page
- [ ] P.S. in launch email restates offer and deadline
- [ ] Scarcity elements are real and trackable (live counter, actual limit)

### Post-Launch Offer Follow-Through

- [ ] Scarcity escalation emails scheduled (reminder, 24hr, last chance)
- [ ] SECOND NOTICE email drafted for 2-3 days post-deadline
- [ ] Deadline enforced (no secret extensions beyond SECOND NOTICE)
- [ ] Post-close email sent ("here's what happened, join the waitlist for next time")
- [ ] All purchase data collected for next launch optimization

## Kennedy Launch Principles

A few rules that tie it all together:

**Rule 5: Brand-building is a side effect, not a goal.** Every launch asset must drive a specific, measurable action. Awareness is a side effect of doing launches well, not the objective.

**Rule 9: Results rule. Period.** Track revenue per launch, cost per acquisition, conversion rates at every stage. Opinions about what "feels right" don't matter. Numbers do.

**Rule 10: Maintain marketing discipline.** Don't soften the scarcity because it feels pushy. Don't skip the follow-up because you're tired. Don't extend the deadline because "a few more people might buy." The discipline IS the strategy.
