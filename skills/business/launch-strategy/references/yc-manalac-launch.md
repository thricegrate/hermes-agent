# YC Framework: How to Launch Again and Again (Kat Manalac)

Source: Y Combinator Startup School 2020 -- Kat Manalac

## Core Philosophy: Destroy the Single-Launch Mindset

Most founders think launching is a one-time event. Wrong. The best companies launch CONTINUOUSLY. Every new feature, improvement, and update is a launch opportunity.

You should be launching something every week or two. Not a "big reveal" -- a steady drumbeat of shipping, announcing, and getting feedback.

## The 8 Launch Types

Use these in sequence, or cycle through them repeatedly. Each one builds on the last.

### 1. Silent Launch
**What:** Minimal landing page. Domain, company name, one-line pitch, email capture.
**When:** Before anything is built. Day 1.
**Purpose:** Start collecting emails. Test if the one-liner resonates.
**Effort:** 1-2 hours.
**Requirements:** Domain name, one-liner, email form.

### 2. Friends & Family Launch
**What:** Share with your immediate network.
**When:** When you have something to show (even a prototype).
**Purpose:** Get honest-ish feedback. Find your first champions.
**Effort:** A few messages/emails.
**Warning:** This is LEARNING, not your customer base. Don't count these as real users.

### 3. Stranger Launch
**What:** Get it in front of people who don't know you.
**When:** After friends/family feedback is incorporated.
**Purpose:** Unbiased reactions. Real signal.
**How:** Cold DMs, in-person events, online communities.

### 4. Community Launch
**What:** Post on relevant online communities.
**When:** When you have a working product or demo.
**Targets by community:**

| Platform | What Works | What Doesn't |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Hacker News | Intellectual curiosity, surprising insights, technical depth | Marketing-speak, self-promotion |
| Product Hunt | Design polish, clear value prop, responsive maker | Unfinished products, no engagement in comments |
| Reddit | Honest, specific subreddit targeting, genuine contribution | Cross-posting, self-promotion, corporate tone |
| Twitter/X | Threads, build-in-public stories, contrarian takes | Generic announcements |
| LinkedIn | Professional value, results, case studies | Viral bait, engagement farming |

**Golden rule:** "Write like you talk, not like a marketing robot."

### 5. Waitlist / Request for Access Launch
**What:** Create artificial scarcity with a waitlist.
**When:** When demand exceeds what you can serve.
**Viral mechanic:** Skip-the-line for referrals. "Invite 3 friends to move up."
**Why it works:** Exclusivity creates FOMO. People want what they can't have.
**Example:** Superhuman's invite-only model generated years of word-of-mouth.

### 6. Blogger / Influencer Launch
**What:** Get coverage from relevant content creators.
**When:** When you have a compelling story + working product.
**How:** Personal outreach (not mass email). Lead with what's interesting about your story, not what you want them to do.
**Key:** Give them a unique angle or exclusive. "Generic startup launches product" is not a story.

### 7. Press Launch
**What:** Traditional media coverage.
**When:** When you have a significant milestone (funding, major customer, impressive growth).
**Reality check:** Press is overrated for most early-stage products. Community launch and word-of-mouth are more effective for initial traction.

### 8. New Feature / Product Launch
**What:** Every significant update is a new launch.
**When:** Ongoing, forever.
**Why:** Each launch re-engages existing users and attracts new ones. Compounding effect.

## The Minimal Viable Launch Page

You need exactly these elements:

1. **Company/product name**
2. **One-liner:** What it does, in one sentence. No jargon.
3. **Call-to-action:** Email signup, waitlist, or "try it now"
4. **Optional:** One screenshot, one testimonial, or one key metric

That's it. Everything else is polish you can add later.

**One-liner formula:**
"[Product] helps [specific person] [do specific thing] [in specific way]."

Examples:
- "Dropbox synchronizes files from your computer with your team's computers"
- "Tandem is building virtual offices for remote teams"

Bad one-liners:
- "IndieCloud is a knowhow and synergy platform" (meaningless)
- "We leverage AI to optimize your workflow" (could be anything)

## Continuous Launch Cadence

For a fast-iteration product business (launching apps every ~3 days):

| Day | Activity | Launch Type |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Build + silent launch (landing page) | Type 1 |
| 2 | Share with list segment + social | Type 2-3 |
| 3 | Community posts + email to full list | Type 4 |
| 4-7 | Gather data, iterate based on feedback | -- |
| 8 | Launch improved version or next product | Type 8 |

**Every launch should build the email list.** Even if the product flops, every visitor who enters their email is a future customer for the next launch.

## Key Principles

1. **Build email lists from day one.** Every person you talk to about your product should be added (with permission).
2. **A/B test your messaging.** Different communities respond to different angles. What works on HN won't work on LinkedIn.
3. **Presales and waitlists with skip-the-line mechanics create viral growth.** People share when they get something for sharing.
4. **Don't wait until it's perfect.** Ship, get feedback, improve, re-launch.
5. **Each launch is a learning event.** What resonated? What fell flat? Adjust.
6. **Write like you talk.** No marketing-speak. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
