# High-Converting Landing Page Rules (Velocity Framework)

**Source:** Daniel Bustamante (Velocity) -- 30+ lead magnets, consistently high opt-in rates (83%+ on best performers).

## 7 Principles for Lead Magnet Landing Pages

### 1. Single CTA Only
No competing links, navigation, or social links. One action: opt in.

### 2. Outcome-Focused Headlines
Lead with what they GET, not what it IS.
- Bad: "Download Our Free Email Guide"
- Good: "The 6-Figure Newsletter Starter Pack"

### 3. Compelling Asset Name
Use naming formula: [Desired Outcome] + [Topic] + [Container Word]
Container words: Kit, Prompt, Blueprint, Template, Playbook, Roadmap, Swipe File, Launchpad

### 4. Above-the-Fold Opt-In
Email capture visible without scrolling. No "scroll down to sign up."

### 5. Tangible Social Proof
Numbers, testimonials, or logos near the CTA.
- "9,000+ subscribers"
- "Used by founders at [Company]"

### 6. Address Objections Upfront
Answer "why free?" and "what's the catch?" before they ask.
- "Full transparency: This is best suited for established businesses with a proven offer."
- "Zero downside to throwing your hat in the ring."

### 7. Professional Mockup Image
Book-style or software box format. NOT stock photos, basic Canva, or no visual.
Two mistakes that kill conversion: generic names + ugly visuals.

## Confirmation Page as Sales Page

The confirmation/thank-you page is the highest-attention moment. Don't waste it.

### Velocity's Confirmation Page Model
1. **Deliver immediately** -- "As promised, here's your [Lead Magnet]" (builds trust)
2. **Transition to upsell** -- "Now if you'd like [Higher Value Thing] for free, read on..."
3. **Personal authority intro** -- who you are, why you're credible
4. **Application model** -- free offer gated behind qualification
   - "Not everyone will qualify" (scarcity)
   - "Best suited for [ideal client]" (honest filter)
   - "Even if not selected, you get [consolation resource]" (zero risk)

### Why the Application Model Works
- Creates exclusivity without being sleazy
- Qualifies leads (only serious people apply)
- Survey data = free market research
- Non-qualifiers still get value (goodwill)
- Positions the free offer as high-value (if you have to qualify, it must be worth something)

## Offer Stacking on Landing Pages

Never present one monolithic thing. Stack components:
- "2 templates + 2 AI prompts + 1 guide + 1 swipe file"
- Each bullet = separate perceived value
- Price anchor if possible ("Worth $297")
