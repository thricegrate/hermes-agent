## 1. Five Horsemen of Retention (Deep Version)

Full detail from interviewing gym owners with <3% monthly churn. These five practices, applied together, consistently dropped churn from 9% to 3% -- a 3.3x increase in LTV.

### 1.1 Track Attendance

- **Retention threshold:** 3x/week = stays. <2x/week = churns.
- **Decay pattern:** 3 -> 2 -> 2 -> 1 -> CANCEL
- **Rescue window:** Reach out when attendance drops to 2 sessions. This is the last reliable intervention point.
- **Recovery pattern:** 3 -> 2 (reach out) -> 3 -> 3 -> 3
- The decay pattern is predictable. If you catch it at the first drop to 2, you save the customer. If you wait until they hit 1, it's almost always too late.

### 1.2 Reach Out 2x/Week

- Praise both participation AND progress.
- Solve small problems before they become cancellation reasons.
- Two touchpoints per week keeps you top of mind and signals that you notice them.
- This is not automated email. This is personal, human outreach.

### 1.3 Handwritten Cards

- **When to send:**
  - At signup (welcome)
  - At 3-month milestone
  - At 6-month milestone
  - At 12-month milestone
  - For referral asks
  - Holidays
  - Birthdays
- "Never a bad time to send a handwritten card."
- The physical nature of the card creates an emotional anchor that digital communication cannot replicate.

### 1.4 Member Events

- Regular internal calendar with consistent cadence.
- **Cadence options:** Every 21, 42, or 63 days. Pick one and stick with it.
- Send handwritten invites (not email blasts).
- Dual benefit: reduces churn AND generates referrals.
- Events create social bonds between members. Social bonds are harder to break than transactional ones.

### 1.5 Exit Interviews

- Save ~50% of phone/email cancellations.
- **Setup:** Set the expectation during onboarding that a cancellation call is required.
- **Trigger:** Cancellation notice triggers the interview.
- **Realistic math:** Assume half show up for the call. Save 50% of those. Net: cut churn by 25%.
- 25% churn reduction = 33% increase in LTV.

### 1.6 Shaking the Tree (What to Expect)

When you implement these five horsemen, churn follows a predictable pattern:

- **Month 1:** Churn UP 50%. This is people who would've canceled anyway plus zombie billing (people who forgot they were paying and now notice).
- **Month 2:** Churn DOWN 50% from baseline.
- **Month 3:** Churn DOWN another 50%.
- **Net trajectory:** 10% -> 15% -> 7% -> 3% churn.
- Expect the initial spike. Don't panic. It's a feature, not a bug.

**Benchmark:** Reduction from 9% to 3% churn = 3.3x increase in LTV.

## 2. 9-Step Churn Checklist (Full with All Sub-Steps)

Used with every new Acquisition.com portfolio company. Applied in order.

### Step 1: Figure Out Activation Points

- Find churned customers who stayed 3+ months.
- Take top 20% by spend.
- Learn their demographics, psychographics, and usage patterns.
- Common factors across that top 20% = activation point candidates.
- **Template:** "Every customer that does (X) or gets (Y result) stays longer."
- Your activation point is what your onboarding needs to drive toward.

### Step 2: Onboard Customers

- Drive every new customer to their activation point as fast as possible.
- **Onboarding hierarchy (best to worst):**
  - Custom > Generic
  - Personal > Group
  - Live > Recorded
  - Carrots > Sticks
  - Some > None
- One portfolio company went from $2M/mo to $2M/week after switching from group onboarding to 1-on-1 onboarding. That single change gave a 25% boost in ascensions.

### Step 3: Incentivize Customer Activation

- Create unlockables tied to engagement levels:
  - Courses
  - 1-on-1 calls
  - Live events
  - Custom badges
  - Free lifetime access at level 8
- **Critical placement:** Put incentives just AFTER major churn points.
- If most people churn at month 3, put an unlockable at month 3.5.

### Step 4: Community Linking

- Group events
- Manual member introductions (not algorithmic -- personal)
- Community podcast
- Elevate micro-celebrities within your community
- "They come for the bikini, they stay for the community."

### Step 5: Fire or Correct Bad Customers

- Delete bad posts + explain why. Show what good looks like.
- 3 strikes policy.
- Pin best 1-2 new posts daily to set the standard.
- **Four community categories to encourage:**
  - Wins
  - Fun
  - Discovered (things they learned)
  - Meetups

### Step 6: Add Annual Payment Options

- **If average months stayed < 12:** Price annual above the average months stayed.
- **If average months stayed > 12:** "Buy 10 get 2 free" framing.
- **Alternative pricing models:**
  - Big Head/Long Tail: large upfront payment + small ongoing monthly
  - Founder rates: 50% discount for early/loyal members

### Step 7: Cancellation Call/Video

- Save ~50% of callers.
- **Two save methods:**
  - (a) "Let me give it another shot" -- recommit with a plan
  - (b) Upsell to higher program and credit their existing payment toward it
- **For high-volume businesses:** Create a cancellation video that resells the value + shows what they lose by leaving. Route all cancellation requests through it first.

### Step 8: Survey Twice/Year

- **Two key survey questions:**
  1. "If I removed everything but one, what would you keep?"
  2. "If I kept everything but one, which would bother you least to lose?"
- **Between surveys:** Reach out 1-on-1 every 2-3 weeks using the ACA framework (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask).
- **Counterintuitive finding:** Wealthier customers want less handholding, not more.

### Step 9: Four-Step Customer Journey (ATRA)

- **Activate** -> **Testimonial** -> **Refer** -> **Ascend**
- Incentivize each milestone with unlockables or rewards.
- "If someone just bought another thing from you, they're the least likely to churn."

> "I have never not made more money by going through and applying this checklist."

## 3. Inversion Retention Framework

Instead of asking "How should I retain all my customers?", ask: **"What would make ALL my customers leave?"** Then do the opposite.

Eight things that would churn 100% of customers and their inversions:

| What Churns Everyone | The Inversion |
|---|---|
| Ignore them | Talk with them |
| Break promises | Keep promises |
| Miscommunicate | Communicate clearly |
| Treat them poorly | Treat like royalty |
| Set unrealistic expectations | Set realistic expectations |
| Hide progress | Give status updates |
| Keep them from other happy customers | Connect with happy customers |
| Make stuff harder to use | Make it easy to get max value |

If you're doing any of the items in the left column, fix those before adding new retention tactics. The inversion framework surfaces the basics you might be missing.

## 4. Value Per Second vs Seconds of Value

**Core principle: "Less but better is better than more and decent."**

- Overwhelm is the #1 reason for churn.
- A newsletter business making $500K/mo had the lowest churn with just one Q&A call per month + one physical newsletter. Everything added after those core deliverables INCREASED churn.
- Each version of Gym Launch got shorter and simpler. And each version retained better.
- **Retention = making sure they CONSUME value, not overwhelm them with it.**
- "As little and as good as possible."
- When customers stop consuming, contact them more often. Don't add more content -- add more contact.
- Every version of a product should get shorter and simpler, not longer and more complex.

**The trap:** You think adding more features/content = more value. But if they can't consume it, it feels like waste. Waste feels bad. Bad feelings = churn.

## 5. Usage Churn Detection

Leading indicator tracking -- the most underused retention lever.

- **Usage churn:** Customer is still subscribed but no longer uses the product.
- Track product usage independently of billing. These are two separate data streams.
- Set thresholds for "usage churn" alerts (e.g., login frequency drops below X, content consumption drops below Y).
- **Intervene when usage drops, not when they cancel.** By cancellation, it's too late.
- Especially critical for annual contracts.
- If you sell annual but they stop using at month 6, they'll churn at renewal. You have 6 months of warning. Use it.

**Implementation:**
1. Define your key usage metric (logins, content consumed, features used, sessions attended).
2. Set a threshold for "healthy" usage.
3. Set a threshold for "at risk" usage.
4. Automate alerts when someone crosses from healthy to at risk.
5. Trigger personal outreach (ACA framework) on every alert.

## 6. ACA Customer Outreach

Three-step framework for regular customer check-in. Simple, repeatable, effective.

- **A - Acknowledge** what you've seen them do. "I noticed you completed module 3 last week."
- **C - Compliment** them on something specific. "Your implementation of X was really sharp."
- **A - Ask** a question. "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"

**Usage rules:**
- Frequency: every 2-3 weeks, 1-on-1
- This is the same ACA framework used in sales, but applied to retention context.
- **Counterintuitive finding:** Wealthier customers want LESS handholding. Adjust frequency accordingly. Don't over-contact your highest-value customers.

## 7. ATRA Customer Journey

Every customer should pass through four milestones. This is the retention backbone.

### The Four Milestones

1. **Activate** - Hit the activation point. Always first. Non-negotiable.
2. **Testimonial** - Create social proof. Ask for it right after activation when satisfaction peaks.
3. **Refer** - Generate referrals. Easiest ask after they've given a testimonial (consistency bias).
4. **Ascend** - Buy the next thing from you. "If someone just bought another thing from you, they're the least likely to churn."

**Rules:**
- Activation is always first. The other three can happen in any order after that.
- Pair each milestone with an incentive/unlockable.
- A customer who has completed all four milestones has almost zero churn risk.

## 8. Master Churn Checklist (Full Operational Checklist)

The complete operational checklist. Work through top to bottom.

- [ ] **Figure out activation point**
  - [ ] Who are top 10-20% of customers? (= your avatar)
  - [ ] What did top 10-20% DO? (= their activation point)
  - [ ] Update messaging to attract right customers
  - [ ] Update onboarding to drive to activation point
- [ ] **Add onboarding calls**
  - [ ] Resell value, say names multiple times
  - [ ] Tell them how to get value
  - [ ] Tell them about unlockables
  - [ ] Tell them about community linking
  - [ ] Give homework with 7-day deadline
  - [ ] Schedule next call
- [ ] **Use unlockables**
  - [ ] Options: course, 1-on-1 call, live/virtual event, higher tier calls, custom badge/profile, status indicator
  - [ ] Time unlocks: put past avg lifespan, then 30 days after, then 3 months, then 3 months
- [ ] **Community linking**
  - [ ] Manually introduce members
  - [ ] Host group events
  - [ ] Create community podcast
  - [ ] Elevate micro-celebrities
- [ ] **Fire or correct bad customers**
  - [ ] Incentivize good behavior (pin posts, praise, provide value)
  - [ ] Eliminate bad behavior + explain why + show what good looks like (3 strikes)
- [ ] **Add annual pricing**
  - [ ] Annual billing option
  - [ ] Try big upfront with small monthly
- [ ] **Cancellation call/video**
  - [ ] Tell new customers you require cancellation calls
  - [ ] Resell them on why staying helps their goals
  - [ ] Tell them what they'll lose
- [ ] **Survey customers**
  - [ ] Make product easy to consume
  - [ ] Survey 2x/year: "If I removed everything but one..." and "If I kept everything but one..."
  - [ ] Reach out to check in every few weeks (ACA framework)
- [ ] **Basic customer journey**
  - [ ] Implement four milestones: Activate, Testimonial, Refer, Ascend
  - [ ] Activation first, then others in order that makes sense
  - [ ] Don't forget to sell them again

## 10. Churn Prevention Tactics (Compiled)

1. Track product usage independently of billing
2. Intervene when engagement drops, not when they cancel
3. Bill less frequently = lower churn (annual vs monthly = 5.35x LTV)
4. Second form of payment eliminates 24-34% of involuntary churn
5. Overwhelm is #1 reason for churn. Less but better.
6. Core 2-3 deliverables only. Delete everything else.
7. Customer surplus (value - price gap) drives retention
8. Longer billing cycles = bigger lookback window = more forgiveness
9. Put unlockables just AFTER major churn points
10. Cancellation calls save ~50% of callers
11. Handwritten cards at signup, 3/6/12 months, holidays, birthdays
12. Events every 21/42/63 days
13. Reach out 1-on-1 every 2-3 weeks (ACA)
14. Connect members to each other, not just to you
15. "They come for the bikini, they stay for the community"
