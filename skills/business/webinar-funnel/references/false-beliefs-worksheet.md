# False Beliefs Worksheet

Map the 3 false beliefs your audience holds, then build the Epiphany Bridge story that breaks each one.

---

## Instructions

1. Fill out each section completely before scripting your webinar
2. Write beliefs in your audience's exact words (how THEY say it, not how you'd say it)
3. Use the 14-question script to build each Epiphany Bridge story
4. The replacement framework should be teachable in under 5 minutes

---

## False Belief #1: Vehicle Belief

**The doubt:** "This TYPE of solution won't work for [my goal]."

They don't doubt themselves. They doubt the VEHICLE you're offering.

### Mapping

**The belief (in their words):**
> [Write the exact objection. Example: "Webinars don't work anymore. Everyone's seen too many."]

**Why they hold it:**
> [What experience or information created this belief? Example: "They attended a bad webinar that was all pitch and no value. Or they read a blog post saying webinars are dead."]

**What they'd need to see to change their mind:**
> [What proof would break this belief? Example: "Someone like them who built a business through webinars in the last 6 months."]

### Epiphany Bridge Story (14 Questions)

1. **Backstory/context:** Where were you when this story starts?
2. **Your desires:** What did you want at the time?
3. **The wall:** What obstacle or failure did you hit?
4. **The epiphany:** What moment changed your thinking?
5. **The plan:** What new approach did you try?
6. **First attempt feeling:** How did it feel trying the new way?
7. **Conflict during attempt:** What went wrong or was hard?
8. **Achievement:** What result did you get?
9. **Transformation:** Before state vs. after state?
10. **Unexpected lesson:** What surprised you?
11. **Audience connection:** How does this relate to their situation?
12. **Counterfactual:** What would have happened without the epiphany?
13. **One-sentence moral:** What's the takeaway?
14. **Teaching bridge:** How does this lead into the framework you're about to teach?

### Replacement Framework

**Framework name:** [Give it a name they can remember]

**Core idea in one sentence:**
> [Example: "The Invisible Webinar Framework: webinars work better than ever because nobody else is doing them right."]

**Evidence/proof:**
> [Specific data, case study, or result that supports the new belief]

## Filled Example: AI Business Automation Product

### Vehicle Belief
**Belief:** "AI tools are overhyped. They can't actually run a real business."
**Why:** They tried ChatGPT, got generic outputs, and concluded AI is a toy.
**Epiphany Bridge:** Story of the first time you automated an entire workflow and it generated revenue while you slept. The wall was that every AI tool felt like a novelty. The epiphany was realizing individual tools are useless but a SYSTEM of tools changes everything.
**Replacement:** "The AI Operating System Framework: one AI tool is a toy. A system of 50+ tools running together is a business engine."
**Proof:** "My AI system runs 5 newsletters reaching 300K+ subscribers. It writes, schedules, analyzes, and optimizes without me touching it daily."

### Internal Belief
**Belief:** "I'm not technical enough to build AI automations."
**Why:** They opened a coding tutorial once, got lost in 5 minutes, and decided "I'm not a tech person."
**Epiphany Bridge:** Story of a client/student who was a complete non-technical person (teacher, nurse, retiree) who built their first automation using templates in under an hour. The wall was believing you need to code. The epiphany was that modern AI tools are drag-and-drop and template-driven.
**Replacement:** "The Template-First Method: you don't build from scratch. You customize from proven templates. If you can fill in a form, you can build an AI automation."
**Proof:** "[Name], a [non-technical profession], built a working email automation in 47 minutes using the templates inside the program."

### External Belief
**Belief:** "The AI space moves too fast. By the time I learn this, it'll be outdated."
**Why:** They see new AI tools launching every week. It feels like a treadmill.
**Epiphany Bridge:** Story of how the PRINCIPLES haven't changed even though the tools have. Email marketing principles from 2005 still work. Sales psychology from 1960 still works. The tools are just faster delivery mechanisms.
**Replacement:** "The Evergreen Automation Principle: tools change every month, but the automation PATTERNS are the same ones that worked 20 years ago. Learn the pattern, swap the tool."
**Proof:** "The core automation patterns in the product have been stable for 18 months despite 3 major AI model upgrades. We swap the engine, the car still drives the same."

## Quality Checks

Before moving to webinar scripting, verify:

- [ ] Each belief is written in the audience's language (not yours)
- [ ] Each belief has a clear "why they hold it" explanation
- [ ] Each Epiphany Bridge story follows the 14-question structure
- [ ] Each story has emotional detail (not just facts)
- [ ] Each replacement framework has a memorable name
- [ ] Each framework has specific proof (not vague claims)
- [ ] The Vehicle story is your ORIGIN story (or closely related)
- [ ] The Internal story features someone LIKE the audience who overcame the limitation
- [ ] The External story includes data or undeniable proof
- [ ] All 3 beliefs, when removed, lead directly to the Big Domino being accepted
