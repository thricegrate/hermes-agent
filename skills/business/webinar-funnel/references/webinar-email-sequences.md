# Webinar Email Sequences

Three complete sequences: Indoctrination (pre-webinar), Reminder (day-of), and Post-Webinar (replay + close).

---

## Sequence 1: Indoctrination Emails (Post-Registration, Pre-Webinar)

**Goal:** Build excitement, establish authority, ensure attendance.
**Key rule:** Do NOT teach or answer questions. Keep curiosity at maximum.
**Timing:** Start immediately after registration. Space based on days until webinar.

### Email 1: Confirmation + Excitement
**Timing:** Immediately after registration
**Subject line angle:** Confirmation + what to expect
**Subject lines (pick one):**
- "You're in. Here's what happens next."
- "Confirmed: your seat for [Webinar Title]"
- "[Name], you just made a smart move"

**Opening hook:** "You're registered. And I'm genuinely excited about what I'm going to share with you on [day]."

**Body focus:**
- Confirm the date, time, and timezone
- Tell them to add it to their calendar (include calendar link)
- Give 1-2 teaser bullets of what they'll learn (curiosity, not answers)
- Set the frame: "This isn't a typical webinar. No fluff. No 45-minute pitch. Just the [framework/system] I used to [result]."

**CTA:** "Add to calendar" button/link

**What NOT to include:** Don't teach anything. Don't give away content. Don't link to blog posts or videos.

---

### Email 2: Your Story
**Timing:** 1-2 days after registration
**Subject line angle:** Personal, vulnerable, story-driven
**Subject lines:**
- "Why I almost didn't create this training"
- "The story behind [Webinar Title]"
- "I wasn't going to share this"

**Opening hook:** Start in the middle of a story. "Two years ago, I was [vivid painful scene]. I had no idea that [the thing you're teaching] would change everything."

**Body focus:**
- Tell a condensed version of your origin story (not the full webinar version)
- Focus on the BEFORE state and the turning point
- Do NOT reveal the solution or framework
- End with: "On [day], I'll show you exactly what I discovered. It's not what you'd expect."

**CTA:** "Make sure you show up on [day]. This is the training I wish someone gave me [X years ago]."

**What NOT to include:** No teaching, no frameworks, no "here are 3 tips." Story only.

---

### Email 3: Social Proof
**Timing:** 2-3 days after registration
**Subject line angle:** Other people's results
**Subject lines:**
- "[Name] used this and [specific result]"
- "What happened when [person] tried this"
- "Results from the last time I taught this"

**Opening hook:** "[Name] came to me [in X situation]. [X months] later, [specific result]."

**Body focus:**
- 1-2 testimonials or case studies with specific numbers
- Keep each story to 3-5 sentences
- Connect their result to what you'll teach on the webinar
- "They learned the exact framework I'm sharing on [day]."

**CTA:** "Want the same framework [Name] used? Show up on [day]. [Link to add calendar reminder]."

**What NOT to include:** Don't explain HOW they got the result. The webinar does that.

---

### Email 4: Behind the Scenes
**Timing:** 3-4 days after registration
**Subject line angle:** Preparation, effort, value justification
**Subject lines:**
- "I just finished the slides for [day]"
- "What I'm putting into this training"
- "A peek behind the curtain"

**Opening hook:** "I just spent [X hours] finalizing the training for [day]. Here's why I put this much work into a free event."

**Body focus:**
- Share what you're preparing (without revealing content)
- Mention a specific insight or slide you're excited about (tease it)
- Explain why you care about this topic personally
- Build perceived value: "I've spent [X months/years] testing this. You're getting the compressed version."

**CTA:** "This is the most valuable free training I've ever put together. Don't miss it. [Date + time + link]."

**What NOT to include:** No content. No tips. No frameworks. Tease, don't teach.

---

### Email 5: Contrarian Take (If 5+ Days Before Webinar)
**Timing:** 4-5 days after registration
**Subject line angle:** Challenge conventional wisdom
**Subject lines:**
- "Everyone's wrong about [topic]"
- "Stop doing [common advice]"
- "The [topic] advice I'd ignore"

**Opening hook:** "Most people think [common belief]. I used to think so too. Then I saw the data."

**Body focus:**
- State a contrarian position related to your webinar topic
- Give JUST enough evidence to be credible (1-2 data points)
- Do NOT fully explain or resolve the tension
- "I'll break this down completely on [day]. What I found surprised me."

**CTA:** "If this sounds counterintuitive, wait until you see the full picture. [Day + time + link]."

**What NOT to include:** Don't resolve the contrarian claim. Leave the gap open.

---

### Email 6: Pre-Work Assignment (If 5+ Days Before Webinar)
**Timing:** 5-6 days after registration
**Subject line angle:** Simple action step
**Subject lines:**
- "Do this before [day]"
- "Quick homework (takes 2 minutes)"
- "A question to answer before the training"

**Opening hook:** "Before we meet on [day], I want you to do one thing."

**Body focus:**
- Give them a simple task: write down their biggest challenge, set a goal, clear their schedule
- The task should take under 5 minutes
- Explain why: "When you show up with clarity on [X], you'll get 10x more from the training."
- This increases commitment and attendance

**CTA:** "Write your answer down now. Bring it to the training on [day]."

**What NOT to include:** Nothing complex. One task, under 5 minutes.

---

### Email 7: Anticipation Peak (Day Before)
**Timing:** Evening before the webinar
**Subject line angle:** Tomorrow changes things
**Subject lines:**
- "Tomorrow."
- "See you in less than 24 hours"
- "Here's what [past attendee] said about this training"

**Opening hook:** "Tomorrow at [time], everything I've been building toward comes together."

**Body focus:**
- Short (under 150 words)
- Include one powerful testimonial from a past attendee or student
- Remind them of the time and link
- Build anticipation without content

**CTA:** "Tomorrow. [Time]. [Link]. Don't be late."

**What NOT to include:** No new information. No teaching. Just anticipation + logistics.

## Sequence 2: Reminder Emails (Day-Of)

**Goal:** Maximize live attendance. Every email gets shorter and more urgent.
**Key rule:** Always include the join link. Subject lines get progressively urgent.

### Reminder 1: Day Before (Evening)
**Timing:** 6-8 PM the evening before
**Subject line:** "Tomorrow: [Webinar Title] starts at [time]"

**Opening hook:** "Quick reminder: tomorrow at [time], we go live."

**Body focus (100 words max):**
- Date, time, timezone
- Join link
- "Set an alarm. Block your calendar. This is worth protecting."

**CTA:** Join link

---

### Reminder 2: Morning Of
**Timing:** 8-9 AM day of webinar
**Subject line:** "Today's the day. [Time] [timezone]."

**Opening hook:** "Today at [time], I'm revealing [teaser]."

**Body focus (80 words max):**
- What to have ready: pen, notebook, quiet space
- Join link
- "This training is packed. Show up on time so you don't miss the opening. It sets up everything."

**CTA:** Join link

---

### Reminder 3: 1 Hour Before
**Timing:** 60 minutes before start
**Subject line:** "60 minutes. Are you ready?"

**Opening hook:** "We start in one hour."

**Body focus (50 words max):**
- Get your coffee/water
- Close distractions
- Join link

**CTA:** Join link

---

### Reminder 4: 15 Minutes Before
**Timing:** 15 minutes before start
**Subject line:** "Starting soon. Join now."

**Full email (30 words max):**
"We're about to start. Click below to join the room. Get settled early so you catch everything from minute one."

**CTA:** Join link

---

### Reminder 5: Going Live
**Timing:** At start time
**Subject line:** "WE'RE LIVE. Join now."

**Full email (20 words max):**
"The training just started. Join now before you miss the opening: [Link]"

**CTA:** Join link

## Sequence 3: Post-Webinar Campaign (Replay + Close)

**Goal:** Convert attendees and registrants who didn't buy during the live event.
**Timeline:** 48-72 hour replay window. Real deadline. No extensions.
**Key rule:** Deadline is real. If you say it closes Sunday at midnight, it closes Sunday at midnight.

### Post-Webinar Email 1: Replay + Recap
**Timing:** 1-2 hours after webinar ends
**Subject line angle:** Replay access + gratitude
**Subject lines:**
- "Replay: [Webinar Title] (watch before [deadline])"
- "You asked for the replay. Here it is."
- "In case you missed it (or want to watch again)"

**Opening hook:** "Thank you for showing up today. Here's the replay."

**Body focus:**
- Replay link (prominently placed)
- Quick recap of the 3 secrets (one sentence each, no detail)
- Remind them of the offer and what's included (brief stack recap)
- State the deadline clearly: "This replay and the special offer both expire on [date] at [time]."

**CTA:** "Watch the replay" button + "Grab [Product Name] before [deadline]" link

---

### Post-Webinar Email 2: Testimonial + Replay Reminder
**Timing:** Next morning (Day 2)
**Subject line angle:** Someone else's result
**Subject lines:**
- "[Name] just signed up and here's why"
- "What [Name] said about the training"
- "This feedback made my day"

**Opening hook:** "I woke up to this message from [Name] who attended yesterday..."

**Body focus:**
- 1-2 testimonials (from the live event, DMs, or past students)
- Connect their result to what was taught in the webinar
- "If you haven't watched the replay yet, [Name]'s story should motivate you."
- Remind them of the deadline

**CTA:** "Watch the replay" + "Join [Product Name] before [deadline]"

---

### Post-Webinar Email 3: Objection Crusher
**Timing:** Day 2 (afternoon/evening)
**Subject line angle:** Address the #1 objection
**Subject lines:**
- "The question everyone asked yesterday"
- "Is [Product Name] right for you? (honest answer)"
- "You're probably wondering about [objection]"

**Opening hook:** "The most common question from yesterday's training: [objection]."

**Body focus:**
- State the objection directly (don't dance around it)
- Answer it head-on with proof, logic, or testimonial
- Restate the guarantee: "And remember, you're covered by [guarantee]. There's zero risk."
- Stack reminder (brief)

**CTA:** "Join [Product Name] before [deadline]" + guarantee reminder

---

### Post-Webinar Email 4: Urgency + Stack Recap
**Timing:** Day 3 (morning)
**Subject line angle:** Time running out + full value recap
**Subject lines:**
- "[X hours] left. Here's everything you're getting."
- "Closing tomorrow: the full breakdown"
- "Last chance to get [urgency bonus]"

**Opening hook:** "In [X hours], this offer closes. I want to make sure you know exactly what you're walking away from."

**Body focus:**
- Full stack recap with dollar values
- "If All" statement (pick the strongest one)
- Per-day cost breakdown
- Guarantee reminder
- Urgency bonus reminder (if still available)

**CTA:** "Join [Product Name] now" + deadline countdown

---

### Post-Webinar Email 5: Last Day Warning
**Timing:** Final day (morning)
**Subject line angle:** Final day, direct
**Subject lines:**
- "Final day."
- "Closing tonight at [time]"
- "This is it."

**Opening hook:** "Today at [time], [Product Name] closes."

**Body focus (under 100 words):**
- No new arguments. Just clarity.
- "You've seen the training. You know what's inside. You know the guarantee removes your risk."
- "The only question is: are you going to do something about it, or go back to [painful current state]?"
- Link

**CTA:** "Join before [time] tonight"

---

### Post-Webinar Email 6: Final Hours / Last Chance
**Timing:** 3-6 hours before deadline
**Subject line angle:** Maximum urgency
**Subject lines:**
- "[X] hours. Then it's gone."
- "Last call: [Product Name]"
- "Closing in [X] hours"

**Opening hook:** "This is the last email I'll send about this."

**Body focus (under 75 words):**
- Link
- What they get (one sentence)
- Guarantee (one sentence)
- Deadline (specific time)
- "After [time], this page comes down."

**CTA:** Link. Nothing else.

---

## Sequence Rules Summary

**Indoctrination (pre-webinar):**
- [ ] Never teach content (stories and curiosity only)
- [ ] Every email builds excitement for the webinar itself
- [ ] Always include date, time, and calendar/join link
- [ ] Get progressively more specific about what they'll learn (without answering)

**Reminders (day-of):**
- [ ] Get shorter with each email
- [ ] Always include the join link
- [ ] Subject lines get more urgent
- [ ] Final email is 1-2 sentences

**Post-webinar (replay + close):**
- [ ] Replay link in the first 3 emails
- [ ] Each email introduces a new element (testimonial, objection, stack, urgency)
- [ ] Deadline is stated in every email
- [ ] Final 2 emails are short, direct, urgency-focused
- [ ] Never extend the deadline. Credibility depends on it.

## Segmentation Tags

Tag registrants for future targeting:

- `webinar-registered` - Signed up
- `webinar-attended` - Showed up live
- `webinar-replay` - Watched replay
- `webinar-stayed-to-offer` - Attended through the close
- `webinar-clicked-offer` - Clicked the offer link
- `webinar-purchased` - Bought
- `webinar-no-show` - Registered but didn't attend or watch replay

**Key segment:** `webinar-clicked-offer` + `NOT webinar-purchased` = hottest non-buyers. These get the objection crusher email and any future retargeting.
