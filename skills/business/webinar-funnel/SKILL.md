# Webinar Funnel (Perfect Webinar)

You are an expert in Russell Brunson's Perfect Webinar methodology. Your goal is to build a complete webinar funnel: the script, the slides, the offer stack, the email sequences, and the automation layer.

## Prerequisites

- Offer details (from `product-offer` or user-provided): price, bonuses, value proposition, guarantee
- Target customer profile and primary problem (from `niche-finder`)
- Origin story and personal results (from user)
- Optional: testimonials, case studies, existing email list, webinar platform choice

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Big Domino

The ONE belief that, if the audience accepts it, makes every other objection irrelevant.

**Template:**
> "If I can get them to believe that [new opportunity] is the key to [desire], and show them my [framework], all other objections become irrelevant."

**How to find it:**
- [ ] What is the NEW opportunity you're presenting? (Not an improvement, a replacement)
- [ ] What desire does the audience already have?
- [ ] What is YOUR unique framework/vehicle for getting there?
- [ ] If they fully believed this one statement, would they buy? If not, refine it.

**Big Domino test:** Read it out loud. Does it sound like a single, clear, believable claim? If it has "and" in the middle connecting two separate ideas, it's two dominoes. Pick the one that matters more.

### Step 2: Map 3 False Beliefs

Every prospect has 3 categories of doubt. Each needs an Epiphany Bridge story to shatter it.

**Vehicle Belief (the solution type):**
- "This type of solution won't work for [goal]"
- They doubt the VEHICLE, not themselves
- Example: "Webinars don't work anymore" or "AI tools are overhyped"

**Internal Belief (their own capability):**
- "I can't do this because [personal limitation]"
- They doubt THEMSELVES
- Example: "I'm not technical enough" or "I don't have an audience"

**External Belief (outside forces):**
- "Even if it works and I could do it, [outside force] will stop me"
- They doubt the ENVIRONMENT
- Example: "My market is too saturated" or "I don't have enough time"

For each belief:
- [ ] Write the exact belief statement (in their words)
- [ ] Why do they hold it? (What experience created it?)
- [ ] What Epiphany Bridge story breaks it?
- [ ] What framework/evidence replaces the old belief?

See `references/false-beliefs-worksheet.md` for the full template with examples.

### Step 3: Write Epiphany Bridge Stories

You need 4 stories total: 1 origin story + 3 belief-breaking stories.

**The 14-Question Epiphany Bridge Script:**

For each story, answer these questions to build the narrative:

1. What is the backstory that gives context?
2. What were your desires at the time?
3. What was the wall you hit? (The conflict)
4. What was the epiphany? (The moment everything changed)
5. What was the plan that emerged from the epiphany?
6. How did it feel when you first tried the new approach?
7. What specific conflict or setback happened during the attempt?
8. What was the achievement or result?
9. What was the transformation? (Before state vs. after state)
10. What did you learn that you didn't expect?
11. How does this connect to the audience's situation?
12. What would have happened if you never had the epiphany?
13. What's the one-sentence moral of the story?
14. How does this lead into the teaching point?

**Story assignments:**
- **Origin story** (intro section): How you discovered the new opportunity
- **Story 1** (Secret 1): Breaks the Vehicle false belief
- **Story 2** (Secret 2): Breaks the Internal false belief
- **Story 3** (Secret 3): Breaks the External false belief

**Story rules:**
- Start with the character in a similar situation to the audience
- Include emotional details (what you felt, not just what happened)
- The epiphany should feel earned, not lectured
- End each story by connecting back to the teaching framework
- 3-5 minutes per story when delivered live

### Step 4: Build the Stack

The stack is every element of the offer, displayed with dollar values, building cumulatively.

**Stack elements to define:**
- [ ] Core product/program (name + description + standalone value)
- [ ] Bonus 1 (solves an immediate objection, assign dollar value)
- [ ] Bonus 2 (accelerates their result, assign dollar value)
- [ ] Bonus 3 (removes a friction point, assign dollar value)
- [ ] Optional: Bonus 4-6 (additional value layers)
- [ ] Calculate total stack value (sum of all elements)
- [ ] Set "If All" anchoring statements (3-4 variations)
- [ ] Set real price (stack value should be 3-10x the real price)
- [ ] Calculate per-day cost justification
- [ ] Define guarantee (type + terms + duration)
- [ ] Define urgency bonus (available only during webinar or within X hours)

**"If All" statement formula:**
> "If all this did was [specific outcome], would it be worth [price]?"

Write 3-4 variations, each targeting a different desire or pain point.

### Step 5: Script the Perfect Webinar

Three sections. 90 minutes total. Every minute is scripted.

#### Section 1: Intro (0-15 minutes)

**Goal:** Build rapport, establish authority, create curiosity, state the Big Domino.

- [ ] **Title Slide** -- Curiosity-driven title. Don't reveal the content. Pattern: "How to [outcome] without [thing they hate]"
- [ ] **Rapport via Blair Warren's 5 Levers:**
  1. Encourage their dreams ("You want X, and that's exactly right")
  2. Justify their failures ("It's not your fault, here's why...")
  3. Allay their fears ("What you're worried about won't happen because...")
  4. Confirm their suspicions ("You've always suspected X, and you're right")
  5. Help them throw rocks at enemies ("The gurus who told you X were wrong")
- [ ] **The Ruler:** Draw a line. "You are HERE (current painful state). By the end of this, you'll be HERE (desired state). I'm going to show you exactly how to get from one to the other."
- [ ] **Big Domino Statement:** State the one belief clearly
- [ ] **Qualify Yourself:** 2-3 credibility markers. Results, not resume. Show, don't list.
- [ ] **Origin Epiphany Bridge Story:** Your discovery story (from Step 3)
- [ ] **Hook to End:** "By the end of this training, you'll know exactly how to [X], [Y], and [Z]." Give them 3 specific reasons to stay.
- [ ] **Command Attention:** "Grab a pen. Close your other tabs. The next 60 minutes could change everything."
- [ ] **Future Pace:** "Imagine 90 days from now, you've [specific outcome]. That's what we're building toward today."

#### Section 2: Three Secrets (15-60 minutes)

**Goal:** Break all 3 false beliefs through teaching and stories.

**Structure for EACH secret (15 min each):**

1. [ ] State the false belief the audience holds
2. [ ] Tell the Epiphany Bridge story that breaks it (from Step 3)
3. [ ] Teach the framework that replaces the old belief
4. [ ] Show a case study or proof (results, screenshots, data)
5. [ ] Trial close: "Can you see how [framework] would work for your [situation]?"

**Secret 1 (Vehicle):** Breaks "this type of solution won't work"
- Teach WHAT the framework is, not HOW to implement every detail
- Show that the vehicle works with proof
- Trial close: "Now that you've seen [proof], do you believe [vehicle] could work for you?"

**Secret 2 (Internal):** Breaks "I can't do this"
- Show someone like them who succeeded despite the same limitation
- Teach the simplest version of the framework
- Trial close: "If [person in story] could do this starting from [low point], do you think you could too?"

**Secret 3 (External):** Breaks "outside forces will stop me"
- Address the environmental objection head-on with data or story
- Show how the framework accounts for that obstacle
- Trial close: "Now that you know [external obstacle] isn't actually a barrier, what's stopping you?"

**Teaching rules:**
- Teach WHAT, not HOW. The webinar creates desire for the how. The product delivers the how.
- Each secret should feel like a complete, valuable insight on its own
- Use visuals: frameworks, diagrams, before/after, screenshots
- Trial closes are questions, not pitches. They get mental agreement.

#### Section 3: Stack & Close (60-90 minutes)

**Goal:** Present the offer, build the stack, handle objections, close.

- [ ] **Recap 3 Secrets:** "Today you learned [Secret 1], [Secret 2], and [Secret 3]."
- [ ] **Transition Question:** "Let me ask you something... Now that you know [these 3 things], what would it be worth to have someone hand you the complete system?"
- [ ] **Build Stack Element by Element:**
  - Introduce each element with its standalone value
  - After each element, re-show all previous elements (cumulative display)
  - The slide grows with each addition
- [ ] **Big Stack Slide:** Show everything together with total value
- [ ] **"If All" Anchoring (3-4x):**
  - "If all this did was [outcome 1], would it be worth [price]?"
  - "If all this did was [outcome 2], would it be worth [price]?"
  - "If all this did was [outcome 3], would it be worth [price]?"
  - "But it does ALL of these things."
- [ ] **"I Had Two Choices" Story:** "When I figured this out, I had two choices. Keep it to myself, or package it and share it. I chose to share it because [reason]."
- [ ] **Price Anchor:** "Others charge [2x your price] for less." or "I paid [higher amount] to learn this."
- [ ] **Price Reveal:** State the actual price
- [ ] **Per-Day Justification:** "[Price] / 365 = [daily cost]. That's less than [relatable daily expense]."
- [ ] **Guarantee:** State it clearly. Remove risk entirely.
- [ ] **Urgency Bonus:** "If you sign up in the next [X hours/minutes], you also get [bonus] worth [value]."
- [ ] **Final CTA:** Clear URL or button. Repeat it 3x. "Go to [URL] right now."
- [ ] **Q&A:** Answer questions, but reframe each answer back to the offer. Every answer ends with "...and that's exactly what you get inside [product name]."

### Step 6: Registration Page

**Goal:** Maximize registrations through curiosity (not content reveals).

- [ ] **Headline:** Curiosity-driven. Pattern: "Free Training: How to [outcome] without [objection]"
- [ ] **Subheadline:** "Even if [common doubt]"
- [ ] **Image:** Slightly odd or pattern-interrupt photo (not corporate headshot). You presenting, whiteboard, unusual angle.
- [ ] **3 Bullet Points:** What they'll learn (curiosity-based, not content-based)
  - "The [counterintuitive truth] about [topic] that [surprising outcome]"
  - "Why [common approach] is keeping you stuck (and what to do instead)"
  - "The [number]-step framework for [result] that [credibility proof]"
- [ ] **Countdown Timer:** To the live event (or evergreen simulated time)
- [ ] **Social Proof:** "[Number] people already registered" or testimonial snippet
- [ ] **Registration Form:** Name + email. Nothing else. Every extra field kills conversion.

**Registration page rules:**
- Do NOT reveal what you'll teach. Curiosity gets registrations. Answers get skips.
- No navigation menu. No footer links. No distractions.
- Mobile-first design (60%+ will register on mobile)

### Step 7: Indoctrination Emails (Post-Registration, Pre-Webinar)

**Goal:** Build excitement and ensure they show up.

**Key rule: Do NOT answer their questions in these emails.** Keep curiosity high. Every email should make them MORE eager to attend, not less.

4-7 emails depending on lead time:

- [ ] **Email 1 (Immediately):** Confirmation + excitement. "You're in! Here's when to show up. Here's what to expect. Block your calendar NOW."
- [ ] **Email 2 (Day 1-2):** Personal story. Why you created this training. What you went through before you figured it out. No teaching.
- [ ] **Email 3 (Day 2-3):** Testimonial or case study. "[Name] used this exact framework and [result]." Build belief through others' results.
- [ ] **Email 4 (Day 3-4):** Behind-the-scenes. "I spent [X hours/months] putting this training together because..." Show preparation, build perceived value.
- [ ] **Email 5 (Day 4-5, if time allows):** Contrarian take. "Most people think [common belief], but I discovered [surprising truth]. I'll reveal exactly how on [date]."
- [ ] **Email 6 (Day 5-6, if time allows):** "What to prepare." Give them a simple pre-work task (write down their biggest challenge, clear their schedule). Increases commitment.
- [ ] **Email 7 (Day before):** Anticipation peak. "Tomorrow changes everything. Here's what [past attendee] said about the last time I taught this."

See `references/webinar-email-sequences.md` for full templates.

### Step 8: Reminder Emails

**Goal:** Maximize live attendance.

- [ ] **Day Before (evening):** "Tomorrow is the day. Here's the link. Set an alarm."
- [ ] **Morning Of:** "Today's the day. We start at [time]. Here's what you need: [link], a pen, and an open mind."
- [ ] **1 Hour Before:** "60 minutes. Get your coffee. Close your tabs. See you soon."
- [ ] **15 Minutes Before:** "We're about to start. Click here to join now: [link]"
- [ ] **Going Live Now:** "WE'RE LIVE. Join now: [link]. Don't miss the opening, it sets up everything."

**Reminder rules:**
- Subject lines get progressively more urgent
- Each email is shorter than the last
- Always include the join link
- The "going live" email is 2-3 sentences max

### Step 9: Post-Webinar Campaign

**Goal:** Convert attendees who didn't buy during the live event.

**Timeline (if live webinar is Thursday):**

- [ ] **Thursday (post-webinar):** "Thanks for attending" + replay link + offer recap + deadline
- [ ] **Friday:** Replay reminder + new testimonial from a live attendee + "here's what you missed if you left early" (recap the stack)
- [ ] **Saturday:** Urgency email. Testimonial-heavy. Address the #1 objection from Q&A. "48 hours left."
- [ ] **Sunday AM:** "Final day" email. Stack recap. Guarantee reminder. Per-day cost breakdown.
- [ ] **Sunday PM (3-6 hours before deadline):** "Last chance." Short, direct. Link + deadline. Nothing else.
- [ ] **Sunday (1 hour before deadline):** "Closing in 60 minutes." 2-3 sentences.

**Replay rules:**
- Replay window: 48-72 hours max. Real deadline.
- Include the offer link below/beside the replay video
- Do NOT extend the deadline. Credibility depends on following through.

See `references/webinar-email-sequences.md` for full templates.

### Step 10: Evergreen Automation

**Goal:** Run the webinar on autopilot with automated sequences.

- [ ] **Recorded webinar:** Use the best live recording or record a clean version
- [ ] **Automated countdown timer:** Shows personalized deadline based on registration time (48-72hr window)
- [ ] **Simulated "live" elements:**
  - Chat simulation (pre-loaded questions and reactions)
  - "Starting in X minutes" holding page
  - Disable fast-forward on replay
- [ ] **Automated email sequences:** All indoctrination + reminder + post-webinar emails triggered by registration timestamp
- [ ] **Dynamic deadlines:** Each registrant gets their own 48-72hr offer window
- [ ] **Segmentation:** Tag registrants by behavior (registered, attended, watched X%, clicked offer, purchased)
- [ ] **Ongoing traffic:** Registration page stays live. Ads, content, and funnels drive traffic continuously.

**Evergreen rules:**
- Test the full sequence yourself before going live
- Monitor conversion rates weekly. If below 2-3% of registrants, revisit the script.
- Refresh the recording every 3-6 months (references get stale)
- Never fake "live" if someone asks directly. "This is a recorded training" is fine.

**Platform options:** EverWebinar, WebinarKit, Demio (hybrid), or custom (Wistia + countdown plugin).

## Writing Style

- Conversational, high-energy but not hype
- Short paragraphs. Punchy sentences.
- Speak to ONE person in the audience, not a crowd
- Stories > statistics (but use both)
- Never say "I'm going to teach you." Say "Let me show you."
