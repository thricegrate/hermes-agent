## Core Four

The only four ways to let anyone know about anything:

| Channel | Type | Audience | Best For |
|---------|------|----------|----------|
| **Warm Outreach** | 1-to-1 | People who know you | Quickest results, highest conversion |
| **Cold Outreach** | 1-to-1 | Strangers | Scale without content, predictable |
| **Post Free Content** | 1-to-many | Your network/followers | Compounds over time, builds brand |
| **Paid Ads** | 1-to-many | Cold audiences | Fastest scale, requires budget |

**Strategic sequence:**
1. Start with warm outreach (free, highest conversion)
2. Layer in content (builds over time)
3. Add cold outreach (scalable, systematic)
4. Add paid ads last (requires cash flow to sustain)

**More → Better → New:**
- **More**: Do more of what's currently working until it breaks
- **Better**: Optimize when CAC gets too high
- **New**: Only try new channels after maxing current ones

**Key insight:** Every time you try something new, there's a learning curve that fragments attention. This hurts what's already working.

**Coaching questions:**
- "Which of the Core Four are you currently using?"
- "What's your volume on each? Give me the exact numbers."
- "Which channel has the best CAC? Are you maxing it out?"
- "Why are you trying new channels before maxing your best one?"

## Lead Magnet Framework

**7 steps to an irresistible lead magnet:**

1. **Figure out the problem**: Narrow problem for your ideal customer
2. **Figure out how to solve it**: Complete solution to that narrow problem
3. **Figure out how to deliver it**: Lowest friction format
4. **Make the name interesting and clear**: Use MAGIC naming formula
5. **Make it easy to consume**: 5-15 minutes max
6. **Make sure it's darn good**: Good enough someone might pay
7. **Tell them what to do next**: Clear CTA to your core offer

**Three types:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Problem Revealers | Show problems they didn't know they had | "AI Readiness Audit: Find the 10 Tasks You Should Have Automated Yesterday" |
| Samplers & Trials | Give a taste of your solution | "7-Day AI Productivity Challenge" |
| One Step of the Process | Solve one part of a bigger problem | "The AI Tool Stack Template: Set Up in 30 Minutes" |

**Quality standard:** "If your lead magnet is not good enough that people tell other people about it, you have failed."

**Delivery methods:** Software (spreadsheets, calculators), information (courses, guides), services (audits, consultations), physical products.

## Rule of 100

**Formula:** 100 primary actions per day for 100 days straight.

**Application by channel:**
| Channel | 100/Day Looks Like |
|---------|-------------------|
| Warm outreach | 100 emails, texts, DMs, or calls |
| Cold outreach | 100 cold emails, DMs, cold calls |
| Content | 100 minutes per day making content |
| Paid ads | $100 per day in ad spend |

**Why it works:**
- Volume creates skill (you get better by doing)
- Volume creates data (you learn what works)
- Volume creates results (law of large numbers)
- Consistency compounds (audience builds over time)

**Promise:** "If you do 100 primary actions per day for 100 days straight, you will get more engaged leads and will never go hungry again."

**Coaching challenge:** "What are your 100 daily primary actions? If you can't name them, that's your problem."

## CTA Framework

**Three-part structure:**
1. **Compelling hook**: Stop the scroll, get attention
2. **Substantive content**: Deliver real value (the "meat")
3. **Clear CTA**: One specific next step

**CTA rules:**
- "Stupid simple" directions with no confusion about next steps
- Write at a third-grade reading level
- One CTA per piece of content (not multiple)
- For video: Place CTA ~1/3 through (not at the end when they've left)
- Keep CTA brief but clear about what's offered

**Strong CTAs:**
- "Subscribe at [link]: free AI tools every Tuesday"
- "Reply 'AUDIT' and I'll review your newsletter"
- "Grab the template: [link]"

**Weak CTAs:**
- "Check out my newsletter" (no benefit stated)
- "Follow me for more" (not actionable)
- "Link in bio" (too many steps)

**Two approaches:**
1. **Organic plus CTA**: Add CTA to content that already performs organically
2. **Direct ads**: Strong hooks (80% of ad effectiveness) + clear CTA

## Content & Media Scaling

**Repurposing system:**
Create one long-form piece → slice into 30+ bite-sized assets:
- 1 YouTube video or podcast episode
- → 5-10 TikTok/Reels/Shorts clips
- → 5-10 LinkedIn/Twitter posts
- → 3-5 email newsletter excerpts
- → 1-2 blog posts
- → Carousel graphics
- → Quote images

**Time allocation:** 93% doing business, 7% on content creation (2 days/month or 4 hours/week).

**Volume target:** 250+ pieces per week across all platforms (through repurposing, not original creation).

**Brand as leverage:** "Brand is the best way to improve LTV and reduce CAC at the same time."
- Makes it cheaper to acquire customers (fixed media expenses, unlimited upside in eyeballs)
- Increases LTV (continued content makes customers likely to keep purchasing)

**Scaling approach:** One product → one avatar → one channel. Master it before adding the next element.

## Industry Example: Newsletter Business

*The frameworks above apply to ANY business. Below is one industry example to illustrate application.*

### Subscriber acquisition (Core Four applied):
- **Warm outreach**: Personal emails/DMs to ideal subscribers, ask for referrals, partner cross-promotions
- **Cold outreach**: Cold email to prospects who'd benefit from your content
- **Content**: Social media posts that showcase newsletter value, thread/carousel teasers
- **Paid ads**: Meta ads, co-registration networks, Beehiiv Boosts, SparkLoop swaps

### Lead magnet for newsletter growth:
- Free AI tool guides, templates, checklists
- Mini email courses (3-5 day series)
- AI readiness audits
- Tool comparison spreadsheets
- Prompt libraries

### Co-promotion optimization:
- Track which partner newsletters send highest-quality subscribers (open rate, not just volume)
- Measure subscriber quality by source: open rate, CTR, revenue per subscriber by cohort
- Cut partners whose subscribers have <20% open rate after 30 days
- Double spend on partners whose subscribers have >30% open rate

### Meta ads optimization:
- Test 5-10 creatives per week (Rule of 100 applied to ad testing)
- Measure CAC by creative, not by campaign
- Target: CAC < $2 per subscriber (for free newsletter)
- Kill underperformers fast, scale winners immediately

## Offer Types for Lead Generation (Hormozi Lost Chapters)

Three distinct offer types that serve different strategic purposes:

### Premium Offers
Full-price or above-market offers that maximize revenue per customer. Use when you have proven product-market fit and strong proof.

**190:1 Volume-to-Profit comparison:**
- Low-ticket: 190 sales x $50 profit = $9,500
- High-ticket: 5% of 190 people = 9.5 sales x $9,500 profit = $90,250
- Same leads, 9.5x more profitable. Fewer clients to manage (9 vs 190).

### Free Offers
$0 price point designed for maximum volume. Free is NOT a discount -- it's a fundamentally different psychological category.

**The Penny Gap (Dan Ariely):** 9x more people take a free Hershey kiss than one sold for a penny. The gap between $0 and $0.01 is the biggest conversion gap in all of marketing.

**If your free offer doesn't work:** (a) they don't want it, (b) they don't believe you, or (c) wrong audience. It's never "free doesn't work."

### Discount Offers
Below-market price designed as a "promotional wrapper" around your Grand Slam Offer. The core offer stays the same -- only the wrapper changes.

**Key reframe:** "We shouldn't see people as customers if they buy the discount. We should see them as qualified leads." A discount buyer is a lead who proved willingness to act.

## Five Types of Friction (Lead Quality Dial)

Five specific friction mechanisms you can add to any offer to increase lead quality at the cost of volume. Think of it as a quality dial.

1. **Qualifications** -- "Must be a business owner making $X+" (repeat in copy, creative, landing pages)
2. **Information Requirements** -- More fields = more friction. Phone number > first name. Open-ended > multiple choice.
3. **Number of Steps** -- Each step drops people. One-step optin beats 5-step form.
4. **Forced Consumption** -- Make prospects watch a 40-min video before CTA appears. Pre-indoctrinates. Best when eyeballs are cheap.
5. **Advertisement Length** -- Longer ads/copy/videos before CTA = fewer but higher-quality clicks.

**Coaching application:** When a client says "I'm getting lots of leads but they're all junk," add friction. When they say "I'm not getting enough leads," remove friction. It's a dial, not a switch.

**Key insight:** Free does NOT mean unqualified. You control lead quality through friction, not price.

## The Penny Gap

**Source:** Dan Ariely research, applied by Hormozi

The biggest drop in conversion happens between free and $0.01 (not between $0.01 and $1, or $1 and $10). The gap between free and any price at all is disproportionately large.

**Application:** If you're going to charge, charge a meaningful amount. The conversion hit from $0 to $1 is almost the same as $0 to $100. So either go free (for volume) or go premium (for profit). The middle is the worst of both worlds.

**Coaching question:** "Is your lead magnet free? If not, why not? If it is, is it good enough that people would pay for it?"

## Flow Sequence

**Source:** $100M Lost Chapters + Bonus Chapters

Three-phase approach to building any market entry:

1. **Get Flow** -- First priority: get people in the door, even if free or at a loss. Volume first.
2. **Monetize Flow** -- Once you have consistent traffic/leads, introduce your paid offer.
3. **Add Friction** -- Once monetized, selectively add friction to improve lead quality and filter out tire-kickers.

**Key mistake:** Adding friction (applications, qualifications, high prices) BEFORE you have flow. You can't filter what you don't have.

> "Get Flow. Monetize Flow. Then Add Friction."

**Coaching diagnostic:**
- "Do you have flow? How many leads per week?"
- "Are you monetizing that flow? What's your conversion rate?"
- "Have you added friction too early? Are you qualifying people before you have volume?"

If they're adding applications, long questionnaires, or high prices before they have consistent lead flow, they've skipped a step.
