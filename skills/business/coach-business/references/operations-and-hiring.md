## Phone Test

**The diagnostic question:** "Can your business grow during a three-month founder absence?"

If yes → You have systems. Scale them.
If no → You ARE the bottleneck. Fix this first.

**How to run the Phone Test:**
1. List every task you do in a week
2. Categorize: (a) Only I can do, (b) Someone else could do with training, (c) Should already be delegated
3. Category (c) items → delegate immediately
4. Category (b) items → document SOPs, hire/train, delegate within 90 days
5. Category (a) items → this is your actual job. Everything else is overhead.

**Key quote:** "The less you do, the more you make."

**Coaching questions:**
- "If you disappeared for 3 months, would revenue go up, down, or stay flat?"
- "What tasks do you personally do every day? List them."
- "Which of those tasks REQUIRE your specific expertise vs. just your attention?"

## First 5 Hires

In reverse order of when to hire (hire #1 first):

| Hire | Role | Why | When |
|------|------|-----|------|
| 1 | **Admin / Executive Assistant** | Free up your time for high-value work | Immediately (you're drowning in admin) |
| 2 | **Customer Support** | Protect customer experience at scale | When support tickets take >2 hours/day |
| 3 | **Salesperson** | Remove you from sales calls | When you have a repeatable sales process |
| 4 | **Marketing Assistant** | Scale content and lead gen | When you have a working marketing channel |
| 5 | **Fractional Bookkeeper** | Financial clarity without full-time cost | When revenue exceeds $10K/mo |

**Hiring philosophy:**
- "Hiring talented people is one of the easiest 10x-100x ROI opportunities"
- "There's always underpriced talent": look outside your industry
- Pay for outcomes, not hours
- Hire for character first, skills second (skills can be taught)

## Five Core Functions

Every business scales through five functions. Each needs a dedicated owner:

| Function | Description | KPI |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| **Application Generation** | Getting people to raise their hand | # of leads/applications per week |
| **Application Nurture** | Moving leads toward a decision | Conversion rate (lead → call) |
| **Interviewing (Selling)** | Closing the deal | Close rate + revenue per sale |
| **Fulfillment** | Delivering the product/service | Customer satisfaction + completion rate |
| **Retention / Ascension** | Keeping customers + upgrading them | Churn rate + LTV |

**Scaling rule:** Each function needs a single-threaded owner. One person, one KPI, full accountability.

**Three-step career progression:**
1. Learn to sell (master the skill)
2. Teach others to sell (build a team)
3. Build and recruit the team (become a leader)

## Delegation Method

**Scorecards + single-threaded owners:**
- Every role has a scorecard with 3-5 KPIs
- KPIs must be controllable inputs, not just outputs
- Review weekly: "What are your numbers? What are you doing about the ones that are off?"

**Delegation framework:**
1. **Document**: Write the SOP before delegating
2. **Demonstrate**: Show how you do it (screen record, walk through)
3. **Duplicate**: Have them do it while you watch
4. **Delegate**: They do it independently, you review output
5. **Distance**: They own it fully, you only check KPIs

**Leaders get latitude:** Set the destination and KPIs. Let them design the systems to get there. Report weekly on controllable inputs.

**Meeting cadence:**
- Daily: 15-min standup (what's stuck?)
- Weekly: 1-hour review (KPIs, priorities, blockers)
- Monthly: Strategic review (are we on track for quarterly goals?)
- Quarterly: Planning (set next quarter's priorities)

## Recruitment as Acquisition

**Apply the marketing funnel to hiring:**

| Marketing Funnel | Hiring Funnel |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Lead generation | Job posting distribution |
| Nurture | Employer brand content |
| Sales call | Interview (you're selling the role) |
| Onboarding | Employee onboarding |
| Retention | Culture + growth path |

**Rapid scaling method:**
- Use 6 recruiting firms, each providing 10 candidates = 60 candidates
- Can add 40+ salespeople in 2 weeks using this approach
- Volume of candidates → better selection → better hires

**Interview test:** "Can they teach me something new in the interview?" Tests for:
- Depth of knowledge
- Communication ability
- Preparation and effort
- Intellectual curiosity

**Compensation strategy:**
- Base + performance bonus aligned with KPIs
- Commission structures for revenue-generating roles
- Profit sharing for leadership roles
- Equity/ownership for key executives

## Industry Example: Newsletter Business

*The frameworks above apply to ANY business. Below is one industry example to illustrate application.*

### Team structure for 1 newsletter ($5K/mo)
- 1 writer (part-time or freelance)
- 1 growth manager (part-time, handles ads + co-promos)
- You handle strategy, monetization, ad sales

### Team structure for 5 newsletters ($25K/mo)
- 2-3 writers (specialize by newsletter)
- 1 full-time growth manager
- 1 editor/QA person
- You handle strategy, monetization, ad sales, partnerships

### Team structure for 10+ newsletters ($50K+/mo)
- 4-6 writers (2 newsletters each)
- 1 head of growth (manages all acquisition channels)
- 1 head of content (manages editorial quality + calendar)
- 1 ad sales / monetization person
- 1 operations / automation manager
- You handle strategy, partnerships, new newsletter launches

### Newsletter portfolio SOPs needed:
1. **Content SOP**: Topic selection → research → draft → edit → publish → distribute
2. **Growth SOP**: Meta ads setup → creative testing → budget allocation → co-promo management
3. **Monetization SOP**: Ad network integration → sponsor outreach → rate card → reporting
4. **Launch SOP**: Niche validation → branding → first 10 issues → growth kickoff
5. **Flip SOP**: Build to 100K → document metrics → find buyer → transition support

### Automation opportunities:
- Scheduled publishing (Beehiiv automation)
- Social media distribution (Buffer/Hootsuite)
- Subscriber onboarding sequences (Beehiiv automation)
- Ad performance reporting (automated dashboards)
- Content calendar management (shared Google Sheets/Notion)
- Co-promotion tracking (SparkLoop + Google Sheets)

### The constraint at scale:
- At 1-5 newsletters: Constraint is usually content creation or growth
- At 5-10 newsletters: Constraint shifts to editorial quality control
- At 10+ newsletters: Constraint shifts to management overhead and monetization per subscriber
- Apply TOC: Only invest in the constraint. Don't hire more writers if the bottleneck is monetization.
