## The Growth Formula

```
Stress + Rest = Growth
```

In athletic training, stress is the workout. Rest is when the body adapts and gets stronger. Skip stress, nothing changes. Skip rest, performance breaks. Business works the same way.

Pressure, standards, deadlines, and intensity drive growth. But when stress is constant and never turns off, it stops helping. Judgment gets worse. Creativity drops. Everything feels urgent even when it is not.

**Maps to Performance Equation variable R (Regulation).** See `coach-core/references/performance-equation.md`. When R approaches zero, the equation collapses the same way a zero in Direction does -- just for different reasons.

## The High Performer Mistake

The biggest mistake high performers make: thinking constant pressure proves commitment. It does not. It proves you do not know how to recover.

### Signal Phrases (listen for these)

- "I'll rest when the launch is over"
- "Grinding is how I got here"
- "I can sleep when I'm dead"
- "If I stop, everything falls apart"
- "Rest feels like giving up"
- "I just need to push through this quarter"
- "I can't afford to take a day off right now"
- "My team needs to see me working hard"

### Coaching Response

"The fact that you see rest as weakness is the problem, not the solution. The best performers I've worked with treat recovery as part of the job, not a reward for finishing it. Right now you're running a sprint pace on a marathon course. That is not toughness. That is a crash waiting to happen."

## Two Toolbelts

Elite performers carry two sets of tools:

| Toolbelt | Purpose | Examples |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Push Tools** | Create intensity, drive output | Discipline, deadlines, focus blocks, accountability, competitive drive |
| **Rest Tools** | Create recovery, restore capacity | Decompression rituals, off-days, recovery blocks, transitions between work and rest, real hobbies |

Most high performers only have push tools. They never built the rest toolbelt.

### Diagnostic Table

| Push Tools | Rest Tools | Diagnosis |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Strong | Strong | Sustainable performer. Monitor and maintain. |
| Strong | Missing/Weak | **Burnout trajectory.** Deploy this framework. |
| Missing/Weak | Strong | Not a recovery problem. Route to Direction Deficit (`coach-core/references/performance-equation.md`). |
| Missing/Weak | Missing/Weak | Foundational problem. Route to `coach-personal` for state management. |

### Coaching Question

"Tell me about your push tools -- what do you use to get into gear? Good. Now tell me about your rest tools -- what do you use to get OUT of gear?"

If the second answer is blank or "I don't know," that is the problem.

## Recovery as a Skill

Rest is not lowering the bar. Rest is part of performance. It is when lessons sink in, thinking gets clearer, and energy comes back so the next push is actually strong instead of forced.

**Business impact of missing recovery:**
- Founders who never recover make worse decisions on hiring, pricing, product, and strategy
- The cost is invisible until the damage is done
- Teams mirror the founder's burnout -- one burned-out leader creates a burned-out team

**Coaching question:** "When was the last time you made a business decision you later regretted? Were you rested or exhausted when you made it?"

**Key insight:** If you only have push tools and you are wondering why you are not further along -- there is your answer.

## Recovery Before Push

Plan cooldown windows BEFORE the sprint, not after the crash.

### The Principle

If you know an intense period is coming -- a launch, an event, a filming cycle, a big planning push -- put a recovery window on the calendar BEFORE the work starts. No more back-to-back sprints without a decompression window built in.

### Integration with Sprint Planning

Add to every 90-day commitment (the Mark Protocol) and every weekly sprint:

1. **Pre-sprint:** Schedule the recovery window alongside the project plan
2. **During sprint:** Protect at least one off-day per week
3. **Post-sprint:** 3-5 day decompression window before the next push

### Coaching Question

"Show me your project plan. Now show me where recovery is scheduled. If it is not on the calendar, it will not happen."

**Ask about cooldown plans alongside project plans.** Recovery is not what you do when the sprint fails. It is what you schedule before the sprint starts.

## The Full Day Off Rule

One day per week. Fully off. No Slack, no email, no work documents. Not mostly off. Fully off.

**Why it works:**
- One day off makes the remaining days more productive
- Quality of work increases for the rest of the week
- Judgment stays sharp instead of degrading over time
- Decision fatigue resets

**The test:** Can you name your off day? If not, you do not have one.

### Coaching Response

"I don't mean a day where you only check email twice. I mean a day where your laptop stays closed. Your team learns to handle things. And you come back Monday with a brain that actually works."

## Leader Modeling

"They do what you do, not what you say."

If the founder answers Slack at 11pm, the team will too. If the founder never takes a day off, the team will not either. Even if you SAY "take time off," your behavior sets the real culture.

**The multiplication effect:** One leader who does not rest creates a team that does not rest. That team makes worse decisions, burns out faster, and turns over more. The cost compounds.

**Resting is not lack of commitment. It is how you protect your performance long-term.**

### Coaching Questions

- "What message does your calendar send your team about rest? Because that is the policy they follow, not whatever is in the employee handbook."
- "When was the last time your team saw you take a full day off?"
- "If your best employee told you they were burned out, what would you tell them? Now tell yourself the same thing."

## Bad Recovery vs Real Recovery

Not all rest is recovery. The forms of recovery you think help probably do not.

| Bad Recovery (does not count) | Real Recovery (does count) |
|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Scrolling phone | Walking outside without phone |
| Watching TV while checking email | Fully immersive hobby (cooking, sport, music) |
| "Relaxing" while thinking about work | Time with people where work is not discussed |
| Sleeping poorly because you checked Slack at 11pm | Sleep with a hard cutoff on screens |
| Vacation with laptop | Vacation where the team handles things |

### The Litmus Test

"Did your brain actually leave work, or did it just change rooms?"

### Coaching Application

When a client says "I do rest" but is still burned out, audit the quality of their rest. Most high performers have never learned to rest hard. They default to passive consumption (phone, TV) which does not restore capacity.

## The "I Can't Afford Rest" Signal

If your first reaction to this framework is "I can't afford to rest right now," that is the single clearest sign you need to.

**Why this matters:** It means you have been operating without recovery for so long that you do not even know how to recover. The resistance itself is the symptom.

**Parallel to the Biological Fix Trap** (see `coach-core/references/performance-equation.md`): the person most resistant to the fix is the one who needs it most. Just as people chase biological explanations to avoid the direction problem, people chase "more effort" to avoid the recovery problem.

For many high performers, rest IS the harder thing. That makes it the highest form of commitment.

### Coaching Response

"You just said you can't afford to rest. That is the strongest signal I have that rest is the intervention you need. The person who can't stop is not strong. They have lost the ability to shift gears. And that is a skill gap, not a virtue."

## Coaching Application

### Module Routing Table

| Situation | Deploy |
|-----------|--------|
| Founder/leader showing burnout symptoms | Growth Formula + Two Toolbelts diagnostic |
| Team performance declining despite high effort | Leader Modeling audit |
| Sprint planning with no recovery built in | Recovery Before Push integration |
| Client says "I'll rest when..." or "I can't stop now" | High Performer Mistake reframe |
| Client's idea of rest is phone scrolling or TV | Bad vs. Real Recovery distinction |
| Quarterly planning or 90-day commitment design | Full Day Off Rule + Recovery scheduling |
| Client resists the idea of rest entirely | "I Can't Afford Rest" Signal |

### Immediate Actions (prescribe in session)

1. Schedule one full day off in the next two weeks
2. Put a recovery window on the calendar for the next big push
3. Name three real recovery activities (not phone, not TV)
4. If you lead a team: take the day off visibly so they see it

## Think Day (Leila Hormozi)

Full-day calendar block. No meetings. No team. No operations. No notifications.

> "I step away, I reflect, I audit the business, and I look at the next chapter WITHOUT distractions."

### Purpose

Think Day is strategic rest. Not rest for rest's sake. Rest for clarity. It's a structured Free Day where the goal is to step out of the noise long enough to get clear on what the next chapter actually requires.

### 6 Core Reflection Questions

1. **Lessons learned:** What were the most important lessons this quarter about the business, your team, and yourself that you don't want to repeat or ignore?
2. **Division requirements:** What must your business accomplish next quarter, and what must change for those outcomes to be possible?
3. **What you need:** What do you need from your team, your peers, and your resources to lead at the level the next stage demands?
4. **What to cut:** If you had to remove one thing you're doing today to double impact next quarter, what would you cut? Why haven't you cut it yet?
5. **Player-coach vs director:** Where are you still leading like a player-coach when the business now requires you to lead like a director? Also consider the opposite: where should you still be hands-on?
6. **Independent company test:** If your business were an independent company with real shareholders, what would your strategy be for the next 12 months? What would you hold yourself accountable for that you currently don't?

### When to Deploy

- Before holidays or quarter-end (best time: before the break, not after)
- When strategic drift signals appear (client has been in execution mode 8+ weeks without review)
- When client says "I'm in the weeds" or "I've lost the big picture"
- Quarterly, minimum. Monthly if the business is in a high-change period.

### Coaching Prescription

> "When was the last time you spent a full day just thinking about the business, with no meetings, no Slack, no fires? That's the assignment."

Use `templates/think-day.md` as the session worksheet.
