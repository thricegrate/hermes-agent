## Overview

The Fast Cash Play is a complete quarterly promotion system for extracting maximum cash from warm audiences. The core mechanic: limited time offers to existing and previous customers, priced 10-50x normal, with layered bonuses revealed over 7 days.

Despite being roughly 15% of annual revenue, Fast Cash Plays can represent nearly 50% of profit. The margins are enormous because you already paid to acquire these customers. Every dollar above delivery cost drops straight to the bottom line.

This is not about getting new customers. This is about monetizing the ones you already have at a level most business owners never consider.

---

## Target Audience

**Who gets the offer:**

- Existing customers (currently paying you)
- Previous customers (bought before, not currently active)
- Engaged leads (warm only -- they know you, trust you, have interacted recently)

**Who does NOT get the offer:**

- Cold leads who have never bought
- Cooled-off leads (6+ months with no contact) -- warm them up first, sell to them next quarter
- Anyone who doesn't already know and trust you

The entire system depends on existing trust and relationship. You are not selling strangers. You are offering your best people something they cannot get anywhere else.

## Premium Value Levers

There are 10 levers you can pull to make an offer feel worth 10-50x more than your standard. You don't need all 10. Pick the ones that fit your business and stack them.

### 1. Attention (1-on-1 or Many-on-1)

Give them more of you. Instead of group coaching, they get private sessions. Instead of one coach, they get a team assigned to them. The ratio of provider-to-customer goes way up.

### 2. Personalization (Customized Solutions)

Nothing off the shelf. Everything is built for them specifically. Custom plans, custom strategies, custom deliverables. Their name is on it. Their situation drives every decision.

### 3. Convenience (24/7 Support, Extended Hours)

Remove all friction. They can reach you anytime. Weekend support. Holiday availability. Slack channel with same-day responses. You bend your schedule around theirs.

### 4. Status (Special Perks, Badges, Recognition)

Make them feel like VIPs. Exclusive titles, public recognition, special badges, featured in your content. Human beings pay enormous premiums to feel important.

### 5. Duration (Longer Than Standard)

If your normal program is 8 weeks, make this one 12 months. If you normally do 30-minute calls, make these 90 minutes. Extend the timeline and the depth of engagement.

### 6. Speed (Priority, First in Line, Fast Response)

They skip the line. Guaranteed response within hours, not days. Priority scheduling. First access to everything. Speed is one of the most underpriced value levers.

### 7. Experience (White Glove, Concierge, Dinners)

Make the experience itself memorable. Private dinners, curated events, white-glove onboarding, concierge-level service. People remember how you made them feel.

### 8. Access (In-Person, Retreats, Behind-the-Scenes)

Physical proximity and behind-the-curtain access. In-person meetups, retreats, office visits, ride-alongs. Things they literally cannot buy anywhere else.

### 9. Network (Connection with Other Top Customers)

Put your best customers in a room together. The network effect is worth more than anything you could teach. Mastermind dinners, private groups, curated introductions.

### 10. Secrets (Exclusive Bonuses, Trade Secrets, "Secret Menu")

Things you don't share publicly. Your actual playbooks, your real numbers, your behind-the-scenes processes. The "secret menu" that only insiders know about. Exclusivity drives perceived value through the roof.

## Offer Structure and Pricing

### How Many to Sell

Cap at 5-10% of your customer base. The goal is to sell out fast. Scarcity must be real, not manufactured. If you have 200 customers, you are selling 10-20 spots maximum.

### How to Price

10x to 50x your average transaction value. The price must be beyond comparison to your standard offer. If your normal thing costs $500, this is $5,000 to $25,000. The point is to make it a completely different category.

### How to Sell

Two methods depending on price point:

- **Consult (high-ticket):** Push people to schedule a call. You or a closer walks them through the offer, handles objections, closes on the phone. Use this for $5,000+ offers.
- **Automated checkout (hot lists):** For audiences that are already bought-in and the price point is lower, send them straight to a checkout page. Use this for offers under $5,000 or when your list is extremely hot.

### Structure

- **1-3 core components** (the main deliverables -- what they are buying)
- **3 bonuses** (revealed one at a time during the sequence -- each one makes the offer more irresistible)

### Frequency

Once per quarter is the sweet spot. Four times a year. Enough to be significant revenue without burning out your list.

### Margins

Run at 90%+ gross margins. Since these are premium offers to existing customers, your acquisition cost is zero. Keep delivery lean and high-touch. The math works because you are not scaling -- you are going deep with a small group.

## Push-to-Consult 7-Day Sequence

Use this sequence when selling high-ticket offers ($5,000+) that require a sales conversation.

### Day 7 Out (1 Week Before Close)

**Channel:** Text only

**Action:** Send a teaser text. No details. Just intrigue.

Example: "Something big is coming tomorrow. Keep an eye on your inbox."

The goal is to prime attention. Nothing more.

### Day 6 Out (Announcement Day)

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Send the announcement email with 1-3 core components of the offer. Include a clear CTA to schedule a consult. Follow up with a text pointing to the email.

This is the big reveal. Lay out what the offer is, who it's for, and why it's different from anything you've done before. Do not reveal bonuses yet.

### Day 5 Out

**Channel:** Email

**Action:** Recap the offer. Reveal the first bonus. Re-send the CTA to schedule a consult.

The recap catches anyone who missed Day 6. The first bonus adds fuel. People who were on the fence start leaning forward.

### Day 3 Out

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Sales update -- how many spots have sold, how many are left. Text reminder to book if they haven't.

Social proof in real time. "7 of 15 spots are gone" creates real urgency because the scarcity is real.

### Day 2 Out

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** "X spots left" + reveal the second bonus. Text drives to email.

Two bonuses are now stacked on top of the core offer. The perceived value keeps climbing.

### Day 1 AM (Morning of Final Day)

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Warning -- few hours or spots left. Reveal the BEST bonus. This final bonus should be worth more than the entire price tag on its own.

This is the nuclear option. The best bonus drops last. It should make people feel stupid for not buying.

### Day 1 PM (Afternoon/Evening of Final Day)

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Sold out notification. Thank everyone. Create FOMO for next quarter.

Even if you didn't technically sell out, the close is the close. Create anticipation for the next one.

### Optional: Back-Out Spot

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** "Someone's payment didn't go through / someone backed out. One spot just opened up."

This is one of the highest-converting emails in the entire sequence. The people who wanted it but hesitated will jump on this.

## Push-to-Automated-Checkout 5-Day Sequence

Use this sequence when selling to a hot list at a price point that doesn't require a sales call, or when you want speed over high-touch.

### Day 5 Out

**Channel:** Text only

**Action:** Teaser text. Same energy as the consult sequence. Build anticipation.

### Day 4 Out

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Announcement email with full offer details. Text drives to email.

### Day 2 Out

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Cart opens in 48 hours. Build anticipation. Text reminder.

### Day 1 Out

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Reveal the first bonus. Tell them to set an alarm for cart open. Text reinforces.

### 60 Minutes Before Cart Open

**Channel:** Text only

**Action:** "Set your alarm. Have your payment info ready." Pure urgency.

### Cart Open

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Cart is live. Reveal the best bonus. Link to checkout. Text drives to email.

### 60 Minutes Post-Open

**Channel:** Text only

**Action:** Sales update. "X spots sold in the first hour. Y left."

### 6 Hours Post-Open

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Extra bonus added. "X spots left." This is the mid-day push for people who saw it but didn't act.

### 12 Hours Post-Open

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Sold out notification. Thank everyone. Build anticipation for next quarter.

### Next Day (Optional)

**Channel:** Email + Text

**Action:** Back-out spot available if someone's payment failed or they cancelled. Same high-conversion mechanic as the consult sequence.

## 4Rs Post-Promotion Collection

After the promotion closes and you begin delivering, collect these four things immediately. Do not wait. The energy is highest right after purchase.

### 1. Reviews

Get social proof while the excitement is fresh. Ask for video testimonials, written reviews, social media posts. These become your ammunition for the next quarterly promotion.

### 2. Results

Document everything. Before/after data, case studies, screenshots, metrics. Even early wins count. Results sell the next round better than any copy you could write.

### 3. Referrals

Ask for warm introductions to people like them. "Who else do you know who would want something like this?" Your best customers know other potential best customers.

### 4. Resells

Lock them in for next time. Offer prepaid renewals at a discount. Upsell additional services. The best time to sell someone is right after they've already bought and are feeling great about it.

## Fast Cash Checklist (10 Steps)

### Step 1: Pick and Assemble Your List

Compile contact info for all past and present customers. Segment by engagement level. Exclude anyone who has been cold for 6+ months (warm them up for next quarter).

### Step 2: Pick Your Offer

Design something that maxes out unscalable value. Stack the premium value levers. This should be the best thing you have ever offered. Think: "What would I build if I only had 10 customers and wanted to change their lives?"

### Step 3: Pick Your Price

10-50x your normal price. Be bold. If it doesn't make you slightly uncomfortable, you priced it too low. The price itself signals value. Beyond comparison means they cannot mentally anchor it to your standard offer.

### Step 4: Choose Your Sell Method

Consult for high-ticket ($5,000+). Automated checkout for hot lists at lower price points. Match the method to the price and the warmth of your audience.

### Step 5: Prep Emails and Texts with CTAs

Write every email and text in advance. Each one needs a clear, single CTA. No confusion about what to do next. Every message moves them toward buying or booking.

### Step 6: Send the Sequence

Execute the sequence (announcement, recap, countdown, bonus reveals, sold out). Stick to the schedule. Do not deviate. The timing is part of the psychology.

### Step 7: Deliver the Stuff

Over-deliver on what you promised. The experience must justify the premium price. Happy premium customers become your marketing department.

### Step 8: Collect the 4Rs

Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells. Do this immediately after delivery begins. Do not wait until the program ends. Early momentum matters.

### Step 9: Cooldown the List

Return to value-giving mode. Do not pitch again for at least a few weeks. Give them free content, useful insights, no-strings value. Rebuild goodwill before the next promotion.

### Step 10: Set the Next Date

Schedule the next Fast Cash Play for next quarter. Put it on the calendar. This is now a recurring profit engine, not a one-off event.

## ROI Example

**Business: Gym doing $25K/month in revenue**

### Before Fast Cash Plays

- Annual revenue: $300,000
- Annual profit: $75,000

### After 4 Quarterly Fast Cash Plays

- Annual revenue: $529,000
- Annual profit: $281,000

### Result

- 76% revenue increase
- 3.75x profit increase
- 22 new customers added (from referrals and resells)

The profit multiplication is the key number. Revenue went up 76%, but profit went up 275%. That is the power of selling premium to existing customers at 90%+ margins.

## 10x the 10% Rule

The math is simple and powerful:

If you get 10% of your customers to pay 10x the price, you double your revenue.

Here is why this works so well:

- You already spent money to acquire those customers. That cost is sunk.
- The extra revenue from the premium offer (minus delivery cost) drops straight to the bottom line.
- 1 out of 9 Americans is a millionaire. Your customer base contains people who can and will pay premium prices. You have just never asked.

Limit spots to attract big spenders. When something is exclusive and scarce, the people with money are the ones who act fastest. Open enrollment attracts bargain hunters. Limited spots attract serious buyers.

## Important Operational Points

### Cooled-Off Leads

Exclude anyone who has not engaged with you in 6+ months. Do not pitch them a premium offer cold. Instead, warm them up with value content over the next quarter and include them in the next promotion.

### Waitlist Mechanics

When people try to buy but you are sold out, put them on a waitlist. If a spot opens up (payment failure, cancellation), charge the waitlist person immediately. The waitlist is a profit center, not just a list.

### Mid-Promotion Adjustments

If you have not sold out by Day 3-4, add to the offer before sending the remaining emails. Do not discount. Add value instead. Stack another bonus, extend a timeline, include something unexpected.

### The Back-Out Spot

The "back-out spot available" email is one of the highest converters in the entire system. People who wanted to buy but hesitated will jump on a "second chance" framing. Use it.

## Coaching Application

### When to Deploy This Framework

- Client has an existing customer base but flat or declining revenue
- Client needs a quick cash injection (cash flow crunch, wants to fund a new project)
- Client has never run a premium promotion before (low-hanging fruit)
- Client's revenue is 100% from standard offers with no premium tier
- Client has a warm list they are under-monetizing

### Discovery Questions

**"How many past and current customers do you have contact info for?"**

This establishes the size of the opportunity. Even 100 customers with contact info is enough to run a Fast Cash Play. Most businesses are sitting on hundreds or thousands of past buyers they never re-engage.

**"What's your average transaction value?"**

This sets the pricing anchor. Whatever their average is, multiply by 10-50x. Watch their face when you say the number. The discomfort is a signal you are in the right range.

**"Have you ever offered something at 10x your normal price?"**

Almost always the answer is no. This opens the conversation about why they are leaving money on the table. Their best customers want to pay more. They just have not been given the option.

**"What unscalable, high-touch value could you offer a small group?"**

This gets them thinking about the premium value levers. What could they do for 10 people that they could never do for 100? That is the offer.

### Coaching Progression

1. Start with the 10 premium value levers. Have the client pick 3-5 that fit their business.
2. Build the offer structure (1-3 core components + 3 bonuses).
3. Set the price using the 10-50x rule.
4. Choose the sell method (consult vs automated).
5. Write the sequence together.
6. Run the first promotion.
7. Collect the 4Rs.
8. Debrief and optimize for next quarter.
