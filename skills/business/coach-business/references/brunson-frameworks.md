## Expert Development Path

Five stages from "I have an idea" to "people pay me for my expertise." Use this to diagnose where a coaching client sits and prescribe the exact next stage.

### The 5 Stages

| Stage | Name | Signal Phrases | What They Have | What They Lack |
|-------|------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Dreamer** | "I want to start something," "I have this idea" | Desire, vision | Action, knowledge, proof |
| 2 | **Reporter** | "I've been researching," "I interviewed this expert" | Curiosity, consumption habits | Original frameworks, results |
| 3 | **Framework Builder** | "I noticed a pattern," "Here's my system for..." | Organized knowledge, teachable systems | Real-world proof, paying clients |
| 4 | **Free Service Provider** | "I've helped a few people," "I did this for a friend" | Proof of concept, early results | Revenue, positioning, scalability |
| 5 | **Expert** | "Clients pay me," "Here's my methodology" | Paying clients, proven frameworks, authority | (Next challenge: scaling, movement building) |

### Stage-Specific Coaching Prescriptions

**Stage 1 (Dreamer) --> Stage 2 (Reporter):**
- Assign 30-day research sprint: consume the top 10 books/courses/podcasts in their niche
- Interview 5 people who have the problem they want to solve
- Document everything in a research journal
- Key question: "What are you willing to spend 1,000 hours learning about?"
- Red flag: If they can't pick a topic after 2 weeks, route to Mode B (identity work)

**Stage 2 (Reporter) --> Stage 3 (Framework Builder):**
- Take all research and find the patterns
- Create their first framework: name it, draw it, explain it in 3 steps or fewer
- Write a "manifesto" post or article using their new framework
- Key question: "What's the one pattern you keep seeing that nobody else is talking about?"
- Red flag: Endless consumption without synthesis = hiding from creation. Call it out.

**Stage 3 (Framework Builder) --> Stage 4 (Free Service Provider):**
- Deliver results for 3-5 people using their framework, for free
- Document every result obsessively (screenshots, metrics, testimonials)
- Refine the framework based on real-world feedback
- Key question: "Who can you help THIS WEEK using what you've built?"
- Red flag: Perfecting the framework without testing it = fear of judgment. Route to Mode B, Step B5 (Identity Protection).

**Stage 4 (Free Service Provider) --> Stage 5 (Expert):**
- Package results into a case study portfolio (minimum 3 documented wins)
- Set a price. Any price. The first dollar matters more than the amount.
- Create an offer using the Value Equation (see `value-and-offers.md`)
- Key question: "You have proof it works. What's stopping you from charging?"
- Red flag: "I need more case studies" after 5+ free clients = fear of rejection. Name it.

### Diagnostic Questions

Use these to place the client on the path:

1. "Have you helped anyone get results with your knowledge?" (Stages 4-5 if yes, 1-3 if no)
2. "Do you have a named framework or system?" (Stage 3+ if yes, 1-2 if no)
3. "Are people paying you?" (Stage 5 if yes)
4. "Have you done serious research in your niche?" (Stage 2+ if yes, Stage 1 if no)

**Integration with Kennedy Authority Roadmap:** The Expert Development Path feeds directly into Kennedy's Authority Ladder (see `kennedy-coaching-frameworks.md`, Framework 12). Brunson gets them to Stage 5 (Expert). Kennedy's ladder takes them from Expert to Published Authority to Celebrity Expert.

## Movement Building

A business sells products. A movement creates followers who recruit other followers. Three elements must be present. Without all three, you have a company but not a movement.

### The 3 Core Elements

**1. Charismatic Leader (The Attractive Character)**

Not "charisma" in the Hollywood sense. This means: a person people want to follow because they are transparent, opinionated, and going somewhere.

Four Attractive Character types:
- **The Leader:** "Follow me, I've done this. I'll show you the way."
- **The Adventurer:** "I'm figuring this out in real-time. Come along for the ride."
- **The Reporter:** "I interview the best, synthesize, and share what works."
- **The Reluctant Hero:** "I didn't want to teach this, but people kept asking."

Coaching questions:
- "Which of these four types feels most natural to you?" (Don't let them pick the one that sounds coolest. Pick the one that's true.)
- "What personal story led you to this work?" (No story = no connection. Help them find it.)
- "What do you believe that most people in your industry disagree with?" (Polarization creates followers, not fence-sitting.)

**2. New Opportunity (Not Improvement)**

See full framework in the [New Opportunity vs. Improvement](#new-opportunity-vs-improvement) section below. The movement must be built around a NEW vehicle, not a better version of an old one.

**3. Future-Based Cause**

The tribe needs a shared destination they're moving toward together. Not "buy my product" but "we're building something bigger than any of us."

Diagnostic questions:
- "Where are you taking your audience?" (If the answer is just "to buy my course," there's no cause.)
- "What's the world you want to create for your people?"
- "Can your audience describe what you stand for in one sentence?"

### Movement Tactics (Deploy After the 3 Elements Are in Place)

| Tactic | What It Does | Implementation |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Tribal Name** | Creates "us vs. them" identity | Name your group. Not "my audience." A name they call themselves. |
| **"I Am" Statement** | Gives members an identity declaration | "I am a [tribal name]. I believe [core belief]." |
| **Manifesto / Title of Liberty** | Captures what the movement stands for | One-page document: what you believe, what you reject, where you're going. |
| **Milestone Awards** | Recognizes progress within the tribe | "You hit [milestone]? You're now a [level name]." Public recognition. |
| **Social Mission** | Ties the business to something bigger | For every X sold, we do Y. Or: our mission is to [impact statement]. |

**Coaching application:** Walk the client through each tactic as a 15-minute exercise. Most clients have zero of these. Even one or two creates stickier engagement than any discount or bonus.

**Red flag:** If the client wants to skip straight to tactics without establishing the three core elements, stop them. Tactics without a real leader, a new opportunity, and a cause produce hollow marketing that doesn't stick.

## Big Domino Coaching Tool

Every offer faces dozens of objections. The Big Domino is the ONE belief that, if true, makes all other objections irrelevant.

### The Big Domino Template

```
If I can get them to believe that [MY NEW OPPORTUNITY]
is the key to [THEIR DESIRED RESULT],
and show them [MY UNIQUE FRAMEWORK/MECHANISM],
then all other objections become irrelevant.
```

### The Big Domino Process (Coaching Exercise)

**Step 1: List every objection.**

Have the client brain-dump every reason someone says no or hesitates:
- "It's too expensive"
- "I don't have time"
- "I've tried something like this before"
- "I'm not technical enough"
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"
- "I need to think about it"
- (Get at least 10-15)

**Step 2: Find the master belief.**

Ask: "If your prospect believed ONE thing with absolute certainty, which of these objections would disappear?"

Usually the master belief is about the VEHICLE, not the person:
- "This approach actually works" (vehicle belief)
- "This approach works for someone like ME" (internal belief)
- "I can execute this specific framework" (internal belief about the mechanism)

**Step 3: Craft the Big Domino statement.**

Fill in the template. Test it: does believing this statement truly dissolve the other objections?

**Step 4: Build all content and sales around this one belief.**

Every email, every social post, every sales page should be engineering this single belief. Not 15 different arguments. One domino that topples the rest.

### Coaching Application

| Client Situation | Big Domino Approach |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Low conversion on good product | Their sales message is fighting 15 objections. Find the one belief. |
| Client can't articulate why they're different | The Big Domino IS the differentiation. It's the belief only they can deliver on. |
| Content feels scattered, no clear message | Every piece of content should point to the Big Domino belief. |
| Webinar/presentation that doesn't convert | The presentation structure should be: establish the Big Domino belief, then present the offer. |

**Integration with Kennedy Risk Reversal:** Once the Big Domino belief is established, Kennedy's Risk Reversal (see `kennedy-coaching-frameworks.md`, Framework 8) removes the remaining friction. Big Domino handles "do I believe this works?" Risk Reversal handles "what if I'm wrong?"

## New Opportunity vs. Improvement

This is the single most important positioning decision a business makes. Most businesses sell improvement. The market is 50x larger for new opportunities.

### The Core Distinction

| | Improvement Offer | New Opportunity |
|--|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Frame** | "Do what you're doing, but better" | "Stop doing that. Do THIS instead." |
| **Market size** | ~2% of addressable market | ~98% of addressable market |
| **Emotional response** | Guilt, obligation, "I should" | Excitement, hope, "I want to" |
| **Competition** | Competing with every other improvement | Category of one (if positioned right) |
| **Buyer psychology** | Reminds them of past failures | Clean slate, fresh start |
| **Examples** | "Better diet," "improve your ads," "optimize your funnel" | "Carnivore lifestyle," "build-in-public as marketing," "skill-based business model" |

### Why Improvements Fail (Coaching Explanation)

People have tried to improve before. They failed. When you sell improvement, you're not just fighting competitors. You're fighting every failed attempt they've already made. The prospect's internal monologue: "I've tried improving my diet 10 times. Why would the 11th work?"

A new opportunity sidesteps this entirely. It's not the 11th diet. It's a completely different approach they've never tried.

### Client Diagnostic: Is It Improvement or New Opportunity?

Ask the client:
1. "When you describe your offer, do people say 'oh, like [competitor]'?" --> Improvement (bad)
2. "Does your prospect need to admit they were wrong about something to buy?" --> Improvement (very bad)
3. "Is there a moment where you say 'forget everything you've been told about X'?" --> New Opportunity (good)
4. "Can people immediately compare your price to a competitor?" --> Improvement (commodity)

### The 4-Step Reframe Process

When a client is selling improvement, walk them through this:

**Step 1: Define the desired result.**
"What does your customer actually want? Not your product. The end state."

**Step 2: Identify all the failed vehicles.**
"What has your customer already tried to get this result? List every approach they've attempted."

**Step 3: Opportunity Switch.**
"What is the fundamentally different vehicle you offer? Not a better version of what they tried. A different path entirely."

Test: Can you say "It's not about [old vehicle]. It's about [new vehicle]" and have it feel like a revelation?

**Step 4: Opportunity Stack.**
Once the new opportunity is clear, stack the delivery modes:
- **DIY:** Template, course, playbook ($27-197)
- **DIWY (Done-It-With-You):** Coaching, cohort, mastermind ($997-5K)
- **DFY (Done-For-You):** Full service, implementation ($5K-25K+)

Same new opportunity, three fulfillment levels. See `value-and-offers.md` (3 Levels of Customers) for pricing psychology per level.

### Coaching Examples

| Client's Current Offer (Improvement) | Reframed as New Opportunity |
|--------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| "I help you write better emails" | "I built an AI system that writes, tests, and optimizes emails while you sleep" |
| "I'll improve your ad performance" | "Stop running ads. Build a media machine that makes ads unnecessary" |
| "Better productivity coaching" | "The Direction Deficit Protocol: it's not about doing more, it's about doing ONE thing" |
| "Improve your newsletter open rates" | "The Bot-Per-Email Model: every email IS the product" |

## Value Ladder for Client Businesses

Help clients design a complete value ladder where each rung naturally leads to the next. This extends Kennedy's Ascension Ladder (see `kennedy-coaching-frameworks.md`) with fulfillment mode mapping and consumption format progression.

> NOTE: For offer pricing, guarantees, and naming, use `value-and-offers.md`. For money model stacking and 30-day payback math, use `money-models.md`. This section focuses on the ARCHITECTURE of the ladder, not the economics.

### The Full Ladder Template

```
BAIT (Free)
  Purpose: Acquire leads, demonstrate value
  Format: Free tool, calculator, quiz, PDF, mini-course
  Fulfillment: Self-serve digital
  Transition trigger: "Want the full version?"

FRONT END ($7-97)
  Purpose: Turn leads into buyers (the most important conversion)
  Format: Template pack, playbook, challenge, workshop recording
  Fulfillment: DIY (digital download, self-paced)
  Transition trigger: "Want help implementing this?"

MIDDLE ($197-997)
  Purpose: Deliver deeper transformation with some guidance
  Format: Full course, live workshop, group Q&A
  Fulfillment: DIWY (done-it-with-you), light touch
  Transition trigger: "Want us to do the heavy lifting?"

BACK END ($2K-10K)
  Purpose: High-touch, high-result, high-margin
  Format: Cohort program, mastermind, intensive
  Fulfillment: DIWY (done-it-with-you), heavy touch
  Transition trigger: "Want it completely done for you?"

PEAK ($5K-25K+)
  Purpose: Maximum transformation, maximum price
  Format: 1:1 consulting, DFY implementation, VIP
  Fulfillment: DFY (done-for-you)
  Transition trigger: N/A (or referral program)

CONTINUITY ($27-297/mo)
  Purpose: Recurring revenue, ongoing relationship
  Format: Membership, community, monthly deliverables
  Fulfillment: Mix of DIY content + DIWY access
  Runs alongside: Any rung (not sequential)
```

### Consumption Format Progression

Each rung should increase intimacy of delivery:

```
Bait: Written (PDF, email)
Front End: Written + Video (course, playbook with walkthroughs)
Middle: Video + Live (recorded course + live Q&A)
Back End: Live + Interactive (live sessions, hot seats, group work)
Peak: Interactive + 1:1 (private calls, custom implementation)
```

This matters because higher prices require higher-touch delivery. A $47 PDF is fine. A $5K PDF is not.

### The Ladder Audit (Coaching Exercise)

Draw the ladder with the client and fill in what exists:

1. **Map current offers** to rungs. Where are the gaps?
2. **Check transitions.** Does each rung naturally lead to the next? Or is it a random jump?
3. **Check fulfillment modes.** Is DIY offered at every level? (Common mistake: only having DFY.)
4. **Check the bait.** Is the free offer compelling enough to start the relationship? (See `lead-generation.md` for lead magnet framework.)
5. **Check continuity.** Is there recurring revenue running alongside the ladder?

**Common ladder problems:**

| Problem | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Only one offer | No ladder exists | Build the rung above AND below their current offer |
| Big price gap between rungs | Prospect can't justify the jump | Add a middle rung to bridge the gap |
| No free offer | Relying on cold traffic to buy immediately | Build a bait offer using lead magnet framework |
| No continuity | Revenue resets to zero each month | Add membership or community alongside any rung |
| All DIY | Leaving money on the table | Add DIWY and DFY versions of existing offers |
| All DFY | Can't scale (time-constrained) | Add DIY and DIWY versions to serve more people |

## Identity Shift Framework

A new opportunity only works if the customer adopts a new identity along with it. Without identity shift, the offer feels like a transaction. With it, the offer feels like a transformation.

### Why Identity Matters

When someone buys an improvement, their identity stays the same. "I'm still a person who struggles with email marketing, just with a slightly better tool."

When someone buys a new opportunity with an identity shift, they become someone new. "I'm not an email marketer. I'm an AI automation builder." That identity shift is what creates loyalty, community, and lifetime value.

### Identity Shift Components

| Component | What It Does | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Tribal Name** | Gives members a label they're proud of | "Builders," "Funnel Hackers," "Automation Operators" |
| **"I Am" Statement** | Personal declaration of new identity | "I build useful systems that create measurable outcomes." |
| **Manifesto** | Written declaration of beliefs and values | One-page document: what we believe, what we reject, where we're going |
| **Milestone Awards** | Marks progress and creates status within the tribe | "First Automation Built," "10K Subscriber Club," "$1K Product Launch" |
| **Social Mission** | Connects the business to impact beyond profit | "We're democratizing AI business tools so anyone can build a profitable business" |

### The Identity Shift Diagnostic

Use when a client has a good product but customers don't stick, don't refer, or don't ascend the value ladder.

Questions:
1. "Do your customers call themselves anything?" (If no: no tribal identity.)
2. "After buying, do customers feel different about themselves, or just about their problem?" (Transaction vs. transformation.)
3. "Would a customer proudly tell a friend they're part of your world?" (If no: no identity worth sharing.)
4. "Do customers see your product as a tool they USE, or a community they BELONG TO?" (Usage vs. identity.)

### Building Identity Into Offers (Coaching Exercise)

Walk the client through each component (15 min per component):

**1. Tribal Name** (5 min)
- Brainstorm 10 names. Pick one that: sounds cool, people would put on a t-shirt, creates an "us."
- Test: "I'm a ____." Does it feel like a badge or a label?

**2. "I Am" Statement** (5 min)
- Template: "I am a [tribal name]. I believe [core belief]. I [action that defines the tribe]."
- Must be something members WANT to say, not something they're told to say.

**3. Manifesto** (15 min)
- Structure: What we believe (3-5 bullets) + What we reject (3-5 bullets) + Where we're going (1 vision statement)
- Must be polarizing. If everyone agrees with your manifesto, it's not a manifesto. It's a brochure.

**4. Milestone Awards** (10 min)
- Map 4-6 milestones that mark real progress in the client's methodology
- Each milestone gets a name and public recognition
- Milestones should align with the value ladder: free tier milestone, front-end milestone, back-end milestone

**5. Social Mission** (10 min)
- Must be authentic. Forced social missions are worse than none.
- Template: "We exist to [impact] so that [audience] can [transformation]."
- Bonus: tie a tangible action to it ("For every member who hits $10K/mo, we sponsor one AI scholarship")

### Integration with Movement Building

Identity Shift is the ENGINE of Movement Building. The three movement elements (Charismatic Leader, New Opportunity, Future-Based Cause) create the environment. Identity Shift is what happens INSIDE the customer when all three are working.

```
Movement Building = external structure
Identity Shift = internal transformation
Both required. Neither sufficient alone.
```
