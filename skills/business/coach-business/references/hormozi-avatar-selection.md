## 1. Vista Equity Customer Scoring Method

**Source:** $100M Offers Lost Chapter ("Your First Avatar")

A 4-step methodology for choosing your ideal customer avatar. Named after Vista Equity Partners' approach to evaluating businesses -- applied to customer selection.

**The Process:**

**Step 1: List your options**
Write down every type of person or business you could serve. Don't filter yet. Cast wide.

**Step 2: Score each on four criteria (1-10 scale):**
- **Pain:** How badly do they need what you sell? (10 = desperate, 1 = nice to have)
- **Purchasing Power:** Can they afford it? (10 = money is no object, 1 = broke)
- **Easy to Target:** Can you find and reach them? (10 = concentrated + accessible, 1 = scattered)
- **Growing Market:** Is this group expanding? (10 = explosive growth, 1 = shrinking)

**Step 3: Multiply scores**
Pain x Purchasing Power x Easy to Target x Growing Market = Total Score

IMPORTANT: This is multiplicative, not additive. A zero in any category = zero total. All four criteria must be at least moderate.

**Step 4: Pick the highest score**
That's your first avatar.

Then reverse-engineer their buyer journey:
- Where do they hang out?
- What do they search?
- Who do they already follow?
- What events do they attend?

### Coaching Application

This is the #1 framework for clients who say "I don't know who to sell to" or "I serve everyone." Most clients skip this step entirely and wonder why their marketing doesn't work.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "Who are you currently selling to? List every type."
- "Let's score each one: how much pain do they have, can they pay, can you find them, is the market growing?"
- "Which avatar scored highest? Is that who you're actually targeting?"
- "If your current avatar has low purchasing power, why are you still targeting them?"

**Common mistakes:**
- Picking the avatar they WANT to work with instead of the one that scores highest
- Ignoring purchasing power (passion projects with broke customers)
- Ignoring targetability (great customer but impossible to find at scale)
- Adding scores instead of multiplying (hides the zero problem)

## 2. BANT Framework

**Source:** $100M Lost Chapters (referenced)

Four qualifying questions every lead must pass before you invest sales time:

- **B - Budget:** Can they afford it?
- **A - Authority:** Are they the decision maker?
- **N - Need:** Do they have the problem you solve?
- **T - Timing:** Are they ready to act now?

All four must be "yes" for a qualified lead.

### Coaching Application

When a client says "I'm getting lots of leads but nobody's buying," run BANT:
- "Are you qualifying for budget before the sales call?"
- "Are you talking to the decision maker or a gatekeeper?"
- "Do they actually have the problem you solve, or are they just curious?"
- "Is the timing right -- are they in pain NOW or just researching for someday?"

If any BANT element is consistently failing, the problem is upstream (marketing/targeting), not downstream (sales).

## 3. Five Lead Stages

**Source:** $100M Lost Chapters

Sequential customer journey framework showing where each lead sits and what action moves them forward:

```
Uncontacted -> Contacted -> Engaged -> Qualified -> Sold
```

**Stage 1: Uncontacted**
They don't know you exist yet.
Action: Advertise (Core Four: warm outreach, cold outreach, content, paid ads)

**Stage 2: Contacted**
They've seen your message.
Action: Incentivize engagement with lead magnet or offer

**Stage 3: Engaged**
They've shown interest (clicked, replied, opted in, attended).
Action: Qualify them (problem fit + money situation)

**Stage 4: Qualified**
They have the problem AND the money.
Action: Sell (make an offer)

**Stage 5: Sold**
They gave you money.
Action: Fulfill and begin retention/ascension journey

### Coaching Application

Most clients don't know where their pipeline breaks. Map their funnel to these 5 stages and find the drop-off.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "How many people per week move through each stage?"
- "Where's the biggest drop-off?"
- "Are you trying to sell people who are only at Stage 2 (contacted) without qualifying them first?"
- "What's your qualification process? How do you know they have the problem AND the money?"

**Common problems by stage:**
- Stage 1 -> 2 drop: Marketing/messaging problem
- Stage 2 -> 3 drop: Lead magnet or offer not compelling enough
- Stage 3 -> 4 drop: Qualification process missing or weak
- Stage 4 -> 5 drop: Sales/closing problem (see `hormozi-closing-deep.md`)

## 4. Internal Core Four (Employee Acquisition = Customer Acquisition)

**Source:** $100M Lost Chapters

Getting employees uses the exact same methods as getting customers. The Core Four maps directly:

| Customer Acquisition | Employee Acquisition |
|---|---|
| Warm Outreach | Asking Your Network |
| Cold Outreach | Recruiting |
| Post Content | Posting Job Openings |
| Paid Ads | Promoting Job Postings |
| Customer Referrals | Employee Referrals |
| Affiliates | Associations, Guilds, Listservs |
| Agencies | Staffing Firms |

**Key insight:** "You have two types of customers: The customers who pay you and the customers you pay -- your employees."

### Coaching Application

When a client says "I can't find good people," apply the same Core Four diagnosis you'd use for customer acquisition:
- "Which of the four channels are you using to find employees?"
- "What's your volume? How many people are you reaching out to per week?"
- "What's your 'offer' to employees? Why would an A-player choose you?"
- "Are you running 'paid ads' for hiring? Employee referral bonuses?"

If they're only posting on one job board and hoping, that's the equivalent of running one ad campaign and wondering why they have no customers.
