## 1. Onion of Blame Framework

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook

People avoid buying decisions by casting blame outward in layers, like peeling an onion. The closer you get to the core (self), the closer you are to a genuine decision.

**Three Layers:**

**Layer 1 - External Circumstances:** "I can't because of X outside my control"
- Time: "I'm too busy right now"
- Money: "I can't afford it"
- Fit: "It's not quite right for my situation"

**Layer 2 - Other People:** "I can't because of someone else"
- Spouse/partner: "I need to talk to my wife/husband"
- Past vendors: "I've been burned before"
- Business partner: "My co-founder won't go for it"

**Layer 3 - Self:** "I can't because of me"
- Preferences: "It's not for me"
- Fear: "What if it doesn't work?"
- Avoidance: "I need to think about it"
- Rushed: "I don't want to make a hasty decision"

**Core insight:** When someone swaps objections, you're closing correctly. Keep peeling layers until they have enough power to make a genuine decision. If you handle "money" and they switch to "spouse," that's progress, not failure.

**Coaching Application:**
Use this to diagnose WHY a client's sales team can't close. Most sales teams give up at Layer 1. Train them to expect objection-swapping as a positive signal. When coaching a client who says "my customers keep saying it's too expensive," ask: "When you handle the price objection, what do they say NEXT?" If they don't know, they're not peeling the onion.

**Diagnostic questions for clients:**
- "When a prospect says no, what's the reason they give?"
- "When you handle that reason, what do they say next?"
- "How many layers deep do your salespeople typically go?"
- "Are your salespeople treating objection-swapping as failure or progress?"

## 2. Decision-Making Power Model

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook

Closing is NOT about getting prospects to buy. It's about giving them enough decision-making power to overcome avoidance. People on the fence lack power to decide. The goal is a genuine decision (yes OR no), not a forced yes.

**Three steps:**
1. Understand what they want (from earlier in the sales conversation)
2. Put what they want next to the options they have
3. Make sure they come to a genuine decision

**Key insight:** "The goal of closing isn't to get the prospect to buy. Closing gives the prospect enough power to overcome decision avoidance."

**Coaching Application:**
Reframes closing for clients who feel "salesy." Most coaching clients hate selling because they think it's manipulation. This model reframes it as SERVICE. You're helping someone make a decision they already want to make but can't pull the trigger on.

**Questions for clients struggling with sales identity:**
- "Do you believe your product genuinely helps people?"
- "If someone needs your help but can't decide, is it more ethical to help them decide or let them stay stuck?"
- "What's the cost to your prospect of NOT making a decision?"

## 3. Belief Continuum Model

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist Playbook

Belief is not binary (believe/don't believe). It's a continuum from 100% certainty to 100% doubt. Prospects start closer to doubt. Every piece of proof moves them toward certainty.

**The Continuum:**
```
100% Doubt ←──────────────────→ 100% Certainty
```

**Key insight:** It's not DO they believe, it's HOW MUCH do they believe. Every testimonial, case study, demonstration, and piece of proof nudges the needle. The closer to absolute certainty, the more compelling the proof needs to be.

**Application to coaching:**
- Help clients understand that no single piece of proof closes the sale. It's cumulative.
- A client with weak proof needs more touchpoints before the ask.
- Map where prospects typically are on the belief continuum at each stage of the funnel.
- "Instead of trying to prove your claim, claim your proof." -- Don't make promises and then scramble for evidence. Collect your data, THEN make claims the data already supports.

**Coaching diagnostic:**
- "How much proof do you currently have? (testimonials, case studies, data)"
- "At what point in your funnel do prospects encounter their first proof?"
- "What claims are you making that you can't back up with data?"
- "What results do you HAVE that you're not claiming?"

## 4. The 28 Rules of Closing (Coach-Adapted Summary)

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook

These aren't scripts. They're a philosophy for how closing should be conducted. Use them to diagnose what's wrong with a client's sales process.

**The Rules (grouped by coaching diagnostic category):**

### Listening & Rapport Rules
1. When in doubt, repeat back what they said (buys time + shows listening)
2. Acknowledge or agree, never disagree ("totally understand" before any close)
3. Don't assume objections mean they won't buy (sometimes they're processing out loud)
12. People want to believe you. Help their brains justify the decision they already want to make.

### Tactical Rules
4. Stack closes: if first doesn't land, hit from another angle
5. You only need 2-3 closes per obstacle
6. Once they say yes, shut up
7. Never change your price on the spot
8. Only ask for the sale when you think you've got them
9. Before getting real, get permission ("Can I be a coach instead of a friend for a second?")

### Mindset Rules
10. Yellows are golds: practice on unqualified leads ("be a trashman")
11. Volume negates luck
13. Selling happens before the ask. Closing happens after.
14. The person who cares the most about the prospect wins the sale.
15. Selling is a transference of belief over a bridge of trust.
16. Belief and trust are a continuum, not binaries.
19. Record all your sales to learn from them.

### Tone & Delivery Rules
17. If you're going to say something offensive, say it about someone like them or your past self (not them directly)
5b. You don't need to memorize, you need to understand (use your own words)

**Coaching Application:**
Use these rules as a diagnostic checklist. When a client says "my close rate is low," go through the rules and identify which ones they're breaking.

**Top diagnostic questions based on the rules:**
- "Are your salespeople recording their calls?" (Rule 19)
- "What happens when someone objects? Do they acknowledge first or jump to rebuttal?" (Rule 2)
- "After the prospect says yes, what happens next? Do they keep talking?" (Rule 6/18 -- shut up after yes)
- "How much volume are they doing? Are they practicing on unqualified leads?" (Rules 7/11)
- "Do they believe in the product?" (Rule 15 -- conviction is the multiplier)
- "Are they selling before they ask, or just asking?" (Rule 13)

**Key quote:** "Selling is a transference of belief over a bridge of trust."
