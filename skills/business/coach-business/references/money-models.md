## What Is a Money Model?

A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers -- what you offer, when you offer, and how you offer it -- designed so that **one customer generates enough profit within 30 days to pay for their own acquisition plus one more customer.**

When that happens, cash stops being the growth bottleneck. You can scale without needing outside capital, without waiting for revenue to trickle in, and without hoping your margins hold up.

**The north star metric:** 30-day payback. If a customer costs $50 to acquire, they need to generate $100+ in profit within 30 days (their own cost + the next customer's cost).

**Why this matters:** Most businesses only have ONE offer. They acquire a customer, sell them one thing, and hope for the best. A Money Model stacks 4 offer types in a deliberate sequence so every customer interaction generates maximum cash as fast as possible.

**The four sales channels** (all money models apply across these):
- Salesman in-person (gym, retail, events)
- Salesman online (Zoom, phone, DMs)
- Self-checkout in-person (counter sales, kiosks)
- Self-checkout online (e-commerce, SaaS, digital products)

## The Three Stages

### Stage I: Get Cash
**Attraction Offers** -- acquire customers cheaply or at breakeven. The goal is to get a first purchase, not to profit. Revenue comes from what follows.

### Stage II: Get More Cash
**Upsell + Downsell Offers** -- extract more revenue immediately after the first purchase. Upsells increase spend. Downsells capture people who said no.

### Stage III: Get The Most Cash
**Continuity Offers** -- subscriptions, memberships, recurring revenue. This is where lifetime value lives. This is the offer type that makes a business sellable.

## 15 Named Plays

### Attraction Offers (5 Plays)

| Play | Mechanics | Best For | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Win Your Money Back** | Customer pays upfront for a challenge/program. Hits a goal = gets money back as credit toward next purchase. You keep the cash either way (as revenue or as credit toward upsell). | Fitness, coaching, programs with measurable outcomes | Gym: $500 challenge, hit goal = $500 credit toward membership. Result: +$200 cash flow day one. |
| **Giveaways** | Give away something genuinely valuable (not a PDF). The giveaway IS the product -- it just has a $0 price tag. Capture contact info. | List building, product launches, audience growth | Free AI tool, free audit, free mini-course with email gate |
| **Dummy Offers** | Intentionally unprofitable offer designed purely to acquire a customer into your ecosystem. You LOSE money on this offer. Profit comes from upsells/backend. | High-LTV businesses where backend makes up the loss | $1 trial, below-cost intro, loss-leader product |
| **Buy X Get Y Free** | Purchase one product, get a complementary product free. The "free" product introduces them to your next offer category. | Cross-selling between product lines, expanding product awareness | Buy the course, get the template pack free (templates lead to DFY service) |
| **Pay Less Now / More Later** | Low entry price that graduates to full price. Removes the initial price objection entirely. | Subscriptions, memberships, recurring services | "$1 first month, $49/mo after" or "$29/mo for 3 months, then $97/mo" |

**Key principle:** Attraction offers should be valuable enough to sell standalone. Make it SO good they can't believe it's free/cheap. The offer itself is the marketing.

### Upsell Offers (4 Plays)

| Play | Mechanics | Best For | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Classic Upsell** | Reveal the next problem. After they buy the solution to Problem A, show them Problem B (which naturally follows). Price: 1.5-3x the initial purchase. | Sequential problems, natural "what's next" progressions | "You got the templates. Want the video walkthroughs showing exactly how to use them?" |
| **Menu Upsell** | Present multiple add-on options at checkout like a restaurant menu. Consultant-style: "Based on what you picked, you'd also benefit from X and Y." Let them choose. | Products with natural add-ons, service businesses, checkout flows | Prestige Labs: Unsell (what they don't need) > Prescribe (what they do) > Offer A or B > Make Easy. Result: $270K to $1.6M/mo. Same products. |
| **Anchor Upsell** | Show the most expensive option first. The expensive option makes the mid-tier feel cheap by comparison. The anchor isn't meant to sell -- it's meant to reframe the price of what you actually want them to buy. | Tiered products, premium positioning, pricing pages | "Enterprise: $2,000/mo" shown first. "Pro: $197/mo" suddenly feels reasonable. |
| **Rollover Upsell** | Offer a future deal at the point of current purchase. Lock them into the next transaction before they leave. | Subscriptions, events, consumables, appointment-based businesses | "Lock in your annual rate now before it goes up." "Book your next session today at 20% off." |

### Downsell Offers (3 Plays)

| Play | Mechanics | Best For | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Payment Plan** | Same product, different payment structure. Never discount the core price. Spread the cost over time. | High-ticket products where price is the objection | "$97 one-time" becomes "3 payments of $37" (total $111 -- you actually make MORE) |
| **Feature-Limited** | Fewer features, lower price. Remove the parts they don't need rather than discounting the whole thing. | SaaS, course bundles, service packages | "Pro" vs "Basic" tier. Same core, fewer extras. |
| **Free Trial** | Full access for limited time. They experience the value, then decide to pay. | Subscription products, communities, software | "14 days free, then $47/mo." Conversion rates: 15-30% for good products. |

**Key rule:** Never discount the CORE price. Change payment terms, features, or access duration instead. Discounting trains customers to wait for sales.

### Continuity Offers (3 Plays)

| Play | Mechanics | Best For | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Bonus Continuity** | Subscriber gets an exclusive bonus that goes away if they cancel. The bonus is the lock-in. Make the bonus painful to lose. | Communities, memberships, subscriptions | Gym Launch: Added exclusive perk only for recurring members. Monthly revenue: $16K to $380K-$1.76M. Became PE-sellable. |
| **Discount Continuity** | Subscriber saves money by committing to a longer term. Annual vs monthly, multi-year vs annual. The savings increase with commitment length. | SaaS, media subscriptions, membership sites | "Monthly: $49/mo. Annual: $39/mo (save $120/yr)." "Founding rate: $29/mo locked forever." |
| **Waive Fee Continuity** | Subscriber avoids a penalty or fee by staying subscribed. Canceling costs them something beyond just losing access. | Gyms, premium services, platforms with setup costs | "Setup fee: $200 (waived for active members)." Cancel and rejoin = pay setup again. |

## Case Studies

### Gym: "Win Your Money Back" (Attraction Offer)
- **Before:** Traditional model. $300 customer acquisition cost. 3-month breakeven.
- **After:** Customers pay $500 upfront for a fitness challenge. Hit their goal = $500 store credit toward membership. Miss it = gym keeps $500 as revenue.
- **Result:** $200 positive cash flow on day one per customer. Scaled from 1 to 6 locations in 3 years.
- **Why it works:** Customer is psychologically committed ($500 on the line). Those who succeed become the best long-term members. Those who don't still generated revenue.

### Prestige Labs: Menu Upsell
- **Before:** Selling supplements online. $270K/mo revenue.
- **After:** Implemented 4-part menu framework at checkout: Unsell (clarify what they DON'T need), Prescribe (recommend based on their goals), Offer A or B (choice framing), Make Easy (simplified checkout).
- **Result:** $270K to $1.6M/mo in 8 weeks. Same products. Same traffic. Different offer architecture.
- **Why it works:** Customers felt guided, not sold. The "unsell" step built trust. Choice framing removed decision paralysis.

### Gym Launch: Bonus Continuity
- **Before:** Selling one-time $16K packages to gym owners.
- **After:** Added exclusive ongoing perk only available to recurring members. One-time buyers couldn't access it.
- **Result:** Monthly revenue scaled from $16K to $380K-$1.76M/mo. Business became PE-sellable because of predictable recurring revenue.
- **Why it works:** The bonus was genuinely valuable and exclusive. Canceling meant losing access to something they used daily.

### Skool: Games as Attraction Offer
- **Before:** Community platform with 1M users.
- **After:** Added "Skool Games" -- a competition/rewards system that functioned as an acquisition mechanism. Users invited others to earn points and status.
- **Result:** 1M to 15M+ users in 18 months.
- **Why it works:** The attraction offer was baked into the product itself. Users acquired other users at zero cost.

## Revenue Optimization

**Layer all four offer types across the customer lifecycle:**

```
Stranger --> Attraction Offer --> Customer --> Upsell --> Higher-value customer
                                       |
                                   Downsell --> Lower-value customer (still revenue)
                                       |
                                 Continuity --> Recurring revenue (lifetime value)
```

**Revenue per customer optimization:**
1. Increase average order value (upsells, bundles)
2. Increase purchase frequency (downsells, consumables)
3. Increase customer lifetime (continuity, retention)
4. Decrease cost to serve (automation, productization)

**The compound effect:**
- 10% more customers x 10% higher AOV x 10% longer retention = 33% revenue increase
- Small improvements stack multiplicatively

**The 30-day payback math:**
```
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): $[X]
Day 1 revenue (attraction offer + upsells): $[Y]
Day 1-30 revenue (continuity + downsells): $[Z]
30-day total: $[Y + Z]
Payback ratio: $[Y + Z] / $[X]

Target: Payback ratio >= 2.0 (covers this customer + funds next one)
```

## Recurring Revenue

**Optimal churn targets:**
| Level | Monthly Churn | Annual Retention |
|-------|--------------|------------------|
| Critical | >5% | <54% (business is dying) |
| Okay | 3-5% | 54-82% |
| Good | 1-3% | 70-89% |
| Elite | <1% | >89% |

**Optimal recurring price points:** $600-$1,200/month = lowest churn rates
- Too cheap (<$50/mo): Low commitment, easy to cancel
- Sweet spot ($100-$300/mo): Enough to feel invested, not enough to constantly re-evaluate
- Premium ($600-$1,200/mo): Highest retention because customers are deeply committed

**Building recurring revenue:**
1. Start with one-time offers to generate cash flow
2. Add continuity layer (community, ongoing content, retainer)
3. Transition existing customers to recurring where possible
4. Treat recurring model like launch model: continuously create new reasons to stay

**Retention > acquisition:** A 5% improvement in retention = 25-95% increase in profit (depending on industry). Focus on keeping customers BEFORE spending more on acquisition.

## Customer Journey Architecture

**Optimal flow:**

```
Lead Magnet (free) --> Email Nurture --> Attraction Offer ($)
    --> Onboarding --> Goal-Setting Call --> Core Offer ($$)
        --> Upsell ($$$) --> Continuity ($/mo)
            --> Referral --> New Leads (cycle repeats)
```

**Each transition point needs:**
1. Clear next step (one CTA, not three)
2. Value demonstration (they got results from the previous step)
3. Natural progression (the next offer solves the next problem)

**Onboarding --> Goal-Setting Call --> Closer Call:**
- Onboarding creates early wins (first 48 hours)
- Goal-setting call deepens commitment
- Closer call presents higher-value offering at the moment of maximum belief

## Money Model Diagnostics

Use these questions during coaching sessions to diagnose money model gaps:

**Stage 0: Do they have a money model at all?**
- "Walk me through what happens after someone first pays you. What's the next offer they see? And after that?"
- If the answer is "nothing" or "we just hope they come back" -- they don't have a money model.

**30-Day Payback Check:**
- "What does one customer cost you to acquire?"
- "How much revenue does that customer generate in their first 30 days?"
- "Is that enough to cover their acquisition AND fund the next customer?"
- If no: the money model has a hole. Identify which stage is missing (attraction, upsell, downsell, or continuity).

**Offer Stack Audit:**
- "How many of the 4 offer types do you currently have?" (Most businesses have 1, maybe 2)
- "Which of the 15 plays could work for your business?" (Run through the plays table)
- "What happens when someone says NO to your main offer?" (If nothing: no downsell)
- "What recurring revenue do you have?" (If none: no continuity)

**Revenue Layer Diagnostic:**
- "Where is most of your revenue coming from -- first purchase or repeat purchases?"
- "What percentage of customers buy more than once?"
- "What's your average customer lifetime in months?"

**Route to action:**
- Missing attraction offer --> design one using the 5 attraction plays
- Missing upsells --> design using 4 upsell plays, route to `product-offer`
- Missing downsells --> design using 3 downsell plays
- Missing continuity --> design using 3 continuity plays
- Has all 4 but economics don't work --> optimize pricing, reduce CAC, or increase conversion between stages

## Content Frameworks (YouTube / Workshops)

### Hook, Retain, Reward

Three-stage content structure tied to how algorithms AND humans evaluate content:

| Stage | What It Does | Algorithm Signal | Human Signal |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Hook** | Stop the scroll. First 1-3 seconds. | CTR (click-through rate) | "Wait, what?" |
| **Retain** | Keep them consuming. Open loops, stories, pattern interrupts. | Watch time / read time (30%+ retention is good) | "I need to see what happens" |
| **Reward** | Deliver on the promise. Close the loop. | Shares, saves, comments | "That was worth my time" |

**Application to newsletters:** Subject line = Hook. Opening paragraph = Retain. Tool demo or insight = Reward.

### Proof, Promise, Path

Lead with proof, then promise, then plan. Most creators flip this (promise first), which triggers skepticism.

1. **PROOF** - Why they should listen (results, credentials, story)
2. **PROMISE** - What they'll get (specific outcome)
3. **PATH** - What happens next (clear next step)

**Why sequence matters:** Proof creates receptivity. Without it, promises sound like hype.

### Hook, Meat, CTA (Ad Assembly)

Break every ad/content into three swappable components. Generate variations by swapping independently: 5 hooks x 5 meats x 5 CTAs = 125 combinations. Test 10 hooks for every 1 piece of content. Hook determines 80% of performance.

### Long-Term Greedy

Give away your best content free - better than what competitors charge for. Build trust so deep that when you sell, people assume the paid product must be extraordinary. Free content that's genuinely excellent -> audience trusts you -> product launch conversion 3-5x higher than cold launches.

## Advertising Frameworks (YouTube / Workshops)

### 70/20/10 Creative Testing

| Bucket | % of Budget | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **70% - More** | Exploit winners | Minor variations of winning ads: different filters, music, narrator, thumbnail |
| **20% - Adjacent** | Meaningful variations | New hook, new CTA, new social proof on proven concept |
| **10% - New** | Fresh concepts | Completely different angle, format, or message |

Prevents over-testing. Ensures winners get exploited before moving on.

### Organic-First Validation

Validate hooks and offers organically (social/email) before paying for distribution:
1. Post hook variations as social content
2. Track which hooks get highest engagement
3. Use top-performing organic hooks as ad hooks

Broad targeting + strong creative beats narrow targeting + weak creative.

| Hybrid (some human element) | 4:1 | 6:1+ | Below 3:1 |
| Services / coaching | 6:1 | 8-9:1 | Below 4:1 |

## Sales Supplements (Beyond CLOSER/AAA)

> See `sales-and-objections.md` for full CLOSER and AAA frameworks.

### Belief Continuum

Belief is not binary. It's a spectrum from 0 to 10.

```
Sales skill x Conviction = Close rate
```

Conviction counts twice: it affects how hard you work AND how persuasive you are. The real lever is educating salespeople about the customer's transformation, not the product features. A salesperson who has seen 50 transformations closes at 2-3x the rate of one who just knows the product.

### 13-Point Proof Checklist

Structured system for capturing proof that moves prospects along the Belief Continuum:

1. Before/after screenshots or photos
2. Specific metrics (revenue, subscribers, time saved)
3. Timeline of results (how fast)
4. Starting conditions (proves it works for "people like me")
5. Video testimonial (highest trust)
6. Written testimonial with full name
7. Case study with process detail
8. Third-party validation (press, awards, certifications)
9. Social media mentions (unsolicited)
10. Aggregated results ("helped 500+ businesses")
11. Live demonstration or proof of concept
12. Guarantee language (what happens if it doesn't work)
13. Competitor comparison (why this vs. alternatives)

Collect proof continuously, not just when you need it.

## Retention Supplements (Beyond Churn Protocol)

> See `retention-and-growth.md` for full churn prevention protocol and referral framework.

### Five Horsemen of Retention

Five drivers that determine whether customers stay. Score each 1-10. Lowest score = churn driver. Fix that one first.

1. **Onboarding velocity** - How fast they get first result (see Conversion Velocity in value-and-offers.md)
2. **Community & belonging** - Peer connections, identity as member
3. **Ongoing value delivery** - New content, features, updates (not just initial purchase value)
4. **Sunk cost accumulation** - Progress tracking, achievements, history painful to abandon
5. **Switching cost** - Integrations, data, relationships that make leaving hard

### Crazy 8 LTV Framework

Eight methods to maximize transactions per customer (priority order):

1. Fix retention first (cheapest lever)
2. Upsells - Higher tier of same product
3. Cross-sells - Complementary products
4. Downsells - Lower tier for non-buyers
5. Bundles - Package multiple products at discount
6. Price increases - Raise prices for new customers
7. Subscription conversion - Move one-time buyers to recurring
8. Reactivation - Win back lapsed customers

### R-A-I-S-E Pricing Method

Structured framework for raising prices:

- **R - Reason**: Legitimate reason for increase (costs, value added, market rates)
- **A - Advance notice**: 30-60 days minimum before implementation
- **I - Incentive**: Existing customers get loyalty rate or locked price
- **S - Stack value**: Add something new alongside the increase so net value goes up
- **E - Execute confidently**: State it as fact with value justification. Don't apologize.

20% should say "too expensive" after increase. If nobody complains, you didn't raise enough.

## Management Frameworks (YouTube / VAM Workshops)

> See `operations-and-hiring.md` for delegation, Phone Test, and Five Core Functions.

### Management Diamond

Five-point diagnostic when someone doesn't complete a task:

1. **What** - Didn't know what was expected
2. **How** - Didn't know how to do it
3. **When** - Didn't know the deadline
4. **Incentive** - Knew everything but lacked motivation
5. **Blockers** - External obstacles prevented completion

Most managers assume motivation problem. Usually it's a clarity problem. Diagnose before prescribing.

### Variable Reward System

Three levers for reinforcing desired behaviors (operant conditioning):

1. **Quickly** - Pair reward with behavior immediately (not end-of-quarter)
2. **Randomly** - Vary timing to prevent habituation (slot machine effect)
3. **Publicly** - Celebrate in front of team for status amplification

### Employee Retention Triad

Three non-monetary retention drivers:

1. **Mentorship** - Learning opportunities beyond current role
2. **Identity** - Help them define unique contribution ("You're our brand voice expert")
3. **Leadership** - Give them something to own and lead, not just tasks

## Scaling Frameworks (YouTube / Workshops)

### 4 Levels of Growth

| Level | Revenue | Priority | Hiring |
|-------|---------|----------|--------|
| **1** | $0-$1M | Validate core offer. Founder does everything. | Don't hire. Learn every role. |
| **2** | $1-$3M | Avoid distraction. Double down on what works. Add volume. | First hire: marketing/sales support. |
| **3** | $3-$10M | Build team that executes independently. "Who Before How." | Hire vertical experts. Delegate functions. |
| **4** | $10-$100M | Hire leaders who hire their own teams. | Hire VPs, not doers. |

Level 2 is the "distraction danger zone." Most entrepreneurs start new things instead of scaling what works.

### 20% Performance Dip Rule

Any organizational change causes a temporary performance decrease (2-6 weeks). New hires, systems, processes all dip before improving. Budget for it. New initiatives need 40%+ ROI hurdle to justify switching cost + dip.

### Brand-Driven Economics

Content creation simultaneously affects both sides of LTV:CAC:
- **Reduces CAC**: Fixed labor cost, unlimited reach (marginal cost of one more viewer = $0)
- **Increases LTV**: Continued content deepens trust, customers associate quality with purchases

At scale, brand becomes a multiplier on both sides. Content is infrastructure, not marketing.

### Logic, Evidence, Utility

Three-question stress test for any claim or business advice:
1. **Logic**: "What does this mean?" (Define terms precisely)
2. **Evidence**: "How do you know?" (Demand proof, not anecdotes)
3. **Utility**: "So what?" (What specific action does it lead to?)

### Money Model Applied to Newsletters

```
Stage I (Attraction): Free newsletter + free AI tool demo (giveaway play)
Stage II (Upsell): $27-47 Skill Pack immediately after tool signup (classic upsell)
         (Downsell): "Not ready? Try just one skill for $9" (feature-limited play)
Stage III (Continuity): $47-97/mo membership with exclusive tools (bonus continuity play)
Backend: $2.5-3.5K cohort, $5-15K DFY (sold via awareness bridge + calls)
```

### Revenue streams ranked by scalability

| Revenue Stream | $/mo Potential | Scalability | Effort to Maintain |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Ad network display** | $0.05-0.15/sub | High (scales with subscribers) | Low (passive) |
| **Sponsored content** | $0.10-0.50/sub | Medium (limited inventory) | Medium (sales + integration) |
| **Affiliate commissions** | Variable | High (passive once set up) | Low |
| **Paid subscriptions** | $5-50/subscriber/mo | Medium (requires premium content) | High |
| **Digital products** | One-time + evergreen | High (create once, sell forever) | Medium (marketing) |
| **Agency services** | $2K-10K/client/mo | Low (time-constrained) | Very high |
| **Newsletter sales** | $50K+ per flip | One-time windfall | High (build to sell) |

### Revenue stacking strategy
```
Base: Free newsletter monetized by ad networks ($0.10/sub x 100K = $10K/mo)
Layer 1: Sponsored content (2 sponsors/mo x $3K = $6K/mo)
Layer 2: Affiliate deals ($2K/mo)
Layer 3: Digital product (course/templates, $2K/mo evergreen)
Layer 4: Paid premium tier (500 paying subs x $10/mo = $5K/mo)
Layer 5: Agency services (5 clients x $3K/mo = $15K/mo)
Total: $40K/mo from one newsletter + agency

Scale: 3 newsletters at this model = $120K/mo
Minus: 10 newsletters x ad revenue only = $50K/mo (less effort but lower ceiling)
```

### Money model decision tree
- **"I want passive revenue"** --> Ad networks + affiliates + digital products
- **"I want maximum revenue per subscriber"** --> Paid subscriptions + sponsored content + products
- **"I want maximum total revenue"** --> All of the above + agency services
- **"I want to build and sell assets"** --> Newsletter flipping (build to 100K, sell for $50K+, repeat)

## 10 Instant Profit Pricing Plays (Hormozi Pricing Playbook)

Ten specific tactical pricing changes that can add 26.8% to 63.8% to revenue with minimal impact on sales. Designed to stack.

### Top 5 Highest-Impact Plays:

**Play #5: Annual Billing Options (+10-15%)**
Annual billing = 2% monthly churn vs 10.7% for monthly billing (5.35x LTV gain). 10-15% select annual on page, 30% if default, 35-40% over phone. Offer "buy 10 get 2 free" framing.

**Play #9: Ultra High Ticket Anchor (+10-15%)**
Add 10x priced "mac daddy" version. Even 10% take rate doubles LTV. Sneaky benefit: anchoring makes core offer seem cheaper, boosting close rates. Example: Before: 80% x $500 + 20% x $200 = $440 avg. After: 10% x $5000 + 70% x $500 + 20% x $200 = $890 avg (2x!).

**Play #8: Automatic Continuity (+10%)**
After main offering ends, roll into 5-20% priced stripped-down version. Sunk cost keeps them. Example: $2000/mo x 4 months -> $200/mo continuity = +32% LTV.

**Play #1: Monthly to 28-Day Billing (+8.3%)**
13 billing cycles/year vs 12. Display weekly, bill every 4 weeks. At 20% margins: takes you to 26.1% margins (+41.5% profit increase).

**Play #4: Annual CPI Price Increases (+3-10%/yr compounding)**
Built into contracts. Script: "to reinvest in keeping our integrity and delivering at the highest quality, we keep prices standard with the CPI." 5% annual = 22% increase over 4 years.

### Other 5 Plays (Brief):
- **Play #2:** Processing fees + second form of payment (+3-4% revenue OR +14-51% LTV)
- **Play #3:** Sales tax pass-through (0-10%)
- **Play #6:** Round up prices from 7s to 9s (+1-3%)
- **Play #7:** Annual renewal fee -- monthly rate + annual fee of 1-3x monthly (+10%)
- **Play #10:** Guarantee/warranty upsells (+5-20%)

**Coaching application:** Walk a client through all 10 plays. Most can implement 3-5 immediately with zero additional marketing spend. Stack the easy ones first.

## Big Head, Long Tail Pricing Model (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

Charge a large one-time fee upfront for high-value deliverable (education, setup, implementation), then a small recurring fee for ongoing value (community, support).

**Example:** $6,800 upfront + $199/mo recurring. LTV goes from $6,800 to $12,800+.

**Two entry paths:**
1. Sell high-ticket in free community, then upsell to paid recurring
2. Sell paid recurring first, then upsell high-ticket over phone

**Critical rule:** Include the recurring fee WITH the upfront fee, not as a separate sale. Preserves the price anchor.

**Coaching application:** For clients with one-time products and no recurring revenue. "What ongoing value could you add that justifies $99-$299/mo? Community? Accountability calls? Tool access?"

## Favorite Upsell Formula (Hormozi LTV Playbook)

Hormozi's favorite upsell of all time, used to 2.2x LTV and enable 5x advertising scale:

> "More of, or more help with, what they just bought... but with faster results, less risk, less effort, less hassle... for more money."

**Key components:**
1. More of what they just bought (or more help with it)
2. Faster results
3. Less risk
4. Less effort / less hassle
5. For more money
6. Must solve "their next problem" not your passion project

**Test method:** Ask customers which they prefer (survey 2-3 upsell concepts).

**Coaching question:** "What's the next problem your customer hits AFTER they use your product? That's your upsell."

> "If customers like your stuff, they want to buy more stuff like it!"
