## More Better New

**The scaling sequence (in this order):**

| Step | What | When to Move On |
|------|------|-----------------|
| **1. MORE** | Do more of what's working | When returns diminish (CAC rises) |
| **2. BETTER** | Optimize what you're doing | When optimization hits ceiling |
| **3. NEW** | Try a new channel/approach | Only after maxing more + better |

**Key insight:** Every time you go for "new," there's a learning curve that fragments attention. This could hurt what's already working.

**Applied example:**
- MORE: Posting 3x/week on LinkedIn? Go to 7x/week. Spending $50/day on ads? Go to $200/day.
- BETTER: 7x/week but engagement dropping? Improve hook quality, test new formats, A/B test CTAs.
- NEW: LinkedIn maxed out? Now try YouTube or TikTok.

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's your best-performing channel right now?"
- "Have you truly maxed it out, or are you just bored?"
- "What would 'more' look like for your top channel?"

## 9 Stages of Business Growth

| Stage | Name | Revenue Range | Primary Focus |
|-------|------|---------------|---------------|
| 0 | Improvise | $0 | Create something, get first customer |
| 1 | Monetize | $0-$100K | Establish revenue generation |
| 2 | Advertise | $100K-$500K | Build customer acquisition systems |
| 3 | Stabilize | $500K-$1M | Create operational consistency |
| 4 | Prioritize | $1M-$3M | Strategic focus and delegation |
| 5 | Productize | $3M-$5M | Systematize offerings |
| 6 | Optimize | $5M-$10M | Efficiency improvements |
| 7 | Categorize | $10M-$25M | Organizational structure |
| 8 | Specialize | $25M-$50M | Department expertise |
| 9 | Capitalize | $50M+ | Enterprise-level operations |

**Key principle:** Master each stage before moving to the next. Most businesses try to skip stages and stall.

**Coaching question:** "What stage are you at? What does the NEXT stage require that you haven't built yet?"

## LTV / CAC / PPD Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
```
CAC = Total cost spent on acquiring customers ÷ Number of new customers
```
Include: ad spend, sales team costs, marketing tools, content creation costs, co-promotion fees.

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
```
LTV = Revenue per customer × Gross profit margin
```
For recurring: LTGP = Gross Profit per Customer ÷ Churn Rate

**Example:** $200/mo gross profit ÷ 5% monthly churn = $4,000 LTGP

### Payback Period (PPD)
Time to recover acquisition cost through gross profit.

**Target:** PPD < 90 days for healthy growth. PPD < 30 days for aggressive scaling.

### Target Ratios by Business Type
| Business Type | LTV:CAC Target |
|--------------|----------------|
| Automated (ads, newsletters) | 3:1 minimum |
| Manual/service businesses | 20:1 or even 30:1 |
| Aggressive scaling | 8:1+ |

### The Growth Engine
```
Lower CAC + Higher LTV = Reinvest profits faster = Compound growth
```

Only two metrics to improve: make customers worth more OR reduce acquisition costs.

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's your CAC by channel? Give me exact numbers."
- "What's your LTV? How long does the average customer stay?"
- "What's your payback period? Can you fund growth from cash flow?"

## Scaling Diagnostics

### Supply vs. Demand Constraint

**First diagnostic question:** "Is your business supply-constrained or demand-constrained?"

| Constraint | Symptoms | Focus |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Demand-constrained** | Can serve more customers but don't have them | Marketing, lead gen, sales |
| **Supply-constrained** | More customers than you can serve | Operations, hiring, systems |

This single distinction determines every strategic decision.

### Revenue scaling math
```
Revenue = Traffic × Conversion Rate × Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency
```
Identify which variable has the most room for improvement. A 10% improvement in EACH variable = 46% revenue increase (compound effect).

**Going from $3M to $10M EBITDA:**
- 3.3x EBITDA growth + multiple increase (1x → 4x) = 13.2x return on value
- This is where majority of business value is created

## Five Customer Expansion Moves

| Move | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Upmarket** | Serve higher-paying customers | Free newsletter → premium paid tier |
| **Downmarket** | Serve lower-budget customers | Full agency → self-serve templates |
| **Adjacent** | Serve nearby needs | Newsletter → courses → community |
| **Broader** | Serve wider audience | "AI for marketers" → "AI for professionals" |
| **Narrower** | Serve deeper niche | "AI newsletters" → "AI newsletters for finance" |

**Decision framework:** Pick the move with the least competition AND highest willingness to pay.

## Daily Priority Rule

**First 4 hours of every workday on ONE attention activity:**
- Cold outreach
- Warm outreach
- Content creation
- Paid ads management

**Do NOT get into operations work until attention work is done:** No delivery, no systems, no admin, no website tweaks, no Slack.

**Rationale:** Operations feel productive but don't generate new revenue. Attention activities are the only thing that grows the business.

**Coaching challenge:** "What did you do in the first 4 hours today? Was it attention or operations?"

## Industry Example: Newsletter Business

*The frameworks above apply to ANY business. Below is one industry example to illustrate application.*

### Key newsletter metrics to track
| Metric | Benchmark | Great |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Open rate | 30-40% | 45%+ |
| CTR | 2-4% | 5%+ |
| Subscriber growth rate | 5-10%/mo | 15%+ |
| Revenue per subscriber/mo | $0.05-$0.15 | $0.20+ |
| Subscriber CAC | $1-$3 | <$1 |
| Monthly churn | 2-5% | <2% |
| Ad revenue CPM | $15-$30 | $40+ |

### Revenue per subscriber math
```
Target: $100K/mo revenue
Current: $20K/mo with ~200K subscribers
Revenue/subscriber = ~$0.10/mo

Path to $100K/mo (pick one or combine):
Option A: 5x subscribers (600K) at same $0.10/sub = $100K/mo
Option B: Same subscribers, 5x revenue/sub ($0.50/sub) = $100K/mo
Option C: 2.5x subscribers (300K) + 2x revenue/sub ($0.20) = $100K/mo
```

### Newsletter portfolio math
```
Current: 1 main newsletter + a couple more
If each newsletter at 100-120K subs = $4-5K/mo revenue
10 newsletters × $5K = $50K/mo
20 newsletters × $5K = $100K/mo

But: Each newsletter needs content, growth, operations.
Constraint: Can you produce/manage 10-20 newsletters? (supply constraint)
```
