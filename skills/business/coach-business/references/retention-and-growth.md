## Retention Framework

**Hormozi's shift from traditional marketing:**

| Traditional | Hormozi |
|------------|---------|
| Reach | **Retain** |
| Reputation | **Review** |
| Renew | **Refer** |
| Refer | **Resell** |

**Priority order:** Retain first, then review (get feedback), then refer (turn happy customers into advocates), then resell (expand wallet share).

**Core philosophy:** "Permanent customers > constant acquisition." Retention compounds; acquisition is linear.

**The math:**
- 5% improvement in retention = 25-95% increase in profit
- Acquiring new customer costs 5-25x more than retaining existing one
- Retained customers have higher AOV, refer more, and cost less to serve

## Churn Prevention

**Churn targets:**
- Critical: >5% monthly (business is shrinking)
- Okay: 3-5% monthly
- Good: 1-3% monthly
- Elite: <1% monthly

**Nine-step churn elimination protocol:**

1. **Measure churn by cohort**: Not just overall churn; break it by month of acquisition, source, plan type
2. **Survey churned customers**: Within 24 hours of cancellation, ask "What was the #1 reason?"
3. **Identify the churn cliff**: When do most customers leave? (Usually month 1-3 or after annual renewal)
4. **Fix onboarding**: Shift from 1-to-many to 1-to-1 onboarding. Get to first win in <48 hours.
5. **Create milestone celebrations**: Recognize progress at key points (7 days, 30 days, 90 days)
6. **Build community**: People stay for people, not products
7. **Continuous value creation**: Treat recurring model like a launch model. New features, content, events monthly.
8. **Cancel flow optimization**: Save offers, pause options, downgrade paths, exit surveys
9. **Win-back campaigns**: Re-engage churned customers at 30, 60, 90 days post-churn

**Key insight:** "Most churn is decided in the first 30 days. Fix onboarding and you fix 60% of churn."

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's your monthly churn rate? By cohort?"
- "When do most customers leave?"
- "What does your onboarding look like? Walk me through the first 48 hours."
- "What's your cancel flow? Save offers? Downgrade options?"

## Referral Framework

**Five customer acquisition channels:**
1. Ads
2. Content
3. Affiliates
4. Referrals
5. Outreach

"No other way to grow."

**Unconventional referral structure:**
Instead of standard referral commissions (e.g., 20%), let affiliates keep **100% of the revenue** from a specific subset of your offerings. Carve out a portion they sell entirely for themselves.

**Why this works:** Affiliates are dramatically more motivated when they keep 100% of something vs. 20% of everything. You acquire customers at zero marginal cost, then monetize them on your other offerings.

**Referral zone strategy:**
1. Overdeliver first (they need to genuinely love the product)
2. Make referring effortless (one-click, pre-written messages)
3. Reward both parties (referrer AND referred get something)
4. Create social proof loops (leaderboards, public recognition)
5. Ask at moments of peak satisfaction (right after a win)

## Customer Avatar & Market Selection

**Market selection criteria (before anything else):**
| Criteria | Question | Test |
|----------|----------|------|
| **Pain** | Are they suffering enough? | Will they pay to make it stop? |
| **Money** | Do they have buying power? | Can they afford your solution without hardship? |
| **Targeting** | Are they easy to find? | Can you reach them with ads, content, or outreach? |
| **Growing** | Is the market expanding? | Are more people entering this market each year? |

**Niching strategy:**
- "Niching down will make you more money": allows premium pricing
- From zero to $1M: "One product to one avatar on one channel"
- Know the EXACT type of person you're selling to

**Commitment rule:** "Pick then commit. You must stick with whatever you pick long enough to have trial and error. You will fail far longer if you keep changing who you market to because you must start from the beginning each time."

**Avatar definition questions:**
- What's their job title / role?
- What's their annual income?
- What problem keeps them up at night?
- Where do they spend time online?
- What have they already tried?
- What do they believe is true about their problem?

## Content Strategy

**"Give until they ask"**: Flood audience with value until they can't help but reach out.

**Hormozi's 30-day business-only content test results:**
- Higher RPMs and long-form views
- +25% comments per view
- +24.6% subscriber conversion

- 93% doing business, 7% content creation
- One long-form → 30+ short-form pieces

## Industry Example: Newsletter Business

*The frameworks above apply to ANY business. Below is one industry example to illustrate application.*

### Subscriber retention (keeping email subscribers)
- **Welcome sequence**: First 7 emails are critical. Set expectations, deliver immediate value, get first win.
- **Engagement loops**: Ask questions, run polls, request replies. Active subscribers don't unsubscribe.
- **Content consistency**: Same day, same time, same format. Predictability builds habit.
- **Value density**: Every email must justify its existence. No filler. No "just checking in."
- **Re-engagement**: Unengaged after 30 days → re-engagement sequence. After 60 days → prune.

### Agency client retention
**The retention problem you described (low agency retention):**

Root cause diagnosis (pick the ones that apply):
1. **Unclear expectations** → Fix: Written SOPs, concrete KPIs, weekly reports
2. **Slow time-to-value** → Fix: Quick win in first 2 weeks (e.g., one published newsletter issue)
3. **No accountability** → Fix: Monthly reviews with metrics, 90-day milestones
4. **Generic service** → Fix: Customized strategy per client, not template-based
5. **No community** → Fix: Client Slack/Discord, peer learning, shared wins
6. **Price-value gap** → Fix: Results-based pricing, conditional guarantees

**Retention-boosting tactics:**
- 90-day success roadmap (co-created with client)
- Weekly 15-minute check-in calls
- Monthly strategy review with data
- Quarterly business review (higher-level, strategic)
- Referral incentives (retain + grow via referrals)
- "Graduation" model: clients who hit targets get upgraded to next tier, not churned

## Five Horsemen Deep Version (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

Full detail on the five retention tactics discovered by interviewing gym owners with less than 3% monthly churn over 6 months. Includes the specific attendance decay pattern, timing of intervention, and the "Shaking the Tree" phenomenon.

### 1. Track Attendance
The specific decay pattern: 3x/week -> 2x/week -> 2x/week -> 1x/week -> CANCEL

**Rescue window:** Reach out when attendance drops to 2 sessions. Don't wait for the cancel.
**Recovery pattern:** 3 -> 2 (reach out) -> 3 -> 3 -> 3

### 2. Reach Out 2x/Week
Praise participation AND progress. Solve small problems before they become cancel reasons.

### 3. Handwritten Cards
At signup, at 3/6/12 month milestones, for referral asks, holidays, birthdays. "Never a bad time to send a handwritten card."

### 4. Member Events
Regular internal calendar. **Cadence:** every 21, 42, or 63 days. Handwritten invites. Reduces churn AND generates referrals.

### 5. Exit Interviews
Save ~50% of phone/email cancellations. Set expectation during onboarding that cancellation requires a call. **Realistic math:** Save 50% of callers, but only 50% show up for the call, so net = 25% churn reduction = 33% LTV increase.

### Shaking the Tree Phenomenon
When you implement the Five Horsemen, expect this pattern:
- **Month 1:** Churn UP 50% (people who would've canceled eventually + zombie billing exposed)
- **Month 2:** Churn DOWN 50%
- **Month 3:** Churn DOWN another 50%
- **Net trajectory:** 10% -> 15% -> 7% -> 3% monthly churn
- Reduction from 9% to 3% = **3.3x increase in LTV**

Don't panic at Month 1. It's cleaning house.

## Crazy Eight LTV Framework (Hormozi LTV Playbook)

Eight specific ways to increase customer lifetime value, broken into upsells and downsells across multiple dimensions.

### The 8 Ways:

1. **Raise Prices** -- Test every quarter. 20% price increase on 10% margin business = 3x profit. Nudge 20% every 10 sales until dramatic drop, then go back to sweet spot.

2. **Decrease Costs** -- 9 tactics: increase employee:customer ratio, offshore talent, productize delivery, DFY to DWY, cap usage, lifetime to annual, in-person to remote, cut meeting times, buy in bulk/prepay for vendor discounts.

3. **Increase Purchase Frequency** -- 3 methods: add recurring (membership/subscription), decrease churn, follow-up (quarterly promotions + ongoing value to list).

4. **Cross-Sell Something Different** -- Complementary product. LTV math: old LTV + (conversion rate x gross profit of cross-sell).

5. **Upsell Quantity (Sell More)** -- 3 types: bulk (prepay for year), more often (monthly to every 3 weeks), bigger (1 hour to 3 hours). Offer the upsell first, then downsell to standard.

6. **Upsell Quality (Sell Better)** -- 17 premium dimensions: response times, availability, session length, location access, speed, service ratio, communication method, provider qualifications, live vs recorded, DIY/DWY/DFY, personalization, etc.

7. **Downsell Quantity (Sell Fewer)** -- Fewer units, less frequent, smaller version. ONLY to unqualified prospects.

8. **Downsell Quality (Sell Worse)** -- Lower quality version. ONLY to unqualified prospects. "I forbid my sales team from selling a qualified person a downsell."

**Coaching application:** Run through all 8 with a client. Most businesses only use 1-2. Low-hanging fruit is always #1 (raise prices) and #3 (increase frequency).

## Inversion Retention Framework (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

Instead of asking "How should I retain all my customers?", ask: **"What would make ALL my customers leave?"** Then do the exact opposite.

| To Churn 100% | To Retain 100% |
|---|---|
| Ignore them | Talk with them |
| Break promises | Keep promises |
| Miscommunicate | Communicate clearly |
| Treat them poorly | Treat like royalty |
| Set unrealistic expectations | Set realistic expectations |
| Hide progress | Give status updates |
| Keep them from other happy customers | Connect with happy customers |
| Make stuff harder to use | Make it easy to get max value |

**Coaching application:** When a client says "I don't know why people are leaving," run the inversion: "If you WANTED to lose every customer, what would you do?" Then check which of those things they're accidentally doing.

## Value Per Second vs Seconds of Value (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

**"Less but better is better than more and decent."**

Overwhelm is the #1 reason for churn. A newsletter business making $500K/mo had lowest churn with just one Q&A call per month + one physical newsletter. Everything added after that INCREASED churn.

**Key principles:**
1. Overwhelm = #1 reason for churn
2. Core 2-3 deliverables, make them really good
3. Retention = making sure they CONSUME value, not overwhelming them with it
4. "As little and as good as possible"
5. When customers stop consuming, contact them more often
6. Every version of a product should get shorter and simpler

**Coaching question:** "What could you REMOVE from your product that would actually make it better?" Most clients resist this. The data says simplify.

## ATRA Customer Journey (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

Every customer should pass through four milestones:

1. **A - Activate** -- Hit the activation point (always first). Find churned customers who stayed 3+ months. Top 20% by spend. Learn their patterns. Whatever they did = your activation point.

2. **T - Testimonial** -- Social proof creation. Make it easy. Give prompts, templates, incentives.

3. **R - Refer** -- Referral generation. Ask at moments of peak satisfaction (right after a win).

4. **A - Ascend** -- Buy the next thing from you. "If someone just bought another thing from you, they're the least likely to churn." Customers get an itch to buy more -- they'll buy from you or the competitor.

After activation, the other three can happen in any order. Pair each milestone with an incentive/unlockable.

**Coaching application:** Map the client's customer journey to ATRA. Most are missing 2+ milestones. The biggest miss is usually Ascend -- they don't have a "next thing" to sell.

## 9-Step Churn Checklist (Hormozi Retention Playbook)

Operational checklist used with every new Acquisition.com portfolio company. "I have never not made more money by going through and applying this checklist."

1. **Figure Out Activation Points** -- Find top 20% customers by spend. What did they DO that others didn't? Template: "Every customer that does (X) or gets (Y result) stays longer."

2. **Onboard Customers** -- Drive to activation point. Hierarchy: custom > generic, personal > group, live > recorded, carrots > sticks. 1-on-1 onboarding gave 25% boost at one portfolio company.

3. **Incentivize Activation** -- Unlockables tied to engagement: courses, 1-on-1 calls, live events, badges. Put incentives just AFTER major churn points.

4. **Community Linking** -- Group events, manual member introductions, community podcast, elevate micro-celebrities. "They come for the bikini, they stay for the community."

5. **Fire or Correct Bad Customers** -- Delete bad posts + explain why. 3 strikes. Pin best posts daily. Four community categories: wins, fun, discovered, meetups.

6. **Add Annual Payment Options** -- Price above average months stayed if under 12. "Buy 10 get 2 free" if over 12. Alternative: Big Head/Long Tail. Alternative: Founder rates.

7. **Cancellation Call/Video** -- Save ~50% of callers. Two save methods: (a) "Let me give it another shot," (b) upsell to higher program and credit payment.

8. **Survey Twice/Year** -- "If I removed everything but one, what would you keep?" + "If I kept everything but one, which would bother you least to lose?" Between surveys: reach out 1-on-1 every 2-3 weeks using ACA (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask).

9. **ATRA Journey** -- Activate -> Testimonial -> Refer -> Ascend. Incentivize each milestone.

> "There is no magic bullet for churn. It's 100 tiny things that all save a few people each. And those people stack up into mountains of cash over time."
