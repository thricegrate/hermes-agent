## 1. North Star Metric Framework (Cheung)

Every business needs ONE metric that captures the core value delivered to users. Not revenue. Not signups. The metric that, if it improves, EVERYTHING improves.

**How to choose your North Star:**
1. Does improving this metric mean users are getting more value?
2. Does this metric predict revenue and retention?
3. Can you influence it directly with your actions?

If yes to all three, that's your North Star.

**Examples by business type:**
| Business | North Star | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Newsletter | Active subscribers (open 2+ of last 5) | Measures real engagement, not list bloat |
| SaaS tool | Weekly active users completing core action | Shows product delivers value regularly |
| Course/product | Completion rate | If they finish, they got value and will buy more |
| Marketplace | Transactions per week | Supply meeting demand |
| Community | Posts + replies per week | Engagement depth |

**Example hybrid newsletter/app business:** North Star could be "subscribers who clicked an app CTA this week" -- measures both newsletter health and product interest.

## 2. Leading vs Lagging Indicators (Cheung)

**Lagging indicators** = outcomes (revenue, churn, signups). You see them AFTER the fact.
**Leading indicators** = predictors (engagement, clicks, demo completions). You see them BEFORE outcomes.

**The power of leading indicators:** By the time revenue drops, the damage is done. Leading indicators give you time to react.

| Lagging (too late) | Leading (early warning) |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Monthly revenue | Email CTR this week |
| Churn rate | Login frequency |
| Refund rate | Support tickets |
| List growth | Opt-in rate on latest content |
| Course completion | Module 1 completion rate |

**Rule:** Track 1-2 lagging indicators (confirm results) + 2-3 leading indicators (predict results). Review leading indicators WEEKLY.

## 3. The 9 Business Models and Their Metrics (Hariharan)

99% of businesses fit one of these models. Each has specific metrics that matter.

### Relevant to a hybrid newsletter/app business:

**Advertising Model (current newsletter business):**
- Metrics: DAU, MAU, % logged in, engagement depth
- Key: Define "active" precisely (opens email? clicks link? uses tool?)
- Danger: Don't conflate subscriber count with active users

**Subscription Model (future membership):**
- Metrics: Monthly growth rate, unit churn (not dollar churn), paid CAC
- Key: Measure UNIT churn (% of people who cancel), not dollar churn
- Target: <5% monthly churn for consumer subscription

**E-Commerce Model (digital products):**
- Metrics: Monthly revenue, compounded monthly growth, gross margin, paid CAC
- Key: Include ALL costs in gross margin (payment processing, support, platform fees)
- Rule: Compounded monthly growth rate, not average (average hides decline)

**Transactional Model (if selling tools/services):**
- Metrics: Total transaction volume, net revenue (your cut), user retention by cohort
- Key: Don't confuse GMV with revenue. Your cut is what matters.

### Universal Metric Rules (Hariharan):
1. **Compounded monthly growth rate** -- not average. Average hides bad months.
2. **Show absolute numbers** -- not just percentages. "100% growth" from 1 to 2 is not impressive.
3. **Never show cumulative charts** -- they always go up. They tell you nothing.
4. **Focus on 2-3 metrics** -- not 10. More metrics = more noise.
5. **Track burn rate, cash balance, runway** -- always know how long your money lasts.

## 4. Growth Frameworks (Alstromer)

### The Growth Pyramid

Don't chase growth until the foundation is solid:

```
        /\
       /  \  SCALE
      / 3  \  (pour fuel on what works)
     /------\
    /        \  OPTIMIZE
   /    2     \  (improve conversion + retention)
  /------------\
 /              \  PRODUCT-MARKET FIT
/       1        \  (people want what you built)
/------------------\
```

**Level 1: Product-Market Fit**
- People use it AND come back without being asked
- More employees don't help reach PMF
- "I need more salespeople" does NOT mean you have PMF
- Stay lean while finding what works

**Level 2: Optimize**
- Improve activation (first use experience)
- Improve retention (keep them coming back)
- Reduce churn (figure out why people leave)
- Retention is cheaper to improve than acquisition

**Level 3: Scale**
- Pour budget into channels that are already working
- Test 10-15 growth channels; most won't work
- Double down on the 1-2 showing promise
- Don't scale until unit economics are positive

### The Growth Loop

```
Acquisition -> Activation -> Engagement -> Retention -> Revenue -> Referral
     ^                                                              |
     |______________________________________________________________|
```

Each stage feeds the next. The loop is only as strong as its weakest link.

**Example hybrid newsletter/app loop:**
- Acquisition: Newsletter + social (owned channels)
- Activation: First app experience (must deliver value in <2 minutes)
- Engagement: Return visits, tool usage depth
- Retention: Weekly active use
- Revenue: Product purchases
- Referral: "Share this tool with a friend" mechanics

### Unit Economics Rules

**LTV > 3x CAC** -- If lifetime value isn't 3x acquisition cost, you'll run out of money scaling.

**CAC payback < 12 months** -- If it takes more than a year to recoup acquisition cost, growth eats cash.

**Blended vs paid CAC:** Always measure PAID CAC separately from organic. Blending hides true acquisition costs. If paid CAC alone isn't sustainable, you're dependent on organic growth continuing.

## 5. Time Prioritization for Founders (Cheung)

### High-Leverage Activities Framework

Not all hours are equal. Spend 80% of time on activities with disproportionate impact.

**Highest leverage for a product business:**
1. Talking to users (validates direction)
2. Building/shipping product (creates value)
3. Selling (generates revenue)

**Everything else is lower leverage:**
- Optimizing systems that work OK
- Learning about tangential topics
- Building infrastructure for hypothetical scale
- Meetings without clear outcomes

### The "Only You" Filter

For every task, ask: "Am I the only person who can do this?"

- If YES: Do it. This is your highest-value work.
- If NO: Delegate, automate, or eliminate.

**For CC team:**
- Only Yar: Strategy, skills architecture, product vision, sales
- Max can do: Writing, content production
- Julia can do: Social, community, polls
- Oleg can do: App development, design
- AI can do: Research, drafts, analysis, data processing

### Energy Optimization

Schedule high-leverage work during peak energy. Save admin tasks for low-energy hours.

## 6. Financial Health Basics (Nathoo)

**Know these numbers weekly:**
- Bank balance
- Monthly burn (money out - money in, averaged over 3 months)
- Runway (bank balance / burn = months left)
- Growth rate (this month revenue / last month revenue)

**Default Alive Test:**
If current burn rate and current growth rate continue unchanged, will you reach profitability before cash runs out?

- Default alive = yes -> you have options
- Default dead = no -> act now (cut costs or accelerate revenue)

**Revenue per employee** = best efficiency metric. Higher = doing more with less.

**Hiring rule:** Every hire is an investment. What's the ROI? Don't hire to reach PMF -- hire to scale AFTER PMF.
