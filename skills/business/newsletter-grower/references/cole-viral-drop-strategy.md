# Viral Drop Strategy (Cole/Bush)

Source: Nicolas Cole & Dickie Bush (Ship 30 for 30), Low-Ticket Product M&M Course, Module 10 (Bonus)

---

## What Is a Viral Drop

A social post offering a free asset in exchange for engagement (like + comment + sometimes share/retweet).

The engagement surge triggers the platform algorithm to distribute the post to a much wider audience. Every social platform works this way -- when content receives disproportionate engagement in a short window, the algorithm amplifies it.

**Benchmark:** Cole's Premium Ghostwriting viral drop captured 1,000 emails in 2 hours. At $7.82 CAC via paid ads, that equals $7,820 worth of acquisition -- for free.

The key insight: just asking "comment below!" doesn't work. You need to tell people WHAT THEY GET IN RETURN. It's a trade -- their engagement for access to something valuable.

---

## 6 Required Assets

Every Viral Drop needs these supporting pieces:

1. **Landing Page** -- where readers enter their email to get the asset and join your list
2. **Giveaway Post** -- the social post that drives engagement and goes viral
3. **Delivery Message (DM)** -- sent automatically (or manually) to people who engage
4. **Follow-Up Post** -- reply to original post after 24 hours with direct link
5. **Promo Email** -- (optional but recommended) broadcast to existing list to boost engagement
6. **Asset Delivery Email Sequence** -- 2 emails: deliver the asset + introduce newsletter/products

---

## 3-Part Asset Qualification Test

Your giveaway asset must pass all 3 checks:

1. **Delivers a tangible outcome** -- e.g., "How to make your first $1,000 as a course creator" (specific, measurable)
2. **Distillation of hard-to-get knowledge** -- you spent days/months/years perfecting what's inside
3. **Feels like a product** -- has a name and a type (Blueprint, Ultimate Guide, Dashboard, Template Pack, Kit, Vault, etc.)

---

## Asset Naming Formula

Three elements in the title/subtitle:

1. **Clear product name** -- e.g., "The Premium Ghostwriting Blueprint"
2. **Audience call-out** -- either directly or indirectly names who it's for
3. **Tangible promise with numbers** -- e.g., "5 Steps to Become a Premium Ghostwriter"

### Examples from Cole:
- *The Digital Course Kit: 3 Checklists To Package Your Expertise & Make Your First $1,000*
- *The Yearly Review: Your In-Depth Reflection Guide for 2024*
- *The Ghostwriter Prompt Vault: 14 Easy-To-Use Prompts To Start And Scale Your 1-Person Ghostwriting Business*

## Asset Content Sources

### Bucket 1: Repurposed Content
- **Expand popular social posts/newsletters** -- take proven content, expand it, package into a product
- **Repackage newsletters** -- bundle individual issues into an Ultimate Guide, Blueprint, Step-by-Step guide
- **Snippets from paid product** -- give away a section that delivers an outcome, but hold back templates/key pieces

### Bucket 2: New Content
- New frameworks or ideas packaged into an asset
- Mix in proven content to bump up perceived value

### Asset Format
- Build in Notion (Cole's preference) -- clear roadmap structure, easy navigation between steps
- Can also use Skool, Circle, or similar platforms
- Free stuff should feel as good as paid stuff

## Complete Templates

### X/Twitter Giveaway Post Template

```
Want to {Desirable Outcome} as a {Target Reader/Customer}?

I spent {COST} creating {ASSET} to help you:

- {Benefit #1}
- {Benefit #2}
- {Benefit #3}

Want access?

Like & reply "{emoji}" and I'll send it for FREE!
```

**Template breakdown (Outcome-Obstacle-Benefits-CTA-Directions):**
- **Outcome:** Speak to what target reader wants + name the target reader specifically
- **Obstacle:** Show blood/sweat/tears -- TIME spent, COST invested, or ACCESS to experts
- **Benefits:** Tangible > intangible. "Land your first 5K client" beats "Make more money"
- **CTA:** "Want access?" -- creates red velvet rope effect (exclusivity)
- **Directions:** Exact trade instructions. Like + Comment at minimum. Keep comment simple (emoji or short phrase)

## 6-Step Execution Process

### Step 1: Set Up Your Landing Page
- Use Carrd or your ESP's built-in landing page builder
- Register a dedicated domain on Namecheap (makes it feel like a real product) or use an ESP sub-domain
- Track opt-in rate with analytics (e.g., Fathom)
- Landing page guidelines: clear headline (not clever), product image, "Here's What You'll Get Inside" section listing benefits

### Step 2: Create Asset Delivery Email Sequence
- Set up the 2-email automation in your ESP
- At minimum, include the Asset Delivery email (Email 1)
- New subscribers can flow into your regular Welcome Sequence after
- Existing subscribers don't need Email 2 (newsletter intro), but you can use it to remind them of benefits or pitch a product

### Step 3: Set Up Social Post Automation
- **Twitter/X:** Use Hypefury for auto-DMs. Set trigger action (reply to tweet) + trigger word/emoji + DM copy with landing page link. Schedule the tweet.
- **LinkedIn:** Avoid automation tools (LinkedIn penalizes automation, especially sudden heavy use). Instead, require a connection request in the post so you can manually DM the link. Keep sending manually.

### Step 4: Launch the Post
- Leave the giveaway post live for at least 24 hours -- force people to like + comment
- Let auto-DM tools handle delivery on X
- Expected issues:
  - People DMing they didn't receive link (probably didn't follow instructions exactly) -- send manually or let them find it in follow-up
  - DMs may break on X (1,000/day limit) -- Twitter pauses for 24 hours, no big deal
  - People commenting "I didn't receive it!" is actually GOOD -- creates demand/FOMO for others watching

### Step 5: Send the Promo Email
- Broadcast the promo email to your existing list
- Include the link to the social post (not the landing page directly)
- Drives more engagement on the post, causing it to go viral faster
- Also delivers value to existing subscribers

### Step 6: Post the Follow-Up (after 24 hours)
- **X/Twitter:** Reply to original post with the direct landing page link. Frame it as "This went more viral than I thought! My DMs are flooded. Here's the link!"
- **LinkedIn:** Either edit the original post to add the landing page link, OR keep sending DMs manually. LinkedIn posts can keep collecting subs for weeks.

## Platform Notes

### X/Twitter
- DM limit: 1,000/day (Twitter pauses DMs for 24 hours if you hit it)
- Use Hypefury for auto-DM campaigns
- Ask for reply with specific emoji or word to trigger auto-DM
- After 24 hours, drop the direct link in a reply to the original post

### LinkedIn
- Require connection request first (so you CAN DM them)
- Manual DMs recommended -- avoid automation tools (ban risk)
- Post lifecycle is longer -- viral drops can run for weeks
- Can edit post to add direct link, or keep manually DMing commenters
