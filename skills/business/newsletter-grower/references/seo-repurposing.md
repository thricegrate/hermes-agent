# Newsletter to Blog Post SEO Repurposing

Every newsletter issue you write is a blog post waiting to happen. By repurposing your newsletters as SEO-optimized blog posts on Beehiiv's built-in website, you create a second subscriber acquisition channel that works passively through organic search traffic.

## Step 1: Keyword Research (Lightweight)

You don't need expensive SEO tools. These free methods give you 80% of the value:

### Google Autocomplete
- Type your newsletter topic into Google and note the autocomplete suggestions
- These are real searches that real people are making right now
- Example: typing "AI tools for" might suggest "AI tools for small business", "AI tools for writing", "AI tools for marketing"
- Each suggestion is a potential blog post title

### "People Also Ask" Boxes
- Search for your topic and look for the "People Also Ask" expandable questions
- These are goldmines: Google is literally telling you what questions people have about your topic
- Turn each question into a blog post that answers it thoroughly
- Example: "What are the best AI tools for content creation?" becomes a blog post titled "7 Best AI Tools for Content Creation in 2026"

### Related Searches
- Scroll to the bottom of Google search results
- The "Related Searches" section shows adjacent topics people search for
- Use these to find angles you might not have considered

### Your Own Data
- Check your newsletter analytics: which issues had the highest open rates?
- High open rate = your audience cares about that topic = other people probably search for it
- Repurpose your best-performing issues first

## Step 2: Reformat for SEO

Newsletter formatting and blog post formatting are different. Here's what to change:

### Title
- **Email subject line:** Designed to generate curiosity and opens (e.g., "This changed everything for me")
- **Blog post title:** Designed to match search queries (e.g., "How to Use AI Tools to Automate Your Content Creation Workflow")
- Include your target keyword in the title, ideally near the beginning
- Keep it under 60 characters so it doesn't get truncated in search results

### Headers (H2/H3)
- Emails are often one long flow of text: blog posts need structure
- Add H2 headers for major sections and H3 headers for subsections
- Include keywords in headers where it sounds natural (don't force it)
- Headers help both readers (scanability) and Google (understanding content structure)

### Meta Description
- Write a 150-160 character description that includes your target keyword
- This appears in search results under the title: it's your "ad copy" for the blog post
- Make it compelling: what will the reader learn or gain?
- Example: "Learn the exact 5-step framework I use to create a week's worth of AI-powered content in 2 hours. Free templates included."

### Internal Links
- Link to other blog posts / newsletter issues on your Beehiiv site
- Internal links help Google understand your site structure and keep readers on your site longer
- Link 2-3 related posts within each blog post

### Image Alt Text
- Add descriptive alt text to every image
- Include keywords where relevant but keep it descriptive of the actual image
- Example: alt="Screenshot of AI content creation workflow in Notion" (not alt="AI tools best AI tools 2026")

## Step 3: Publish on Beehiiv Website

Beehiiv has a built-in website/blog feature that makes this straightforward:

### Publishing Setup
- Go to your Beehiiv dashboard > Website section
- Create a new blog post (separate from your newsletter issue)
- Copy and paste your reformatted content
- Set the URL slug to include your target keyword (e.g., /ai-content-creation-workflow)

### Canonical URL
- **Important:** Set the canonical URL properly to avoid duplicate content issues
- If the blog post is substantially different from the newsletter issue (different title, added headers, expanded sections), the blog post can be the canonical
- If you're republishing nearly identical content, set the canonical to whichever version you want Google to rank

### Email Gating
- Enable email gating: require newsletter signup to read the full post
- Show the first 40-60% of the post for free (enough for Google to index the content)
- Gate the most valuable section (templates, step-by-step guides, frameworks)
- This turns every blog visitor into a potential subscriber

## Step 4: Cross-Post (Optional)

Extend your reach by republishing on other platforms. Always set canonical URLs to point back to your Beehiiv blog.

### Medium
- Republish the full post on Medium using Medium's "Import Story" feature (it sets canonical automatically)
- Or manually publish and set the canonical URL in Medium's settings
- Medium has built-in distribution through topics and recommendations
- Add a CTA at the end: "This post originally appeared in [Newsletter Name]. Subscribe for free: [link]"

### LinkedIn Articles
- Republish as a LinkedIn Article (not a regular post: Articles are long-form and stay on your profile)
- LinkedIn Articles get indexed by Google, adding another entry point
- Include a strong CTA to subscribe at the end

### Reddit and Communities
- Don't just drop links: provide genuine value in the community first
- Find relevant subreddits or communities where your topic is discussed
- Share key insights from the post with a link to the full version
- Follow each community's self-promotion rules carefully

## Repurposing Checklist

For each newsletter issue you repurpose, check off:

- [ ] Identified target keyword (Google autocomplete / People Also Ask)
- [ ] Rewrote title for SEO (includes keyword, under 60 characters)
- [ ] Added H2/H3 headers throughout
- [ ] Wrote meta description (150-160 characters, includes keyword)
- [ ] Added 2-3 internal links to other blog posts
- [ ] Added alt text to all images
- [ ] Set URL slug with target keyword
- [ ] Configured canonical URL
- [ ] Enabled email gating on the valuable section
- [ ] Published on Beehiiv website
- [ ] (Optional) Cross-posted to Medium with canonical
- [ ] (Optional) Shared in relevant communities
