# Guest Post Outreach Templates

Guest posting in larger newsletters is one of the fastest ways to acquire high-quality subscribers. These people already read newsletters, so the conversion barrier is low. Use these 3 templates to pitch collaborations.

## Before You Pitch

- Subscribe to their newsletter and read at least 3-5 issues
- Reference something specific you liked (shows you're a real reader, not spam)
- Make sure your audiences overlap but don't directly compete
- Have your newsletter link and subscriber count ready

---

### Template 1: Value-First Pitch

Best for: Pitching a guest article to a larger newsletter in your niche.

```
Subject: Guest post idea for [Their Newsletter Name]

Hey [Name],

Big fan of [Their Newsletter]. Especially loved your recent issue on [specific topic]: [specific thing you liked about it].

I run [Your Newsletter Name], where I write about [your topic] for [your audience]. I'd love to write a guest post for your readers on:

"[Proposed Title]: [1-sentence description of the value]"

This would cover:
- [Key point 1]
- [Key point 2]
- [Key point 3]

I think your audience would find this valuable because [reason tied to THEIR audience's interests].

Happy to send a draft if you're interested. No worries if the timing isn't right.

[Your Name]
[Your Newsletter link]
```

---

### Template 2: Cross-Promotion Swap

Best for: Newsletters of similar size where a mutual swap makes sense.

```
Subject: Newsletter swap idea?

Hey [Name],

I write [Your Newsletter Name] ([subscriber count] subscribers) about [your topic].

Would you be open to a newsletter swap? I'd feature your newsletter in my next issue, and you'd feature mine in yours.

Here's a blurb you could use:
"[2-3 sentence description of your newsletter + CTA]"

Let me know if you're interested and I'll write one for yours too.

[Your Name]
```

---

### Template 3: Interview/Collaboration

Best for: Building a relationship first, with the cross-promotion happening naturally.

```
Subject: Quick interview for [Your Newsletter Name]?

Hey [Name],

I'm putting together a [roundup/series/feature] for [Your Newsletter Name] on [topic], and I'd love to include your perspective.

Would you be up for answering 3-5 quick questions via email? I'll link back to [their newsletter/product] in the post.

Here's an example of a similar piece I did: [link]

Thanks!
[Your Name]
```

---

## Follow-Up Template

If you don't hear back within 5-7 days, send one follow-up. If no response after that, move on.

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hey [Name],

Just bumping this in case it got buried. Totally understand if the timing isn't right, happy to revisit down the road.

[Your Name]
```

## Tips for Higher Response Rates

- **Keep it short.** The templates above are already close to the maximum length. Shorter is better.
- **Personalize the first line.** Generic pitches get ignored. Reference a specific issue or insight.
- **Make it easy to say yes.** Offer to send a draft, not just an idea. Reduce their effort.
- **Don't pitch too big too soon.** Start with similarly-sized or slightly larger newsletters. Work your way up.
- **Track your outreach.** Keep a simple spreadsheet: Name, Newsletter, Date Pitched, Status, Notes.
