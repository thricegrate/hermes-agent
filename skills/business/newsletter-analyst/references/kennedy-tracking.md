# Kennedy Tracking Doctrine

Dan Kennedy's direct response measurement rules applied to newsletter analytics. Two rules. No wiggle room.

---

## Rule #4: If You Can't Measure It, Kill It

Every newsletter tactic, every growth channel, every content format needs a number attached to it. If you can't measure whether it's working, stop doing it.

**What this means in practice:**

- Every link in your newsletter gets UTM tracking. No naked links.
- Every CTA gets a click rate. If a CTA doesn't get clicked for 3 consecutive issues, change it or remove it.
- Every growth channel gets a cost-per-subscriber calculation. If you can't calculate CPA for a channel, you can't scale it intelligently.
- Every content pillar gets an engagement score. Track which topics drive opens, clicks, and replies. Cut the losers.
- Every product offer gets conversion tracking from email click to purchase. The gap between "clicked" and "bought" is where money dies.

**The measurement hierarchy:**
1. Revenue per subscriber (the ultimate health metric)
2. Open rate trend (are people still paying attention?)
3. Click rate on primary CTA (are they taking action?)
4. Unsubscribe rate per issue (are you pushing too hard or boring them?)
5. Referral rate (do they like you enough to tell friends?)

Track all five. Report weekly. If any trend line goes the wrong direction for 3 consecutive weeks, diagnose and fix.

---

## Rule #9: Results Rule. Period.

The market votes with purchases. Not with compliments. Not with "great newsletter!" replies. Not with social media shares.

**Apply this to newsletter analytics:**

- A high open rate with zero product sales means your content entertains but doesn't convert. That's a content problem, not a win.
- A low open rate with high revenue per subscriber means your subject lines need work, but your core audience is golden. Fix the easy thing.
- Lots of replies saying "love this!" but no one buying? You've built an audience of fans, not buyers. Shift your content to include offers and CTAs.
- A "failed" launch that still generated 10 sales? Those 10 buyers are worth more than 10,000 passive readers. Build from there.

**The only metrics that actually matter are tied to money:**
- Revenue per subscriber per month
- Customer acquisition cost vs. customer lifetime value
- Conversion rate from free to paid (at each rung of your ladder)
- Revenue per email sent

Everything else is a leading indicator at best. Track them, but don't confuse activity metrics with results.

---

## Tracking Requirements Checklist

Before running any newsletter analytics report, confirm these are in place:

- [ ] UTM parameters on all newsletter links
- [ ] Click tracking enabled for every CTA
- [ ] Revenue attributed to specific emails (not just "from newsletter" generally)
- [ ] Cost-per-subscriber calculated for each acquisition channel
- [ ] Weekly trend dashboard for the 5 core metrics
- [ ] Content pillar performance tracked (which topics drive revenue, not just opens)
- [ ] A/B test data being collected and reviewed (not just run and ignored)

If any of these aren't in place, set them up before analyzing. You can't optimize what you're not measuring.
