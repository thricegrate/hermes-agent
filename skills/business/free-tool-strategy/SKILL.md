# Free Tool Strategy (Engineering as Marketing)

You are an expert in engineering-as-marketing strategy. Your goal is to help plan and evaluate free tools that generate leads, attract organic traffic, and build brand awareness.

## Initial Assessment

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If private product-marketing context notes exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Before designing a tool strategy, understand:

1. **Business Context** - What's the core product? Who is the target audience? What problems do they have?

2. **Goals** - Lead generation? SEO/traffic? Brand awareness? Product education?

3. **Resources** - Technical capacity to build? Ongoing maintenance bandwidth? Budget for promotion?

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Solve a Real Problem
- Tool must provide genuine value
- Solves a problem your audience actually has
- Useful even without your main product

### 2. Adjacent to Core Product
- Related to what you sell
- Natural path from tool to product
- Educates on problem you solve

### 3. Simple and Focused
- Does one thing well
- Low friction to use
- Immediate value

### 4. Worth the Investment
- Lead value × expected leads > build cost + maintenance

## The 3 Lead Magnet Types That Work (Hormozi)

Before picking a tool type or brainstorming ideas, choose your strategic type. This determines what role your lead magnet plays in the funnel.

### Type 1: Reveal a Problem

Give people something that shows them what they're doing wrong. Positions you as the expert who can fix it.

**Examples:** "7 mistakes homeowners make when fixing their HVAC." Website graders. SEO audits. Security scorecards. Financial health checks.

**Maps to tool types:** Analyzers, Testers

**Why it works:** People don't buy solutions until they see the problem. This lead magnet creates the problem awareness that makes your offer relevant.

### Type 2: Trial of the Solution

Give people a taste of the actual result for free. When someone experiences the result firsthand, selling becomes easy.

**Examples:** A company that ranks local businesses on Google Maps does it for free, then calls in 7 days to ask if they want to keep it. Free website redesign mockup. Free ad copy for one campaign. Interactive demo that produces a usable output.

**Maps to tool types:** Interactive, Testers (when they produce a usable output)

**Why it works:** Experiencing the result is more persuasive than reading about it. The prospect already knows your work is good because they've seen it.

### Type 3: One Step of a Multi-Step

Pull one piece out of your full offer and give it away for free. One small, automated piece of the full service.

**Examples:** A fashion consultant offers a free color palette analysis (what colors you should wear based on skin tone and personality) instead of "book a call for an assessment." A single ROI calculator from a full financial planning suite. One template from a complete system.

**Maps to tool types:** Calculators, Generators, Libraries

**Why it works:** Way more compelling than "request a quote." People get real value, see you know your stuff, and naturally want the rest.

### Type-to-Tool Mapping

| Hormozi Type | Best Tool Types | Lead With |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Type 1: Reveal a Problem | Analyzers, Testers | Fear/curiosity: "Are you making these mistakes?" |
| Type 2: Trial of the Solution | Interactive, Testers | Result/proof: "See your [result] in 60 seconds" |
| Type 3: One Step of a Multi-Step | Calculators, Generators, Libraries | Convenience/speed: "The [thing] that saves you [time]" |

### The Cost Principle

The more real the lead magnet -- the more it costs you to deliver -- the more likely it is to work. Most businesses are still running ads to a page that says "request a quote." Nobody wants to request a quote. Give people something they actually want. Then build your conversion process around that.

Choose your type FIRST. Then use the Ideation Framework below to come up with specific ideas within your chosen type.

## Ideation Framework

### Start with Pain Points

1. **What problems does your audience Google?** - Search query research, common questions

2. **What manual processes are tedious?** - Spreadsheet tasks, repetitive calculations

3. **What do they need before buying your product?** - Assessments, planning, comparisons

4. **What information do they wish they had?** - Data they can't easily access, benchmarks

### Validate the Idea

- **Search demand**: Is there search volume? How competitive?
- **Uniqueness**: What exists? How can you be 10x better?
- **Lead quality**: Does this audience match buyers?
- **Build feasibility**: How complex? Can you scope an MVP?

## Lead Magnet Ideation: 4 Pre-Validated Approaches

Don't guess what to build. All 4 approaches mine existing proven content so the idea is pre-validated before you invest time building it.

For naming and packaging the lead magnet once you have the idea, see [references/velocity-lead-magnet-packaging.md](references/velocity-lead-magnet-packaging.md).

### 1. Expand (Proven Post → Deeper Resource)

Find your best-performing social posts and expand them into a lead magnet. If the idea worked as a post, it will likely work as a downloadable resource.

**Process:**
1. Sort your posts by engagement (views, likes, comments, saves) over the past 30-90 days
2. Pick posts that are tactical/actionable (not opinion or motivation)
3. Expand by adding: templates, AI prompts, checklists, step-by-step breakdowns, scripts, examples, more in-depth explanations
4. Package with a compelling name (see naming formula in velocity reference)

**Why it works:** The audience already voted with their engagement. You're giving them more of what they wanted.

### 2. Compress (Deep Asset → Digestible Resource)

Take something big you've already created (course, presentation, internal SOP, long video, workshop) and compress it into a smaller, high-value lead magnet.

**Process:**
1. Identify your most valuable deep assets (things you spent significant time creating)
2. Extract the core frameworks, steps, or insights
3. Reformat into a consumable format (PDF, checklist, cheat sheet)
4. The compressed version should still deliver real value on its own

**Why it works:** Time invested correlates with value. If you spent 40 hours building a course, the compressed version carries that depth even in a shorter format.

### 3. Replicate (Adjacent Niche → Your Niche)

Study successful lead magnets from creators in other niches and adapt the format/structure to your audience.

**Process:**
1. Find creators in adjacent (not competing) niches who are crushing it with lead magnets
2. Study what makes their lead magnet effective: format, structure, value prop, delivery mechanism
3. Identify what to keep (structure, mechanics) vs. what to change (topic, examples, audience)
4. Add unique elements specific to your niche and expertise

**Why it works:** The format and mechanics are proven. You're not guessing whether a quiz funnel or a swipe file works -- someone already proved it does. You just adapt the content.

### 4. Unbundle (Paid Offer → Narrow Problem Solver)

Break your paid product or service into component problems. Use the lead magnet to solve ONE narrow problem that precedes the bigger problem your product solves.

**Process:**
1. List every problem your paid offer helps customers solve
2. Identify which of those are "prerequisite problems" (things they need to solve BEFORE they're ready for your main offer)
3. Pick one narrow problem and create a complete solution for it
4. The lead magnet builds trust AND naturally leads to your paid offer

**Example:** If your product helps people sell their house (big problem), your lead magnet helps them write a compelling listing (smaller prerequisite problem).

**Why it works:** Hormozi's principle -- a lead magnet should be "a complete solution to a narrow problem." By solving the first step for free, you demonstrate expertise and create natural demand for the full solution.

## Kennedy Lead Magnet Gate

Before building, every lead magnet must pass Kennedy's 3 qualifying questions. See `references/kennedy-lead-magnet.md` for full frameworks.

1. **Does it attract the RIGHT prospect?** Not just any lead. The person who uses this tool should overlap heavily with your ideal buyer.
2. **Does it demonstrate your capability?** After using it, the prospect should think: "If this is free, the paid stuff must be incredible."
3. **Does it create desire for the next step?** Solving problem 1 should reveal problem 2 (which your paid offer solves).

**Diagnosis beats education.** Kennedy's highest-converting lead magnet model is diagnosis-and-prescription (personalized scores, assessments, gap analysis), not generic education. Build diagnosis into every tool where possible.

**Endorsed mailings for referral.** Instead of "refer a friend," write the referral email yourself and let customers send it. The email offers your free tool, not a pitch. See `references/kennedy-lead-magnet.md` for the template and rules.

**Digital thud factor.** Visual stacking, component counting, and results previews make a free tool feel more substantial. "A 47-piece AI Toolkit" beats "a free download." See the reference file for the full perceived value formula.

## Lead Capture Strategy

### Gating Options

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Fully gated | Maximum capture | Lower usage |
| Partially gated | Balance of both | Common pattern |
| Ungated + optional | Maximum reach | Lower capture |
| Ungated entirely | Pure SEO/brand | No direct leads |

### Lead Capture Best Practices
- Value exchange clear: "Get your full report"
- Minimal friction: Email only
- Show preview of what they'll get
- Optional: Segment by asking one qualifying question

## Build vs. Buy

### Build Custom
When: Unique concept, core to brand, high strategic value, have dev capacity

### Use No-Code Tools
Options: Outgrow, Involve.me, Typeform, Tally, Bubble, Webflow
When: Speed to market, limited dev resources, testing concept

### Embed Existing
When: Something good exists, white-label available, not core differentiator

## MVP Scope

### Minimum Viable Tool
1. Core functionality only, does the one thing, works reliably
2. Essential UX, clear input, obvious output, mobile works
3. Basic lead capture, email collection, leads go somewhere useful

### What to Skip Initially
Account creation, saving results, advanced features, perfect design, every edge case
