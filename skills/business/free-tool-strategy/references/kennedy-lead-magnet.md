# Kennedy Lead Magnet Strategy

Dan Kennedy's direct response frameworks applied to free tool and lead magnet design. These layer on top of the existing Hormozi lead magnet types and ideation framework.

---

## Kennedy's 3 Qualifying Questions for Lead Magnets

Before building any lead magnet or free tool, it must pass all 3. If it fails even one, redesign before investing time.

### 1. Does It Attract the RIGHT Prospect?

Not just any prospect. The right one. A free tool that attracts freeloaders who will never buy is worse than no tool at all.

**Test:** Describe the person who would use this tool. Now describe your ideal buyer. How much overlap is there? If the answer is "some," that's not enough. You want near-complete overlap.

**Example pass:** An AI automation ROI calculator attracts people who are already thinking about automating their business. Those are buyers.

**Example fail:** A generic "AI news quiz" attracts curiosity-seekers. Fun, but they're not buying anything.

### 2. Does It Demonstrate Your Capability?

The lead magnet should make the prospect think: "If this is what they give away for free, the paid stuff must be incredible."

**Test:** After using your tool, does the prospect have MORE confidence in your ability to solve their problem? Or did they just get a fun distraction?

**Example pass:** A free business diagnostic tool that surfaces 3 specific problems they didn't know they had. Now they trust your expertise.

**Example fail:** A PDF checklist with generic tips. No demonstration of unique capability.

### 3. Does It Create Desire for the Next Step?

The lead magnet should leave them wanting more. Not because you withheld value, but because solving problem 1 reveals problem 2 (which your paid offer solves).

**Test:** What's the natural "and then what?" after they use your tool? If there's no obvious next step, the lead magnet is a dead end.

**Example pass:** A free AI skill demo that automates one task. The natural next thought: "I want this for my other 20 tasks."

**Example fail:** A comprehensive guide that answers every question. Nothing left to buy.

## The Diagnosis and Prescription Model (Framework 12)

Kennedy's highest-converting lead magnet architecture. Borrowed from medicine: diagnose first, prescribe second.

### Why Diagnosis Converts Better Than Education

Education says: "Here are 10 ways to grow your business." The prospect thinks: "Cool, I'll bookmark this."

Diagnosis says: "Let me look at YOUR business and tell you what's broken." The prospect thinks: "I need to know."

The difference: education is generic and passive. Diagnosis is personal and urgent. Personal and urgent sells.

### The 4-Step Diagnosis Model

**Step 1: Ask targeted questions.**
Use 5-10 questions that surface their specific situation. Not generic. Questions that make them think "this person knows my world."

**Step 2: Generate a personalized score or assessment.**
Turn their answers into something concrete. A score. A grade. A category. "Your AI Readiness Score is 34 out of 100" hits differently than "here are some tips."

**Step 3: Identify specific gaps.**
Based on their score, show them exactly where they're falling short. Not vague. "Your email automation is missing 3 critical sequences" is actionable. "You could improve your marketing" is noise.

**Step 4: Prescribe the solution.**
Now, and only now, present your offer as the logical fix for the gaps you just identified. The diagnosis created the problem awareness. The prescription is your product.

### Diagnosis-Based Free Tool Examples

| Business Type | Diagnosis Tool | What It Reveals | Prescription |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| AI automation | Business Automation Scorecard | Which processes are manual that should be automated | the product system |
| Newsletter | Newsletter Health Check | Open rate benchmarks, monetization gaps, growth blockers | Newsletter coaching |
| E-commerce | Store Conversion Audit | Cart abandonment causes, page speed issues, UX friction | CRO service |
| Coaching | Business Growth Diagnostic | Revenue ceiling cause, missing systems, biggest leverage point | Coaching program |

## Endorsed Mailing as Referral Tool (Framework 14)

Kennedy's most overlooked lead gen strategy. Instead of asking customers to "refer a friend," ask them to send a letter (email) to their network that YOU write.

### How It Works

1. **You write the referral email.** Not the customer. You craft the message because you know what converts.
2. **The customer sends it from their email.** Their name, their reputation, their relationship. This is borrowed trust at its most powerful.
3. **The email offers your free tool/lead magnet.** Not a pitch. Not "buy my thing." It offers the free diagnosis, the calculator, the quiz.

### Why It Works

- A recommendation from a friend converts 4-10x better than a cold ad
- You control the message quality (no "hey check out this thing I found")
- The lead magnet does the selling, not the referral
- No bribes needed. Just make sharing easy and give them words to use.

### Endorsed Mailing Template

```
Subject: [Friend's Name], this [tool/quiz] was eye-opening

Hey [Name],

I just used [Tool Name] from [Your Brand] and it showed me
[specific insight I got]. Took about 2 minutes.

Thought you'd find it useful since you've been [dealing with
same problem].

Here's the link: [URL]

[Customer Name]
```

### Key Rules
- Never bribe for referrals. Bribes attract mercenaries, not advocates.
- Be specific about who you want referred. "Know any business owners doing $10K+/mo who want to automate?" beats "tell your friends."
- Make sharing effortless. Pre-written email, one-click share link, copy-paste social post.
- The lead magnet is the hero, not the referral request.

## Thud Factor for Digital Lead Magnets (Framework 7)

Kennedy's "thud factor" is about perceived volume and value. A thick package that lands on the desk with a thud feels more valuable. For digital products, create the equivalent.

### Digital Thud Tactics

**Visual stacking:** Show mockup images of everything included. A visual of "47 templates, 12 checklists, 8 video walkthroughs" stacked together creates perceived mass.

**Component counting:** "Your free toolkit includes: 23 AI prompts, 5 workflow templates, 3 diagnostic checklists, and a 15-minute video walkthrough." Specificity creates perceived value.

**Results previews:** Show a sample output from the tool. A screenshot of "Your AI Readiness Score: 67/100" with a partially visible gap analysis creates curiosity and perceived depth.

**Delivery experience:** Don't just email a PDF link. Create a "resource portal" page where they can see everything laid out. The experience of scrolling through all included materials creates digital thud.

### The Perceived Value Formula

```
Perceived value = (Number of components) x (Specificity of each) x (Visual presentation)
```

A "Free AI Toolkit" is worth $0 in the prospect's mind.
A "47-Piece AI Automation Toolkit (23 Prompts + 12 Templates + 8 SOPs + 4 Checklists)" feels worth $200+.

Same content. Different thud.

## Kennedy Lead Magnet Checklist

Run this before launching any free tool or lead magnet:

- [ ] Passes all 3 qualifying questions (right prospect, demonstrates capability, creates desire)
- [ ] Uses diagnosis-and-prescription model (not just education)
- [ ] Personalized output (score, assessment, recommendation), not generic
- [ ] Endorsed mailing template created for customer referrals
- [ ] Digital thud factor built in (visual stacking, component counting)
- [ ] Specific next step after the lead magnet (what do they buy?)
- [ ] Follow-up sequence planned for leads who don't convert immediately
