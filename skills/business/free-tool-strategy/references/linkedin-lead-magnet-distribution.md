# LinkedIn Lead Magnet Distribution Playbook

How to create, promote, and distribute a lead magnet specifically through LinkedIn. This reference bridges `free-tool-strategy` (concept + build), `content-social` (promotion post), and `gtm-outreach-strategy` (DM scripts).

---

## Step 1: Choose the Right Lead Magnet Concept

Use the Hormozi lead magnet types from `free-tool-strategy`:

| Type | What It Does | LinkedIn Fit |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Type 1: Reveal the Problem** | Shows them what's broken (audit, scorecard, assessment) | HIGH -- "Comment AUDIT and I'll review your X" drives massive engagement |
| **Type 2: Trial of Solution** | Lets them try your method (template, tool, mini-course) | HIGH -- tangible, immediate value, screenshot-worthy |
| **Type 3: One Step of Multi-Step** | Gives step 1 of your process (first module, first framework) | MEDIUM -- works but needs stronger packaging for LinkedIn's fast-scroll environment |

**Best for LinkedIn:** Type 1 (audits/scorecards) and Type 2 (templates/tools). LinkedIn audiences are time-poor professionals -- they want something they can use in 10 minutes, not a 47-page ebook.

### Lead Magnet Naming Rules

- Name must be instantly understandable to a stranger
- Include what it does OR what result it gives, not a branded name alone
- Short enough to say in one breath

**Good:** "The $10K Client Checklist" / "LinkedIn Profile Scorecard" / "Sales Call Script Template"
**Bad:** "The Velocity Framework Accelerator System 2.0"

### Ideation (3 Concepts)

For each concept, define:
1. **What it is** (PDF, template, tool, checklist, quiz, swipe file)
2. **What problem it solves** (specific, painful, urgent)
3. **Why your ideal client would comment for it** (desire + low friction)
4. **Format:** Keep it 3-10 pages. Concise beats comprehensive on LinkedIn.

## Step 2: Build the Lead Magnet

Use the full build process from `free-tool-strategy` for the chosen concept.

**Quick build checklist:**
- [ ] Title and subtitle (clear, benefit-driven)
- [ ] Full outline with sections
- [ ] Content for each section (written out, actionable)
- [ ] Reader can implement something immediately after reading
- [ ] Format: PDF, Notion page, Google Doc, or Claude Artifact
- [ ] Design: clean, scannable, branded (use `infographic-gen` or Canva if visual)

## Step 3: Write the LinkedIn Promotion Post

Use the Lead Magnet Drop format from `content-social` Tier 2, adapted for comment-gating.

### Post Structure

```
[HOOK: 1-2 lines that name the pain this resource solves -- under 60 chars]

[AGITATE: 2-3 lines expanding on why this problem is costing them]

[TEASE: Name the resource. What's in it. What they'll be able to do after using it.]

[PROOF: One specific result from using this resource -- yours or a client's]

[CTA: "Comment '[KEYWORD]' and I'll send it over."]

[REPOST NUDGE: "Repost this so someone in your network can grab it too."]
```

### Keyword Selection
- Short (1 word ideal, 2 max)
- Easy to type on mobile
- Related to the topic
- Examples: "AUDIT", "CHECKLIST", "SCRIPT", "SYSTEM", "GUIDE", "READY"

### Post Rules
- No hashtags
- No emojis (or max 1 if it adds clarity)
- Short paragraphs, white space between every thought
- Don't describe the resource in vague terms -- be specific about what's inside
- Run through `content-social` LinkedIn Pre-Flight before posting

## Step 4: Comment-Gated Distribution

This is where leads come in. The workflow:

### Posting Day
1. Post the lead magnet promotion post
2. Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours (LinkedIn algorithm rewards early engagement)
3. Like every comment (signals to LinkedIn the post is getting traction)
4. Send the DM to each commenter (see Step 5 below)

### Ongoing (48-72 hours after posting)
5. Continue sending DMs to new commenters
6. Reply to comments that ask questions (public replies boost visibility)
7. If engagement is high, consider a follow-up comment on your own post with additional context

### Tracking
- Count total comments
- Count DMs sent
- Count DM replies (conversations started)
- Count calls booked or next-step taken

## Step 5: DM Scripts

Three scripts for different situations. Adapt `gtm-outreach-strategy` Step 3 message construction to LinkedIn DM format.

### Script A: Warm DM (They Commented)

```
Hey [NAME] -- thanks for commenting!

Here's the [RESOURCE NAME]: [LINK]

Quick question -- what made you want to grab this? I'm always curious what resonates.
```

**Why this works:**
- Delivers immediately (no friction)
- Opens a conversation without pitching
- The question feels genuine, not salesy
- Their answer tells you if they're a potential client

### Script B: Follow-Up (They Replied to Script A)

If they answer the question:

```
That makes total sense.

[1-2 sentences acknowledging what they said -- show you actually read it]

If you want, I can [SPECIFIC SMALL OFFER -- "take a quick look at your X", "share what's working for my clients on this", "send you one more thing that goes deeper on this"].

No pressure either way.
```

**Why this works:**
- Validates their response (builds trust)
- Offers specific, relevant help (not generic)
- "No pressure" removes the sales tension
- If they say yes, you've earned a deeper conversation

### Script C: Cold DM with Lead Magnet (No Prior Interaction)

Use sparingly. Only for people who fit your ICP but didn't see the post.

```
Hey [NAME] -- I put together a [RESOURCE NAME] for [WHO IT'S FOR].

Thought it might be useful based on [SPECIFIC THING YOU NOTICED -- their role, a post they wrote, their company].

Here's the link if you want it: [LINK]

No catch -- just sharing something I think you'd find useful.
```

**Why this works:**
- Leads with value, not a pitch
- Personalized (references something specific about them)
- "No catch" disarms the cold DM resistance
- If they engage, follow up with Script B logic

### DM Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

| Don't | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| "I help [WHO] [OUTCOME]..." | Sounds like a pitch bot | Lead with the resource, not your offer |
| 3-paragraph DM | Too long for LinkedIn DMs | Keep under 4 sentences |
| Immediate pitch after sending resource | Kills trust | Wait for them to engage, then offer help |
| Copy-paste identical DM to everyone | Gets flagged by LinkedIn + feels automated | Personalize at least one line per person |
| Follow up 3x if no reply | Annoying and desperate | One follow-up max, then move on |

## Step 6: Landing Page (Optional)

For lead magnets that need email capture before delivery:

- Use `design-page` for page design
- Use `copy-writing` for page copy
- Keep it simple: headline, 3 bullets of what they get, email field, button
- Or skip the page entirely and deliver via DM (lower friction, fewer leads but higher quality conversations)

**LinkedIn-specific consideration:** Many LinkedIn users won't click external links. Comment-gating + DM delivery often converts 2-3x better than "click this link" posts.

---

## Measuring Success

| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| Comments on post | 50+ | 200+ |
| DM reply rate | 30%+ | 50%+ |
| Conversations started | 10+ | 30+ |
| Calls booked | 2-3 | 5+ |

**Lead magnet ROI check:** If the lead magnet generates fewer than 20 comments in 48 hours, test a different concept. The topic or packaging isn't resonating.
