# Lead Magnet Naming, Packaging & Pre-Validation (Velocity Framework)

**Source:** Daniel Bustamante (Velocity) -- 30+ lead magnets built, millions of impressions, 9K+ email subs from LinkedIn.

## The #1 Reason Lead Magnets Get Ignored

It's NOT weak content. It's poor packaging and presentation.

"If your lead magnet doesn't *sound* compelling or *look* like something worth grabbing -- it will get ignored."

## Two Critical Mistakes

1. **Generic naming:** "Free PDF Guide", "Email Templates", "Marketing Checklist"
2. **Low-quality visuals:** Stock photos with overlays, basic Canva templates, no mockup image

## The Naming Formula

**[Desired Outcome] + [Topic] + [Container Word]**

### Real Examples
- Million-Dollar Sales Page AI Prompt
- The Infinite Newsletter Idea Generator
- The 6-Figure Newsletter Starter Pack
- Viral Claude Lead Magnet Swipe File

### Container Words That Add Perceived Value
Kit, Prompt, Blueprint, Template, Playbook, Roadmap, Swipe File, Launchpad, Starter Pack, Generator

### Naming Rules
- Lead with the outcome (what they GET), not the format
- Use specific numbers or aspirational modifiers ("Million-Dollar", "6-Figure", "Infinite")
- The container word signals what TYPE of asset it is
- Never use generic format words alone ("PDF", "Guide", "Checklist")

## Professional Mockup Design

Two proven mockup formats:
1. **Book-style mockup** -- physical book cover treatment
2. **Software box format** -- SaaS/product box treatment

Both dramatically increase perceived value vs. flat images or no visual.

## Pre-Validation Method (Find Ideas Before Building)

Don't guess what lead magnets to build. Mine your own top-performing content.

### Step 1: Identify Top Performers
- Use Taplio (or native analytics) to sort posts by views/likes/comments
- Look at past 30-90 days
- LinkedIn's native analytics are weak at post level -- use third-party tools

### Step 2: Spot Lead Magnet Potential
Not every viral post should become a lead magnet. Filter for:
- Tactical or actionable content (not opinion/motivation)
- Alignment with core content pillars
- Strong engagement metrics (especially saves and comments)

### Step 3: Brainstorm Expansion
Transform the post into a deeper resource by adding:
- More specific examples
- Detailed step-by-step explanations
- Templates, prompts, and checklists
- "Value = usefulness." Only expand content that genuinely helps implementation.

## Offer Stacking (Increase Perceived Value)

Never present a lead magnet as one thing. Stack the components:

**Instead of:** "Get my free newsletter guide"
**Say:** "Get: 2 templates + 2 AI prompts + 1 eight-step guide + 1 swipe file"

Each component is a bullet point. Price anchor if possible ("Worth $297 -- yours free").

## Landing Page Rules (7 Principles)

1. Single CTA only -- no competing links
2. Outcome-focused headlines (what they GET, not what it IS)
3. Compelling asset name (use naming formula above)
4. Above-the-fold opt-in box
5. Tangible social proof (numbers, testimonials, logos)
6. Address objections upfront
7. Professional mockup image (book or box format)
