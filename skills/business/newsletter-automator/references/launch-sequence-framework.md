# Pre-Launch & Product Launch Sequence Frameworks

Two complementary sequences that work together: the Pre-Launch builds desire over 10 days, then the Product Launch converts that desire into sales over 10 days. Combined, this is a 20-day launch engine.

---

## Pre-Launch Sequence (10 Emails, 10 Days Before Launch)

**Trigger:** Manual start, 10 days before product launch date
**Goal:** Educate, build anticipation, and warm up the audience so they're ready to buy on launch day
**Key principle:** Each email is 80% value, 20% anticipation building. End every email with a teaser about what's coming.

### Day 1: Tips

**Purpose:** Deliver quick, actionable tips related to the product topic. Plant the first seed.

**Structure:**
- Open with 3-5 actionable tips on [product topic]
- Each tip should be genuinely useful on its own
- Close with: "I've been working on something that takes this to the next level. More soon..."

**Subject line pattern:** "[X] quick tips for [desired outcome]"

**Key elements:** Practical value, easy wins, subtle tease at the end

**Length:** 300-400 words

### Day 2: Stats

**Purpose:** Use data to create urgency around the problem your product solves.

**Structure:**
- Lead with a surprising or alarming statistic
- Explain what the stat means for your audience
- Connect it to the pain point your product addresses
- Close with: "This is exactly why I've been building something..."

**Subject line pattern:** "Did you know [surprising stat]?"

**Key elements:** Credible data source, emotional impact, problem awareness

**Length:** 300-400 words

### Day 3: Steps

**Purpose:** Show your expertise by giving a step-by-step process.

**Structure:**
- Present a 5-7 step process for [solving a specific problem]
- Make each step clear and actionable
- Hint that your product makes these steps faster/easier
- Close with: "Tomorrow I'm sharing the biggest lesson I learned about this..."

**Subject line pattern:** "My step-by-step process for [outcome]"

**Key elements:** Numbered steps, clear actions, expertise demonstration

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 4: Lessons

**Purpose:** Share hard-won wisdom. Build credibility through vulnerability.

**Structure:**
- Tell a story about a mistake or failure related to the product topic
- Extract the lesson learned
- Explain how this lesson shaped your approach
- Close with: "This lesson is baked into everything I'm about to share with you..."

**Subject line pattern:** "The biggest lesson I learned about [topic]"

**Key elements:** Personal story, honest reflection, lesson extraction

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 5: Benefits

**Purpose:** Paint the picture of life after solving the problem.

**Structure:**
- List 5-7 benefits of [doing the thing correctly]
- For each benefit, describe the tangible outcome
- Help the reader visualize their transformed state
- Close with: "I've seen these benefits firsthand. Want to know what most people get wrong?"

**Subject line pattern:** "[X] benefits of [doing thing right]"

**Key elements:** Outcome-focused, aspirational, specific

**Length:** 300-400 words

### Day 6: Reasons

**Purpose:** Position your approach as superior. Create intellectual buy-in.

**Structure:**
- Present your approach vs. the common approach
- Give 3-5 reasons why your way works better
- Back each reason with logic, data, or experience
- Close with: "Most people learn this the hard way. Tomorrow I'll show you the mistakes to avoid..."

**Subject line pattern:** "Why [your approach] works better than [common approach]"

**Key elements:** Contrast, logic, authority positioning

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 7: Mistakes

**Purpose:** Create pain around inaction. Show what happens when people do it wrong.

**Structure:**
- List the 5-7 biggest mistakes in your niche
- For each mistake, explain the consequence
- Position awareness of these mistakes as an advantage
- Close with: "Knowing what NOT to do is half the battle. Tomorrow, I'll show you what it looks like when it's done right..."

**Subject line pattern:** "The [X] biggest mistakes I see with [topic]"

**Key elements:** Specific errors, real consequences, implicit solution

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 8: Examples

**Purpose:** Make the concept concrete with a real-world example.

**Structure:**
- Walk through a real example of your concept in action
- Show the before and after
- Break down WHY it worked
- Close with: "Want to replicate this? I've got something coming that makes it easy..."

**Subject line pattern:** "Let me show you a real example of [concept]"

**Key elements:** Specificity, proof of concept, visual if possible

**Length:** 500-600 words

### Day 9: Templates

**Purpose:** Deliver high-value freebie. Create reciprocity right before launch.

**Structure:**
- Share a genuinely useful template, checklist, or framework
- Explain how to use it
- Show the result they can expect
- Close with: "This template is a taste of what I've been building. Tomorrow, I'm sharing it with you first..."

**Subject line pattern:** "My [template/framework] for [specific outcome]"

**Key elements:** Immediately usable, high perceived value, direct utility

**Length:** 400-500 words (plus template)

### Day 10: Secret Prompt / Exclusive Tool

**Purpose:** Create insider exclusivity. Maximum anticipation for tomorrow's launch.

**Structure:**
- Frame this as something you're sharing with them before anyone else
- Deliver a genuinely valuable resource (prompt, tool, technique)
- Build maximum anticipation for the launch
- Close with: "Tomorrow, I'm opening the doors to [Product Name]. You'll be the first to know."

**Subject line pattern:** "I wanted to share this with you before anyone else"

**Key elements:** Exclusivity, high value, maximum anticipation for Day 1 of launch

**Length:** 300-400 words

## Product Launch Sequence (10 Emails, 10 Days During Launch)

**Trigger:** Launch day (day after Pre-Launch Day 10)
**Goal:** Convert warm leads into buyers during a defined launch window
**Key principle:** Urgency escalates with each email. Early emails educate and excite. Late emails create FOMO and deadlines.

### Day 1: Doors Open

**Purpose:** Announce the product. This is the main event.

**Structure:**
- Clear announcement: [Product Name] is live
- What's inside (feature/benefit list)
- Who it's for (and who it's NOT for)
- Price and any launch-day bonuses
- Strong CTA: "Get [Product] now"
- PS: Mention the guarantee

**Subject line patterns:**
- "It's here: [Product Name]"
- "[Product Name] is officially live"
- "Doors are open"

**Key elements:** Clarity, excitement, clear CTA, launch-day bonus if applicable

**Length:** 500-600 words

### Day 2: Bonuses

**Purpose:** Stack value. Make the offer irresistible with bonuses.

**Structure:**
- Highlight each bonus with its standalone value
- Calculate total value vs. price paid
- Create urgency: "Early-bird bonuses disappear in [X] hours"
- Restate the core offer briefly
- CTA: "Grab [Product] + all bonuses before they're gone"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "The bonuses alone are worth [X]"
- "Don't miss these (disappearing soon)"

**Key elements:** Value stacking, urgency on early-bird bonuses, clear math

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 3: Testimonials / Social Proof

**Purpose:** Let others sell for you. Remove doubt with real results.

**Structure:**
- 3-5 testimonials from beta testers, early buyers, or past customers
- Include specific outcomes (numbers, timeframes, before/after)
- Mix formats: quotes, screenshots, short stories
- After each testimonial, connect it to the reader: "This could be you"
- CTA: "Join [X] others who already have [Product]"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "What [Name] said about [Product]"
- "[X] people already got [Product]. Here's what they're saying"

**Key elements:** Specificity, relatability, momentum (show that others are buying)

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 4: FAQ

**Purpose:** Remove friction by answering every question a buyer might have.

**Structure:**
- Top 5-7 frequently asked questions
- Answer each clearly and concisely
- Address concerns about: price, time commitment, skill level, guarantees, access
- End with: "Still have a question? Reply to this email: I read every one."
- CTA: "Ready? Get [Product] here"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "Your questions about [Product], answered"
- "Quick answers to your top questions"

**Key elements:** Objection removal, transparency, accessibility

**Length:** 500-600 words

### Day 5: Myth-Busting

**Purpose:** Directly confront the objections preventing purchase.

**Structure:**
- Name the top 3-5 myths/objections:
  - "I can't afford it" -> Reframe as investment, show ROI
  - "I don't have time" -> Show how little time it actually takes
  - "Will this work for me?" -> Show diverse success stories
  - "I can find this for free" -> Explain the difference between information and transformation
- For each myth, bust it with logic + proof
- CTA: "Don't let a myth keep you from [desired outcome]"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "Let's bust some myths about [Product/topic]"
- "The truth about [common objection]"

**Key elements:** Empathy first, then reframe, then proof

**Length:** 500-600 words

### Day 6: Behind-the-Scenes

**Purpose:** Build trust and connection by showing how you created the product.

**Structure:**
- Share the creation journey: how long it took, what went into it
- Show your process, tools, decisions
- Explain WHY you made specific choices
- Include a personal moment or challenge from the creation process
- CTA: "See the final result for yourself"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "How I built [Product] (behind the scenes)"
- "The story behind [Product]"

**Key elements:** Transparency, craftsmanship, personal investment

**Length:** 400-500 words

### Day 7: Case Study

**Purpose:** Go deep on one success story. Make the transformation real and tangible.

**Structure:**
- Pick one customer/beta tester
- Tell their full story: where they were, what they tried, what changed
- Show specific results with numbers and timeframes
- Quote them directly
- Connect their story to the reader's situation
- CTA: "Ready for your own transformation?"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "How [Name] went from [before] to [after]"
- "[Name]'s story will change how you think about [topic]"

**Key elements:** Narrative arc, specific numbers, relatable starting point

**Length:** 500-600 words

### Day 8: Last Chance Bonus

**Purpose:** Create a mini-deadline within the launch. Re-engage fence-sitters.

**Structure:**
- Announce a bonus that disappears in 48 hours
- Make the bonus genuinely valuable and relevant
- Recap the core offer briefly
- Emphasize: "After [date/time], this bonus is gone forever"
- CTA: "Get [Product] + [Bonus] before [deadline]"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "48 hours left for [Bonus]"
- "This disappears on [day]"

**Key elements:** Real deadline, high-value bonus, urgency without desperation

**Length:** 300-400 words

### Day 9: Urgency

**Purpose:** Turn up the heat. Price is going up or doors are closing soon.

**Structure:**
- Open with urgency: "Just 24 hours left"
- Share momentum: "[X] people have joined so far"
- Quick recap of what's included
- Address the final objection: "If not now, when?"
- Restate the guarantee to reduce risk
- CTA: "Last chance at this price"

**Subject line patterns:**
- "24 hours left"
- "This is your last chance at [price/bonus]"

**Key elements:** Real scarcity, social proof of momentum, guarantee reminder

**Length:** 300-400 words

### Day 10: Doors Closing

**Purpose:** Final email. Maximum urgency. This drives the most sales.

**Structure:**
- Clear statement: "Doors close tonight at [time]"
- Recap EVERYTHING: what's inside, bonuses, testimonials, guarantee
- Handle the last-minute objection: "You can always get a refund if it's not right for you"
- Final CTA: "Get [Product] before doors close"
- PS: Restate the guarantee and the deadline

**Subject line patterns:**
- "Doors close at midnight"
- "Final call: [Product Name]"
- "Last email about [Product]"

**Key elements:** Finality, comprehensive recap, guarantee, strong PS

**Length:** 400-500 words

## Launch Sequence Summary

| Phase | Days | Emails | Goal |
|-------|------|--------|------|
| Pre-Launch | -10 to -1 | 10 | Educate and build desire |
| Launch | 1 to 10 | 10 | Convert desire into sales |
| **Total** | **20 days** | **20 emails** | **Full launch engine** |

**Key metrics to track:**
- Open rates (should be 40%+ for engaged subscribers)
- Click-through rates (aim for 5-10% on launch emails)
- Conversion rate (2-5% of list is a strong launch)
- Revenue per email (helps you optimize future sequences)
- Unsubscribe rate (acceptable: 0.5-1% per launch email; red flag: >2%)
