## The 4-Part Email Launch System

The playbook has 4 coordinated parts. Each part has a specific job. Together, they form a system that consistently generates 6-figure results.

```
Part 1: Pre-Launch (5-7 emails, 1-2 weeks before)
    ↓ builds anticipation + waitlist
Part 2: Launch (10 emails over 7 days)
    ↓ drives sales during launch window
Part 3: Objection-Handling (4 PM sends during launch)
    ↓ converts high-intent clickers who didn't buy
Part 4: Abandoned Cart (3 automated emails)
    ↓ recovers people who almost bought
```

Key difference from our existing sequences: Parts 3 and 4 run IN PARALLEL with Part 2, not after it. The launch emails go to everyone. The objection emails go only to sales page clickers. The cart emails trigger on 2 clicks without purchase.

## Part 1: Pre-Launch Emails (5-7 Emails)

### Philosophy: Sell the POV, Not the Product

This is the biggest difference from our existing pre-launch approach. Our current pre-launch (10 emails) uses a value-drip approach: tips, stats, steps, lessons, benefits, etc. Bustamante's approach is different:

**Sell the methodology.** Pre-launch emails should educate the audience on the approach or philosophy behind the offer. By the time you open the cart, they should already believe in the "method" so buying feels like the logical next step.

For the Substack Starter Sprint, the pre-launch emails made the case for WHY a Substack newsletter is the most powerful asset a writer/creator can build right now. They weren't pitching the product. They were pitching the POV.

### When to Send
- 1-2 weeks before cart open
- Preferably 5-7 consecutive emails the week before

### Pre-Launch Email Themes (from Swipe File)

1. **"Is this right for you?" diagnostic** -- List 10 yes/no questions the reader identifies with. Then present 3 customer archetypes who get the most value. Waitlist CTA with social proof count.
2. **"What's keeping you?"** -- Name the 7 problems your product solves, one by one. Each problem gets 2-3 paragraphs showing you understand it deeply. Build waitlist anticipation.
3. **"Where you could be one year from now"** -- Future-pacing email. Paint the picture of life after the transformation. 3 specific changes they'll experience. End with "Does this seem too good to be true? It shouldn't."
4. **"How we built a $X/year [thing]"** -- Origin story of how you achieved the outcome you're now teaching. Specific numbers, specific milestones, specific decisions. "The playbook behind all of it is exactly what we're teaching."
5. **"5 pillars of [desired outcome]"** -- Framework overview. Each pillar gets a section explaining the concept + why most people get it wrong. Position your product as the vehicle that installs all pillars.
6. **"[TOMORROW] Product Name"** -- Final reminder. Repeat the 5 pillars (or condense). Announce tomorrow's open. Sprint details (dates, sessions, what's included). Waitlist CTA.

### Pre-Launch Mechanics
- Every email includes a waitlist CTA with live count ("Join 1,534+ others on the waitlist")
- Social proof counter updates in each email
- Each email ends with PS teasing what comes next or what the waitlist gets early access to
- The waitlist creates psychological commitment before they even see the offer

## Part 2: Launch Emails (10 Emails Over 7 Days)

### Cadence Architecture

This is NOT one email per day. The cadence varies:

| Day | Emails | Themes |
|-----|--------|--------|
| Day 1 (Mon) | 2 emails | #1 Announcement + #2 Bump |
| Day 2 (Tue) | 1 email | #3 Success Story |
| Day 3 (Wed) | 1 email | #4 Forward-Leaning |
| Day 4 (Thu) | 1 email | #5 Bonuses Overview |
| Day 5 (Fri) | 1 email | #6 Testimonial Roundup |
| Day 6 (Sat) | 1 email | #7 FAQ |
| Day 7 (Sun) | 3 emails | #8 Last Chance + #9 Last Chance + #10 Final Hours |

**Day 1 gets a double-send** because not everyone opens the first email. Day 7 gets a triple-send because the final day is often the highest-converting day of the entire launch.

"Isn't 10 emails a lot?" Not everyone opens every email. Multiple touchpoints are how you reach as many people as possible. More emails = more revenue.

### 10-Email Theme Map

1. **Announcement** -- Doors are open. Here's what it is, who it's for, and how to get it. Full offer breakdown (sessions, bonuses, pricing). This is the longest email of the launch.
2. **Bump** -- Short follow-up for people who missed Email 1. "In case you missed it." Much shorter than Email 1. Same CTA.
3. **Success Story** -- One specific student/customer transformation. Relatable and aspirational. Show the before state, the journey, the after state.
4. **Forward-Leaning** -- Paint the picture of life after the transformation. "Imagine it's [date] one year from now..." Future pacing with specific, tangible changes.
5. **Bonuses Overview** -- Stack the value. Show everything included beyond the core offer. Individual dollar values for each bonus. Total value vs. price comparison.
6. **Testimonial Roundup** -- Multiple testimonials. Different archetypes (beginner, intermediate, advanced). More proof = more conversions.
7. **FAQ** -- Answer the most common objections before they become reasons not to buy. Frame each FAQ as a buying concern, then resolve it.
8. **Last Chance #1** -- Cart closes soon. Remind them what they'll lose if they don't act. Recap the core transformation.
9. **Last Chance #2** -- New angle or scarcity reinforcement. Minimize perceived risk (guarantee, payment plan, etc.).
10. **Last Chance #3 (Final Hours)** -- Final push. Door closes tonight. Often the highest-converting email of the launch. Short, urgent, emotional.

## Part 3: Objection-Handling Emails (PM Sends)

This is the most innovative part of the playbook and is **completely missing** from our existing skills.

### How It Works
- Sent once daily during the launch (afternoon/evening)
- Sent ONLY to people who've clicked the sales page but haven't bought
- NOT sent to the full list
- Runs in parallel with the Part 2 launch emails

### The 3 Universal Objections

Every product has specific objections, but these 3 are universal:

1. **Price** -- Reframe investment vs. cost. What's the ROI? What does it cost them NOT to solve this?
2. **"Will it work for me?"** -- Show it works for people like them. Reduce the "I'm different" objection.
3. **Comparison to alternatives** -- Why this over other courses, coaches, or DIY approaches?

### Sequence Structure

- **Email 1:** Address Price objection
- **Email 2:** Address "Will it work for me?" objection
- **Email 3:** Address Comparison to alternatives
- **Email 4+:** Any offer-specific objections (time commitment, technical ability, etc.)

### Beehiiv Implementation

1. Create a segment: "Clicked sales page URL" AND "Has NOT purchased" (use purchase tag)
2. Send to this segment only, as manual sends (not automation)
3. Schedule for afternoon/evening (4-6 PM) so it doesn't compete with the morning launch email
4. Update the segment daily (remove new purchasers)

## Part 4: Abandoned Cart Sequence (Automated)

### Trigger Mechanics

Someone clicks your sales page **twice** without buying -> wait 20-30 minutes -> if no purchase, they enter the sequence automatically.

Key difference from our existing cart sequence: the trigger is 2 clicks (not 1), the delay is 20-30 min (not 2 hours), and Email 2 uses a self-segmentation survey.

### 3-Email Flow

**Email 1: Story-Based Nudge**
- Why you created this product
- A moment of realization
- The problem it was built to solve
- Link back to checkout

**Email 2: Survey Email (Self-Segmentation)**
"What's holding you back?" with 3 clickable links:
- "It's the price"
- "I don't have time right now"
- "I'm not sure I'm ready"

This is the key innovation. Instead of guessing the objection, you let them TELL you.

**Email 3: Targeted Objection Response**
Based on which link they clicked in Email 2:
- **Clicked "Price"** -> ROI breakdown, payment plan option, cost of inaction
- **Clicked "Time"** -> Time-saving angle, quick wins, "less time than you think"
- **Clicked "Not ready"** -> Handle the limiting belief. Waiting makes the problem worse. Action creates readiness.

### Beehiiv Implementation

1. Automation trigger: clicked sales page link >= 2 times AND no purchase tag
2. Wait 20-30 minutes
3. Send Email 1 (story nudge)
4. Wait 24 hours
5. Send Email 2 (survey with 3 tagged links)
6. Branch based on link click tag:
   - Tag "objection-price" -> send Price response email
   - Tag "objection-time" -> send Time response email
   - Tag "objection-not-ready" -> send Not Ready response email

## Patterns to Extract

### Subject Line Patterns by Phase

**Pre-launch:** Question-based ("Is X right for you?"), Problem-based ("What's keeping you from X?"), Future-pacing ("Where you could be..."), Story-based ("How we built a $X/year..."), Framework-based ("5 pillars of..."), Urgency ("TOMORROW")

**Launch:** Announcement ("[LAUNCH DAY!]"), Bump ("did you see this?"), Story ("Meet [Name]..."), Future ("Picture this..."), Value ("You also get all of this..."), Proof ("Don't take our word for it..."), FAQ ("Your questions, answered"), Urgency ("Doors close tonight", "Before tonight...", "[FINAL HOURS]")

**Cart:** Story ("Why I built this"), Question ("Quick question"), Targeted ("About the investment...", "I hear you on the time thing...", "When IS the right time?")

### Copy Structures
- **Pre-launch emails:** 80% worldview education, 20% waitlist CTA with live count
- **Launch Email #1 (Announcement):** Longest email. Full offer breakdown. Session-by-session value stack. Bonus list. Pricing. CTA.
- **Launch Email #2 (Bump):** Shortest email. 3-5 sentences. "In case you missed it."
- **Last Chance emails:** Increasingly short and urgent. #8 is moderate length, #9 is shorter, #10 is shortest.
- **Objection emails:** One objection per email. State the objection in the reader's voice. Reframe. Proof. CTA.
- **Cart Email #2 (Survey):** Ultra-short. One question. Three clickable options. No selling.

### Revenue Maximization Plays
1. **Waitlist with live counter** -- psychological commitment + social proof
2. **Day 1 double-send + Day 7 triple-send** -- catch more opens on the most important days
3. **PM objection emails to clickers only** -- high-intent targeting without fatiguing the full list
4. **Self-segmentation survey in cart** -- let them tell you the objection so you can address it precisely
5. **VIP tier at 3x base price** -- 4% conversion = meaningful revenue lift
6. **Community free trial bundled with purchase** -- 60% uptake creates recurring revenue pipeline
7. **2-pay option** -- reduces friction, only 14% chose it (most paid in full anyway)
