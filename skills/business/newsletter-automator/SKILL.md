# Newsletter Automator

Builds all newsletter email automation sequences on Beehiiv. This skill covers newsletter-business sequences (welcome, launch, onboarding, etc.). For product-funnel sequences (EEC, objection-handling, nurture), use `email-sequence` instead.

## Prerequisites

- **Newsletter identity** from `newsletter-positioner` (brand voice, 1-liner, audience definition)
- **Product details** if building launch sequences (from `product-offer`)
- **Beehiiv account** with automation access (Growth plan or higher)

## Workflow

### Step 1: Determine Which Sequences to Build

Assess what the user needs and prioritize accordingly. Not every newsletter needs all sequences on day one. Build in priority order:

| Priority | Sequence | Purpose | # of Emails |
|----------|----------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Welcome Sequence | Turn new subscribers into loyal fans | 7 |
| 2 | Pre-Launch Sequence | Warm up audience before product drop | 10 |
| 3 | Product Launch Sequence | Maximize sales during launch window | 10 |
| 4 | Objection-Handling Sequence | Convert high-intent clickers (PM sends) | 4 |
| 5 | Abandoned Cart Sequence | Convert warm leads who didn't buy | 3-4 |
| 6 | Post-Purchase/Onboarding | Onboard customers, gather feedback | 6 |
| 7 | Book-A-Call Sequence | Get qualified leads on the phone | 5 |
| 8 | Million Dollar Sequence | Complete 20-email marketing system | 20 |

**Rule of thumb:** Every newsletter should have a Welcome Sequence (Priority 1). Add launch sequences when you have a product. Add the rest as you grow.

### Step 2: Build Welcome Sequence (7 Emails)

The Welcome Sequence is the most important automation you'll build. It runs automatically for every new subscriber and sets the tone for your entire relationship.

**Email breakdown:**

1. **Email 1: What to Expect**: Set expectations: what you'll send, how often, what value they'll get. Include a personal welcome. Ask 2-3 segmentation questions to personalize future content. (Send immediately)
2. **Email 2: Greatest Hits / Best Resources**: Share your 3-5 best resources: top newsletter issues, favorite posts, recommended tools. Help new subscribers catch up on your best work. (Day 2)
3. **Email 3: The Niche Problem**: Deep dive on the #1 problem in your niche. Show you understand their pain. Don't sell anything: just demonstrate expertise and empathy. (Day 3)
4. **Email 4: The AHA Moment**: Share a paradigm shift or counterintuitive insight. Something that made you see your niche differently. Make the reader have their own "aha." (Day 5)
5. **Email 5: Origin Story**: Tell your story: how you got into this space, what you struggled with, what you learned, why you started this newsletter. Build connection. (Day 7)
6. **Email 6: Social Proof / Testimonials**: Share testimonials, results, or community stories. Show that others are getting value from your content and/or products. (Day 9)
7. **Email 7: The Soft Pitch**: Introduce your product/service naturally. Frame it as "if you want to go deeper..." Not a hard sell. Include a clear CTA. (Day 11)

See `references/welcome-sequence-framework.md` for detailed breakdown of each email.
See `references/brunson-frameworks.md` Section 1 for the Soap Opera Sequence overlay (narrative tension + cliffhangers on top of this structure).

### Step 3: Build Pre-Launch Sequence (10 Emails)

Run this sequence in the 10 days before a product launch. Each email maps to one of 10 proven content angles that deliver value while building anticipation:

1. **Day 1: Tips**: Share actionable tips related to the product topic. Tease something coming.
2. **Day 2: Stats**: Lead with a surprising statistic. Connect it to the problem your product solves.
3. **Day 3: Steps**: Give a step-by-step process. Show your expertise.
4. **Day 4: Lessons**: Share the biggest lesson you learned about the topic.
5. **Day 5: Benefits**: Outline the benefits of solving the problem correctly.
6. **Day 6: Reasons**: Explain why your approach works better than the common approach.
7. **Day 7: Mistakes**: Reveal the biggest mistakes people make with this topic.
8. **Day 8: Examples**: Show a real example of your concept in action.
9. **Day 9: Templates**: Share a useful template related to the product topic.
10. **Day 10: Secret Prompt/Tool**: Share an exclusive resource. "I wanted to share this with you before anyone else..."

**Key principle:** Each email is 80% value, 20% anticipation building. End each with a teaser about what's coming.

**Alternative: Methodology-First Pre-Launch (Bustamante).** Instead of value-drip (tips, stats, steps), sell the WORLDVIEW behind your product. Educate on the approach/philosophy so buying feels like the logical next step. 5-7 emails, 1-2 weeks before. See `references/bustamante-launch-playbook.md` Part 1 for the full framework + swipe file.

See `references/launch-sequence-framework.md` for detailed breakdown.

### Step 4: Build Product Launch Sequence (10 Emails)

Run this sequence during the 10-day launch window. Urgency escalates with each email:

1. **Day 1: Doors Open**: Announce the product, what's inside, who it's for, CTA
2. **Day 2: Bonuses**: Highlight bonus stack, create urgency with early-bird bonuses
3. **Day 3: Testimonials / Social Proof**: Share results from beta testers or past customers
4. **Day 4: FAQ**: Address top 5 questions, remove friction
5. **Day 5: Myth-Busting**: Debunk objections (too expensive, not enough time, etc.)
6. **Day 6: Behind-the-Scenes**: Show the creation process, build trust
7. **Day 7: Case Study**: Detailed success story of someone who achieved the outcome
8. **Day 8: Last Chance Bonus**: Disappearing bonus, 48-hour deadline
9. **Day 9: Urgency**: Price going up / doors closing soon, social proof of momentum
10. **Day 10: Doors Closing**: Final email, recap everything, last CTA, PS with guarantee

**Cadence variant (Bustamante):** Day 1 double-send (Announcement AM + Bump PM), Day 7 triple-send (Last Chance AM + Midday + Final Hours PM). More emails on the highest-converting days. See `references/bustamante-launch-playbook.md` Part 2 for the 10-email theme map + swipe file.

See `references/launch-sequence-framework.md` for detailed breakdown.
See `references/brunson-frameworks.md` Section 3 for the Challenge Funnel structure (5-10 day free challenge that bridges into this launch sequence).

### Step 4b: Build Objection-Handling Sequence (4 PM Emails)

Runs IN PARALLEL with the launch sequence. Sent only to subscribers who clicked your sales page but haven't purchased. Afternoon/evening sends (4-6 PM) so they don't compete with morning launch emails.

1. **Email 1: Price Objection**: Reframe investment vs. cost. ROI math. Payment plan. Cost of inaction. (Day 2 PM)
2. **Email 2: "Will it work for me?"**: Show 3 different archetypes who succeeded. Beginner, intermediate, advanced. (Day 3 PM)
3. **Email 3: Comparison to Alternatives**: Why this vs. free content, competitors, or DIY. (Day 4 PM)
4. **Email 4: Offer-Specific Objection**: Address the #1 objection unique to your product. (Day 5 PM)

**Beehiiv setup:** Create a segment "Clicked sales page AND no purchase tag." Send as manual broadcasts to this segment. Update daily to remove new purchasers.

See `references/bustamante-launch-playbook.md` Part 3 for the full framework.
See `templates/objection-cart-sequence.md` Sequence A for copy-paste templates.

### Step 5: Build Abandoned Cart Sequence (3-4 Emails)

Targets warm leads who clicked your product page but didn't buy. Two approaches:

**Standard Cart (4 emails, existing template):**
1. **Email 1: Gentle Reminder**: "Noticed you were checking out [Product]..." (2 hours after click)
2. **Email 2: Overcome #1 Objection**: Address the most common reason people don't buy (Day 1)
3. **Email 3: Social Proof + Urgency**: Testimonials + scarcity/deadline (Day 3)
4. **Email 4: Final Chance**: Last call with bonus or discount to tip them over the edge (Day 5)

**Self-Segmentation Cart (3 emails, Bustamante):**
Trigger: 2 clicks on sales page without purchase. Wait 20-30 min.
1. **Email 1: Story-Based Nudge**: Origin story. Why you built this. Personal. Not salesy. (20-30 min after 2nd click)
2. **Email 2: Survey Self-Segmentation**: "What's holding you back?" with 3 clickable tagged links: Price / Time / Not Ready. (Day 1)
3. **Email 3: Targeted Response**: Based on which link they clicked -- Price gets ROI math, Time gets quick-wins angle, Not Ready gets limiting-belief reframe. (Day 2)

The self-segmentation approach lets the prospect tell you their objection instead of guessing. See `references/bustamante-launch-playbook.md` Part 4 for details. See `templates/objection-cart-sequence.md` Sequence B for copy-paste templates.

### Step 6: Build Post-Purchase / Onboarding Sequence (6 Emails)

Starts immediately after purchase. Goal: reduce refunds, build loyalty, create advocates, set up the next sale.

1. **Email 1: Warm Welcome**: Access instructions, what to do first, set expectations (Immediately)
2. **Email 2: Quick Follow-Up**: "Have you started? Here's how to get going..." (Day 2)
3. **Email 3: Quick Win**: Help them get their first result fast (Day 4)
4. **Email 4: Social Proof / Community**: Show what others are achieving, invite to community (Day 7)
5. **Email 5: Check-in Survey**: Gather feedback, identify additional needs (Day 14)
6. **Email 6: Upsell**: Introduce next product/service naturally (Day 21)

### Step 7: Build Book-A-Call Sequence (5 Emails)

For high-ticket services ($1,000+). Goal: get qualified leads to book a discovery call.

1. **Email 1: Introduction + Credibility**: Who you are, what you do, who you help, results you've achieved
2. **Email 2: Case Study**: Detailed story of a client transformation
3. **Email 3: Framework Preview**: Share your methodology at a high level. Show you have a system.
4. **Email 4: Objection Handling**: Address "I can't afford it," "I don't have time," "Will this work for me?"
5. **Email 5: Direct CTA**: Clear invitation to book a call. Include calendar link. Add urgency (limited spots).

## Kennedy Welcome Sequence Principles

The first 7 days after signup is the highest-engagement window you'll ever get. Kennedy's direct response rules make it count. Apply these across all welcome and early sequences:

1. **Damaging Admission in the origin story email (Email 5).** Openly state a flaw or limitation before the reader finds it. "I'm not a developer. I couldn't write a line of code when I started." Credibility on everything else goes up instantly.
2. **Risk reversal in the soft pitch (Email 7).** Remove buyer risk completely. Guarantee, free trial, "try it and if you hate it, tell me." Kennedy: the person who takes on the risk makes the sale.
3. **Authority positioning from Email 1.** Don't wait until Email 5 to establish credibility. Drop one specific proof point in the very first email. A number, a result, a named client. Something concrete.
4. **Follow-up frequency matters.** Most conversions happen after touch 5-7 (Kennedy Rule #6). Don't cut sequences short because you're afraid of "annoying" people. The people who unsubscribe from a 7-email welcome sequence were never going to buy anyway.
5. **Every email has an offer.** Not a hard sell in every email. But every email should have a clear next step: reply, click, read, try, share. Kennedy Rule #1: always include a specific ask.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not sending enough emails.** More emails = more sales. This is counterintuitive but consistently true. People need 7-12 touchpoints before buying. Don't cut your sequence short because you're afraid of "annoying" people.

2. **Making every email a hard sell.** Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% of your emails should deliver value, 20% should pitch. Even your pitch emails should lead with value before the CTA.

3. **Not adding sequence-specific opt-out links.** Let readers opt out of a specific sequence (e.g., "Not interested in this product? Click here to stop these emails") without unsubscribing from your newsletter entirely. Beehiiv supports this with automation tags.

4. **Skipping the welcome sequence.** New subscribers are your most engaged audience. Their open rates will never be higher than in the first 48 hours. Don't waste this window.

5. **Writing generic, template-sounding emails.** Every email should sound like YOU. Use your brand voice from `newsletter-positioner`. Write like you're emailing a friend who happens to need what you offer.

## Output Format

All email sequences are delivered as copy-paste-ready content with:
- **Subject line** (with A/B variant when appropriate)
- **Preview text** (the snippet that shows in inbox)
- **Full email body** (with placeholders for personalization)
- **Send timing** (delay after trigger or previous email)
- **CTA** (button text and destination)

Sequences are formatted for direct input into Beehiiv's automation builder.
