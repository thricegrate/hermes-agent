# Waitlist Announcement Email Template

Use this to announce your product/offer to your existing list BEFORE it's built.

The goal: get people to raise their hand and join the waitlist. One CTA. No hard sell. Build anticipation.

---

## Subject Line Options (pick one, test the others)

1. [Announcement] I'm building something new for you
2. First look: [PRODUCT NAME] is coming
3. You're the first to know about this

**Preview text:** (want early access? link inside)

## Email Body

Hey [AUDIENCE NAME / "there"],

Quick heads up -- I'm working on something I'm really excited about.

[1-2 sentences of personal context. Why are you building this? What happened that made you realize this needed to exist? Keep it real, keep it short.]

It's called **[PRODUCT NAME]**: [one-line description of what it is and who it's for].

Here's what it'll help you do:

- [Fascination bullet 1: tease a specific insight or outcome, not a vague topic]
- [Fascination bullet 2: tease a specific insight or outcome]
- [Fascination bullet 3: tease a specific insight or outcome]

I'm still building it out, but I wanted to give you first dibs since you're already part of [NEWSLETTER/COMMUNITY NAME].

**[Click here to join the waitlist for early access](WAITLIST LINK)**

More details coming soon. I think you're going to dig this one.

[YOUR SIGN-OFF],
[YOUR NAME]

P.S. -- If you've been stuck on [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT] and want a shortcut, this is exactly what I'm building for you. [Join the waitlist here.](WAITLIST LINK)

---

## Template Rules

- Keep the body under 200 words (not counting subject/preview)
- ONE link. ONE CTA. Don't split attention.
- Tone matches your audience relationship (casual by default)
- The 3 bullets tease specific insights, not generic topics
  - Bad: "How to use AI in your business"
  - Good: "The 3-prompt workflow that writes a week of newsletters in 20 minutes"
- The P.S. restates the CTA with the pain point angle
- No fake urgency. "Early access" and "first dibs" are real exclusivity, not manufactured scarcity.
- Must pass the coffee shop rule: read it out loud. Does it sound like you talking to a friend? If not, rewrite.
- Run through `humanizer` + `content-review` before sending
