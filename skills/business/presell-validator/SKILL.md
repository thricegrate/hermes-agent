# Presell Validator

The graveyard of digital products is full of things nobody asked for. Courses with zero students. Guides collecting dust. Tools nobody opens after day one.

This skill exists to kill bad ideas fast and green-light good ones with proof. Before you spend weeks building something, spend 48 hours finding out if anyone actually wants it.

## Core Concept: The 48-Hour Demand Test

Announce what you're building. Measure who cares. Decide whether to build it.

That's it. Three assets (email, social post, landing page), 48 hours of data, one decision: BUILD, PIVOT, or KILL.

This comes from the Bustamante presell method (see `references/bustamante-presell-method.md`). A tech creator used this exact approach to get 800+ waitlist signups in 48 hours with an 80%+ opt-in rate -- for something that didn't exist yet.

The Commitment Principle applies here too: preselling IS the elimination of alternatives. You test, you get a signal, you commit. No more "I think people might want this, maybe."

## Prerequisites

Before starting, you need:

- **A product idea** -- from `niche-finder`, `coach-business`, survey data, or just your gut. Doesn't need to be polished.
- **An existing audience** -- email list, social following, or both. You need people to announce to.
- **Minimum viable info** -- who it's for, what problem it solves, and a rough sense of the format (course, guide, tool, membership).

Check private product-marketing context notes if it exists for positioning context.

If you don't have an audience yet, this skill isn't the right starting point. Build the audience first (`newsletter-grower`, `content-social`), then come back.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Build the Presell Hypothesis

**Named framework: The Presell Hypothesis Canvas**

Before creating any presell content, nail down what you're testing. Use `templates/presell-hypothesis-canvas.md`.

Interview the user to fill in:

1. **What are you building?** (format + working title)
2. **Who is it for?** (specific person, not a vague segment)
3. **What problem does it solve?** (in their audience's words)
4. **What outcome does it deliver?** (what changes after they use it?)
5. **What makes yours different?** (unique mechanism or approach)
6. **Rough price range?** (free lead magnet, low-ticket, mid-ticket, high-ticket)

Then the critical part: **define pass/fail criteria BEFORE testing.**

This prevents the most common trap in validation: moving the goalposts after you see lukewarm results. "Well, 30 signups isn't bad..." Yes it is, if you set the threshold at 200. Define the number. Write it down. Honor it later.

Use `references/demand-benchmarks.md` to set realistic thresholds based on audience size.

Output: completed Presell Hypothesis Canvas with specific BUILD and KILL thresholds.

---

### Step 2: Write the Waitlist Announcement Email

Use `templates/waitlist-announcement-email.md`.

This goes to your existing email list. The structure is simple (Bustamante method):

1. **Personal hook** -- 1-2 sentences. Why you're building this. What happened.
2. **The reveal** -- what it is, who it's for. One paragraph max.
3. **Fascination bullets** -- 3 specific teasers. Not topics ("AI automation"), but outcomes ("The 3-prompt workflow that writes a week of newsletters in 20 minutes").
4. **The CTA** -- one link. Join the waitlist. That's the only ask.
5. **P.S.** -- restate the CTA with the pain point angle.

Key rules:
- Under 200 words in the body
- ONE CTA. Don't split attention between the waitlist and your latest post or anything else.
- No hard sell. This is an announcement, not a pitch. "I'm building something. Want early access?"
- Must pass the coffee shop rule. Read it out loud. Does it sound like you talking to a friend?

For the full Bustamante interview prompt that generates all three assets at once, see `references/bustamante-presell-method.md`.

Read private voice-reference notes before writing. Content must match the founder's voice.

Run through `humanizer` + `content-review` before sending.

Output: complete email draft ready for Beehiiv (or whatever ESP you use).

---

### Step 3: Create the Social Post Version

Use `templates/waitlist-social-post.md`.

Compress the announcement into platform-native formats:

- **X/Twitter**: Hook + what + who + 3 bullets + CTA (or comment-gated: "Drop EARLY in replies")
- **LinkedIn**: Personal story opener + what + who + bullets + CTA
- **Instagram**: Scroll-stopping first line + behind-the-scenes angle + "link in bio" or "DM me [KEYWORD]"

Comment-gated CTAs ("write [KEYWORD] in comments") are powerful here. They boost engagement AND qualify interest. Someone who comments is more interested than someone who just scrolls past.

Post across all active platforms within 24 hours of the email blast.

Cross-ref: use `content-social` for platform-specific formatting rules.

Output: 2-3 platform-specific post variants.

---

### Step 4: Write the Waitlist Landing Page Copy

Use `templates/waitlist-landing-page-copy.md`.

This is NOT a sales page. `sales-page-writer` handles those. This is a "coming soon" page with one job: capture an email address.

Structure:
1. **Headline**: outcome-focused, leads with FREE (if applicable), under 10 words
2. **Subheadline**: who it's for + what they'll get. One sentence.
3. **The promise**: 1-2 sentences max on what's coming
4. **Email capture form**: field + CTA button ("Join The Waitlist", not "Submit")
5. **Trust line**: "We respect your privacy. Unsubscribe at any time."

Optional: credibility line, 3 quick bullets, "who this is for" section. Only add these if the page feels too bare without them.

No pricing. No modules. No bonus stacks. No testimonials (you don't have them yet). Keep it simple enough to convert on clarity alone.

Output: complete page copy with section markers for Beehiiv or any landing page builder.

---

### Step 5: Deploy and Measure

**Deployment checklist:**
- [ ] Landing page is live and tested (click the link yourself, submit a test email)
- [ ] Email scheduled or sent to list
- [ ] Social posts published within 24 hours
- [ ] Tracking in place (email opens/clicks, landing page opt-in rate, social engagement)

**What to measure:**

| Metric | Where to Find It |
|--------|-----------------|
| Email open rate | Beehiiv / ESP dashboard |
| Email CTR | Beehiiv / ESP dashboard |
| Landing page visits | Beehiiv / page builder analytics |
| Landing page opt-in rate | Signups / visits |
| Social engagement | Platform analytics |
| Total waitlist signups | Landing page form submissions |
| Qualitative signals | Replies, DMs, comments, forwards |

**Timeline:** 48 hours minimum to see initial signal. 7 days ideal for a complete picture.

Don't check stats every hour. Send the email, post the content, then check back in 48 hours with fresh eyes.

See `references/demand-benchmarks.md` for what "good" looks like at every audience size.

---

### Step 6: Build-or-Kill Decision

**Named framework: The Build-or-Kill Scorecard**

Use `templates/build-or-kill-scorecard.md`.

After the test window closes, fill in the scorecard honestly. Compare your results against the pass/fail criteria you set in Step 1.

**Three possible verdicts:**

**BUILD** -- Clear demand. Multiple green metrics. Unprompted interest (replies, DMs, shares). Your audience confirmed they want this. Proceed to `product-architect` with the validated hypothesis + waitlist data. For the launch email pipeline (pre-launch methodology emails -> 4-part launch system), see `references/bustamante-presell-method.md` section "From Waitlist to Launch" and `newsletter-automator` skill.

**PIVOT** -- Some interest, wrong angle. The audience cares about the space but not this specific offer. Go back to Step 1 with a revised hypothesis. Change the promise, the format, or the target audience. Re-test.

**KILL** -- Insufficient demand. Two or more kill switches triggered. Zero qualitative enthusiasm. Save yourself weeks of wasted building. Move on. Not every idea deserves to exist.

**Pre-validation (before 48-Hour Test):** For finding the RIGHT idea to test, use Cole's Niching Down Framework (6 methods), 10 Biggest Problems methodology, and Product Idea Stress-Test Checklist. See `references/cole-idea-validation.md`.

**YC idea evaluation:** Kevin Hale's Problem Validation Matrix (Severity x Frequency), Painkiller vs Vitamin test, Founder-Problem Fit assessment, Competitive Moat Framework, market timing signals, and the "start narrow, expand systematically" principle. Use the quick-score checklist (5 dimensions, multiply scores) for rapid gut-checks before committing to a 48-hour test. See `references/yc-hale-idea-evaluation.md`.

**Kill switches (hard lines):**
- Email CTR < 2%
- Landing page opt-in (warm traffic) < 20%
- Waitlist below threshold for your list size (see benchmarks)
- Zero unprompted replies/DMs in 7 days
- Unsubscribe spike > 1%

One red flag = investigate. Two or more = kill it or pivot hard.

The hardest part: being honest when results are lukewarm. "But 47 signups isn't terrible..." If your threshold was 200, it failed. Honor the number you set.

## The Presell-to-Product Bridge

When the verdict is BUILD, hand off this data to downstream skills:

- **`product-architect`**: validated problem + audience profile + top questions from waitlist replies. This shapes the curriculum/outline.
- **`product-offer`**: price sensitivity signals + audience size + demand proof. This de-risks pricing decisions.
- **`email-sequence`**: waitlist becomes the pre-launch email segment. These people are warm -- they raised their hand. Design the nurture sequence to keep them engaged until launch.
- **`launch-strategy`**: presell results inform the launch approach (you already know your best-performing channel and messaging angle).

The waitlist isn't just validation data. It's your launch audience. Treat it that way.


This skill is the recommended first step for new product ideas. You can skip it if you already have demand data (e.g., from surveys, sales calls, or existing customer requests). But if you're going on a hunch, test it here first.
