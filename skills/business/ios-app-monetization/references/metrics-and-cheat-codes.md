# Metrics, Optimization, and Cheat Codes

The numbers that decide whether an iOS app launch is making money or burning cash, plus the cheat codes that founders consistently leave on the table.

## The 3 numbers that matter

Daily metric check, under 60 seconds:

| Metric | Target | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| CPI (Cost Per Install) | < $2.00 | Hook and creative are stopping the scroll |
| CTR (Click-Through Rate) | > 1.00% | Ad copy is matching the audience |
| CPA (Cost Per Acquisition / paid subscriber) | < LTV | Unit economics are working |

LTV with the standard pricing ($12.99/week + $59.99/year + $44.99 retention offer) typically lands around $30/paying user. CPA must be under $30 for the campaign to be profitable.

If CPA is $25 and LTV is $30: $5 profit/subscriber. Scale.
If CPA is $35 and LTV is $30: $5 loss/subscriber. Kill or fix.

## Daily decisions

### Scale (when metrics are green)

- All 3 metrics hitting target for at least 3 days
- Action: increase budget by +30% maximum
- Wait another 3 days before scaling again

### Hold (when metrics are mixed)

- 2 of 3 metrics hitting target
- Action: leave the campaign alone for another 2-3 days
- Watch which direction the third metric drifts

### Kill (when metrics are red)

- All 3 metrics missing target after 3 days
- Action: kill the ad
- Make a new creative
- Test again

The creative is almost always the problem. Do not touch campaign settings when ads fail.

## Weekly review

Every Monday (or whichever day you treat as the start), pull the data and answer:

1. **Which ad is performing best?** (Lowest CPA, highest CTR)
2. **Which ads are dead?** (CPA above LTV after 5+ days)
3. **What pattern do the winners share?** (Hook style, AI persona, audio, comment-bait element)
4. **What variants do we spin next?** (Same hook, different person; same person, different hook; same audio, different visual)
5. **Are we ready to scale?** (Hit metrics + budget headroom + cashflow runway)

The pattern in step 3 is the highest-leverage output. Once you know what shape your winner is, every new ad you produce should fit that shape until the audience saturates.

## The cheat codes (free money)

Most founders leave these on the table.

### Cheat code 1: TikTok ad credit

TikTok matches your first $6,000 in ad spend. Auto-applies on new TikTok Ads accounts.

The math:
- You spend $6,000
- TikTok credits $6,000 back
- You have $12,000 in distribution

This effectively refunds the entire first month or two of spend. The credit makes the early-launch math much more forgiving:

- $50/day × 60 days = $3,000 spend
- TikTok matches: $3,000
- Effective spend: $0 net out-of-pocket for the first 60 days of testing

If you do not use the credit in the first months, you lose it. Use it.

### Cheat code 2: Apple Small Business Program

Apple cuts the standard 30% fee to 15% for businesses making under $1M/year on the App Store. Free to apply.

The math at $12.99/week:
- Standard 30%: Apple takes $3.90/week per subscriber
- Small Business 15%: Apple takes $1.95/week per subscriber
- Difference: $1.95/week back to you

Across 1,000 active weekly subscribers: $1,950/week saved. Across 10,000: $19,500/week saved.

Apple does NOT auto-enroll you. If you forget to apply, you pay 30% indefinitely.

How to apply:
1. Log into App Store Connect
2. Go to Agreements, Tax, and Banking
3. Find the Small Business Program section
4. Submit the enrollment form
5. Approval typically takes 1-2 weeks

Apply on day one of the Apple Developer account. Do not wait until you have revenue.

### Cheat code 3: Cashflow planning around the 45-day Apple payout

Apple holds revenue for ~45 days before paying out. The first dollar you earn arrives in your bank account 45-60 days after launch.

The implication: you need cash on hand to fund ad spend until the first Apple payout arrives.

Cashflow scenarios:

**$1,000 budget total**
- Split it across 2 months: $500/month for ad spend
- That works out to ~$16/day
- Below the $50/day starting recommendation, so test smaller and slower
- Once first Apple payout arrives at day 45-60, reinvest into ad spend
- The TikTok ad credit doubles your effective distribution

**$3,000 budget total**
- $1,500/month for 2 months ($50/day)
- Standard launch budget at recommended velocity
- TikTok ad credit doubles distribution to $3,000/month effective
- Apple payout arrives day 45-60, reinvest into ad spend

**$10,000+ budget total**
- $50/day for testing weeks 1-2
- Scale to $100-200/day once a winner is found
- TikTok ad credit covers a meaningful portion
- Cashflow comfortable; can scale aggressively when winner appears

**$0 budget**
- Prove profitability at $50/day for 2-3 weeks first (this needs SOME budget; either $300-500 from savings or a small loan)
- Once profitable, raise from friends and family
- This is what the founder of this playbook did

### Putting all 3 cheat codes together

A founder who:
- Applies for the Apple Small Business Program on day 1 (cuts fees from 30% → 15%)
- Uses the TikTok ad credit (doubles ad distribution to $12K)
- Plans cashflow around the 45-day Apple payout (no cash crunch)

...has a meaningfully better launch math than a founder who skips any of these. The cheat codes do not replace good ads or a good app, but they add 30-50% to the effective margin and double the ad distribution. Use all three.

## What the metrics look like across the 8-week launch

A worked sequence of what the data should look like as the launch matures:

### Week 1 (testing)
- Spend: $350 ($50/day × 7 days)
- Ads tested: 4-6
- Most ads fail; 1-2 ads showing signal
- CPA on signal ads: roughly LTV (breakeven)
- Decision: kill failing ads, isolate winning hook

### Week 2 (variant production)
- Spend: $455 ($65/day × 7 days, +30% scale)
- Variants of winning ad: 8-10
- 2-3 variants performing well alongside original
- CPA: starting to drop below LTV
- Decision: scale winners, kill weakest variants

### Week 3-4 (scaling)
- Spend: $1,200-1,800 (steady scaling)
- Profitability appearing
- LTV holding at ~$30
- CPA dropping to $20-25
- Decision: continue scaling +30% every 3 days

### Week 5-6 (saturation watch)
- Spend: $2,000-3,000
- CPA may begin creeping up as audience saturates
- Decision: produce new variants, expand to UK/Canada, or test new hook angles

### Week 7-8 (steady state)
- Spend: $3,000-5,000+
- Profitable unit economics
- Begin testing the next creative angle for the next winner
- First Apple payout arrives (day 45+)
- Cashflow loosens; can scale further

## What to do when revenue does not materialize

If after 4-6 weeks of running ads the metrics are still not green:

### Diagnose the bottleneck

Run through this in order:

1. **Are people clicking the ads?** (CTR > 1%)
   - If no: the ad creative is not working. Test new hooks, new visuals, new variants.

2. **Are clicks installing the app?** (CPI < $2)
   - If no: the App Store listing is not matching the ad promise. Update screenshots, description, app icon. The ad and the listing need to feel consistent.

3. **Are installs reaching the paywall?** (Onboarding completion rate > 60%)
   - If no: onboarding is too long, too confusing, or too hard. Simplify.

4. **Are paywall views converting?** (Paywall conversion > 3-5%)
   - If no: paywall design or pricing is wrong. Test paywall copy, pricing tiers, social proof.

5. **Are subscribers retaining?** (Cancellation rate < 30% in week 1)
   - If no: the app is not delivering on the promise of the ad. Revisit the viral feature and core experience.

The bottleneck is wherever the largest drop happens. Fix that first.

### When to call it

If after 8-12 weeks of testing with multiple creative angles and paywall variants, the unit economics still do not work:

- The concept is the problem
- The audience does not have the desire intensity you assumed
- The price point is wrong for this audience
- The viral feature is not viral enough

In that case, archive the app, take the lessons, build the next one. Most successful founders launch 2-4 apps before one hits.

The lazymaxxers approach is high-velocity, low-effort. The cost of an app that does not work is small (a few weeks of dev time and a few thousand in ad spend). The reward of an app that works is $10K+ MRR. The expected value calculation favors building the next one over over-optimizing a dead one.
