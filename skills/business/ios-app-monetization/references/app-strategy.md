# App Launch Strategy

The single biggest lever in iOS app launches is picking the right concept. Most apps that fail in production fail because of choices made before the first line of code. Get strategy right and execution becomes mechanical.

## The 3-part strategy formula

A profitable iOS app has three things working together:

1. **A core human desire** the app sells to (not a "nice to have" feature)
2. **A viral feature** that produces a "reveal moment" you can screen-record
3. **An improved version of an already-winning concept** in the niche

Take the blueprint from what's already working. Improve the execution. Add your own angle. That is how every successful app founder started, even the ones who do not admit it.

Creativity is the enemy of your first $10K MRR app. So is a copyright strike. Find the middle ground.

## Step 1: Study what is already working

There are dozens of apps printing money right now built by founders who spent years failing. Skip all that. Study the winners. Understand WHY they work.

### Apps to study

Examples of apps actively producing real revenue you can study right now:

- **Cal AI** (food scanning, $3M+/mo)
- **UMax** (face rating / looksmaxxing)
- **Pray Screen** (faith / prayer lock)

The pattern across all three:
- Each one targets a deep emotional desire
- Each one has a viral "reveal" feature
- Each one has a hard paywall at the end of onboarding

### What to extract from each app you study

Download all of them. Use them. Then capture this for each:

- **Onboarding screens** (screenshot every step)
- **Paywall screen** (screenshot the offer, the price, the layout)
- **The "aha moment"** (which screen creates the reveal that makes the user want to pay)
- **TikTok ads** (search the app name on TikTok, watch the top 5-10 ads, screen-record the formats)
- **What emotion the app targets** (write it in one sentence)
- **What viral feature the app has** (the screen-recordable reveal)

For a structured competitor study, see [../templates/competitor-study.md](../templates/competitor-study.md).

The keyword is **STUDY**. Not steal. Not clone. You are looking for patterns.

### Why direct cloning is fatal

If you directly copy someone's app:

1. Apple rejects you
2. Even if Apple does not catch it, the original founder reports you and the app gets pulled
3. Any ad spend you put behind it disappears with the app

Take the blueprint. Improve the execution. Add your own angle. Always.

## Step 2: Sell to a core human desire

Every billion dollar industry is built on the same thing: exploiting a core human desire.

- Beauty brands sell the fear of being ugly
- Insurance sells the fear of losing everything
- Dating apps sell the hope of not being alone
- Fitness brands sell the dream body you do not have yet

The only difference now is you can build an app in a weekend that does the same thing.

### The 9 core human desires that sell

| Desire | What it taps | Sample app niches |
|---|---|---|
| Attractiveness | Insecurity about appearance | Face rating, looksmaxxing, makeup scanner, skincare assessment |
| Health | Fear of decline or illness | Symptom checker, food scanner, fitness scan, sleep tracker |
| Wealth | Fear of falling behind, hope of getting ahead | Money tracker, side hustle finder, investment scanner |
| Sex | Desire and validation | Dating compatibility, attractiveness rater |
| Status | Social hierarchy and standing | Networking analyzer, peer comparison, status apps |
| Intelligence | Fear of being dumb, hope of being smart | IQ test, AI tutor, learning apps |
| Identity | Self-knowledge, self-fashioning | Personality test, soul scanner, reading apps |
| Fear | Specific threats, anxiety | Safety apps, health diagnostics, prepper apps |
| Faith | Belonging, meaning, peace | Prayer apps, meditation, religious community |

The deeper the insecurity, the better the conversion.

"Am I attractive?" will always convert better than "Am I productive?" Nobody is emotionally invested in their to-do list.

### How to test if your app targets a core desire

The test: can you describe the user's emotional state in one sentence using the word "I"?

- Strong: "I want to know if I am still attractive enough for my partner."
- Strong: "I am scared my kids will inherit my health problems."
- Strong: "I do not know if I am as smart as my friends."
- Weak: "I want to be more productive at work."
- Weak: "I want to organize my notes better."

If your app's emotional pull does not start with "I" and a strong feeling, the concept is too cold for paid ads.

## Step 3: The viral feature requirement

This is the cheat code. If you can screen-record someone using your app and show the "aha moment" in a single visual, you have a winner.

### Examples of viral features

- **Face rating**: take a selfie, get a score
- **Height predictor**: enter parents' heights, get child's predicted height
- **Food scanner**: point the camera at a meal, get calories
- **Body scanner**: upload a photo, get body fat estimate
- **Soulmate predictor**: take a selfie, get a match concept
- **Glow up timeline**: see your "best version" face

### The viral feature formula

VIRAL FEATURE + CORE HUMAN DESIRE = $$$

The viral feature is what gets shared, screen-recorded, and posted. The core human desire is what makes people pay.

Without the viral feature, the app does not spread organically and ad costs stay high forever.
Without the core human desire, the viral feature gets shares but no purchases.

You need both.

### How to design a viral feature

The viral feature is whatever produces a "reveal" the user wants to see, share, or compare with friends. Three rules:

1. **One tap to reveal.** The user should not have to do work before the reveal moment.
2. **Personal output.** The reveal is about THEM, not generic content. (A face score about their face, a body scan of their body, a prediction about their life.)
3. **Visual.** The reveal is a screen-recordable moment. Numbers, ratings, charts, side-by-side comparisons. Not text alone.

If the viral feature requires explanation before the reveal, it is too cold for TikTok.

## Step 4: The "study + improve + own angle" formula

Successful app founders almost never invent a new concept from scratch. They:

1. **Study** 3-5 winning apps in their target niche
2. **Improve** one specific dimension (better viral feature, better paywall flow, better onboarding, better target demographic)
3. **Add their own angle** (a different aesthetic, a different cultural reference, a different price point, a different audience age)

### Worked example

Concept: face rating apps.

- **Studied:** UMax, GlowUp, Looksmaxx
- **Improved:** the reveal moment (instead of just a number, show 3 dimensions: jawline, symmetry, glow)
- **Own angle:** target men over 40 (most face rating apps target Gen Z)

The result is a face rating app that is recognizable in concept but distinctly its own. Apple does not reject it. The original founders do not flag it. You launch.

## What NOT to build

Avoid these app types in your first $10K MRR launch:

- **Productivity apps.** Nobody is emotionally invested in their to-do list. Conversion is brutal.
- **Note-taking apps.** Same problem. Cold category.
- **Generic AI chat apps.** Saturated. The viral feature is unclear.
- **Anything where the core desire is "save time."** Time is not a strong enough emotional pull.
- **Anything that requires the user to enter a lot of data before the reveal.** Drop-off kills conversion.

## When to start coding

You are ready to start coding when you can answer all three questions in one sentence each:

1. What is the core human desire? (One emotion + one specific scenario)
2. What is the viral feature? (One screen-recordable reveal moment)
3. Who is the existing winning app you are improving on, and what specifically are you improving? (Name the app, name the dimension)

If any answer is fuzzy, the concept is not ready. Refine first. Code second.
