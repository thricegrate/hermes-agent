# Competitor Study Template

Structured template for studying winning iOS apps in your target niche before building your own. The output of this template feeds directly into the app concept (Mode 1 of the skill).

For the broader strategy framework, see [../references/app-strategy.md](../references/app-strategy.md).

## How to use this template

Pick 3-5 apps that are clearly making money in or adjacent to your target niche. For each app:

1. Download it (use a paid Apple ID if needed, or a sibling's account, or your own)
2. Go through the full onboarding flow
3. Take screenshots at every step
4. Note where the paywall appears and what it offers
5. Search the app name on TikTok and watch the top 5-10 ads
6. Fill in the template below

Spend 30-45 minutes per app. The output is a one-page brief per competitor that you can compare side by side.

## The template

Copy this for each competitor.

```
# Competitor Study: [App Name]

## Basics

- **App name:** [exact name as it appears in App Store]
- **Category:** [App Store category]
- **Estimated revenue:** [if known from Sensor Tower, public statements, or estimated from chart position]
- **Date studied:** [today's date]
- **Studied on:** [iPhone / iPad]

## Concept

- **One-sentence pitch:** [what does the app promise the user?]
- **Core human desire targeted:** [attractiveness / health / wealth / sex / status / intelligence / identity / fear / faith]
- **Specific emotional pull:** [what insecurity or hope does it activate? Phrase as "I want..." or "I am scared of..."]

## The viral feature

- **What is the reveal moment?** [the specific moment that produces a screen-recordable result]
- **How long does it take to reach the reveal?** [in seconds, from app open]
- **How visual is the reveal?** [photo, score, chart, animation, side-by-side, etc.]
- **Could you screen-record this and have it work as a TikTok?** [yes/no, and what frame would be the hook]

## Onboarding flow

List every screen in order. Take screenshots.

| # | Screen description | What it asks / shows |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | [splash / welcome] | [text, art, brand] |
| 2 | [first question] | [age? goal? gender?] |
| 3 | ... | ... |

How many screens before the viral feature?
How many screens before the paywall?
Total time from app open to paywall?

## Paywall

- **Pricing tier 1:** [price + period]
- **Pricing tier 2:** [price + period]
- **Pricing tier 3 (if any):** [price + period]
- **Default selected option:** [which tier]
- **Trial offered?** [yes/no, length if yes]
- **Hard paywall?** [yes/no, is there a way to skip?]
- **Social proof on paywall:** [review count, star rating, testimonial, "X million users", etc.]
- **Urgency element:** [limited time offer, "spots left", countdown, none]
- **CTA text:** [exact button text]

Screenshot the paywall. Note layout, color hierarchy, font sizes.

## Retention offer (if you can find it)

If you let the trial run or paid for a week then went to cancel, what does the cancel flow show?

- **Retention offer shown?** [yes/no]
- **Retention price:** [price + period]
- **Discount from main paywall:** [percentage]
- **Where in the cancel flow does it appear?** [first screen, second screen, after confirmation, etc.]

## TikTok ads

Search the app name on TikTok. Watch the top 5-10 ads (sorted by likes or recent).

For each ad, capture:

| # | Hook (first 2-3 seconds) | Format (before/after, POV, reveal-only, etc.) | Length | Comment-bait element |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [hook line or visual] | [format] | [seconds] | [what makes people comment] |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Note patterns:
- What hook structure repeats across the top ads?
- What visual format dominates?
- What audio is used (trending sounds, custom music, voiceover)?
- What comment-bait elements appear?

## App icon and listing

- **App icon style:** [color, design element, mood]
- **Screenshots in App Store:** [how many, what they show, video preview?]
- **App description first paragraph:** [the hook in the listing]

## What I would copy

The structural patterns I want to apply to my own app:
- [pattern 1]
- [pattern 2]
- [pattern 3]

## What I would improve

The specific dimensions where I see room to do better:
- [opportunity 1]
- [opportunity 2]
- [opportunity 3]

## My own angle

What I will do differently to avoid being a clone:
- [angle 1: aesthetic / target demographic / positioning / feature dimension]
- [angle 2]

## Verdict

- **Worth studying further?** [yes/no]
- **Direct competitor?** [yes/no]
- **Inspiration source for my app?** [yes/no, and how]
```

## After studying 3-5 apps

Once the individual studies are filled in, do a side-by-side comparison.

### Pattern extraction

Look for what is consistent across all 3-5 winners:

- What core human desire do they all target?
- What viral feature shape do they all use? (score, scan, prediction, comparison)
- What pricing tier shape do they all use?
- What ad format dominates across all of them?
- What onboarding pattern repeats?

The patterns that appear in 4 out of 5 studies are the playbook for your niche. Copy the patterns. Customize the execution.

### Differentiation map

List each competitor across the dimensions you could compete on:

| Dimension | Comp 1 | Comp 2 | Comp 3 | My app |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aesthetic | [their style] | [theirs] | [theirs] | [different choice] |
| Target demo | [theirs] | [theirs] | [theirs] | [different segment] |
| Viral feature | [their reveal] | [theirs] | [theirs] | [improved reveal] |
| Pricing | [their tiers] | [theirs] | [theirs] | [your tiers] |
| Hook angle in ads | [theirs] | [theirs] | [theirs] | [your angle] |

The columns where YOUR app differs from all 3-5 competitors is your positioning. The columns where you match are the table stakes you have to deliver.

## Worked example

Studying **UMax** for a face rating app concept:

```
# Competitor Study: UMax

## Basics
- App name: UMax (or Umax, depending on branding)
- Category: Lifestyle / Photo & Video
- Estimated revenue: $1-3M/mo (per chart position in 2025)
- Date studied: 2026-04-10
- Studied on: iPhone

## Concept
- One-sentence pitch: "Get rated on your looks and improve them with AI"
- Core human desire: Attractiveness + status
- Specific emotional pull: "I want to know how attractive I actually am" + "I want to be more attractive"

## The viral feature
- Reveal moment: a numerical attractiveness score (e.g., "7.2/10") with breakdowns by feature (jawline, symmetry, glow)
- Time to reveal: ~30 seconds (1-2 selfies + brief processing)
- Visual: numerical scores in a clean breakdown UI, photo of user with overlays
- Screen-recordable as TikTok: YES, the score reveal is the entire ad

## Onboarding (abbreviated)
1. Splash with "Discover your true score"
2. "What is your gender?" question
3. "Take a front-facing selfie"
4. "Take a side-profile selfie"
5. Loading screen ("Analyzing your bone structure...")
6. Score reveal with breakdowns
7. "See your improvement plan" CTA
8. Hard paywall

Total screens before paywall: 7
Time from open to paywall: ~90 seconds

## Paywall
- Tier 1: $6.99/week
- Tier 2: $39.99/year
- Default: Annual selected
- Trial: None
- Hard paywall: Yes
- Social proof: "10M+ downloads"
- Urgency: None visible
- CTA: "Continue"

## TikTok ads
Top 5 ads share the format:
- Hook: "What is your face score out of 10?" or "POV: you finally figured out you have not been hot since 2019"
- Format: before/after with AI-generated face transformation
- Length: 9-12 seconds
- Comment-bait: argued scores, intentional "wrong" results, demographic comparisons

## What I would copy
- The hard paywall after the reveal
- The breakdown structure (multiple sub-scores feels more accurate)
- The before/after ad format
- The annual-default pricing

## What I would improve
- The reveal could feel more dramatic (UMax is clean but not visually punchy)
- The improvement plan could be more interactive (currently it is text-heavy)
- The paywall could include more social proof (testimonials, not just download count)

## My own angle
- Target men over 40 (UMax skews younger)
- Aesthetic: editorial / magazine, not clinical
- Pricing: $12.99/week (higher; the older audience can pay more)
```

The template makes the comparison structured. After studying 3-5 apps in this format, the patterns and the gaps both become obvious.
