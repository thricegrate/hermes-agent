# Review Mining Playbook

Patterns, examples, and extraction techniques for pulling high-converting ad copy from customer reviews.

---

## Extraction Patterns

### Pattern 1: The Unexpected Reaction
Reviews where someone describes a reaction they didn't expect -- from themselves or others.

**Signal phrases:**
- "I didn't expect..."
- "To my surprise..."
- "I was shocked when..."
- "My [partner/friend/coworker] actually said..."
- "I never thought I'd be the person who..."

**Example:**
> "I didn't expect my husband to notice, but he literally grabbed my arm and said 'your skin looks amazing.' That's never happened before."

**Why it works in ads:** Unexpected reactions bypass skepticism. The reviewer wasn't trying to sell -- they were genuinely surprised.

**Best templates:** #3 (Testimonials), #15 (Social Comment Screenshot), #16 (Curiosity Gap Hook)

---

### Pattern 2: The Competitor Breakup
Reviews where the customer explicitly left a competitor for this product.

**Signal phrases:**
- "I used to use [competitor] but..."
- "After trying [competitor], [competitor], and [competitor]..."
- "I finally switched from..."
- "Nothing else worked until..."
- "I've spent [amount] on other products that..."

**Example:**
> "I've tried literally every protein powder on Amazon. Most taste like chalk mixed with sadness. This is the first one I actually look forward to drinking."

**Why it works in ads:** Competitor breakup stories are pre-made Us vs Them ad copy.

**Best templates:** #7 (Us vs Them), #25 (Color Split), #31 (Comparison Grid)

---

### Pattern 3: The Reorder Signal
Reviews mentioning repeat purchases. Strongest social proof because they're voting with their wallet.

**Signal phrases:**
- "On my [Nth] box/order/bottle"
- "Just reordered..."
- "I've been using this for [months/years]..."
- "This is a permanent part of my routine"
- "I subscribe to this now"

**Example:**
> "On my 7th box. At this point it's not even a choice, it's just what I eat for breakfast. My wife is on box 3."

**Why it works in ads:** Reorder signals are the ultimate proof of product quality. No one reorders something they don't love.

**Best templates:** #6 (Social Proof), #17 (Verified Review Card), #19 (Highlighted Testimonial)

---

### Pattern 4: The Specific Number
Reviews with exact quantities, timeframes, or measurements.

**Signal phrases:**
- Exact timeframes ("Day 3", "Week 2", "After 6 months")
- Exact measurements ("Down 2 sizes", "Lost 15 lbs", "Saved 4 hours/week")
- Exact money amounts ("Saves me $50/month", "Worth every penny of the $89")
- Usage patterns ("I use half a scoop", "One bar keeps me full for 5 hours")

**Example:**
> "Day 4 was when I noticed. By day 12, my coworkers were asking what changed. Month 3 -- I don't even recognize my old skin."

**Why it works in ads:** Specificity creates believability. "Lost weight" is generic. "Down 2 pant sizes in 6 weeks" is credible.

**Best templates:** #13 (Stat Surround), #26 (Stat Callout), #35 (Hero + Stat Bar)

---

### Pattern 5: The Skeptic Conversion
Reviews that start with doubt and end with belief.

**Signal phrases:**
- "I was skeptical..."
- "I almost returned it..."
- "I didn't believe the hype..."
- "I thought this was another gimmick..."
- "I only bought it because [reason]..."

**Example:**
> "Honestly, I thought this was overhyped Instagram nonsense. Bought it because of a dare from my friend. Three weeks later I ordered two more for my sisters."

**Why it works in ads:** Skeptic-to-believer arcs are the highest-converting narrative pattern because the reviewer has already done the objection handling for you.

**Best templates:** #9 (Negative Marketing/Bait), #11 (Pull-Quote Review), #16 (Curiosity Gap)

---

### Pattern 6: The Identity Shift
Reviews where the customer describes becoming a different person.

**Signal phrases:**
- "I feel like a new person"
- "I finally feel like myself again"
- "People keep telling me I look [different/younger/happier]"
- "I have confidence now that..."
- "I went from [old identity] to [new identity]"

**Example:**
> "I went from the guy who dreaded mirror time to someone who actually takes selfies. At 47. My wife thinks aliens replaced me."

**Why it works in ads:** Identity shifts sell the transformation, not the product. People buy the person they want to become.

**Best templates:** #8 (Before & After UGC), #21 (Bold Statement), #36 (Whiteboard Before/After)

## Scoring Framework

Rate each extracted quote on three dimensions:

### Emotional Impact (1-5)
- 1: Functional ("it works well")
- 3: Engaged ("I really like this product")
- 5: Visceral ("I literally cried when I saw the results" / "my husband won't stop talking about it")

### Specificity (1-5)
- 1: Vague ("great product")
- 3: Moderate ("noticed a difference in a few weeks")
- 5: Exact ("day 4 was when my coworker asked what changed, by week 3 I was down 2 sizes")

### Ad Template Fit (1-5)
- 1: Hard to use in any template
- 3: Works in 1-2 templates
- 5: Works across multiple template categories

**Priority score = Emotional Impact x Specificity x Template Fit**

Quotes scoring 60+ (out of 125) are A-tier ad copy. Anything above 30 is usable.

## Source-Specific Tips

### Amazon Reviews
- Sort by "Most Recent" (not "Top Reviews") for fresher language
- Check "Verified Purchase" only -- filters out fake reviews
- Look at 3-star reviews for the most balanced, specific language
- Check Q&A section for objection language

### Shopify/DTC Product Pages
- Reviews often less polished = more authentic language
- Photo reviews are higher quality (customers who bother with photos care more)
- Look for review responses from the brand for context

### Social Media Comments
- Instagram comments under brand posts or influencer posts
- TikTok comments on product reviews (the comments are often better copy than the video)
- Reddit threads about the product category (search r/[niche])
- Facebook group discussions mentioning the product

### App Store Reviews
- Best for SaaS and digital products
- Look at "Most Helpful" reviews
- Feature request reviews often contain pain language about competitors

---

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Extract

- **Generic praise:** "Great product!" "Love it!" "5 stars!" (too vague, no ad value)
- **Company-planted reviews:** Look for suspiciously similar language across reviews
- **Irrelevant complaints:** Shipping issues, wrong size, etc. (not product-related)
- **Reviews that can't be verified:** Claims that seem fabricated or exaggerated
- **Competitor bashing without substance:** "Brand X sucks" without specifics isn't useful

## Workflow: Reviews to Ads in 30 Minutes

1. **Collect** (10 min): Gather 50+ reviews from 1-3 sources
2. **Scan** (10 min): Read all reviews, flag anything that hits a pattern above
3. **Score** (5 min): Rate flagged quotes using the scoring framework
4. **Format** (5 min): Fill in the review mining report template
5. **Hand off** to `ads-designer` batch mode with top quotes mapped to templates
