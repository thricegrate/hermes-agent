# Kennedy Funnel Economics

Dan Kennedy's direct response frameworks applied to funnel diagnosis and optimization. These layer on top of the existing economics framework and bottleneck diagnosis.

---

## "Compete on Economics" (Framework 16)

Kennedy's core funnel principle: the business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins. Not the best product. Not the best content. The one with the best economics.

### Why This Matters for Funnel Diagnosis

Most funnel problems aren't conversion problems. They're economics problems. A funnel converting at 2% with a $500 LTV is more powerful than one converting at 5% with a $50 LTV.

**The math that matters:**

```
Maximum Affordable CPA = Full-Stack LTV x Target ROI margin

Example:
Frontend LTV:    $47 (product) + $12 (OTO1 at 20%) + $5 (OTO2 at 8%) = $64
Backend LTV:     25% of buyers x $3,000 high-ticket = $750 per 100 buyers = $7.50/buyer
Full-Stack LTV:  $64 + $7.50 = $71.50
At 2x ROI:       Max CPA = $35.75
At 3x ROI:       Max CPA = $23.83
```

If your competitor can only afford a $15 CPA and you can afford $35, you win every auction, every placement, every ad slot. You don't need better ads. You need better economics.

### Revenue Distribution Rule

Kennedy's rule: 2/3 to 3/4 of revenue should come from the back end. If your front end is your main revenue source, you're running a lemonade stand, not a business.

**Diagnosis questions:**
- What percentage of total revenue comes from front-end sales? (If >50%, economics problem)
- What's your back-end offer? (If "none," that's the bottleneck)
- What's the ascension rate from front end to back end? (If <10%, awareness bridge problem)

## Follow-Up Sequence Economics (10 Rules, Rule 6)

Kennedy's Rule 6: "Develop follow-up sequences." Most funnels lose money because they give up too early.

### The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up

**Kennedy's numbers:** 80% of sales happen after the 5th contact. Most businesses quit after the 2nd.

**Follow-up sequence economics model:**

```
Email 1 (Day 0, purchase confirmation):     0% additional revenue
Emails 2-3 (Days 1-3, value delivery):      5-10% of non-buyers convert
Emails 4-5 (Days 4-7, objection handling):  3-5% of remaining convert
Emails 6-7 (Days 8-14, social proof):       2-3% of remaining convert
Emails 8-10 (Days 15-30, scarcity):         1-2% of remaining convert
SECOND NOTICE (Day 31+):                    1-3% surprise conversions

Total recovery from follow-up:              15-25% of initial non-buyers
```

**SECOND NOTICE pattern:** Kennedy's follow-up technique. After the initial sequence ends, send a "second notice" email formatted like an official reminder. Subject line: "SECOND NOTICE: [offer name]." This pattern consistently pulls 1-3% of dead leads back in.

### Follow-Up ROI Calculation

```
Non-buyers after initial offer:     1,000 leads
Follow-up conversion rate:          18% (conservative)
Additional customers:               180
Average order value:                 $97
Additional revenue from follow-up:  $17,460
Cost of follow-up (email):          ~$50 (platform costs)
ROI on follow-up:                   349x
```

If you're not running follow-up sequences, this is almost certainly your biggest funnel leak.

## Scarcity Per Funnel Stage (Framework 9)

Scarcity isn't just for the sales page. Kennedy deploys it at every stage of the funnel.

### 5 Scarcity Types

1. **Deadline:** Specific date and time. "Closes Friday at midnight."
2. **Limited quantity:** Real cap. "Only 50 spots available." Must be genuine.
3. **Disappearing bonuses:** "The AI Prompt Library bonus is only available until Thursday."
4. **Rising price:** "Price goes up by $50 after the early bird window."
5. **Social scarcity:** "Not everyone qualifies. Application required."

### Scarcity Mapped to Funnel Stages

| Funnel Stage | Scarcity Type | Example |
|-------------|--------------|---------|
| Lead magnet | Disappearing bonus | "Download in the next 24 hours and get the bonus checklist" |
| Email nurture | Rising price | "Current subscribers get founding member pricing" |
| Sales page | Deadline + quantity | "Enrollment closes March 21. 50 spots." |
| Cart/checkout | Disappearing bonus | "Complete your order now to keep the Fast-Action Bonus" |
| Post-purchase OTO | Time-limited | "This offer disappears when you leave this page" |
| Re-engagement | Social scarcity | "We reopened 10 spots for existing subscribers only" |

### Scarcity Escalation Sequence

For any time-bound offer:
1. **Full offer email** (Day 0): Present the complete offer with deadline
2. **Reminder** (3 days before deadline): "Quick reminder, the [bonus] disappears in 3 days"
3. **24-hour warning** (Day before): "Last 24 hours to get [offer] at this price"
4. **Last chance** (Deadline day): "Closing tonight at midnight"
5. **Closed** (Day after): "We're closed. Here's what happened" (creates FOMO for next launch)

## Message-Market-Media Alignment Per Funnel Stage (Framework 2)

Kennedy's triangle: MESSAGE, MARKET, MEDIA. Most funnels break because one leg of the triangle is misaligned at a specific stage.

### How to Diagnose Misalignment

| If This Is Broken | You'll See | Fix |
|-------------------|-----------|-----|
| Wrong MESSAGE for the stage | High traffic, low conversion. People arrive but don't take action. | Rewrite copy to match where they are in the buyer's journey. Top of funnel = problem aware. Bottom = solution aware. |
| Wrong MARKET | High conversion rate but low volume. Or high volume but wrong people buying. | Audit traffic sources. Are you reaching the high-probability target? |
| Wrong MEDIA | Low reach despite good message and clear market. | Go where your market actually gathers. Not where you prefer to post. |

### Stage-Specific Message Guidelines

**Top of funnel (awareness):**
Message speaks to their problem in their language. Not your solution language. Not industry jargon. The words they use when complaining to friends.

**Middle of funnel (consideration):**
Message bridges from problem to solution. Show proof. Case studies. "Here's someone who had your exact problem."

**Bottom of funnel (decision):**
Message is about the offer, not the problem. They already believe. Now give them the stack, the guarantee, the deadline, and the reason to act today.

### Market Diagnosis: Kennedy's 10 Smart Questions

Before optimizing any funnel, answer these about your target market:

1. What keeps them awake at night, staring at the ceiling?
2. What are they afraid of?
3. What are they angry about? (Who are they angry at?)
4. What are their top 3 daily frustrations?
5. What trends are happening in their world right now?
6. What do they secretly desire most?
7. Do they have a built-in decision-making bias? (Analytical? Emotional? Social proof dependent?)
8. Do they have their own language or jargon?
9. Who else is selling something similar to them? How are they doing it?
10. Who tried to sell them something similar and failed? Why did it fail?

If you can't answer at least 7 of these, you don't know your market well enough to optimize the funnel. The copy will be generic, the targeting will be broad, and the conversions will be mediocre.

## Kennedy Funnel Economics Checklist

Add this to every funnel diagnosis:

- [ ] Full-stack LTV calculated (frontend + OTOs + backend)
- [ ] Maximum affordable CPA determined
- [ ] Back-end revenue is 50%+ of total (if not, economics problem)
- [ ] Follow-up sequence exists and runs 7+ emails
- [ ] SECOND NOTICE deployed on dead leads
- [ ] Scarcity present at every funnel stage (not just sales page)
- [ ] Message-Market-Media aligned at each stage
- [ ] Can answer 7+ of Kennedy's 10 market diagnosis questions
