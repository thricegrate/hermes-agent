# Funnel Bottleneck Analysis (Cole/Bush)

Source: Nicolas Cole & Dickie Bush (Ship 30 for 30), Low-Ticket Product M&M Course, Module 9

---

## 3-Step Bottleneck Analysis Framework

1. **Track each individual number.** You cannot improve something you are not actively measuring. "What gets measured gets improved."
2. **Look for the easiest number to "double."** Doubling ANY single metric in your funnel doubles the entire business. Doubling all of them compounds (e.g., doubling 3 metrics = 8x).
3. **List all initiatives that could double that number, then stack-rank by highest impact + lowest effort.** Execute the top-ranked item first. There is no perfect answer -- it is a hypothesis you test.

Key insight: Going from 3% to 6% conversion is not "a 3% increase" -- it is a 100% increase in sales. Think in multiples, not absolute points.

## 5 Funnel Metrics with Benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Great | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|
| Impression/Visit Ratio | 2% | 3% | -- |
| Newsletter Opt-In Rate | 30% | 40% | 50%+ |
| Subscriber/Product Visit Ratio | 5% | 10% | 10-30% (small lists) |
| Product LP Conversion Rate | 3% | 5% | 5-10% |

### The Math: Same 100K Impressions, Different Outcomes

**"Good" funnel (all metrics at Good):**
- 100,000 impressions x 2% = 2,000 opt-in page visitors
- 2,000 x 30% = 600 subscribers
- 600 x 5% = 30 product page visits
- 30 x 3% = **1 sale**

**"Great" funnel (all metrics at Great):**
- 100,000 impressions x 3% = 3,000 visitors
- 3,000 x 40% = 1,200 subscribers
- 1,200 x 10% = 120 product page visits
- 120 x 5% = **6 sales** (600% increase)

**"Elite" funnel (all metrics at Elite):**
- Same 100K impressions through a fully optimized funnel = **30 sales** (30x increase over Good)

**Elite funnel + 10x traffic (1M impressions):**
- = **300 sales**
- Compare: 1M impressions through "Good" funnel = only ~10 sales
- Proves that optimizing the funnel before scaling traffic is critical

## Funnel Math Formula

```
Monthly Sales = Social Traffic Impressions
  x Impression/Visit Ratio
  x Newsletter Opt-In Rate
  x Subscriber/Product Visit Ratio
  x Product Page Conversion Rate
```

## Priority Matrix

For every bottleneck, list all possible improvements and rank each by two axes:

- **Highest Impact** -- how much will this move the metric?
- **Lowest Effort** -- how quickly/cheaply can you execute?

Do the high-impact, low-effort items first. Skip low-impact work until the high-leverage moves are done.

Example for LP conversion bottleneck:
- Adding images to landing page: low effort, low impact
- Rewriting entire landing page top to bottom: high effort, maybe high impact
- Adding testimonials: low effort, high impact (do this first)

## Fix Playbooks Per Bottleneck

### If Impression/Visit Ratio is below 1% (bottleneck)
- Add more CTAs to social content
- X: use autoplugs on high-performing tweets
- LinkedIn: add link in post or first comment
- Instagram: "check link in bio" at end of posts
- Keep adding CTAs until you hit the 1-3% sweet spot

### If Newsletter Opt-In Rate is below 30% (bottleneck)
Three high-impact, low-effort fixes:

1. **Fix CTA-to-landing-page congruence.** If your social CTA promises "weekly crypto deep dives" but the landing page says "real estate market," that mismatch kills conversions. The CTA promise must match the landing page headline exactly.
2. **Page speed under 3 seconds.** Ship 30's own pages were loading at 9 seconds -- a major conversion killer. Test at pagespeed.web.dev.
3. **Add tangibility/mockups.** Show the product visually -- Skool modules, video previews, book covers, course graphics. Makes the "trade" (email for content) feel more valuable. This was Ship 30's single biggest opt-in page upgrade.

### If Newsletter Opt-In Rate is above 50%
- Do not touch it. Find another bottleneck. Even above 30%, the bottleneck is probably elsewhere.
- Cole/Bush note: they did not start optimizing opt-in pages until after $15M in lifetime revenue. Focus elsewhere first.

### If Subscriber/Product Visit Ratio is the bottleneck
1. **Single CTA per email** -- point every link to the same destination (your product page). Simplifies the email and simplifies click tracking.
2. **Pre-S at the top and PS at the bottom** -- the two most-read parts of every email are above-the-fold preview and the PS. Put product CTAs in both spots.
3. **Write better emails.** If the content genuinely educates, readers want the next step. Every email gets a CTA. No exceptions.

### If LP Conversion Rate is below 1% (bottleneck)
- You likely did not follow the landing page template. Go back to the template.
- Add testimonials as you collect them.
- Expand FAQs with real "on the fence" objection emails you have received.
- Above 1%, this is probably not your bottleneck. Ship 30 converted at ~5% and barely touched the page in 4 years and $7M+ in revenue.

## Specific Benchmarks from Ship 30

- **70% opt-in rate** at startwritingonline.com (their best-performing page)
- **30.7% opt-in rate** on a YouTube-specific landing page (361 opt-ins from 1.2K views)
- **~5% LP conversion rate** for Ship 30 for 30 product page (maintained for 4+ years with minimal changes)
- **$7M+ revenue** from a landing page that is "almost the exact same" as the original
- Only changes over 4 years: more testimonials, expanded FAQs from buyer objection emails, migrated to Webflow for better graphics

## 6-Step Action Plan

1. **Download the analytics tracker** and bookmark it to your browser bar
2. **Set up daily metric tracking** -- add hyperlinks to each data source in the sheet so you can click through one by one
3. **Identify your bottleneck** -- which metric is furthest (percentage-wise) from the "Good" benchmark? That is your bottleneck
4. **List all possible improvements** for that bottleneck (start with the recommended upgrades above)
5. **Execute highest impact + lowest effort first** and watch the metrics in your tracker
6. **Repeat indefinitely** -- once one bottleneck improves, find the next one. This is how you build a massive digital products business

## Key Principles

- **Measure before optimizing.** You would be shocked how many businesses doing hundreds of thousands or millions in revenue do not know their numbers.
- **Traffic is rarely the bottleneck.** Most creators default to "create more content" when the real bottleneck is a leaky funnel. 1M impressions through a bad funnel = ~10 sales. 100K through an elite funnel = 30 sales.
- **Fix the funnel before scaling traffic.** Pouring water into a leaky bucket is not a sustainable strategy.
- **Think in doublings, not points.** A 3% to 6% jump is a 100% business improvement.
- **The push-pull game.** Running a digital product business is a constant back and forth between generating more social traffic and improving funnel metrics. Unless your funnel is elite, improve both in parallel.
