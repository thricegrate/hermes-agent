# Newsletter Ad Style Guide

Visual pattern analysis distilled from 21 high-performing HubSpot newsletter ad creatives. Use this reference when constructing image generation prompts.

## Color Palettes

### Primary Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Orange/Coral | #FF6B35 | CTA accents, circles, highlights |
| Navy | #1B2838 | Dark backgrounds, headline text |
| Cream | #FFF5EB | Light backgrounds |
| Hot Pink | #E91E8C | Accent elements, badges |
| Gold | #FFB800 | Price highlights, star ratings |
| White | #FFFFFF | Text on dark backgrounds |
| Light Gray | #B0B0B0 | Subtitles, secondary text |

### Template-Specific Palettes

**Clean Ebook / Bold Product (warm):**
- Background: cream (#FFF5EB) or off-white (#FAF7F2)
- Headline: navy (#1B2838) or dark charcoal (#2D2D2D)
- Accent shapes: orange (#FF6B35) circles
- Subtitle: gray (#666666)

**Dark Authority:**
- Background: dark navy (#1A2332) or near-black (#0F1923)
- Headline: white (#FFFFFF)
- Accents: coral (#FF6B5A) swoosh lines, horizontal stripes
- Subtitle: light gray (#B0B8C4)

**YouTube Thumbnail (bold):**
- Background: dark textured (#1A1A2E) or charcoal gradient
- Text: white or bright yellow (#FFE600)
- Accents: red (#FF3333), yellow badges, sticker elements
- Outline text or shadow text for impact

**Split Diagonal:**
- Side A: navy (#1B2838) or sage green (#7A9E7E)
- Side B: gold (#FFB800) or mauve (#C4A0B0)
- Text: white on dark side, navy on light side

**Warm Minimal:**
- Background: light cream (#FFFBF5) or very light peach
- Text: navy or dark charcoal
- Accents: minimal orange (#FF6B35) underlines or dots

## Typography

### Headlines
- **Style**: Bold serif typeface (similar to Playfair Display, Lora Bold, or Georgia Bold)
- **Weight**: 700-900 (extra bold)
- **Case**: Title case (not ALL CAPS, except YouTube Thumbnail style)
- **Size**: Large, dominant element in the composition
- **Color**: Navy on light backgrounds, white on dark backgrounds

### Subtitles
- **Style**: Clean sans-serif (similar to Inter, Source Sans, or Helvetica)
- **Weight**: 400-500 (regular to medium)
- **Case**: Sentence case
- **Size**: Roughly 40-50% of headline size
- **Color**: Gray (#666) on light, light gray (#B0B8C4) on dark

### YouTube Thumbnail Exception
- **Headlines**: Bold sans-serif, ALL CAPS
- **Style**: Impact-style or compressed bold
- **Effects**: May include text outline or drop shadow for readability

## Shape and Accent Elements

### Orange Circles
- Large solid circle (roughly 30-40% of image height)
- Positioned behind mockup, slightly off-center
- Color: #FF6B35 or #FF7A45
- Used in: Clean Ebook, Bold Product

### Horizontal Stripe Groups
- 3-4 thin parallel lines
- Usually coral/orange (#FF6B5A)
- Decorative, positioned in corners or beside mockups
- Used in: Dark Authority, Bold Product

### Curved Swoosh Accents
- Organic curved lines or comma-shaped swooshes
- Coral or orange
- Often paired with Dark Authority layout
- Positioned around device mockups

### Geometric Circles (multiple)
- 2-3 overlapping circles of different colors
- Orange, yellow (#FFB800), and lighter orange
- Used in: Bold Product as background elements

### Sticker/Badge Elements (YouTube Thumbnail only)
- "FREE" badge, arrow stickers, star ratings
- Red, yellow, white colors
- Collage-style overlay placement

## Mockup Types by Product

| Product | Mockup Style | Positioning |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Ebook/Guide | 3D book cover, slightly tilted (10-15 degrees) | Right side, overlapping accent circle |
| Report | Flat document pages, slightly fanned | Right side or stacked |
| Tool/Software | Laptop or monitor screen showing UI | Right side, angled slightly |
| Templates | Angled ebook or document stack | Right side with geometric shapes |
| Course | Laptop showing video player or curriculum | Right side |
| Checklist | Clipboard or document with checkmarks | Right side, floating |
| Playbook | Document pages floating with slight shadow | Right side with whitespace |

### Mockup Rules
- Always position mockup on the RIGHT side (except YouTube Thumbnail)
- Mockup should have subtle drop shadow for depth
- Tilt ebook covers 10-15 degrees for 3D effect
- Show partial content on screens (blurred or placeholder text)
- Never show real brand names or logos on mockup screens

## 6 Layout Templates (Expanded)

### 1. Clean Ebook

**Composition:**
- Left 55%: headline (bold serif) + subtitle (sans-serif) + optional "Free Guide" tag
- Right 45%: 3D ebook mockup with large orange circle behind it
- Background: uniform cream/off-white

**Key elements:**
- Large orange circle (#FF6B35) behind the ebook, partially cropped by right edge
- Ebook cover tilted 10-15 degrees
- Ebook shows a simplified cover design (title + abstract graphic)
- Plenty of padding on left side for text breathing room

**Best for:** Ebooks, guides, reports, free downloads

### 2. Dark Authority

**Composition:**
- Left 55%: white serif headline + gray subtitle stacked below
- Right 45%: device mockup (laptop or monitor) showing content
- Background: solid dark navy (#1A2332)

**Key elements:**
- Decorative coral swoosh or curved accent near the mockup
- 3-4 thin horizontal stripe lines (coral) as corner decoration
- Device screen shows blurred/abstract content
- High contrast: white text on dark background

**Best for:** Tools, premium courses, software, authority-building offers

### 3. Bold Product

**Composition:**
- Left 55%: extra-large bold serif headline (can be 2-3 lines)
- Right 45%: angled ebook cover overlapping orange + yellow circles
- Background: cream base

**Key elements:**
- 2-3 overlapping geometric circles (orange, yellow, light orange) behind mockup
- Ebook cover angled more aggressively (15-20 degrees)
- Optional small icon element (book emoji, lightning bolt)
- Headline is the dominant visual element

**Best for:** Template packs, prompt collections, swipe files, bundles

### 4. YouTube Thumbnail

**Composition:**
- Full-bleed dark/textured background
- Large sans-serif ALL CAPS text (centered or left-aligned)
- Collage of cutout images, icons, arrows, stickers scattered around text
- Background: dark texture, gradient, or abstract pattern

**Key elements:**
- Red + yellow accent stickers, badges, arrows
- "FREE" badge or similar call-to-action overlay
- Cutout images of relevant objects (laptops, phones, documents)
- Intentionally "busy" and attention-grabbing (unlike other templates)

**Best for:** Annual reports, viral content, bold claims, data-heavy offers

### 5. Split Diagonal

**Composition:**
- Diagonal line splits image into two color zones (roughly 55/45)
- Left zone (darker): serif headline in white
- Right zone (lighter): stacked screenshots or document previews
- Background: two-tone diagonal

**Key elements:**
- Clean diagonal cut (top-left to bottom-right, or similar angle)
- Color pairs: navy + gold, sage green + mauve, dark teal + peach
- Screenshots/documents stacked with slight rotation and shadow
- Text stays on one side, visuals on the other

**Best for:** Prompt collections, multi-page resources, comparison content

### 6. Warm Minimal

**Composition:**
- Left 50%: serif headline + subtitle with generous whitespace
- Right 50%: floating screenshots or document pages, optional person photo
- Background: very light cream, almost white

**Key elements:**
- Maximum whitespace (most "airy" of all templates)
- Floating elements with soft shadows (no hard edges)
- Optional: small person headshot (authentic, not stock-looking)
- Minimal orange accents (a single underline, a small dot, a thin line)

**Best for:** Playbooks, courses, professional guides, coaching offers

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use bold serif fonts for headlines (Playfair Display style)
- Include quantified value in headlines (numbers: 50, 100+, 200, 1000+)
- Position mockups on the right side
- Use orange/coral as the primary accent color
- Keep text to 2 elements max (headline + subtitle)
- Add the NO logos/NO brand names exclusion block to every prompt
- Specify exact text content in the prompt
- Start prompts with aspect ratio

### Don't
- Use more than 2-3 colors per ad
- Put text on both sides (pick a side for text, other for visuals)
- Use ALL CAPS except in YouTube Thumbnail style
- Include real brand logos or company names
- Overcrowd the layout (whitespace is a feature)
- Use em dashes in any copy
- Skip the review step before delivering images
- Use generic stock photo backgrounds
- Add borders or frames (ads sit inline in email, borders look dated)
