# Niche-Within-Niche Framework Reference

## The Drill-Down Method

Start broad, get specific. Each dimension narrows your niche further.

### 6 Drill-Down Dimensions

1. **Industry** - The broad sector (Healthcare, Finance, Education, Tech, Fitness)
2. **Demographic** - Who specifically (Remote Workers, Single Mothers, Gen Z, Retirees)
3. **Physical Location** - Where (Chicago, Rural Areas, East Coast, Southeast Asia)
4. **Digital Platform** - Where they hang out (LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, Substack)
5. **Price/Socioeconomic** - Budget constraints (Bootstrapped Startups, Enterprise, Budget-Conscious)
6. **Distribution Method** - How they consume (On Zoom, Self-Paced, In-Person, Async)

### Examples of Niche Drill-Down

**Example 1: Writing**
- Mega-Category: Writing
- Niche: Internet Writing
- Niche-Within-Niche: Internet Writing For Beginners

**Example 2: Healthcare**
- Mega-Category: Healthcare
- Niche: Physical Therapy
- Niche-Within-Niche: Physical Therapy For Remote Workers On LinkedIn

**Example 3: Finance**
- Mega-Category: Finance
- Niche: Personal Finance
- Niche-Within-Niche: Budgeting For Freelancers Who Use Notion

## 5-Point Validation Checklist (Detailed)

### Criterion 1: Problem you've successfully solved for yourself
- You must have personal experience solving this problem
- "I read about it" doesn't count - you lived it
- Your personal story becomes your credibility and your best marketing content
- If you haven't solved it: pick a different problem

### Criterion 2: Problem you genuinely care about solving
- It should bother you that this problem exists for others
- You'd work on this even if no one paid you (at least initially)
- If it doesn't excite you: you'll burn out before the revenue kicks in
- Passion sustains you through the first 6-12 months of building

### Criterion 3: Can be objectively measured
- You can define clear before/after metrics
- "Feel better" is not measurable. "Write 30 articles in 30 days" IS measurable.
- Measurable outcomes = better marketing copy, better testimonials, better product
- If you can't measure it: you haven't gotten specific enough about the problem

### Criterion 4: Requires more than just "an answer"
- If someone can Google the answer, it's not a product-worthy problem
- The problem should require implementation, practice, or behavior change
- Products that require action = higher perceived value = worth paying for
- If a single blog post solves it: it's not a $350 problem

### Criterion 5: Correlates to Money, Time, or Status
- **Money:** Helps them earn more or stop losing money
- **Time:** Saves them significant hours/effort
- **Status:** Unlocks credibility, recognition, opportunities
- These are the only 3 things people consistently pay to solve
- If your problem doesn't correlate: reframe it until it does, or pick a different problem

## Problem Brainstorming Prompts

Use these to help identify the Top 10 Problems:
- "What questions do people in [niche] ask most often?"
- "What are people in [niche] constantly complaining about?"
- "What did YOU struggle with most when you were starting in [niche]?"
- "What mistakes do beginners in [niche] make that cost them time/money?"
- "What would make someone in [niche] say 'I would pay anything to fix this'?"
- "What keeps people in [niche] stuck at their current level?"
- "What's the #1 reason people in [niche] quit or give up?"
- "What tool, template, or system would save people in [niche] the most time?"
