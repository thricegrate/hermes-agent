# Cole's Niching Down System (Low-Ticket Launchpad)

> Source: Nicolas Cole, Low-Ticket Launchpad course
> Module: 2 - Low-Ticket Product Idea Generation
> Context: Systematic framework for niching down from broad industry to specific niche-within-niche. Use when helping clients/users find their product niche, identify target problems, and map desirable outcomes. Includes AI prompts for accelerated brainstorming.

---

## Core Principle: The Specificity Game

Everything starts at the mega-category level and gets more specific from there.

The challenge: people start brainstorming in giant mega-categories (Healthcare, Technology, Productivity, Financial Services, Health & Wellness). These are HUGE. Trying to pinpoint a problem inside a mega-category is very challenging because inside each one are:

- Different kinds of people
- Each faced with different problems
- Looking to achieve different goals

**You MUST niche down to create high-quality product ideas. Broader = vaguer ideas. More specific = better ideas.**

"The riches are in the niches." One of the biggest mistakes people make is trying to create something for everyone -- and as a result, they create something for no one.

### The Progression

```
Mega-Category -> Niche -> Niche-Within-Niche
```

Example: Healthcare -> Physical Therapy -> Physical Therapy For Remote Workers

---

## Step 1: Six Methods to Niche Down

Each method takes a mega-category, picks a sub-category (niche), then applies a specific lens to reach the niche-within-niche.

All examples use Healthcare -> Physical Therapy as the base:

| # | Method | Lens | Niche-Within-Niche Example |
|---|--------|------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **By Industry** | Pick a sub-category within the mega-category | Healthcare -> Physical Therapy |
| 2 | **By Demographic** | Pick a specific demographic | Physical Therapy For Remote Workers |
| 3 | **By Physical Location** | Pick a specific geography | Physical Therapists In Chicago |
| 4 | **By Digital Platform** | Pick a specific platform | Physical Therapists On LinkedIn |
| 5 | **By Price** | Pick a price range or socioeconomic status | Physical Therapy For Single Mothers On Food Stamps |
| 6 | **By Distribution** | Pick a specific form of delivery | Physical Therapy On Zoom |

**Pro Tip:** Your niche-within-niche should be something you have personally experienced. This is the easiest place to start because chances are YOU have experienced these very same problems (and ideally found solutions to them for yourself).

### Real-World Example: Ship 30 for 30

- **Mega-Category:** Writing
- **Niche:** Internet Writing (later coined "Digital Writing")
- **Niche-Within-Niche:** Internet Writing For Beginners

What would have killed Ship 30 from the start: saying "we help ALL writers!" They don't help Pulitzer novelists, journalists, or Amazon entrepreneurs. They got specific: "we help beginner writers start writing online."

---

## Step 2: The 10 Biggest Problems Methodology

**DO NOT start this step until you have Mega-Category -> Niche -> Niche-Within-Niche locked in.** If you skip the specificity exercise, you get low-quality ideas.

List the 10 biggest problems in your chosen niche-within-niche.

### Value Filter

Problems must help the target customer in some way to:

- **Make more money**
- **Save money** (or stop losing money)
- **Save a lot of time**
- **Unlock some form of status or industry recognition**

Not all problems are created equal. All 10 problems might be valid, might exist, and might be painful and worth solving -- but not all of them will be as easy to get paid to solve.

### Example: Ship 30 for 30's 10 Biggest Problems (Writing for Beginners)

1. Distractions
2. Over-Editing
3. Perfectionism
4. Procrastination
5. Self-Confidence
6. Generating Ideas
7. Imposter Syndrome
8. Writing Consistently
9. Choosing A Platform
10. Finding Time To Write

---

## Step 3: The 10 Most Desirable Outcomes

For each problem, write the outcome as an "I" statement. Put yourself in the shoes of the customer.

Format: "If I wasn't [problem], I would [desired outcome]"

### Full Example: Ship 30 for 30's Problem-to-Outcome Mappings

1. **Distractions** -> "If I wasn't distracted, I would be so much more productive."
2. **Over-Editing** -> "If I wasn't constantly over-editing, I would save myself hundreds of hours and publish way more often."
3. **Perfectionism** -> "If I wasn't such a perfectionist, I would get out of my own way, publish more, and maybe even live my dream of being a full-time writer."
4. **Procrastination** -> "If I stopped procrastinating, I would finally start making progress toward my writing goals."
5. **Self-Confidence** -> "If I had self-confidence, I would finally share the stories and insights and frameworks I think are valuable but have been afraid to write and publish."
6. **Generating Ideas** -> "If I had a reliable system for generating ideas, I wouldn't feel so reliant on my intuition and so easily talk myself out of writing anytime I was struggling to come up with things to say."
7. **Imposter Syndrome** -> "If I didn't have Imposter Syndrome, I would stop comparing myself to other people, publish more often, and just generally feel happier and more fulfilled with my path in life."
8. **Writing Consistently** -> "If I was writing consistently, my audience would grow faster, my library would grow faster, and I would achieve my goals as a writer way faster."
9. **Choosing A Platform** -> "If I knew which platform to write on, I could finally start my journey of writing online."
10. **Finding Time To Write** -> "If I had more time to write, I would be able to write consistently, grow my audience and library faster, and feel like I was on the path to achieving my goals."

## Step 4: Pick the Most Valuable Problem

Lead with ONE problem for positioning. If you try to lead with all 10, the customer doesn't know which one is the priority. (If they're "all" the priority, then "none of them" are the priority.)

Your product can solve multiple problems, but POSITION around the single most valuable one. Speak to the other problems on the landing page, in marketing materials, in emails and social content.

### Most Valuable Problem Checklist

Run each of your 10 problems through these 5 criteria:

- [ ] **Solved it yourself** -- Is a problem you've successfully solved for yourself
- [ ] **Genuine care** -- Is a problem you genuinely care about solving (it should bother you this problem exists in the world)
- [ ] **Objectively measurable** -- Is a problem that can be OBJECTIVELY measured (meaning you can "prove" that you've solved it)
- [ ] **Requires implementation** -- Is a problem that requires more than just "an answer" in order to solve (meaning some kind of implementation)
- [ ] **Correlated to money/time/status** -- Is a problem that is in some way correlated to either MAKING MONEY, NOT LOSING MONEY, SAVING A TON OF TIME, or UNLOCKING STATUS

### Checklist in Action: "Choosing A Platform" (4/5)

- Solved it yourself? YES -- learned blogs are terrible, X and LinkedIn are better
- Genuine care? YES
- Objectively measurable? YES -- binary, you either switch platforms or you don't
- Requires implementation? NO -- "Start writing on X or LinkedIn" is basically the answer
- Correlated to money/time/status? YES -- audiences on X and LinkedIn led to millions in sales

### Checklist in Action: "Writing Consistently" (5/5) -- WINNER

- Solved it yourself? YES -- life-changing outcomes from daily writing
- Genuine care? YES
- Objectively measurable? YES -- binary, you're either writing every day or not
- Requires implementation? YES -- requires breaking faulty beliefs, education, and accountability
- Correlated to money/time/status? YES -- consistently writing online generated over $15M in revenue

### The Money Anchor Test

Not all problems are created equal in dollar value. Example comparison:

- Problem A: Not knowing how to use Excel as a college student
- Problem B: Not knowing how to automate monthly financials as a marketing agency doing $100K/mo

Both are real problems. But Problem B is WORTH significantly more because it's directly correlated to helping a business save time and money.

**Anytime your digital product helps people MAKE MORE MONEY, NOT LOSE MONEY, SAVE TIME, OR UNLOCK STATUS, it's easier for customers to rationalize buying it -- because they can imagine a clear ROI.**

### Reframing Non-Money Niches

If your niche doesn't obviously connect to money, you have 3 options:

1. Pick a different niche
2. Pick a different problem
3. Get creative with how you anchor the value

Example: "15-minute yoga routines for remote employees" with problem "not having enough time to do yoga."

Reframe the problem: "Suffering from back pain from sitting all day." Now anchor to:
- How much money they spend on physical therapy
- How much money they'll spend later on disc herniation treatment
- How unproductive they are because they're in pain all day

Or reframe the angle: "How much is your monthly yoga studio membership that you never use costing you?"

There's usually nothing wrong with the niche itself. You just haven't found the right angle to get the target customer to recognize the value.

## AI Prompts

### Prompt 1: Niche Exploration (Identify Niche-Within-Niche)

```
I am working on creating a low-ticket digital product (priced at $350, text and video course), but first I need some help picking a specific niche-within-a-niche.

Here is a training on how this exercise works, and why it's so important.

~

One of the challenges people have when it comes to creating digital products (and identifying meaningful problems to solve) is they start their brainstorming in a giant mega-category.

Things like:

- Healthcare
- Technology
- Productivity
- Financial Services
- Health & Wellness
- Etc.

But it's worth recognizing these mega-categories are HUGE.

And trying to pinpoint "a problem" (or even a list of problems) inside one of these mega-categories is very, very challenging.

Why?

Because inside each mega-category are:

- Different kinds of people
- Each of whom are faced with different problems
- Looking to achieve different goals

Take "healthcare" for example.

Inside "healthcare" we could be talking about/to:

- Doctors
- Physical therapists
- Healthcare executives
- Healthcare startup founders
- Healthcare insurance providers
- Etc.

And each of these different "audiences" have VERY different problems.

Which means the first step is to niche down.

Ideally:

- Mega-Category →
- Niche →
- Niche-Within-Niche

It's a game of specificity.

### How To Niche Down

So, let's make this as actionable as possible.

How do you niche down?

- **By Industry: Take the mega-category and pick a sub-category within it.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy."
- **By Demographic: Take the mega-category, pick a sub-category, and then pick a specific demographic.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapy For Remote Workers"
- **By Physical Location: Take the mega-category, pick a sub-category, and then pick a specific physical location or geography.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapists In Chicago."
- **By Digital Platform: Take the mega-category, pick a sub-category, and then pick a specific digital location or platform.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapists On LinkedIn."
- **By Price: Take the mega-category, pick a sub-category, and then pick a price range or socioeconomic status.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapy For Single Mothers On Food Stamps."
- **By Distribution: Take the mega-category, pick a sub-category, and then pick a specific form of delivery.** For example: "Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapy On Zoom."

The point is, you will come up with WAY higher-quality digital product ideas if you go through this exercise and get more specific, first.

Whereas if you stay broad, all your ideas are going to be vague.
```

## Integration Notes

- **Cole's framework is about finding the right niche for PRODUCT creation specifically.** This is not general audience-building advice -- it's about identifying what to build and sell.
- **Connects to presell-validator:** After finding your niche and identifying the most valuable problem, validate demand before building.
- **Connects to product-offer:** Niche decisions directly inform offer positioning, price anchoring, and value framing.
- **Connects to copywriting/sales-page-writer:** The 10 problems and 10 desirable outcomes become direct marketing copy (landing pages, emails, social content).
- **Key Q&A insights from Cole:**
  - If you pick the wrong problem, who cares. All problems are worth solving, some are just worth more. You'll learn over time.
  - Your first 100 customers will TELL YOU if you picked the right problem. Talk to them 1:1. They'll hand you the answer.
  - If your niche doesn't connect to money, either pick a different niche, pick a different problem, or get creative with value anchoring.
