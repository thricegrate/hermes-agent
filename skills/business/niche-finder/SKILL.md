# Niche Finder

## Prerequisites

- User's area of expertise, interests, or industry experience
- Willingness to answer guided questions about their background

## Interaction Style (Conversation Mode)

This skill runs as an interactive discovery conversation, not a dump-everything report.

**Rules:**
- **One question at a time.** Never ask 3 things in one message. Ask one, wait for the answer, then proceed. If you need multiple inputs, ask the most important one first.
- **Short responses: 150-250 words max.** No walls of text. If you catch yourself writing more, cut it down.
- **Never restate what the user just said.** They know what they said. Skip straight to your next question or output.
- **Push back on vague inputs.** If the user says "I want to help people" -> "Help people do what, specifically? Give me a concrete outcome someone would pay for." If they say "get better at X" -> "Better at X so they can do what, exactly?" Keep pushing until the answer is measurable or specific enough to build a product around.
- **Think like a buyer, not a teacher.** Every question framed from the customer's wallet: "Would someone pay to solve this?" If the answer is no, say so and redirect.
- **Be honest.** If the idea is weak, say so. If the audience is too broad, push back. Truth now saves weeks of wasted building later.

For full coaching interaction style, see `coach-core` Persona section.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Pick Mega-Category, Niche, and Niche-Within-Niche

Start broad, then drill down to maximum specificity. The riches are in the niches.

**Drill-down dimensions:**
1. **Industry** - "Healthcare" becomes "Physical Therapy"
2. **Demographic** - + "For Remote Workers"
3. **Physical Location** - + "In Chicago"
4. **Digital Platform** - + "On LinkedIn"
5. **Price/Socioeconomic** - + "For Single Mothers"
6. **Distribution Method** - + "On Zoom"

Ask the user to provide:
- Their Mega-Category (broad industry)
- Their Niche (specific area within that industry)
- Their Niche-Within-Niche (the most specific intersection they can identify)

**Pro tip:** Start with a niche they have personally experienced. Personal experience = authentic authority.

**Example:**
- Mega-Category: Writing
- Niche: Internet Writing (digital writing)
- Niche-Within-Niche: Internet Writing For Beginners

### Step 1b: Brunson New Opportunity Positioning Check

Before brainstorming problems, stress-test the niche positioning (see `references/brunson-frameworks.md`):

1. **New opportunity or improvement?** Is this a new vehicle or a "better version of what they've tried"? If improvement, reframe.
2. **Failed vehicles inventory:** List 3-5 solutions the customer has already tried and why each failed. The pattern across failures becomes your positioning wedge.
3. **Identity shift required:** Who is the customer now vs. who do they need to become? Is the gap crossable?

If the niche can't pass as a new opportunity after reframing, reconsider the niche-within-niche.

### Step 2: List Top 10 Biggest Problems

Once the niche-within-niche is locked, brainstorm the 10 biggest problems people in that niche face.

**Rules:**
- Problems must be specific to the niche-within-niche (not generic)
- Focus on problems that correlate to: making money, not losing money, saving time, or unlocking status
- Write them as concrete, recognizable pain points

**Example (Internet Writing For Beginners):**
1. Distractions
2. Over-Editing
3. Perfectionism
4. Procrastination
5. Self-Confidence
6. Generating Ideas
7. Imposter Syndrome
8. Writing Consistently
9. Choosing A Platform
10. Finding Time To Write

**Then rate each problem for urgency:**

Write each problem as something a real person would say out loud to a friend, not a textbook description. Then classify:

- **RED (Urgent):** Actively losing money, health, or time RIGHT NOW. Will pay today to fix it.
- **YELLOW (Nagging):** Bothers them weekly. They've tried free solutions that didn't stick. Will pay if the fix is easy and clear.
- **GREEN (Aspirational):** Nice to have. Will pay only if low-risk and positioned as an identity upgrade.

Only RED and YELLOW problems advance to the next steps. GREEN problems are weak product foundations -- they sell only at low price points with high volume.

Pick the one problem with the most product potential and tell the user why in one sentence. Ask: "Does my top pick match your gut? Or would you rank these differently?"

### Step 2b: Quick Market Validation Check

Before mapping outcomes, validate that the top 2-3 problems (RED/YELLOW) have real market demand. This is a fast sanity check, not deep competitive analysis (that's `competitor-alternatives`).

For each top problem:

1. **Proof people pay:** Describe 2-3 existing products solving this problem -- what format, what price range, what platform. If you can name specific well-known products, books, or creators, do so. If not certain, say "products like this typically sell for..." Never fabricate specific names. Zero products found = market may not exist. 10+ products = validated but crowded.

2. **The gap:** Based on what exists, what's missing or underserved? Too complicated? Too generic? Missing a specific angle? Too expensive? No community? This is where the user's product can win.

3. **Revenue napkin math:** One table showing what it takes to hit $10K/mo:

| Price | Units for $10K/mo | Reality Check |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| $27   | 370/mo            | Need big audience or viral content |
| $47   | 213/mo            | Doable with 10K+ email list |
| $97   | 103/mo            | Sweet spot for validated offers |
| $197  | 51/mo             | Needs warm traffic + strong proof |
| $497  | 21/mo             | Needs sales calls or webinar |

Adjust price points based on what makes sense for this specific market. Products with personal support always command higher prices.

Ask the user: "Based on this, which price range feels right for your first product?"

### Step 3: Map Top 10 Most Desirable Outcomes

For each problem, write the corresponding desirable outcome as an "I" statement from the customer's perspective.

**Format:** "If I wasn't [problem], I would [desirable outcome]."

**Example:**
- Problem: Distractions -> "If I wasn't distracted, I would be so much more productive."
- Problem: Over-Editing -> "If I wasn't constantly over-editing, I would save myself hundreds of hours and publish way more often."
- Problem: Perfectionism -> "If I wasn't such a perfectionist, I would get out of my own way, publish more, and maybe even live my dream of being a full-time writer."

### Step 4: Pick THE Most Valuable Problem

From the 10 problems, pick ONE to position the entire product around. The product can solve multiple problems, but lead with one.

**5-Point Validation Checklist:**

| # | Criteria | Why It Matters |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Is a problem you've successfully solved for yourself | No personal experience = no authentic teaching |
| 2 | Is a problem you genuinely care about solving | Low passion = burnout before results |
| 3 | Can be objectively measured | Vague problems = vague products |
| 4 | Requires more than just "an answer" (needs implementation) | Simple answers aren't worth paying for |
| 5 | Correlates to: Making Money, NOT Losing Money, Saving TIME, or Unlocking STATUS | These are the problems people pay to solve |

All 5 boxes must be checked. If not, pick a different problem from the list.

**Important:** The product CAN address multiple problems. But the primary positioning leads with ONE problem. Other problems appear in secondary messaging (landing page, emails, social content).

### Step 4a: Brunson Big Domino Statement

After picking the primary problem, craft the ONE belief that dissolves all objections (see `references/brunson-frameworks.md`):

**Template:** "If [target customer] believes that [your vehicle/method] is the only way to [achieve their #1 desirable outcome], and that [specific mechanism] makes it possible even for [their current identity], then every other objection disappears."

Fill in using the primary problem from Step 4 and the desirable outcome from Step 3. Validate: is it one belief (not three)? Can you back it with proof? Does it exclude competitors?

### Step 4b: Brunson Value Ladder Position Check

Determine where this niche enters the value ladder before writing the product brief (see `references/brunson-frameworks.md`):

1. **Entry point:** Where does this audience naturally enter? (Free tool? $27 impulse buy? $197 system?)
2. **Rung before:** What lower-priced asset earns the right to pitch your main product?
3. **Rung after:** What do they need after they succeed? (Coaching? DFY? Membership?)

Include the ladder position in the Step 5 Product Idea Brief.

### Step 4c: Build 5-Layer Persona Profile (Optional but High-Leverage)

After picking the most valuable problem, build a deep persona profile using the 5-Layer Persona Depth System. This step is optional for simple product validation but **mandatory for any campaign targeting cold traffic** (UGC, paid social, organic reels).

**The 5 layers:**

1. **Demographic Shell** -- Specific 8-12 year age window, life stage, income range, location (not "adults" or "millennials")
2. **Psychographic Core** -- Values, lifestyle constraints, decision patterns (price-first vs transformation-first vs proof-first), content consumption habits, trust signals
3. **Pain Architecture** -- 3-5 functional + 3-5 emotional pain points in the persona's EXACT language. The "finally someone understands" moment. A specific difficult day narrative. Requires 60 min of review mining (Amazon, Trustpilot, Reddit).
4. **Failed Alternatives Map** -- 3+ specific solutions tried, why each failed, emotional residue, and the PATTERN across all failures (becomes the unique mechanism bridge in scripts)
5. **Transformation Vision** -- Specific functional + emotional transformation, the specific moment they'll know it worked, secondary transformations they care about but won't say first

**Output:** Use `templates/persona-depth-profile.md` to create the complete profile.

**Full methodology + integration guide:** See `references/persona-depth-system.md`

**Includes 5 Resonance Psychology Checklists** to validate every script against the persona before production (persona identification test, pain authenticity test, mechanism bridge test, transformation specificity test, CTA alignment test).

**Why this matters:** The persona profile feeds directly into `video-scriptwriter` (6-section UGC architecture), `video-hook` (UGC cold-traffic hooks), `quiz-funnel` (question design), and `email-sequence` (segmented follow-ups). Deep persona work is the single highest-leverage pre-production investment.

### Step 5: Output Product Idea Brief

Compile the validated product idea into a structured brief:

```
## Product Idea Brief

**Mega-Category:** [X]
**Niche:** [X]
**Niche-Within-Niche:** [X]

**Primary Problem:** [The #1 problem from Step 4]
**Desirable Outcome:** [The corresponding "I" statement]

**Top 10 Problems:**
1. [problem] -> [outcome]
2. [problem] -> [outcome]
...

**Validation:**
- Solved it yourself? [Yes/No + how]
- Care about it? [Yes/No + why]
- Objectively measurable? [Yes/No + metric]
- Requires implementation? [Yes/No + what]
- Money/Time/Status correlation? [Yes/No + which]

**New Opportunity Positioning:** [New vehicle name + how it differs from failed alternatives]
**Big Domino Statement:** [The ONE belief that dissolves objections]
**Value Ladder Entry Point:** [Rung + price range, with rung before and after]

**Target Customer:** [1-2 sentence description]
**Who it's NOT for:** [One sentence. Builds trust and sharpens positioning.]
**Product Working Title:** [Suggested name]

**This Week (3 Actions):**
1. [Validation action]: e.g., "Post in [specific subreddit/Facebook group/community] asking about [problem]. See if people engage."
2. [Content action]: e.g., "Write one social post about [problem] using your personal experience. Measure response."
3. [Revenue action]: e.g., "Find the 3 closest competitors and note their prices. Confirm people actually pay for this."
```

## Hormozi Avatar & Brand Filters

Before committing to a niche, score your top candidate(s) with these Hormozi frameworks:

- **Vista Equity Customer Scoring:** Rate your target avatar on 4 criteria (pain severity, willingness to pay, ability to pay, ease of delivery) using multiplicative scoring. A zero on any dimension kills the niche. See `coach-business/references/hormozi-avatar-selection.md`.
- **Brand Building 4-Step:** Identify what your target audience already likes, associate your newsletter/product with those things, then optimize. Your niche must have clear "things they already like" to anchor against. See `content-strategy/references/hormozi-marketing-machine.md`.
- **Cole's Niching Down System:** 6 methods to niche (Industry, Demographic, Location, Platform, Price, Distribution) with Mega-Category -> Niche -> Niche-Within-Niche progression, 10 Biggest Problems methodology, and AI prompts for niche exploration. See `references/cole-niching-framework.md`.

## Kennedy Niche Ownership Checkpoint

Before committing to a niche, verify these (see `references/kennedy-niche-ownership.md`):

1. **USP test**: Can you complete "I'm the only [X] who [specific promise]" and have it be true?
2. **Narrow enough**: Can you name 3 real people who fit your niche description?
3. **Market size**: Are there enough people to build a business? (1,000 true fans at $100/yr = $100K.)
4. **Willingness to pay**: Are people in this niche already spending money on solutions?
5. **Ownership path**: Can you become the #1 name in this niche within 12 months?

## Troubleshooting

### User can't pick a niche
Ask: "What do people ask YOU for help with?" and "What problem have you solved for yourself that others still struggle with?"

### User picks a niche that's too broad
Push them through each drill-down dimension until they can describe their target customer in one specific sentence.

### None of the 10 problems pass the 5-point checklist
Go back to Step 1 and adjust the niche-within-niche. The niche may be too broad or misaligned with their experience.
