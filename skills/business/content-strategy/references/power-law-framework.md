# Power Law Framework for Content

Most people think content works like a salary. Post twice as much, get twice the results. That's not how any of this works.

Content follows a power law distribution: a tiny number of posts capture almost all the value.

```
y = C * x^(-alpha)
```

In practice: 1% of posts get 90% of all engagement. The other 99% split the remaining 10%. This isn't a bug. It's how networks, attention, and money actually work.

If you don't understand this, you're optimizing the wrong thing.

---

## The Core Math

- **In content:** 1% of posts get ~90% of total engagement. The median post gets almost nothing.
- **In music:** 1% of Spotify artists take ~90% of streaming revenue. 90,000 songs uploaded daily. Median song gets 30 plays. Ever.
- **In startups:** 0.00006% become unicorns. One company in a VC portfolio often returns more than all others combined.
- **In wealth:** Top 1% owns more than the bottom 50% combined.

This means most of your content will get near-zero engagement. That's not failure. That's the math working exactly as expected. Stop beating yourself up when 9 out of 10 things flop. That's literally the distribution.

---

## Volume as Probability

Each post is a lottery ticket. You can't predict which one will hit. But you can buy enough tickets for the math to work in your favor.

If the probability of any single post breaking out is ~0.5%:

```
After 10 posts:   1 - (0.995)^10   =  4.9% chance of at least one hit
After 100 posts:  1 - (0.995)^100  = 39.4% chance
After 500 posts:  1 - (0.995)^500  = 91.8% chance
After 1000 posts: 1 - (0.995)^1000 = 99.3% chance
```

At 500 posts you're almost guaranteed at least one hit. But you can't predict WHICH one. So you need volume to let the power law do its thing.

"Just post more" is bad advice if you think each post contributes equally. It's great advice if you understand each post is a lottery ticket and you need enough tickets for the distribution to kick in.

## 80/20 Effort Allocation

Not all content deserves equal effort. The power law tells you where to invest.

**80% of content = "good enough"** (ship fast, minimal editing)
- Use templates and proven structures
- 60-minute timer workflow: pick a format, fill it in, ship it
- Don't agonize. Don't rewrite three times. Good enough keeps you visible and buys lottery tickets.
- This maps to Tier 1 regular content in content-social.

**20% of content = ALL creative energy** (big swings)
- Original research, bold contrarian takes, deep personal stories, proprietary data
- 3-5x the effort of regular content
- These are your moonshots. Most won't hit. The ones that do will dwarf everything else.
- This maps to Tier 2 value bombs in content-social.

The trap: spending equal time on every piece. That's linear thinking in a power law world. 100 average posts are worth less than 1 exceptional post. Not slightly less. Exponentially less.

## Expected Value Thinking

Stop picking "safe" content topics. Safe is actually the riskiest strategy from a portfolio perspective.

```
Option A (safe):     80% chance of 500 views  = 400 expected views
Option B (moonshot): 5% chance of 50,000 views = 2,500 expected views
```

Option B is 6x better despite being far more likely to "fail."

**Apply to content decisions:**
- For every 5 pieces, 4 can be safe bets (proven topics, known formats). 1 should be a wild swing (bold take, unusual format, risky angle).
- When choosing between a "solid" topic and a "crazy but interesting" topic, run the EV math. The crazy one often wins on expected value.
- Don't optimize every piece to be "good." Optimize your portfolio to include enough moonshots.

## The Double-Down Playbook

This is where most people screw up. Something goes viral. They feel good for a day. Then they move on to the next random thing.

Wrong. A breakout post is the most valuable signal you will ever get. It tells you exactly what your audience wants right now. Ignoring it is like finding gold and walking away.

**When something outperforms (2x+ your baseline metrics):**

1. **SAME DAY:** Write down what made it work. Topic? Format? Hook? Timing? Personal story? Which emotion did it hit?

2. **WITHIN 48 HOURS:** Create a follow-up from a different angle. If the original was a tip, go deeper with a case study. If it was a story, pull out the framework. If it was data, add the personal narrative.

3. **WITHIN 1 WEEK:** Create 3-5 related pieces exploring adjacent topics. Mine every angle of the interest signal.

4. **WITHIN 2 WEEKS:** Build a product or lead magnet around the interest. If people care about this topic, some of them will pay to go deeper.

5. **WITHIN 1 MONTH:** Consider making this a recurring segment or series. If the topic has legs, ride it.

**What NOT to do:** Celebrate and move on. Assume it was luck. Switch to an unrelated topic because "variety." One hit, properly compounded, is worth more than the next 3 months of random posting.

## Career and Business Power Laws

Power laws don't only apply to posts. They apply to skills, relationships, and career moves.

**Skills:** One skill that's 10x better than average is worth more than 10 skills that are 2x above average. Go deep on one thing, broad on supporting skills (T-shaped). Don't spread yourself thin trying to be "well-rounded."

**Relationships:** One connection with the right person can change your entire trajectory. Not networking broadly. Building one real relationship with someone who's 2-3 steps ahead of you.

**Career bets:** One right company at the right time can compress 10 years of linear growth into 2 years of exponential growth.

```
Linear career:    $50K -> $60K -> $70K -> $80K -> $90K (5 years)
Power law career: $50K -> $50K -> $50K -> $200K (right bet hits)
```

The linear path feels safer. The power law path has higher expected value. But only if you can survive the flat years.

## The Survival Strategy (Meta-Rule)

Everything connects into one framework:

1. **Produce at volume** -- buy enough lottery tickets for the power law to work
2. **Invest disproportionately in big swings** -- 20% of content gets 80% of creative energy
3. **When a hit lands, compound it aggressively** -- the Double-Down Playbook
4. **Have enough runway to survive the 99% that doesn't work** -- don't blow up before your hit arrives

**Never judge your content strategy by individual post performance.** Judge by: am I producing enough volume to find hits, and am I compounding the hits I find?

Same framework as EV thinking, Kelly sizing, and process-over-outcome. The power law doesn't care about your feelings. But if you work with it instead of against it, the results are absurd.
