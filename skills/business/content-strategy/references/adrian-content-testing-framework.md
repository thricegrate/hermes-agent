# Content Performance Testing Framework

Source: Adrian Solar's IG acquisition playbook. Practical A/B testing methodology for social content.

## Weekly Performance Tracking

Track every content template and hook style weekly. Performance degrades over time as audiences get saturated.

### Burnout Detection Pattern (Real Data)
- Week 1: 34% engagement (fresh, novel)
- Week 2: 31% (slight decline)
- Week 3: 26% (noticeable drop, market saturating)
- Week 4: 19% (burned, retire immediately)

**Rule:** When any template drops 15%+ from its peak week, retire it and replace with something fresh.

## Content Type Performance Benchmarks

| Content Type | Avg Reach | Avg Engagement | Best For |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Specific number hook | Highest (340k avg on Reels) | High | Awareness, algorithm boost |
| Controversy / hot take | Very high (280k avg) | Very high (polarizing) | Reach, shares, comments |
| "Everyone's wrong about X" | Very high (310k avg) | High | Authority, debate |
| Before/after transformation | High (250k avg) | Very high | Trust, conversion |
| Case study narrative | Medium | Very high trust | Bottom-funnel, sales |
| Tool demo / screen recording | Medium | High conversion | Product leads, DM triggers |
| Personal failure story | Medium | High comments | Relatability, community |
| Generic tips / how-to | Low (dying in AI era) | Low | SEO only, not social |
| Motivational / inspirational | Lowest (4k avg) | Lowest | Avoid entirely |

## Template Rotation System

### The Rules
1. Run 5 different content angles simultaneously
2. Track performance per angle per week (engagement rate, reach, saves, shares)
3. When any angle drops 15%+ from peak, retire it
4. Replace with a fresh angle immediately
5. Never run fewer than 3 active angles

### Refresh Sources
- Flip the format: if threads burned out, try carousels with the same content
- Change the angle: if "pain point" hooks burned out, try "controversy" hooks
- Borrow from another channel: adapt an email subject line that's working into a social hook
- Steal from comments: when followers comment great phrasing, adapt it as a new hook
- Platform hop: what's working on X might be fresh on LinkedIn (and vice versa)

## Send Timing Optimization

When you post matters almost as much as what you post. Based on 12,847+ data points.

### Best Posting Windows (EST)
| Day | Best Window | Engagement Rate | Notes |
|-----|------------|----------------|-------|
| Tuesday | 10am-12pm | 34% | Best overall day |
| Wednesday | 9am-11am | 32% | Strong second |
| Thursday | 2pm-4pm | 29% | Good for B2B audiences |

### Worst Posting Windows
| Day | Worst Window | Engagement Rate | Why |
|-----|-------------|----------------|-----|
| Monday | 8-10am | 14% | Inbox overload from weekend |
| Friday | 4-6pm | 11% | Mentally checked out |
| Weekends | All day | 6% | Not in business mode |

**Key insight:** Shifting 80% of posts to Tue-Thu 9am-4pm EST increased engagement 60% with zero additional content needed.

## A/B Testing Methodology for Social Content

### The One-Variable Rule
Change ONE thing per test. If you change the hook AND the format AND the posting time, you learn nothing.

### Test Priority Order
Test these in order. Each one has the biggest impact on performance:

1. **Hook type** (biggest lever): Test specific number vs controversy vs before/after
2. **Content format**: Test thread vs carousel vs single post vs Reel
3. **Posting time**: Test morning vs afternoon vs evening
4. **CTA style**: Test "DM me [keyword]" vs "link in bio" vs "comment [keyword]"
5. **Emoji usage**: Test with vs without vs different emojis

### Minimum Sample Sizes
- Per variation: minimum 3 posts before drawing conclusions
- Timeframe: minimum 2 weeks per test
- Exception: if one variation gets 3x the engagement in week 1, you can call it early

### What to Track Per Post
| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| Reach | How many people the algorithm showed it to |
| Engagement rate | Saves + shares matter more than likes |
| Comments | Signals depth of interest |
| DMs received | Direct conversion signal |
| Link clicks | If using link-in-bio CTA |
| Follower change | Net new followers from this post |
| Email signups | Ultimate conversion metric |

### The Content Scorecard
Rate each piece of content after 7 days:
- **Reach score**: compared to your 30-day average (above/below/way above)
- **Engagement score**: saves + shares as % of reach (>5% = great, >10% = viral)
- **Conversion score**: DMs + email signups per 1000 reach
- **Reuse score**: can this be repurposed into another format? (thread to carousel, reel to story, etc.)

Posts scoring "way above" on reach AND "great" on engagement are your winning formulas. Make more like those.
