# LinkedIn Positioning Analysis Framework

Analyze 3-5 competitors or peers on LinkedIn to find positioning gaps you can own. This isn't about copying what works -- it's about finding what nobody's saying.

## When to Use

- Before optimizing your LinkedIn profile (feed results into `linkedin-profile`)
- Before building a content strategy (feed results into `content-strategy`)
- When you feel "stuck" because everyone in your niche sounds the same
- When entering a new niche and need to understand the landscape

---

## Step 1: Select 3-5 Competitors / Peers

Choose people who:
- Serve a similar audience to yours
- Are 1-2 steps ahead of where you want to be (not 10 steps -- too different)
- Are actively posting on LinkedIn (at least weekly)
- Have visible engagement (comments, not just likes)

Mix of: direct competitors (same offer, same audience) + adjacent players (different offer, same audience or same offer, different audience).

---

## Step 2: Capture Profile Data

For each competitor, document:

### Profile Elements
- **Headline:** Full text, word for word
- **About section:** First 3 lines (the hook) + overall structure
- **Banner:** What it says, what it shows
- **Featured section:** What's pinned, in what order
- **Experience:** How they describe their current role
- **Follower count:** Approximate
- **Recommendations:** How many, from whom (clients vs peers)

### Content Patterns
- **Topics:** What do they post about most? (List top 3-5 themes)
- **Formats:** Text posts, carousels, video, polls, articles, newsletters?
- **Frequency:** How often per week?
- **Hook style:** Educational, provocative, storytelling, listicle, question?
- **Engagement levels:** Average likes, comments, reposts on recent 10 posts
- **Top performer:** Their single best-performing post in the last 90 days -- what made it work?

### Offer & Funnel
- **What they sell:** Coaching, course, DFY, community, membership, consulting?
- **Price point:** Visible? Implied? (Free discovery call = high-ticket. Direct checkout = low-ticket.)
- **CTA pattern:** How do they move people from content to client? (DMs, link in bio, webinar, application?)
- **Lead magnet:** Do they have one? What is it? How do they distribute it?

---

## Step 3: Build the Positioning Grid

Map all competitors on two axes:

**X-axis: Niche Specificity**
- Left: Broad ("I help businesses grow")
- Right: Narrow ("I help B2B SaaS founders with $1-5M ARR reduce churn by 30%")

**Y-axis: Authority Signals**
- Bottom: Low proof (no case studies, generic claims)
- Top: High proof (specific numbers, named clients, visible results)

```
HIGH PROOF
    |
    |  [Competitor A]         [Competitor C]
    |
    |          [Competitor B]
    |
    |  [You - current]
    |
LOW PROOF
    |________________________
    BROAD              NARROW
```

**Where you want to be:** Top-right quadrant (narrow + high proof). That's where trust and conversions live.

---

## Step 4: Gap Analysis

Answer these questions:

### Positioning Gaps
- What positioning angle is NOBODY using? (Example: everyone says "scale your business" but nobody says "build a business that runs without you")
- What audience segment are they all ignoring? (Example: everyone targets founders but nobody talks to the ops person)
- What proof type is missing? (Example: everyone shares revenue numbers but nobody shows retention or lifestyle results)

### Content Gaps
- What topics do they ALL avoid? (Often: pricing transparency, failures, behind-the-scenes numbers)
- What content format is underused? (If everyone does text posts, carousels are wide open)
- What emotional angle is missing? (If everyone's aspirational, there's a gap for raw honesty)
- What hook style is absent? (If everyone teaches, there's a gap for storytelling or contrarian takes)

### Offer Gaps
- What price point is uncovered? (Everyone has $5K coaching but nobody has a $47 starter product)
- What delivery model is missing? (Everyone does 1:1 but nobody has a community or cohort)
- What CTA is nobody using? (Everyone says "DM me" but nobody has a free tool or quiz)
