# Multi-Account Portfolio Management

## Why Multi-Account

A single account posting at maximum sustainable volume generates a fixed amount of daily organic reach constrained by follower count, algorithmic authority, and platform distribution limits. The multi-account portfolio removes that ceiling.

---

## The 3-Tier Architecture

### Tier 1: Flagship (2-3 accounts)

**Role:** Highest-authority accounts. Receive best content first. Primary vehicle for paid amplification.

**Requirements for Tier 1 status:**
- Minimum 90 days of consistent posting history
- Niche positioning clear in bio (names target persona + transformation)
- Content grid shows thematic consistency across 30+ posts
- Engagement rate above 3.5% on recent content
- Follower base genuinely composed of the target persona (not inflated by broad content)

**Management:**
- First to receive validated top-performing content
- Paid amplification ONLY on content that's already proven organically
- Bio links to quiz funnel (primary conversion path)
- Content refresh: never post the same piece twice on a Flagship account

**Elevation from Tier 2:** An account is promoted when it meets all 5 requirements above. Premature promotion (especially premature paid amplification) is one of the most common portfolio mistakes.

### Tier 2: Building (8-15 accounts)

**Role:** Primary volume engine. Where most daily production flows.

**Posting frequency:** 3-5 posts per day per account
**Content source:** Validated hook/avatar variations from the variation matrix
**Daily engagement:** 20-30 genuine interactions with niche accounts per account
**Monitoring:** Weekly review of watch-through rate, save rate, CTR per account

**Account-specific positioning:** Each Tier 2 account should have a slightly different angle on the same niche. Example: if the product is a fitness supplement, one account focuses on postpartum fitness, another on busy professionals, another on over-40 fitness. Same product, different persona emphasis.

**Tier 2 to Tier 1 promotion signals:**
- Engagement rate consistently above 3.5% for 4+ weeks
- Clear thematic identity developed in the content grid
- Follower quality audit passes (genuine persona-matched followers)
- At least 1 piece of content has generated significant organic reach without paid

### Tier 3: New/Warmth Period (5-8 accounts)

**Role:** Pipeline of fresh accounts graduating into Tier 2.

Always maintain 5-8 accounts in warmth to replace:
- Accounts retired due to algorithm suppression
- Accounts with account health issues
- Accounts retired for strategic repositioning
- Natural churn from platform policy changes

---

## The 28-Day Warmth Protocol

### Day 1-7: Foundation

- Complete profile: niche bio, profile image, 3-highlight covers
- Post 1 piece per day (proven content from Tier 1/2, not untested)
- Engage with 10 niche accounts per day (genuine comments, not generic)
- Follow 20-30 niche-relevant accounts per day
- Do NOT exceed 1 post/day or 30 follows/day

### Day 8-14: Building

- Increase to 2 posts per day
- Increase engagement to 15 accounts per day
- Include 1 piece of original new content per week alongside proven content
- Monitor first 48-hour performance: is the algorithm distributing to relevant audiences?

### Day 15-21: Testing

- Increase to 3 posts per day
- Introduce variation testing content rotation
- Begin tracking engagement rate and watch-through at account level
- Assess: is this account developing healthy Tier 2 engagement patterns?

### Day 22-28: Graduation

- Increase to full Tier 2 volume (3-5 posts/day)
- Warmth period complete
- Begin full performance monitoring
- Include in weekly portfolio review

**Why gradual matters:** The graduated pattern signals genuine organic behavior. Full volume from Day 1 signals content farm. The algorithm treats them differently.

---

## Account Health Signals

### Green (Healthy)
- Engagement rate 3%+ on recent content
- Views per post trending up or stable
- Follower growth from niche-relevant audiences
- No content removal notices

### Yellow (Watch)
- Engagement rate dropping below 2.5%
- Views per post declining 2+ consecutive weeks
- Follower growth stalling
- One content removal notice

**Action:** Reduce posting frequency to 2/day for 1 week. Increase genuine engagement to 30/day. Post only proven top-performing content.

### Red (Intervention Required)
- Engagement rate below 1.5%
- Views per post less than 50% of portfolio average
- Follower loss
- Multiple content removal notices or action block

**Action:** Pause posting for 72 hours. Resume at Day 8-14 warmth levels. If no improvement after 2 weeks, retire the account and replace from Tier 3 pipeline.

---

## Account Retirement Decision

Retire an account when:
- Red status for 3+ consecutive weeks despite intervention
- Account receives a permanent restriction or shadowban
- The account's niche positioning no longer aligns with the campaign strategy
- A newer Tier 2 account has surpassed it in all performance metrics

**Never delete a retired account.** Archive it. Accounts sometimes recover after extended dormancy, and historical content can be referenced.

---

## Portfolio Health Metrics (Track Weekly)

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Portfolio reach | Sum of all account impressions | Should increase week-over-week |
| Average engagement rate | Mean engagement rate across Tier 1+2 | >3% healthy, <2% intervention |
| CTR across portfolio | Total clicks / total impressions | >1.5% for UGC |
| Revenue per account | Total revenue / active Tier 1+2 accounts | Increasing = system working |
| Warmth pipeline fullness | # accounts in Tier 3 | Always 5-8 |
| Tier distribution | Count per tier | Majority should be Tier 2 |
