# Newsletter Sponsorship Guide

## Setting Your Rate

### CPM Method

Calculate your rate based on cost per thousand impressions (opens):

**Formula:** (Subscribers x Average Open Rate) x CPM rate = price per issue

**Example:**
- 10,000 subscribers x 45% open rate = 4,500 opens
- At $30 CPM = $135 per issue
- At $50 CPM = $225 per issue

### Flat Fee Method

Base rate + premium for niche audience. Niche newsletters command higher rates than general ones because sponsors reach a more targeted, higher-intent audience.

### Industry Benchmarks

| Audience Type | CPM Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Broad/General | $20-30 | Lifestyle, news, entertainment |
| Niche | $30-50 | Specific industry, hobby, or profession |
| Highly Targeted B2B | $50-100 | Decision makers, executives, specific job titles |

Factors that increase your rate:
- High open rates (40%+ is strong)
- High click-through rates on sponsor links
- Engaged audience that replies and shares
- Proven conversion data from past sponsors
- Audience with purchasing power (B2B, professionals, high-income)

## Where to Find Sponsors

### Direct Outreach

List companies whose products your readers already use. These are the easiest sponsors to land because the audience fit is obvious.

Steps:
1. Survey your readers: "What tools do you use for [topic]?"
2. Make a list of 20-30 companies that serve your audience
3. Find their marketing team contact (LinkedIn, company website)
4. Send the pitch template below
5. Follow up once after 5-7 days if no response

### Newsletter Ad Marketplaces

- **Swapstack**: Connects newsletter creators with brands. Good for mid-size newsletters.
- **Paved**: Marketplace for newsletter advertising. Handles payments and matching.
- **Letterhead**: Newsletter advertising platform with brand-safe matching.
- **ConvertKit Sponsor Network**: Built into ConvertKit but works with other platforms too.

### Your Own Readers

Companies that subscribe to your newsletter are already fans. They understand your audience because they ARE your audience.

Look for:
- Founders and marketers who engage with your content
- Companies that share your newsletter on social media
- Brands mentioned positively in reader replies

### Social Proof for Your Pitch

Share these metrics with potential sponsors:
- Total subscribers and growth rate
- Average open rate (last 30 days)
- Average click-through rate
- Audience demographics (job titles, industries, company size)
- Testimonials from past sponsors (if available)
- Screenshots of engagement (replies, shares)

## Pitch Template

**Subject:** Newsletter sponsorship opportunity: [Your Newsletter Name]

---

Hi [Name],

I run [Newsletter Name], a [frequency] newsletter about [topic] read by [subscriber count] [audience description, e.g., "marketing leaders at B2B SaaS companies"].

Our audience is a strong fit for [Company Name] because [1 sentence on why].

**Quick stats:**
- [X] subscribers
- [X]% average open rate
- [X]% average click-through rate

We offer several sponsorship placements starting at $[lowest rate]/issue. I've attached our rate card with full details.

Would you be open to a quick call to discuss? [Calendar link]

Best,
[Your Name]

---

## Ad Placement Best Practices

### Header Sponsor
- Logo + 1-line description at the top of the newsletter
- Seen by 100% of openers (highest visibility)
- Least intrusive: readers are used to seeing "brought to you by" headers
- Lowest rate because it's minimal real estate
- Best for: Brand awareness campaigns

### Mid-Roll
- 3-5 sentences placed in the middle of your content
- Best engagement because readers are actively reading at this point
- Should feel native: write it in your voice, not the sponsor's marketing copy
- Include a clear CTA (link, discount code, etc.)
- Best for: Product signups, free trial offers

### Dedicated Section
- Full paragraph with image/logo and CTA
- Most detailed placement: room to explain the product's value
- Highest rate because it gives the sponsor the most space
- Visually separated from your content (box, divider, or heading)
- Best for: Product launches, detailed offers

### PS Mention
- 1-2 sentences at the very end of the newsletter
- Casual, personal recommendation feel ("PS: I've been using [tool] and it's great for [use case]")
- Lower rate but high trust signal because it reads as a personal endorsement
- Best for: Tools, resources, quick wins

### Rules for Sponsorship Placement

- **Max 1-2 sponsors per issue.** Never let ads overwhelm content.
- **Write sponsor copy in your voice.** Readers trust you, not generic ad copy.
- **Only promote products you'd actually recommend.** Your credibility is worth more than any sponsorship fee.
- **Disclose sponsorships clearly.** Use "Sponsored" or "Partner" labels.
- **Track performance.** Share click and conversion data with sponsors: it helps you retain them and justify rate increases.
