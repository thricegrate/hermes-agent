# Paid Subscription Strategy on Beehiiv

## When to Go Paid

Don't launch a paid tier too early. You need a foundation first.

**You're ready when:**
- You have 5,000+ free subscribers
- Consistent open rates above 35%
- Readers actively replying and engaging with your content
- Clear demand for deeper content (readers asking for more detail, templates, implementation guides)
- You've been publishing consistently for 3+ months

**You're NOT ready when:**
- Under 2,000 subscribers (not enough base to convert)
- Open rates below 25% (engagement too low)
- No clear differentiation between what you'd offer free vs. paid
- You haven't validated that readers want more depth

## Pricing Psychology

### Monthly vs. Annual

Always offer both. Annual pricing encourages commitment and reduces churn.

**Standard formula:** Annual = Monthly x 10 (giving 2 months free)

Examples:
- $10/month or $100/year (save $20)
- $15/month or $150/year (save $30)
- $8/month or $80/year (save $16)

### The $10 Sweet Spot

Most successful solo newsletters charge $8-15/month. This price point works because:
- Low enough to be an impulse decision
- High enough to signal real value
- Comparable to a Netflix subscription (familiar price anchor)
- Generates meaningful revenue at scale ($10 x 500 paid subs = $5,000/month)

### Price Anchoring

Show the annual price savings prominently. Make it obvious that annual is the better deal:

> **Monthly:** $10/month
> **Annual:** $100/year (save $20, that's 2 months free!)

Most readers will choose annual when the savings are clear, which improves your cash flow and reduces monthly churn.

## Content Splitting Strategy

### What Stays Free

- High-level insights and analysis
- News curation and commentary
- Opinion pieces and hot takes
- Community engagement and reader Q&A
- Broad strategies and frameworks

### What Goes Behind the Paywall

- Implementation details and step-by-step guides
- Templates, swipe files, and tools
- Behind-the-scenes breakdowns
- Exclusive interviews and case studies
- Advanced strategies and tactics
- Data, research, and original analysis
- Access to archives of premium content

### The Golden Rule

Free content should be good enough that people WANT to pay. If your free content is mediocre, no one will believe your paid content is worth it.

Paid content delivers on the promise of "going deeper." It takes the insights from the free tier and turns them into action.

## The Paywall Cliffhanger

### How to Place the Paywall

Show 60-70% of the issue for free. Place the paywall RIGHT BEFORE the most actionable section.

**Bad paywall placement:**
- Random cutoff mid-paragraph
- Cutting off before any value is delivered
- Placing it so early that readers can't judge quality

**Good paywall placement:**
- "Here's the framework. Now, below the paywall, I'll give you the exact templates and step-by-step implementation..."
- "I analyzed 50 campaigns and found 3 patterns. Here's the first one [free]. The other two, plus the checklist I built from this research, are below..."
- "Here's what happened and why it matters. Premium subscribers get the playbook for applying this to their own [topic]..."

### Why This Works

The free section proves you have valuable insights. The paywall creates a gap between "knowing the concept" and "having the implementation." Readers who are serious about results will pay to close that gap.

## Launch Strategy for Going Paid

### Timeline: 2 Weeks Before Launch

**Week 1: Announcement**
- Email 1: Announce that a paid tier is coming
- Explain what will be included (be specific)
- Emphasize that the free newsletter isn't going away
- Introduce the founding member discount

**Week 2: Build Anticipation**
- Email 2: Reminder with a preview of premium content
- Share a sample of what paid subscribers will get
- Social proof if available (beta reader testimonials)
- Countdown to launch

**Launch Day:**
- Email 3: The paid tier is live
- Last chance for founding member rate
- Clear CTA to subscribe
- Reiterate the value proposition

### Founding Member Discount

Offer the first 100 subscribers 50% off for life. This works because:
- Creates urgency (limited spots)
- Rewards early adopters
- Generates social proof quickly
- "For life" makes it feel like a no-brainer
- You get 100 paying subscribers fast, which validates the model

### Grandfathering Existing Content

Going paid does NOT mean removing old free content. That destroys trust.

- All previously published free content stays free
- New issues follow the 80/20 split (80% free, 20% premium)
- Premium archives grow over time, increasing the value of the paid tier

### Post-Launch

- Share subscriber milestones publicly (builds social proof)
- Ask paid subscribers for testimonials
- Iterate on what premium content resonates most
- Survey paid subscribers quarterly on what they want more of
- Consider adding perks over time (community access, AMAs, office hours)
