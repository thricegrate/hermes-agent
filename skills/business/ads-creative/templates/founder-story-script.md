# Template: Founder Story Script

**Phase**: 3 (Scripting engine)
**Use when**: Founder-led ad for product-aware or most-aware audiences. The founder is authentic, slightly unpolished, intensely passionate about the problem. This is not a pitch. It is a mission statement plus an offer.
**Output**: Scene setup, script with timing beats, creative tracker name.
**Cross-link**: `skills/video-scriptwriter/references/frameworks.md` (Story Bridge framework), `skills/storyteller/references/sheridan-framework.md` (open-question variant).

---

## The prompt

```
SYSTEM IDENTITY
You are a brand storyteller. Your job is to script Founder-Led ads. You understand that founders convert when they are authentic, slightly unpolished, and intensely passionate about the problem they solved. This ad is not a pitch. It is a mission statement combined with an irresistible offer.

OPERATING RULES
Establish authority early. Why did the founder build this? (Usually out of personal frustration).
Show behind the scenes. Direct the visuals to show the office, the warehouse, or walking the dog. Not a studio backdrop.
Express gratitude. Use community-driven language (e.g., "Welcome to the family," "Thousands of you have...").
Make the offer feel exclusive.

YOUR TASK
Write a founder-led script using the provided product details:

SECTION 1: SCENE SETUP
Where is the founder filming this? What are they doing while talking?

SECTION 2: THE SCRIPT
0:00: The Origin Hook.
0:05: The Problem the industry was ignoring.
0:15: The Solution they built and the mechanism behind it.
0:30: The Social Proof / Gratitude (mentioning thousands of happy customers).
0:45: The specific CTA (e.g., "Click below to join our private group/get the offer").

SECTION 3: CREATIVE TRACKER NAMING CONVENTION
Output the exact file name string for this ad so I can log it in my tracker.

[PASTE FOUNDER INFO, PRODUCT MISSION, AND OFFER BELOW]
```

---

## What to feed in

1. Founder info: name, real backstory, the personal experience that led to building the product, current role
2. Product mission: what the founder is trying to change about the industry/category
3. The current offer: what is being promoted, what makes it exclusive or time-limited
4. Customer count or community signal (real numbers, not vague claims)
5. The behind-the-scenes setting where the founder is comfortable filming

## What to do with the output

1. Save in `private project script notes` as `[creative-tracker-name].md`.
2. The founder reads the script. Coffee shop rule applies. If the founder reads it aloud and it sounds like they would never say that, rewrite.
3. Run the script through `skills/humanizer/SKILL.md` + `skills/content-review/SKILL.md` before the founder records.
4. Production note: the founder should NOT memorize the script word-for-word. Bullet points are better. The authenticity comes from improvised delivery within the structure.

## Common mistakes

- Studio backdrop. Defeats the format. Film in the warehouse, the home office, the car, the kitchen.
- Polished delivery. The format works because of the slight unpolish. Hire a director who keeps the takes loose.
- Skipping the personal frustration origin. "I noticed a gap in the market" is not a story. "My daughter cried for 3 nights in a row before I tried building this" is.
- Generic gratitude. "Thousands of customers" is generic. "The 4,200 of you who emailed me about this" is specific.
- CTA that feels corporate. The founder format earns a casual CTA. "Just click the link, we will take care of you" beats "Get yours now at [URL]."
