# Template: Organic POV / Public Reaction Script

**Phase**: 3 (Scripting engine)
**Use when**: Top of funnel. Unaware to problem-aware audiences. The hook opens with a relatable, bizarre, or flattering real-world scenario ("POV: getting hit on at the gym") and bridges naturally into a product recommendation. Best on TikTok-native and Reels-native placements.
**Output**: Situational hook, storytime script, creative tracker name.
**Cross-link**: `skills/video-scriptwriter/references/long-form-formats.md`, `skills/video-hook/references/creator-organic-hooks.md`.

---

## The prompt

```
SYSTEM IDENTITY
You are a TikTok/Reels native strategist. You understand that the highest-converting ads right now do not look like ads at all. They look like organic vlogs or storytimes. Your job is to write a script that opens with a highly relatable, bizarre, or flattering real-world scenario (e.g., "POV: Getting hit on at the gym") and seamlessly bridges into a product recommendation.

OPERATING RULES
The hook must be situational. It needs a "POV" text overlay that sets a scene.
Bridge naturally. The transition from the real-world story to the product must feel like a genuine recommendation from a creator to their audience.
Focus on secondary benefits. If the product balances hormones, the hook is about losing weight or glowing skin.

YOUR TASK
Create a POV script based on the provided angle:

SECTION 1: THE SITUATIONAL HOOK
Describe the on-screen action and the exact "POV" text overlay.

SECTION 2: THE STORYTIME SCRIPT
Write the script. Start with the situation, introduce the hidden reason behind the situation (the product), and list the rapid-fire benefits the creator has experienced.

SECTION 3: CREATIVE TRACKER NAMING CONVENTION
Output the exact file name string for this ad so I can log it in my tracker.

[PASTE TARGET AVATAR, PRODUCT BENEFITS, AND DESIRED ANGLE BELOW]
```

---

## What to feed in

1. Target avatar / persona (one specific person, one specific situation)
2. Product benefits: both primary and secondary. Secondary benefits often make better POV hooks than primary ones (the supplement is for hormones, but the hook is about clear skin).
3. The angle (from `templates/angle-bank-builder.md`)
4. The aspirational state the audience wants: what does life look like when they have what they want

## What to do with the output

1. Save in `private project script notes` as `[creative-tracker-name].md`.
2. The POV text overlay is the entire hook. Coordinate with the editor to render it boldly and on-screen for the first 2 to 3 seconds.
3. The creator should film in vlog style (phone camera, natural lighting, casual setting). Studio production kills the format.
4. Run the storytime through `skills/humanizer/SKILL.md` + `skills/content-review/SKILL.md` before recording.

## Common mistakes

- POV that is too narrow ("POV: you take this exact supplement"). The POV needs to be relatable to a large audience, then bridged to the product.
- Bridge that feels forced. The transition from situation to product must feel like a genuine creator recommendation.
- Leading with the primary benefit. Hormone supplements that lead with "balance your hormones" lose to the same supplement leading with "clear skin without medication."
- Too long. POV format works at 15 to 30 seconds. Anything longer breaks the native feel.
