# Hook Library 2025

Complete catalog of ad hooks synthesized from 14 social-media-marketing transcripts. Covers 24 tested hook templates, platform-specific adaptations, systematic testing framework, fatigue signals, and categorization by emotional trigger.

---

## Part 1: 24 Ad Hook Templates for 2025

Sourced from The Social Savannah's client-tested collection. Each hook is a fill-in-the-blank template. For every hook: name, formula, example, platform best-fit, and a variation.

---

### Hook 1: Temporal Departure

**Formula:** "We're leaving [X] in [year]..."
**Example:** "We're leaving overpriced gym memberships in 2024."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, Instagram Stories
**Why it works:** Creates contrast between the old (rejected) and the new (the offer). The audience self-selects by agreeing the old thing deserves to be left behind.
**Variation:** "We're done with [X] in 2025. Here's what we're doing instead."

---

### Hook 2: Permission-Granting

**Formula:** "It's OK if you think this is [provocative/different/weird]..."
**Example:** "It's OK if you think this skincare routine sounds too simple to work."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, TikTok
**Why it works:** Disarms resistance before it forms. Viewer who would normally scroll away pauses to find out what unusual thing they might be judging too fast.
**Variation:** "Most people think this is unnecessary. They're wrong."

---

### Hook 3: New Year Statement

**Formula:** "[Your new year statement / your resolution to change something]"
**Example:** "This year, I'm only buying products that actually work."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, Instagram (January-February, also September when back-to-school mindset is high)
**Why it works:** Temporal hooks earn relevance by meeting the audience exactly where their mental energy already is. New year = new decision-making mode.
**Variation:** "2025 is the year I stop [negative behavior]." (pairs well with a before/after resolution product)

---

### Hook 4: Resolution Alignment

**Formula:** "If one of your [resolutions / goals] is [X]..."
**Example:** "If one of your goals this year is to actually get out of debt..."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, Google Display
**Why it works:** Immediately qualifies the viewer. People who have that specific goal self-identify as relevant and keep watching. People who don't have that goal scroll -- and that's the correct outcome.
**Variation:** "If getting [outcome] is on your list this year, read this before you try anything else."

---

### Hook 5: Partner Testimony

**Formula:** "My husband/wife/partner thinks [surprising or controversial opinion about product]..."
**Example:** "My husband thinks I'm crazy for spending money on this -- until he saw my skin."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, Instagram, TikTok
**Why it works:** Instantly creates a narrative. The relationship frame adds social depth. The "until" sets up a reversal the viewer wants to see resolved.
**Variation:** "My partner didn't believe this would work. Now he won't stop using it."

---

### Hook 6: Identity Opener

**Formula:** "As someone who [identity/situation the viewer holds]..."
**Example:** "As someone who has tried every productivity system and failed, I need you to know about this."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok
**Why it works:** Creates instant recognition and mirrors. If the viewer holds that identity, they are locked in. Best hook for niche audiences where the identity is precise.
**Variation:** "If you're someone who [shared struggle], this was written for you."

---

### Hook 7: Near-Miss Scarcity

**Formula:** "We [almost] didn't release this..."
**Example:** "We almost didn't release this formula -- our team thought it was too good to share."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, Email-retargeting display, Instagram Stories
**Why it works:** Implies the offer is rare, exclusive, or superior enough that withholding it was considered. Creates urgency without a countdown timer.
**Variation:** "I debated whether to post this. But you need to know."

---

### Hook 8: Persona-Specific Product

**Formula:** "[Brand/Product] for [specific person / situation]..."
**Example:** "The skincare routine for women over 40 who are done with products that don't work."
**Platform best-fit:** All platforms -- best on Meta and TikTok where demographic targeting is broad
**Why it works:** Filters for the right audience immediately. CPAs drop because unqualified viewers self-sort out. The right person sees themselves named and leans in.
**Variation:** "Built specifically for [exact role/situation] -- not a generic version of it."

---

### Hook 9: Conditional Invitation

**Formula:** "Try this if you want to [specific outcome]..."
**Example:** "Try this if you want to double your reply rate without cold calling."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok
**Why it works:** The conditional frame ("if you want") signals that this is not for everyone -- which makes it more compelling for those who do want it.
**Variation:** "Do this one thing if [desired outcome] is what you're after."

---

### Hook 10: Numbered Outcome List

**Formula:** "[Number] ways to [goal/outcome] in 2025..."
**Example:** "7 ways to cut your grocery bill in half this year -- without coupons."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, Instagram, Google Display, LinkedIn
**Why it works:** Numbered lists set a clear expectation of value. "7 ways" implies completeness and structure -- the viewer knows what they're getting into.
**Variation:** "[Number] things [specific audience] should stop doing in 2025 -- and what to do instead."

---

### Hook 11: PSA Format

**Formula:** "PSA: [attention-grabbing statement about a common mistake or overlooked truth]"
**Example:** "PSA: Your sunscreen is not protecting you the way you think it is."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, Meta
**Why it works:** PSA format implies authority and urgency. It bypasses the usual "is this relevant to me" filter because it sounds like important information rather than an ad.
**Variation:** "Important: If you've been doing [X], you need to stop."

---

### Hook 12: Pattern Interrupt (Should Be Illegal)

**Formula:** "[This product/website/offer] should be illegal"
**Example:** "This tool should be illegal. It cuts a 4-hour task down to 11 minutes."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Meta (mobile feed)
**Why it works:** Hyperbole stops the scroll. "Should be illegal" triggers curiosity -- the viewer wants to know what's so good that it crosses a line. Works especially well for productivity, beauty, and finance categories.
**Variation:** "I feel guilty about how easy this made [task]. It shouldn't be this simple."

---

### Hook 13: Educate-and-Agitate Sequence

**Formula:** "Tired of [pain]? Here's what's actually causing it -- and the fix."
**Example:** "Tired of makeup that melts off by noon? That foundation is clogging your pores and wrecking your skin. Here's the fix."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, YouTube pre-roll
**Why it works:** Three-step sequence -- name the pain, agitate the consequence, offer the solution. Longer watch time lowers CPMs and pushes conversions up. This is a full hook + bridge, not just a hook.
**Variation:** "The real reason [pain] keeps happening -- and what actually works."

---

### Hook 14: GRWM Testimonial

**Formula:** Get Ready With Me (GRWM) format + embedded product testimonial
**Example:** [On camera, getting ready] "I've been using [product] for three weeks and I need to talk about it."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Instagram Reels
**Why it works:** GRWM is a native TikTok/Reels behavior. Embedding a product review inside it feels organic, not like an ad. The casual format lowers resistance.
**Variation:** POV: You're watching someone get ready and they can't stop talking about [product].

---

### Hook 15: Search Intent Mirror

**Formula:** "If you're googling [specific search query], you need to do THIS instead"
**Example:** "If you're googling 'how to get rid of back pain,' you need to do this first."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Meta (works particularly well on retargeting warm audiences who searched a related term)
**Why it works:** Mirrors the exact mental state of the viewer at the moment of highest need. If someone is actively searching for this, they are already in problem-awareness mode.
**Variation:** "Stop Googling [solution]. Do this instead."

---

### Hook 16: Behavioral Challenge

**Formula:** "Wait, you still [old behavior]? WHY???"
**Example:** "Wait, you're still paying for [subscription] every month? WHY???"
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Instagram Reels
**Why it works:** Creates mild defensiveness. The viewer pauses because they recognize the behavior and want to know why it's being questioned. Keeps them watching to resolve the dissonance.
**Variation:** "You're still doing [X]? We need to talk."

---

### Hook 17: Discovery / Where Has This Been

**Formula:** "Where has [product/approach] been my entire life?!"
**Example:** "Where has this supplement been my entire life?! I slept 8 hours for the first time in years."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Meta Feed
**Why it works:** Communicates transformation through emotional expression. "Entire life" implies this problem is chronic and deeply felt. The resolution sounds like relief.
**Variation:** "I'm genuinely upset that nobody told me about this sooner."

---

### Hook 18: Feature Justification

**Formula:** "[Number] things about [product] that just make sense"
**Example:** "5 things about this blender that just make sense -- and make me use it every single day."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Carousel, TikTok
**Why it works:** "Just makes sense" implies logic and reason -- it signals a practical purchase without needing hype. Audiences who are skeptical of marketing claims respond well to logical framing.
**Variation:** "Here's why [product] is the only [category] that actually works the way I need it to."

---

### Hook 19: Skeptic Conversion

**Formula:** "I thought [product/approach] was a gimmick until..."
**Example:** "I thought blue light glasses were a total gimmick until I wore them for a week."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, TikTok, Instagram
**Why it works:** The creator embodies the viewer's own skepticism. Skeptical viewers who would scroll away identify with the hook and stay to hear why they were wrong.
**Variation:** "I was the last person who thought [product] would work for me. Then..."

---

### Hook 20: Value Defense

**Formula:** "Things about [product] that make it worth every penny"
**Example:** "Things about this mattress that make it worth every single penny -- even at full price."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta, YouTube pre-roll
**Why it works:** Addresses the price objection before it's raised. Saying "worth every penny" signals that the viewer may have hesitated at price -- and the ad is about to justify it.
**Variation:** "I almost didn't buy this because of the price. Now I can't imagine not having it."

---

### Hook 21: Money-Saving Promise

**Formula:** "I'm about to save you so much money"
**Example:** "I'm about to save you so much money on [category] -- nobody talks about this."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Meta (finance, subscription, utility products)
**Why it works:** Immediate, self-interested hook. "I'm about to save you" implies the payoff is coming right now. The viewer's incentive to watch is clear.
**Variation:** "You're probably wasting [amount] on [category] every month. Let me show you."

---

### Hook 22: Exclusive Secret

**Formula:** "Don't spill my secret"
**Example:** "Don't spill my secret -- this is the [product] every [professional] uses but never mentions publicly."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Instagram Reels
**Why it works:** Activates the "insider" desire. The viewer wants to be in on a secret. The implicit social proof (used by professionals who keep it quiet) elevates the product's perceived value.
**Variation:** "I probably shouldn't tell you this, but [product secret/strategy]."

---

### Hook 23: Confession / Recanted Opinion

**Formula:** "A purchase I was wrong about..."
**Example:** "A purchase I was wrong about: I returned this after 2 days. Then I bought it again."
**Platform best-fit:** TikTok, Meta, Instagram Reels
**Why it works:** Confession signals honesty. Audiences reward vulnerability with attention. The story arc (bought, returned, rebought) is inherently compelling and creates a mystery the viewer wants to solve.
**Variation:** "I talked badly about this product for months. I owe it an apology."

---

### Hook 24: Category Rejection

**Formula:** "Why I'm never going back to traditional [category]"
**Example:** "Why I'm never going back to traditional banking -- and it's not just about the fees."
**Platform best-fit:** Meta Feed, TikTok
**Why it works:** Positions the product as the final, definitive upgrade. "Never going back" signals conviction. Works in any category where there's a clear old way vs. new way narrative.
**Variation:** "I tried every [traditional approach]. None of them worked. Until I found this."

## Part 2: Platform-Specific Hook Adaptations

What works on each platform is driven by user intent, content format, and psychological state at the time of exposure.

---

### Meta Image Ads (Static)

**User state:** Scrolling feed, passive, looking at lifestyle and social content.
**Hook lives in:** Primary text (first 125 characters before truncation) and the image/headline.
**What works:**
- Bold declarative statements: "This changed my morning routine permanently."
- Persona-specific openers: "For freelancers who are done charging hourly."
- Quantified outcomes: "Lost 23 lbs. Didn't change my diet."
- The image carries the hook -- put the strongest text directly on the creative.

**Avoid:** Question hooks without strong answers nearby. Long setup before the payoff. Generic benefit statements ("improves your life").

---

### Meta Video Ads

**User state:** Scrolling feed or Stories, passive. Sound may be off.
**Hook lives in:** First 3 seconds of video + overlaid text (assume no audio).
**What works:**
- Visual pattern interrupt: something unexpected in the first frame
- Overlaid text hook (readable without sound): "The skincare mistake aging you faster"
- Motion that demands attention: fast movement, close-ups, transformation cuts
- Educate-and-agitate sequence (Hook 13) works well when the full sequence fits in 15 seconds

**Avoid:** Logos or brand names in the first 3 seconds. Slow fade-in intros. Anything that assumes audio is on.

---

### TikTok

**User state:** Active scrolling, high stimulation, algorithmic discovery. Cold audience (often).
**Hook lives in:** First 1-3 seconds (verbal + visual simultaneously).
**What works:**
- Native formats: GRWM, ASMR Unboxing, Reply to Comment (Hooks 14, 26, 27)
- Behavioral challenge: "Wait, you still [X]?" (Hook 16)
- PSA format (Hook 11) -- sounds like organic content
- Confession-style (Hook 23) -- rewards honesty
- Energy must be immediate; hesitation causes a scroll

**TikTok-specific rule:** The hook must work visually AND verbally in the first 2 seconds. Most TikTok users watch with sound on (opposite of Instagram), so verbal hooks land. But the visual must still stop them before they decide whether to listen.

---

### LinkedIn

**User state:** Professional context, actively looking for career/business value.
**Hook lives in:** First line of post (before "see more" truncation -- typically 150-200 characters) or first sentence of ad intro text.
**What works:**
- Statement that makes you question something (not a question -- an observation): "Most SaaS companies are solving the wrong retention problem."
- Data-led hooks: "Our CPL dropped 40% when we stopped optimizing for clicks."
- Contrarian professional takes: "The advice everyone gets about LinkedIn is backwards."
- Identity opener (Hook 6): "As someone who has grown 3 companies from zero..."

**LinkedIn-specific rule:** The rehook technique -- after the first hook line, add a single-line break, then a 45-55 character rehook that pulls the reader past "see more." This is the LinkedIn-native version of the two-part hook structure.

**Avoid:** Consumer-style hype ("Amazing results!"). Hard sells. Question hooks that read as engagement bait.

---

### Instagram Feed (Image)

**User state:** Aesthetic-driven feed browsing, highly visual.
**Hook lives in:** The image first, then the caption.
**What works:**
- Aspirational visual + minimal text on image
- Discovery / "where has this been" hooks (Hook 17) in caption
- Partner testimony (Hook 5) -- feels personal and authentic
- The image IS the hook; caption amplifies it

**Avoid:** Text-heavy images. Anything that looks like a stock photo or generic ad. Over-designed graphics.

---

### Instagram Stories / Reels

**User state:** Sequential, immersive, full-screen attention.
**Hook lives in:** First frame (image) or first 2 seconds (video).
**What works:**
- Near-miss scarcity (Hook 7): "We almost didn't release this" -- works in Stories as a direct-to-camera moment
- Conditional invitation (Hook 9): casual, direct-to-camera
- Text overlay hooks (assume audio off)
- Platform-native swipe-up or link-sticker integration at the end

**Avoid:** Anything that requires more than one viewing to understand.

## Part 3: Hook Testing Framework

### The Single-Variable Rule

The most repeated principle across all 14 transcripts: **change one variable per test cycle**. When multiple variables change simultaneously, no result is interpretable.

**Test sequence:**
1. Hook only (same visual, same body copy, different opening line)
2. Visual only (same hook, same body copy, different image/video)
3. CTA only (same hook, same visual, different call to action)
4. Angle (different emotional trigger or value proposition -- this is a full new creative, not a variation)

---

### Minimum Sample Before Decision

The transcripts consistently warn against pulling creatives too early. Guidance from the "How to Analyze Ad Creatives Like a Pro" framework:

| Traffic level | Minimum impressions before decision |
|---------------|-------------------------------------|
| Low spend (<$50/day) | 1,000 impressions + 3-5 days |
| Medium spend ($50-200/day) | 2,000 impressions + 48-72 hours |
| High spend (>$200/day) | 5,000 impressions or statistically significant conversion data |

**What "significant" means:** Enough conversions to see a pattern -- typically 25-50 conversions per variation for Meta, fewer for high-intent Google traffic.

---

### A/B Test Setup

**Step 1:** Name your creatives systematically so patterns are legible in reporting:
```
[platform]_[hook-type]_[angle]_[version]
meta_persona_womover40_v1
meta_patterninterrupt_illegal_v2
tiktok_confession_recanted_v1
```

**Step 2:** Run hooks against each other with identical body copy and creative.

**Step 3:** Primary metric hierarchy:
- If optimizing for sales: cost per purchase (not CTR, not ROAS in isolation)
- If optimizing for leads: cost per qualified lead (not CPL for unqualified volume)
- CTR is a directional signal, not a decision metric. High CTR with low conversion = hook is misleading.

**Step 4:** Read results correctly:
- Hook A CTR 4%, conversion rate 0.5% -- the hook attracts but the ad doesn't deliver
- Hook B CTR 1.5%, conversion rate 2% -- lower attention but higher quality attention
- Hook B wins

---

### How to Read Hook Performance

| Signal | What it means | What to do |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| High CTR, low conversion | Hook over-promises or attracts wrong audience | Revise hook to match offer, or tighten targeting |
| Low CTR, high conversion | Hook self-selects well but reach is limited | Test broader hook with same offer framing |
| High CTR, high conversion | Winner -- scale this | Increase budget, build variations on this hook |
| Low CTR, low conversion | Wrong audience OR wrong offer | Diagnose: is it a targeting problem or a creative problem? |

---

### Naming Convention for Hooks by Emotional Trigger

When building a test battery, aim for at least one hook per trigger type. This prevents blind spots:

- **Curiosity:** Hooks 2, 11, 22 (Permission-Granting, PSA, Exclusive Secret)
- **Fear / Consequence:** Hooks 13, 15 (Educate-Agitate, Search Mirror)
- **Aspiration / Dream outcome:** Hooks 17, 20, 24 (Discovery, Value Defense, Category Rejection)
- **Social proof / peer:** Hooks 5, 14, 23 (Partner Testimony, GRWM, Confession)
- **Identity:** Hooks 6, 8 (Identity Opener, Persona-Specific)
- **Shock / Pattern interrupt:** Hooks 12, 16 (Should Be Illegal, Behavioral Challenge)
- **Humor / relatability:** Hook 1, 19 (Temporal Departure, Skeptic Conversion)
- **Urgency / scarcity:** Hook 7, 21 (Near-Miss Scarcity, Money-Saving Promise)

## Part 4: Hook Refresh Cadence and Creative Fatigue

### Creative Fatigue: What It Is

Creative fatigue occurs when the same audience has seen the same creative enough times that engagement metrics decline -- not because the offer is bad, but because the creative has lost novelty. Frequency is the primary driver.

From the "9 Tips to Sharpen Your Ads" transcript: "Rotate new images, video, and copy before fatigue sets in. The same creative shown too many times stops getting clicks -- not because the offer is bad, but because people stop seeing it."

---

### Fatigue Signals: Metrics to Watch

Monitor these metrics weekly (not just at month-end):

| Metric | Healthy range | Fatigue signal | Alarm threshold |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Frequency (Meta) | 1.5 - 3.0 | 3.0 - 5.0 | >5.0 |
| CTR trend | Stable or rising | Gradual decline over 7 days | -30% from peak |
| CPM trend | Stable | Rising week-over-week | +40% from baseline |
| Relevance score / Quality ranking | Above average | Average | Below average |
| Comment sentiment | Neutral to positive | Neutral | Negative pattern |

**Key insight from the "why-ads-fail" transcript:** Format monotony accelerates fatigue. Running only static images or only video burns through the same audience faster than rotating formats.

---

### Refresh vs. Rebuild Decision

**Refresh** (lower effort): Same angle, same offer, new hook or new visual. Use when:
- CTR has declined but conversion rate remains stable
- Frequency is above 4 but below 7
- The underlying angle is still resonating (comments remain positive, just declining in volume)

**Rebuild** (higher effort): New angle, new emotional trigger, potentially new format. Use when:
- Both CTR and conversion rate have declined
- Frequency is above 7
- Competitor has entered with a similar hook (audience has become fatigued across the category)
- Negative comments increase ("seen this a million times")

---

### Refresh Cadence by Campaign Type

| Campaign type | Typical fatigue window | Refresh trigger |
|---------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Cold prospecting (Meta) | 2-4 weeks | Frequency >4 |
| Retargeting | 1-2 weeks | Frequency >6 (smaller audience) |
| Google Display | 4-8 weeks | CTR decline -25% from peak |
| TikTok (cold audience) | 3-7 days | Engagement rate drop -40% |
| LinkedIn sponsored | 4-6 weeks | CTR decline -30% |

**TikTok note:** TikTok audiences fatigue faster than Meta because the algorithm serves the same ad to the same person repeatedly in a compressed time window. Refresh content weekly, not monthly, on TikTok.

## Part 5: Hook Categorization by Emotional Trigger

### Curiosity Hooks

**Mechanism:** Opens an information gap the viewer must close by watching.
**Strength:** Works broadly across audiences. Does not require specific pain awareness.
**Risk:** Can attract low-quality attention (clicks without intent to buy) if the gap is created by sensationalism rather than genuine information value.

Templates:
- "I probably shouldn't tell you this, but..."
- "The [industry] secret that [authority figure] won't talk about."
- "What nobody tells you about [familiar topic]."
- "I found something [professionals] have been quietly using."
- Hook 22 (Exclusive Secret): "Don't spill my secret -- this is the [product] every [professional] uses."

---

### Shock / Surprise Hooks

**Mechanism:** Violates expectations. Forces the brain to stop autopilot and attend.
**Strength:** Highest stopping power for cold audiences. Effective on TikTok and Instagram where scroll speed is fast.
**Risk:** Shock wears off immediately after the surprise is explained. The body copy must deliver substance or the viewer leaves more frustrated than engaged.

Templates:
- Hook 12 (Should Be Illegal): "[This product] should be illegal."
- "I can't believe this is free."
- "This result shouldn't be possible in [timeframe]."
- "Nobody told me [outcome] was this easy."

---

### Aspiration Hooks

**Mechanism:** Paints the destination, not the problem. Activates latent desire.
**Strength:** Drives desire-based engagement -- highest conversion correlation when paired with relatable delivery.
**Risk:** If the aspiration is too distant from the viewer's current situation, relatability fails and they scroll.

Templates:
- Hook 17 (Discovery): "Where has [product] been my entire life?!"
- Hook 24 (Category Rejection): "Why I'm never going back to [old approach]."
- "This is what it feels like to finally [outcome]."
- "I never thought [transformation] was possible for me. Then..."

---

### Fear Hooks

**Mechanism:** Activates threat-detection. The viewer stays to learn whether they are at risk.
**Strength:** High urgency. Effective in health, finance, security, and parenting categories.
**Risk:** Must be credible -- fabricated or exaggerated fear destroys trust. Must pair with a genuine solution or viewer feels manipulated.

Templates:
- Hook 13 (Educate-Agitate): "Tired of [pain]? That [behavior] is [consequence]. Here's the fix."
- "If you've been doing [common behavior], you need to read this."
- "The [mistake] that's costing you [outcome] -- and how to stop."
- PSA format (Hook 11): "PSA: Your [product/approach] is not [working the way you think]."

---

### Social Proof Hooks

**Mechanism:** Leverages peer behavior or testimony to reduce skepticism.
**Strength:** Works at every funnel stage. Particularly effective for cold audiences who have no prior brand awareness.
**Risk:** Generic social proof ("thousands of happy customers") is ignored. Proof must be specific and concrete.

Templates:
- Hook 5 (Partner Testimony): "My husband/wife/partner thinks [opinion]..."
- Hook 14 (GRWM Testimonial): Get Ready With Me + embedded product review
- Hook 23 (Confession): "A purchase I was wrong about..."
- "[Number] people made [outcome] last month. Here's the one thing they all did."

---

### Humor Hooks

**Mechanism:** Triggers dopamine response. Lowers the viewer's defensive posture.
**Strength:** High shareability. Works particularly well on TikTok and for brands that can afford to be less formal.
**Risk:** Humor is subjective and culturally specific. Misses badly when the audience doesn't share the reference. Also: humor that masks a weak offer doesn't convert.

Templates:
- Hook 19 (Skeptic Conversion): "I talked badly about this product for months. I owe it an apology."
- "Me before [product]: [frustrating situation]. Me after [product]: [absurdly positive outcome]."
- "[Relatable bad habit] is my personality. Until [product] fixed it."
- Exaggerated confession hooks with self-aware tone.

---

### Controversy Hooks

**Mechanism:** Takes a position. Divides the audience into "I agree" and "prove it" camps. Both stay.
**Strength:** High comment volume, high engagement. LinkedIn-specific data from the viral-content transcript: controversy hooks drive more engagement than any other type.
**Risk:** Cannot be faked. Hollow controversy ("unpopular opinion: [obvious thing]") is immediately spotted and damages credibility.

Templates:
- "Unpopular opinion: [contrarian position on familiar topic in your industry]."
- "The advice everyone gets about [category] is backwards. Here's what actually works."
- "I'm going to say something the [industry] doesn't want you to hear."
- "[Common advice] is keeping you stuck. This is what I did instead."

---

## Summary: Hook Selection Guide

| Goal | Best hook type | Best specific hooks |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Cold traffic, max reach | Aspiration, curiosity | Hook 17, 22, 24 |
| Retargeting warm visitors | Fear, social proof | Hook 13, 23, 7 |
| Niche audience qualification | Identity, persona-specific | Hook 6, 8, 4 |
| Skeptical audience | Skeptic conversion, confession | Hook 19, 23, 2 |
| Finance, health, utility | Fear, loss aversion, PSA | Hook 11, 13, 21 |
| TikTok cold traffic | Pattern interrupt, GRWM, PSA | Hook 12, 14, 11 |
| LinkedIn B2B | Identity, controversy, data-led | Hook 6, contrarian |
| Testing multiple angles fast | Temporal, numbered list | Hook 3, 10 |

## Part 6: Reels-Specific Hook Formulas (Adrian Playbook Data)

Source: Analysis of 100+ Instagram Reels. Performance data from accounts with 5k-100k followers.

### Template 1: Specific Number Hook
**Formula:** "I [verb] [specific large number] [things] and here's the only [thing] that works"
**Example:** "I've sent 847,000 Instagram DMs and here's the only opener that works"
**Performance:** 340k avg views
**Why it works:** Large specific numbers signal experience. "Only" creates curiosity gap.

### Template 2: Controversy / Hot Take
**Formula:** "Everyone says [common belief]. They're [wrong/broke/lying]. Here's why."
**Example:** "Everyone says Instagram is dead. They're poor. Here's why."
**Performance:** 280k avg views (310k for "everyone's wrong" variant)
**Why it works:** Disagreement triggers the need to comment. Comments boost distribution.

### Template 3: Before/After Transformation
**Formula:** "How I went from [bad metric] to [good metric] in [short timeframe] with one change"
**Example:** "How I went from $8k/mo to $50k/mo in 6 weeks with one change"
**Performance:** 250k avg views
**Why it works:** Specific metrics create believability. Short timeframe creates urgency.

### Template 4: "Everyone's Wrong"
**Formula:** "Here's why everyone's wrong about [topic everyone has an opinion on]"
**Example:** "Here's why everyone's wrong about using VAs for DM management"
**Performance:** 310k avg views
**Why it works:** Positions you as the contrarian thinker. People watch to see if you can back it up.

### Case Study Ad Format (Social-First)
Structure for ads built around a customer success story:

1. **Old metrics** (the painful before): "This ecom brand was getting 2-3 DMs/day, converting maybe 1/week"
2. **The setup** (what changed): "Set them up with our AI DM automation. Took 15 minutes."
3. **New metrics** (the proof): "Now 20-30 DMs/day. AI handles all of them. Books 8-12 calls/week."
4. **Revenue result** (the payoff): "Closed $43k in sales last month from Instagram DMs alone. Owner was on vacation."
5. **CTA** (the action): "DM me 'AUTO' and I'll show you how it works"

This format works because each beat answers the next logical question the viewer has. Don't skip beats or reorder.

### Flop Patterns to Avoid in Ads

| Pattern | Avg Performance | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| Generic motivational | 4k views | Replace with specific proof |
| No hook in 3 seconds | 3k views | Lead with number or hot take |
| Too polished/corporate | 5k views | Make it scrappy, authentic |
| Self-focused ("I achieved") | 2k views | Reframe to viewer's problem |
