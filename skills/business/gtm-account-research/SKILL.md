# GTM Account Research

Build a deep, actionable understanding of a target account before outreach, a sales meeting, pipeline review, or strategic account planning. The output is a commercial picture -- why this company, why now, and what angle to take. Run this before building an outreach strategy. Works best paired with gtm-qualification-scoring to prioritize effort.

## Context Gathering

Before starting, collect from the user:

- **Your company** -- name, what you sell, value proposition, key differentiators
- **Your ICP** -- ideal customer profile, disqualifiers
- **Your buyer** -- primary persona (titles), secondary personas, what they care about
- **Competitive landscape** (optional) -- main competitors, how you win, how you lose
- **The target account** -- company name, any prior relationship or context

## What This Skill Produces

A commercial account picture: who they are, what's happening in their world right now, how well they fit your ICP, who the right people are, what pain they're experiencing, and what your angle should be.

---

## Research Methodology

Work through the following areas in order. Synthesize as you go -- don't just list facts. At each step, ask: **what does this mean for us?**

### Step 1 -- Company Foundation

Understand what this company actually does and where it stands.

- **Business model:** What do they sell, to whom, and how?
- **Size & scale:** Headcount, revenue (if known), offices, geographic footprint
- **Growth trajectory:** Scaling fast, plateauing, or contracting? Headcount trend, funding history, expansion signals
- **Funding:** Total raised, last round, lead investors, when. Series stage signals urgency and budget
- **Ownership:** Public, private, PE-backed, bootstrapped? Affects buying behavior and budget cycles

**So what:** Is this company spending or saving? Scaling into problems your product solves?

### Step 2 -- Strategic Moment

Identify what's happening right now that creates relevance.

Look for:
- Recent funding announcements
- Leadership changes (new CRO, CMO, VP of Sales, etc.)
- Acquisitions or being acquired
- Product launches or market expansion
- Hiring surges in specific functions
- Layoffs or restructuring
- Press coverage or earnings commentary
- Industry headwinds/tailwinds

**So what:** What's the most relevant "why now" for reaching out? What changed in the last 90 days?

### Step 3 -- ICP Assessment

Evaluate fit against your ideal customer profile.

| Criterion | What you see | Fit (Strong / Partial / Weak) |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Industry | | |
| Company size | | |
| Growth stage | | |
| Tech stack / infrastructure | | |
| Business model | | |
| Personas present | | |
| Pain signals | | |

**Overall ICP verdict:** Strong fit / Partial fit / Weak fit -- and why in one sentence.

> If fit is weak, flag it clearly. Don't manufacture a pitch.

### Step 4 -- The Buying Committee

Map the people involved in a decision like yours.

- **Economic buyer:** Who controls budget? (Usually VP or C-level)
- **Champion candidate:** Who feels the pain most acutely and would advocate internally?
- **Influencers:** Who else touches this decision (IT, legal, finance, ops)?
- **Blocker risk:** Anyone likely to resist or own the incumbent solution?

For each key person, note: name, title, tenure, LinkedIn activity, and signals of pain or initiative relevant to your product.

**So what:** Who is the right first contact? What's their likely priority right now?

### Step 5 -- Pain & Opportunity Mapping

Connect what you know to the problems your product solves.

- What symptoms of the problem can you observe externally? (job postings, tech stack, company stage, public statements)
- What are they likely doing today to solve this? (manual work, a competitor, nothing)
- What's the cost of inaction -- what gets worse if they don't address this?

Map to your value proposition:

> "They are likely experiencing [specific pain], which costs them [impact]. We solve this by [approach], which would specifically help them [concrete outcome]."

### Step 6 -- Competitive Context

- Already using a competing solution? (Check job descriptions, tech stack data, LinkedIn, G2/Capterra reviews)
- If yes: satisfaction level? Signals of dissatisfaction?
- If no: what are they using instead? (point solutions, manual, nothing)

**So what:** Displacing something, filling a gap, or pioneering? This shapes your angle.

### Step 7 -- Account Summary

Synthesize into a single commercial picture. 150-250 words, scannable in 60 seconds.

Structure:
1. Who they are (1-2 sentences)
2. Where they are right now -- strategic moment, growth stage
3. ICP fit verdict -- and the key reason
4. The angle -- most credible reason to reach out, tied to a specific signal
5. Recommended first contact -- who, why them, what to lead with
6. Open questions -- what you still don't know that would change your approach

### Step 8 -- Next Actions

Recommend the single best next step:

- **Initiate outreach** (specify: email, LinkedIn, call -- and to whom)
- **Escalate for review** (weak ICP, complex situation, or existing relationship)
- **Deprioritize** (poor fit -- explain why)
- **Monitor** (good fit but wrong timing -- set a trigger for when to revisit)

---

## Quality Check

Before delivering output, verify:
- You have a clear "why now" -- not just "this company looks interesting"
- The ICP assessment is honest, not optimistic
- The recommended contact is a real person, not a generic title
- The next action is specific and actionable
- You haven't reported facts without interpreting what they mean commercially
