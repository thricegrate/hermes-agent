# Pricing Frameworks Reference

## The 3 Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1: Single Asset ($9-99)
**Product types:**
- Single eBook or print book
- Stand-alone ultimate guide
- Swipe file (curated examples with breakdowns)
- Paid educational email course
- Single valuable template
- Template pack

**Rule:** Smaller asset = lower price. Larger/more in-depth = up to $99 ceiling.

### Tier 2: Bundle of Assets ($99-199)
**Product types:**
- Bundle of eBooks/books
- Bundle of ultimate guides
- Bundle of swipe files
- Bundle of templates/template packs

**Rule:** "More" of Tier 1. Bundling multiple smaller assets together.

### Tier 3: Full Text & Video Course ($199-350)
**Product types:**
- Complete course with multiple modules
- Video + text lessons
- Includes exercises, templates, community access

**Sweet spot: $350.** This is the recommended default.

## Why $350 Is the Sweet Spot

- Below $350: You're leaving money on the table. Most people would have paid $350 anyway.
- Above $350: Crosses the "impulse buy" threshold. Requires more deliberation, longer sales cycle.
- At $350: Maximizes per-sale revenue while staying in "low-ticket" territory.

## The Backward-Planning Method

1. Set price at $350
2. Ask: "What does my customer need to accomplish to make $350 a no-brainer?"
3. Calculate the 5x-10x upside: If the product enables $1,750-$3,500+ in value, $350 is cheap
4. Value types: money earned, money saved, time saved, stress reduced, status/opportunity gained

## 5 Proven Bonus Types

### 1. Templates
Bundle of actionable assets that help accomplish the product's core promise.
- Example: "The [X] Template Pack" - 10 plug-and-play templates
- Value range: $49-149

### 2. AI Prompts
Curated AI prompts that accelerate results.
- Example: "The [X] AI Prompt Library" - 25 tested prompts
- Value range: $29-99

### 3. Automations
Walkthrough guides for setting up automations related to the core promise.
- Example: "The [X] Automation Toolkit" - Zapier/Make workflows
- Value range: $49-149

### 4. Swipe Files
Curated collection of proven examples with breakdowns.
- Example: "The [X] Swipe File" - 50 analyzed examples
- Value range: $49-99

### 5. Behind-the-Scenes
Your proprietary process exposed.
- Example: "Inside My [X] Process" - video walkthrough
- Value range: $99-199

## Bonus Pricing Rules

1. Every bonus MUST have a dollar value attached
2. Without a dollar value, bonuses are perceived as worthless
3. Price each bonus as if you sold it independently
4. Total bonus value should EXCEED the product price
5. The math should make the customer feel like they're getting a steal

## The 3 Digital Business Models

### Recurring ($9-99/month)
- Paid newsletters, memberships, SaaS, communities
- High time commitment, ongoing customer expectations
- Examples: Skool groups, Patreon, paid Substacks

### Stand-Alone ($9-350) - START HERE
- One-time purchase, one-time delivery
- Lowest time commitment, easiest to start
- Building this teaches skills needed for all other models

### Experiential ($350-10,000)
- Cohort courses, coaching, masterminds, live events
- Most operational complexity
- Highest revenue per customer

### High-Ticket DFY/Coaching ($3,000-35,000+)
- Done-for-you services, 1:1 coaching, agency retainers
- Highest revenue per customer, lowest volume required
- Requires sales calls (see `high-ticket-closer`)
- This is the backend of the Automatic Clients model

## The 3 Levels of Customers (Pricing Strategy)

Every prospect falls into one of three tiers. Design offers for each:

### Level 1: DIY (Do It Yourself)
- **Profile**: Has time, limited budget, wants to learn
- **Price sensitivity**: High. They compare prices.
- **What to sell**: Frontend products ($5-97), courses ($99-350)
- **Pricing psychology**: Price anchoring, bonus stacking, unconditional guarantee

### Level 2: DWY (Done With You)
- **Profile**: Has some time and budget, wants guidance
- **Price sensitivity**: Medium. They compare value, not price.
- **What to sell**: Group coaching, masterminds, cohort programs ($997-$10K)
- **Pricing psychology**: Payment plans, conditional guarantee, ROI framing, scarcity (limited spots)

### Level 3: DFY (Done For You)
- **Profile**: Has budget, limited time, wants results fast
- **Price sensitivity**: Low. They care about outcomes.
- **What to sell**: Agency services, 1:1 implementation ($5K-$35K+)
- **Pricing psychology**: Performance guarantee, premium positioning, exclusivity

### Pricing across all 3 levels:
The most powerful business model serves all three levels:
- **Level 1 frontend** feeds buyer volume (hundreds/day)
- **Level 2 program** converts engaged buyers (dozens/month)
- **Level 3 service** converts premium clients (handful/month)
- Total revenue = (L1 volume x L1 price) + (L2 volume x L2 price) + (L3 volume x L3 price)

## OTO (One-Time Offer) Pricing

### OTO Pricing Rules:
- **OTO 1**: 1.5-3x the frontend price. Complements the main purchase directly.
- **OTO 2**: 0.5-1x the frontend price. Downsell or related offer.
- **Each OTO**: Must deliver standalone value. Buyer should not regret purchase even without the frontend.

### OTO Conversion Benchmarks:
| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| OTO 1 take rate | 15-25% |
| OTO 2 take rate | 5-10% |
| Revenue multiplier | 1.3-1.8x frontend price per buyer |

### OTO Design Principles:
- One offer per page. Zero distractions.
- "This offer is only available right now" urgency.
- Single CTA: "Yes, Add This" / "No Thanks"
- The OTO solves the NEXT problem the buyer will face after consuming the frontend.

## Guarantees Framework

### 4 Guarantee Types:

**1. Unconditional (Money-Back)**
- Full refund within X days, no questions asked
- Best for: Tier 0-1 ($5-99)
- Removes ALL buyer risk
- Example: "30-day money-back guarantee"

**2. Conditional (Action-Based)**
- Full refund IF the buyer completes specific actions and doesn't get results
- Best for: Tier 2-3 ($99-350)
- Shows confidence + ensures buyer effort
- Example: "Complete all modules within 60 days. If you don't see results, full refund."

**3. Performance-Based (Outcome)**
- Specific measurable outcome guaranteed or money back
- Best for: Tier 4 ($3K+) where you control implementation
- Strongest trust signal, but only use if you can deliver
- Example: "If you don't get 3 new clients in 90 days, we refund 100%."

**4. Anti-Guarantee (No Refund)**
- All sales final, with strong justification
- Best for: Premium/limited, personalized services
- Screens for committed buyers only
- Example: "Due to the custom nature of this work, all sales are final."

### Guarantee Selection Matrix:
| Price Point | Recommended | Why |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| $5-99 | Unconditional | Risk is low, refund requests are minimal, removes friction |
| $99-350 | Conditional | Balances buyer protection with ensuring they do the work |
| $3K-$10K | Performance or Conditional | Must be backed by real confidence in your delivery |
| $10K+ | Performance or Anti | At this level, buyers expect either strong guarantees or premium exclusivity |

## Value Stack Methodology

A value stack is NOT just a list of bonuses. Each item in the stack must solve a specific OBSTACLE that stands between the buyer and the promised result.

### The Obstacle-Based Value Stack:

1. **Identify the promised result** (the main transformation)
2. **List every obstacle** between the buyer and that result (5-10 obstacles)
3. **For each obstacle**, design a specific solution (bonus, tool, resource, access)
4. **Assign a dollar value** based on what it would cost to solve that obstacle independently
5. **Stack the solutions** so the total value dramatically exceeds the price

### Example:
**Product**: "Launch Your Newsletter" course ($350)
**Promised result**: Launch a newsletter with 1,000 subscribers in 90 days

| Obstacle | Solution (Bonus) | Value |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| "I don't know what to write about" | 52-Week Content Calendar Template | $97 |
| "I can't design a good landing page" | 5 High-Converting Landing Page Templates | $149 |
| "I don't know how to write emails" | Email Swipe File (50 Subject Lines + 10 Full Emails) | $79 |
| "I don't have time to post on social media" | 30-Day Social Media Content Pack | $99 |

**Total bonus value**: $424
**Product price**: $350
**Perceived value**: $774 for $350 = "steal"

### Key principle:
Every bonus answers a specific "but what about..." objection. This is different from random extras. Each bonus is a strategic choice designed to remove one more excuse not to buy.
