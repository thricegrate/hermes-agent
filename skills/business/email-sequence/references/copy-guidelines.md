## Structure

1. **Hook**: First line grabs attention
2. **Context**: Why this matters to them
3. **Value**: The useful content
4. **CTA**: What to do next
5. **Sign-off**: Human, warm close

## Formatting

- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- White space between sections
- Bullet points for scanability
- Bold for emphasis (sparingly)
- Mobile-first (most read on phone)

## Tone

- Conversational, not formal
- First-person (I/we) and second-person (you)
- Active voice
- Match your brand but lean friendly
- Read it out loud, does it sound human?

## Length

- Shorter is usually better
- 50-125 words for transactional
- 150-300 words for educational
- 300-500 words for story-driven
- If it's long, it better be good

## CTA Buttons vs. Links

- Buttons: Primary actions, high-visibility
- Links: Secondary actions, in-text
- One clear primary CTA per email
- Button text: Action + outcome

## Personalization

Personalized emails drive 760% more revenue than generic blasts (CampaignMonitor). The goal: every email should feel like it was written for THIS person at THIS moment.

### Merge Fields
- First name (fallback to "there" or "friend" -- never send bare `{{first_name}}`)
- Company name (B2B)
- Relevant data (usage, plan, last purchase, etc.)
- Always test what happens when data is missing before sending

### Personalized CTAs (202% more conversions)
Personalized calls-to-action convert 202% better than generic ones (HubSpot). Match the CTA to the reader's context:
- Purchase history: "Loved [Previous Purchase]? Try this"
- Browse behavior: "Get back to your wishlist, [Name]"
- Journey stage: New subscriber CTA differs from long-term customer CTA
- Content interest: "Dive deeper into [Topic], [Name]"
- Abandoned cart: "[Name], forgot something?"

### Dynamic Content
- Based on segment (different content blocks for different audiences)
- Based on behavior (openers get different emails than non-openers)
- Based on stage (trial, paid, long-term, at-risk)
- P.S. section: personalize by engagement level or interest area

### Sender Identity
- Emails from a person outperform emails from a brand name
- Use a real name and face in the "From" field
- Consistent sender builds recognition and trust

### Triggered Emails
- Action-based sends (more relevant than time-based)
- Behavioral triggers: feature used, milestone hit, inactivity, link clicked, cart abandoned
- Conditional follow-ups: IF no open → resend with new subject. IF clicked but didn't buy → objection-handling sequence.

### Don't Be Creepy
- Personalization should make readers feel valued, not watched
- Provide context when referencing behavior ("Since you downloaded our guide on X...")
- Avoid overly specific tracking language ("We noticed you visited our pricing page 3 times")
- Respect privacy: CAN-SPAM, GDPR, clear opt-out

## Segmentation Strategies

### By Behavior
- Openers vs. non-openers
- Clickers vs. non-clickers
- Active vs. inactive

### By Stage
- Trial vs. paid
- New vs. long-term
- Engaged vs. at-risk

### By Profile
- Industry/role (B2B)
- Use case / goal
- Company size

## Testing and Optimization

### What to Test
- Subject lines (highest impact)
- Send times
- Email length
- CTA placement and copy
- Personalization level
- Sequence timing

### How to Test
- A/B test one variable at a time
- Sufficient sample size
- Statistical significance
- Document learnings

### Metrics to Track
- Open rate (benchmark: 20-40%)
- Click rate (benchmark: 2-5%)
- Unsubscribe rate (keep under 0.5%)
- Conversion rate (specific to sequence goal)
- Revenue per email (if applicable)
