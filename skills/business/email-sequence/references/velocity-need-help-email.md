# The "Need Help?" Email Framework (Velocity)

**Source:** Daniel Bustamante (Velocity). One email generated 11 warm lead applications + 120 cohort waitlist signups. His company sends 2 of these per week, generating 130+ qualified calls.

## When to Use

Send 1-2 "Need Help?" emails per month minimum. These are direct-response conversion emails that feel helpful, not pushy. Best placed:
- After a value-heavy sequence (3-5 value emails, then 1 ask)
- When launching a new offer or service
- When reactivating a cold segment
- Monthly as part of regular newsletter cadence

## The 6-Part Framework

### 1. Personal Open
Start casual and direct. One-to-one feeling despite mass send.
- No "Dear subscriber" or formal greeting
- Write like texting a friend who asked for advice
- Example: "Hey -- quick question for you today."

### 2. Pain Point Recognition
Identify specific, timely challenges the audience faces RIGHT NOW.
- Be specific, not generic ("struggling to get leads" not "having trouble with marketing")
- Reference current events or seasonal context when possible
- Show you understand their situation (builds trust before the ask)

### 3. Value Offer
Present yourself as having solutions. Keep it brief -- don't over-explain.
- 1-2 sentences max
- Focus on the OUTCOME you deliver, not the process
- Example: "I've been helping founders build lead magnet funnels that convert at 40%+."

### 4. Offer Showcase
Outline 1-2 specific offers or ways you could help. Bullet points, outcome-focused.
- Each bullet = a specific result or deliverable
- Use numbers and specifics ("5-email welcome sequence" not "email help")
- Maximum 2 offers -- more = decision paralysis

### 5. Proof
1-2 sentences of credibility. Brief. Not a case study.
- "Last month we helped [type of client] achieve [specific result]"
- Or: "[X] founders have used this to [outcome]"
- Social proof, not self-promotion

### 6. Call-to-Action
ONE simple, low-friction action step.
- "Reply to this email with [keyword]"
- Or: "Book a 15-min call here: [link]"
- NEVER two CTAs. One action. Make it obvious.

## Key Tactical Insights

**Frequency matters:** "At least 1-2 well-executed ask emails per month." Velocity sends 2 per week and generates 130+ qualified calls. Most people under-ask.

**Tone is critical:** Helpful, not pushy. The email should feel like a friend offering to help, not a salesperson closing.

**Results over features:** Every line should focus on outcomes. "Build a funnel that converts at 40%" not "Get access to our funnel building methodology."

## Confirmation Page Upsell (Post-Opt-In)

After someone opts in to a lead magnet, the confirmation/thank-you page is the highest-attention moment. Don't waste it with just "check your email."

Velocity's model:
1. Deliver the promised lead magnet immediately (builds trust)
2. Transition to upsell while attention is highest
3. Personal intro establishing authority
4. Free offer with exclusivity gate (application = lead qualification)

Example: "As promised, here's your [Lead Magnet]. Now if you'd like a personalized [Higher Value Thing] for free, read on..."

The "application" model qualifies leads without being salesy:
- "Not everyone will qualify" (scarcity)
- "Best suited for established businesses" (honest filter)
- "Even if not selected, you get a free resource" (zero risk)
- Survey data = market research gold regardless of conversion
