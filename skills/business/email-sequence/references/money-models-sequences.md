## Upsell Ladder Sequence

**Trigger:** 3-7 days after initial purchase delivery (buyer has had time to consume the product)
**Length:** 5-7 emails over 10-14 days
**Goal:** Move buyers through the upsell tier using Classic, Menu, and Anchor plays
**Pitch level:** 40-60% (value-led but with clear offers)

### When to use
- After any frontend purchase ($5-$97)
- After a challenge/program completion
- After a service delivery milestone

### Sequence Blueprint

**Email 1: Value Check-In (Day 3)**
- Subject pattern: "Quick question about [product they bought]"
- Body: Ask if they've used it. Share one quick tip they might have missed. Zero pitch.
- CTA: Reply with their biggest takeaway or question
- Purpose: Re-engage, ensure consumption, open a conversation

**Email 2: Case Study / Result (Day 5)**
- Subject pattern: "What [Name] did after [product] that changed everything"
- Body: Share a case study of someone who bought the same product and then went deeper. Show the specific result they got from the NEXT step.
- CTA: "Want to see how?" (soft tease of upsell, no price yet)
- Purpose: Plant the seed that there IS a next level

**Email 3: Classic Upsell -- Reveal the Next Problem (Day 7)**
- Subject pattern: "The part nobody talks about after [result from product]"
- Body: Name the next problem that naturally follows solving the first one. "You've done X. But now you're probably noticing Y." Position the upsell as the solution to Y.
- CTA: Link to upsell sales page
- Price: 1.5-3x the initial purchase
- Purpose: Direct offer for the next logical step

**Email 4: Menu Upsell -- Options (Day 9)**
- Subject pattern: "3 ways to go further (pick the one that fits)"
- Body: Present 2-3 options at different price points or with different focuses. Use the Prestige Labs framework: Unsell (what they DON'T need), Prescribe (what fits their situation), Offer A or B.
- CTA: Choose your path
- Purpose: Give choice, not yes/no. Increases conversion by removing decision paralysis.

**Email 5: Anchor Reveal (Day 11)**
- Subject pattern: "The $[big number] version (and why most people don't need it)"
- Body: Show the premium/enterprise/done-for-you option with its full price. Then reframe: "Most people get everything they need with [mid-tier] for just $[price]." The anchor makes the mid-tier feel cheap.
- CTA: Link to mid-tier offer
- Purpose: Price anchoring. The big number reframes everything.

**Email 6: Social Proof Stack (Day 12)**
- Subject pattern: "[3 results] from people who took the next step"
- Body: 3 short testimonials/results from people who upgraded. Each one should address a different objection (time, money, complexity).
- CTA: "Ready to join them?"
- Purpose: Overcome remaining objections through proof

**Email 7: Soft Urgency (Day 14)**
- Subject pattern: "Last chance for [specific benefit or bonus]"
- Body: If there's a genuine enrollment window, bonus expiration, or price change coming, state it. If not, use natural urgency: "The longer you wait, the longer before you see [result]."
- CTA: Final link to upsell
- Purpose: Close the sequence. Move non-buyers to nurture list.

## Downsell Recovery Sequence

**Trigger:** After someone declines an upsell, abandons a higher-tier checkout, OR at the end of the upsell ladder sequence (non-buyers)
**Length:** 3-5 emails over 5-7 days
**Goal:** Capture revenue from "no" through alternative payment structures, feature-limited options, or trials
**Pitch level:** 30-50% (empathetic, not pushy)

**Key principle:** Never discount the core price. Change the terms instead.

### When to use
- After upsell decline (checkout abandonment or ignored upsell emails)
- After a refund request (offer alternative before processing)
- After subscription cancellation (save offer)

### Sequence Blueprint

**Email 1: Acknowledgment + Question (Day 1 after decline)**
- Subject pattern: "No worries -- one quick question though"
- Body: Acknowledge they passed on the offer. Don't be passive-aggressive. Ask ONE question: "Was it the price, the timing, or something else?" This intel informs which downsell to present.
- CTA: Reply with their reason
- Purpose: Gather objection data AND re-engage

**Email 2: Payment Plan Alternative (Day 3)**
- Subject pattern: "What if you could spread it out?"
- Body: "A lot of people loved [product] but the timing wasn't right for a single payment." Offer installments that total slightly MORE than the one-time price (15-25% premium). Frame as convenience, not discount.
- CTA: Link to payment plan checkout
- Purpose: Capture price-objection buyers without discounting

**Email 3: Feature-Limited Alternative (Day 5)**
- Subject pattern: "A lighter version that might be a better fit"
- Body: Present a stripped-down version at 40-60% of the full price. Be transparent about what's included and what's not. Position it as "everything you need to get [core result] without the extras."
- CTA: Link to basic tier
- Purpose: Capture buyers who don't need/want everything

**Email 4: Free Trial (Day 6, optional)**
- Subject pattern: "Try it for [7/14] days -- no commitment"
- Body: If applicable, offer full access for a limited time. Require credit card. "If it's not for you after [X] days, cancel with one click."
- CTA: Start free trial
- Purpose: Remove all risk. Let the product sell itself.

**Email 5: Final Alternative + Close (Day 7)**
- Subject pattern: "One more option before I stop asking"
- Body: Summarize all available options (full price, payment plan, basic tier, trial). "I've shown you every way in. Pick what works or tell me to stop." Direct, honest, no manipulation.
- CTA: Links to all options
- Purpose: Final touch. Move non-buyers to long-term nurture.

## Continuity Conversion Sequence

**Trigger:** After one-time purchase, at end of a program/cohort, or after 30-60 days of product usage
**Length:** 5 emails over 10-15 days
**Goal:** Convert one-time buyers into recurring members/subscribers
**Pitch level:** 30-50% (value demonstration, not hard sell)

This sequence has 3 sub-variants matching the 3 continuity plays. Choose the variant that fits your business model, or blend elements from multiple variants.

### Variant A: Bonus Continuity ("You'll lose access to...")

Best for: Communities, memberships, platforms with exclusive features.

**Email 1: Exclusive Preview (Day 1)**
- Subject: "Something only [product] buyers get to see"
- Body: Share an exclusive resource, tool, or content piece available only to active members. Don't pitch yet. Just deliver value and mention it's a members-only thing.

**Email 2: Community Proof (Day 4)**
- Subject: "[Name] just shared this in the members area"
- Body: Show a real result/conversation from the community. The reader should feel like they're missing out on an active, valuable group.

**Email 3: The "What You'd Lose" Email (Day 7)**
- Subject: "Here's what goes away if you don't join"
- Body: Clearly list what's exclusive to members. Frame it as loss, not gain. People are more motivated by avoiding loss than acquiring gain.

**Email 4: Invitation (Day 10)**
- Subject: "Your spot is open"
- Body: Direct invitation to join with pricing. Emphasize the exclusive bonus they keep as long as they're active.

**Email 5: Urgency / Scarcity (Day 13)**
- Subject: "Closing enrollment [date]" or "Founding member rate ends [date]"
- Body: If genuine scarcity exists, state it. Close the sequence.

### Variant B: Discount Continuity ("Lock in this rate...")

Best for: SaaS, media subscriptions, membership sites.

**Email 1-2:** Same as Variant A (value + proof)

**Email 3: Rate Comparison (Day 7)**
- Subject: "Monthly vs annual -- the math"
- Body: Show the savings clearly. "$49/mo = $588/yr. Annual = $468/yr. You save $120." Simple math, no manipulation.

**Email 4: Founding Rate (Day 10)**
- Subject: "Lock in $29/mo forever (before it goes to $49)"
- Body: If offering a founding/early rate, make it genuinely limited. "First 100 members get this rate locked forever. 37 spots left."

**Email 5: Deadline (Day 13)**
- Subject: "Rate goes up [date]"
- Body: Final call with clear deadline.

### Variant C: Waive Fee Continuity ("Your setup fee is waived...")

Best for: Gyms, premium services, platforms with onboarding costs.

**Email 1-2:** Same as Variant A (value + proof)

**Email 3: Setup Fee Education (Day 7)**
- Subject: "Why we charge a setup fee (and how to skip it)"
- Body: Explain the real cost of onboarding/setup. Then: "Active members never pay this. It's waived as long as you're subscribed."

**Email 4: Activation Offer (Day 10)**
- Subject: "Start now, setup fee waived"
- Body: Direct offer. "Join today, your $200 setup fee is waived. Cancel and rejoin later? You'll pay it."

**Email 5: Pause Option (Day 13)**
- Subject: "Not ready? Pause instead of canceling"
- Body: Offer a pause option that preserves the fee waiver. "Pause for up to 60 days. Your rate and waiver stay intact."

## Sequence Integration Notes

**How these connect to existing sequences:**
- Upsell Ladder runs AFTER the standard post-purchase sequence (see `references/sequence-blueprints.md` Part 4)
- Downsell Recovery triggers on upsell non-conversion (branching logic in email platform)
- Continuity Conversion runs AFTER a one-time product lifecycle ends (course completion, program end, 30-60 day mark)

**Sequencing order:**
```
Purchase --> Post-Purchase (existing) --> Upsell Ladder --> [converts?]
                                                              |
                                              Yes: Continuity Conversion (30-60 days later)
                                              No: Downsell Recovery --> [converts?]
                                                                          |
                                                          Yes: Continuity Conversion
                                                          No: Long-term nurture
```

**For the 15 named plays these sequences implement**, see `product-offer/references/money-models-plays.md`.
