# Email Marketing Frameworks Reference

## The Million-Dollar Funnel Email Strategy

Email is the real revenue channel. Social media drives traffic. Email converts.

**Key insight:** The average number of emails needed to convert a free subscriber to a buyer is far more than most creators think. More emails = more money. All data confirms this.

## Email Cadence

### Beginner Cadence
- **1 email per week** (free-value) to entire list
- Send on the same day every week (Monday recommended)
- 800-1,500 words
- Never miss a week - consistency builds trust

### Advanced Cadence
- **Monday:** Free-value email to entire list
- **Thursday:** Objection-handling email to "Clickers" ONLY
- "Clickers" = people who clicked the product landing page link but did NOT purchase

## Email Segments

| Segment | Who | Receives |
|---------|-----|----------|
| All Subscribers | Everyone on list | Weekly nurture emails |
| EEC In-Progress | Taking the 5-day course | EEC emails only |
| EEC Completed | Finished 5-day course | Weekly nurture + transition email |
| Clickers | Clicked product link, didn't buy | Objection-handling emails |
| Customers | Have purchased | Post-purchase emails, excluded from sales emails |

## Educational Email Course (EEC) Framework

### Purpose
Free 5-day email course used as an opt-in lead magnet. Teaches a quick win related to the product's core problem.

### Design Principles
- Pick ONE specific, achievable quick win
- Each email = one lesson
- 400-800 words per email
- Day 5 bridges naturally to the paid product
- Subject lines should be specific and curiosity-driven

### 5-Day Arc
1. **Day 1 - The Hook:** Introduce the problem, your credibility, preview what they'll learn
2. **Day 2 - The Foundation:** Teach the core concept/framework they need first
3. **Day 3 - The How-To:** Step-by-step methodology with a concrete example
4. **Day 4 - The Mistake:** Common mistake or myth (what NOT to do)
5. **Day 5 - The Bridge:** Celebrate progress, show the gap, pitch the paid product

## Weekly Nurture Email Structure

1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) - Story, observation, or provocative statement
2. **Lesson** (main body) - One concept from the product's problem space
3. **Example** - Show the concept in action with real/realistic scenario
4. **Takeaway** - One clear action item the reader can do today
5. **CTA** - Link to product landing page (present every time)
6. **P.S.** - Optional second CTA or personal note

## Objection-Handling Email Structure

### 3-Part Framework
1. **The Question:** State the objection they have (as a question from a hypothetical reader)
2. **The Answer:** Address it directly, reframe their belief
3. **Social Proof:** Testimonial from someone who had the same objection and bought anyway

### 7 Universal Objections

| # | Objection | Reframe Strategy |
|---|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | "Not sure this is for me" | Describe the ideal student profile clearly |
| 2 | "Don't have time" | Show minimal time commitment + ROI on time invested |
| 3 | "Can find this for free" | Structured framework vs. scattered free content |
| 4 | "Too expensive" | Price anchor against ROI and bonus stack |
| 5 | "Not ready yet" | Readiness is a myth - you become ready by starting |
| 6 | "What if it doesn't work?" | Guarantee + testimonials of specific results |
| 7 | "Tried similar things" | What makes this approach uniquely effective |

## Post-Purchase Email Principles

- **Immediate:** Send welcome email right after purchase
- **Clear:** Micro-step instructions for accessing the product
- **Warm:** Congratulatory tone, excitement for their journey
- **Actionable:** Tell them exactly what to do first
- **Follow-up:** Skool invite email for platform access

## Subject Line Patterns That Work

- **Curiosity gap:** "The [concept] nobody talks about"
- **Specificity:** "How I [specific result] in [timeframe]"
- **Direct benefit:** "[Number] ways to [desirable outcome]"
- **Story hook:** "I almost [dramatic moment]..."
- **Question:** "Are you making this [niche] mistake?"
- **Social proof:** "[Number] [people] already [doing/using] this"
