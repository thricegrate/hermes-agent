## 1. The Two-Phase Launch Model

### Educational Phase (~2 weeks, near-daily emails)
Goal: Build rapport, frame yourself as authority, educate subscribers on the problem your course solves.
- Welcome email (immediate)
- Question email ("What is your #1 challenge with [topic]?")
- Newsletter/blog content emails (case studies, ebooks, videos, webinars)

### Selling Phase (6 emails in sequence)
1. Announce the course (link to prior content, explain modules)
2. Open the course (2-3 CTAs to sales page, statistics, testimonials)
3. Give bonuses (bonus gift to incentivize buying now)
4. Retention email (re-engage apathetic subscribers, show what they're missing)
5. Thank-you email (social proof, squeeze additional sales)
6. Announce cart closes (up to 3 emails on closing day)

**Principle:** "In the beginning you have to build rapport and educate your subscribers and then you must try to sell."

## 2. Email Templates by Type

### Welcome Emails (3 + 1 Bonus)

**Welcome #1 — Pain-Agitate-Solution Opener**
- Subject: "I have a great announcement to make."
- Structure: Open with years of struggle → announce course coming in [X] days → set expectations
- CTA: Reply with their #1 challenge

**Welcome #2 — Expectation Setting**
- Subject: "Welcome to [company]"
- Structure: Set email frequency → share recent favorites → ask about hopes/dreams
- CTA: Click a link to read best content

**Welcome #3 — Segmentation Question**
- Subject: "Welcome to [Company]"
- Structure: Ask subscriber's #1 challenge → direct to social media
- CTA: Reply with challenge (data for segmentation)

**Welcome Bonus — Post-Registration**
- Subject: "Welcome on-board!"
- Structure: Orient new student to community, profile, blog
- CTA: Complete profile, join community

### Question Emails (2)

**Question #1 — Direct Ask**
- Subject: "Let's get to know each other better!"
- Structure: "What is your biggest struggle in [subject]?" Short, conversational, no selling.
- Purpose: Segmentation data + deliverability boost (replies)

**Question #2 — Framed as Helping Them**
- Subject: "I have been wanting to ask you..."
- Structure: "Which is the best piece of advice to receive for [subject]?"
- Purpose: Content ideas + engagement

### Newsletter/Content Emails (5)

**Newsletter #1 — Problem Agitation**
- Subject: "How to break out of [adverse situation]"
- Structure: 2 personal questions → call out pain → offer top strategies via content link

**Newsletter #2 — Contrarian**
- Subject: "[unwanted behavior] will backfire. Do this instead."
- Structure: Dream/magic wand opener → links to blog post → drives to video

**Newsletter #3 — Reverse Psychology**
- Subject: "This video is probably not for you [name]"
- Structure: Build curiosity → screen the right audience with a question → reveal controversial techniques

**Newsletter #4 — Curated Content**
- Subject: "[Name] I hand-picked these articles for you!"
- Structure: 3 curated articles on course topic

**Newsletter #5 — Guide Primer**
- Subject: "[Name], have you read this?"
- Structure: Drives to comprehensive guide as course primer

### Course Announcement Emails (4)

**Announcement #1 — Standard Pain-Solution**
- Subject: "I have a great announcement to make."
- Structure: Pain acknowledgment → "This is why I created..." → benefit bullets

**Announcement #2 — Empathy Bridge**
- Subject: "I think you might like that."
- Structure: Acknowledge they're tired of [problem] → bridge to course → learning bullets

**Announcement #3 — Transformation Promise**
- Subject: "You are lucky [name]"
- Structure: Transformation promise → limited spots → testimonials → launch date

**Announcement #4 — Mindset Shift**
- Subject: "The Effortless Way to [desire]"
- Structure: Mindset shift framing → course section breakdown → "less effort than what you're doing now"

### Course Opening Emails (3)

**Opening #1 — Social Proof Launch**
- Subject: "[Course] is open! (Go, go, go...)"
- Structure: "200 people signed up" → testimonial → discount deadline urgency

**Opening #2 — Interrupt Pattern**
- Subject: "Stop what you are doing."
- Structure: Course is live CTA → FAQ in body → "helicopter ride to the top" analogy

**Opening #3 — Self-Responsibility**
- Subject: "This is standing in the way of the life you want."
- Structure: Bonus disappears at midnight → real transformation stories → "if anything is standing in your way, it's YOU"

### Bonus/Gift Emails (3)

**Bonus #1 — Simple Discount**
- Subject: "I am saving an offer for you"
- Structure: Short, direct → 20% off discount code → link

**Bonus #2 — Stacked Gifts**
- Subject: "I almost NEVER do this, but I'm doing it for you."
- Structure: 72-hour window → 2+ exclusive gifts (webinar, resource) → stacked urgency

**Bonus #3 — Free Resource**
- Subject: "Free [Ebook/Course] Inside!"
- Structure: Benefit-listed free resource → limited time only

### Cart Close Emails (2)

**Cart Close #1 — Deadline + Bonuses**
- Subject: "Last chance to claim your spot on [course]"
- Structure: "Tonight it closes" → 2 bonuses for enrolling before midnight → P.S. with testimonials

**Cart Close #2 — Scarcity**
- Subject: "Last chance to claim your spot."
- Structure: "Almost completely sold out" → specific spot count → "if you were sitting around thinking... it won't be there"

### Review Request Emails (2)

**Review #1 — Direct Ask**
- Subject: "Tell me something [name]"
- Structure: Direct feedback request → "I truly LOVE my students" personalization

**Review #2 — Survey First**
- Subject: "Your opinion matters..."
- Structure: 5-question survey (3 minutes) → then review site link

### Re-Engagement Emails (2)

**Re-engagement #1 — Discount Win-Back**
- Subject: "How's everything?"
- Structure: Very short → 25% off discount → no explanation of absence

**Re-engagement #2 — Preference Center**
- Subject: "[Name], is everything OK?"
- Structure: Acknowledges not opening → 4 clickable reasons why (preference center) → offers unsubscribe

### Thank You Emails (2)

- Subject: Various
- Structure: "I have to pinch myself that I get to wake up every day and do this for a living." Relationship-building close, no selling.

### Nurture/Behind-the-Scenes Emails (2)

**Nurture #1 — Event Tease**
- Subject: "I have good news!"
- Structure: Instructor travels weekly for classes → check schedule

**Nurture #2 — Behind the Scenes**
- Subject: "So, here's something you didn't know."
- Structure: Update on school growth → tease upcoming course material

## 4. Copy Frameworks

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
1. **Before:** Show painful current situation
2. **After:** Show what life looks like with problem solved
3. **Bridge:** Present your course/product as the bridge

### Recurring Structural Pattern
1. Pain acknowledgment opener ("I know exactly what you went through")
2. Bridge to solution ("This is why I created...")
3. Benefit bullets (3-4 items)
4. Testimonial placement (before or after CTA)
5. Urgency mechanism (deadline, limited spots, midnight cutoff)
6. P.S. as second testimonial or CTA

## 5. Segmentation: The "Ask" Bucket Model (Ryan Levesque)

**Minimum segmentation:** Split into two buckets — Leads and Customers. Never send buy-now messages to existing buyers.

**Expanded:** Warm leads, hot leads, customers (3 buckets, each receives different emails).

**Segmentation question:** "What is the #1 most significant challenge you have related to [topic]?" — asked early in the sequence. Use responses to create buckets.

## 6. Benchmark Data

| Email Type | Open Rate | CTR |
|------------|-----------|-----|
| Triggered emails | 45.70% | 10.75% |
| Autoresponder emails | 34.80% | 6.56% |
| Newsletter emails | 22.83% | 3.48% |

**Key insight:** Triggered emails outperform broadcast by 2x on opens and 3x on clicks. Frame launches as triggered sequences, not broadcast campaigns.

**Single CTA rule:** One CTA per email increases clicks 371% and sales 1617% (WordStream).

**Deliverability move:** In welcome email, ask subscribers to drag from Promotions to Primary folder or add to contact list.

## 7. Psychology Tactics in Templates

| Tactic | How to Apply | Where |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Priming** | Repeat key language so subscribers associate a word with your brand | All emails |
| **Social Proof** | Testimonials, case studies, community size numbers | Opening, cart close |
| **Clustering** | Bullet points for scannability and memory retention | Feature lists |
| **Scarcity** | Limited seats, midnight deadline, specific remaining numbers | Cart close |
| **Curiosity Gap** | "DON'T click this link yet" reverse psychology | Newsletter |
