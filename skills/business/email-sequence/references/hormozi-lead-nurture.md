## Core Framework: Respond / Schedule / Show

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook

The entire lead nurture system boils down to three numbers:

- **Schedule Rate** = % of engaged leads who schedule an appointment
- **Show Rate** = % of scheduled leads who actually show up
- **Throughput** = Schedule Rate x Show Rate = total % of engaged leads that show

**Example:** 100 leads, 50% schedule, 50% show = 25% throughput. Improving either rate by even 10% has a compounding effect on revenue because these multiply together, not add.

Everything in this reference file exists to push Schedule Rate and Show Rate higher. No additional ad spend required. Just better handling of leads you already paid for.

## Four Pillars of Lead Nurture

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 5-37
**Basis:** Machine learning analysis of 4,000+ daily appointments (ALAN software). Data-driven, not opinion-based.

Optimizing all four pillars can increase revenue 20-40% with zero additional ad spend. This covers medium-term (30-day) nurture, not long-term follow-up.

### Pillar I: Availability

The most correlated factor with throughput. More open slots = more scheduled appointments = more revenue. Simple math most businesses ignore.

**Requirements:**
- **7 days/week** appointment availability (including weekends)
- **Extended hours:** 6am-6pm PST for nationwide reach (adjust for local)
- **15-minute scheduling increments** (4 slots per hour, not 2 or 3)
- **Three scheduling paths:** inbound (they call you), outbound (you call them), self-scheduling (they book online)
- **Self-scheduling page optimization:** header confirms they're in the right place, visible dates/times without scrolling, eliminate every redundant form field

**Why it matters:** Every hour you're closed is an hour a lead can't book. Every 30-minute increment you use instead of 15-minute means half the slots. Most businesses restrict availability out of comfort, not strategy.

### Pillar II: Speed

Three distinct sub-types of speed, each independently important:

**1. Speed to First Contact**
- Target: under 5 minutes from opt-in
- Ideal: under 60 seconds
- **Stat: 391% increase in conversions when contacted within 60 seconds vs. waiting even 5 minutes**
- The lead is hottest the instant they raise their hand. Every minute that passes, they cool off, get distracted, or opt in somewhere else.

**2. Speed to First Appointment**
- Target: within 72 hours of first contact
- Ideal: same-day appointment
- Use the Pull-Forward Script (Section 7) to move appointments up
- The longer between contact and appointment, the lower the show rate

**3. Speed of Response**
- Respond to lead messages immediately between appointments
- If a lead texts you at 2pm asking a question, answer before your 2:30 appointment
- Speed of response signals competence and care. Slow response signals "you're not important."

### Pillar III: Personalization

Six specific tactics, each independently effective:

**Tactic 1: Use the lead's preferred communication method**
- Contact them everywhere first (call, text, email, DM)
- Continue the conversation on whichever channel they respond to
- Don't force phone calls on people who prefer texting

**Tactic 2: Qualify leads**
- Use application-based filtering to remove bad fits before they take a slot
- Cancel unqualified appointments (politely, with free value as a parting gift)
- Unqualified leads on your calendar steal slots from qualified leads

**Tactic 3: Best leads to best closers**
- Score leads 1-5 or Red/Yellow/Green
- Route high-score leads to your highest-performing closers
- Don't let your best leads get handled by your newest rep

**Tactic 4: Segment messaging**
- Easy version: spend 5 minutes researching each lead before their call (LinkedIn, company, past purchases)
- Hard version: build automated sequences segmented by lead source, industry, or pain point
- Generic follow-up gets generic results

**Tactic 5: Incentivize showing up**
- Use Push gifts and Pull bribes (see Push/Pull section below)
- Make attendance feel valuable beyond the pitch itself

**Tactic 6: Demonstrate matched proof**
- Match testimonials and case studies to the lead's demographics
- If the lead is a dentist, show them dentist case studies
- If the lead is a woman in her 40s, show proof from women in their 40s
- Generic proof is weak. Matched proof is compelling.

### Pillar IV: Volume

The number of reach-out attempts before giving up. This is where most salespeople fail hardest.

**The stat:** Most salespeople quit after 1.3 attempts. One call, maybe a follow-up text. Done. That's leaving money on the sidewalk.

**The cadence (full detail in Section 4):**
- Day 1: double-dial + text, 3 times throughout the day
- Days 2-3: 2 calls per day (early + late) + text after second call
- Days 4-7: 1 call + 1 text per day
- After Day 7: transition to long-term nurture

**Principle:** Volume negates luck. If you contact enough leads enough times, the math works regardless of any single interaction.

## Push/Pull Incentive System

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 24-25

Two types of incentives to increase show rates. Both are effective. Use them together for maximum impact.

### Push Incentives (Gifts)

Send a no-strings gift BEFORE the appointment. Triggers reciprocity.

**How it works:**
- "Hey [Name]! I just sent you a $5 gift card to make sure you're caffeinated for our meeting tomorrow."
- The gift arrives before the appointment, no conditions attached
- Works even when people know exactly what you're doing
- The reciprocity instinct is hardwired. Knowing about it doesn't neutralize it.

**Gift ideas:** Coffee gift cards, relevant small tools, branded swag, short helpful resource

### Pull Incentives (Bribes / A-B Choice)

Promise something valuable AT the appointment. Uses A/B choice framing to assume attendance.

**How it works:**
- "Do you prefer the black or the pink shirt?" (Both options assume they're showing up)
- The lead should genuinely want the thing
- It should connect to your offer
- You should show you incurred real cost preparing it

**A/B Offer Categories:**
- Quantity ("Would you prefer the 3-pack or the 5-pack?")
- Start dates ("Would Monday or Wednesday work better?")
- Type ("Are you more interested in the group or private option?")
- Payment preference ("Would you rather do monthly or save with annual?")
- Flavors / variations
- Time slots
- Media format ("Want the video version or the workbook?")
- Delivery speed
- Sizes / colors / materials
- Personnel ("Would you prefer to work with Sarah or Mike?")
- Communication method ("Do you prefer Zoom or phone?")

**Key insight:** The A/B choice bypasses the yes/no decision entirely. Both options assume they're moving forward. The only question is which flavor.

## 7-Day Contact Cadence

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 28-29

A specific day-by-day outreach protocol for new leads. Front-loaded for maximum contact in the first 24 hours, tapering over 7 days.

### Day 1 (Aggressive)
1. Call within 5 minutes of opt-in
2. Double-dial (call, hang up, call again immediately -- increases pick-up rate)
3. Leave a voicemail on second call
4. Text immediately after voicemail
5. Wait a few hours
6. Repeat double-dial + text
7. Wait a few more hours
8. Repeat double-dial + text a third time

**Total Day 1:** 6 calls + 3 texts minimum

### Days 2-3 (Sustained)
- Call 2x per day (one early morning, one late afternoon)
- Text after the second call each day

**Total Days 2-3:** 4 calls + 2 texts per day

### Days 4-7 (Tapering)
- Call 1x per day
- Text 1x per day

**Total Days 4-7:** 1 call + 1 text per day

### After Day 7 (Long-Term Nurture)
- Transition to value-based nurture
- Free content, soft CTAs, re-engagement offers
- When they re-engage, restart the cadence from the top

**Why this works:** Most competitors quit after one or two attempts. By Day 4, you're the only one still reaching out. The lead doesn't think "this person is annoying." They think "this person actually gives a damn."

## Automated + Manual Reminder Layering

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 29-31

A two-layer reminder system that combines automated sequences with personal human touches. Neither alone is sufficient. Together they dramatically increase show rates.

### Layer 1: Automated Reminders

Sent from your system/CRM. People know these are automated, and that's fine. They serve as informational checkpoints.

1. **Immediately after booking:** Confirmation with date, time, calling number, and name of person they'll meet with
2. **24 hours before:** Reminder with same details
3. **12 hours before:** Reminder
4. **3 hours before:** Final automated reminder

### Layer 2: Manual Reminders (From a Real Cell Phone)

Sent from an actual phone number. These feel personal. They cut through the noise of automated messages.

1. **Night before:** "Hey [Name], looking forward to chatting tomorrow at [time]. Anything specific you want to make sure we cover?"
2. **Morning of:** "Good morning! Just confirming our call at [time] today. Talk soon."
3. **60 minutes before:** "Hey, just a heads up -- we're about an hour out from our call. Talk to you at [time]!"

### Tips for Maximum Effect
- **Include the area code you'll call from** in the confirmation. Leads pick up calls from known numbers at 2x the rate of unknown numbers.
- **Use the lead's local time zone** in all messages. "3pm your time" not "3pm EST."
- **Make automated messages clearly automated.** Don't pretend a CRM email is personal. Let the manual messages carry the personal weight.
- **Use the Acknowledge-Compliment-Ask pattern** in manual messages to keep the conversation going and build rapport before the call.

## BAMFAM (Book A Meeting From A Meeting)

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, p. 31
**Origin:** Sharran Srivatsaa

**The rule:** Never end a call without scheduling the next one. Period.

**Why it works:**
- If you can't schedule the next appointment while you're both on the phone right now, you certainly won't after they've put you in their rearview mirror
- The lead is warmest during the current conversation. Momentum dies the second the call ends.
- Internally called "BAMFAM as a way of life" -- it applies to everything, not just sales

**How to execute:**
1. Near the end of the current call, pull up your calendar
2. Propose 2-3 specific times for the next meeting
3. Pick the time together in real-time while you're both looking at calendars
4. Confirm it before hanging up

**Handling resistance:**
- If they dodge scheduling ("let me check and get back to you"), treat it as an objection
- Being dodgy about the next call = they have a concern they haven't voiced
- Address the concern, then ask again
- "I totally understand. Usually when someone hesitates on the next step, there's something on their mind. What's your main concern?"

**Applies to:** Sales calls, support calls, coaching calls, onboarding calls, any meeting with a next step.

## Appointment Pull-Forward Script

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 16-17

A specific script for calling self-scheduled leads to qualify them AND pull their appointment forward to an earlier time (ideally same-day). The sooner they meet, the higher the show rate and close rate.

### The Script Flow

**Open with confirmation framing:**
> "I saw you booked a time for [topic] on [day]. Just calling to confirm some details."

**Qualify with three questions:**
1. WHO are they? (Name, business, role)
2. WHAT do they want? (Desired outcome)
3. WHAT are they struggling with? (Current pain)

**Offer the earlier time:**
> "I actually have an opening that canceled later today at 4pm... unless now is a good time?"

### Five Possible Outcomes

1. **Doesn't respond:** Remove from calendar or double-book the slot (don't hold it hostage)
2. **Unqualified:** Cancel the appointment, give them something free and valuable as a parting gift ("Here's a resource that'll help with what you described. Not the right fit for our program but I didn't want to leave you empty-handed.")
3. **Qualified + free right now:** Close them on the spot or do a hot handoff to a closer
4. **Qualified + agrees to pull forward:** Move to the earlier time
5. **Qualified + keeps original time:** Confirm and move on

### Hot Handoff Edification Script

When transferring a qualified lead to a closer in real-time:

> "You're in luck. You're going to speak with one of our top experts on [their problem]. He's helped [number] businesses achieve [the thing they said they wanted]. Let me connect you right now."

**Key:** Edify the closer before the transfer. Build them up. The lead should feel like they're getting upgraded, not passed off.

## Push-to-Consult Sequence (7-Day)

**Source:** $100M Fast Cash Playbook, pp. 12-13

A 7-day email + text promotion sequence for selling high-ticket offers through consultations. Opens access immediately but spreads consults over the week. Each message adds a new bonus to build momentum.

### Day-by-Day Breakdown

**Day 7 Out (Text Only)**
- Teaser text: "Something big coming tomorrow. Keep an eye on your inbox."
- No details. Pure curiosity.

**Day 6 Out (Announcement)**
- Email: Full announcement of the offer (1-3 core components explained)
- Text: Short version + link to full email
- CTA: Schedule a consult

**Day 5 Out (Recap + First Bonus)**
- Email: Recap the core offer for anyone who missed Day 6
- Reveal the first bonus
- Text: "Did you see the bonus we just added?"

**Day 3 Out (Sales Update)**
- Email: Social proof -- how many sold, how many spots left
- Text: Reminder with urgency

**Day 2 Out (Second Bonus + Scarcity)**
- Email: "X spots left" + reveal the second bonus
- Text: Updated availability

**Day 1 AM (Warning + Best Bonus)**
- Email: "Few hours/spots left" + reveal the BEST bonus (worth more than the entire price tag)
- This is the most important bonus. Save your strongest card for last.
- Text: Urgency push

**Day 1 PM (Sold Out)**
- Email: Sold out notification
- Text: Confirmation it's closed

**Optional: Back-Out Spot**
- If someone cancels or doesn't pay, email the waitlist
- "A spot just opened up" -- this email converts at an extremely high rate

### Implementation Notes
- Each bonus should be revealed one at a time, never all at once
- The bonuses create a "fear of missing the next reveal" that keeps people opening emails
- The sold-out notification creates urgency for the next promotion
- Waitlist the people who tried to buy but couldn't. Charge them immediately if a spot opens.

## Push-to-Automated-Checkout Sequence (5-Day)

**Source:** $100M Fast Cash Playbook, pp. 13-14

A 5-day stampede-style promotion sequence for automated checkout. No consult needed. Builds tension before cart opens, then creates first-come-first-serve urgency. Designed for hot lists and lower-ticket premium offers.

### Day-by-Day Breakdown

**Day 5 Out (Text Only)**
- Teaser text: "Something's coming. You'll want to be ready."

**Day 4 Out (Announcement)**
- Email: Full announcement of the offer
- Text: Short version + "watch your inbox"

**Day 2 Out (Countdown)**
- Email: "Cart opens in 48 hours"
- Text: Build anticipation

**Day 1 Out (First Bonus + Set Alarm)**
- Email: Reveal the first bonus + tell them to set an alarm for cart open time
- Text: "Set your alarm for [time] tomorrow. Trust me."

**60 Minutes Out (Text Only)**
- Text: "60 minutes. Set alarm. Have payment ready."
- No email. Text only. This is tactical urgency.

**Cart Open (Best Bonus)**
- Email: Cart is open + reveal the BEST bonus (available only at cart open)
- Text: "It's live. Link: [link]"

**60 Minutes Post-Open (Sales Update)**
- Email: "X sold in the first hour. Y spots left."
- Text: Social proof update

**6 Hours Post-Open (Extra Bonus + Scarcity)**
- Email: Add an extra bonus for anyone still on the fence + "X spots left"
- Text: "Added one more thing. Check your email."

**12 Hours Post-Open (Sold Out)**
- Email: Sold out notification
- Text: "That's it. Sold out."

**Next Day (Back-Out Spot)**
- If someone's payment fails or they cancel, email the waitlist
- "One spot just opened" -- high-conversion email

### Implementation Notes
- The "set your alarm" tactic is critical. It turns passive interest into active commitment before cart opens.
- Payment must be instant (Stripe, checkout page). No friction. No "request access."
- The 60-minute pre-open text is the highest-leverage single message in the sequence.
- If you haven't sold out by 6 hours post-open, the extra bonus email is your save.

## Pull-Forward Script

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 16-17

Same-day pull-forward technique used during confirmation calls. The goal: collapse the gap between "interested" and "meeting."

**The move:**
1. Call to "confirm details" for their scheduled appointment
2. Qualify them (who, what they want, what they're struggling with)
3. Mention a cancellation: "I actually had someone cancel their [earlier time] slot today"
4. Offer the opening: "Unless right now works?"
5. If they accept, either close them yourself or hot-handoff to a closer

**Why same-day matters:**
- Show rates for same-day appointments are dramatically higher than next-week appointments
- The lead hasn't had time to talk themselves out of it, get busy, or forget
- "Strike while the iron is hot" is not a cliche. It's the operating principle of lead nurture.

**If they can't pull forward:**
- Confirm the original time
- Set a manual reminder (Section 5)
- Send a push incentive (Section 3)

## Execution Culture

**Source:** $100M Lead Nurture Playbook, pp. 27-37

The system only works if the culture enforces it. Frameworks without accountability are just documents.

### Track Everything, By Rep
- **Show rate** by rep (not just team average)
- **Close rate** by rep
- **Lead-to-close ratio** by rep
- Individual tracking exposes who's following the cadence and who's cutting corners

### "Yellows Are the New Gold"
- Don't judge less-qualified leads as worthless
- "Yellow" leads (medium quality) are practice reps that sharpen your closers
- The rep who takes all the "bad" leads and converts even 10% of them is printing money everyone else left on the table

### Volume Negates Luck
- If you rely on a few leads to close, one bad day tanks your month
- If you work massive volume, the math smooths out
- You stop needing "good days" and start having predictable months

### "Be a Garbage Man"
- Take all the leads other reps reject
- Every lead is free practice
- The best closers in any organization started by working the leads nobody else wanted

### Best Closers = Best Setters
- The skills that make someone great at setting appointments (rapport, qualifying, speed) are the same skills that make them great at closing
- If your setter is underperforming, they probably need closing training, not setting training
