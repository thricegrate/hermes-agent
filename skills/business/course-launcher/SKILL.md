# Course Launcher

End-to-end guide for launching and scaling online courses, cohorts, and digital education products. This skill covers the operational lifecycle that `launch-strategy` (general launches) and `webinar-funnel` (Brunson script) don't: the specific tactics for course businesses from pre-sell through 6-figure scaling.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If private product-marketing context notes exists, read it before asking questions.

Gather this context (ask if not provided):

### 1. Course Details
- What's the course topic and transformation promise?
- Who is the ideal student? (Role, experience level, goal)
- What format? (Self-paced, live cohort, hybrid, membership)
- What's the curriculum structure? (Modules, lessons, duration)

### 2. Launch Stage
- Pre-launch (idea stage, no course yet)?
- First launch (course exists, no sales yet)?
- Re-launch (improving on previous launch)?
- Scaling (optimizing existing successful launch)?

### 3. Assets
- Existing email list? Size?
- Existing audience (social, YouTube, podcast)?
- Testimonials or case studies available?
- Landing page / sales page ready?

### 4. Goals
- Revenue target for this launch?
- Student enrollment target?
- Price point (or range being considered)?

## The 4-Stage Launch Lifecycle

### Stage 1: Pre-Launch Setup

**The Pre-Sell Model: Sell Before You Create**

Why: Pre-selling validates demand, funds production, and creates accountability.

1. **Share the idea from day one** -- tell your audience what you're building and why
2. **Set a deadline** to create urgency (enrollment opens [date])
3. **Collect beta testers** for feedback and first testimonials
4. **Use pre-sale money** to improve course quality
5. **Early bird pricing** -- reward early believers with a discount

**Pre-launch checklist:**
- [ ] Set up platform + upload any existing content
- [ ] Create customer avatar (ICP)
- [ ] Set up Facebook Pixel and Google remarketing tags
- [ ] Set up affiliate program (see `growth-referral`)
- [ ] Find 5-10 beta testers to collect reviews before launch
- [ ] Keyword research + SEO page optimization
- [ ] Create pre-launch email sequence (see `newsletter-automator/references/course-launch-sequences.md`)
- [ ] Create or select lead magnet

**Lead Magnet Types (8):**
1. Guide / ebook
2. Training / free video
3. Discount / coupon
4. Survey / quiz
5. Free trial / mini-course
6. Cheat sheet
7. Toolkit
8. Assessment / test

**Lead Magnet Creation (5 steps):**
1. Choose target audience
2. Set value proposition (something they already need)
3. Give it a name (headline = value proposition)
4. Choose type based on your strength
5. Create it

**Free Course Hack:** Copy your premium course, delete most content, leave only intro or first chapter of each module. Instant lead magnet without creating new content.

### Stage 2: Launch Execution

**Launch Email Sequence (7 emails):**
1. Pre-launch warm-up (1+ month out): share preview, blog post, expert interview
2. Early bird offer email
3. Official launch registration email
4. Welcome email with upsell opportunity
5. First reviews request email
6. Closing registrations thank-you email
7. Last reminder (24 hours + 2-3 hours before close)

For detailed day-by-day blueprints (7/15/30-day), see `newsletter-automator/references/course-launch-sequences.md`.
For launch email templates, see `email-sequence/references/course-email-swipe-file.md`.

**Webinar Launch Funnel (9 Steps):**
1. Define subject (narrow, specific outcome)
2. Create content (outline, script, slides, offer)
3. Set date (2-3 weeks out for promotion)
4. Build landing page (registration page)
5. Create emails (registration confirmation, reminder, follow-up, replay)
6. Drive traffic (Facebook ads + email + social)
7. Deliver webinar (stand up, use chat assistant, Q&A, exercises)
8. Export and offer replay (with countdown timer before takedown)
9. Review analytics (drop-off at 15 or 30 min = red flag)

**Replay with Timer:** Post webinar replay with countdown before it comes down. Preserves urgency of live event. Disable fast-forward to simulate live experience.

### Stage 3: Post-Launch Optimization

**Sell More After Launch:**
- [ ] Add regular community content
- [ ] Add engagement activities: quizzes, comment competitions, assignments
- [ ] Add completion certificates
- [ ] Create coupons, bundles, limited-time offers
- [ ] Run retargeting campaigns (see `ads-strategy/references/retargeting-lookalike-playbook.md`)
- [ ] Run competition giving free access to non-buyers (re-engagement hook)
- [ ] Create email sequence for non-buyers (segmented win-back)
- [ ] Send special discount coupon to non-buyers (limited time, 7-10 days max)
- [ ] Interview students, publish success stories (video testimonials > written)
- [ ] Add Thank You page upsells

**Non-Buyer Re-engagement Tactics:**
1. Competition for free access (not just discounts -- give them a chance to win)
2. Time-limited discount to non-buyers as separate sequence
3. Retarget with Facebook ads acknowledging they visited but didn't buy
4. Export leads who registered but didn't purchase -- treat as separate warm segment

## Pricing Psychology

### The $100 Floor Rule

Never price a course below $100. Six arguments:

1. **Value perception:** Low price signals low quality. Buyers assume expensive = better.
2. **Customer quality:** Low prices attract low-commitment students who don't complete the course.
3. **Ad economics:** You can't profitably advertise a $20 course. Higher prices fund acquisition.
4. **Engagement:** Higher-paying students are more engaged and complete at higher rates.
5. **JV/Affiliate attraction:** Partners want meaningful commissions. 50% of $20 doesn't motivate anyone.
6. **Margin for improvement:** Higher revenue per student lets you invest in better content and support.

**When low pricing IS appropriate:**
- Pre-launch testing (validate concept before full pricing)
- Lead generation (free or very low to build list)
- Last-resort discount (time-limited, not permanent)

### Tripwire Psychology

Get prospects into the psychological state of spending their first dollar. A small purchase (even $7-27) breaks the mental barrier to buying bigger.

**Tripwire model:**
- Break large courses into smaller ones
- Offer a mini-course or module at low price
- Bundle: 5 courses at $50 each, offer bundle for $200 (sells 5 at price of 4)
- Free course as lead magnet, then upsell to premium

### Difficulty as Value Signal

Making enrollment harder increases perceived value:
- Application required
- Approval-gated access
- Test or quiz to enter
- Gamification, sharing contests, or competitive elements

### Price Testing

Start at a reasonable price point (not bottom). Increase until you see sales resistance. Payment plans as separate product options increase accessibility without lowering price.

## Sales Page Formula

### Landing Page Copy Structure
1. Brief but benefit-focused headline (not just course title)
2. Important content above the fold (users skim then leave)
3. Short and simple description
4. Action words: "discover," "emerge," "explore"
5. Social proof (emails, tweets, screenshots, testimonials)
6. Video on sales page (increases quality perception and sales)

### Video Types for Sales Pages (ranked by effectiveness)
1. Emotional appeal video (story + background + benefits)
2. How-to video (show skill, end with CTA to course)
3. Testimonial video (handpicked students)
4. News/commentary video (current events + credibility)
5. Behind-the-scenes video (personal connection)

### CTA Words
JOIN, GET, BUY NOW, TRY. Follow with course title or proposed solution. Include time/deadline ("be fit in 6 weeks").

## 6 Urgency/Scarcity Types

1. **Limited spots/access** -- "Only 20 seats available"
2. **Countdown timers** with urgency language (now, fast, quick, hurry, approaching, instant)
3. **Increased difficulty** -- application required, test to enter (raises perceived value)
4. **Loss aversion framing** -- "Missing this class might cost you the next promotion"
5. **Unpleasant situation framing** -- paint the pain of inaction, offer course as escape
6. **Pulling the offer** -- last-minute, one-off, time-limited, "this won't be available again"

---

## The 9-to-1 Rule

Give 9 parts value/education for every 1 part promotion. Newsletters, social posts, and content must earn trust before selling. If you're promoting more than 10% of the time, you're eroding trust faster than you're building it.

## Content Upgrade as Value Boost

Create a downloadable worksheet matching your course curriculum. Give on purchase. Increases perceived value at near-zero cost.

## Amazon Kindle as Authority Play

A Kindle book appears in Amazon search (critical for research topics). The 10% sample is a discovery hook. Author bio links back to your school/course. Low effort, high authority signal.
