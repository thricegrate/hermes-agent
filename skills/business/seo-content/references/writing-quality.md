## Engagement Hook Types

Four proven hook types for opening sections and articles. Vary them across sections -- never use the same type twice in a row.

### Curiosity Gap

Present a knowledge gap the reader needs to close. State a surprising outcome, then withhold the "how" until after the hook.

**Format:** [Counterintuitive result or claim]. [Brief context]. [Implication that pulls reader forward].

**Example:**
> Pages ranking #7 in Google get cited by AI Overviews more than pages ranking #1. The difference isn't backlinks. It's how the content is structured.

### Pain Point

Name a specific frustration the reader is experiencing right now. Describe the problem in concrete, felt terms. No abstract language.

**Format:** [Specific situation the reader recognizes]. [The frustrating outcome]. [Pivot toward solution].

**Example:**
> You published 50 blog posts this year. Organic traffic went down. The content wasn't bad. It was invisible to the systems that now decide what gets seen.

### Surprising Fact

Lead with a statistic or claim that challenges assumptions.

**Format:** [Counterintuitive claim]. [Source]. [Why it matters].

**Example:**
> Keyword stuffing reduces AI visibility by 10%, according to the Princeton GEO study (KDD 2024). The opposite of what most SEO guides still teach.

### Quick Win

Promise an immediate, achievable result with a specific timeframe.

**Format:** [Specific action] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe].

**Example:**
> Add FAQ schema to your top 5 pages this week. Perplexity citations typically appear within 14 days.

## Information Density Rules

Every sentence in SEO content must earn its place. Apply these four tests:

1. **Adds new information** -- Does this sentence tell the reader something they didn't know from the previous sentence? If not, cut it.
2. **Is specific** -- Does it contain a concrete detail (number, name, example, step)? Vague sentences ("This is very important") fail this test.
3. **Is actionable** -- Can the reader do something with this information? If it's pure commentary ("This trend is fascinating"), cut or rewrite as an action.
4. **Advances understanding** -- Does it move the reader closer to the page's goal? Tangents and background that don't serve the search query get cut.

**The editing test:** Read each paragraph after writing. If you can remove a sentence and the paragraph loses nothing, remove it.

### Sentence and Paragraph Rules

- Short sentences: 8-15 words average
- Alternate short and long sentences for rhythm
- Short sentence after a complex idea = emphasis
- Never start 3+ consecutive sentences the same way
- Maximum 4 sentences per paragraph
- Mix short (1-2 sentence) paragraphs with longer ones
- Active voice. Subject-verb-object.

### Lists and Tables

- Use bullet lists for 3+ inline items (don't bury lists in prose)
- Use tables for comparisons (features, pricing, tools)
- Don't overdo it. Articles shouldn't look like documentation.

## Quotability and Snippet Optimization

Write key claims so AI systems and Google can extract them as featured snippets.

### Rules for Extractable Content

- State facts as complete, self-contained sentences (no pronouns referencing prior paragraphs)
- Lead sections with the answer, not the question buildup
- Keep extractable answer passages to **40-60 words** (optimal for featured snippets and AI citation)
- Use precise language: "increases by 37%" not "significantly improves"
- Format definitions as "[Term] is [definition]" -- AI systems parse this pattern reliably
- Tables beat prose for comparison data
- Numbered lists beat paragraphs for sequential processes

### NLP-Friendly Formatting

**Bad:** "There are various benefits to using this approach."

**Good:** "Using [approach] reduces [metric] by [X]%, according to [source]."

**Bad:** "It's really important to consider this carefully."

**Good:** "Schema markup increases click-through rates by 30% for pages with FAQ rich results (Google Search Central, 2024)."

## Word Count Guidance

### Defaults by Content Type

| Content Type | Word Count | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Competitive informational queries | 1,500-2,500 | Enough depth to outrank, not so long readers bounce |
| Long-form guides | 3,000-4,000 | Only when genuine depth justifies it |
| Commercial/transactional pages | 800-1,500 | Intent is to act, not to read |
| FAQ pages | 1,000-2,000 | Depends on question count and answer depth |
| Landing pages | 500-1,000 | Focused on conversion, not education |

### Section Proportionality

- No section should exceed 40% of total word count. If one section dominates, it probably needs its own page.
- Introduction should be brief (1-3 sentences, under 100 words)
- Conclusion should be shorter than body sections (50-150 words)

### Cutting Rules

- After drafting, cut 10-20% on the editing pass
- Remove every sentence that fails the information density test
- Never pad with filler to hit a word count target
- HubSpot data: long-form content (2,000+ words) earns 77% more backlinks than short articles, but only when the length is justified by depth

## E-E-A-T Content Signals

How to demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness through writing itself, not just author bios and schema markup.

### First-Hand Experience (the first E)

This is the most important signal for AI citation. AI systems prefer content from people who actually did the thing.

- Use first-person when describing processes: "When I tested this on 50 pages..." not "When one tests this..."
- Include specific details only someone who did the work would know (error messages, unexpected results, time it took, tools that didn't work)
- Show process, not just outcomes: before/after, what didn't work, what you'd do differently
- Screenshots, original data, and real examples over stock photos and hypotheticals
- "I tested this for two weeks" beats "many experts recommend" every time

### Expertise Signals in Content

- Use correct technical terminology (then explain it for broader audiences)
- Cite primary sources, not secondary summaries
- Acknowledge nuance and exceptions: "This works for X, but not Y because..."
- Provide context that only an expert would think to mention
- Include specific numbers with original sources and dates

### Authoritativeness Signals

- Named authors with visible credentials
- Expert quotes with titles and organizations
- "Last updated: [date]" prominently displayed
- Regular content refreshes (quarterly minimum for competitive topics)
- Reference your own published content where relevant

### Trustworthiness in Writing

- State limitations: "This data is from 2024 and may not reflect current..."
- Disclose conflicts of interest and affiliate relationships
- Link to sources for every statistical claim
- Correct and update content when information changes
- Present honest pros AND cons, not just the positive angle

## Quality Scoring Checklist

Pre-publish checklist for SEO content. Score each item pass/fail. Target: 0 failures.

### Structure

- [ ] Exactly one H1 with primary keyword
- [ ] H2/H3 hierarchy with no skipped levels
- [ ] First H2 contains or closely relates to target keyword
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words (bolded once)
- [ ] Primary keyword appears 1-2 times per H2 section (natural placement)
- [ ] 3-5 secondary/LSI keywords distributed naturally throughout
- [ ] FAQ section with 3-5 questions phrased as users search (if applicable)
- [ ] SEO title under 65 characters with keyword near beginning
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters with keyword included

### Content Quality

- [ ] Every sentence passes at least one information density test
- [ ] Opening uses one of the four engagement hook types
- [ ] Key claims written as self-contained, extractable passages (40-60 words)
- [ ] Statistics include sources and dates
- [ ] No filler paragraphs or padding sections
- [ ] E-E-A-T signals present (first-hand experience, expert context, honest assessment)
- [ ] Honest pros AND cons where relevant (not one-sided)

### Links and Media

- [ ] 3-5 internal links with descriptive anchor text
- [ ] 3-5 external links to high-authority sources (official docs, research, reputable publications)
- [ ] External links open in new tab (target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer")
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text (10-15 words with relevant keyword)
- [ ] Images in WebP format with keyword-rich kebab-case filenames
- [ ] No broken links (verify before publishing)

### AI Writing Check

- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] No banned words or phrases (see seo/references/ai-writing-detection.md)
- [ ] No structural anti-patterns (binary contrast addiction, triple threat, infomercial transitions)
- [ ] Varied sentence lengths and structures
- [ ] No three-word phrase repeated more than once in the article
- [ ] No sentence starts with the same two words as the previous sentence
- [ ] Reads naturally when spoken aloud

### Scoring

- **0 failures:** Ship it.
- **1-2 failures:** Fix them. Re-check.
- **3+ failures:** Rewrite affected sections. Run full checklist again.
