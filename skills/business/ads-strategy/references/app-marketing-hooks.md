<a name="how-to"></a>
## How to use this library

**Workflow:**
1. Pick a category that fits the product + audience emotion you want to trigger.
2. Swap every `[BRACKETED VARIABLE]` with a concrete, visual detail — never generic.
3. Test 3–5 variants per week. Double down on what gets traction.
4. Run every hook through the 5 Insights checklist before posting.

**Coffee-shop rule:** Every hook should sound like something a real person would say to a friend at a cafe. If it sounds like a stage or LinkedIn guru, rewrite it. Kill em-dashes per project rules.

**When to use which category:**
- Tool demo video (Artifact, bot, skill) → Curious Creator, Impossible Claim, Unintentional Find
- Launch campaign (new app, new newsletter) → Gatekeeper, Comparison, Authority Endorsement
- Newsletter subject line / email opener → Regret Reveal, Impossible Claim, Gatekeeper
- Organic Reel or short → any category; pair with Trend Hijack for algorithm lift
- Paid ad creative → Impossible Claim, Comparison, Regret Reveal (highest CTR performers)

<a name="section-3-insights"></a>
## Section 3: 5 High-Performance Insights

Data-backed lessons that separate hooks with a few hundred views from hooks that go viral.

### 1. Story Opener Hook — Use specific details, not vague claims

Specific visual details ("a ring behind my plant", "red hairs under my pillow") outperform vague claims 2–3x. Specificity creates a mental image the viewer can't ignore.

Replace every vague word with a concrete, visual detail. Not "something suspicious" — but "a receipt from a restaurant I've never heard of." For app marketing: not "this tool is amazing" — but "this tool just wrote 47 tweets in the time it took me to finish my coffee."

### 2. Relatable Struggle Hook — Emotional delivery matters enormously

7.49% engagement rate reported for emotionally charged delivery, well above platform averages. Viewers mirror the creator's emotion. Calm and relatable is forgettable. Exaggerated and relatable is shareable.

For app marketing: if the video is about frustration ("I was stuck in this spreadsheet for 3 hours"), actually show frustration. Don't narrate it flat. Performance IS the hook.

### 3. Bold Claim Hook — Contradict common advice

Common advice slides past the brain. Counter-intuitive claims force the viewer to resolve the contradiction before they can scroll past. "Never post this type of photo" works because everyone posts that type of photo.

For the product content: what do most AI creators tell you that's wrong? Lead with that contradiction. "Everyone says AI replaces you. Actually, AI amplifies your weirdest edges. Here's proof."

### 4. Curiosity Gap Hook — Add interactive elements

Passive curiosity ("I wonder what happens") loses to active curiosity ("let me guess / rate / decide"). Rating hooks like "what would you rate him?" invite mental participation.

For tool demos: "Guess what this bot did in 10 seconds." "Rate this the product output 1–10." "First person to spot the trick in the comments wins." Make the viewer DO something mentally.

### 5. Transformation Tease Hook — Make it feel achievable

Celebrity transformations = higher views, lower engagement. Relatable everyday transformations = lower views but higher engagement, shares, saves. Viewers project themselves into achievable transformations.

For app marketing: show the transformation of someone who looks like the target user — a regular 35–50 year old running a small business, not a 22-year-old millionaire. Relatability beats spectacle every time for driving action.

<a name="section-4-dialogue"></a>
## Section 4: Dialogue & Interview Formats

These hooks use two or more voices (real or implied) instead of a single monologue. They stop the scroll differently: the viewer is eavesdropping, not being pitched. Works especially well for:
- AI voiceover / UGC skits with 2 AI characters
- Real two-person founder content (YAR + Julia, YAR + Oleg, YAR + Max)
- Man-on-street / interviewer sketches for app demos
- POV self-talk scripts where the creator plays both sides

**Production note:** Replace `[X]`, `[problem]`, `[desired outcome]`, `[common mistake]` with concrete, visual details (Insight #1). The dialogue structure does the heavy lifting; specificity makes it real.

<a name="dialogue-ab"></a>
### 4.1 A/B Two-Person Dialogue

Two people talking. One knows, one doesn't. The ignorance is the hook — viewer wants to know what A knows.

### 4.4 Skeptic-to-Believer

Objection-style. One voice pushes back. The pushback IS the hook — viewer stays to see if the answer holds up.

1. A: "I don't believe you. If [X] was that simple, everyone would be doing it."
   B: "That's what I thought too. Until I tried it."
   A: "And?"
   B: "And now I actually get [desired outcome] without the headache."
2. A: "Why does no one talk about how easy [desired outcome] can be?"
   B: "Because if they did, nobody would be wasting time on [common mistake]."
   A: "Wait... so I've been struggling for nothing?"
3. A: "You're lying. There's no way [X] actually fixes [problem]."
   B: "Try it once and tell me I'm wrong."
   A: "...Fine, but if this doesn't work, I'm coming back."
4. A: "I've literally tried everything for [problem] and nothing works."
   B: "You sure? Because I did one thing differently and it changed everything."
   A: "Ok, what is it??"
5. A: "I just assumed [problem] was something you have to live with."
   B: "Nope. That's just what they want you to think."
   A: "Who's they??"
6. A: "Wait, you're telling me you got [desired result] without [common struggle]?"
   B: "Yeah, and it was way easier than I expected."
   A: "There's no way. How?"

<a name="checklist"></a>
## Usage Checklist

Before posting any hook, run it through this:

- [ ] **Specificity:** Did I swap every vague word for a concrete visual detail?
- [ ] **Emotion:** Is the delivery actually emotional or am I reciting it flat?
- [ ] **Contradiction:** Does this challenge what the audience already believes, or does it just confirm it?
- [ ] **Interactivity:** Am I making the viewer DO something mentally (rate, guess, decide)?
- [ ] **Achievability:** Does the transformation feel within reach for the target audience?

If any box is empty, rewrite before posting.
