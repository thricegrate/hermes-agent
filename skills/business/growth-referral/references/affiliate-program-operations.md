# Affiliate Program Operations Guide

Source: LearnWorlds "Thrive in Affiliate Marketing"

## Core Thesis: The "Involved" Affiliate

Three affiliate relationship types:
1. **Unattached** — Pure PPC affiliate, no niche connection. Lowest quality promotions.
2. **Related** — Niche-adjacent but not a product user. Medium quality.
3. **Involved** — Affiliate is also a customer/fan. Highest quality promotions because they speak from experience.

**Always pursue Involved affiliates first.** They know the product, have seen results, and produce qualitatively better content.

## 1. Compensation Models

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Pay Per Sale** | Commission on each sale referred | Digital products (recommended) |
| **Pay Per Click** | Payment per click to your site | Traffic-focused campaigns |
| **Pay Per Lead** | Payment per lead generated | Lead gen (higher fraud risk) |
| **Pay Per Call** | Payment per phone call generated | High-ticket/service businesses |
| **Pay Per Impression** | Payment per 1,000 views | Brand awareness only |

**For digital products/courses:** Pay Per Sale is the default. Typical commission: 10-50%. For new programs in competitive markets, offer 50%+ to attract top partners.

## 2. Commission Structure Tactics

### Standard Structures
- **Flat rate:** Fixed percentage on all sales (simplest)
- **Tiered escalating:** More referrals = higher commission rate (incentivizes volume)
- **Performance bonuses:** Bonus payments at sales volume thresholds (milestone rewards)

### Advanced Tactics
- **Super affiliate tier:** Custom higher rates for top performers generating consistent high volume
- **Temporary launch increases:** Higher commission during new product launches or seasonal pushes
- **Cookie expiration:** Set between 15-60 days (standard)
- **Commission maturity/payout delay:** Minimum 30 days to protect against refunds and chargebacks. Recommended: pay in month+2 (e.g., pay October for August sales)

### The EPC Principle
Commission rate alone doesn't determine affiliate appeal. What matters:
> **EPC (Earnings Per Click) = Commission Rate x Your Site's Conversion Rate**

A 20% commission on a site converting at 5% is more attractive than 50% commission on a site converting at 0.5%.

## 3. Recruitment Process

### Phase 1: Start With Your Customers
Your existing customers/students are your first affiliates. They know the product, have seen results, and are easiest to approach. Treat affiliate recruitment like talent acquisition — compete for the best.

### Phase 2: Active Networking
1. **Research:** Google "[niche] + blog," explore Facebook/LinkedIn groups, use Pinterest for niche communities
2. **Outreach timing:** Best after a trial signup, opt-in, or click-but-no-sale action (warm contact), not cold
3. **Propose agreement:** Lead with what's in it for them (commission, free product access, audience growth)

### Phase 3: Affiliate Marketplaces
- JVZoo
- ShareASale
- ClickBank
- Zaxaa
- PayDotCom
- e-Junkie

### Phase 4: Events & Influencers
- Don't pitch at first contact. Exchange details, follow up via email/LinkedIn, mention program later.
- Offer free course/product access to prospective affiliates before asking them to commit
- Influencer selection: reach, engagement rate, audience demographics, brand alignment — not just follower count

### Ideal Affiliate Qualities
- Genuine belief in the product
- Relevant audience
- Steady qualified traffic
- Large email list or social following
- Willingness to post honest reviews
- Aligned work ethic
- Prior affiliate marketing experience (ideal but not required)

## 4. Promotional Material Kit

Supply affiliates with:
- [ ] Custom banners with affiliate link CTAs
- [ ] Email swipes proven to convert
- [ ] Ebooks to use as lead magnets
- [ ] Dedicated affiliate newsletter (content ideas, upcoming launches, testimonials)
- [ ] Deep links to specific product/course pages (not just homepage)
- [ ] Coupon codes embedded in affiliate links (increases conversion)

---

## 5. Affiliate Terms & Conditions Checklist

Every affiliate agreement must cover:
1. Commission rate and structure
2. When commissions are awarded (on sale, on payment, after refund window)
3. Refund period handling (commission clawback rules)
4. Cookie tracking duration
5. Data storage and privacy policy
6. Payout schedule and method (PayPal, bank transfer, wire)
7. Brand representation rules (logo usage, name, messaging guidelines)
8. Whether affiliates can use paid ads to promote
9. Brand keyword bidding rules (critical: prevent internal SEO competition)
10. FTC disclosure requirements
11. Termination conditions
12. Dispute resolution process

---

## 6. FTC Disclosure Requirements

All affiliate promotions must disclose the affiliate relationship:
- **Required:** Web pages, blog posts, social media posts, videos, webinars, podcasts, ebooks, infographics
- **Exempt:** Banner ads

Disclosure must be clear, conspicuous, and near the affiliate link/recommendation.

---

## 7. Affiliate Engagement & Retention

Keep affiliates active and motivated:
- Free course/product access
- Tool access and resources
- Bonuses and incentives for hitting targets
- Contests and quizzes among affiliates
- Regular one-on-one check-ins with top performers
- Dedicated affiliate newsletter with content ideas and upcoming launches
- Review commission structure at least annually

### One-to-One Management
Identify top performers, set specific targets (sales, leads, clicks), work more closely with them. Quality over quantity: a few highly committed affiliates outperform many passive ones.

---

## 8. SEO Coordination

**Critical rule:** If affiliates and vendor both bid on the same keywords, it causes internal competition and drives up costs. Set clear rules upfront:
- Define which brand keywords affiliates can/cannot bid on
- Specify whether affiliates can run PPC campaigns and on which platforms
- Include in Terms & Conditions before onboarding

---

## 9. Data & Leads as Assets

Affiliates generate a list of people who registered but didn't buy. This is a separate, valuable segment:
- Export as separate list
- Run targeted email campaigns to these warm leads
- Different messaging than cold leads (they already showed interest)
