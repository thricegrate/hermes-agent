# Emotional Personal Story Hook Generator

Generate 3 emotional, personal story hooks to open any newsletter email. Each hook uses a completely different approach to grab attention and pull the reader into the issue.

This reference complements the Emotional Hook Rule in SKILL.md and the storytelling techniques in `references/storytelling-for-newsletters.md`. Use it as the final step after writing the newsletter body to craft the opening.

---

## Rules

- 3 hooks per newsletter issue, each using a completely different approach
- 2 paragraphs per hook (2-3 sentences each)
- Easy-going, conversational tone
- Never use em-dashes
- Never use low-frequency words
- Humanize: must sound like a real person wrote it, not AI
- Default audience: 35+ year old American interested in personal development/productivity
- Override audience per newsletter (e.g., 50+ for Feel Better, local residents for Naples/Fort Myers)

## When to Use

After the newsletter body is written, run this generator against the finished text. Pick the hook that best matches the issue's emotional target (from the 12-emotion list in SKILL.md: WOW, WTF, OMG, LOL, UGH, WHOA, NAH, YEP, OOF, HMM, DAMN, SMH).

## Welsh Scene Hook (For Welsh Narrative Template)

When using the Welsh Narrative template, the opening IS the story -- not a hook that leads into a body. The scene hook drops the reader into a specific moment, place, and person. No emotions, no abbreviations, no questions. Just a scene.

### 6 Scene-Hook Patterns

**1. Location Drop** -- Name a specific place and what's happening there.
- "We were sitting at Bar Louie in Columbus, Ohio, after I'd driven two hours from Toledo."
- "Sunday afternoon walk into the Healdsburg Bubble Bar. 12 seats. No beer or cocktails."

**2. Other Person Opens** -- Start with someone else doing something.
- "Jennifer was scrolling Instagram, her mouth half-open in disbelief."
- "I watched a 30-year-old American woman throw herself down a mountain at 70 miles per hour."

**3. Conversation Starter** -- Drop into a specific exchange.
- "My friend Cassie bluntly asked me how much money I was making."
- "'You're smart. Figure it out,' my boss said."

**4. The Observation** -- Something you noticed that triggered the essay.
- "Everyone keeps telling me how hard it is to win right now."
- "At some point in the last few years, I got really good at my job."

**5. The Confession** -- Start with something you're admitting.
- "Maybe I'll quit all this and open a little brewery someday. I say it on podcasts. People laugh. But it's not really a joke."

**6. The Discovery** -- Something you encountered that sparked a realization.
- "A hidden sushi restaurant in Sonoma County. Accessible only through a concealed entrance. Eight seats."
- "Eleven years ago, I was sitting in the Financial District WeWork, watching one of my sales guys tape together a paper pyramid."

### Rules
- First sentence = scene or moment. Never a generality, tip, or emotion label.
- Include at least one sensory/spatial detail.
- 1-2 paragraphs max.
- The hook creates curiosity about where this is going, not what you'll teach.
