# Newsletter Template: Roundup (Morning Brew / The Rundown Style)

Use this template for daily or 3-5x/week news-driven issues covering multiple stories per issue. Best for AI/tech newsletters where speed and editorial voice matter. This is the #1 newsletter format in the AI/tech/business space.

**Distinct from Curation:** Roundup uses a repeatable story micro-template with visuals and editor's takes. Curation is commentary-heavy on fewer links with longer analysis per item.

## Writing Notes

**Target length:** 600-1,000 words total (across all sections)

**Tone:** Informed curator with personality. Think newsroom meets group chat. Not formal reporting, not try-hard casual. You're the smart friend who reads everything and tells people what matters.

**Story count:** 3 headline stories + 5 quick hits is the default. Scale down to 2 headline stories + 3 quick hits for lighter days. Never fewer than 2 stories.

**Images:** Every headline story should have a visual. Screenshots, product images, diagrams, charts. Visuals break up the text and increase scan-ability.

**The "Why it matters" section is the differentiator.** Without your take, this is an RSS feed. With it, it's a newsletter worth subscribing to. Don't be neutral. Have an opinion. Say what you actually think.

**Quick hits CTR:** These short links often get MORE clicks than main stories because they're scannable and low-commitment. Don't treat them as filler.

**Sponsor placement:** ALWAYS immediately after Story #1. This is peak attention. Moving it lower kills CPMs.

**Frequency:** Best at 3-5x/week. Works daily but requires a reliable pipeline of stories. If you can't sustain daily quality, drop to 3x/week.

**When to use Roundup vs other templates:**
- Roundup when you have 3+ stories and want to cover breadth
- Curation when you want deeper commentary on fewer items
- Trends Report when focusing on shifts/patterns and why they matter
- Deep Dive when going deep on a single topic
