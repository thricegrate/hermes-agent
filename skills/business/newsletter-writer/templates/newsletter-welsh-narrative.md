# Welsh Narrative Newsletter Template

A story-driven newsletter template based on Justin Welsh's 8-beat narrative arc. One story, one lesson, one question. No listicles. No curated links. No frameworks-first.

Full framework reference: `references/welsh-narrative-framework.md`

---

## Voice Toggle (Pick One Per Issue)

**[ ] Captain YAR Voice** -- Welsh's 8-beat structure + short sentences, blunt humor, self-deprecating, simple words. Read private voice-reference notes before writing. Sounds like: a buddy telling you a story at a bar. No fancy words. No long sentences. Hits hard, moves fast.

**[ ] Welsh Tone** -- Welsh's 8-beat structure + polished, reflective, measured. Sounds like: a thoughtful essay from someone who's been thinking about this all week. Longer sentences, introspective, warm but not casual.

---

## Pre-Writing Checklist

Before writing, answer these:

1. **What's the personal story?** (Specific moment, place, person. What happened? What went wrong or what surprised you?)
2. **What's the one lesson?** (One sentence. If you can't say it in one sentence, you have two issues, not one.)
3. **What's the parallel?** (A second story from a different context that illustrates the same lesson. Friend, colleague, public figure, or your own past.)
4. **What's the reply question?** (What specific question invites the reader to share their version?)

## The 8-Beat Template

### Beat 1: SCENE HOOK
<!-- 1-2 paragraphs. Drop reader into a specific moment. -->
<!-- Pick one scene-hook pattern: Location Drop, Other Person Opens, Conversation Starter, Observation, Confession, or Discovery -->
<!-- First sentence = scene or specific moment. Never a generality. -->
<!-- Include at least one: place name, person name, sensory detail, or specific action -->

[SCENE: Where are you? Who is there? What are you seeing/doing/hearing?]

[TENSION: What detail makes the reader want to know where this is going?]


### Beat 2: THE STORY
<!-- 3-6 paragraphs. One primary personal story. -->
<!-- Include: named characters, specific numbers, exact locations, embarrassing details -->
<!-- Use "but/therefore" transitions, never "and then" -->

[SETUP: What was the situation? Who was involved? What were the stakes?]

[DEVELOPMENT: What happened? What did you try? What went wrong?]

[THE MOMENT: What was the specific moment of realization, failure, or surprise? Use sensory detail.]

[CONSEQUENCE: What did you do? What changed? Or what didn't change -- and why?]


### Beat 3: THE TURN
<!-- 1-2 paragraphs. Pivot from story to meaning. -->
<!-- Use a turn phrase: "But here's what I kept thinking about..." / "What this actually means is..." / "Looking back..." -->

[BRIDGE: Connect the specific story to the universal pattern. What does this mean beyond your situation?]


### Beat 4: THE PARALLEL
<!-- 2-4 paragraphs. A second story that reinforces the theme from a different angle. -->
<!-- Shorter than the primary story. Different context, same lesson. -->
<!-- Include specific details even here: names, numbers, places. -->

[PARALLEL STORY: Who else experienced a version of this? What happened to them?]

[CONNECTION: How does this second story illuminate the same truth from a different direction?]


### Beat 5: THE LESSON
<!-- 1-3 paragraphs. The distilled insight. -->
<!-- Frame as "what I noticed" not "what you should do" -->
<!-- If using a framework: 3-6 items max, embedded in prose -->
<!-- Include one "I don't know" admission to stay anti-guru -->

[INSIGHT: What did you learn? What pattern did you see? What do you believe now?]

[UNCERTAINTY (optional): Where are you still unsure? What are you still figuring out?]


### Beat 6: THE BOTTOM LINE
<!-- 1 paragraph. The quotable, shareable version of the whole essay. -->

**The bottom line:**

[ONE QUOTABLE LINE: The tweet-sized version of everything above. Must work on its own without context.]


### Beat 7: THE REPLY QUESTION
<!-- 1-2 specific questions. Never "let me know your thoughts." -->

[SPECIFIC QUESTION: Ask the reader to apply the lesson to their own life. E.g., "What's your brewery?" / "Where are you rushing because you feel behind?"]

Reply and tell me about it.


### Beat 8: SIGN-OFF + CTAs
<!-- Separated from content. Never inside the editorial. -->

That's all for this week. See you next [DAY].

## Post-Writing Quality Checklist

Run through these before publishing:

- [ ] **Scene hook**: Does it open with a specific moment, place, or person? (Not a tip, not a question, not a claim)
- [ ] **Specificity**: Does the story include at least 3 of: named character, specific dollar amount, exact location, specific date, embarrassing detail?
- [ ] **One lesson**: Can you state the lesson in one sentence? If not, cut until you can.
- [ ] **Parallel story**: Is there a second story from a different context that reinforces the same theme?
- [ ] **Anti-guru**: Does the lesson come from "what I noticed" rather than "what you should do"?
- [ ] **Bottom line**: Is there a quotable one-liner that works on its own?
- [ ] **Reply question**: Is it specific enough to answer? (Not "thoughts?")
- [ ] **CTA separation**: Is the product CTA physically separated from the editorial content?
- [ ] **Word count**: 800-1,100 words?
- [ ] **Voice consistency**: Does the writing match the selected voice toggle (YAR or Welsh tone)?
- [ ] **No listicles**: No numbered tip lists in the body?
- [ ] **No curated links**: No link roundup section?
- [ ] **Dialogue**: Any referenced conversations written as dialogue, not summary?
- [ ] **"But/therefore"**: Story transitions use conflict/consequence, not "and then"?
- [ ] **Banned words check**: Run against `references/banned-words.md`?

## Subject Line Guidance

For Welsh Narrative issues, use the Welsh Fragment Style (see `references/subject-line-swipe-file.md`, Welsh Fragment section):
- 2-5 words, provocative, with a period
- Examples: "Thief of joy." / "Rock bottom." / "Get out." / "You've changed."
- Create curiosity gap without revealing the topic
- A/B test against a standard hook-style subject line
