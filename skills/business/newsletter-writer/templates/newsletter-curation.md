## Writing Notes

**Target length:** 500-1,000 words for the full issue
**Tone:** Knowledgeable curator. You've done the reading so they don't have to. Add your perspective: don't just list links.
**The key differentiator:** Your commentary and point of view. Anyone can share links. Your value is in telling readers WHY each item matters and WHAT to do with the information.
**Formatting tips:**
- Each curated item should be scannable (readers will jump to what interests them)
- Bold the item name/headline so readers can skim
- Keep your commentary concise: 2-4 sentences per item max
- Use consistent emoji patterns for different section types
- Make sure every link actually works before publishing

### Variant B Writing Notes

**Target length:** 300-500 words (brevity is the format's strength)
**The 3 ideas must be YOURS.** Not summaries of others' work. Not repackaged quotes. Your thinking.
**Quotes should come from diverse sources:** books, speeches, articles, conversations, historical figures.
**The question should make readers pause.** Not "let me know your thoughts" but something specific like "Who do you secretly envy, and what does that reveal about what you truly value?"
**Works best as a weekly ritual:** Same day every week. Readers build a habit around it.
**Personal brand requirement:** This format requires readers to trust your ideas. If they don't know you yet, use standard curation first to build that trust.
