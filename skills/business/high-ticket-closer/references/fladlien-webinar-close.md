## 14-Step Webinar Framework

The backbone of Fladlien's system. Russell Brunson's "Perfect Webinar" is explicitly based on this.

### Opening (Steps 1-5)

**Step 1: Hook**
One compelling statement that cuts through noise.
- "In the next 60 minutes I'm going to show you how to turn a simple presentation into a 7-figure income stream."
- For high-ticket: lead with the transformation, not the price. The hook must earn them staying for the next 75 minutes.

**Step 2: Pain**
Articulate specific pain points the audience already lives with. Don't invent problems -- name the ones they've already identified.
- High-ticket adaptation: Name the pain of having tried cheaper solutions that didn't work. "You've bought the courses. You've watched the YouTube videos. You're still stuck at the same revenue."

**Step 3: Tease**
Create open loops. Hint at content without revealing. Creates micro-commitment to stay.
- "I'm going to share the exact process that took one of our clients from $3K/mo to $27K/mo in 90 days. But first, I need you to understand WHY most approaches fail."

**Step 4: Excite**
Highlight transformations and outcomes. Move from "you have pain" to "here's what's possible."
- Use real case studies. At high-ticket, proof is non-negotiable. Vague promises don't close $5K+ deals.

**Step 5: Position**
Credentials, experience, results. Not bragging -- earned trust.
- High-ticket adaptation: Position through results delivered, not personal accolades. "I've helped 47 clients add $10K+/mo to their businesses" beats "I'm a certified coach with 10 years experience."

### Content (Steps 6-8)

**Step 6: Paradigm (THE Most Important Step)**
Identify the biggest excuse/false belief stopping action. The ENTIRE content section exists to destroy this ONE belief. Create the "aha" moment.
- This is the Big Domino from Brunson's framework. If this belief falls, all other objections fall with it.
- For high-ticket: The paradigm is usually "I can do this myself for less." Destroy that belief with evidence of the cost of doing it alone (time, mistakes, opportunity cost).

**Step 7: Mechanisms**
Teach the vital inputs that lead to the outcome. 2-4 mechanisms is enough -- don't go comprehensive.
- Show HOW the new worldview translates into steps
- For high-ticket: Each mechanism should demonstrate complexity that justifies expert help. "Here are the 4 components. I'll teach you all 4 today, but implementing them alone takes most people 12-18 months. With us, it takes 90 days."

**Step 8: Commitment**
Bridge between teaching and selling. Get micro-agreements.
- "Did you find this helpful? Will you use this?"
- Sequential "yes" responses condition for purchase
- These affirmation questions are critical. Each "yes" reduces resistance to the next "yes" -- including the buying decision.

### Close (Steps 9-14)

**Step 9: Transition**
Recap value in one minute. Restate transformation. Naturally introduce offer.
- "We've covered [mechanism 1], [mechanism 2], and [mechanism 3]. You now understand the system. The question is: do you want to implement it alone, or do you want help?"

**Step 10: Offer**
Present solution clearly. Every deliverable named and explained.
- For high-ticket: Present the 3 Levels (DIY/DWY/DFY). Let them self-select into the right tier.

**Step 11: Price**
Link pricing to demonstrated value from content section.
- Use Theory of Persuasional Relativity: "The investment is $5,000. You've been spending $2,000/month on tactics that aren't working. That's $24K/year on the wrong approach. $5K for the right approach pays for itself in the first month."

**Step 12: Bonuses (Where the Real Close Happens)**
Bonuses must be more valuable than core offer.
- **The $500M Switch:** Don't say "buy X and get bonuses." Say "access premium bonuses when you join today." Focus moves from cost to unlock.
- For high-ticket bonuses: 1:1 strategy call, done-for-you audit, private community access, implementation support

**Step 13: Risk Mitigation**
- **Double Your Money-Back Guarantee:** "Implement for 60 days with documentation. If you don't earn back 2x your investment, we refund 2x your payment."
- For high-ticket: Conditional guarantees outperform unconditional. They signal confidence and filter for serious buyers.

**Step 14: Scarcity**

**7 Types of Scarcity:**
1. Price escalation (price goes up after deadline)
2. Bonus expiration (bonuses disappear after deadline)
3. Offer removal (entire offer disappears)
4. Reduced future access (fewer spots, less support next time)
5. Event-based constraints (cohort starts on X date, can't join late)
6. Collector's edition (this version with these specific bonuses won't repeat)
7. Stacking multiple simultaneously (combine 2-3 for maximum urgency)

**Rules:** Fake scarcity kills trust permanently. Only use genuine constraints. Honor every limit you set.

## Murder the Objection Methodology

### 10-80-10 Theory
- 10% will buy almost anything
- 10% will never buy
- 80% could go either way
- All closing energy targets the 80%

### The 3 Objection Tiers

| Tier | Objection | Reality | Approach |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Surface | **Price** | Almost never the real issue | Address 5-9 different ways |
| Secondary | **Time** | Multi-layered concern | Address 12+ different ways |
| Root | **Belief** | THE true barrier ("I can't do this") | Address 19+ different ways |

### Cycling Strategy
Alternate between price, time, and belief objections rather than drilling one. Prevents audience fatigue. Rather than 3 price closes, do 6. Rather than 4 time responses, do 12.

**High-ticket adaptation:** On a sales call, you have less time than a 3-hour webinar. Focus cycling on the primary and secondary objection only. Use 3-4 different angles per objection instead of exhaustive cycling.

### The 5 Pillars
1. **Authenticity** -- mean what you say
2. **Emotional Safety** -- create a safe environment for honesty
3. **Philosophy** -- mindset that supports ethical selling
4. **Strategy** -- implementation tactics
5. **Techniques** -- specific closing approaches

### Named Closing Techniques for High-Ticket

**Fear of Success Close:** "Do you fear failure or success? Most fear the unknown of winning."
- Use when a qualified prospect hesitates despite having budget and need. They may fear the accountability that comes with investing.

**Reverse Frame Close:** "I forbid you to make this much money."
- Makes them argue for why they CAN succeed. They sell themselves.

**Child Development Close:** "You learned to walk, talk, read without formal training. Those capabilities still exist."
- Use when self-doubt is the barrier: "I'm not technical enough," "I've never done this before."

**Talk to Strangers Close:** Acknowledge childhood conditioning, reframe its expiration date for adults.
- Use when cold outreach or networking is part of the program and the prospect fears it.

**Time Dilation Technique:** Temporal reframing for both future and past.
- "Will you remember this $5K investment in 5 years? Or will you remember the business you built because of it?"

## Webinar Delivery Principles

### Live vs. Automated
- **Start live.** Always. Automated requires completely different strategy and lower conversion expectations.
- Live allows real-time objection handling, which is where high-ticket closes happen.

### Optimal Length
- 75-90 min formal presentation
- Extended to 3-4+ hours through live interaction
- "The real webinar begins after all information is presented." -- the Q&A / live close is where deals happen.

### Sunk Cost Principle
More time spent = more value perceived = higher purchase likelihood. At high-ticket, this is critical. A 30-minute webinar won't close a $10K offer. A 3-hour workshop will.

### Content Strategy
- Content is the LEAST important section. Select 2-4 topics.
- "Make people pay MORE for LESS." Deep on 2 topics beats shallow on 10.
- The content section's job is to earn the right to sell, not to comprehensively educate.

### Affirmation Questions in Transitions
Between content sections, ask: "Did you find this helpful? Will you use this?"
- Multiple small yeses build momentum toward the big yes (purchase).
- On a sales call: "Does that make sense?" / "Can you see how that would work for your business?" Same principle, adapted format.

### Reverse Risk Model
Show prospects what it's like to be your customer BEFORE they buy. Give them the experience of working with you during the webinar itself.
- For high-ticket: Do a mini-diagnosis live. Solve one small problem publicly. They experience your coaching before paying.

### Dual-Level Communication
Simultaneously teach mechanics (overt) while covertly shifting limiting beliefs.
- Overt: "Here's how to structure your offer"
- Covert: "You ARE capable of selling at premium prices. You don't need more qualifications."

### Earned the Right to Sell
The entire content section answers one question: have we earned the right to sell?
- Don't rush to the pitch. If the audience doesn't feel they received genuine value, no close technique will save it.

### Selfish Motivation Transparency
"I'm doing this because it benefits me financially." Removes hidden agenda suspicion.
- At high-ticket: "I'm selective about who I work with because your results are my reputation. I need you to succeed."

### Vulnerability Trust Threshold
Customers need approximately 3 hours of interaction before revealing genuine obstacles.
- This is why short discovery calls often miss the real objection. Extended webinars or multi-touch sequences give prospects time to lower their guard.

## Offer Stacking / Value Stacking

### The $500M Switch
Not "buy this product and get bonuses" but "access premium bonuses when joining our program today."
- Focus shifts from spending to unlocking
- High-ticket: "When you join the accelerator, you unlock: private Slack access, monthly 1:1 calls, our entire template library, and priority support."

### For Every 1 Step Asked, Take 10 Steps For Them
Reduce cognitive load on buyer. At high-ticket, the offer should feel like LESS work for them, not more.

### Conditional Bonuses
Layer bonuses with contingencies.
- Example: $1,750 consulting call conditioned on generating $10K in sales first
- Worst case = great testimonial. Best case = high-ticket upsell
- At high-ticket: "Hit your first $10K month within the program, and I'll do a private strategy session to plan your next $50K month."

### Caveman Strategy
Give exactly what competition gives, then add more on top. Research what competitors include at the same price point, then stack additional value.

### Resale Rights Products
Include 3 ready-made products with resale rights as bonuses. Eliminates "I don't have a product" objection.
- High-ticket adaptation: Include done-for-you templates, swipe files, or frameworks the client can use with their own customers.

### Build the Offer Slide First
Create one master slide with every deliverable, guarantee, price, proof. Then expand each into 3-4 supporting slides. Content follows offer.
- For sales calls: Know your complete offer stack cold before the call. Every deliverable, every bonus, every guarantee. Written on one page.
