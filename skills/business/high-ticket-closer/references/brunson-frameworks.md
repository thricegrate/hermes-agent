# Brunson High-Ticket Frameworks

Russell Brunson's frameworks adapted for high-ticket sales calls and application funnels. These complement the existing Diagnose-Prescribe-Close methodology and Kennedy authority principles.

---

## 1. The 3 False Beliefs Diagnostic

Every prospect who hesitates on a high-ticket offer is blocked by one (or more) of three belief categories. Map all three BEFORE the call. The one you miss is the one that kills the deal.

### The Three Categories

**Vehicle Beliefs** -- "This type of solution won't work."
- "Coaching/consulting doesn't work for my industry"
- "I've tried programs like this before"
- "AI tools are overhyped, they won't actually save me time"
- "My business is different, this approach won't translate"

**Internal Beliefs** -- "Even if the vehicle works, I can't make it work."
- "I'm not technical enough"
- "I don't have the discipline to follow through"
- "I'm too old/young/inexperienced for this"
- "I always start things and never finish"
- "Other people can do this, but I'm not smart enough"

**External Beliefs** -- "Outside forces will prevent my success."
- "I don't have enough time"
- "I don't have the money to invest"
- "My spouse/partner won't support this"
- "The market is too saturated"
- "The economy is bad right now"

### Pre-Call Preparation

For every offer, build a False Belief Map:

```
VEHICLE BELIEFS          INTERNAL BELIEFS         EXTERNAL BELIEFS
---------------------    ---------------------    ---------------------
1. [Specific to offer]   1. [Specific to buyer]   1. [Specific to market]
2. ...                   2. ...                   2. ...
3. ...                   3. ...                   3. ...

EPIPHANY STORY:          EPIPHANY STORY:          EPIPHANY STORY:
[Your story that         [Your story that         [Your story that
breaks this belief]      breaks this belief]      breaks this belief]
```

### How to Build Epiphany Bridge Stories

An Epiphany Bridge story is a personal narrative that walks the prospect through YOUR belief shift. Not a logical argument. A story.

Structure for each story:
1. **Backstory**: "I used to believe [the same false belief]..."
2. **The wall**: "I hit a point where [pain/frustration]..."
3. **The epiphany**: "Then I realized [the insight that changed everything]..."
4. **The transformation**: "Once I saw it differently, [specific result]..."
5. **The framework**: "Now I teach this as [your method/system]..."

Rules:
- One story per belief category minimum. Three is ideal.
- Stories must be TRUE. Prospects smell fabrication instantly on a live call.
- Keep each story under 90 seconds when spoken aloud.
- The best stories include a specific moment of realization, not a gradual shift.

### Using the Diagnostic on the Call

During discovery (Phase 2), listen for which belief category dominates:

| They say...                                    | Category  | Priority |
|-----------------------------------------------|-----------|----------|
| "I've tried similar things"                    | Vehicle   | High     |
| "Programs like this don't work in my niche"    | Vehicle   | High     |
| "I'm not sure I could actually implement it"   | Internal  | High     |
| "I always get stuck halfway through"           | Internal  | High     |
| "I just don't have the time right now"         | External  | Medium   |
| "My partner would kill me"                     | External  | Medium   |

**The rule:** Identify the DOMINANT belief. Address it FIRST with your Epiphany Bridge story. Then handle the secondary beliefs. If you address an external belief when the real block is internal, you waste the close.

**Transition phrase after the story:** "Does that make sense? Can you see how that would change things for you?" (This doubles as a trial close.)

## 2. Trial Closes Throughout the Conversation

Most closers wait until Phase 4 to ask for commitment. That's too late. Brunson's approach: seed micro-commitments throughout the entire call so the final close is just the last in a chain of "yes" responses.

### The Trial Close Phrases

Use these after every major point, story, or insight:

- **"Am I right?"** -- After restating their problem
- **"Can you see how that would work?"** -- After explaining your methodology
- **"Would you agree?"** -- After a logical point about their situation
- **"Isn't that interesting?"** -- After sharing a counterintuitive insight
- **"Does that make sense?"** -- After walking through a process or timeline
- **"Can you imagine what that would look like for you?"** -- After painting the outcome

### Placement Map

| Call Phase               | Trial Close After...                          | Example                                                        |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Discovery (Phase 2)      | Restating their problem                       | "So the real issue isn't X, it's Y. Am I right?"              |
| Discovery (Phase 2)      | Cost of inaction calculation                  | "That's $50K left on the table this year. Would you agree?"   |
| Prescription (Phase 3)   | Each Epiphany Bridge story                    | "Can you see how that changes things?"                        |
| Prescription (Phase 3)   | Methodology walkthrough                       | "Does that make sense for your situation?"                    |
| Prescription (Phase 3)   | Case study                                    | "Isn't that interesting? They were in the same spot you are." |
| Pre-Close (Phase 3-4)    | Transformation arc (Month 1, 2, 3)            | "Can you imagine what that would look like for your business?"|

### The Critical Rule: Stop on "No"

If the prospect says "no," hesitates, or gives a non-committal response to ANY trial close:

1. **STOP.** Do not continue to the next point.
2. **Explore.** "Tell me more about that. What part doesn't sit right?"
3. **Address.** Resolve their concern with a story, proof point, or reframe.
4. **Re-trial.** Ask the trial close again (rephrased).
5. **Only then** continue forward.

Moving past a "no" on a trial close guarantees a "no" on the final close. Every unresolved micro-objection compounds.

### Counting Your Trial Closes

Target: 5-8 trial closes before the formal close. If you get to Phase 4 with fewer than 5, you've been presenting, not selling. Go back and confirm understanding at each point.

After 5+ "yes" responses, the final close feels like a natural conclusion, not a pressure moment.

## 3. Application Funnel Qualification

Brunson's application funnel principle: make the prospect WORK to get on the call. By the time they finish the application, they've already invested emotionally and started selling themselves.

### The Psychology

Standard funnel: "Book a free call!" -> Low commitment -> High no-show rate -> Tire-kickers on calls.

Application funnel: "Apply for a strategy session" -> Higher commitment -> Lower no-show rate -> Pre-qualified prospects on calls.

The act of filling out an application shifts the prospect's identity. They go from "someone browsing" to "someone who applied." That identity shift carries into the call.

### Application Form Structure

**Section 1: Context (Easy questions first -- build momentum)**
- "What's your business/what do you do?" (Open text)
- "How long have you been doing this?" (Dropdown)
- "What's your current monthly revenue?" (Range dropdown)

**Section 2: Goals (Make them articulate their desire)**
- "What's your #1 goal for the next 90 days?" (Open text)
- "What would achieving that goal mean for you personally?" (Open text)
- "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to making this happen?" (Scale)

**Section 3: Self-Qualification (They convince themselves)**
- "What have you already tried to solve this?" (Open text)
- "Why do you think it didn't work?" (Open text)
- "Why are you a good fit for this program?" (Open text -- this is the key question)
- "Why now? What's changed that makes this the right time?" (Open text)

**Section 4: Investment Readiness (Filter before the call)**
- "This program requires an investment of [$range]. Are you prepared to invest at this level if it's the right fit?" (Yes/No/Need to discuss with partner)
- "If accepted, how quickly could you get started?" (Dropdown: immediately, within a week, within a month)

### Why "Why are you a good fit?" is the most important question

When someone writes out why THEY are a good fit for YOUR program, they're doing your selling for you. They're listing their own qualifications, their own readiness, their own reasons. On the call, you can literally read their answer back to them: "You said you're a good fit because [their words]. I agree. Here's why..."

### Application Review Before the Call

Use their application answers to:
1. Pre-identify their dominant false belief (from Section 3 answers)
2. Prepare the right Epiphany Bridge story
3. Know their revenue and commitment level (no awkward money questions on the call)
4. Spot red flags before investing 45 minutes (save the call slot for qualified prospects)

### Reverse Squeeze Page Concept

For cold or warm traffic that isn't ready to apply yet:

1. **Lead with value**: Free tool, assessment, or training that delivers a genuine result
2. **Capture email**: After they've received value (not before)
3. **Nurture**: 3-5 emails that build belief and demonstrate expertise
4. **Invite to apply**: "Based on your results with [free tool], you might be a fit for [program]. Apply here."

This flips the traditional model. Instead of asking for something first (email), you give something first (value). The reciprocity principle makes the application feel like a natural next step, not a cold ask.

## 4. Change the Selling Environment

Brunson's principle for $3K+ offers: the environment where selling happens determines the close rate more than the script does.

### The Environment Ladder

| Price Point   | Selling Environment           | Close Rate Benchmark |
|---------------|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| $27-$297      | Online sales page             | 1-5%                |
| $297-$997     | Webinar or VSL                | 5-15%               |
| $997-$2,997   | Webinar + follow-up call      | 10-20%              |
| $3,000-$9,999 | Application + 1:1 call        | 20-35%              |
| $10,000+      | Application + call + in-person| 30-50%              |

### The Rule

**The higher the price, the more personal the environment needs to be.**

You cannot consistently close $5K+ offers with a sales page alone. The online funnel's job for high-ticket is NOT to close. It's to:
1. Build awareness and belief (content, emails, free tools)
2. Qualify interest (application)
3. Schedule the human conversation (call booking)

The CALL closes. The funnel delivers qualified, educated, emotionally invested prospects to the call.

### Structuring the Funnel for Calls, Not Checkouts

Common mistake: putting a "Buy Now" button on a $5K offer page. Even if someone clicks it, buyer's remorse rates are sky-high because they haven't had their questions answered or their beliefs addressed.

Instead:

**Funnel flow for $3K-$10K:**
1. Lead magnet or free tool (captures email, demonstrates expertise)
2. Value sequence (3-7 emails building belief, sharing Epiphany Bridge stories)
3. Application page (self-qualification, investment readiness)
4. Call booking (calendar + confirmation page with pre-call content)
5. Sales call (Diagnose-Prescribe-Close)
6. Payment + onboarding

**Funnel flow for $10K+:**
All of the above, plus:
- Pre-call video or training they must watch before the call
- Possible second call ("let me think about it" gets a scheduled follow-up, not silence)
- In-person option if geographically feasible (dinner, event, office visit)

### Environment Signals That Increase Close Rates

Small details that shift the selling environment from "transactional" to "premium":

- **Personalized call confirmation**: "Looking forward to speaking with you, [name]. Based on your application, I've prepared some thoughts on [their specific challenge]."
- **Pre-call content**: A 10-minute video addressing their likely false beliefs BEFORE the call
- **Professional call setup**: Good audio, camera on, clean background, no distractions
- **Unhurried energy**: Never rush. Never check the clock visibly. Premium feels spacious.
- **Post-call follow-up speed**: Personalized email within 2 hours, not a generic autoresponder

### When to Break This Rule

The only time you can sell $3K+ without a call:
- The buyer already knows you well (existing customer upgrading)
- You have overwhelming social proof specific to their situation
- There's genuine scarcity (limited spots, cohort closing)
- They explicitly ask to buy without a call

Even then, offer the call. "Happy to take your payment now, but I'd also love to hop on a quick call to make sure we set you up for success." This reduces refund rates dramatically.
