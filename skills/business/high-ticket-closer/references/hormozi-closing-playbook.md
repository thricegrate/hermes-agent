## Onion of Blame Framework

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, pp.13-15

People avoid buying decisions by casting blame outward in layers, like peeling an onion. The closer you get to the core (self), the closer you are to a genuine decision.

**Three layers:**

1. **Layer 1 -- External Circumstances:** "I can't because of X outside my control." Time, money, fit. The outermost layer. Easiest to address because it's impersonal.
2. **Layer 2 -- Other People:** "I can't because of someone else." Spouse, partner, bad past experiences with other vendors. Harder because now they're pointing at a human.
3. **Layer 3 -- Self:** "I can't because of me." Preferences, fear, avoidance, rushed-decision anxiety. The core. This is where the real decision lives.

**Core insight:** When someone swaps objections ("OK, it's not the money, but I need to talk to my wife"), you're closing correctly. They peeled one layer and revealed the next. Keep peeling until they have enough power to make a genuine decision.

**How to use it:**
- Diagnose which layer the objection comes from before selecting a close
- Expect 2-3 objection swaps per prospect. That's normal, not failure.
- If you handle Layer 1 and they jump to Layer 3, you skipped a layer. Good. They trust you enough to be honest.
- Every close in the 50-script library below maps to a specific layer

## Three-Bucket Audience Split

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, pp.9-10

Advertising splits your audience into three buckets, not two. Most businesses only capture Bucket 1 and miss the massive profit pool in Bucket 3.

**The three buckets:**

1. **YESes (Lay-downs):** Advertising already closed them. Accept payment. Don't overcomplicate it. Don't keep selling after they said yes.
2. **NOs (Unqualified):** Wrong fit. This is an advertising problem, not a sales problem. Don't burn time closing unqualified people.
3. **MAYBEs (On the fence):** They value what you sell but have reservations. THIS IS WHERE THE MONEY IS. Most interested people fall here.

**Why it matters:** If you only capture lay-downs, you're leaving the majority of your revenue on the table. Closing is the skill that captures Bucket 3. Every script in this playbook exists to move MAYBEs to YES.

## STAR Qualification

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, p.29

Qualify prospects before selling by checking four dimensions. Unqualified prospects are an advertising problem, not a sales problem. But prospects fib, so you still train closing.

- **S -- Situation:** Do they have the problem to solve?
- **T -- Timing:** Do they have the time to solve it?
- **A -- Authority:** Do they have the authority to decide?
- **R -- Resources:** Do they have the money to spend?

If any of these are genuinely missing, closing won't fix it. Route to marketing.

## Decision-Making Power Model

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, pp.11-12

Closing is not about getting prospects to buy. It's about giving them enough decision-making power to overcome avoidance.

People on the fence lack the power to decide. The goal is a genuine decision (yes OR no), not a forced yes.

**Three steps:**
1. Understand what they want (from earlier in the sales conversation)
2. Put what they want next to the options they have
3. Make sure they come to a genuine decision

A genuine "no" is better than a wishy-washy "maybe" that ghosts you later. Give them enough clarity and power to decide. If the offer is right for them, they'll say yes. If it's not, a clear no saves everyone's time.

## 28 Rules of Closing

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, pp.16-19

Not a script. A philosophy. These govern how all specific closes should be delivered.

1. When in doubt, repeat back what they said (buys time + shows listening)
2. Acknowledge or agree, never disagree (say "totally understand" before any close)
3. Before getting real, get permission ("Can I be a coach instead of a friend for a second?")
4. Stack closes: if the first doesn't land, hit from another angle
5. You don't need to memorize, you need to understand (use your own words)
6. You only need 2-3 closes per obstacle
7. Yellows are golds: practice on unqualified leads ("be a trashman")
8. Volume negates luck
9. Don't assume objections mean they won't buy (sometimes they're just processing out loud)
10. Never change your price on the spot
11. Only ask for the sale when you think you've got them
12. People want to believe you. Help their brains justify the decision they already want to make.
13. Selling happens before the ask. Closing happens after.
14. The person who cares the most about the prospect wins the sale.
15. Selling is a transference of belief over a bridge of trust.
16. Belief and trust are a continuum, not binaries.
17. If you're going to say something offensive, say it about someone like them or your past self (not them directly).
18. Once they say yes, shut up.
19. Record all your sales to learn from them.

**Rules 20-28 (extracted from context throughout the playbook):**

20. The close starts when they say no. Everything before that is selling.
21. A prospect who swaps objections is a prospect who's getting closer to yes.
22. Never negotiate against yourself. Let them name the specific concern.
23. The best closers ask better questions, not give better pitches.
24. If you wouldn't buy it at this price, you can't sell it at this price. Believe in the offer.
25. Tone matters more than words. Same script, different energy, different result.
26. Silence is a close. After you ask, wait. Don't fill the gap.
27. Handle the objection, then re-ask. Don't handle and hope.
28. The guarantee close is the last resort, not the first move. Lead with value, not safety nets.

## 10x the 10% Rule

**Source:** $100M Fast Cash Playbook, p.8

**The math:**
- If you get 10% of your customers to pay 10x the price, you double your revenue.
- Since you already spent money to acquire those customers, the extra revenue (minus delivery) drops straight to the bottom line.
- Can 2-4x revenue from one simple premium offer.

**Why it works:**
- 1 out of 9 Americans is a millionaire (hidden purchasing power in every audience)
- 10% of customers at 10x price = 2x revenue
- No additional acquisition cost = nearly all profit
- Limit spots to attract big spenders (scarcity selects for quality)

**Application:** After closing your standard offer, identify the top 10% of buyers and pitch a premium tier. The premium tier stacks attention, personalization, speed, and access on top of the core offer. This is how you build your product ladder upward without needing a single new lead.

## ID-to-Card Payment Close

**Source:** $100M Closing Playbook, p.23

A physical sales technique that prevents "forgot my wallet" objections by getting their ID first. Works in-person and has a remote adaptation.

**Steps:**
1. Get verbal agreement ("So you're ready to get started?")
2. Ask for their ID ("I just need your ID to get the paperwork going")
3. Wallet comes out. Card is now visible.
4. Use ID to fill out the contract. Make small talk about their photo, hometown.
5. Gesture with ID toward their open wallet: "I'll trade you for the card you wanna use."
6. Process payment.

**Remote adaptation:**
- Ask them to bring their ID to the screen
- Use it to fill out the contract visually

**Fallback -- "Card Not On Me" Close:**
- "Pull up your banking app, go to statements, find account number on top right. I'll Google the routing number."
- Removes every physical barrier to payment

## 50 Named Closing Scripts

Organized by blame layer from the Onion of Blame framework. Each close maps to a specific objection type.

### All-Purpose Closes

These cut through smoke and mirrors. The 20% of closes that work 80% of the time. Use when you can't pinpoint which layer the blame is on, or as openers before drilling into specifics.

**1. "What's Your Main Concern" Close**
"What's your main concern? What are you afraid of happening?" If they give fluff, escalate: "So you're afraid of spending money and me stealing it and spending it in Vegas?" They laugh, then give the real objection.

**2. "Reason" Close**
"The reason you're telling yourself not to do this IS the reason you should do it."
- Money version: "The fact you don't have money is the very reason you need this more than anyone."
- Time version: "The fact you don't have time is the very reason you need this."

**3. "Hypothetical" Close**
"If this were perfect, would you do it? Then what's the difference between perfect and what we've got?" If they can't name anything: "Sounds like it's nothing to do with the program. What are you afraid of happening?"

**4. "Zoom Out" Close**
"Let's zoom out. You want X. We sell X. Do you think overanalyzing details is why you haven't made it happen yet?"

**5. "1 to 10" Close**
"On a scale of 1-10, where are you?" If 9 or less: "What would it take to get you to a 10?" Handle that one thing, then: "Feeling 10ish now?"

**6. "Best Case Worst Case" Close**
Three variations. Core logic: both options are risk-free (with guarantee), only one GUARANTEES you don't get what you want -- walking out the door.

**7. "Card Not On Me" Close**
"Pull up your banking app, go to statements, find account number on top right. I'll Google the routing number."

### Blame: Time Closes

**8. "Better To Start When You're Busy"**
"The busier you are, the better time it is to start. If you can't make it work during busy seasons, it isn't going to last."

**9. "You're Gonna Get Busy Again"**
"Even if you wait, you'd get busy again, right? So learn to make it work when busy, and you'll stick with it no matter what."

**10. "It's Not About Timing, It's About Priorities"**
"What are you doing that's more important than this right now? Either your goal is more important than these interruptions, or it's not."

**11. "Smartphone Close"**
Story about wife pulling up screen time showing 20 hours/week on social media. "I guess I found you some time."

**12. "When/Then" Close**
"This is a when/then issue. 'When I get healthy, then I'll go to the doctor.' It flips the order."

### Blame: Money Closes

#### Not Enough Value (price too high = value too low)

**13. "It's Good That It's A Lot"**
"The fact this feels like a big investment means you're more likely to take it seriously."

**14. "Good Things Aren't Cheap"**
"How many free PDFs have you downloaded and made no more money?"

**15. "Would You Even Believe Me If It Were Less?"**
"Would you believe it could do what I say at 1/3 the price? (No) Then what are we talking about?"

**16. "It's Not A Lot When You Think About It"**
"If this got you an extra $10K/mo, would it be worth it? Then it's not the price, it's whether you believe you can do it."

**17. "You're Gonna Spend The Money Either Way"**
"In the next 12 months, you're gonna spend this amount either way. All we're deciding is WHERE it goes."

**18. "You Pay The Price Either Way"**
"Years of your own life to figure it out, or a payment today to use time other people already invested."

**19. "Some Now Or More Later"**
"It's only gonna become more expensive. The amount of time and money will only increase as you put it off."

**20. "Future Favor"**
"If 10 years ago you could have made a decision that would have made the last 10 years the best of your life, would that be worth it? Let's do future you a favor."

#### Can't Afford It

**21. "Resourcefulness Not Resources"**
"The difference between a self-made millionaire when they had nothing and someone who has nothing? Nothing financially. It was resourcefulness, not resources."

**22. "Had It Worse And Done Better"**
"Do you think there's anyone else who's been in your situation who's achieved this? What's the difference between them and you?"

**23. "Everyone Starts At Zero"**
"All self-made millionaires have something in common: they all started at zero." Nudge: "If you needed life-saving surgery, would you find the money?"

#### Competitor Is Cheaper

**24. "Cheap Comparison Close"**
"If we were the same price, which would you pick? (You) Why? (They list reasons) Those are awesome reasons. Now you understand why we aren't the same price."

**25. "Cheap Or What You Need"**
"You can buy something cheap, or what you need."

#### Haggling for Discount

**26. "We Could Do It For More" Close**
"Uhh...we could do it for more." Ends haggling immediately.

**27. "It's Not, Not About The Money"**
"If this were free, would you do it? (Yes) So it's not, not about the money. Lock in savings now."

### Blame: Other People Closes

#### Decision Maker Sequence (6 steps)

**28. Step 1: "What if they say no?"**
One-third say "I'd do it anyway." Close immediately if they do.

**29. Step 2: "What specifically would they object to?"**
Redirects from vague fear to a solvable obstacle.

**30. Step 3: "If they know you're not happy, why would they stop you?"**
Reframe: the spouse/partner would approve if they knew the full picture.

**31. Step 4: "Ask for support, not permission."**
"If you give that power to them and two years from now you're still struggling, who are you going to blame? That's when resentment sets in."

**32. Step 5: Humorous extreme hypothetical**
"If he tells you he wants you to stay overweight, sit on the couch, reach for the Cheetos...you have him call me."

**33. Three Day No-Sweat Guarantee downsell**
Put card down, delay payment 24-72 hours. "Lock in your spot but still have time to chat."

#### Bad Past Experiences

**34. "Don't Let It Burn You Twice"**
"That bad decision burned you once when you made the poor investment. It'll burn you twice if it prevents you from making a good one."

### Blame: Self Closes

#### Preferences ("Not for me")

**35. "Isolate And Solve"**
"What specifically would make it for you vs. not for you?"

**36. "We Vote With Our Dollars"**
Sephora story about little girls stepping into a new identity. "When you have a new identity, you have new priorities."

**37. "Pain Of Change vs. Pain Of Staying The Same"**
"We change when the pain of staying the same is higher than the pain of change."

**38. "You Gotta Change To Change"**
"What you've been doing has been getting you what you've been getting."

**39. "Change The Variables, Change The Outcome"**
"We could change this thing you don't like, but then you'd get an outcome you don't like."

**40. "Mechanic Close"**
"That's a great question. Answering it would be like telling a mechanic what's wrong with your car before bringing it in."

**41. "Surgeon Secretary Close"**
"For me to answer that would be like a secretary at a heart surgeon's office telling you what the doctor's gonna do."

#### Rushed Decisions / Need to Think

**42. "What Would Make It A No?"**
"If you don't mind my asking, what would make this a no?" If they can't name what makes it a no, they're simply unsure, just like every other great customer.

**43. "Don't Repeat The Same Mistake"**
"Do you think maybe you're in this situation because you've delayed making the decision?"

**44. "How Much Has Not Deciding Cost You?"**
"The question isn't what it costs you. It's how much has it cost you to NOT decide up to this point?"

**45. "Another Year of Almost"**
"No one wants another year of almost hitting your goals, almost getting to 10K/mo, almost retiring your wife..."

**46. "It Doesn't Take Time, It Takes Information"**
"You're not gonna go home, sit in a rocking chair and think 'hmm, am I going to do this program?' You're gonna get in your car, and life takes over."

**47. "The Gameplan Close"**
Walk them through 3 yes-or-no questions:
1. Will this help you get closer to your goals?
2. Do you have access to the money?
3. Can I help you better than going alone?

**48. "This Isn't A Fast Decision"**
"You kinda made this decision years ago. You saw an ad, clicked, opted in, set time aside, showed up. You're six inches from gold, don't stop digging."

#### Guarantee Closes

**49. "Both Options Are Risk Free"**
"I'm not asking you to make a decision. I'm asking you to make an INFORMED decision, which you can only do once you try it."

**50. "Decadere"**
"De-cadere is Latin for 'to kill off.' Doing this is a decision. Not doing this is a decision. Which future are you cutting off?"

## Setter Sequence

**Source:** $100M Leads Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.1-3

Complete messenger "setter" script for converting DM conversations into phone calls. The entire point is to get a 10-minute warm-up call.

**Golden Response Rules (every message follows these):**
1. Acknowledge / encourage / build rapport
2. Ask next question in sequence

**The 9-step sequence:**

**Step 1 -- Open:**
"Hey Name - what's going on in your world today?"

**Step 2 -- Relate:**
Connect their engagement to your content/page. Relate back to not having enough time.

**Step 3 -- Transition:**
"What do you do for work?" -> "Does it keep you busy?" -> "Does it make it hard to [do the thing they want]?"

**Step 4 -- Clarify Goal:**
"What are you trying to do right now [niche]-wise?"

**Step 5 -- Label With Problem:**
"So you're trying to [restate what they said]. What's been your biggest struggle?"

**Step 6 -- Overview Pain:**
"What have you tried before? How'd that work for you?"

**Step 7 -- Create Curiosity:**
"I have a bunch of clients in the same boat. Have some suggestions if you want to hear them."

**Step 8 -- Get on Phone:**
"It would take 23498 pages to type out haha but whenever we have a minute we can hop on a quick call."

**Step 9 -- TKAD (Time Kills All Deals):**
Complete the entire process in 48 hours or less. Every hour you wait, the lead gets colder. If they have time to DM you, they have 5 minutes to get on a call.

## BDR Cold Call Script

**Source:** $100M Leads Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.15-18

Full phone script for cold-calling prospects. Uses industry problems as hooks.

**Structure:**

1. **Open with social proof:** "We've worked with about [#] of [Avatar]"
2. **Present industry problems:** "They're struggling with X and Y"
3. **Ask about their situation:** "How has the past year been for you?"
4. **If negative (no pain):** Redirect to specific problem categories
5. **Offer free 30-min consultation:** "[outcome] in first [time] without [pain]"
6. **If still no:** Send info + free training, then re-pitch the call
7. **Always end with referral ask:** "Do you know any [Avatar] that would like [goal]?"

The referral ask at the end turns every cold call into a potential warm introduction, even if the prospect themselves isn't interested. Never skip it.

## B2B Setter Script

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads pp.19-22 / Warm Outreach Downloads pp.1-4 (identical script)

Full phone script for calling back prospects who responded to cold outreach (email, LinkedIn, etc). Key tactic: uses a pre-meeting video that the prospect "chooses" to watch (ownership psychology).

**Structure:**

1. **Reference how they responded:** "You responded to [an email/LinkedIn message]"
2. **Compliment their company:** "I've done research, I'm on your website right now"
3. **Ask why they responded** (surfaces real motivation)
4. **Get current situation + goal + timeline**
5. **Recap and ask:** "What made you reach out again?" (highlights pain a second time)
6. **Offer video:** "Two options -- watch on next call or I send it" (prospect takes ownership of choice)
7. **Book within 36 hours** (TKAD principle)
8. **Qualify decision makers:** "Is there anyone else beneficial to have on the call?"
9. **Triple confirmation:** Calendar invite received? Video received? Any reason you won't show?

The triple confirmation at the end kills no-shows. Each question creates a micro-commitment. By the third one, they've publicly stated three times that they'll be there.

## BAMFAM

**Book A Meeting From A Meeting.**

**Source:** $100M Leads framework (referenced across multiple Hormozi materials)

Never leave a meeting without the next meeting booked. Whether it's a discovery call, a demo, a check-in, or a casual chat, the last 2 minutes are always:

"Before we wrap up, let's get the next one on the calendar."

**Why it works:**
- Momentum is highest at the end of a productive conversation
- "I'll follow up" = they forget, you chase, energy dies
- Calendar commitment while both parties are engaged = 3x higher show rates
- Removes the entire follow-up dance

**Application:** Use at the end of every setter call, every discovery call, every sales presentation. If they say they need to think about it, BAMFAM still applies: "Let's book a follow-up for Thursday so we can discuss. If you decide before then, just let me know."

## Appointment Pull-Forward Script

**Source:** $100M Leads Warm Outreach Downloads

Same-day pull-forward technique. When a prospect books for a future date, attempt to pull them forward to today.

**The ask:** "Hey, I actually had a cancellation today at [time]. Want to jump on sooner rather than later?"

**Five outcomes:**

1. **They accept** -- you talk to them today while motivation is peak
2. **They reschedule to tomorrow** -- still faster than original date
3. **They confirm original date** -- at minimum you've re-engaged them, which reduces no-show risk
4. **They ghost the pull-forward** -- red flag. Increase follow-up frequency.
5. **They cancel entirely** -- they were going to no-show anyway. Better to know now.

Every outcome gives you useful information. There is no downside to attempting the pull-forward.

## Double Dial Technique

**Source:** $100M Leads Warm Outreach Downloads, p.5

If a prospect doesn't pick up the first call or it goes to voicemail, hang up and call back immediately.

**Why it works:**
- Second call signals urgency and legitimacy
- People assume telemarketers don't double-dial
- Dramatically increases connect rates with zero extra effort

**Process:**
1. First call goes to voicemail -> hang up
2. Call back immediately (same number)
3. If they pick up: "Hey, sorry about that, call dropped. Glad I caught you."
4. If voicemail again: leave the Voicemail Open Loop (see below)

## Voicemail Open Loop

**Source:** $100M Leads Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.23-24

A 15-second voicemail script designed for maximum callback rate. Uses three psychological triggers: brevity, relevance, and an open loop.

**The script:**
"Hi [NAME], it's [YOUR NAME]. I'm calling in reference to [THEIR COMPETITOR / KNOWN CLIENT]. Please give me a call back at [NUMBER]."

**Three rules:**
1. Say "It's [name]" not "This is [name]" (casual, not corporate)
2. Never mention your company name (kills curiosity)
3. Always follow up (voicemail is step 1, not a one-shot)

**Why it works:**
- Short: no time to overthink whether to call back
- Relevant: competitor name grabs attention
- Open loop: humans hate unresolved curiosity. They HAVE to know what you're calling about.

## Card-On-File Close

**Source:** $100M Leads Warm Outreach Downloads, pp.5-8

Full script for booking a free assessment/consultation while capturing card on file. Combines deep discovery with scarcity positioning.

### PAIN / DIG / GET REAL Discovery Sequence

**PAIN phase:**
- "What motivated you to reach out?"
- "Where are you right now? Where do you want to be?"
- "Which gap bothers you the most?"
- "How long has this been bothering you?"
- "What bad habits got you here?"

**DIG phase:**
- "What negative impact has this had on your life?" (kids, marriage, work, body image, sleep)
- Probe each area they mention. Don't settle for surface answers.

**GET REAL phase:**
- "I need you to be REAL with me."
- "Ten years down the road, if nothing changes, who are you going to be?"
- Let silence do the work after this question.

### The Close

1. **Scarcity position:** "Let me see if we have anything available..." (check calendar/system)
2. **Open the spot:** "I actually have one spot open on [date]"
3. **Card capture:** "We require a card on file to hold your spot and reserve this discount"
4. **Post-booking:** Text them the address + parking info + a selfie of who they'll meet

### Three Overcomes for Card Objection

1. **Hair/nails analogy:** "Just like any service -- hair salon, nail appointment -- you put a card down to reserve your spot."
2. **No-show story:** "We used to have a 10% show rate without it. People would book and never come. The card just makes sure the spot goes to someone who's serious."
3. **Humor disarm:** "Is the fear that I'm going on a shopping spree with your card? Or that you won't actually show up?" (They laugh. Then they give the card.)
