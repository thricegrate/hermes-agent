# Sales Call Structure

Minute-by-minute breakdown of a high-ticket sales call using the
Diagnose-Prescribe-Close framework from Automatic Clients.

Total call length: 35-45 minutes. Never go over 60 minutes.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes before)

Before every call, review:
- Their application form answers
- Their frontend purchase history (what they bought, when)
- Any awareness bridge content they've engaged with
- Their business/social media presence (quick scan)
- Any previous email interactions or support tickets

**Goal**: Walk in knowing their story so they feel heard, not interrogated.

---

## Phase 1: Rapport & Frame Setting (2-3 minutes)

### Opening script framework:
"Hey [name], good to connect. Thanks for booking this call. Before we dive in,
let me tell you how this is going to work: I'm going to ask you a bunch of
questions to really understand where you are and where you want to go. Then,
if I genuinely believe I can help you get there, I'll share exactly how. And
if I don't think we're the right fit, I'll tell you that too and point you in
the right direction. Sound good?"

### Why this frame matters:
- Removes pressure: "I might say no to you" is disarming
- Establishes authority: You're the one qualifying THEM
- Sets expectations: They know what's coming
- Builds trust: Honesty from the first 30 seconds

### Transition:
"Great. So tell me, what made you book this call today? What's going on in
your business right now?"

---

## Phase 2: Discovery / Diagnosis (15-20 minutes)

This is the most important phase. Whoever asks the questions controls the
conversation. Listen 80%, talk 20%.

### Question flow (adapt based on responses):

**Current Situation:**
- "Walk me through your business right now. What do you do, who do you serve?"
- "What's your revenue looking like? Monthly? Annually?"
- "How are you currently getting clients/customers?"
- "What does a typical week look like for you?"

**Desired Outcome:**
- "Where do you want to be in 6-12 months?"
- "What would your business look like if [their problem] was solved?"
- "What's the number you're trying to hit?"
- "Why is THAT the goal? What does that unlock for you?"

**The Gap:**
- "So you're at [current] and you want to be at [desired]. What do you think
  is standing in the way?"
- "What have you tried so far to solve this?"
- "What happened? Why didn't it work?"
- "What do you think you're missing?"

**Cost of Inaction:**
- "What happens if nothing changes in the next 6-12 months?"
- "How long have you been dealing with this?"
- "What is this costing you? Not just in money, but in time, stress, missed
  opportunities?"

**Timeline & Readiness:**
- "When do you need this solved by?"
- "If we could help you get [their desired outcome], would you be ready to
  start right away?"
- "What would need to be true for you to say yes today?"

### During discovery, listen for:
- **Pain points**: The deeper and more emotional, the more motivated they are
- **Previous failures**: These tell you what objections to preempt
- **Language patterns**: Use their exact words back to them in Phase 3
- **Red flags**: Blame-shifting, unrealistic expectations (see closing-framework.md)

---

## Phase 3: Prescription / Solution (10-15 minutes)

### Bridge from diagnosis to prescription:

"Let me make sure I've got this right. You're at [current situation], doing
[current approach], and you're stuck because [gap they identified]. You want
to get to [desired outcome] by [timeline]. Does that sound accurate?"

(Wait for confirmation. If they correct anything, adjust.)

### Present the solution:

"Based on everything you've told me, here's what I think is going on:
[diagnosis in their words]. The reason [their previous approach] didn't work
is because [specific insight based on your expertise].

What we do is [your methodology in simple terms]. Let me walk you through
what that would look like for you specifically:

**Month 1**: [Specific milestones relevant to their situation]
**Month 2**: [Next phase of transformation]
**Month 3**: [Expected outcomes and results]

And the reason I know this works is because [client name] was in almost the
exact same situation as you. They were [similar problem], and after
[timeframe], they [specific result]."

### Match to the right level:

Present the option that fits their situation:

- **If they're a DWY buyer**: "The way this works is we do it together. I give
  you the exact system, the templates, the frameworks, and then we meet
  [weekly/biweekly] so I can guide you through implementation and troubleshoot
  anything that comes up."

- **If they're a DFY buyer**: "The way this works is my team and I handle
  everything. We build [specific deliverables], and you just need to
  [minimal input required]. You get the results without the learning curve."

---

## Phase 4: The Close (5-10 minutes)

### Trial close:
"Based on everything we've discussed, does this sound like what you need?"

(If yes, continue. If hesitation, ask "What part are you unsure about?")

### Present the investment:

"The investment for [program name] is [price]. That includes [list what's
included: calls, resources, access, support, guarantee].

We also offer [payment plan option: 2-pay of $X, or 3-pay of $X].

And here's our guarantee: [specific guarantee from product-offer]."

### Ask for the decision:

"So here's what I'd recommend: [specific recommendation based on their
situation]. If you're ready to get started, I can send you the enrollment
link right now and we can schedule your kickoff for [specific date]. What
do you think?"

### If yes:
- "Great. Let me send you the payment link right now."
- Stay on the call until payment is confirmed
- Schedule kickoff call before hanging up
- "You're going to receive a welcome email within the hour with everything
  you need to get started."

### If objection:
- Use LAER framework from closing-framework.md
- Address the specific objection
- Return to close: "Does that address your concern?"

### If not now:
- "I completely understand. Let me do this: I'll send you a recap of everything
  we discussed along with [relevant case study]. Take some time, and let's
  touch base on [specific day]. Does [day] at [time] work?"
- Schedule a specific follow-up (don't leave it open-ended)
- Add them to post-call follow-up sequence

### If not a fit:
- Be direct: "Based on what you've shared, I don't think this is the right
  program for you right now. Here's why: [honest reason]. What I'd recommend
  instead is [alternative]."
- This builds enormous trust and generates referrals

---

## Phase 5: Post-Call (immediate)

### If they bought:
1. Send payment link (if not done on call)
2. Send welcome email with:
   - What they purchased confirmation
   - Onboarding checklist (what to do first)
   - Community access (Skool, Slack, etc.)
   - Kickoff call link/scheduling
3. Add to customer CRM
4. Remove from sales sequences
5. Begin onboarding sequence

### If they didn't buy:
1. Send personalized email within 2 hours
2. Add to post-call follow-up sequence
3. Note their specific objection in CRM
4. Set follow-up reminder for the scheduled date

### If no-show:
1. Send immediate SMS ("I'm on the call, want me to wait?")
2. Follow no-show cadence from closing-framework.md

---

## Call Recording & Review

Record every sales call (with permission) for:

1. **Self-improvement**: Review your own calls weekly. Where did you lose them?
2. **Training**: Use successful calls to train new closers
3. **Pattern recognition**: What objections come up most? Address them earlier.
4. **Compliance**: Ensure no prohibited claims are made

### Review checklist:
- Did I listen more than I talked? (80/20 rule)
- Did I diagnose before prescribing?
- Did I use their language, not mine?
- Did I address the real objection?
- Did I establish urgency naturally?
- Was there a clear next step?
