## 1. "Are You Still Looking" Cold Opener

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, p.5

Hormozi's #1 cold opener. No images, no links, no formatting. Just one question. He calls this "among the first things I do when I invest in a new business."

**Format:**
```
Are you still looking to [4 word desire]?
```

**Rules:**
- No images. No links. No formatting. Just the question.
- Remove "still" for first-touch cold outreach (they haven't talked to you before, so "still" doesn't make sense)
- Keep the desire to 4 words or fewer
- Works across email, DM, LinkedIn, text

**First-touch variant:**
```
Are you looking to [4 word desire]?
```

**Examples:**
- "Are you still looking to buy your dream home?"
- "Are you still looking to get more sales leads?"
- "Are you still looking to build your personal brand?"
- "Are you looking to grow your newsletter?" (first-touch, no "still")
- "Are you looking to automate your business?"

**Why it works:**
- Binary question. Easy to answer. Low friction.
- "Still" implies they expressed interest before (even if they didn't, it triggers curiosity)
- No pitch. No positioning. No proof. Just a question that either resonates or doesn't.
- Self-qualifies: people who respond are actively seeking that outcome

## 2. "Are You Open?" Cold Email Template

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.6-7

A complete cold email with subject line, body structure, case study proof section, and binary close. The most fully-developed cold email template in the Hormozi system.

**Full template:**

```
Subject: Are you open?

Hi [NAME],

Are you guys open right now?

We specialize in helping [Avatar] get [dream outcome] in under [time period]
without [biggest pain/sacrifice].

We've helped [X] clients in [time period] generate [specific results].
Notable clients include [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3].

We have an irresistible offer right now that I'd love to tell you about.

Do you have 15 minutes for a quick call this week?
[Calendar booking link]

Here are some case studies from clients like you:
- [Case study 1 link]
- [Case study 2 link]
- [Case study 3 link]
- [Case study 4 link]
- [Case study 5 link]
(Include 5-12 case study links)

The way I see it, you have two options:

1. Do nothing, keep doing what you're doing, and keep getting
   the same results.

2. Risk 15 minutes of your time, and potentially [dream outcome].

Just respond "no" if you're not interested and I won't follow up.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Breakdown of each element:**

1. **Subject: "Are you open?"** -- Pattern interrupt. Sounds personal, not salesy. High open rate because it's ambiguous enough to be curious about.

2. **"Are you guys open right now?"** -- The opening line continues the pattern interrupt. Feels conversational, not templated.

3. **Positioning statement** -- One sentence. Avatar + dream outcome + time frame + pain removal. Hits all four levers of the Value Equation in a single line.

4. **Proof** -- Number of clients + results + name drops. Concrete, specific, verifiable.

5. **Irresistible offer mention** -- Doesn't explain the offer. Creates curiosity. The call is where the offer gets presented.

6. **CTA** -- 15 minutes. Low commitment. Calendar link removes friction (no back-and-forth scheduling).

7. **Case studies** -- 5-12 links. Volume of proof overwhelms skepticism. Even if they don't click all of them, the sheer number signals credibility.

8. **Binary close** -- "Do nothing and XYZ" vs. "Risk it and get paid." Forces a mental comparison. Frames inaction as a conscious choice with consequences.

9. **Opt-out** -- "Just respond 'no' if not interested." Reduces anxiety. Also: any response (even "no") boosts deliverability and keeps the thread alive.

## 3. Loom/Video Personalization Cold Email

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, p.10

A cold email that uses a personalized video (Loom-style) as the hook instead of a text pitch. You audit the prospect's website or funnel and record a short video showing them a specific opportunity. The email doesn't sell. The video sells.

**Full template:**

```
Subject: I'm watching you :)

Hi [NAME],

Was checking out your site and noticed a cool opportunity
with your [specific area -- email opt-in, pricing page, funnel, etc.].

Put together a short video to show you exactly what I found
that might [specific outcome -- double your email revenue,
increase your conversion rate, etc.].

[LOOM/VIDEO LINK]

Do you have 15 minutes in the next couple of days?

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Key principles:**
- **No pitch in the email.** The video does all the selling. The email's only job is to get them to click the video.
- **Subject line creates curiosity with humor.** "I'm watching you :)" is playful, slightly edgy, impossible to ignore.
- **Specificity matters.** "Your email opt-in" is better than "your site." The more specific, the more it proves you actually looked.
- **The video should be short (2-3 min).** Show a real opportunity you spotted. Don't pitch your service in the video. Just show them the gap.
- **CTA is soft.** "Do you have 15 minutes?" Not "Book a call now." Conversational, not transactional.

**When to use this vs. "Are You Open?":**
- Use video personalization when the prospect is high-value enough to justify 5-10 minutes of research per email
- Use "Are You Open?" for volume outreach where you can't personalize each one
- Video emails have higher conversion per send but lower volume capacity

## 4. Positive/Negative Reply Handlers

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, p.8

What to do when someone responds to your cold outreach. Two paths: positive (interested) and negative (not interested).

**Positive reply handler:**
```
That's great to hear! I'd love to hop on a quick 3.5-minute call
to see if there's a fit.

Here's my calendar -- grab whatever time works for you:
[Calendar link]

Talk soon,
[YOUR NAME]
```

**Key details on positive handler:**
- "3.5-minute call" -- not 15, not 30. The odd number (3.5) stands out and signals brevity. Nobody says 3.5 minutes unless they actually mean a short call.
- Calendar link immediately. Don't go back and forth on timing.
- "See if there's a fit" -- frames it as mutual evaluation, not a sales pitch.

**Negative reply handler:**
```
Totally understand, [NAME]. Appreciate you letting me know.

If anything changes down the road, feel free to reach out.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

**Key details on negative handler:**
- Gracious exit. No arguing, no "are you sure?", no guilt trip.
- "If anything changes" plants a seed for future re-engagement.
- Preserves the relationship. Today's "no" might be next quarter's "yes."
- Short. Don't over-explain or try to save it.

## 5. BDR Cold Call Script

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.15-18

Full phone script for Business Development Rep cold calls. Uses industry problems as hooks, handles both positive and negative responses, and always ends with a referral ask.

**Full script flow:**

### Step 1: Open with Social Proof
```
"Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with [COMPANY].

We've worked with about [#] of [Avatar -- e.g., gym owners,
SaaS founders, newsletter operators] in the [AREA/INDUSTRY]."
```

### Step 2: Present Industry Problems
```
"And what we've been seeing is that a lot of them are
struggling with [Problem 1] and [Problem 2]."
```

### Step 3: Ask About Their Situation
```
"How has the past year been for you?"
```

### Step 4a: If They Share Pain (Positive Response)
```
"I hear that a lot. That's actually why we built [solution].

We offer a free 30-minute consultation where we show you
how to [specific outcome] in the first [time period]
without [biggest pain/sacrifice].

Would you be open to that?"
```

### Step 4b: If They Say "Fine" or "Good" (No Pain)
```
"That's great to hear. When you say fine, I'm curious --
are you where you want to be with [specific category 1]?
What about [specific category 2]?"
```

Redirect to specific problem categories until you find the pain point. Common categories:
- Lead generation / getting new customers
- Keeping existing customers / retention
- Revenue per customer / margins
- Time management / working too many hours
- Team / hiring / delegation

### Step 5: If Still No Interest
```
"I totally understand. Would it be okay if I sent you some info
and a free training we put together on [topic]?

No obligation at all. Just some stuff our clients have found
really helpful."
```

Then follow up in 3-5 days and re-pitch the consultation.

### Step 6: Always End with Referral Ask
```
"One last thing -- do you know any [Avatar] that would like
to [dream outcome]?

We're looking to help a few more [Avatar] this month."
```

**Key principles:**
- Never open with your pitch. Open with social proof + industry problems.
- "How has the past year been for you?" is an open-ended question that lets them reveal their own pain.
- If they say "fine" -- they're guarded, not pain-free. Redirect to specific categories.
- Free consultation is positioned with outcome + time + no pain (Value Equation).
- The info/training fallback keeps the door open even with a hard "no."
- Referral ask turns every call into a potential lead source, even the ones that say no.

## 6. 8-Day Follow-Up Cadence

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.11-13

A multi-channel follow-up sequence for cold B2B outreach. Alternates between calls, emails, and texts. Each touchpoint escalates urgency while staying helpful.

### Day 1: Call + Email + Text

**Call:** Standard cold call or leave voicemail (see Section 7).

**Email:**
```
Subject: Quick question

Hi [NAME],

I wanted to connect quickly. We help [Avatar] [dream outcome]
in [time period] without [pain].

Do you have a few minutes this week?

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Text (sent after email):**
```
Hi [NAME], I just sent you an email about [topic].
Take a look when you get a chance.
```

### Day 2: Call
Call again. No email, no text. Just the call.

### Day 3: Email (Reply in Same Thread)
```
Subject: RE: Quick question

Friendly follow-up. Checking to see if you still have plans
to [goal/dream outcome]?

[YOUR NAME]
```

Key: Reply in the same thread as Day 1. Don't start a new thread. Same-thread replies have higher open rates and signal persistence without being pushy.

### Day 4: Text + Call
```
Just wanted to bump this. Did you see my email?
```

Then call.

### Days 5-7: Call
Call each day. No emails, no texts. Persistence without being annoying across channels.

### Day 8: Email (Third and Final Email)
```
Subject: RE: Quick question

Hi [NAME],

I know you're busy. I've tried to reach out a few times and
I want to respect your time.

Here's the thing:

Problem: [Hitting goal/dream outcome] takes a TON of time,
especially when you're already stretched thin running [their business].

Solution: 5 minutes. That's all I need to explain our process
and show you how we've helped [#] other [Avatar] [specific result].

If it's not a fit, I'll be the first to tell you.

[Calendar link]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Key principles:**
- Multi-channel (call + email + text) on Day 1 creates maximum initial awareness
- Days 2-7 are mostly calls because calls are harder to ignore than emails
- Day 3 and Day 8 emails reply in the same thread (builds on existing conversation)
- Day 8 reframe: acknowledge they're busy, restate the problem, make the ask tiny ("5 minutes")
- The "I'll be the first to tell you if it's not a fit" line reduces risk and builds trust
- After Day 8, stop. Move to next prospect. Circle back in 30-60 days.

## 7. Voicemail Open Loop

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.23-24

A 15-second voicemail script designed for maximum callback rate using three psychological triggers: brevity, relevance, and an open loop.

**Script:**
```
"Hi [NAME], it's [YOUR NAME]. I'm calling in reference to
[THEIR COMPETITOR or KNOWN CLIENT in their space].
Please give me a call back at [YOUR NUMBER]."
```

**Three rules:**

1. **Don't say "This is [Name]." Say "It's [Name]."**
   - "This is" sounds formal and corporate. It signals sales call.
   - "It's" sounds casual. Like a friend calling. Like someone they should already know.

2. **Don't mention your company.**
   - The moment you say your company name, they categorize you as a salesperson and delete.
   - Leave it out. Let curiosity do the work.

3. **Always follow up.**
   - The voicemail is not the close. It's the door opener.
   - If they call back, you're in a conversation they initiated (massive frame advantage).
   - If they don't call back, follow up per the 8-Day Cadence.

**Why it works:**
- **Short.** Under 15 seconds. No time to overthink or tune out.
- **Relevant.** Their competitor's name or a known client in their industry grabs attention instantly.
- **Open loop.** "In reference to [competitor]" -- what about them? Humans hate unresolved curiosity. They have to call back to close the loop.

**Examples:**
- "Hi Sarah, it's Mike. I'm calling in reference to [Their Direct Competitor]. Give me a call back at 555-0123."
- "Hi David, it's Rachel. Calling in reference to [Well-Known Client You've Worked With]. Call me back at 555-0456."

## 8. Cold B2B Setter Script (Inbound Response Handler)

**Source:** Cold Outreach Downloads, pp.19-22 + Warm Outreach Downloads, pp.1-4 (identical script)

Full phone script for calling back prospects who responded to your cold outreach (email, LinkedIn, DM, etc.). The critical insight: use a pre-meeting video that the prospect "chooses" to watch, creating ownership psychology before the actual sales call.

**Full script flow:**

### Step 1: Reference How They Responded
```
"Hey [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME]. You responded to
[an email / a LinkedIn message / a DM] about [topic].
I wanted to follow up on that."
```

### Step 2: Compliment Their Company
```
"I've actually done some research on [COMPANY NAME].
I'm on your website right now, and I really like
what you guys are doing with [specific thing]."
```

Key: Be specific. "I like your website" is generic. "I like how you structured your pricing page with the comparison table" is specific and proves you actually looked.

### Step 3: Ask Why They Responded
```
"I'm curious -- what made you respond?"
```

This surfaces their motivation in their own words. Whatever they say, acknowledge it and let them keep talking.

### Step 4: Get Current Situation + Goal + Timeline
```
"Tell me a little about where you're at right now with [topic].
Where are you trying to get to?
What's your timeline for making that happen?"
```

### Step 5: Recap and Re-Surface Pain
```
"So if I'm hearing you right, you're at [current state],
you want to get to [goal state], and you'd like to do it
by [timeline]. What made you reach out again?"
```

The "what made you reach out again?" question forces them to re-articulate their pain. Hearing themselves say it twice deepens commitment.

### Step 6: Offer the Video (Ownership Choice)
```
"I'd love to show you exactly how we help [Avatar] do this.
I've got a short video that walks through our process.

Two options: we can watch it together on our next call,
or I can send it to you and you can watch it on your own time.
What works better for you?"
```

Key tactic: Giving them the choice ("watch together or watch alone") creates ownership. They chose to watch it. It wasn't pushed on them. This dramatically increases the chance they actually watch it and show up for the next call.

### Step 7: Book Within 36 Hours
```
"Let's get something on the calendar. Do you have
[specific time] or [specific time] in the next day or two?"
```

TKAD (Time Kills All Deals). Book within 36 hours. Beyond that, motivation fades and no-show rates spike.

### Step 8: Qualify Decision Makers
```
"Is there anyone else on your team who would be beneficial
to have on the call? A business partner, spouse, or
decision maker who'd want to be part of this?"
```

Get all decision makers on the call. Nothing kills deals faster than "I need to talk to my partner first."

### Step 9: Triple Confirmation
```
"Great. Three quick things:

1. Did you get the calendar invite? [Wait for yes]
2. Did you get the video? [Wait for yes]
3. Is there any reason you won't be able to make it? [Wait for no]"
```

Triple confirmation reduces no-shows dramatically. The third question ("any reason you won't make it?") is the most important. By saying "no," they've made a verbal commitment to show up.

## 9. Raw Quotes

> **"TKAD. Time Kills All Deals."**
> -- Cold Outreach Downloads, p.3. Complete the setter process in 48 hours or less. Every hour of delay between first contact and booked call is conversion leaking out of your funnel.

> **"Simple scales. Fancy fails."**
> -- Warm Outreach Downloads, p.10. Keep outreach systems dead simple. The moment you add complexity (fancy automation, multi-step conditional logic, AI-generated personalization at scale), things break. Simple sequences, done consistently, beat sophisticated systems done inconsistently.

> **"Volume negates luck."**
> -- $100M Leads. You don't need the perfect email, the perfect script, or the perfect prospect list. You need enough volume that the math works in your favor. 1,000 cold emails at 1% response rate = 10 conversations. That's not luck. That's arithmetic.

> **"If they have time to be messaging you, they have 5 min to get on a call."**
> -- Cold Outreach Downloads, p.3. Never accept "I'm too busy" from someone who's actively DMing you back and forth. If they can type 15 messages, they can talk for 5 minutes.

> **"Among the first things I do when I invest in a new business."**
> -- Cold Outreach Downloads, p.5 (on the "Are you still looking" opener). This isn't a cute tactic. This is what Hormozi deploys at portfolio companies worth tens of millions. That's how fundamental it is.
