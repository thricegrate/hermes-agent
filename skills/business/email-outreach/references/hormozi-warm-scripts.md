## 1. ACA Framework

**Acknowledge-Compliment-Ask.** Three-step framework for responding to any prospect message in warm DM outreach. Keeps conversations moving forward while building rapport.

### The 3 Steps

1. **Acknowledge** -- Restate what they said in your own words (shows active listening)
   - Example: "Two kids...and you're an accountant..."
2. **Compliment** -- Tie to a positive character trait
   - Example: "Wow! Supermom! So Hardworking!"
3. **Ask** -- Lead the conversation toward your offer with the next question
   - Example: "Do you get time for yourself?" / "Do you have time for workouts?"

### How It Works

Every single response in a DM conversation cycles through this pattern. You acknowledge what they said, compliment something about it, then steer with a question. The question always moves closer to your offer without being salesy.

### Application

- Warm DM outreach
- Customer retention check-ins (see `growth-retention/references/hormozi-retention-checklist.md`)
- Post-purchase relationship building
- Any conversation where you need to build rapport while steering toward a goal

> Also used in retention context: optimal frequency for customer check-ins is every 2-3 weeks using ACA. Counterintuitive finding: wealthier customers want LESS handholding.

## 2. Organic DM Strategy

**A complete system for converting followers/likers who don't take action on your CTAs into paying customers.**

### The Pipeline

1. Lead follows your personal or business account
2. Setter with a trusted profile reaches out via DM
3. DM uses 3-message framework:
   - (a) How you found them
   - (b) Personalized compliment
   - (c) Question
4. Build rapport using ACA Framework (Section 1 above)
5. Use voice memos to break up conversation (pattern interrupt, builds trust)
6. Set up a call: "Do you mind if I give you a quick call? I might have some free resources for you as well."
7. Follow up with: story slide-ups, memes, up arrows (^^)

### Volume Target

**100 reach-outs per day.** This is non-negotiable. Volume negates luck.

### Key Principles

- The setter profile must look like a real person (not a brand account)
- Voice memos are secret weapons -- they break the text pattern and build personal connection
- Never pitch in the DMs. The entire goal is to get them on a call.
- Follow-up is everything. Story slide-ups and memes keep you top of mind without being pushy.

> "Simple scales. Fancy fails." -- Hormozi

## 3. Setter Sequence

**Complete messenger "setter" script for converting DM conversations into phone calls.** The entire point is to get a 10-minute warm-up call.

### Golden Response Rules

Every single message must do two things:
1. Acknowledge/encourage/build rapport
2. Ask the next question in the sequence

### The Full Sequence

**Step 1 -- Open:**
> "Hey Name - what's going on in your world today?"

**Step 2 -- Relate:**
Connect their engagement to your content/page. Relate back to not having time for things.

**Step 3 -- Transition:**
> "What do you do for work?"
> "Does it keep you busy?"
> "Does it make it hard to [do thing]?"

**Step 4 -- Clarify Goal:**
> "What are you trying to do right now [niche]-wise?"

**Step 5 -- Label With Problem:**
> "So you're trying to [restate]. What's been your biggest struggle?"

**Step 6 -- Overview Pain:**
> "What have you tried before? How'd that work for you?"

**Step 7 -- Create Curiosity:**
> "I have a bunch of clients in the same boat. Have some suggestions if you want to hear them."

**Step 8 -- Get on Phone:**
> "It would take 23498 pages to type out haha but whenever we have a minute we can hop on a quick call"

**Step 9 -- TKAD (Time Kills All Deals):**
Complete the entire process in 48 hours or less. Urgency is everything.

> "If they have time to be messaging you, they have 5 min to get on a call." -- Hormozi

## 4. Card-On-File Close

**Full script for booking a free assessment/consultation while capturing a card on file.** Uses discovery questions to dig into pain, then positions the free consultation as exclusive (scarcity), requires card to hold the spot.

### Discovery Sequence: PAIN / DIG / GET REAL

**PAIN:**
- What motivated you?
- Current state? Goal state?
- Which bothers you most?
- How long has this been bothering you?
- What bad habits got you here?

**DIG:**
- What negative impact on your life? (kids, marriage, work, body image, sleep)
- "I need you to be REAL with me"

**GET REAL:**
- Future projection: "Ten years down the road, who are you going to be?"

### The Close

> "Let me see if we have anything available..." (scarcity)
> "I actually have one spot open."

### Card Capture

> "We require a card on file to hold your spot and reserve this discount."

### Post-Booking

Text them immediately:
- Address + parking instructions
- Selfie of who they'll meet (builds familiarity, reduces no-shows)

### Three Overcomes for Card Objection

1. **Hair/nails analogy:** "Just like any service, you put a card down to reserve your spot."
2. **No-show story:** "We had a 10% show rate without it. Now it's over 85%."
3. **Humor:** "Is the fear that I'm going on a shopping spree? Or that you won't show up?"

### Why It Works

Card-on-file eliminates friction for future purchases (like Amazon one-click). Also eliminates no-shows because people show up for things they paid for (85-90%+ show rate). Can charge no-show penalties because card is already captured.

## 5. Referral One-Liner

**A fill-in-the-blank one-liner for generating referrals via any channel.**

### Template

> "I help [ideal customer] get [dream outcome] in [time period] without [effort & sacrifice] and [increased perceived likelihood of achievement]. Does anyone you like come to mind?"

### The Fallback (if they say no)

> "Anyone you hate?"

This disarms with humor and often gets a real referral.

### Works Across All Channels

- Email
- Text
- DM
- Phone calls
- In-person conversations
- Post-purchase follow-up
- End of coaching calls

## 6. Double Dial Technique

**If a prospect doesn't pick up the first call or it goes to voicemail, hang up and call back immediately.**

### Why It Works

1. First call goes to voicemail -- hang up
2. Call back immediately (same number)
3. Second call signals urgency and legitimacy
4. People assume telemarketers don't double-dial
5. Dramatically increases connect rates

### When to Use

- Cold calling campaigns
- Following up on warm leads who responded to outreach
- Any time a first call goes unanswered
- Pair with the Voicemail Open Loop (see `hormozi-cold-scripts.md`) if they still don't pick up

## 7. B2B Setter Script

**Full phone script for calling back prospects who responded to cold outreach (email, LinkedIn, etc).** Key tactic: uses a pre-meeting video that the prospect "chooses" to watch (ownership psychology).

### The Full Script

**Step 1 -- Reference Response:**
> "You responded to [an email/LinkedIn message]"

**Step 2 -- Compliment:**
> "I've done research, I'm on your website right now"

**Step 3 -- Surface Motivation:**
Ask why they responded (surfaces their real motivation)

**Step 4 -- Current Situation:**
Get current situation + goal + timeline

**Step 5 -- Recap + Re-Highlight Pain:**
> "What made you reach out again?" (highlights pain)

**Step 6 -- Video Offer (Ownership Choice):**
> "Two options -- you can watch the video on our next call together, or I can send it to you and you can watch it on your own time."

The prospect takes ownership of their choice. Both options lead to the same outcome.

**Step 7 -- Book Within 36 Hours:**
Never let more than 36 hours pass between this call and the next meeting. TKAD.

**Step 8 -- Qualify Decision Makers:**
> "Is there anyone else who would be beneficial to have on the call?"

**Step 9 -- Triple Confirmation:**
- Calendar invite received?
- Video received?
- Any reason you won't show up?

### Key Insight

The video ownership choice is a psychological master move. When people choose to do something (rather than being told to), they're far more likely to follow through. This single tactic dramatically increases show rates.
