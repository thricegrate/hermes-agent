# Pre-Validated Lead Magnet & Content Idea Mining (Velocity Framework)

**Source:** Daniel Bustamante (Velocity) -- 30+ lead magnets built using this exact process. Core principle: never guess what to create. Mine what already works.

## The Problem

Most creators brainstorm lead magnet ideas from scratch, guess what the audience wants, build it, then hope it converts. This is backwards.

## The Pre-Validation Process

### Step 1: Identify Top Performers
- Use Taplio (or any analytics tool) to sort your posts by views, likes, or comments
- Look at the past 30-90 days
- LinkedIn's native analytics are weak at post level -- use third-party tools
- Sort by COMMENTS first (highest intent signal), then saves, then likes

### Step 2: Filter for Lead Magnet Potential
Not every viral post should become a lead magnet. Three filters:

1. **Tactical/Actionable** -- post teaches something implementable (not opinion, motivation, or hot takes)
2. **Aligned with content pillars** -- fits your core topics (for us: AI automation, newsletter building, email marketing)
3. **Strong engagement** -- especially comments asking for more detail, saves, and "how do I do this?" replies

### Step 3: Brainstorm Expansion
Transform the winning post into a deeper resource:
- Add more specific examples (3-5 real-world cases)
- Write detailed step-by-step explanations
- Create templates, prompts, and checklists
- Build a swipe file of real examples

**Critical rule:** "Value = usefulness." Only expand content that genuinely helps readers implement. Don't pad for length.

## Naming the Lead Magnet

Use the naming formula: **[Desired Outcome] + [Topic] + [Container Word]**

Container words: Kit, Prompt, Blueprint, Template, Playbook, Roadmap, Swipe File, Launchpad, Starter Pack, Generator

**Examples from Velocity:**
- Million-Dollar Sales Page AI Prompt
- The Infinite Newsletter Idea Generator
- The 6-Figure Newsletter Starter Pack

## Content Pillar Mapping

Daniel focuses on three pillars: email marketing, newsletter growth/monetization, funnel building.

For brainstorming, every idea should map to a pillar. If it doesn't fit a pillar, it's not a lead magnet -- it's a one-off post.

## The Series Format (Idea Engine)

"30 Days of [Topic]" format:
- Each day = one piece of content on a specific sub-topic
- Provides creative constraints (no decision fatigue)
- High virality potential (novel format)
- Entire series repurposes into: newsletter issues, lead magnets, paid products
- Example: "30 Days of AI Prompts" became a content library AND multiple lead magnets

## From Idea to Distribution

Once you have a validated idea:
1. Build the lead magnet (expand the post into templates/prompts/checklists)
2. Name it using the formula
3. Create professional mockup (book or box style)
4. Write a 6-part viral giveaway post for LinkedIn distribution
5. Comment-gate the CTA ("Comment [keyword] and I'll DM you")
6. DM landing page link
7. Capture email on landing page
8. Upsell on confirmation page
