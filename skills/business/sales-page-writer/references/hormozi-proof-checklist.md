## Belief Continuum Model

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist Playbook, p.8

Belief is not binary. People don't simply "believe" or "not believe." Belief is a spectrum that runs from 100% certainty on one end to 100% doubt on the other. Every prospect starts somewhere closer to doubt. Every piece of proof on your sales page nudges them toward certainty.

The question is never "do they believe me?" The question is "how much do they believe me?"

**Application to sales pages:**
- Stack proof elements throughout the page, not just in one "testimonials" section
- Each proof piece moves the needle a little further toward certainty
- The closer you get them to absolute certainty, the more compelling each additional proof piece becomes
- A single devastating proof element (huge result, famous endorsement, undeniable data) can move the needle more than 20 weak ones

**The implication:** Your sales page is a belief-building machine. Every section either increases certainty or increases doubt. There is no neutral content.

## Claim Your Proof Model

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist Playbook, p.7

Most marketers build backwards. They make a big claim, then scramble to find proof that supports it. That's the hard way.

The easier, more honest, and more effective approach: collect your data first. Look at what your customers actually achieved. Then make claims that the data already supports.

**The inversion:** "Instead of trying to prove your claim, claim your proof."

If your proof isn't compelling, that's not a marketing problem. That's a reality problem. Fix reality. Improve the product. Get better results. Then claim those results.

**Why this matters for sales pages:**
- Your promise is not a differentiator. Every competitor can make the same promise. Your proof is the differentiator. Nobody else can have the same proof.
- When you claim your proof instead of proving your claims, everything on the page rings true because it IS true
- Prospects can smell the difference between "I have data and I'm showing you" vs. "I made a claim and now I'm cherry-picking evidence"

**Practical steps:**
1. Audit your actual customer results (numbers, timelines, transformations)
2. Identify the most impressive patterns in that data
3. Build your headline claims around the data you already have
4. If the data doesn't support a compelling claim, improve the product until it does

## 80% Winning Ads Discovery

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist Playbook, p.6

After analyzing thousands of ads, Hormozi found that 80% of winning ads shared four traits. They were not what most marketers expect:

1. Had no educational stuff
2. Were not focused on the offer
3. Didn't have the founder center-stage
4. Told one story: **"This. Product. Works."**

**The difference between top performers and bottom performers was one thing: proof.**

**Application to sales pages:**
- The hero of your sales page should be the proof, not you, not the features, not the curriculum
- Educational content on a sales page is a crutch. If you're teaching on the sales page, you don't have enough proof
- Stop explaining what the product does. Show that it works.
- The founder story has a place (credibility, relatability), but the proof is the star
- Every section should ladder back to "this product works, and here's the evidence"

## Expanded 13-Point Proof Checklist

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist Playbook, pp.7-19

This is the complete framework for evaluating and upgrading proof quality on any sales page. Each point includes the principle, why it matters, and specific "Make It Happen" implementation actions.

### 1. In-Person > Virtual

Proof that happens in front of someone's eyes beats proof delivered through a screen. Physical presence creates trust that digital cannot replicate.

**Make It Happen:**
- Clean pavement in front of prospects (literal or metaphorical: show up where they are)
- Fly your best customers in for events where new prospects can see them
- If you run live events, capture proof in that environment
- In-person proof on video is still better than purely virtual proof

### 2. Live > Recorded

A live testimonial has more weight than a pre-recorded one. The audience knows a live person can't be edited, scripted, or faked.

**Make It Happen:**
- Have customers lined up live during pitches, webinars, or presentations
- Bring customers on stage at events to share their experience in real time
- During live webinars, have past customers ready in the chat or on camera
- Even a live Zoom testimonial beats a polished pre-recorded video

### 3. Raw > Processed

An unedited iPhone video of someone sharing their result is more believable than a polished production. Less tampering equals more authentic. The rougher it looks, the more real it feels.

**Make It Happen:**
- Give customers simple templates and prompts to record their own testimonials
- Offer incentives (discounts, bonuses, recognition) for sending in raw video
- Build testimonial prompting into customer journey milestones (after first win, after 30 days, after big result)
- Resist the urge to over-produce. A shaky iPhone video with genuine emotion outsells a studio shoot every time

### 4. Show > Tell

A video showing someone's hair transformation is more powerful than describing how their hair changed. Visible evidence beats verbal claims.

**Make It Happen:**
- Collect before/after photos and videos as standard practice
- Unboxing videos work because they show exactly what the customer gets
- Record client experiences at breakthrough moments (the "aha" moment, the first sale, the milestone hit)
- Ask customers to document their results visually, not just describe them
- Give incentives if needed to get them to capture the moment
- Screenshots of dashboards, bank accounts, analytics, results are "show" proof

### 5. Other People Saying > You Saying

No one cares what YOU think about your product. A stranger's opinion carries more weight than anything you could write. Third-party endorsement is always stronger than self-promotion.

**Make It Happen:**
- Put a review/testimonial request step directly into your customer journey (automated email at key milestones)
- Ask for reviews on external platforms (Google, Trustpilot, G2) not just your own site
- Make it easy: give them a direct link, a template, specific questions to answer
- The volume of "other people saying" matters as much as the quality

### 6. Identical to Them > Opposite of Them

Testimonials work best when the prospect sees someone who looks like them, talks like them, and had the same problem. People trust people who are similar to themselves.

**Make It Happen:**
- Collect testimonials from all ages, genders, ethnicities, experience levels, and industries
- Show the widest possible range of customer types on your sales page
- Female business owners featured = more female buyers. The math is straightforward.
- Include as many demographic variations as possible
- Segment testimonials if needed: "See results from people in YOUR industry"

### 7. Personal > Generic

"I was broke. I got rich." is boring and forgettable. "I was lying awake at 3am doing math on whether I could afford both groceries and my kid's field trip" is unforgettable. Specificity creates believability.

**Make It Happen:**
- In testimonial interviews, ask probing questions: "Why was this so important to you?" "What specific things happened?" "What was the worst moment?"
- Get customers to describe both pre-transformation pain AND post-transformation relief in specific, visceral detail
- The more sensory and emotional the detail, the more powerful the proof
- Generic: "Great program, highly recommend." Personal: "I was sleeping in my car between Uber shifts. Four months later I signed my first $5K client."

### 8. Big Results > Small Results > No Results

Bigger results are more compelling. Period. A customer who made $100K is better proof than one who made $1K. One standout testimonial can outperform all others combined.

**Make It Happen:**
- Reach out to your longest-tenure and biggest-success customers specifically
- Pay money and invest time to capture the BIG results properly (fly them in, hire a crew, whatever it takes)
- Create a "hall of fame" featuring your top performers
- One jaw-dropping result is worth more than 50 mediocre ones
- Ask your best customers: "Would you be willing to share your story?"

### 9. Newer Proof > Older Proof

Constantly update your proof. If your best testimonial is years old, prospects assume you haven't had good results since. Old proof signals stagnation.

**Make It Happen:**
- Set a recurring process to collect fresh testimonials (monthly or quarterly)
- Date-stamp testimonials when possible ("Just hit $20K/mo -- March 2026")
- Rotate old testimonials off the page and replace with recent ones
- "Last month alone, 47 customers..." is more persuasive than "Over the past 3 years..."
- Fresh proof signals momentum. Old proof signals decline.

### 10. More Proof > Less Proof

Conquer with quantity. One testimonial is a data point. A hundred testimonials is a pattern. A thousand testimonials is undeniable.

**Make It Happen:**
- Build "floor-to-ceiling" proof on your sales page: a wall of results that feels overwhelming
- Create a gallery with hundreds or thousands of reviews
- One page with 5,000+ testimonials works. Place a CTA every 3-5 testimonials so they can buy whenever they're convinced
- Screenshot walls, review compilations, result galleries
- The sheer volume of proof is itself persuasive
- Pro tip for local businesses: print 5-star reviews, frame them, line your lobby floor-to-ceiling

### 11. Third Party Verification > Zero Verification

Accreditations, industry awards, certifications, and reviews on independent platforms carry weight that self-reported claims never will. A trusted third party vouching for you removes the "of course they'd say that" objection.

**Make It Happen:**
- Display Amazon reviews on your non-Amazon page
- Apply for industry awards (even if it feels silly). Winning or being nominated is proof
- Show accreditations, certifications, and professional memberships
- Feature logos of publications that have mentioned you
- Brand endorsements and partnerships strengthen proof
- Independent review site ratings (Trustpilot, G2, Capterra) embedded on your page

### 12. Proof With Numbers > Proof Without Numbers

Hard to argue with clear data. "Our customers love us" is weak. "94.2% of customers report positive ROI within 60 days" is strong. Numbers create specificity, and specificity creates believability.

**Make It Happen:**
- Collect data at regular intervals: sign-up, onboarding, 30 days, 60 days, 90 days
- If your data isn't compelling, fix the product (see "Claim Your Proof" above)
- Use charts and graphs to visualize results
- Match claims to proof: Claim = "We help people start businesses." Proof = "54.1% of our communities generate revenue within 90 days."
- Specific numbers beat round numbers: "$12,847" is more believable than "$13,000"
- Collect milestone data: completion rates, average results, time-to-first-win

### 13. Metaphors > Technical Jargon

If you sell something complex, don't explain it technically. Compare it to something familiar. A good metaphor makes the unfamiliar feel intuitive and the complex feel simple.

**Make It Happen:**
- "Ads are like working for commissions." -- Instantly understood.
- "SEO is more like an investment account." -- No technical explanation needed.
- Come up with 2-3 metaphors for your most complex product features
- Teach metaphors to your sales staff so everyone uses the same language
- Good metaphors are incredibly persuasive because they bypass the analytical brain and land in the intuitive brain
- Test metaphors in conversation first, then use the ones that get instant "oh, I get it" reactions

## CTA Formula (Show + Tell)

**Source:** $100M Goated Ads Playbook, pp.20-24

A strong call-to-action has five elements. Most CTAs only hit one or two. Hit all five and demonstrate visually what happens when they act. Clear beats clever. Always.

### The Five Elements

1. **What to do:** "Take advantage of this offer by..."
2. **How to do it:** "...tapping the button below" / "...clicking the green button on this page"
3. **When to do it:** "...before enrollment closes Friday" / "...before it expires"
4. **What they get for doing it:** "...you'll get instant access to the full system plus $1,000 in bonuses"
5. **What happens next:** "...delivered straight to your inbox within 60 seconds"

### The Visual Demonstration Layer

Don't just tell them what to do. Show them. On video: screen-record the click, the checkout, the confirmation page. On a sales page: use arrows, highlighted buttons, and visual cues that make the action physically obvious.

**Application to sales pages:**
- Every CTA button on the page should answer at least 3 of the 5 elements
- Below the button, a single line can handle elements 4 and 5 ("You'll get instant access. First lesson hits your inbox in 60 seconds.")
- Place CTAs every 3-5 proof elements (see Point 10 above) so the prospect can act the moment they're convinced
- Never assume they know what to do. Spell it out.

## Six-Point Testimonial Script

**Source:** $100M Marketing Machine, pp.15-16

A structured framework for capturing testimonials that hit all emotional and logical proof points. Use this for customer interviews, bonus unlocks, testimonial competitions, and event capture. The most important part is the hook: have the customer say a few different hooks before diving into the story.

### The Six Points

**1. Internal Struggle**
- "What was rock bottom before doing this?"
- "What did you have to do that you hated?"
- "What things did you want to do that you couldn't?"

**2. External Struggle**
- "What were your objective metrics?" (revenue, weight, score, subscribers, whatever the measurable thing is)
- Get the hard numbers of where they started

**3. Skepticism**
- "What were your main concerns about buying/clicking/joining?"
- This is gold because it mirrors the prospect's current objections

**4. Overcome**
- "What made you do it anyway?"
- The answer to this question answers the prospect's unspoken objection

**5. External Victory**
- "What are your objective metrics now?"
- Hard numbers of where they are after using the product

**6. Internal Victory**
- "What's been the best moment since?"
- "What can you do now that you couldn't do before?"
- "What things did you hate doing that you don't have to do anymore?"

### Why This Structure Works on Sales Pages

The six points map perfectly to the prospect's mental journey:

- Points 1-2 (Internal/External Struggle): "That person was exactly where I am"
- Point 3 (Skepticism): "They had the same concerns I have right now"
- Point 4 (Overcome): "Here's why they decided to act despite those concerns"
- Points 5-6 (External/Internal Victory): "Look what happened. That could be me."

**Capture tips:**
- Record multiple hooks before the story (have them say it 3-4 different ways)
- Let them ramble in the Internal Struggle section. The gold is in the details.
- For External Struggle and Victory, insist on actual numbers
- Raw iPhone quality. Don't over-produce.

## Proof Implementation Checklist

An operational checklist for implementing the Hormozi proof framework on any sales page. Work through this systematically before launching or revising a page.

### Audit Phase
- [ ] List every claim made on the page
- [ ] For each claim, identify what proof currently supports it
- [ ] Score each claim's proof against the 13-point checklist (which points does it hit?)
- [ ] Identify claims with zero proof -- these are liabilities
- [ ] Identify your single strongest proof element. Is it prominently placed?

### Collection Phase
- [ ] Set up automated testimonial request at key customer milestones
- [ ] Create a testimonial template using the Six-Point Script
- [ ] Offer incentives for raw video testimonials (discount, bonus, recognition)
- [ ] Reach out to top 10 customers for detailed case studies
- [ ] Collect numerical data: completion rates, average results, time-to-first-win
- [ ] Gather third-party proof: awards, certifications, review site ratings, press mentions
- [ ] Capture before/after screenshots, photos, or videos from customers
- [ ] Build a "proof bank" (folder/database) for ongoing storage

### Page Construction Phase
- [ ] Lead with proof, not promises (80% Winning Ads principle)
- [ ] Place strongest proof element above the fold or immediately after the headline
- [ ] Include testimonials from people who look like the target customer (Point 6)
- [ ] Ensure testimonials include specific, personal detail (Point 7)
- [ ] Feature your biggest results prominently (Point 8)
- [ ] Date-stamp proof and prioritize recent results (Point 9)
- [ ] Build a "wall of proof" section with volume (Point 10)
- [ ] Include third-party verification: logos, awards, review site embeds (Point 11)
- [ ] Add numerical proof: charts, specific percentages, milestone data (Point 12)
- [ ] Replace jargon with metaphors throughout (Point 13)
- [ ] Place CTA buttons every 3-5 proof elements
- [ ] Every CTA hits at least 3 of the 5 CTA Formula elements

### Ongoing Maintenance
- [ ] Monthly: collect new testimonials, retire oldest ones
- [ ] Quarterly: update numerical data and claims to match current proof
- [ ] After every big customer win: capture it immediately ("Proof is like an animal in the wild. You have to capture it.")
- [ ] Review page against 13-point checklist every 90 days
