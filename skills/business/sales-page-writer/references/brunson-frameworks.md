# Russell Brunson Sales Page Frameworks

Brunson-specific persuasion structures for sales pages, webinar closes, and offer presentations. These complement (not replace) the Kennedy offer stack in `kennedy-sales-letter.md` and the conversion psychology in `conversion-psychology.md`.

---

## 1. Stack Slide Methodology

The Stack Slide is how you present the offer so the total value feels undeniable before the price ever appears. Different from a simple value stack -- the key is CUMULATIVE display.

### How It Works

Every time you introduce a new offer element, you re-show the entire stack up to that point. The buyer sees the pile growing in real time.

### Step-by-Step Execution

1. **Introduce Element 1** (the core offer)
   - Name it. One-sentence description. Assign a dollar value.
   - Show it on the slide/page alone.

2. **Introduce Element 2** (first bonus or module)
   - Name it. One-sentence description. Assign a dollar value.
   - Show Elements 1 + 2 together. Running total visible.

3. **Introduce Element 3** (second bonus)
   - Name it. One-sentence description. Assign a dollar value.
   - Show Elements 1 + 2 + 3 together. Running total visible.

4. **Repeat for every element** (typically 5-8 total)
   - Each time, the full stack re-appears with the new addition highlighted.
   - Running total keeps climbing.

5. **Big Stack Slide** (the complete offer)
   - Show ALL elements together one final time.
   - Display the total value prominently: "Total Value: $X,XXX"
   - Let it sit. Do NOT reveal the price yet.

6. **Price Reveal Sequence**
   - Anchor at approximately 2x the real price: "If I charged $X, that would be fair, right?"
   - Pause. Let the anchor settle.
   - Reveal the real price: "But you're not going to pay $X. Your investment today is just $Y."
   - Justify per-day cost: "That's less than $Z per day."

### The "If All" Anchoring Technique

After the Big Stack Slide but before the price reveal, run 3-4 "If All" statements:

- "If all this did was [benefit #1], would it be worth [price]?"
- "If all this did was [benefit #2], would it be worth [price]?"
- "If all this did was [benefit #3], would it be worth [price]?"
- "If all this did was [benefit #4], would it be worth [price]?"

Each statement isolates one benefit and makes the price feel justified by that benefit ALONE. By the time you've done 3-4, the buyer believes the offer is worth multiples of the price.

### Checklist: Stack Slide on a Sales Page

- [ ] Each offer element has its own named section with a dollar value
- [ ] Elements are introduced one at a time (not dumped in a list)
- [ ] After each new element, the running total is visible
- [ ] The Big Stack shows all elements together before the price
- [ ] 3-4 "If All" statements appear between the stack and the price
- [ ] Price is anchored at 2x before the real price reveal
- [ ] Per-day or per-use cost is calculated after the price

### Sales Page Implementation

On a written sales page (not a live webinar), the Stack Slide translates to:

- **Curriculum section**: Introduce each module with a value line
- **Bonus section**: Each bonus gets its own mini-block (name, description, value)
- **Value summary block**: A visual box showing all components + total value
- **"If All" block**: 3-4 standalone lines, each bold, each its own paragraph
- **Price reveal block**: Anchored price, crossed out, real price below it

## 2. Star, Story, Solution (SSS) Script

A 30-step sales letter framework. Use this as a structural spine for long-form sales pages, VSL scripts, and webinar pitches.

### Opening (Steps 1-4): Hook and Promise

- [ ] **Step 1: Pattern Interrupt Headline** -- Break whatever the reader expects. A question, a bold claim, a contradiction. Not "How To [Outcome]." Something that stops the scroll.
- [ ] **Step 2: Core Desire Statement** -- Name the one thing they want most. "What if you could [dream outcome] in [timeframe]?"
- [ ] **Step 3: Agitate Past Failures** -- List the things they've already tried that didn't work. "You've tried [X]. You've tried [Y]. None of it stuck." Make them feel understood.
- [ ] **Step 4: One Big Promise** -- The single transformation this offer delivers. One sentence. "By the end of this, you'll have [specific measurable outcome]."

### Story (Steps 5-11): The Attractive Character Arc

- [ ] **Step 5: Introduce the Attractive Character** -- Who are you? Not a resume. A relatable human with the same problem they have. "Two years ago, I was [in their exact situation]."
- [ ] **Step 6: High Drama Moment** -- The lowest point. The moment that forced change. Paint it vividly. Specific details make it real.
- [ ] **Step 7: Backstory Wall** -- What prevented you from solving it earlier? What did you believe that was wrong? This sets up the epiphany.
- [ ] **Step 8: Epiphany** -- The single insight that changed everything. Not a gradual realization. A lightning bolt. "Then I realized [the one thing]."
- [ ] **Step 9: Path to the Solution** -- What you did after the epiphany. The steps you took. Keep it brief -- this is a bridge, not the curriculum.
- [ ] **Step 10: First Success** -- The first result. Specific. Measurable. "Within [timeframe], I [specific result]."
- [ ] **Step 11: "This Changed Everything"** -- Expand from the first success to the bigger transformation. Show the compound effect.

### Authority (Steps 12-17): Build the Case

- [ ] **Step 12: The Conspiracy** -- "It's not your fault." Identify the external force that kept them stuck. The industry. The gurus. The algorithm. The outdated advice.
- [ ] **Step 13: The Big Lie** -- Name the specific belief they held that was wrong. "You've been told [common advice]. That's the lie."
- [ ] **Step 14: The Common Enemy** -- Give the lie a face. Who benefits from them staying stuck? This creates us-vs-them bonding.
- [ ] **Step 15: Rapid Growth Proof** -- Show your results scaling. Screenshots, numbers, timeline. Compress months into a paragraph.
- [ ] **Step 16: Case Studies** -- 2-3 other people who used this method and got results. Different situations, same method, similar outcomes.
- [ ] **Step 17: Hidden Benefits** -- Benefits they didn't expect. "Not only did [primary result], but I also [unexpected benefit]."

### Close (Steps 18-30): The Offer and Conversion

- [ ] **Step 18: Formal Product Introduction** -- "That's why I created [Product Name]." First time you name the product. Everything before this was the story.
- [ ] **Step 19: Creation Effort** -- How much work went into building this. "It took [X months/years] and [investment] to develop."
- [ ] **Step 20: Ease of Use** -- "But you don't have to go through all that. I've packaged it so you can [shortcut]."
- [ ] **Step 21: Social Proof Dump** -- Testimonials, screenshots, results. Stack them. 3-5 minimum.
- [ ] **Step 22: Value Stack** -- Use the Stack Slide Methodology (see Section 1 above).
- [ ] **Step 23: Price Anchor** -- Anchor high before revealing the real price.
- [ ] **Step 24: "If All" Statements** -- 3-4 "If all this did was..." lines.
- [ ] **Step 25: Price Reveal** -- Real price. Per-day breakdown.
- [ ] **Step 26: Guarantee** -- State it clearly and confidently.
- [ ] **Step 27: Scarcity/Urgency** -- Real deadline or limited quantity.
- [ ] **Step 28: Future Pacing** -- "Imagine 90 days from now. You wake up and [describe their new reality]."
- [ ] **Step 29: Call to Action** -- Direct. "Click the button below."
- [ ] **Step 30: Warning/Takeaway Close** -- "If you leave this page, [what they lose]." Or: "This isn't for everyone. If you're not ready to [commit], that's okay. But if you are..."

### Mapping SSS to Sales Page Sections

| SSS Steps | Sales Page Section |
|---|---|
| 1-4 (Opening) | Headline + Problem Agitation |
| 5-11 (Story) | Solution Reveal (with origin story) |
| 12-17 (Authority) | Social Proof + Who This Is For |
| 18-21 (Product Intro) | What's Inside / Curriculum |
| 22-25 (Value + Price) | Bonus Stack + Final CTA |
| 26-27 (Guarantee + Scarcity) | Guarantee + Urgency |
| 28-30 (Future Pace + Close) | Final CTA + P.S. |

## 3. The 16 Mini Closes

Micro-persuasion lines you weave throughout the sales page. Not saved for the end. Dropped into the copy at natural transition points between sections.

### Belief-Shifting Closes (Use in Problem Agitation + Solution Reveal)

1. **Money Replenishes Close**: "Money comes back every two weeks. Time is gone forever. What's the cost of waiting another 6 months?"

2. **Break Old Habits Close**: "If you keep doing what you've been doing, you'll keep getting what you've been getting. Something has to change."

3. **Information Alone Close**: "You don't need more information. You need a guide. A system. Someone who's already walked the path."

4. **I Had Two Choices Close**: "I had two choices: keep struggling on my own, or invest in a shortcut. I chose the shortcut. That decision changed everything."

### Identity Closes (Use in Who This Is For)

5. **Us vs. Them Close**: "There are two types of people. Doers and dabblers. Doers invest in themselves and take action. Dabblers bookmark things and never start. Which one are you?"

6. **Their Two Choices Close**: "You have two options right now. Option 1: Close this page and try to figure it out alone. Option 2: Let me hand you the exact system and save yourself [time/money/pain]."

7. **Money or Excuses Close**: "You can make money or you can make excuses. You can't make both."

8. **Reluctant Hero Close**: "I didn't plan to teach this. I built it for myself. But when people kept asking how I did it, I couldn't say no."

### Value Closes (Use in Bonus Stack + Before Price Reveal)

9. **If You Only Got Close**: "If the only thing you got was [one specific component], that alone would be worth [price]. But you're getting everything."

10. **Say Goodbye Close**: "Say goodbye to [pain point #1]. Say goodbye to [pain point #2]. Say goodbye to [pain point #3]. This is where that ends."

### Trial Closes (Weave Throughout -- Every 2-3 Sections)

Trial closes are micro-agreements. They keep the reader nodding along. Drop one after every major claim or story beat.

11. **"Am I right?"** -- After describing a frustration they share.
12. **"Isn't that cool?"** -- After revealing a feature or result.
13. **"Can you see yourself [doing X]?"** -- After future pacing.
14. **"Would you agree that...?"** -- After stating a principle.
15. **"Makes sense, right?"** -- After explaining the mechanism.
16. **"Sound familiar?"** -- After describing their current situation.

### Mini Close Placement Map

| Page Section | Best Mini Closes |
|---|---|
| Problem Agitation | Money Replenishes, Break Old Habits, Sound Familiar? |
| Solution Reveal | Information Alone, I Had Two Choices, Am I Right? |
| Curriculum / What's Inside | Isn't That Cool?, Can You See Yourself? |
| Who This Is For | Us vs. Them, Their Two Choices, Money or Excuses |
| Bonus Stack | If You Only Got, Would You Agree? |
| Final CTA | Say Goodbye, Reluctant Hero, Makes Sense Right? |

## 4. Three False Beliefs Framework

Every prospect has three categories of false beliefs that prevent them from buying. Each needs its own Epiphany Bridge story to break it.

### The Three Beliefs

**1. Vehicle Belief** (about the method/product)
- "This type of solution doesn't work."
- "I've tried something like this before and it failed."
- "This sounds too good to be true."

Maps to: **Solution Reveal section** of the sales page. The origin story and epiphany break this belief.

**2. Internal Belief** (about themselves)
- "I'm not smart/technical/experienced enough."
- "I don't have time."
- "I'm too old / too young / too [whatever]."

Maps to: **Who This Is For section.** Case studies of people like them break this belief. "If [person in their situation] did it, so can you."

**3. External Belief** (about outside forces)
- "My market is too competitive."
- "The economy is bad."
- "My spouse/boss/situation won't allow it."

Maps to: **FAQ / Objection Handling section.** Direct answers to the external excuses break this belief.

### Epiphany Bridge Story Structure (for each belief)

Each false belief gets its own mini-story:

1. **State the false belief** -- "You might be thinking [false belief]."
2. **Acknowledge it** -- "I get it. I thought that too." (or cite someone who did)
3. **Tell the backstory** -- Brief context of who held this belief and why it made sense.
4. **The epiphany moment** -- The specific event or realization that broke the belief.
5. **The new belief** -- What replaced the old one. State it directly.
6. **Proof** -- Result that came from the new belief.

### Checklist: Three False Beliefs on a Sales Page

- [ ] Vehicle belief identified and addressed in Solution Reveal
- [ ] Vehicle belief has an Epiphany Bridge story (yours or a case study)
- [ ] Internal belief identified and addressed in Who This Is For
- [ ] Internal belief has a case study of someone like them succeeding
- [ ] External belief identified and addressed in FAQ section
- [ ] External belief has a direct, no-excuse answer with proof
- [ ] All three beliefs are addressed BEFORE the price reveal
- [ ] No false belief is left unbroken by the time the reader hits the CTA

### Common False Beliefs by Market

**Make money / business:**
- Vehicle: "Courses don't work." / "AI is just hype."
- Internal: "I'm not technical." / "I don't have an audience."
- External: "The market is saturated." / "I don't have startup capital."

**Health / fitness:**
- Vehicle: "Diets don't work long-term."
- Internal: "I have no willpower."
- External: "I don't have time to cook / exercise."

**Skills / education:**
- Vehicle: "Online courses are a waste of money."
- Internal: "I'm not a fast learner."
- External: "My job doesn't give me time to study."

## Quick Reference: When to Use Each Framework

| Situation | Primary Framework |
|---|---|
| Writing a full sales page from scratch | SSS Script (30 steps) as the structural spine |
| Building the offer/pricing section | Stack Slide Methodology |
| Sales page feels flat or passive | Add Mini Closes throughout |
| Prospects say "sounds great but..." | Identify and break the Three False Beliefs |
| Webinar or VSL pitch section | Stack Slide + Mini Closes for the close |
| Page converts visitors but not at price reveal | Strengthen "If All" anchoring + per-day cost |
