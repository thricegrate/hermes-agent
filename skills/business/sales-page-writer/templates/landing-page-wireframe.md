# Landing Page Wireframe Template

Fillable wireframe for standard 6-section landing pages, lead magnet opt-in pages, and FAQ sections. Each section includes copy prompts, word count guides, and placeholder structure.

Use this template to plan the page structure before writing full copy. Fill in the prompts, then expand into complete copy using `SKILL.md` and the supporting references.

## FAQ Section Template: Objection-Based Structure

Use this structure for any FAQ section on any page. 8 question types organized by the objection each resolves.

*Instructions: Adapt each question to your specific offer. Do not use generic questions ("What is your return policy?") unless they are genuinely among the most common concerns buyers have.*

---

**TYPE 1: Fit and Readiness**
*Resolves: "I'm not sure this is for someone like me."*

Q: "Is this right for me if I'm [specific starting situation, e.g., a complete beginner / don't have a big audience / already tried X]?"
A: [State clearly who this is for and who it is not for. If there is an "even if" qualifier, state it here.]

---

**TYPE 2: Results Timeline**
*Resolves: "I don't know if this will actually work or how long it will take."*

Q: "How long until I see [specific result]?"
A: [Give a realistic, specific timeline with the condition: "Most [audience] see [result] within [timeframe] when they [do X]."]

---

**TYPE 3: Differentiation**
*Resolves: "I've tried things like this before and they didn't work."*

Q: "How is this different from [specific competitor / free option / previous solution they know]?"
A: [Name the specific operational difference. Not "we're better" -- explain the mechanism that makes the outcome different.]

---

**TYPE 4: Risk and Guarantee**
*Resolves: "What if I spend the money and it doesn't work?"*

Q: "What if it doesn't work for me? Can I get my money back?"
A: [Restate the guarantee in full: what triggers it, the timeframe, and exactly how to claim it. Make it sound easy.]

---

**TYPE 5: Access and Delivery**
*Resolves: "I'm not sure what I'm actually getting or how it works."*

Q: "How do I access [the product]? How is it delivered?"
A: [Clear, specific, step by step. Remove all ambiguity about what happens after they buy.]

---

**TYPE 6: Time Commitment**
*Resolves: "I'm busy and I don't want to commit to something I won't finish."*

Q: "How much time do I need each week to get results from this?"
A: [Honest specific number. "Most [audience] spend [X hours] per week and see [result] in [timeframe]."]

---

**TYPE 7: Offer Clarity**
*Resolves: "I'm not completely sure what I'm buying."*

Q: "What exactly do I get when I buy?"
A: [Complete itemized list of every deliverable. Main product, bonuses, access duration, support included.]

---

**TYPE 8: Specific Situation (Custom)**
*Resolves: The most common objection specific to your offer that is not covered above.*

Q: "[Most common question from your actual prospects that is not covered by the 7 types above]"
A: [Direct answer.]

## CTA Button Copy: 10 Templates with Context

Match the template to the situation. The right CTA for a free opt-in is wrong for a $500 course.

---

**1. "Get Instant Access"**
Context: Paid digital products ($7-500). Emphasizes speed and removes the "how long until I can use it?" question. Works for both impulse and deliberate purchases.

---

**2. "Yes, I Want [Specific Outcome]"**
Context: Any price point. First-person "Yes" creates pre-commitment. Specific outcome in the button makes it feel like a personal decision, not a brand command. Strong for lead magnets and mid-ticket.

---

**3. "Send Me the [Specific Name]"**
Context: Lead magnets and free opt-ins. Customer-voice phrasing. The visitor is "requesting" the free resource -- not "submitting" a form. Reduces friction at the opt-in moment.

---

**4. "Start [Achieving Outcome] Today"**
Context: Mid-ticket courses and programs ($97-500). "Today" adds immediacy. "[Achieving Outcome]" names the transformation they are buying. Works well when the outcome is active (losing weight, getting clients, building a skill).

---

**5. "Claim My [Bonus/Spot/Discount] Now"**
Context: When there is genuine scarcity or an expiring bonus stack. "Claim" implies something is being taken -- ownership framing. Only use "Now" if there is a real deadline.

---

**6. "Join [Number]+ [Audience] Inside"**
Context: Community products, courses with an active student body, or any offer where belonging is part of the value. Triggers social proof at the CTA. Only use if the number is real and substantial.

---

**7. "Unlock [Product Name]"**
Context: Tools, platforms, and access-based products. "Unlock" implies exclusive access being granted. Effective when the product has a VIP or members-only framing.

---

**8. "Book My Free [Outcome] Call"**
Context: High-ticket discovery calls and strategy sessions. Names the call as a "free [outcome]" call, not a "sales call." Frames the CTA as a service, not a commitment to buy.

---

**9. "Download the Free [Specific Name]"**
Context: Lead magnet PDFs, checklists, and guides. "Download" is clear and expected. Specificity of the name ("Download the Free Newsletter Monetization Checklist") performs better than "Download Now."

---

**10. "Show Me How It Works"**
Context: Secondary CTAs for visitors who are not yet ready to buy but are interested. Leads to a demo, explainer video, or how-it-works section. Converts curious visitors without forcing a purchase decision.

---

*Final note on CTA selection: When in doubt, test the outcome-focused version against the action-focused version. "Get Instant Access" vs. "Yes, I Want to [Outcome]." The winner depends on where the visitor is in the awareness journey. Product-aware visitors respond to access. Transformation-seeking visitors respond to outcome. Run the test and let the data decide.*
