# Sales Page Writer

## Prerequisites

- Offer details (from `product-offer` or user-provided): price, bonuses with dollar values, value proposition, guarantee type
- Product curriculum overview (from `product-architect`)
- Target customer profile and primary problem (from `niche-finder`)
- Optional: OTO brief (from `product-offer`), testimonials, social proof, credentials
- Reference: `references/conversion-psychology.md` for awareness levels and persuasion frameworks

## Workflow

### Kennedy Offer Stack Checklist (MANDATORY before writing)

Before writing the sales page, verify all offer elements are present. If any are missing, flag them and resolve before proceeding.

1. Core product clearly defined in one sentence
2. Value stacked before price reveal (comparison, ROI, or minimization)
3. 2-3 bonuses, each solving a remaining objection
4. Real deadline with specific date (not "limited time")
5. Fast-action incentive (disappearing bonus or early-bird price)
6. Late-response penalty (price increase after deadline)
7. Guarantee prominently displayed (not fine print)
8. Visual "thud factor" (mockups, screenshots, stacked component visuals)

See `references/kennedy-sales-letter.md` for the full 28-step sales letter structure, irresistible offer stack, risk reversal strategies, scarcity mechanics, and premium pricing rules.

### Step 1: Gather Core Messaging Inputs

Before writing, confirm these elements:
- **Primary problem** the product solves
- **Desirable outcome** (the transformation)
- **Target customer** (who is this for, specifically?)
- **Price** and bonus stack
- **Credibility markers** (results, testimonials, credentials, audience size)

### Step 2: Write the Sales Page Sections

Write each section in order. The page follows a proven conversion structure:

#### Section 1: Headline + Subheadline
- **Headline:** Lead with the outcome or the pain point
- **Subheadline:** Expand on who it's for and what they'll achieve
- Keep it under 15 words for the headline

**Patterns that work:**
- Outcome-first: "Learn to [desirable outcome] in [timeframe]"
- Problem-first: "Stop [painful problem] and start [desirable outcome]"
- Curiosity: "The [framework/method] for [outcome] (without [common objection])"

#### Section 2: Problem Agitation
- Describe the painful current state (2-3 paragraphs)
- Use "you" language: "You've tried X, but..."
- List 3-5 specific frustrations they experience
- Make them feel SEEN (they should think "this is exactly me")

#### Section 3: Solution Reveal
- Introduce the product as the answer
- Name it, describe it in one sentence
- Explain WHY this approach works (the unique mechanism)
- Transition from pain to possibility

#### Section 4: What's Inside (Curriculum Overview)
- List each module with a clear benefit statement
- Format: "Module [N]: [Name] - [What you'll learn to DO]"
- Don't list every lesson - keep it scannable
- Focus on outcomes per module, not features

#### Section 5: Bonus Stack
- List each bonus with its dollar value
- For each bonus: name, what it is (1 sentence), why it matters (1 sentence)
- Show the math: total value vs. actual price
- For Brunson's Stack Slide methodology (cumulative display, "If All" anchoring, price reveal sequence), see `references/brunson-frameworks.md`.

#### Section 6: Who This Is For / Not For
- "This is for you if..." (3-5 bullet points)
- "This is NOT for you if..." (2-3 bullet points)
- Qualifies the buyer and reduces refund risk

#### Section 7: Social Proof
- Testimonials (with name, context, and specific result)
- Screenshots of results or feedback
- Numbers (students served, results achieved)
- If no testimonials yet: use personal results, credentials, or "as seen in" logos

#### Section 8: FAQ / Objection Handling
- 5-7 most common objections as questions
- Answer each directly and concisely
- Map objections to THIS specific offer (don't use generic objections)
- Common objections: "Is this for beginners?", "How long do I have access?", "What if it doesn't work for me?", "How is this different from [free content]?"

#### Section 8b: Guarantee
- State the guarantee type clearly (from `product-offer`)
- For unconditional: "30-day money-back guarantee. If you're not satisfied, email us for a full refund. No questions asked."
- For conditional: "Complete [specific actions] within [timeframe]. If you don't see [specific result], we'll refund 100%."
- For performance-based: "[Specific measurable outcome] or your money back."
- The guarantee should feel confident, not desperate. Frame as: "We're so confident you'll get results that..."

#### Section 9: Final CTA + Checkout
- Restate the offer (product + bonuses + price)
- Restate the guarantee
- Urgency or scarcity if applicable (but never fake)
- Clear call to action button copy
- For Brunson's 16 Mini Closes and Three False Beliefs framework, see `references/brunson-frameworks.md`.

### Step 3: Write the Thank You Page Copy

Short page shown after purchase:
- Congratulate them
- Tell them to check their email inbox
- Set expectations for what happens next (welcome email, Skool invite)
- 3-5 sentences max

### Step 3b: Write OTO Page Copy (If Applicable)

If the funnel includes one-time offers (from `product-offer` OTO brief), write urgency-driven OTO pages. Each OTO page follows a stripped-down structure:

**OTO Page Structure:**
1. **Headline**: "Wait! Your Order Is Not Complete" or "Special One-Time Offer"
2. **Hook** (2-3 sentences): Connect to what they just purchased. "Since you just got [frontend], you're going to need [this] to [specific benefit]."
3. **What it is** (3-5 bullets): Quick description of the OTO deliverables
4. **Why now** (1 paragraph): "This offer is only available right now, on this page. Once you leave, it's gone forever."
5. **Price**: State clearly with anchoring ("normally $[higher price], yours today for just $[OTO price]")
6. **CTA Button**: "Yes! Add This To My Order for Just $[Price]"
7. **Decline Link**: "No thanks, I'll figure it out on my own" (text link, not button)

**OTO copy rules:**
- One offer per page. No navigation, no menu, no distractions.
- Copy is shorter than a full sales page (500-800 words max).
- Urgency must feel authentic. The offer genuinely is only available on this page.
- The decline option must always be visible. Never hide it.

### Step 3c: Write Call Booking Page Copy (If Applicable)

If the funnel includes a high-ticket backend with sales calls (from `high-ticket-closer`), write the call booking page and confirmation page copy.

**Call Booking Page Structure:**
1. **Headline**: Frame as strategy session, not sales call. "Book Your Free [Strategy/Growth/Discovery] Call"
2. **Subheadline**: "In [X minutes], I'll show you exactly how to [outcome] without [thing they want to avoid]"
3. **Who this is for / not for**: 3-4 qualifiers and 2-3 disqualifiers
4. **What happens on the call**: Set expectations (3 bullet points: diagnose, map path, determine fit)
5. **Social proof**: 3-5 client results with specific numbers
6. **Application form**: 5-8 qualifying questions (see `high-ticket-closer/templates/call-booking-page.md`)
7. **Calendar embed**: Integration point for scheduling tool

**Call Confirmation Page Structure:**
1. **Confirmation headline**: "You're Booked!"
2. **Next steps**: What to prepare for the call
3. **Pre-call content**: Video or article to consume before the call
4. **Reminder opt-in**: SMS reminder signup

### Step 4: Output the Complete Copy Document

Structure the output as clearly labeled sections ready to hand off to `design-page`:

```
# [Product Name] - Sales Page Copy

## HERO SECTION
Headline: [X]
Subheadline: [X]
CTA Button: [X]

## PROBLEM SECTION
[Full copy]

## SOLUTION SECTION
[Full copy]

## CURRICULUM SECTION
[Module list with benefits]

## BONUS SECTION
[Bonus stack with values]

## WHO THIS IS FOR
[For/Not for lists]

## SOCIAL PROOF
[Testimonials and proof]

## FAQ
[Q&A pairs]

## FINAL CTA
[Closing copy + offer summary]

---

# Thank You Page Copy
[Post-purchase page text]
```

## Writing Style

- Conversational but confident
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Use bold for key phrases
- Break up text with subheadings
- Speak to ONE person, not a crowd
- Benefits over features, always

## Hormozi Proof & Persuasion Frameworks

Deep proof and conversion frameworks from $100M Proof Checklist and Closing Playbooks. See `references/hormozi-proof-checklist.md` for full detail.

### Frameworks Available:

| Framework | What It Does | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **13-Point Proof Checklist (Expanded)** | 13 proof types ranked by persuasive power with "Make It Happen" actions | Auditing/strengthening proof on any sales page |
| **Belief Continuum** | Belief as spectrum (not binary) -- proof nudges the needle | Understanding how much proof is needed at each funnel stage |
| **Claim Your Proof** | Inversion: collect data first, then claim what data supports | When claims outpace available proof |
| **CTA Formula** | 5-element show+tell CTA structure | Writing CTAs that convert |

### Integration with Existing Steps:

- **Section 7 (Social Proof):** Use the 13-Point Proof Checklist to audit proof quality. Prioritize: in-person > live > raw > show > other people > identical to them > personal > big results > newer > more > third party > numbers > metaphors.
- **Section 8 (FAQ):** Use Belief Continuum to map where prospects are on doubt-certainty spectrum. Each FAQ answer should move the needle.
- **Section 9 (Final CTA):** Apply CTA Formula (5 elements) for the closing call to action.

**Key insight:** "80% of winning ads share four traits: no educational stuff, not focused on the offer, no founder center-stage. They tell one story: This. Product. Works." Apply this to social proof sections.

> "Everyone can make the same promise, but no one can have the same proof."
