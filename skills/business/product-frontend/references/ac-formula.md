# The Automatic Clients Formula

Based on "Automatic Clients" by Alen Sultanic & Robert Neckelius.
Core methodology for acquiring hundreds of low-ticket buyers daily and
converting them to $5,000-$35,000+ high-ticket clients.

---

## The 3-Step AC Model

### Step 1: Build a Break-Even Sales Funnel

Sell a low-dollar product (typically $5-47) that allows you to acquire hundreds
of buyers from any ad network on any given day. The funnel includes:
- A compelling frontend product (book, toolkit, masterclass, etc.)
- One-time offers (OTO 1 + OTO 2) that recover ad spend
- Automated delivery (100% digital, zero ongoing fulfillment)

**The math**: Frontend sale + OTOs = Ad spend to acquire that buyer.
Result: Every customer is free to acquire. Backend revenue is pure profit.

### Step 2: Bombard Buyers with Value (Value Bombs + Awareness Bridge)

After purchase, engineer the buyer's journey through increasing awareness:
- Deliver 3+ "value bombs" (high-value content that proves your method works)
- Move them through the "awareness bridge" (strategic content series)
- Each piece increases their belief in your method and desire for your help

**The goal**: By the time they book a call, they are already pre-sold on your
method and pre-qualified as a serious buyer.

### Step 3: Convert to High-Ticket Clients

Convert the most engaged buyers to high-ticket backend offers ($3K-$35K+):
- Call booking page with qualification criteria
- Consultative sales call (diagnose, prescribe, close)
- Backend delivery (coaching, DFY service, mastermind, course + community)

**The goal**: 3-8% of frontend buyers eventually ascend to the backend.

## The ECA Method (Educate-Convert-Ascend)

The ECA method governs every piece of the AC model:

### Educate
- The frontend product educates the buyer on your method/approach
- Value bombs educate on specific breakthroughs and possibilities
- The awareness bridge educates on the system and results
- Education = proof of competence = trust building

### Convert
- The frontend purchase converts a stranger into a customer (conversion event #1)
- OTOs convert a customer into a committed buyer (conversion event #2)
- The awareness bridge converts a buyer into a qualified prospect (conversion event #3)

### Ascend
- The ascension email sequence moves buyers toward the backend
- The sales call converts qualified prospects into high-ticket clients
- Ongoing delivery creates clients for life (referrals, renewals, expansion)

## The 3 Levels of Customers

Every person in your audience falls into one of three levels:

### Level 1: DIY (Do It Yourself)
- **Profile**: Has time, limited budget, wants to learn and implement
- **What they need**: Information, templates, frameworks
- **What they buy**: Frontend products ($5-97)
- **Your relationship**: Content consumer, email subscriber, potential future upgrader
- **Revenue**: Low per person, high volume

### Level 2: DWY (Done With You)
- **Profile**: Has some time and budget, wants guidance and accountability
- **What they need**: Coaching, mentorship, community, structured programs
- **What they buy**: Group coaching, masterminds, courses with support ($997-$10K)
- **Your relationship**: Student, member, coaching client
- **Revenue**: Medium per person, medium volume

### Level 3: DFY (Done For You)
- **Profile**: Has budget, limited time, wants results without the learning curve
- **What they need**: Someone to execute on their behalf
- **What they buy**: Agency services, 1:1 implementation, managed services ($5K-$35K+)
- **Your relationship**: Premium client
- **Revenue**: High per person, low volume

### How This Maps to the AC Model:
- **Frontend product** serves Level 1 customers and identifies Level 2/3 prospects
- **Backend group program** serves Level 2 customers
- **Backend DFY service** serves Level 3 customers
- The frontend is profitable at Level 1 (break-even on ad spend) and wildly
  profitable at Levels 2-3 (backend revenue)

## Conversion Velocity

The speed at which a prospect moves through your funnel determines your cash flow
and ability to scale. Faster conversion = more revenue per unit of time = more
ad budget = more growth.

### Factors That Increase Velocity:
1. **Low frontend price** ($5-27 = impulse buy, no decision fatigue)
2. **OTOs presented immediately** (capitalize on buying momentum)
3. **Fast product delivery** (digital, instant access)
4. **Value bombs within 48 hours** (keep engagement high post-purchase)
5. **Awareness bridge starts Day 1** (don't let the relationship cool)
6. **Pre-call education** (more content consumed before the call = faster close)

### Factors That Decrease Velocity:
1. High frontend price (requires more consideration)
2. Slow fulfillment (physical products, delayed access)
3. No post-purchase engagement (buyer forgets about you)
4. Long gaps between touchpoints
5. Weak value bombs (don't prove your competence)
6. No call booking CTA (buyers want to buy but don't know how)

## Compete on Economics, Not Marketing

The AC model's key advantage: you do not need better ads, better copy, or better
creative than your competitors. You need better ECONOMICS.

### The Math:
- **Competitor A**: Sells a $997 course via webinar. CPA to acquire a webinar
  attendee is $50. 5% close rate = 1 in 20 buy = $997/20 = **$49.85 revenue per lead**.
  They can afford $49.85 per lead.

- **You (AC model)**: Sell a $5.60 book. CPA is $6. Break even on book + OTOs.
  3% of buyers ascend to $10,000 backend. For every 100 buyers: $560 in frontend
  revenue + 3 x $10,000 = $30,000 in backend = **$30,560 per 100 buyers = $305.60 per buyer**.
  You can afford $305.60 per buyer.

- **Result**: You can outspend Competitor A by 6x on advertising. Over time,
  you dominate the ad network because you can afford to pay more per impression
  than anyone else in your market.

### Why This Matters:
- The ad networks (Facebook, Google, YouTube) are the "great filters" of online business
- Whoever can afford to pay the most per customer acquisition WINS
- This is not about clever ad hacks. It is about business model design.
- The backend is what makes the economics work. Without a high-ticket backend,
  the book funnel is just a book business.
