# Create The LinkedIn Post For A Viral Giveaway Lead Magnet

Write a viral giveaway LinkedIn post that generates hundreds of high-quality leads from a single post, using a proven 6-element framework engineered for maximum engagement and DM opt-ins.

---

## Why This Works

A Viral Giveaway LinkedIn post offers a free valuable lead magnet in exchange for engagement (comments). When someone comments, you DM them a link to a landing page where they opt-in. This framework has been used to generate 1,500+ sign-ups from single posts. The 6 elements work together to create a curiosity gap, build desire, establish trust, and make the CTA irresistible.

---

## How to Use This Skill

When triggered, ask the user for:

> To write your viral giveaway LinkedIn post, I need:
>
> 1. **Lead magnet name**: What's the free resource called?
> 2. **What's inside**: What does the lead magnet include? (templates, frameworks, examples, etc.)
> 3. **Target audience**: Who is this for?
> 4. **Big outcome/promise**: What problem does it solve or result does it deliver?
> 5. **Credibility/social proof** (optional): Any results, numbers, or proof that backs up the promise?
> 6. **Comment trigger word** (optional): What should people comment to get it? (e.g., "LEADS", "SEND", etc.)

---

## The 6-Element Viral Giveaway Framework

### Element 1: The Hook
**Goal:** Create a curiosity gap that makes people click "see more."

**Execution rules:**
- Use 2 short, punchy single-sentence paragraphs with a line break between them, OR a 2-3 line paragraph under 140 characters
- The last sentence of the hook should always end with ":"
- Hooks with big numbers or money figures tend to outperform
- Must fit above the "see more" fold on mobile

### Element 2: The Big Promise
**Goal:** Briefly explain the big outcome or problem you'll help them solve with the lead magnet. The more specific, the better.

**Execution rules:**
- Use short sentences and paragraphs
- Avoid paragraphs longer than 3 lines on mobile
- Use bulleted lists or "…" to break up sentences
- If you can show social proof while explaining the outcome, even better

### Element 3: The Reason Why You're Sharing It
**Goal:** Explain why you're giving this away for free. Research shows that explaining the reason behind a request multiplies the likelihood of compliance.

**Execution rules:**
- Keep it brief and authentic (1-3 short paragraphs)
- Common reasons: "I created this for a paid product but decided to share it," "I found this so helpful that I wanted to pass it along," "I spent X hours building this and want to help others"

### Element 4: What's Inside
**Goal:** Break down exactly what they're getting: make the value tangible and specific.

**Execution rules:**
- Use a bulleted list (3-6 items)
- Each bullet should highlight a specific component or benefit
- Be specific: "5 email templates" beats "email templates"
- If applicable, assign value estimates

### Element 5: The CTA (Call to Action)
**Goal:** Tell people exactly what to do to get the lead magnet.

**Execution rules:**
- Make it dead simple: "Comment [WORD] below"
- Optionally add: "Like this post" (boosts algorithmic reach)
- Keep the comment trigger to one simple word
- Add "I'll DM you" so they know what to expect

### Element 6: The PS
**Goal:** Catch anyone who scrolled to the bottom and add a secondary CTA or connection request.

**Execution rules:**
- "PS - If we're not connected yet, please send me a connection request so I can DM you"
- Optionally add a PPS with a secondary offer or newsletter link
- Keep it 1-2 sentences max

## Output Format

Deliver the complete LinkedIn post as a single block of text, formatted for LinkedIn (short paragraphs, line breaks, bullets where appropriate). Include:

1. The full post copy with all 6 elements
2. A brief note on the recommended comment trigger word
3. Suggested DM template for fulfillment (2-3 sentences to send after they comment)

---

## LinkedIn Formatting Guidelines

- Maximum 3 lines per paragraph (as viewed on mobile)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use "•" for bullet points
- Strategic use of line breaks for readability
- No hashtags in the body (optional 3-5 at the very end)
- Emojis: use sparingly (0-3 max)

---

## Quality Criteria

The post must:
- **Stop the scroll**: Hook must create genuine curiosity
- **Be specific**: Vague promises don't convert; exact numbers and deliverables do
- **Feel authentic**: Not salesy or desperate; like sharing something genuinely valuable
- **Have a frictionless CTA**: One comment word, that's it
- **Be mobile-optimized**: Short paragraphs, scannable format
- **Be ready to publish**: Copy/paste directly into LinkedIn
