# Priestley Scorecard Framework

Daniel Priestley's 3-phase, 20-question quiz architecture. Co-founder of ScoreApp, used across 150+ industries. The core insight: it's a lead qualification system disguised as a quiz people actually get value from.

---

## When to Use This Framework

**Green lights:**
- Service business, coaching, consulting, or B2B offer
- Multi-tier product ladder (free content to done-for-you)
- Lead qualification matters as much as conversion (sales team needs to know who's worth a call)
- Audience willing to invest 3 minutes for personalized results
- High lifetime value per customer justifies longer quiz

**Red flags:**
- E-commerce with physical products (use Product Match quiz instead)
- Single product at a single price (no routing needed)
- Younger/casual demographics who won't tolerate 20 questions
- Low-ticket impulse purchases where quiz friction kills momentum

## Phase 1: Contact-First Capture (Questions 1-4)

Most quiz builders put contact info at the end. Priestley puts it first.

The logic is simple: if someone drops off at question 8, you've got nothing with end-capture. With contact-first, even a 50% completion rate still gives you the lead.

**Q1: Name**
First name minimum. Frame it as personalization: "First, what should we call you?"

**Q2: Email**
Frame as delivery mechanism: "Where should we send your personalized scorecard?"

**Q3: Phone (optional)**
Mark clearly as optional. Frame as premium access: "Want a personalized follow-up if your score qualifies? Drop your number (totally optional)."

**Q4: Location**
Capture automatically via IP when possible. Only ask explicitly if location matters for the offer (local services, timezone-specific programs).

### Contact-First vs End-Capture: When to Use Which

| Factor | Contact-First | End-Capture |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Lead value | High (service/coaching, $1K+ LTV) | Lower (e-commerce, impulse) |
| Partial completion value | High (sales team can follow up) | Low (no context without answers) |
| Quiz start friction | Higher (asking for info upfront) | Lower (jump straight into questions) |
| Total leads generated | More (captures partials) | Fewer (only completions) |
| Lead quality signal | Weaker on partials | Stronger (completed = committed) |

**Rule of thumb:** If a partial lead is worth following up on, capture contact first. If you only want people who complete the whole thing, capture at the end.

## Phase 2: Best Practices Scoring (Questions 5-14)

10 yes/no questions about whether the prospect is doing the things they should be doing. Binary format keeps momentum high and friction low.

These questions serve two purposes simultaneously:
1. **Generate a score** (e.g., 7 out of 10 = 70/100) that becomes the centerpiece of the results page
2. **Reveal specific gaps** -- every "No" answer tells you exactly where they're struggling, which informs your follow-up

### How to Write Best Practices Questions

Each question follows the pattern: "Are you currently [doing this best practice]?"

The best practices should be things your offer addresses. Every "No" is a gap your product/service fills.

## Phase 3: The Big 5 Qualification Questions (Questions 15-19)

This is where the real qualification happens. Five specific question types that tell you everything you need to route someone to the right offer -- without feeling like an interrogation.

### Q15: Current Situation

"Which best describes your current situation?"

Give 4-5 options that map to different starting points and experience levels. This tells you where they are right now.

**Example (business coaching):**
- Just getting started (pre-revenue)
- Early stage ($0-$5K/month)
- Growing ($5K-$25K/month)
- Scaling ($25K-$100K/month)
- Established ($100K+/month)

**Example (fitness):**
- Complete beginner
- Used to be active, fell off
- Workout occasionally but no structure
- Consistent but plateaued
- Experienced, looking for next level

### Q16: Desired Outcome in 90 Days

"What would you most like to achieve in the next 90 days?"

The 90-day anchor is intentional. It's specific enough to reveal urgency and ambition, short enough to feel actionable.

Give 3-5 specific outcomes to choose from.

**Example (business coaching):**
- Land my first 3 paying clients
- Hit $10K/month consistently
- Build a team so I'm not doing everything myself
- Launch a new revenue stream
- Systemize my business so it runs without me

### Q17: Obstacles / What Hasn't Worked

"What's the biggest thing holding you back? Or what have you tried that hasn't worked?"

This question does double duty:
1. Reveals their biggest pain point
2. Tells you what solutions they've already tried (so you know what objections to address)

Give 3-4 common obstacles to choose from.

**Example (business coaching):**
- I don't know where to start -- too many options
- I've tried courses/programs but couldn't implement
- I can't afford to invest right now
- I don't have enough time -- too busy with my day job

### Q18: Solution Preference

"Which type of support would suit you best?"

This is the most strategically powerful question. It reveals their budget without asking directly. Someone who picks "done-for-you" signals a completely different budget than someone who picks "self-paced learning."

Options should map to your actual offer tiers:

**Example:**
- Free resources and self-guided learning -> Low-qualified, nurture with content
- Online course or training program ($50-200 range signal) -> Mid-qualified, course/workshop CTA
- Group coaching or community ($100-500/month signal) -> Mid-high qualified, membership/cohort CTA
- 1-on-1 coaching or mentorship ($500-5K/month signal) -> High-qualified, call CTA
- Done-for-you service ($2K-15K+ signal) -> Highest-qualified, sales call CTA

Only include options that match offers you actually sell. Don't list "done-for-you" if you don't offer it.

### Q19: Anything Else?

"Is there anything else we should know about your situation?"

Open text box. Optional.

Most people skip it. But some reveal gold: "I have budget but need to spend it by end of month," "My boss asked me to find a solution this week," "I've been following you for 2 years."

Always include it. The upside is too high for zero effort.

## The Results Page

After all 20 questions, the prospect gets a customized results page with three components:

### 1. Their Overall Score

Display prominently. "Your Business Health Score: 65 out of 100."

People love knowing where they stand. The score itself is the value exchange -- it's what makes the quiz feel worthwhile rather than like a lead form.

### 2. Three Personalized Insights

Based on their specific gaps (the "No" answers from Phase 2) and their goals/obstacles (from Phase 3):

- "Your biggest opportunity is in [gap area] -- this is where most people at your stage see the fastest improvement"
- "Based on your goal of [Q16 answer], the critical next step is [specific action]"
- "You mentioned [Q17 answer] as your main obstacle. Here's why that happens and what to do about it"

These insights should feel genuinely useful, not just a sales pitch dressed as advice.

### 3. Tiered Next Steps (CTA Routing)

Route to different CTAs based on qualification signals from Phase 3:

| Qualification Level | Signals | CTA | Why |
|---------------------|---------|-----|-----|
| High | Q18 = coaching/DFY + Q16 = ambitious goal + Q17 = tried things that didn't work | Book a call | Ready to invest, clear pain, proven intent |
| Mid | Q18 = course/community + Q16 = clear goal + Q15 = early/growing stage | Register for webinar/workshop | Needs education + social proof before buying |
| Low | Q18 = free resources + Q17 = can't afford / no time + Q15 = just starting | Download free guide/resource | Not ready to buy, nurture with value |

The prospect doesn't see the qualification logic. They see a personalized next step that feels tailored to their situation.

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Scorecard Quiz | Standard 5-7 Question Quiz |
|--------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Page-to-full-completion | 20-40% | 24-45% (start x completion) |
| Time to complete | ~3 minutes | ~90 seconds |
| Contact capture on partials | 60-80% (captured upfront) | 0% (captured at end) |
| Total leads per 1,000 visitors | 200-400 complete + 200-400 partial | 250-450 complete only |
| Data points per lead | 20 (rich qualification) | 5-7 (basic segmentation) |
| Lead quality signal | High (qualification built in) | Moderate (segmentation only) |

The scorecard generates fewer completions but more total leads (counting partials) and dramatically richer data per lead. For high-ticket offers where lead quality matters more than volume, this is the better trade.

## Email Follow-Up by Qualification Tier

### High-Qualified Leads (Book a Call CTA)

If they booked: confirmation + prep email + reminder sequence.
If they didn't book:
- Email 1 (Day 0): Full scorecard results + "Based on your score, you'd benefit most from a conversation about [specific gap]"
- Email 2 (Day 2): Case study from someone with a similar score who booked a call and got results
- Email 3 (Day 4): "Your scorecard showed [specific gaps]. Here's what we'd cover in a call"
- Email 4 (Day 7): Last chance + urgency

### Mid-Qualified Leads (Webinar/Workshop CTA)

- Email 1 (Day 0): Scorecard results + webinar invite with specific angle tied to their gaps
- Email 2 (Day 2): Content piece addressing their Q17 obstacle
- Email 3 (Day 4): Webinar reminder + what they'll learn
- Email 4 (Day 7): Replay or next session if they missed it
- Email 5 (Day 14): Upgrade path -- "Ready for more hands-on support?"

### Low-Qualified Leads (Free Resource CTA)

- Email 1 (Day 0): Scorecard results + free resource delivery
- Email 2 (Day 3): Quick win related to their lowest-scoring area
- Email 3 (Day 7): Story of someone who started where they are
- Email 4 (Day 14): Re-engagement quiz or poll
- Email 5 (Day 30): Re-qualification offer ("Want to retake the scorecard and see your progress?")

## Integration with the product Product Ladder

The scorecard framework maps directly to a multi-tier product ladder:

| Q18 Answer | Maps To | Price Signal |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| Free resources | Freemium tool / lead magnet | $0 |
| Course/training | Skill Pack or Playbook | $27-197 |
| Group coaching/community | Membership or Cohort | $47-97/mo or $2.5-3.5K |
| 1-on-1 coaching | High-touch program | $2.5-5K |
| Done-for-you | DFY service | $5-15K |

The quiz doesn't just generate leads -- it pre-sorts them into the right tier of your product ladder before you ever talk to them.
