## Personalization Techniques

Personalized emails drive 760% more revenue than generic blasts (CampaignMonitor). The key: personalize beyond the name across subject lines, CTAs, body content, and timing.

### Subject Line Personalization

**Basic (First Name):**
Lifts open rates 26-81%. Most effective in plain-text and conversational contexts. Use sparingly (not every email) to maintain impact.

**Behavioral (Beyond First Name):**
- **Purchase history**: "Based on your recent purchase of [product]..."
- **Browse behavior**: "Still thinking about [product name]?"
- **Engagement-based**: "You opened 3 of our last 5 emails. This one's for you."
- **Stated interest**: "Your [interest area] guide is ready"

**Segment-Specific:**
- High-value VIPs: "You've earned this. Exclusive VIP access."
- At-risk: "We don't want to lose you. Here's why."
- One-time buyers: "Score a free [product] with your next order"
- New subscribers: "[First Name], here's exactly what to do first"

### CTA Personalization (202% More Conversions)

Personalized CTAs convert 202% better than generic ones (HubSpot). This is the highest-ROI personalization technique.

| Context | Generic CTA | Personalized CTA |
|---|---|---|
| User name | "Claim your offer!" | "Claim your special offer, [Name]!" |
| Purchase history | "Buy more!" | "Loved [Previous Purchase]? Try this!" |
| Browse behavior | "Check our collection!" | "Get back to your wishlist, [Name]!" |
| Content interest | "Learn more!" | "Dive deeper into [Topic], [Name]!" |
| Abandoned cart | "Complete your purchase!" | "[Name], forgot something?" |
| Previous interaction | "Welcome back!" | "Welcome back, [Name]! Check what's new." |

### Body & P.S. Personalization

- **Dynamic content blocks**: Show different content to different segments (beginners vs. advanced, by interest area)
- **P.S. section**: Personalize by engagement level or interest area (most-read section after subject line)
- **Sender identity**: Emails from a person outperform emails from a brand name in the "From" field
- **Personalized attachments**: Custom PDFs, video thumbnails, or resources based on reader profile

### Merge Tag Best Practices

- Always set fallbacks: `{{first_name | "there"}}` -- never send bare `{{first_name}}`
- Test with empty data fields before launching any sequence
- Common fallbacks: "there", "friend", "fellow [audience term]"

### Don't Be Creepy

Effective personalization makes readers feel valued, not watched.
- Good: "Since you downloaded our guide on [topic]..." (provides context)
- Bad: "We noticed you visited our pricing page 3 times" (surveillance vibes)
- Never reference tracking data without a natural reason
- Always provide clear opt-out and respect privacy (CAN-SPAM, GDPR)

### Segmentation Dimensions for Personalization

- **Demographics**: Age, role, location, company size
- **Behavior**: Opens, clicks, purchases, browse history, feature usage
- **Psychographics**: Goals, values, interests, self-identified category (from surveys/quizzes)
- **Customer journey**: New subscriber, trial, active, at-risk, churned
- **Engagement level**: Power readers, occasional, inactive (30+ days)

## A/B Testing Methodology

### Priority Order (highest leverage first)
1. Subject line
2. Sender name
3. Preview text
4. Send time
5. CTA copy
6. Email length
7. Format (text-only vs. HTML)

### How to Run Clean Tests
- One variable at a time
- 1,000+ recipients per variant for statistical significance
- 72 hours for open rate tests; full purchase cycle for conversion
- Declare winner at 5%+ lift for opens, 10%+ for conversion

### Interpreting Results
- Higher opens, same CTR = subject line win, body was fine
- Same opens, higher CTR = body/CTA win, keep subject
- Higher opens, lower CTR = subject over-promised, mismatch

## Spam Trigger Words & Deliverability (2026 Guide)

160 billion spam emails are sent daily. Spam filters use content scanning, sender reputation, engagement signals, blacklists, and authentication checks. A single bad word won't kill you, but stacking multiple triggers will.

### The 7 Spam Word Categories

**1. Money & Financial** (highest risk)
$$$, 100% free, earn cash, earn extra income, double your wealth, make money, cash bonus, free money, instant earnings, easy income, billion dollars, get out of debt, save big money, credit card offers, why pay more

**2. Scam & Too-Good-to-Be-True**
100% guaranteed, guaranteed results, no catch, no obligation, no strings attached, once in a lifetime, risk-free, pure profit, double your money, potential earnings, will not believe, can't miss

**3. Marketing Overpromises**
#1, 100% satisfied, amazing deal, be amazed, best offer, big bucks, drastically reduced, extra cash, fantastic deal, incredible deal, join millions, lowest price, satisfaction guaranteed, unbeatable offer, unbelievable, unlimited, you will not believe your eyes

**4. Urgency & Pressure Tactics**
Act now!, buy now, call now!, click here, click below, deal ending soon, don't hesitate, don't waste time, expires today, final call, hurry up, immediately, limited time, order now, supplies are limited, take action now, this won't last, urgent, while supplies last

**5. Health & Pharma**
Miracle cure, lose weight fast, no prescription needed, secret formula, scientifically proven, guaranteed weight loss, anti-aging, natural remedy

**6. Tech & Security** (looks like phishing)
Verify identity, confirm your details, password reset, account update, action required, click to verify, immediate action required, last warning, security breach

**7. Gambling & Blacklisted**
Casino, jackpot, lottery winner, free spins, spin to win, bet now, click to win, XXX

### Subject Line Spam Rules

- **No ALL CAPS words** in subject lines. "HUGE SALE" = spam. Sentence case always.
- **No excessive punctuation** -- !!! or ??? or $$$. One exclamation mark max, and only rarely.
- **"Free" is context-dependent**: "FREE GIFT ACT NOW" = spam. "Your free template is ready" = fine. Natural context wins.
- **Emojis**: 1 emoji max per subject line. Place at the end, not beginning. Avoid flagged emojis (💰🔞🏆🎰). Our pirate flag should rotate (not every email).
- **Avoid these words in subject lines**: unlock, discover, revolutionary, game-changing, don't miss, act now, click here, buy now, guaranteed, risk-free, limited time, urgent, congratulations, winner, selected, exclusive deal
- **Safe replacements**: "FREE" -> "your free template" or "complimentary guide." "Act now" -> natural CTA in body. "Guaranteed" -> share proof instead. "Click here" -> descriptive link text.

### Email Body Spam Rules

- **Link count**: 2-3 links per email is safe. Our "link diet" goal (1 primary CTA + 3 secondary max) aligns perfectly with spam filter best practices.
- **No URL shorteners** (bit.ly, tinyurl) -- commonly used in phishing. Use clean, full HTTPS links.
- **Text-to-image ratio**: At least 60% text, 40% images. Image-only emails get flagged.
- **No deceptive anchor text** -- display URL must match actual link destination.
- **Unsubscribe link**: Always visible. Required by CAN-SPAM/GDPR. Missing = spam report instead of unsub.

### Technical Deliverability Checklist
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured and passing
- Bounce rate below 2%
- Spam complaint rate below 0.1%
- Only opted-in subscribers
- Unsubscribe link visible in every email
- No purchased or scraped lists
- Clean sending domain reputation (check with Google Postmaster Tools)

### The Reply Tactic
Ask welcome email subscribers to reply with a keyword. Trains inbox placement algorithms that your emails belong in primary inbox. Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo all use engagement signals to decide inbox vs spam.

### Free Spam-Check Tools
- **Mail Tester** (mail-tester.com): Full spam analysis including domain auth and content
- **GlockApps**: Tests inbox placement across Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo
- **SpamAssassin**: Open-source spam filter scoring
- Run subject lines through a spam checker before A/B testing

## Preview Text Optimization

### What Not to Do
- Leave blank (defaults to "View this email in your browser")
- Repeat the subject line
- Start with "Hi [First Name],"

### Formulas That Work
- **Extend the hook**: "...and we're not bringing it back."
- **Micro-incentive**: "...plus the free template inside."
- **Tease the best part**: "The one change that adds 20-30% more opens."
- **Call-to-open**: "Open to see what you left behind."

### Character Limits
- Ideal: 40-90 characters
- Maximum before truncation: 90-100 characters
- Write as if only first 60 characters will be seen

## Cold Email Subject Lines (Different Game)

Newsletter subject lines and cold email subject lines follow **opposite rules**. Newsletter subjects need hooks and curiosity gaps because your reader opted in and expects content. Cold email subjects need to look like internal messages because the prospect doesn't know you and will delete anything that looks like sales.

### The "Boring = Better" Principle

The highest-performing cold email subject lines are deliberately mundane:

- **"Quick question"** - Most-used cold email subject globally. Works at scale through ambiguity.
- **"{{Company}}?"** - Company name creates instant relevance. Question mark adds curiosity.
- **"Do you do outreach?"** - Qualifying question the right prospect can't resist answering.
- **"Hey" / "yooo"** - Ultra-casual pattern interrupts. High risk, high reward.
- **"Followup"** - Implies prior relationship. Use only in positions 2-5 of sequences.

### Why This Contradicts Newsletter Advice

- Newsletter: "5 dead-simple steps to automating your weekly report" (hook-driven, specific)
- Cold email: "Quick question" (ambiguous, internal-looking)

Both work because the contexts are different. Newsletter readers chose to be there. Cold prospects are filtering for spam. The cold email subject's only job is to not get deleted.

### When Cold Email Patterns Cross Over

The underlying principle transfers to newsletters in one case: **subject lines that feel like personal messages outperform subject lines that feel like marketing.** Test ultra-simple subjects like "this is wild" or "question for you" alongside your hook-driven subjects.
