# Story Brief Templates

Fillable planning documents for any story format. Complete the brief before writing. A 5-minute brief produces a 50% better story than starting from a blank document.

---

## How to Use These Templates

1. Pick the template that matches your format
2. Fill in every field -- do not skip fields you find difficult (those are usually the story's weakest points)
3. Write the story from the completed brief, not from the blank page
4. Apply narrative devices from `references/narrative-devices.md` during execution

---

**STAKES:**

_______________

**TURNING POINT:**
What specific insight, decision, or action changed things?

_______________

**TRANSFORMATION / RESULT:**
What is the specific "after" state? (Quantify wherever possible)

Before: _______________
After: _______________
Timeframe: _______________

**LESSON:**
In one sentence, what should the reader understand or believe after reading this story?

_______________

**APPLICATION:**
How does the lesson apply to the reader's specific situation?
"If you are [reader situation], this means..."

_______________

**CTA:**
What should the reader do next? What is the logical next step that follows from the lesson?

_______________

**NARRATIVE DEVICES TO USE:**
Which devices will make this story vivid? (Check all that apply)
[ ] Specificity -- add exact numbers/names/dates
[ ] Metaphor -- connect unfamiliar to familiar
[ ] Contrast -- juxtapose before/after or expected/actual

[ ] Sensory detail -- add sight/sound/feel to the key moment
[ ] Vulnerability -- include the failure before the win

[ ] In Medias Res (start at the most intense moment)
[ ] Reversal (state the surprising conclusion first)
[ ] Confession (honest admission)

## Template 4: Email Story Opener

For email newsletters, marketing emails, and any message where a story opens the content and bridges to the main payload.

---

**CORE MESSAGE OF THIS EMAIL:**
What is the one thing the reader should understand or do by the end?

_______________

**STORY BEAT 1 -- Hook (1 sentence, In Medias Res):**
Start at the most interesting or tense moment. No context yet.

_______________

**STORY BEAT 2 -- Context (2-3 sentences):**
Now give the context that makes Beat 1 make sense. Who, when, what was happening.

_______________

**STORY BEAT 3 -- Conflict (1-2 sentences):**
Name the specific problem or tension that the story is about.

_______________

**STORY BEAT 4 -- Resolution or Open Loop:**
Pick one:
- If this is a complete story: deliver the resolution in 1-2 sentences.
- If this is a curiosity gap opener: withhold the resolution and tease it.

_______________

**BRIDGE TO LESSON (1-2 sentences):**
Connect the story to what the email is about. "Here is what this taught me about [topic]..."

_______________

**TRANSITION TO MAIN CONTENT:**
One sentence that moves from the story into the body of the email.

_______________

**NARRATIVE DEVICES SELECTED:**
[ ] Specificity (numbers/names/dates in the hook)
[ ] Sensory detail (in Beat 1 or Beat 2)
[ ] Dialogue (at least one quoted line)
[ ] Curiosity gap (if using open loop)
[ ] Vulnerability (if the story involves a failure)

## Template 5: Social Story Template

For LinkedIn posts, X threads, Instagram captions, and short-form social content built around a story.

---

**PLATFORM:**
[ ] LinkedIn (longer, narrative paragraphs)
[ ] X/Twitter (punchy, can thread)
[ ] Instagram (caption below visual)
[ ] Other: _______________

**STORY IN ONE LINE:**
What happened? Compress the entire story into one sentence before writing it out.

_______________

**BEAT 1 -- Attention Hook (1 sentence):**
The most surprising, counterintuitive, or emotionally charged fact or moment from the story.

(This is the sentence that stops the scroll. It must not require context to be interesting.)

_______________

**BEAT 2 -- Protagonist and Context (1-2 sentences):**
Who is this about? Just enough context to create identification.

_______________

**BEAT 3 -- Conflict (1-2 sentences):**
The specific problem or tension. Quantify if possible.

_______________

**BEAT 4 -- Transformation or Insight (2-3 sentences):**
What changed? What was learned? What is the specific result?

_______________

**BEAT 5 -- CTA or Invitation (1 sentence):**
What should the reader do, think, or share?

[ ] Comment with their experience
[ ] Follow for more
[ ] Click link
[ ] Share with someone who needs this
[ ] Reflect / no explicit CTA (let the story close itself)

_______________

**LENGTH CHECK:**
- LinkedIn: 150-300 words (readable paragraphs, 1-2 sentences each)
- X/Twitter: Single post (under 280 chars) or thread (2-6 tweets)
- Instagram caption: 100-200 words + 3-5 hashtags

**NARRATIVE DEVICES SELECTED:**
[ ] Specificity (numbers make it real)
[ ] Contrast (before/after in one sentence)
[ ] Vulnerability (failure before the win)
[ ] Curiosity gap (if threading -- open the loop in tweet 1)

## Quick Reference: Story-to-CTA Arc

Every story in marketing content should follow this four-step arc from story to action:

**Step 1 -- The Story:** The narrative that opens emotional connection.
**Step 2 -- The Lesson:** What the story means. The insight it produces.
**Step 3 -- The Application:** How the lesson applies to the reader's specific situation.
**Step 4 -- The CTA:** The logical next step that follows from the lesson.

The most common failure: great story, no lesson. Or great lesson, no application bridge. Complete all four steps every time.

## Quick Reference: Which Framework to Use

| Story length | Goal | Framework |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 sentences | Hook or illustration | Mini-Story |
| 1 paragraph | Email/social opener | Pixar Spine (compressed) |
| 3-5 paragraphs | Newsletter story | TORS or SBI |
| Full email or article | Deep case study or personal story | Hero's Journey or TORS + SBI |
| Full campaign | Brand narrative or customer spotlight | 6-Element Campaign Arc |
| Brand positioning | About page or pitch | Story Brand |
