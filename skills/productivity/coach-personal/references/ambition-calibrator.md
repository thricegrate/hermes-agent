# Ambition Calibrator

Resolves the tension between "10x thinking" (Hardy/Sullivan) and the "Edison Principle" (Manson/Locke). Both are right. They operate at different altitudes.

## The Paradox

Two frameworks that appear to contradict each other:
- **10x Thinking:** Set an impossible goal. Think bigger. Eliminate everything that doesn't serve the 10x vision. "10x is easier than 2x."
- **Edison Principle:** Lower your ambitions until they feel doable. Accumulate small wins. Goals only motivate when believed attainable. Too-ambitious goals cause demotivation.

Both are supported by research. Both work in practice. The key is knowing WHEN to apply each.

## The Two Altitudes

| Altitude | Tool | Purpose | Example |
|----------|------|---------|---------|
| **Vision** (where you're going) | 10x Thinking | Set direction. Expand what's possible. Force radical simplification. | "Build a $1M/year product business" |
| **Action** (what you do next) | Edison Principle | Make the next step laughably doable. Build momentum through small wins. | "Test one filament. Ship one email. Write one paragraph." |

**The formula: 10x the destination. 0.1x the next step.**

## The Staircase Model

```
10x Frame (top of staircase)
    |
    |  [Chunked sub-goals in between]
    |
    |  Edison: next step (bottom)
```

- **10x** = the top of the staircase (where you're going)
- **Chunking** = the steps in between (breaking the journey into manageable pieces)
- **Edison** = the next step (what you do right now)

They are complementary, not competing. Using 10x on the next action paralyzes. Using Edison on the vision creates mediocrity.

## Diagnostic: Which Mode Is Needed?

| Situation | Signal Phrases | Apply |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Thinking too small, playing it safe | "I just want to double my revenue", "I don't want to take big risks" | **10x Thinking.** Challenge the Frame. Push them to think bigger. |
| Can't start, paralyzed by the goal | "It feels overwhelming", "I don't know where to begin", "It's too big" | **Edison Principle.** Shrink the next step until it feels laughably easy. |
| Big vision + consistent action | "I know where I'm going and I'm moving" | **Neither.** Don't fix what's working. Check in and support. |
| Big vision + zero action | "I have big dreams but I'm stuck" | **Both.** Keep the 10x Frame. Apply Edison to the next action. Split altitude. |
| Big vision + action but retreating | "I need to be realistic", "What if it doesn't work?", "I keep pulling back" | **Delusional Confidence.** Vision and action are right, but doubt keeps pulling them back. See `references/delusional-confidence.md`. |
| Burned out from trying too hard | "I'm exhausted", "Nothing I do is enough" | **Edison + Gap/Gain.** Lower the daily bar. Measure backward, not forward. |
| Comfortable and coasting | "Things are fine", "I'm doing okay" | **10x Thinking.** Comfort is the enemy of 10x. Challenge the Frame. |

## The Key Question

"Is the block that you're thinking too small, that you can't start, or that you keep pulling back?"

- **Thinking too small** → push 10x
- **Can't start** → apply Edison
- **Taking action but retreating** → apply Delusional Confidence (`references/delusional-confidence.md`)
- **Both thinking small + can't start** → split altitude (10x vision, Edison action)

## When to Challenge vs When to Ease

### Challenge (Push 10x) When:
- Playing too small
- Comfort zone
- 2x thinking
- No urgency
- Settling for "good enough"
- Same strategy, same results, year after year

### Ease (Apply Edison) When:
- Paralyzed by ambition
- Overwhelmed
- Chronic procrastination on a specific goal
- Has abandoned ambitious goals before
- Low energy, low confidence
- Needs momentum more than direction

## Integration with Other Frameworks

| Framework | Vision Level (10x) | Action Level (Edison) |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Frame/Floor/Focus | Frame = 10x goal | Focus = which small wins to pursue |
| 91% Rule | The goal itself | The implementation intention (when/where) |
| Chunking | The full staircase | Each individual step |
| Hugo's Jail Cell | The novel to be written | Today's writing session |
| Next Physical Action | The destination | The literal next step |
| Gap/Gain | The ideal (compass, not measure) | The daily Three Wins |

## When the Calibrator Isn't Enough

The Ambition Calibrator resolves the tension between vision altitude and action altitude. But sometimes a client has the right altitude at both levels and still stalls.

The missing ingredient is conviction -- the ability to sustain belief through the messy middle where evidence hasn't arrived yet.

When you prescribe 10x thinking and the client immediately says "but that's not realistic," the problem isn't altitude. It's that doubt keeps pulling them back to the safe middle ground.

Route to `references/delusional-confidence.md` for:
- The Belief-Action Flywheel (belief -> action -> evidence -> stronger belief)
- The Realism Reframe ("being realistic is the most unrealistic thing if you want a different life")
- Daily Fear Rewiring Protocol (one Growth Zone action per day to reprogram the survival brain)
