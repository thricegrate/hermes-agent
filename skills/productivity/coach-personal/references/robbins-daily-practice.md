# Robbins Daily Practice

Concrete daily, weekly, and quarterly practice protocols. Integrates Priming, RPM (Rapid Planning Method), CANI (Constant And Never-Ending Improvement), Wheel of Life, and existing Three Wins practice into a unified system.

## When to Use

- Client needs a structured daily practice (not just "journal more")
- Client has good intentions but no system for maintaining state and momentum
- Client asks "what should my morning routine look like?"
- After any breakthrough session, to lock in the gains with daily habits
- Quarterly review or life audit moments

---

## The Daily Stack

### Morning: Priming Ritual (10-15 min)

Manufacture your state before the day hits you. Full protocol in `robbins-state-management.md`. Use `templates/priming-ritual.md` to set up.

**Condensed daily version:**
1. Breath of Fire -- 3 sets of 30 breaths (3 min)
2. Gratitude -- 3 specific moments, fully felt (3 min)
3. Healing/Loving-Kindness -- light visualization expanding outward (3 min)
4. Three to Thrive -- 3 outcomes visualized as already done (3 min)

**Minimum viable version (5 min):**
1. 1 set of 30 breaths
2. 1 gratitude moment (fully felt, not listed)
3. 1 outcome visualized as done

The non-negotiable: do SOMETHING before the day starts. Even the 5-minute version beats nothing.

### Planning: RPM (15 min, morning or night before)

RPM = Rapid Planning Method. NOT a to-do list. A system of thinking.

**Three questions (order is critical):**

1. **R = Result:** "What do I REALLY want today?" Not a task ("write the report") but an outcome ("publish a report that positions us as category leader").
2. **P = Purpose:** "WHY do I want it?" Multiple emotional reasons. Without compelling "why," execution stalls at the first obstacle.
3. **MAP = Massive Action Plan:** "WHAT actions will get me there?" Prioritized steps. Start with: "If I could only do ONE thing today that creates the most progress toward my Result, what would it be?"

**RPM vs. GSD Lists:**
Traditional to-do lists track activity. RPM tracks outcomes. Use RPM when there's a motivation problem (why am I doing this?). Use GSD when there isn't (just need to execute).

### Evening: Three Wins + CANI (5 min)

**Three Wins (existing practice, from Gap/Gain):**
1. Something you accomplished today
2. Something you learned today
3. Something you're grateful for today

**Add the CANI question:**
4. "What's ONE thing I'll improve tomorrow?"

Not revolutionary change -- 1% better. Compounded daily, 1% improvement = 37x improvement in a year.

**Integration:** Three Wins measures backward (Gain). CANI points forward (improvement). Together they create a daily cycle of appreciation + growth.

## The Weekly Stack

### Weekly RPM Review (30 min, Sunday or Monday morning)

Review all life categories. For each:
- What's the Result I want this week?
- Why does it matter?
- What's my MAP?

**Life categories to review:**
1. Health/Fitness
2. Key Relationship(s)
3. Career/Business
4. Finances
5. Personal Growth
6. Fun/Recreation

Identify the ONE most important Result for the week. Everything else supports it.

### Weekly Three Wins (5 min, Friday or Sunday)

1. Biggest accomplishment this week
2. Biggest lesson this week
3. Biggest thing you're grateful for this week

### Weekly CANI Review

"What did I do well this week? What could I do better next week? What's the ONE improvement I'm making?"

## The Quarterly Stack

### Wheel of Life Review (30-45 min)

Use `templates/wheel-of-life.md`. Rate each domain 1-10 (current satisfaction). Compare to previous quarter.

**10 Domains:**
1. Health & Fitness
2. Relationships / Romance
3. Family
4. Career / Business
5. Finances
6. Personal Growth / Learning
7. Fun & Recreation
8. Physical Environment
9. Contribution / Giving
10. Spirituality / Inner Peace

**What to look for:**
- "Flat tire" pattern: one or two areas dramatically low while others are high. This explains the "I feel off but can't explain why" feeling.
- Domains scoring lowest often explain dissatisfaction the client can't articulate.
- Migration: which areas improved since last quarter? Which declined? Why?

**Output:** Select top 2 priority domains. Create 90-day action plan for each.

### Quarterly Backward Measurement

Pull out the baseline from 90 days ago. Compare every dimension. This is your Gain. Use the Backward Measurement Protocol from `gap-and-gain.md`.

### How Priming + Three Wins Work Together

Priming is PROACTIVE state creation (manufacturing the state you need for the day). Three Wins is REFLECTIVE momentum building (recognizing what went right). Priming as morning bookend, Three Wins as evening bookend. Together they create a daily cycle of intentional state + measured progress.

## CANI: Constant And Never-Ending Improvement

Robbins' adaptation of Japanese Kaizen philosophy.

### The Core Practice

At the end of each day, ask:
1. "What did I do well today?"
2. "What could I do better tomorrow?"
3. "What's ONE thing I'll improve?"

### The Compounding Effect

Not revolutionary change. 1% better, every day. Applied across all life domains: relationships, health, business, skills, communication.

The enemy of CANI: "good enough." Complacency is the death of growth. "Progress is happiness" -- not achievement, progress.

### Integration with RPM

Every goal in RPM has a CANI component: "What is one thing I can improve about this today?" RPM provides direction. CANI provides the daily improvement habit.

## Coaching Application

### Prescribing the Stack

Don't dump the entire system on a client at once. Layer it in:

**Week 1:** Morning Priming (even 5-min version) + Evening Three Wins
**Week 2:** Add CANI question to evening practice
**Week 3:** Add RPM to morning (start with daily only)
**Week 4:** Add Weekly RPM review

**Quarterly:** Introduce Wheel of Life at natural check-in points (quarter-end, birthday, New Year, life transitions).

### Common Resistance

- "I don't have time for morning routines" -- Start with the 5-minute version. Negotiate up from there.
- "I forget to do it" -- Stack it with an existing habit (coffee, brushing teeth). Set a phone alarm for the first 30 days.
- "I did it for a week and stopped" -- Normal. Don't shame it. Restart with the minimum viable version. Consistency beats intensity.
- "It feels weird/forced" -- It will for the first 2 weeks. That's the old neural pathways resisting. Push through 30 days and reassess.

### Tracking

Use `templates/priming-ritual.md` for 30-day streak tracking.
Use existing `templates/accountability-tracker.md` if the client needs external accountability.
