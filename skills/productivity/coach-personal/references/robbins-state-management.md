# Robbins State Management

State management through the Triad model, Priming ritual, and Incantations. Use when a client shows up in an unresourceful state (flat, anxious, shut down, low energy) -- change state BEFORE cognitive coaching.

## When to Use

- Client arrives with energy below 6/10
- Client is stuck in a loop of negative thinking
- Client intellectually knows what to do but "can't get started"
- Before any heavy coaching session (Dickens Process, belief work, relationship work)
- When motivation has collapsed despite a clear plan

---

## The Triad

Three elements create every emotional state. Change any one and the state shifts.

### 1. Physiology (Fastest Lever)

Motion creates emotion. The nervous system reads body position and creates matching neurochemistry.

**Depression pathway:** Collapsed posture + shallow breathing + downward gaze.
**Energy pathway:** Upright posture + deep breathing + eyes up + movement.

**Coaching move:** When someone is stuck in a low state, don't ask them to think differently first. Move their body first. Stand up, walk, breathe deeply, change posture. THEN think.

### 2. Focus

"Where focus goes, energy flows." The brain cannot hold two contradictory focuses at full intensity simultaneously.

**Victim focus:** "Why does this always happen to me?"
**Resourceful focus:** "What can I learn from this and what can I do now?"

Same situation, completely different neurological response. Focus is directed by the questions you ask. Change the habitual questions and you change the habitual focus.

### 3. Language/Meaning

Words don't just describe experience -- they create it. The label you put on an emotion determines its intensity.

"Furious" vs. "a bit annoyed" produces different physical and psychological responses for the same stimulus. The stories you tell about events create meaning. "I failed" vs. "I learned what doesn't work" vs. "I'm building resilience" -- same event, three different meanings, three different emotional experiences.

See also: Transformational Vocabulary in `robbins-belief-change.md`.

## Quick State Change Protocol (30 Seconds)

Deploy when client energy is below 6/10 at session start.

1. **3 deep breaths** -- inhale through nose (4 count), hold (4 count), exhale through mouth (8 count)
2. **Posture shift** -- stand up, shoulders back, eyes up
3. **Gratitude hit** -- "What are you grateful for right now? Say it out loud."

This shifts all three Triad elements simultaneously: physiology (breath + posture), focus (gratitude redirects attention), meaning (gratitude reframes current experience).

## Full State Change: Priming Ritual (10-15 Minutes)

Use when the quick protocol isn't enough, or as a daily practice prescription.

### The Sequence

**1. Breath of Fire (3 minutes)**
Three sets of 30 sharp, forceful nasal breaths. Arms pump overhead with each breath. 15-second pause between sets. Oxygenates the brain, triggers alert/energized state, breaks residual low-energy physiology. The physical intensity is the point -- this is not calm breathing.

**2. Gratitude Visualization (3 minutes)**
Three specific moments you're grateful for, one at a time. Fully re-enter each experience -- feel it in the body. See it, hear it, feel the physical sensation. Include at least one small/mundane thing (wind on face, morning coffee warmth) to train appreciation of ordinary moments.

Neuroscience: gratitude activates the same neural circuits as love/connection. Nearly impossible to be simultaneously grateful and anxious/angry.

**3. Healing/Loving-Kindness (3 minutes)**
Visualize light entering the body, healing any pain or tension. Expand that light outward -- to family, friends, colleagues, and finally to adversaries or difficult people. Conscious choice to radiate goodwill, including toward people who trigger you.

**4. Three to Thrive (3 minutes)**
Visualize three outcomes you want to achieve as ALREADY DONE. Not hoping they happen -- experiencing them from the inside as if they're complete. Inhabit the experience, don't watch it like a movie.

### Extended Versions

- **30 Minutes to Thrive:** Priming + 15 minutes of exercise (walking, jump rope, etc.)
- **Hour of Power:** Priming + exercise + incantations (spoken aloud with full physical intensity during movement)
- **15 Minutes of Fulfillment:** Shortened version for time-crunched days

## Incantations

NOT affirmations. Affirmations are passive statements repeated mentally. Incantations are spoken ALOUD with FULL PHYSICAL INTENSITY, MOVEMENT, and EMOTION. The goal is to engage the whole nervous system, not just the cognitive mind.

### The Difference

- **Affirmation:** Sitting quietly, eyes closed, whispering "I am confident" (engages cognition only)
- **Incantation:** Standing, walking with power, fists pumping, SPEAKING WITH FORCE: "I NOW COMMAND my subconscious mind to direct me in helping as many people as possible today by giving me the STRENGTH, the EMOTION, the PERSUASION, whatever it takes to SHOW these people and GET these people to CHANGE THEIR LIVES NOW!"

### Why Incantations Work When Affirmations Don't

Affirmations try to convince the brain through repetition alone. The brain knows you're lying. Incantations bypass cognitive resistance by engaging the body -- the physical intensity creates a state that matches the words. When your body is in a power state, your brain stops arguing with the words.

### How to Build an Incantation

1. Start with what you want to feel/be/achieve
2. Write it as a command, not a wish ("I AM" or "I NOW COMMAND" not "I hope" or "I want")
3. Include emotional intensity words (strength, power, certainty, fire, unstoppable)
4. Design it to be spoken while moving
5. Practice daily during Hour of Power or morning walks

## Pattern Interrupt Techniques

Use to break a client out of an automatic emotional loop before installing a new pattern.

- Sudden humor or absurd question
- Physical movement (stand up, jump, clap)
- Cold water, loud sound
- Doing the opposite of the expected response
- Rapid topic shift, then redirect

The goal is to BREAK the automatic loop before it completes. A gap opens where a new pattern can be inserted.

## State Diagnostic Questions

Use at session start or whenever energy drops mid-session.

- "How are you showing up right now? Energy 1-10?"
- "What's your body doing right now?" (checks physiology)
- "What are you focused on right now?" (checks focus)
- "What story are you telling yourself about this?" (checks meaning)
- "If you could snap your fingers and feel any way right now, what would you choose?"

## Coaching Application

### The Rule: State Before Strategy

A person in an unresourceful state cannot access resourceful thinking. No framework lands when someone is shut down. ALWAYS check state before coaching content.

### Session Protocol

1. First 2 minutes: state check ("How are you showing up? Energy 1-10?")
2. If below 6: deploy Quick State Change (30 sec) or Full Priming (10-15 min)
3. If above 6: proceed with coaching content
4. Monitor state throughout session -- if energy drops, pause and reset

### Integration with Other Frameworks

- Before Dickens Process: client MUST be in a resourceful state to access deep emotional work
- Before belief work: state determines whether new beliefs can land
- Before goal setting: a person in a depleted state sets depleted goals
- During weekly planning: start with priming to ensure clarity, not reactivity

### The Philosophical Point

You don't FIND a peak state -- you CREATE it. Waiting to "feel" good before taking action is backwards. The priming ritual is a manufacturing process for the state you need. It's not meditation (passive observation) -- it's active state construction.
