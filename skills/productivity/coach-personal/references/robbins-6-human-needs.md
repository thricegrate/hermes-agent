# Robbins 6 Human Needs

The foundation of Robbins' psychology. All human behavior is driven by six needs operating in paradox pairs. Use after Three Buckets identifies WHAT is stuck -- 6 Needs identifies WHY.

## When to Use

- Client's behavior doesn't make rational sense ("I know I should stop but I can't")
- Procrastination that isn't fear-based or knowledge-based
- Self-sabotage patterns that repeat despite awareness
- Overwork/burnout that persists despite permission to rest
- Any behavior the client wants to change but can't through willpower
- After identifying WHAT is stuck (Three Buckets), to diagnose WHY

---

## The 6 Needs

### Needs of the Personality (First Four)

**1. Certainty** -- Need for safety, predictability, control, comfort.

Met positively: skills, preparation, planning, savings.
Met destructively: controlling others, micromanagement, never leaving comfort zone, refusing all risk, over-planning as procrastination.

**2. Uncertainty/Variety** -- Need for novelty, surprise, stimulation, adventure.

Met positively: travel, new challenges, creative projects, learning new skills.
Met destructively: drama creation, picking fights, substance use, affairs, constantly switching projects before completion.

**3. Significance** -- Need to feel unique, important, needed, special.

Met positively: achievement, excellence, expertise, contribution.
Met destructively: putting others down, victimhood narratives, extreme wealth/status signaling, being the "hardest worker" as identity, one-upping.

**4. Love/Connection** -- Need for closeness, belonging, intimacy, being understood.

Met positively: deep relationships, community, vulnerability, teamwork.
Met destructively: codependency, people-pleasing, sacrificing identity for approval, avoiding conflict at all costs.

### Needs of the Spirit (Last Two) -- Only These Produce Lasting Fulfillment

**5. Growth** -- Expansion of capability, knowledge, understanding.

"If you're not growing, you're dying." Stagnation = psychological death even when external life looks fine.

**6. Contribution** -- Giving beyond yourself, service, legacy, making a difference.

The deepest source of meaning. Fulfillment lives here.

## Top-Two Needs Diagnosis

Every person has a TOP TWO needs hierarchy that drives almost everything they do. Identifying someone's top two explains behavior that looks irrational on the surface.

### Common Top-Two Patterns

- **Certainty + Significance:** The perfectionist/controller. Won't delegate, needs to be the expert, paralyzed by risk.
- **Variety + Significance:** The serial entrepreneur/shiny object chaser. Starts everything, finishes nothing. Gets bored once the novelty wears off.
- **Significance + Love/Connection:** Torn between standing out and fitting in. People-pleaser who also needs to be seen as special.
- **Certainty + Love/Connection:** Stays in bad situations (jobs, relationships) because leaving threatens both safety and belonging.
- **Growth + Contribution:** The fulfilled achiever. Constantly improving and giving back. This is the target state.

### How to Identify Top Two

Ask: "What does [the behavior you want to change] give you? What do you get from it?"

If they say "I don't know," push: "If you couldn't do [behavior] anymore, what would you lose?"

The answer reveals which needs the behavior meets.

## The Addiction Rule

Any behavior that simultaneously meets 3 or more needs becomes an addiction. This is why willpower alone can't break it -- the behavior is meeting too many needs at once.

### Examples

- **Social media scrolling:** Certainty (predictable dopamine), variety (endless new content), significance (likes/followers), connection (comments/DMs) = 4 needs. Willpower doesn't stand a chance.
- **Overwork:** Certainty (routine), significance (identity as "hard worker"), connection (team bonds), growth (learning) = 4 needs. This is why "just take a vacation" doesn't work.
- **Drama/conflict:** Variety (unpredictable), significance (being the center), connection (intensity feels like intimacy) = 3 needs.

### The Coaching Intervention

NOT "stop doing that." Instead: "Find an empowering vehicle that meets the SAME needs."

1. Identify which needs the behavior meets (usually 3-4)
2. Find a positive behavior that meets those same needs
3. Install the new behavior using NAC (see `robbins-belief-change.md`)

## The Vehicle Concept

A "vehicle" is the means by which a need is met. Vehicles can be empowering or destructive. Same need, different vehicle = different life.

### Examples

| Need | Destructive Vehicle | Empowering Vehicle |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Significance | Putting others down | Mastery of a craft |
| Certainty | Controlling others | Building skills and systems |
| Variety | Drama and conflict | Creative projects and travel |
| Connection | Codependency | Vulnerable, honest relationships |
| Growth | Obsessive comparison | Deliberate practice and learning |
| Contribution | Martyrdom | Strategic giving with boundaries |

The need itself is never the problem. The vehicle is.

## The Certainty/Variety Tension

The most fundamental conflict in human psychology.

- Too much certainty = boredom, relationship death, no growth, stagnation
- Too much variety = anxiety, broken commitments, no foundation, chaos
- Peak state lives in the zone between -- enough certainty to feel safe, enough variety to feel alive

### Coaching Application

When a client feels "stuck but can't explain why," check this tension:
- "Do you have too much routine or too little?"
- "When was the last time something surprised you in a good way?"
- "What's the last risk you took?"

## Needs-Based Behavior Decoding

### Procrastination Through the Needs Lens

- Certainty-driven procrastination: "If I don't try, I can't fail" (protecting certainty of self-image)
- Significance-driven procrastination: "If I don't finish, I can't be judged" (protecting significance)
- Connection-driven procrastination: "If I succeed, I'll outgrow my circle" (protecting connection)

### Self-Sabotage Through the Needs Lens

- "I was about to succeed and then I blew it" -- often protecting certainty (success is unfamiliar/uncertain) or connection (success threatens belonging with current social group)
- Ask: "What would you lose if you actually succeeded?"

### Overwork Through the Needs Lens

- When overwork persists despite permission to rest, it's meeting multiple needs
- Common pattern: significance (identity as hard worker) + certainty (routine/control) + connection (team/clients need me)
- The intervention isn't "work less" -- it's "find another way to meet significance, certainty, and connection"

---

## Diagnostic Questions Per Need

**Certainty:** "What are you most afraid of losing right now?"
**Variety:** "When was the last time you felt genuinely excited or surprised?"
**Significance:** "Where do you feel most important? What would happen if that went away?"
**Connection:** "Who do you feel truly seen by? What would you do to keep that?"
**Growth:** "Where are you growing right now? Where have you plateaued?"
**Contribution:** "Who are you helping? How does that feel?"

### The Master Question

"When do you feel most alive? Most fulfilled?" -- reveals which spirit needs are (or aren't) being activated.

## Coaching Application

### Session Flow

1. Identify the behavior the client wants to change
2. Ask: "What does this behavior give you?" (surface the needs it meets)
3. If it meets 3+ needs: explain the addiction rule. Willpower won't work.
4. Map out which needs are met and whether the vehicles are empowering or destructive
5. Design an empowering replacement that meets the same needs
6. Install using NAC (see `robbins-belief-change.md`)

### Integration with Other Frameworks

- **Three Buckets (Don't Know How / No Energy / Fear):** Three Buckets identifies the surface block. 6 Needs identifies the underlying driver.
- **Hardy's Future Self:** The future self exercise reveals what needs the client wants to prioritize. Compare to current top-two.
- **Gap/Gain:** Chronic Gap thinking often serves significance ("I'm never satisfied" = "I have high standards" = significance vehicle).
- **Procrastination as Identity Protection (Step B5):** Identity protection is a certainty + significance behavior. The 6 Needs lens adds precision to the diagnosis.
