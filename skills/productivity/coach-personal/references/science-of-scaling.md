## Frame/Floor/Focus

### The Three Levers of Scaling

| Lever | What It Is | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Frame** | The impossible goal that redefines success | Expands your thinking beyond incremental improvement |
| **Floor** | The minimum acceptable standard | Eliminates everything below your new standard |
| **Focus** | The 1-3 pathways that matter | Concentrates all resources on highest-leverage activities |

### How They Work Together
1. **Frame** stretches your ambition (10x, not 2x)
2. **Floor** eliminates the bottom (raise standards, cut what's below)
3. **Focus** narrows the path (1-3 things, not 10)

The combination is what creates exponential results. Any one lever alone produces incremental improvement. All three together create quantum leaps.

### The Sequence
1. Set the Frame FIRST (the impossible goal)
2. Raise the Floor SECOND (what do you stop tolerating?)
3. Sharpen the Focus LAST (what 1-3 things will get you to the Frame?)

### Why This Order Matters
- Without a Frame, you don't know what Floor to set (standards depend on the goal)
- Without a Floor, you can't Focus (you're still doing too many low-standard things)
- Focus without Frame and Floor = optimizing the wrong things

## Time Compression Principle

### The Core Insight
**Bigger goals with more focus take LESS time than smaller goals with scattered effort.**

### Why Time Compresses
| Small Goal (2x) | Big Goal (10x) |
|-----------------|----------------|
| "I can get there gradually" | "I need to change fundamentally" |
| Permits scattered effort | Forces focused effort |
| Allows procrastination | Creates urgency |
| Incremental tweaks | Radical simplification |
| Years of slow progress | Months of intense transformation |

### Example
- **2x goal:** "Double revenue in 2 years" → Keep doing the same things, add more → 24+ months, exhausting
- **10x goal:** "10x revenue in 2 years" → Must eliminate 80%, hire Whos, systematize → Forces radical action NOW → Often achieves 3-5x in 12 months (more than the 2x goal would have!)

### The Paradox
You don't have to reach 10x to benefit from 10x thinking. The ACT of pursuing 10x creates more progress than pursuing 2x, even if you "only" achieve 5x.

### Coaching Application
- "If you had to hit this goal in half the time, what would you do differently?"
- "What are you doing slowly that you could do immediately if the deadline were next month?"
- "What would your future self, who already achieved this, tell you to stop wasting time on?"

## Scaling Through Subtraction

### The Core Principle
Growth comes from elimination, not addition. Every "yes" to something unimportant is a "no" to something that matters.

### The Math
- Adding 10 new initiatives: spreads resources across 10 things → 10% improvement each → net: chaos
- Eliminating 8 of 10 initiatives: concentrates resources on 2 things → 500% improvement each → net: transformation

### What to Subtract

| Category | What to Eliminate | How to Decide |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| **Activities** | Tasks that aren't in your 20% | "Would my 10x self do this?" |
| **Clients** | Low-margin, high-maintenance clients | "Do they meet my new Floor?" |
| **Products** | Low-margin, low-demand products | "Does this align with my Focus?" |
| **Commitments** | Obligations that drain without return | "Does this create or destroy Freedoms?" |
| **Relationships** | People who pull you backward | "Does this person energize or drain me?" |
| **Processes** | Unnecessary steps in your pipeline | "If we removed this, what breaks?" |
| **Meetings** | Meetings without clear outcomes | "Could this be an email or async message?" |

### The Emotional Challenge
Subtraction is harder than addition because:
- Sunk cost fallacy: "I've invested so much in this already"
- Loss aversion: "What if I need this later?"
- Identity attachment: "But this is what I DO"
- Social pressure: "People expect me to keep doing this"

### Coaching Through Subtraction
1. "Make the list of everything you do."
2. "Now imagine you could only keep 3 things. Which 3?"
3. "The gap between your list and those 3 things: that's what needs to go."
4. "I know it's hard. But 10x is on the other side of elimination, not accumulation."

## Case Studies

### Mark Young: $50M in 6 Weeks
**Before:** Multiple services, scattered focus, decent revenue but no scaling pathway.
**Frame:** Set an impossible revenue target that seemed unreachable with current model.
**Floor:** Raised minimum client value dramatically. Stopped working with small clients.
**Focus:** Narrowed to ONE service pathway with the highest leverage.
**Result:** $50M in 6 weeks by concentrating all resources on the highest-value pathway.
**Lesson:** The revenue was always available: it was hidden behind too many mediocre activities.

### Alicia Ault: 10 to 8,000 Units in 12 Months
**Before:** 10 units, small operation, limited thinking about what was possible.
**Frame:** 8,000 units: a number that seemed impossible at current trajectory.
**Floor:** Raised quality and delivery standards. Cut anything that didn't meet the new bar.
**Focus:** Focused distribution on the channel with highest leverage. Didn't try to be everywhere.
**Result:** 800x growth in 12 months.
**Lesson:** The constraint wasn't capability: it was standards (Floor) and focus.

### Xavier Martine: 2x Revenue in 90 Days
**Before:** Multiple service offerings, decent business, but growth stalled.
**Frame:** Double revenue in 90 days (aggressive but clear).
**Floor:** Eliminated 80% of service offerings. Only kept the highest-margin work.
**Focus:** Poured all sales and marketing energy into the ONE highest-margin service.
**Result:** 2x revenue in 90 days with LESS work and higher margins.
**Lesson:** Subtraction is the fastest path to growth. Xavier didn't add anything: he removed everything that wasn't the best thing.

### Common Pattern Across All Cases
1. Set an impossible Frame
2. Raise the Floor (eliminate low-standard activities/clients/products)
3. Focus on 1-3 highest-leverage pathways
4. Growth came from SUBTRACTION, not addition

### Step 3: Select 1-3 Pathways
Based on scores, select the 1-3 pathways with the best combination of:
- Highest revenue potential
- Lowest effort (or highest leverage)
- Best alignment with your impossible goal
- Above your new standards

### Step 4: Eliminate Everything Else
"These 1-3 pathways are your Focus. Everything else stops."
- What needs to be shut down?
- What needs to be delegated?
- What needs a graceful exit?
- What conversations do you need to have?

### Step 5: All-In
"For the next 90 days, 80% of your time and energy goes to these 1-3 pathways. What does your calendar need to look like?"
