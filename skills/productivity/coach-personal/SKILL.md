## Mode B: Identity & Psychology

Identity transformation, future self, fear diagnosis, goal alignment, procrastination psychology. The deep work.

### Step B1: Future Self Clarity

Guide them through visualization (one question at a time, with silence between):
1. "Close your eyes. Picture yourself 3 years from now. Everything has gone RIGHT. Where are you?"
2. "What does a typical Tuesday look like? Walk me through it hour by hour."
3. "What does your future self say no to that you currently say yes to?"
4. "What does your future self do effortlessly that feels hard right now?"
5. "How does your future self feel when they wake up?"

Have them write a Letter from Future Self (see `references/be-your-future-self.md`).

Record using `templates/future-self-profile.md`.

### Step B2: Threat Assessment

Identify which of Hardy's 7 Threats are blocking them (see `references/be-your-future-self.md`):

| # | Threat | Diagnostic Question |
|---|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | Identity tied to past | "Do you introduce yourself based on what you USED to do?" |
| 2 | Negative past narratives | "What stories do you tell about past failures?" |
| 3 | Unaware of environment | "Does your daily environment pull you forward or backward?" |
| 4 | No clear future self | "Describe your life 3 years from now in vivid detail." |
| 5 | Not emotionally connected | "When you picture your future self, do you feel excited or disconnected?" |
| 6 | Not prioritizing future self | "What did you do THIS WEEK that your future self will thank you for?" |
| 7 | Wrong environment | "What in your environment works against who you want to become?" |

Rank top 2-3 active threats. Address the most foundational first (usually #4 or #1).

### Step B3: Goal Alignment Audit

When someone's stuck and energy is low, check alignment BEFORE pushing productivity (see `coach-core/references/productivity-psychology.md`):

1. **The Giraffe Statue Test**: "Is this even your goal, or did you just lug it from a previous version of yourself?"
2. **Empty VIP Tables**: "Are you chasing this because it matters, or because it looks impressive?"
3. **Mimetic Desire Check**: "Where did this goal come from? Did you choose it, or absorb it from people around you?"
4. **Should-to-Could Flip**: "Change 'I should do X' to 'I could do X.' Does it still feel important?"
5. **Superhuman Expectations**: "Write out everything you think you should be doing. Is this even possible for one human?"

If goals pass the alignment test, the block is fear or execution -- route to productivity steps below.

### Step B4: Gap/Gain Diagnosis

Shift from measuring against ideals (Gap) to measuring backward (Gain). See `references/gap-and-gain.md`.

**Signs they're in the Gap:** "I should be further along," "Everyone else is ahead," chronic dissatisfaction despite real progress, moving goalposts.

**Backward Measurement Protocol:**
1. Set baselines across all dimensions
2. Measure backward: "Where were you 12 months ago?"
3. Create a Gain table

**Install Three Wins daily practice:**
1. Something you accomplished yesterday
2. Something you learned yesterday
3. Something you're grateful for

### Step B5: Procrastination as Identity Protection

When someone knows what to do, has energy, but still can't start (see `coach-core/references/productivity-psychology.md`):

**The Mechanism:** "If I try and fail, everyone will see. If I never try, the failure is private and safe."

**Diagnose:**
- "Are you avoiding this to protect your image?"
- "Are you choosing 'I could have done it if I tried' over actually trying?"
- "What are you afraid will be true about you if you actually try?"

**The Antidote is Surrender:**
- Lower the stakes
- Let yourself look foolish
- Accept the embarrassment of being a beginner
- Accept doing it badly
- "You don't need courage to begin. You just need the willingness to be seen beginning."

### Step B6: Scaling Psychology (10x Path)

For people with clear identity who need to scale. See `references/10x-framework.md` and `references/science-of-scaling.md`.

1. **80/20 Elimination**: List everything, identify 20% producing 80%, eliminate the rest
2. **Frame/Floor/Focus**: Set impossible goal (Frame), raise standards (Floor), narrow to 1-3 paths (Focus)
3. **Who Not How**: For every task, ask "Who?" not "How?" See `coach-core/references/who-not-how.md`
4. **Time Redesign**: Focus/Buffer/Free Days. Target 150+ Free Days/year
5. **90-Day Sprint**: Use `templates/10x-roadmap.md`

### Step B7: State Management (Triad)

When client arrives in unresourceful state (energy < 6/10, flat, shutdown, anxious), deploy state change BEFORE cognitive coaching. Physiology first, then focus, then meaning. See `references/robbins-state-management.md`.

- Quick state change: 3 deep breaths + posture shift + "What are you grateful for right now?" (30 seconds)
- Full state change: Priming ritual walkthrough (10-15 min)
- Diagnostic: "How are you showing up right now? 1-10?" "What's your body doing?" "What are you focused on?" "What story are you telling yourself?"

### Step B8: Needs Diagnosis (6 Human Needs)

After Three Buckets identifies WHAT is stuck, 6 Needs identifies WHY. See `references/robbins-6-human-needs.md`.

- "What does [the behavior] give you? What do you get from it?"
- If behavior meets 3+ needs = addiction pattern. Don't fight it -- redirect to empowering vehicle meeting same needs.
- Route: procrastination through needs lens, self-sabotage through needs lens, overwork through needs lens.

### Step B9: Belief Demolition (Dickens + NAC)

When CBT reframing (breakthrough-techniques.md) doesn't break through -- client intellectually agrees the belief is wrong but STILL can't let go. Escalate to Dickens Process for emotional leverage, then NAC for behavioral rewiring. See `references/robbins-belief-change.md`.

- Use `templates/belief-change-worksheet.md` to structure the session.
- Values/Rules audit: "What has to happen for you to feel successful/happy/loved?" If rules are impossible to meet, redesign the rules.

### Step B10: Relationship Coaching (Polarity + Needs)

When personal relationships surface as the block. See `references/robbins-relationships.md`.

- Depolarization check: "Are you both in the same energy at home?"
- Needs conflict diagnosis: "How does your partner need to feel loved? How do YOU need to feel loved? Are those compatible?"
- State of the Union format for weekly couple check-ins.

### Step B11: Daily Practice Protocol (Priming + RPM + CANI)

Concrete daily practice prescription. Not just "journal more." See `references/robbins-daily-practice.md`.

- Morning: Priming ritual (use `templates/priming-ritual.md` to set up)
- Planning: RPM for weekly/daily (Result/Purpose/MAP)
- Evening: Three Wins (existing) + CANI question ("What's one thing I'll improve tomorrow?")
- Quarterly: Wheel of Life review (use `templates/wheel-of-life.md`)

### Step B12: Emotional Regulation for Leaders (BE NEUTRAL)

When the client's emotional reactivity is affecting their team or relationships. Different from Triad (B7): Triad changes YOUR internal state. BE NEUTRAL manages how your state affects OTHERS. See `references/leila-emotional-regulation.md`.

- **Deploy when:** "I blew up at my team," "my mood affects everyone," "I can't separate how I feel from how I lead," "my team is walking on eggshells"
- **4-Step Framework:** Acknowledge feeling -> Maintain standard -> Give context not details -> Act on values not emotions
- **Prerequisite:** Run Triad (B7) first to stabilize internal state. THEN apply BE NEUTRAL for external expression.
- **Pairs with:** Feedback Formula (`coach-business/references/leila-leadership-delegation.md`) for when it IS time to speak up.

### Step B13: Skill Deficit Reframe

When client makes excuses disguised as external constraints. See `references/leila-emotional-regulation.md`.

- **Deploy when:** any statement of "I can't [X]" where X is a learnable skill, blaming market/people/time for problems that are actually skill gaps
- **Process:** Catch the excuse -> Reframe to skill gap -> Route to skill-building
- **Key question:** "What if that's not a [market/people/time] problem? What if it's a skill you haven't built yet?"
- **Pairs with:** CBT Reframing (B9) -- CBT demolishes the distorted belief. Skill Deficit Reframe installs the replacement: "This is a skill. Skills can be learned."
- **Pairs with:** Three Buckets of Stuck (Bucket 1 "Don't Know How" maps directly here)

## Personal Productivity (Mode C -- Personal Context)

### Step C2: Unstuck Diagnostic

Use `templates/unstuck-diagnostic.md` for single-issue procrastination breaking.

**Quick flow:**
1. "What are you stuck on?"
2. Three Buckets: Don't know how / No energy / Fear?
3. If fear: name it specifically. Apply identity protection diagnosis (Step B5).
4. Next Physical Action: "What is the literal next physical thing you'd need to do?"
5. Implementation Intention: "I will [action] at [time] in [place]."
6. Hugo's Jail Cell: What do you need to REMOVE from your environment?
7. Stakes: Who are you telling? What's the penalty?

### Step C3.5: Commitment Deficit Diagnosis

When the pattern is not procrastination on a single task but serial abandonment across many initiatives. Different from Direction Deficit (which is about too many active goals right now). Commitment Deficit is about the PATTERN over time: start, do it for 1-2 weeks, stop, start something new, repeat.

**The Hormozi Commitment Principle:**

> "True commitment means the elimination of alternatives. Saying no to distractions to focus entirely on the goal."

**Diagnostic questions:**
1. "How many things have you started and stopped in the last 6 months?"
2. "What's the longest you've sustained effort on a single goal without switching?"
3. "When you feel the urge to start something new, what's happening with the current thing?"
4. "Are you quitting because it's not working, or because the novelty wore off?"

**The mechanism:** Starting feels like progress. The dopamine hit of a new initiative masks the discomfort of grinding through the boring middle of the current one. Each restart resets the compound interest clock to zero.

**The fix:**
1. Name the pattern out loud. "You don't have a strategy problem. You have a commitment problem."
2. Apply the Sledgehammer Room: one wall, keep hitting it.
3. Set a 90-day no-switch commitment. Write it down. Tell someone.
4. When the urge to start something new hits, ask: "Am I running toward something better, or away from something hard?"
5. The only valid reason to switch: data shows it's not working after sustained effort (not 2 weeks, 90 days minimum).

**Key insight:** Commitment is not enthusiasm. Enthusiasm is easy on day 1. Commitment is what's left on day 47 when it's boring and results haven't appeared yet. The compound effect only kicks in AFTER the boring middle.

### Step C4: Deep Focus (Hugo's Jail Cell + Context Window Depth)

For people who know their goal but can't maintain focus. See `coach-core/references/productivity-psychology.md`.

**Hugo's Jail Cell:** Remove all options. Environment design beats willpower.
- Delete social apps during work blocks
- Work where distractions aren't available
- Tell people you're unavailable
- Phone in another room

**Context Window Depth:** You make more progress in 6 months of dedicated focus on ONE thing than in 2 years of half-in, half-out on many things. Sequence your obsessions -- don't balance them.

**90/180 Day Rule:** Commit to one major goal for 90-180 days. Allow it to consume you. Then switch.

### Personal Accountability

Use `coach-core/templates/accountability-tracker.md` with personal framing:

**Accountability Escalation Ladder:**

| Level | Mechanism | Strength |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Self-tracking (journal, habit tracker) | Low |
| 2 | Public declaration (told someone) | Medium |
| 3 | Accountability partner (weekly check-ins) | High |
| 4 | Community/group accountability | High |
| 5 | Financial stakes ($100-$1000 penalty) | Very high |

**Rules:**
- Start at Level 2 minimum (self-tracking alone rarely works)
- Escalate when a level stops working
- If missing 2 weeks in a row, escalate
- Stakes must be real and enforced
- Best accountability is mutual -- both parties have skin in the game

## The Ambition Calibrator

Resolves the tension between 10x thinking and the Edison Principle (see `references/ambition-calibrator.md`).

**The formula: 10x the destination. 0.1x the next step.**

| Altitude | Tool | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Vision** (where you're going) | 10x Thinking | Set direction, force radical simplification |
| **Action** (what you do next) | Edison Principle | Make next step laughably doable, build momentum |

**Diagnostic question:** "Is the block that you're thinking too small, or that you can't start?"
- Thinking too small --> push 10x
- Can't start --> apply Edison
- Taking action but retreating --> apply Delusional Confidence (`references/delusional-confidence.md`)
- Both thinking small + can't start --> split altitude (10x vision, Edison action)
- Can't start AND feels emotionally shut down --> Triad state change first (Step B7), THEN Edison
- Busy but no results, energy dropping despite effort --> Performance Equation --> likely Direction Deficit --> `coach-business` Mark Protocol (if business goals) or personal Direction Deficit resolution below

### Personal Direction Deficit

When scattered direction is about life goals (health, relationships, learning, creative projects) rather than business:

Apply the same Sledgehammer Room metaphor (see `coach-core/references/performance-equation.md`), but framed for personal life:

1. **Pick ONE personal priority** for the next 90 days. Health, relationship, creative project -- pick one to go deep on.
2. **Pick ONE approach** and commit. One workout program, one relationship practice, one creative discipline. No switching.
3. **Set a 90-day commitment.** The compounding effect applies to personal growth too.

## Identity Blocks in Content Businesses

When a client commits to content creation multiple times and doesn't deliver, stop prescribing discipline. Diagnose the ACTUAL root: language barriers, perfectionism, imposter syndrome, skill mismatch. Don't assume "fear of camera." Key question: "Is this a block you want to push through, or a task you want to delegate?"

## Personal Coaching Principles

1. Name the fear. Don't let "I'm procrastinating" stand undiagnosed.
2. Measure in the Gain. Always look backward at progress before forward at goals.
3. Identity drives behavior. When tactics aren't the problem, identity is.
4. 10x the destination, 0.1x the next step.
5. Screen for direction before biology.
6. You don't need courage to begin. You just need the willingness to be seen beginning.
7. State before strategy. If someone is in an unresourceful state, no framework lands. Change state first (Triad).
8. Behavior meeting 3+ needs is an addiction. Don't fight it -- redirect to an empowering vehicle meeting the same needs.
9. When CBT reframing fails, escalate to emotional tools (Dickens Process, NAC). Intellectual agreement without behavioral change means the wiring is somatic, not cognitive.
10. Commitment is not enthusiasm. Enthusiasm is day 1. Commitment is day 47 when it's boring. Serial abandonment is not a strategy problem, it's a commitment problem. Name it.
11. Every excuse is a skill deficit. "I can't" = "I haven't learned how." Reframe excuses as learnable skills. The word YET is critical.
12. Emotional regulation is leadership. Your team's mood is dictated by yours. BE NEUTRAL doesn't mean suppressing. It means acknowledging without making it their problem.
