# Robbins Diagnostic Tools

Quick-access diagnostic frameworks from Tony Robbins for use during coaching sessions. Each section includes when to use, diagnostic questions, and routing implications.

For deep identity work using these frameworks (NAC, Dickens Process, full needs rewiring), route to `coach-personal`.

---

## 1. Triad State Check (Session Opening)

**When to use:** First 2 minutes of every session. Before any diagnosis, exploration, or framework application.

**Core principle:** A person in an unresourceful state cannot access resourceful thinking. State change comes before strategy.

**The Triad -- three levers that create every emotional state:**
1. **Physiology** (fastest lever): posture, breathing, facial expression, movement
2. **Focus**: what you're paying attention to determines your emotional experience
3. **Language/Meaning**: the words and stories you use create (not just describe) your state

**Protocol:**
1. "How are you showing up today? Energy 1-10?"
2. If below 6: run a 2-minute state change before proceeding
3. State change sequence: posture shift (stand/sit up) + 3 deep breaths + "What are you grateful for right now?"

**Why this works:** Changing any one element of the Triad shifts the other two. The gratitude question shifts Focus, the breathing shifts Physiology, and the reframe shifts Language/Meaning -- all in 30 seconds.

**Routing:** If a client consistently shows up in low state (3 sessions in a row below 5), this is not a session-level fix. Route to `coach-personal` for deeper state management and identity work.

## 2. 6 Human Needs Diagnostic Lens

**When to use:** After bucket classification (Three Buckets of Stuck) to understand WHY someone is stuck, not just WHAT kind of stuck. Especially powerful when a client keeps repeating a behavior they know is destructive.

**The 6 Needs:**

| Need | Description | Empowering Vehicle | Destructive Vehicle |
|------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Certainty** | Safety, predictability, control | Skills, preparation, savings | Micromanagement, comfort zone, over-planning |
| **Variety** | Novelty, surprise, stimulation | New challenges, learning, travel | Drama creation, substance use, project-hopping |
| **Significance** | Feeling unique, important, needed | Achievement, expertise, contribution | Putting others down, victimhood, status signaling |
| **Connection** | Closeness, belonging, intimacy | Deep relationships, vulnerability | Codependency, people-pleasing, conflict avoidance |
| **Growth** | Expansion of capability and knowledge | Learning, stretching, discomfort | (Rarely destructive -- absence is the problem) |
| **Contribution** | Giving beyond yourself, legacy | Service, mentoring, building | (Rarely destructive -- absence is the problem) |

**Key rules:**
- Everyone has a TOP TWO needs driving most behavior. Identify these and you explain behavior that looks irrational.
- **Addiction rule:** Any behavior meeting 3+ needs simultaneously becomes an addiction. Willpower alone won't break it.
- **Vehicle concept:** The need is never the problem -- the vehicle (how it's met) is. Coaching intervention = find an empowering vehicle that meets the SAME needs.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "What does [the behavior you want to change] give you? What do you get from it?"
- "If you couldn't do [behavior] anymore, what would you lose?"
- "What are you most afraid of losing right now?" (reveals top need)
- "When do you feel most alive? Most fulfilled?" (reveals whether Growth/Contribution are active)
- "Does [behavior] give you certainty, variety, significance, connection, growth, or contribution? Which ones?"

**Routing:** For full needs rewiring (changing vehicles, rebalancing hierarchy), route to `coach-personal` Step B8. Coach-core uses this as a diagnostic lens only -- enough to understand the pattern, then hand off for deep work.

## 3. Wheel of Life Intake Assessment

**When to use:**
- First session / intake (baseline)
- Client says "I feel off but can't explain why"
- Client overperforms in one area but neglects others
- Quarterly review (progress tracking)
- After major life changes

**Domains (rate each 1-10, current satisfaction):**
1. Health & Fitness
2. Relationships / Romance
3. Family
4. Career / Business
5. Finances
6. Personal Growth / Learning
7. Fun & Recreation
8. Physical Environment
9. Contribution / Giving
10. Spirituality / Inner Peace

**How to read results:**
- Look for the "flat tire" -- one or two areas dramatically low while others are high
- The lowest-scoring domains often explain dissatisfaction the client can't articulate
- A domain at 3 while everything else is 7+ is usually the source of "something feels off"

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "Rate each area 1-10 for current satisfaction."
- "Which area surprised you? Which score feels most uncomfortable?"
- "If you could only improve one area in the next 90 days, which would create the biggest ripple effect?"
- "What would a 7 look like in your lowest area? What's one action to move it?"

**Routing:** Once the low-scoring domain is identified, route to the appropriate coaching mode:
- Career/Business/Finances low --> `coach-business`
- Identity/Growth/Relationships low --> `coach-personal`
- Health/Environment low --> action planning (may not need deep coaching, just structure)

## 4. Values/Rules Probing Questions

**When to use:** When a client is stuck despite having the right strategy and tactics. When there's a gap between what they say they want and how they behave. When they say "I should want this but I don't" or "I don't know why I keep sabotaging myself."

**Three levels to probe:**

**Global Beliefs** -- sweeping generalizations that color everything:
- "What do you believe is true about [money/success/relationships/yourself]?"
- "Where did that belief come from? Is it actually true, or just familiar?"
- "What evidence do you have that this is true? What evidence do you have that it's NOT true?"

**Rules** -- conditions required to feel an emotion:
- "What has to happen for you to feel successful?"
- "What has to happen for you to feel happy?"
- "Is that rule serving you, or is it a prison?"
- Check: Are positive states hard to trigger (high bar) and negative states easy to trigger (low bar)? That's the suffering formula.

**References** -- the raw material beliefs are built from:
- "Can you think of a time when the opposite of this belief was true?"
- "Who do you know that proves this belief wrong?"

**Routing:** Surface-level values check stays in coach-core. If deep belief demolition is needed (Dickens Process, NAC rewiring), route to `coach-personal`.

## 5. RPM Action Framing

**When to use:** When designing action items (Session Step 5). When a client has a motivation problem with follow-through. When GSD lists aren't working because the client keeps deprioritizing their own goals.

**RPM = Result / Purpose / MAP:**

1. **Result:** "What do you REALLY want?" -- specific, measurable outcome stated positively. Not a task ("write the report") but an outcome ("publish a report that generates 50 qualified leads").

2. **Purpose:** "WHY do you want it?" -- emotional reasons, the more compelling the better. This is the step most people skip, and it's the step that determines follow-through.

3. **MAP (Massive Action Plan):** "WHAT actions will get you there?" -- prioritized steps. First question: "If you could only do ONE thing today toward this Result, what would it be?"

**When to use RPM vs GSD:**
- **RPM** when there's a motivation problem (they know what to do but don't do it). The "Purpose" step adds emotional fuel.
- **GSD** when there's no motivation problem (they're clear and energized, just need the list).

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "You've got the action items. But WHY do these matter to you? What happens if you nail all three this week?"
- "If motivation dips on Wednesday, what's the reason that'll pull you back?"
- "What's the ONE action that would create the most progress toward your result?"

**Routing:** RPM framing stays in coach-core as an action design tool. For full weekly/daily RPM planning systems, route to `coach-personal` (productivity and planning).
