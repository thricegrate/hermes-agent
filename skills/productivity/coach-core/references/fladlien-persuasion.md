## Model of Reality Technique

Adapted from Milton Erickson's conversational hypnosis. Fladlien's strongest persuasion technique.

### The 7 Steps

1. **Identify the Impoverished Model** -- When someone makes decisions they know are wrong, their model of reality is broken. Don't argue -- help them see the internal contradiction.
2. **Elicit Their Decision-Making Process** -- Ask: "How will you know when you're done thinking about it?" Most can't answer -- their decision-making is unconscious.
3. **Uncover the True Barrier** -- Ask: "How would you know you're confident?" Often reveals an impossible loop ("I'll feel confident once I make money, but I need to invest to start making money").
4. **Operate Without Judgment** -- Compassion, not criticism. Everyone has stuck areas.
5. **Offer an Alternative Framework** -- "Are you open to a different way of working through this?" Permission-based.
6. **Redefine Success Metrics** -- Replace impossible standards with achievable ones.
7. **Unite Heart and Head** -- Sound decisions require emotional AND intellectual alignment.

### Coaching Application

Use when a client is stuck in analysis paralysis or can't commit to a decision they intellectually know is right. The key question -- "How will you know when you're done thinking about it?" -- exposes that most people have no exit criteria for their indecision. They'll think about it forever because there's no defined endpoint.

**In coaching sessions:** When a client says "I need to think about it" (about anything -- hiring, launching, investing, pivoting), deploy steps 2-3. If they can't articulate their decision criteria, the thinking is just dressed-up avoidance.

**For helping clients sell:** Teach them to use this with their own prospects. "Your customer says they need to think about it. Ask them: what specifically do they need to think about? What would make them confident? You'll find they have no answer -- and that's your opening."

## 10-80-10 Theory

The fundamental model for understanding any audience's buying behavior:

- **10%** will buy almost anything you offer (superfans, action-takers)
- **10%** will never buy no matter what (wrong fit, chronic skeptics)
- **80%** could go either way -- this is where the sale happens or dies

### Coaching Application

Stop obsessing over the 10% who won't buy. Stop taking the 10% who always buy for granted. All energy goes to the 80%.

**In coaching context:** When a client's conversion rate is stuck, diagnose which segment they're actually losing. Are they only converting the easy 10% and leaving the 80% untouched? That's an objection-handling problem, not a traffic problem.

**For client mindset:** "You don't need everyone to say yes. You need to get better at converting the 80% who are on the fence. That's a skill, not luck."

## 3 Objection Tiers + Cycling Strategy

Every objection falls into one of three tiers. The tiers are nested -- surface objections mask deeper ones.

### The Tiers

**Tier 1: Price (Surface)**
- Almost never the real issue
- Address 5-9 different ways
- What they say: "It's too expensive," "I can't afford it," "I need to check my budget"

**Tier 2: Time (Secondary)**
- Multi-layered concern (time to implement, time to see results, time commitment)
- Address 12+ different ways
- What they say: "I don't have time," "Maybe next quarter," "I'm too busy right now"

**Tier 3: Belief (Root)**
- THE true barrier: "I can't do this," "I'm not good enough," "I'm deficient in some way"
- Address 19+ different ways
- What they say: "I'm not sure it'll work for ME," "I've tried before," "My situation is different"

### Cycling Strategy

Alternate between price, time, and belief objections rather than drilling one. Prevents audience fatigue. Rather than 3 price closes, do 6. Rather than 4 time responses, do 12.

### Coaching Application

**When coaching a client who can't close sales:** Map their prospect objections to tiers. Most salespeople only address Tier 1 (price) and give up. The real resistance is almost always Tier 3 (belief). Train them to dig deeper: "When they say it's too expensive, that's the surface. Ask what they'd do if it were free. If they hesitate, it was never about price."

**When coaching a client who can't take action:** Their own inaction follows the same tiers. "I don't have time" (Tier 2) usually masks "I don't believe I can pull this off" (Tier 3). Don't solve the time problem -- solve the belief problem.

**Cycling in coaching sessions:** Don't hammer the same reframe. If you addressed the belief once and they didn't shift, come at it from a different angle. Story, then data, then metaphor, then question. Same objection, different approach each time.

## Named Closing Techniques

### Fear of Success Close
"Do you fear failure or success? Most people fear the unknown of winning. You know what failure looks like -- you've survived it. But success? That's uncharted territory."

**Coaching use:** When a client self-sabotages near a breakthrough. They're not afraid of failing -- they're afraid of what changes when they succeed. New expectations, new identity, new problems they don't know how to handle.

### Child Development Close
"You learned to walk, talk, read without formal training -- through curiosity and desire. Those capabilities still exist. You didn't need a course to learn to walk. You needed to want to get somewhere."

**Coaching use:** When a client says "I'm not qualified" or "I need more training before I start." Remind them of their natural learning ability.

### Reverse Frame Close
"I forbid you to make this much money." Makes them prove you wrong.

**Coaching use:** Contrarian challenge. "Don't launch that product. You're probably not ready." Watch them argue for why they ARE ready. They just sold themselves.

### Talk to Strangers Close
Acknowledge childhood conditioning ("don't talk to strangers"), then reframe its expiration date for adults. The safety rule that protected you at 5 is the same rule holding you back at 35.

**Coaching use:** When a client avoids outreach, networking, or sales conversations. Name the conditioning. Make it visible. Then let them decide if a rule designed for a child should govern their adult business.

### Time Dilation Technique
Temporal reframing applied to both future and past perspectives. "In 5 years, will you remember this decision? Will you remember the risk? Or will you only remember what you built?"

**Coaching use:** When a client is paralyzed by a decision that feels huge today but will be insignificant in hindsight. Zoom out the timeline.

### Hot Model Close
"Capable of catapulting income into the next tax bracket." Paint the outcome so vividly it creates desire.

### Michelangelo's David Approach
For countering near-impossible objections. Michelangelo said the sculpture was already in the marble -- he just removed what wasn't David. The client's success is already inside the situation -- coaching just removes what's blocking it.

## Theory of Persuasional Relativity

All value is relative, not absolute. The "Compared to What?" framework:

- **Expensive** compared to what alternative?
- **Lengthy** compared to what sacrifice you've already endured?
- **Difficult** compared to what challenges you've already overcome?

### Key Principles

- Beginners have fresher perspectives than established competitors
- Historical reframing: "If you survived X hardship, current business obstacles diminish by comparison"
- Persuasion works by shifting reference frames, not changing facts

### Coaching Application

**Price objections (client's own or their customers'):** "You're saying $5K is expensive. Compared to what? The $50K in revenue you're leaving on the table every year by not fixing this? The 3 years you've already spent trying to figure it out alone?"

**Client's fear of difficulty:** "You're saying building a sales team sounds hard. You built a 200K subscriber newsletter from zero. That was hard. This is just a different kind of hard -- and you've already proven you can do hard things."

**Help clients use this in their sales:** "Your prospect says your course is expensive. Ask them: compared to what? Compared to the cost of another year stuck at the same revenue? Compared to the free YouTube videos that got them to where they are now -- which is here, still stuck?"

## Beg to Buy Framework

The shift from pushing to pulling. Five steps to make prospects ask YOU to sell them:

1. **Create a Gap** -- Make the disconnect between current state and desired state visible. Emotional experience, not intellectual understanding.
2. **Make the Solution Seem Inevitable** -- Proof + Logic + Social Proof. Not "this might work" but "this is the only logical path."
3. **Add Real Scarcity** -- Genuine constraints only. Honor limits you set. Fake scarcity kills trust permanently.
4. **Remove Friction to Yes** -- Preemptively answer objections, streamline process, offer guarantees.
5. **Position as Guide, Not Hero** -- You are their guide. They are the protagonist. Your success story matters only as proof that the path works.

### Coaching Application

Teach this framework to clients building offers and funnels. The order matters: gap first (they need to FEEL the problem), then inevitability (logic follows emotion), then scarcity (only after desire exists), then friction removal (make it easy when they're ready), then positioning (you're the guide, not the star).

**Common mistake to coach against:** Clients often start with scarcity ("limited time offer!") before establishing the gap. That's manipulation, not persuasion. The gap comes first. Always.

## Dual-Level Communication

Simultaneously teach mechanics (overt) while covertly shifting limiting beliefs.

### How It Works

Every piece of content operates on two levels:
- **Overt level:** The tactical information being taught (how to write a sales email, how to price an offer, how to run ads)
- **Covert level:** The belief shift happening underneath (you ARE capable, this IS achievable, you DON'T need permission)

### Coaching Application

**When a client intellectually agrees but emotionally resists:** The overt teaching isn't working because the covert belief hasn't shifted. Stop teaching tactics. Switch to story, metaphor, or lived experience.

"I could teach you the exact email sequence, but that's not your problem. Your problem is you don't believe anyone would pay you $5K. So let me tell you about the first time someone paid me that. I almost didn't send the invoice..."

**In content creation:** When helping clients build courses, newsletters, or presentations, coach them to embed belief shifts inside tactical content. Every lesson should teach a skill AND dissolve a limiting belief. "Here's how to write a subject line" + "you don't need to be a copywriter to do this well."

## Vulnerability Trust Threshold

Customers (and coaching clients) need approximately 3 hours of interaction before revealing genuine obstacles.

### What This Means

- First session: they tell you the surface problem
- Second session: they reveal more context
- Third session (or after 3+ hours): the real barrier emerges
- Don't mistake the first-session problem statement for the real issue

### Coaching Application

**Don't diagnose too fast.** The problem they bring to session 1 is rarely the real problem. Create enough safety and rapport that the real barrier can surface.

**For clients running sales calls:** "Your 30-minute discovery call isn't long enough to uncover the real objection. If your close rate is low, try extending the call. Give them time to trust you enough to be honest."

**For webinar sellers:** This is why Fladlien's webinars run 3-4+ hours. The real buying happens after the initial resistance wears off and genuine obstacles surface.

## Earned the Right to Sell

The entire content section of any presentation, webinar, or coaching session answers one question: "Have we earned the right to sell?"

### The Principle

You earn the right to prescribe through:
- Demonstrating deep understanding of their problem
- Delivering genuine value before asking for anything
- Proving your methodology works (with evidence, not claims)
- Showing you understand their world better than they expected

### Coaching Application

**Before prescribing action:** Ask yourself -- have you earned the right? Did you listen enough? Did you demonstrate understanding? If you prescribe before earning the right, the client will resist even good advice.

**For clients who sell:** "Your pitch fails because you haven't earned the right to sell yet. You jumped from 'here's what I do' to 'buy this.' The prospect doesn't trust you enough. Earn it first."

**As coaching principle #19:** Earn the right to prescribe before you prescribe. This applies to coaching, sales, content, and offers.

## Selfish Motivation Transparency

"I'm doing this because it benefits me financially." Removes hidden agenda suspicion.

### The Principle

When you're transparent about your incentives, people trust you MORE, not less. Hidden motives create suspicion. Stated motives create clarity.

### Coaching Application

**Model this in coaching:** "I'm going to push you hard on this because when you succeed, you become a case study I can point to. I have skin in the game here."

**Teach clients to use it:** "Tell your customers why you're offering the bonus. 'I'm including 90 days of support because customers who use support get 3x better results, and better results mean better testimonials for me.' That's honest. That's refreshing. That builds trust."
