## Impact Filter

### The 5-Question Tool
Before starting ANY major project, initiative, or goal, complete this:

| # | Question | Your Answer |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **What is the project?** (One sentence) | |
| 2 | **What does success look like?** (Specific, measurable outcome) | |
| 3 | **What does failure look like?** (What to avoid) | |
| 4 | **What is the best result?** (Dream outcome if everything goes perfectly) | |
| 5 | **What is the worst acceptable result?** (Minimum standard, below this = failure) | |

### How to Use It
1. Complete the Impact Filter yourself (this is YOUR job: vision, not execution)
2. Hand it to a WHO (the person who will figure out HOW)
3. Let the WHO design the approach (don't micromanage the How)
4. Check results against #2 and #5 (did they hit success? Is it above the floor?)

### Filled Example: Launch New Product

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| 1 | What is the project? | Launch a premium coaching program for business owners |
| 2 | What does success look like? | 20 founding members at $500/mo within 60 days, 90%+ satisfaction |
| 3 | What does failure look like? | Fewer than 5 members, poor feedback, reputation damage |
| 4 | Best result? | 50 members, waitlist, featured in industry press, 95% satisfaction |
| 5 | Worst acceptable? | 10 members at $500/mo, 80% satisfaction, break-even on launch costs |

### Filled Example: Hire Operations Manager

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| 1 | What is the project? | Hire an operations manager to run daily business |
| 2 | Success? | Hire made within 30 days, fully onboarded in 60, founder doing 50% fewer operational tasks |
| 3 | Failure? | Bad hire, have to fire within 3 months, founder doing MORE work during transition |
| 4 | Best result? | Perfect hire who takes over 80% of operations, introduces better systems, team morale improves |
| 5 | Worst acceptable? | Competent hire who handles 50% of operations reliably within 90 days |

### When to Use the Impact Filter
- Before starting any project that will take more than a week
- Before hiring anyone (define the role's Impact Filter first)
- Before saying yes to a new opportunity (does it align with your 10x Impact Filters?)
- When you're procrastinating on something (the Impact Filter often reveals why)

## Procrastination as Inner Wisdom

### The Reframe
Procrastination is NOT laziness. It's a signal. Listen to it.

### Three Causes of Procrastination

| Cause | Diagnosis | Solution |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Not your job** | "Every time I sit down to do this, I find something else to do", Your unconscious knows this isn't your unique ability | Find a WHO. Delegate it. You're procrastinating because you shouldn't be the one doing it. |
| **Unclear outcome** | "I don't know where to start", The project is too vague | Complete the Impact Filter. Get crystal clear on what success looks like. Then either do it or delegate it. |
| **Fear/identity block** | "I know what to do, I know how to do it, but I keep avoiding it", There's an emotional or identity barrier | This is a future-self problem, not a productivity problem. See `references/be-your-future-self.md` and `references/productivity-psychology.md` for breakthrough techniques. |

### The Procrastination Audit
For every task you've been putting off:

| Task | How Long Delayed? | Cause (Not My Job / Unclear / Fear) | Action |
|------|------------------|-------------------------------------|--------|
| | | | |

### Key Insight
"If you've been procrastinating on something for more than 2 weeks, it's almost certainly not your job. Find a Who."

## Who Mindset Shift

### The Fundamental Question
| Old Question (How) | New Question (Who) |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| "How do I build a website?" | "Who can build a website for me?" |
| "How do I learn accounting?" | "Who already knows accounting?" |
| "How do I scale sales?" | "Who has already scaled sales?" |
| "How do I create content?" | "Who is great at creating content?" |
| "How do I manage this team?" | "Who is a natural team leader?" |

### The Cost of How Thinking
Every time you ask "How do I do this?":
1. You spend time learning (weeks or months)
2. You execute at beginner level (mediocre results)
3. You take time away from your 20% (opportunity cost)
4. You create dependency on yourself (you become the bottleneck)

### The Power of Who Thinking
Every time you ask "Who can do this?":
1. Someone expert does it (better results)
2. You save time (no learning curve)
3. You stay on your 20% (highest-value work)
4. You build a team (scalable, not dependent on you)

### When How IS the Right Question
How thinking is appropriate when:
- It's your unique ability (the 20% only you can do)
- You're in learning mode for a strategic skill you need to OWN
- The task is so small that finding a Who costs more than doing it

### The Rule
"If it takes less than 10 minutes, do it yourself. If it takes more than 10 minutes and isn't your unique ability, find a Who."

## Four Freedoms Audit Mapping

### Map Every Activity to Freedoms

| Activity | Time Freedom (+/-) | Money Freedom (+/-) | Relationship Freedom (+/-) | Purpose Freedom (+/-) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------|
| | | | | |

### Scoring Guide
- **+** = This activity CREATES this freedom
- **-** = This activity DESTROYS this freedom
- **0** = Neutral

### Decision Rules
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 3-4 positives | Keep, this is your 20% |
| 2 positives, 2 neutral | Keep but optimize |
| Mixed (some + some -) | Delegate, let a Who handle it |
| 2+ negatives | Eliminate, this is dragging you down |
| 3-4 negatives | Eliminate IMMEDIATELY, this is poison |

### Freedom Stacking
The best activities create MULTIPLE freedoms simultaneously:
- Teaching creates Purpose + Relationship + Money
- Strategic thinking creates Time + Money + Purpose
- Hiring the right Who creates Time + Money + Relationship

### Anti-Patterns
Activities that destroy multiple freedoms but feel productive:
- Doing all the admin yourself (destroys Time, Purpose)
- Taking on every client (destroys Time, Relationship, Purpose)
- Checking email constantly (destroys Time, Purpose)
- Attending every meeting (destroys Time, Purpose)

## Vision Setting for Collaboration

### How to Work with a Who
The biggest mistake founders make when delegating is micromanaging the How. Here's the right process:

### Step 1: Share the Vision (Impact Filter)
- Give them the completed Impact Filter
- Explain the WHY, not the HOW
- "Here's what success looks like. Here's what failure looks like. How you get there is up to you."

### Step 2: Give Them Autonomy
- Let them design the approach
- Let them make mistakes (within the boundaries of #5: worst acceptable result)
- Check results, not process
- "I don't care how you get there, as long as you hit the target"

### Step 3: Create Feedback Loops
| Cadence | What | How |
|---------|------|-----|
| Daily | Quick check-in (are you stuck?) | 5-min standup or async message |
| Weekly | Progress review | 30-min meeting with numbers |
| Monthly | Strategic review | 1-hour deep dive |
| Quarterly | Big picture | Is this Who working? Reassess |

### Step 4: Celebrate Outcomes
- Celebrate what they achieved, not how they achieved it
- Their way might be different from yours: and it might be BETTER
- "You crushed it. Tell me how you did it: I want to learn from your approach."

### Common Vision-Setting Mistakes
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Telling them HOW to do it | Share the Impact Filter, then step back |
| Checking in too often | Set a cadence and stick to it |
| Fixing their work | Give feedback, let them fix it |
| Taking back the task | If they're struggling, add support (another Who), don't take it back |
| Vague expectations | The Impact Filter exists to prevent this |

## Hiring as Acquisition

### The Mindset
Hiring isn't an expense, it's customer acquisition for your team. Apply the same marketing and sales thinking to recruiting.

### The Funnel

| Marketing Funnel | Hiring Funnel | Application |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| Lead generation | Job distribution | Post on 5+ channels, use recruiters, tap networks |
| Lead magnet | Employer brand | Content about your culture, mission, team |
| Nurture | Candidate engagement | Respond fast, share vision, make them feel wanted |
| Sales call | Interview | You're selling the opportunity, not just evaluating |
| Close | Offer | Make it compelling, not just salary, but vision + growth |
| Onboarding | Employee onboarding | First 90 days, clear goals, fast wins |
| Retention | Culture + growth | Career path, autonomy, recognition, challenging work |

### Volume Approach
- Use multiple recruiting channels simultaneously
- Target: 10+ qualified candidates per role
- More candidates → better selection → better hires
- Speed matters: the best candidates are off the market in 10 days

### Interview Test
Sullivan's interview question: "Can they teach me something new in the interview?"

This tests:
- **Depth of knowledge**: Do they know their craft?
- **Communication ability**: Can they explain complex things simply?
- **Preparation**: Did they research your company?
- **Intellectual curiosity**: Are they a learner?

### Compensation Strategy
| Level | Structure | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Entry | Base + performance bonus | Motivate results, not just attendance |
| Revenue roles | Base + commission/performance | Align incentives with growth |
| Leadership | Base + profit sharing | Align with business outcomes |
| Key executives | Base + equity/ownership | Align with long-term value creation |

### The ROI Frame
Every hire should have a projected ROI:
- "I'm hiring this person at $X/mo. I expect them to generate or enable $Y/mo."
- If ROI isn't clear, the Impact Filter for the role isn't clear enough.
- Track actual ROI at 30, 60, 90 days.
