# Powerful Questions Library

Questions organized by coaching phase. Use one at a time. Don't fill silence, let the question work.

## Usage Rules
1. **One question at a time.** Never stack questions.
2. **Wait after asking.** Count to 10 in your head. The client needs to think.
3. **Match intensity to rapport.** Don't drop a deep identity question in the first 5 minutes.
4. **Follow the energy.** If a question lands, go deeper on that thread. Don't move on to the next planned question.
5. **Questions > answers.** Aim for 3 questions per 1 piece of advice.

---

## Opening Questions

*Use at session start to establish focus and surface what matters most.*

- "What's the most important thing for us to work on today?"
- "Since our last session, what moved? What didn't?"
- "What's taking up the most mental space right now?"
- "If we could only solve one thing today, what would create the most impact?"
- "What did you accomplish that you're proud of since we last talked?"
- "What number are you most excited about right now? Most worried about?"

---

## Deepening Questions

*Use during exploration to get past surface answers and into root causes.*

- "Tell me more about that."
- "What do you mean by [their exact word]?"
- "What does that look like specifically? Give me an example."
- "What's the number?" *(When they give a feeling instead of a metric)*
- "What are you not saying?"
- "What would your team say about this if you weren't in the room?"
- "What's the real reason you haven't done this yet?"
- "If I followed you around for a week, what would I see you spending time on?"
- "What's the assumption underneath that belief?"
- "What would need to be true for that to work?"

---

## Reframing Questions

*Use when the client is stuck in a fixed perspective or limiting belief.*

- "What if the opposite were true?"
- "If everything you believe about this is wrong, what would you do differently?"
- "What would someone who's already solved this tell you?" *(First Principles)*
- "What would guarantee failure here? Are you doing any of those things?" *(Inversion)*
- "What's the smallest version of this that could work?"
- "What would this look like if it were easy?"
- "Is this a problem to solve, or a tension to manage?"
- "You said 'I can't.' Is that literally true, or is it 'I haven't yet'?"
- "What would you advise your best friend to do in this situation?"
- "What's the Gain here? Where were you 90 days ago vs now?" *(Gap/Gain)*

---

## Accountability Questions

*Use when reviewing progress, homework completion, and follow-through.*

- "Did you do what you said you'd do? Yes or no."
- "Show me the numbers."
- "What got in the way?"
- "Was it a priority, or did something else take over?"
- "If you had to bet $10,000 that you'd complete this by next week, would you take the bet? Why or why not?"
- "What would make this a 10 out of 10 commitment instead of a [their number]?"
- "What's the cost of not doing this for another month?"
- "You've told me this three sessions in a row. What's really going on?"
- "What would your future self say about how you're spending your time this week?"

---

## Challenge Questions (Hormozi-style)

*Use when the client needs a push. Direct, blunt, no-BS. Use only when rapport is established.*

- "You're leaving money on the table. What are you going to do about it?"
- "That's a story you're telling yourself. What does the data say?"
- "Is this a real constraint or a comfort zone?"
- "What would Hormozi say if he saw your numbers?"
- "You've been at this revenue level for how long? What's actually going to change?"
- "If I invested $1M in your business, would I be happy with how you're spending your time?"
- "Volume solves most problems. Are you actually doing enough reps?"
- "Stop telling me what you're going to do. What have you DONE?"
- "Is this the #1 constraint, or is this just the easiest thing to work on?"
- "What are you afraid of?"

---

## Constraint Questions (TOC)

*Use when diagnosing bottlenecks, systems issues, or operational problems.*

- "What single thing limits your overall output?" *(5 Focusing Steps - Identify)*
- "Have you fully exploited what you have before spending more money?" *(Exploit)*
- "Is everything else in your business organized to support the constraint?" *(Subordinate)*
- "Where does work pile up in your process?"
- "An hour lost at the bottleneck is an hour lost for the entire system. Where's YOUR bottleneck?"
- "If we removed this step entirely, what would break?" *(Musk 5-Step Algorithm)*
- "Who required this process? Is it still necessary?"
- "What would happen if you did NOTHING about this for 90 days?"
- "You're optimizing a non-constraint. How does that help throughput?"
- "The constraint moved. Where is it now?"

---

## Identity Questions (Hardy/Sullivan)

*Use when the client hits a belief block, self-sabotages, or is stuck despite having the tactics.*

- "Who do you need to BECOME to achieve this goal?"
- "Are you making decisions based on who you were, or who you want to be?"
- "Describe your life 3 years from now if everything goes right. Be specific."
- "What would your future self do in this situation? Do that."
- "What are you tolerating that your future self wouldn't?" *(Floor raising)*
- "Is this a skill gap, a strategy gap, or an identity gap?"
- "What's one thing you did this week that your future self will thank you for?"
- "Which of the 7 Threats is most active for you right now?"
- "Are you measuring yourself against an ideal, or against where you started?" *(Gap/Gain)*
- "What would you attempt if you KNEW you couldn't fail?"
- "You keep saying 'I should.' Your future self says 'I will.' What's the difference?"

---

## State & Needs Questions (Robbins)

*Use when checking state at session start, diagnosing needs driving behavior, or probing values and beliefs. See `references/robbins-diagnostic-tools.md` for full protocols.*

- "How are you showing up right now? If I gave your energy a number 1-10, what is it?"
- "What are you getting from [the behavior you want to change]? What does it give you?"
- "Which matters more to you right now -- certainty or growth? Connection or significance?"
- "What would you need to believe for this to feel easy?"
- "If this belief cost you everything in 20 years, would you still hold onto it?"
- "What has to happen for you to feel successful? Is that rule serving you?"
- "What are you most afraid of losing right now?"
- "When do you feel most alive?"

---

## Closing Questions

*Use at the end of a session to lock in commitments and maintain momentum.*

- "What are your 3 action items? When will each be done?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to each action?"
- "What would make that commitment a 10?"
- "What could get in the way, and how will you handle it?"
- "What's the ONE thing that, if you did it this week, would make everything else easier?"
- "How will you know you succeeded?"
- "What do you want me to hold you accountable to next session?"
- "What was the most useful insight from today?"
- "Looking back at this session, what's the Gain?"
