# Hardy/Sullivan Framework

The fourth pillar of the business coaching system, integrating five books by Dr. Benjamin Hardy and Dan Sullivan:
1. *The Science of Scaling* (Hardy): Frame/Floor/Focus
2. *10x Is Easier Than 2x* (Hardy & Sullivan): Quality over quantity thinking
3. *Who Not How* (Hardy & Sullivan): Delegation and collaboration
4. *The Gap and the Gain* (Hardy & Sullivan): Backward measurement
5. *Be Your Future Self Now* (Hardy): Identity and psychology of growth

## Frame/Floor/Focus (Science of Scaling)

### The Three Levers
| Lever | Definition | Coaching Question |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Frame** | Set an impossible goal that redefines what success looks like | "What goal would change everything if you achieved it, even if it feels impossible right now?" |
| **Floor** | Raise your minimum acceptable standard | "What are you currently tolerating that your future self wouldn't accept?" |
| **Focus** | Narrow to 1-3 pathways that matter | "If you could only work on one thing for the next 90 days, what would create the most leverage?" |

### Application Process
1. **Set the Frame**: Define the impossible goal (10x, not 2x). Make it specific and time-bound.
2. **Raise the Floor**: Identify every area where standards are too low. Raise the minimum. Cut anything below the new floor.
3. **Sharpen the Focus**: Eliminate 80% of activities. Double down on 1-3 pathways.

### Time Compression Principle
When you set a bigger goal, you compress the timeline. A 10x goal pursued with full focus often takes LESS time than a 2x goal pursued with scattered effort.

### Scaling Through Subtraction
Growth comes from elimination, not addition. Every "yes" to something unimportant is a "no" to something that matters.

### Case Studies
- **Mark Young:** $50M in 6 weeks by narrowing from many services to one Frame/Floor/Focus pathway
- **Alicia Ault:** 10 to 8,000 units in 12 months by raising standards (Floor) and focusing distribution (Focus)
- **Xavier Martine:** 2x revenue in 90 days by eliminating 80% of services and focusing on highest-margin offering

## 10x vs 2x Thinking

### The Core Distinction
| 2x Thinking | 10x Thinking |
|-------------|-------------|
| Do more of the same, a little better | Fundamentally reimagine the approach |
| Incremental improvement | Quantum leap |
| Add more activities | Eliminate 80% of activities |
| Work harder | Work on completely different things |
| Feels safe but draining | Feels scary but energizing |
| Linear progress | Exponential progress |

### The 80/20 Elimination Principle
To 10x, you must identify and ELIMINATE the 80% of activities, clients, products, and habits that produce only 20% of results. This isn't optimization, it's radical subtraction.

**The exercise:**
1. List all activities, revenue streams, clients, commitments
2. Identify the 20% producing 80% of results/revenue/satisfaction
3. Eliminate or delegate the other 80%
4. Pour freed-up resources into the 20%

### Four Freedoms (from Sullivan)
| Freedom | Definition | Coaching Question |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Freedom of Time** | Control over your schedule | "How many Free Days did you take last month?" |
| **Freedom of Money** | Financial abundance beyond needs | "At what revenue level would money stop being a constraint?" |
| **Freedom of Relationship** | Surround yourself with energizing people | "Which relationships drain you? Which elevate you?" |
| **Freedom of Purpose** | Meaningful, aligned work | "If money weren't an issue, would you still do this?" |

### Buyer vs Seller Mindset
| Seller Mindset | Buyer Mindset |
|---------------|---------------|
| Trades time for money | Invests money to buy back time |
| Does everything themselves | Hires Whos to do the Hows |
| Sees expenses as costs | Sees expenses as investments |
| Protective, scarcity-driven | Expansive, abundance-driven |

## Who Not How

### The Core Shift
When you have a goal, don't ask "How do I do this?" Ask "WHO can do this for me?"

### Impact Filter (5-Question Tool)
For every project or goal, complete this before starting:

1. **What is the project?** (One sentence)
2. **What does success look like?** (Specific, measurable outcome)
3. **What does failure look like?** (What to avoid)
4. **What is the best result?** (Dream outcome if everything goes perfectly)
5. **What is the worst result?** (Minimum acceptable outcome)

**How to use it:** Complete the Impact Filter, then hand it to a Who. The Who figures out the How. Your job is vision and standards, not execution.

### Procrastination as Inner Wisdom
Procrastination on a task doesn't mean you're lazy. It means one of three things:
1. **You shouldn't be doing it**: It's not your unique ability. Find a Who.
2. **You're not clear on the outcome**: Complete the Impact Filter first.
3. **You're afraid of the outcome**: This is an identity/belief block (use Future Self techniques).

### The Four Freedoms Audit
Map every activity to a Freedom quadrant:

| Activity | Creates/Destroys which Freedom? | Keep / Delegate / Eliminate |
|----------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|
| [activity] | Time: +/- | |
| [activity] | Money: +/- | |
| [activity] | Relationship: +/- | |
| [activity] | Purpose: +/- | |

Activities that destroy multiple freedoms → eliminate immediately.
Activities that create multiple freedoms → these are your 20%.

### Vision Setting for Collaboration
When working with a Who:
1. Share the Impact Filter (what success looks like)
2. Give them autonomy on the How
3. Check results, not process
4. Celebrate outcomes, not effort

## Gap and Gain

### The Core System
| Concept | Definition | Effect |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **The Gap** | Measuring yourself against an ideal you haven't reached | Creates dissatisfaction, anxiety, feeling of failure |
| **The Gain** | Measuring yourself against where you started | Creates confidence, momentum, gratitude |

**The Ideal is a tool, not a measure.** Use ideals to set direction. Measure progress backward.

### Backward Measurement Protocol
1. **Set a baseline**: Document where you are RIGHT NOW (revenue, skills, relationships, health)
2. **At each review point**, measure backward: "Where was I 90 days ago? Where am I now?"
3. **Never measure against the ideal**: The ideal always moves. You'll always feel behind.
4. **Track the trajectory**: The slope of your growth matters more than your current position

### Three Wins Daily Practice
Every day, before anything else, write down:
1. **Win #1:** Something you accomplished yesterday
2. **Win #2:** Something you learned yesterday
3. **Win #3:** Something you're grateful for from yesterday

**Benefits:**
- Trains the brain to notice progress (Gain thinking)
- Builds momentum and confidence
- Prevents the Gap trap (feeling like nothing is enough)
- Takes 3-5 minutes but transforms psychology over time

### Experience Transformation
Every experience, especially setbacks, can be reframed as a Gain:
- "I lost that client" → "I learned what doesn't work in onboarding, and my next 10 clients will benefit"
- "Revenue dropped 20%" → "I discovered which revenue streams are fragile and which are resilient"
- "I failed at that launch" → "I got 6 months of learning in 2 weeks"

### Gap/Gain Check for Coaching Sessions
Add this to every session review:
1. "Looking back at where you were 90 days ago, what's the Gain?"
2. "Are you measuring yourself against an impossible ideal, or against your actual progress?"
3. "What's one setback from this period that you can reframe as a Gain?"

## Future Self

### Core Concept
Your current actions are driven by your identity, who you believe you are. To change behavior, change identity first.

### The 7 Threats to Your Future Self
| # | Threat | Coaching Diagnosis |
|---|--------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Having an identity that's tied to your past** | "Are you making decisions based on who you WERE or who you want to BECOME?" |
| 2 | **Having negative narratives about your past** | "What stories do you tell yourself about past failures? Are they serving you?" |
| 3 | **Being unaware of your environment's influence** | "Describe your daily environment. Does it support or sabotage your goals?" |
| 4 | **Lacking a clear and compelling future self** | "Describe your life 3 years from now in vivid detail. Can you?" |
| 5 | **Not being connected to your future self emotionally** | "When you picture your future self, do you feel excited or disconnected?" |
| 6 | **Not prioritizing your future self** | "What did you do THIS WEEK that your future self will thank you for?" |
| 7 | **Not being in an environment that supports your future self** | "What in your environment needs to change to become who you want to be?" |

### Needing → Wanting → Knowing Ladder
| Level | Mindset | Coaching Approach |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Needing** | "I need to succeed", desperation, scarcity | Address the scarcity, build foundation, stabilize |
| **Wanting** | "I want to succeed", desire, ambition | Channel ambition into focused action, use frameworks |
| **Knowing** | "I know I will succeed", certainty, identity | This person takes action from identity, not motivation. They've already decided. |

The goal of coaching is to move clients from Needing → Wanting → Knowing.

### Integration with Business Coaching
- **Before goal-setting:** "Who do you need to BECOME to achieve this goal?"
- **When stuck:** "Is this a skill problem, a strategy problem, or an identity problem?"
- **When self-sabotaging:** "What threat is active right now?" (Reference 7 Threats)
- **When measuring progress:** Use Gap/Gain, not ideal comparison

## Integration Map

How Hardy/Sullivan maps to existing frameworks:

| Situation | Hormozi Framework | TOC Framework | Hardy/Sullivan Framework |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Revenue is too low | Value Equation, Grand Slam Offer | Identify the constraint | Frame (set impossible goal), Focus (eliminate 80%) |
| Client is doing too much | More → Better → New | Subordinate to the constraint | 80/20 Elimination, Who Not How |
| Client can't delegate | First 5 Hires, Phone Test | Operations constraint | Who Not How (Impact Filter), Four Freedoms Audit |
| Client feels like a failure despite growth |, |, | Gap/Gain (backward measurement, Three Wins) |
| Client knows what to do but won't do it | Accountability rules |, | Future Self (7 Threats, identity shift), Procrastination diagnosis |
| Client is burned out | Daily Priority Rule | Drum-Buffer-Rope | Four Freedoms Audit, Free Days system |
| Client wants to 10x | 9 Stages of Growth | Evaporating Cloud (resolve trade-offs) | 10x vs 2x thinking, Frame/Floor/Focus |
| Client hit a belief/identity block |, |, | Future Self + Breakthrough Techniques |

**Key principle:** Use Hormozi for the tactics (what to do), TOC for the systems (where to focus), and Hardy/Sullivan for the psychology (who to become).
