# LinkedIn About Section Frameworks

Three proven frameworks for writing About sections that convert profile visitors into leads. Pick the one that fits your style and proof points.

## Hard Constraints (All Frameworks)

- **2,600 characters max** (LinkedIn limit)
- **First 300 characters are critical** -- visible before the "see more" fold. This is your hook. If they don't click, nothing else matters.
- **Open with the client's pain, NOT your credentials.** Nobody cares about your journey until they know you understand theirs.
- **End with ONE clear CTA.** Not three. One. (DM me, book a call, download X)
- **Short paragraphs.** 1-3 sentences max. White space between every thought. LinkedIn is mobile-first -- walls of text get skipped.
- **No corporate speak.** Write like you talk. "I help" not "I facilitate the optimization of."
- **Include proof.** Numbers, timeframes, client types. Specificity is credibility.

## Framework 1: Hook-Story-Offer (HSO)

Best for: people with a compelling origin story or transformation. Works when your personal journey IS the proof.

### Structure

**HOOK (first 3 lines, under 300 chars)**
Bold claim, provocative question, or startling stat that makes your ideal client stop scrolling. This must speak directly to their situation.

**STORY (middle section)**
Your origin story, told through the lens of the problem you now solve. Keep it short. Not your whole life -- just the turning point. Why should they trust you? Because you've been where they are.

**OFFER (final section)**
What you do, who you do it for, proof it works, and exactly what to do next.

### Template

```
[BOLD OPENING LINE ABOUT THEIR PAIN/DESIRE]

[Second line that deepens the hook -- specific, emotional, relatable]

[Third line that hints at a solution or transformation -- earns the "see more" click]

---

[1-2 paragraphs: Your story. How you got here. The struggle, the breakthrough, the lesson. Keep it under 400 chars.]

[1 paragraph: The realization that led you to do what you do now.]

---

Here's what I do now:

I help [WHO] [achieve OUTCOME] using [METHOD/FRAMEWORK].

[2-3 bullet points of proof]:
- [Specific result: "47 coaches added $10K+/mo in 90 days"]
- [Social proof: "500+ clients across 12 countries"]
- [Credibility: "Built and sold 2 companies / Featured in X"]

---

[CTA: "Want the same? DM me 'READY' and I'll send you [specific thing]."]
```

### Example (Coach)

```
Most coaches are one bad month away from going back to a job.

Not because they're bad at coaching. Because nobody taught them how to sell without feeling like a used car salesman.

I know because I was that coach. $2K months. Panic every time a client didn't renew.

Then I figured out a system. Not a hack. A repeatable way to sign 3-5 clients per month without cold DMs, without webinars, without spending a dime on ads.

In 18 months I went from $2K to $27K months. Now I teach other coaches to do the same.

What I do:

I help coaches build a client acquisition system that runs on LinkedIn content + DMs.

The results:
- 200+ coaches through my program
- Average result: 4 new clients in the first 60 days
- 93% of clients hit their first $10K month within 90 days

DM me "SYSTEM" and I'll send you the 3-step framework free.
```

## Framework 2: Problem-Proof-Path (PPP)

Best for: data-driven people, consultants, anyone with strong case studies. Works when the RESULTS are your best sales tool.

### Structure

**PROBLEM (first 3 lines, under 300 chars)**
Name the exact problem your ideal client is struggling with. Be so specific they think you're reading their mind.

**PROOF (middle section)**
Show -- don't tell -- that you've solved this problem. Case studies, numbers, before/after. Let the evidence sell.

**PATH (final section)**
Here's what working with you looks like. Remove the mystery. Make the next step obvious and low-risk.

### Template

```
[NAME THEIR EXACT PROBLEM -- specific, painful, urgent]

[Deepen it: what this problem is costing them (money, time, sanity)]

[Hint that there's a fix most people miss]

---

Here's what I've seen work:

[Case study 1: "[Client type] went from [BEFORE] to [AFTER] in [TIME]"]
[Case study 2: "[Client type] was stuck at [PROBLEM]. Now [RESULT]."]
[Case study 3: "[NUMBER] [client type] have used [METHOD] to [OUTCOME]"]

The pattern? [One sentence explaining what these clients have in common]

---

How it works:

1. [Step 1 of your process -- simple, clear]
2. [Step 2 -- what happens next]
3. [Step 3 -- the result they get]

No [common objection]. No [second objection]. Just [the outcome].

---

[CTA: "If you're a [WHO] stuck at [PROBLEM], DM me 'FIX' -- I'll tell you if I can help."]
```

## Framework 3: Letter to the Reader (LTR)

Best for: empathetic communicators, people who connect through vulnerability. Works when trust and relatability are your edge.

### Structure

**OPENING (first 3 lines, under 300 chars)**
Speak directly to the reader in second person. "You" language. Make them feel seen.

**BODY (middle section)**
Alternate between validating their experience and showing you understand what they've tried. Build trust through specificity.

**CLOSE (final section)**
Pivot from "I understand you" to "here's what I can do about it." CTA.

### Template

```
If you're a [WHO] who [SPECIFIC SITUATION], this is for you.

[Second line: validate their experience. Something they feel but don't say out loud.]

[Third line: name what they've tried that hasn't worked]

---

You've probably [tried THING 1]. Maybe [THING 2].

And it's not because you're doing it wrong. It's because [INSIGHT -- the real reason it's not working, something non-obvious].

Here's what nobody tells [WHO]:

[2-3 short paragraphs with your key insight. The thing that changes everything. Written as if you're talking to one person over coffee.]

---

I'm [NAME]. I [WHAT YOU DO] for [WHO].

[1-2 proof points, stated simply]

If any of this hit home, [CTA -- something easy and low-commitment].
```

## Choosing Your Framework

| Your Strength | Best Framework | Why |
|---------------|---------------|-----|
| Compelling personal story | **HSO (Hook-Story-Offer)** | Your transformation IS the proof |
| Strong case studies / data | **PPP (Problem-Proof-Path)** | Let the numbers sell for you |
| Empathy / connection | **LTR (Letter to the Reader)** | Trust-first, especially for sensitive niches |

**Hybrid approach:** You can mix elements. Use PPP's proof section inside HSO's structure. Use LTR's opening inside PPP. The frameworks are guardrails, not prison walls.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Kills Conversions | Fix |
|---------|------------------------|-----|
| Opening with "With 15 years of experience..." | Nobody cares about you until they know you understand them | Open with THEIR pain, not YOUR resume |
| No CTA | They read it, nod, and leave. No conversion. | End with ONE specific next step |
| Wall of text | Mobile users see a block and bounce | Max 3 sentences per paragraph, white space between all |
| Too many CTAs | Confusion kills action | Pick ONE: DM, book call, or download. Not all three. |
| Generic claims | "I help people succeed" -- at what? for whom? | Replace every vague word with a specific: who, what, how much, how fast |
| Humble-bragging | "I was lucky enough to..." -- just state the result | Be direct: "I did X. Here's the result." |
| Keyword stuffing | "Coach \| Mentor \| Speaker \| Author \| Podcast Host" | Pick your primary role. One identity. |
