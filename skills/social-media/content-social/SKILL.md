# Content Social

Creates organic social media content across platforms using a 3-tier strategy: regular daily content, value bombs (breakthrough content), and awareness bridge content (conversion-path). Drives traffic to email list for digital product funnels.

## Prerequisites

- Product idea brief from `niche-finder` (niche, Top 10 problems, Top 10 outcomes)
- Offer details from `product-offer` (product name, price, key benefits)
- User's active social platforms and posting frequency
- Email opt-in URL (newsletter landing page or EEC signup)

**Check for product marketing context first:** If private product-marketing context notes exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered.

## Core Rules

**Content without a CTA to your email list is wasted content.** Social media is the traffic driver. Email is where sales happen. Every piece of content must move people toward the email list.

**The Coffee Shop Rule (ALL content).** If it feels weird to say it to a friend sitting across a cafe table, it will feel weird in your post. Every caption, thread, script, and email must sound like a conversation, not a presentation. Read it aloud. If it sounds performative, rewrite until it sounds like you're talking to one person over coffee. Connection beats perfection. Always.

**Points need paint.** Every major point needs an analogy, metaphor, story, or example. Dry tips get scrolled past. A good analogy stops the thumb. "AI is like teaching a kid what a dog is" lands. "AI trains algorithms on datasets" doesn't.

**Sheridan Scene Format (Alternative for Personal/BTS Posts).** For behind-the-scenes and personal story posts, consider the Sheridan scene format: one scene, one character reveal, one question. No lessons. No tips. Drop into a moment (The Window), show what someone did (The Reveal), ask the question you're still sitting with (The Question). 3-5 lines max. End with a question, not a takeaway. Works best for posts about trade-offs, hard choices, and things that didn't go as planned. Full framework: `skills/storyteller/references/sheridan-framework.md`.

## The 3-Tier Content System

This is the backbone of the entire content strategy. Not all content serves the same purpose.

### Tier 1: Regular Content (Workhorse)

Daily traffic driving, audience growth, brand awareness. 5-7x/week. Standard CTA rotation.

This fills the calendar and drives daily impressions. It's the content that keeps you visible.

### Tier 2: Value Bombs (Standout)

Breakthrough content that proves your methodology, builds authority, generates shares. 1-2x/week. Soft CTAs.

This is what separates you from generic creators. The content people screenshot, share, and remember. Each value bomb should be 3-5x the effort of regular content and good enough to be a lead magnet on its own.

### Tier 3: Awareness Bridge (Strategic)

Conversion-path content that moves warm audience toward buying decisions. 1-2x/week. Progression CTAs that escalate with awareness level.

This bridges "I follow this person" to "I need to work with this person."

**Detailed frameworks for all 3 tiers:** See [references/content-frameworks.md](references/content-frameworks.md)

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context

Ask for what's missing:

**Goals:** Primary objective (brand awareness, leads, traffic, community), desired action, personal vs company brand.

**Audience:** Who are you trying to reach? What platforms are they active on? What content do they engage with?

**Voice:** What's your tone? Any topics to avoid? Terminology or style guidelines?

**Resources:** Time available for social, existing content to repurpose, video capability.

### Step 2: Choose Primary Platforms

Pick 2 platforms max. Master those before adding more.

| Content Type | Primary | Secondary |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| Text-based | X (Twitter), LinkedIn | Medium (republishing) |
| Short-form video | Instagram Reels, TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
| Long-form video | YouTube | Repurpose clips everywhere |

**Platform-specific strategies:** See [references/platforms.md](references/platforms.md)

### Step 3: Extract Content Topics

Mine the product's problem/outcome framework:

**From Top 10 Problems:** Name the problem, explain why it happens, myth-bust, give a quick tip.
**From Top 10 Outcomes:** Paint the desired state, show what's possible, share the path.

Each problem = 4+ pieces. Each outcome = 3+ pieces. **10 problems + 10 outcomes = 70+ content ideas minimum.**

### Step 4: Build Content Pillars

Organize around 3-5 pillars that align with expertise and audience:

| Pillar | Typical % | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Industry insights | 30% | Trends, data, predictions |
| Behind-the-scenes | 25% | Building the company, lessons learned |
| Educational | 25% | How-tos, frameworks, tips |
| Personal | 15% | Stories, values, hot takes |
| Promotional | 5% | Product updates, offers |

For each pillar: What's your unique perspective? What questions does your audience ask? What content has performed well? What can you create consistently?

### Step 5: Create the Content Calendar

**Weekly cadence (text platforms):**
- **Monday:** Framework/educational (teach a concept from your product)
- **Tuesday:** Story/personal experience (relate to a problem you solved)
- **Wednesday:** Myth-busting (common mistake in your niche)
- **Thursday:** Quick tip/actionable advice (immediate value)
- **Friday:** Social proof or case study (results, testimonials)
- **Weekend:** Engagement post (question, poll, conversation starter)

Weave Tier 2 and Tier 3 content into this rhythm (1-2 of each per week).

**Approach to scheduling:** Post, measure, iterate. Each post teaches you something for the next one. Stay nimble. If a post gets comments, double down with a follow-up from a different angle. If nobody engages, kill that angle and try a different one.

### 7-Day Story Sequence Strategy

When building multi-day campaigns (IG Stories, email drips, social series), use this strategic arc. Each day builds on the last. Don't skip steps or the audience isn't primed for the offer.

| Day | Purpose | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | Pain point callout | "Still manually doing X at 2am? Your [current solution] probably sleeping while leads ghost." |
| 2 | Case study proof | "This [niche] brand went from [old metric] to [new metric] with [your solution] in [timeframe]." |
| 3 | Objection handle | "Yes it works for [their niche]. No one can tell it's AI. Here's a real convo." |
| 4 | Urgency/speed | "Just had 3 [results] happen while I was sleeping. Your [current solution] can't do that." |
| 5 | Social proof stack | "[Number] [results] last month from [channel] alone." |
| 6 | Direct offer | "DM me '[KEYWORD]' and I'll show you how it works." |
| 7 | Scarcity close | "Only taking [N] new clients this week then closing doors." |

This arc works for any offer. Swap the specifics. The emotional progression (pain > proof > trust > urgency > action) is what matters. For detailed day-by-day breakdowns with formulas and platform notes, see `references/adrian-virality-data.md`.

### Step 6: Write the Posts

**Emotional Hook Rule (MANDATORY for ALL platforms):** The first 1-2 sentences of every post must trigger a specific emotion. Before writing, pick your target: WOW, WTF, OMG, LOL, UGH, WHOA, NAH, YEP, OOF, HMM, DAMN, SMH. On social media, you CAN use the abbreviation literally as the opening word. In newsletters and long-form, evoke the feeling without the abbreviation. If the hook doesn't make the reader feel something, rewrite it. See `skills/humanizer/SKILL.md` for the full list.

**LinkedIn Pre-Flight (run before finalizing any LinkedIn post):**
1. Who reads this and thinks "this is for me"? If the answer is "everyone," rewrite the hook.
2. What changes after they read it? They must be able to DO something or SEE something differently. If neither, it's filler.
3. Which number should move? Saves, comments, profile visits, link clicks. Pick one. Write toward it.
4. Does this build trust or just fill the feed? If you can't answer "trust," kill the post.
5. Reader test: Remove your name from the post. Does it still deliver value? If not, rewrite. (Source: Ruben Hassid)

See also: fail conditions in `references/platforms.md` (LinkedIn section).

**Video content (60s+):** Use the Creator Video Framework from `skills/video-scriptwriter/SKILL.md`: HOOK > BRAG > VALUE BOMB > INTEGRATION > PAYOFF. Use the South Park "but/therefore" principle. Never "and then... and then..."

**Video delivery (all video content including Reels, TikTok, Shorts):**
- **Coffee shop mode:** Script like you're talking to a friend across a table. If a line sounds like a presentation, kill it.
- **Connect, don't perfect:** Focus on connecting with the viewer, not impressing them. Stiffness kills short-form even faster than long-form.
- **Emotional clarity:** Before recording, name the ONE feeling you want the viewer to have. Inspired? Curious? Validated? Every delivery choice filters through that.
- **Paint every point:** If you're teaching something, use an analogy or example. Viewers can't re-read. If they zone out, they swipe.
- **Warm up first:** 2-3 minutes of body/voice warm-up before recording. Cold starts = stiff delivery = low watch time.

**Platform-specific writing:**

**X/Twitter:** The virality engine. X has the highest organic reach potential for text content. See [references/x-twitter.md](references/x-twitter.md) for the full virality framework, 9 levers, 16 techniques, hook formulas, thread structure, and engagement mechanics.

**LinkedIn:** Hook gets exactly 2 lines (~55 chars each, ~110 total) before "...more". This is a platform constraint. Hook line (scroll-stopping first sentence). 3-5 short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each). Bullet/numbered lists for key points. CTA at end (comment keyword or direct to bio). No external links in post body (kills reach). See [references/post-templates.md](references/post-templates.md) for LinkedIn viral patterns and templates.

**Instagram:** Hook in first line (shows in preview). 3-5 paragraphs of value with line breaks. CTA: "Link in bio" or "DM me [keyword]". 3-5 relevant hashtags. Reels get 2x reach of static posts.

### Reels Scripting Formula (Virality Data)

Not all hooks are equal. Here's what actually performs based on analysis of 100+ Reels:

**Viral patterns (100k+ views avg):**
- **Specific number hook:** "I've sent 847,000 DMs and here's the only opener that works" > 340k avg views
- **Controversy/hot take:** "Everyone says [platform] is dead. They're poor. Here's why." > 280k avg views
- **"Everyone's wrong" variant:** "Here's why everyone's wrong about [topic]" > 310k avg views
- **Before/after transformation:** Show old metrics, explain what changed, show new metrics > 250k avg views

**Flop patterns (<10k views avg):**
- Generic motivational content > 4k avg views
- No hook in first 3 seconds > 3k avg views
- Too polished/produced (feels corporate) > 5k avg views
- Talking about yourself instead of the viewer > 2k avg views

The pattern is clear: specific beats generic, controversy beats consensus, transformation beats theory. Lead with a number or a hot take. Never lead with "In this video I'll show you..."

### Step 7: Create Value Bomb Content (Tier 2)

**7 Value Bomb Themes** (rotate through):
1. **The Counterintuitive Truth** -- Challenges a widely-held belief with evidence
2. **The Framework Reveal** -- Shares a proprietary framework with real numbers
3. **The Behind-the-Scenes** -- Shows your actual results, process, or data
4. **The Mistake Autopsy** -- Breaks down a failure and what you learned
5. **The Speed Run** -- Shows how to get a result faster than expected
6. **The Resource Drop** -- Gives away a genuinely useful template/tool/resource
7. **The Proof Stack** -- Compiles multiple proof points into one piece

Each value bomb must include proof (data, screenshot, testimonial, before/after) and end with a soft CTA (story-close, resource-drop, or DM-trigger).

8. **The Lead Magnet Drop** -- Give away a high-value free resource via comment-gated distribution

This combines Tier 2 (value bomb) + Tier 3 (awareness bridge) in one post. It delivers value AND captures leads simultaneously.

**The flow:**
1. Create a high-value resource ($20-50 perceived value): PDF, template pack, prompt library, cheat sheet
2. Post announcing it with a curiosity hook + value preview (bullet what's inside)
3. CTA: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the link"
4. Reply to every comment with a landing page link that requires email signup to download
5. New subscribers join your newsletter automatically

**Why comment-gating works:** Comments trigger the algorithm more than likes. Each comment extends reach to the commenter's network. The keyword reply gives you tracking and creates visible social proof ("400 people already grabbed this").

**Post structure:** Hook (what the resource is + why it matters) > Value preview (3-5 bullets of what's inside) > Social proof if available ("2,500+ people downloaded the last one") > CTA ("Comment [KEYWORD] below")

**Benchmarks per drop:** 500+ downloads, 200+ email subs, 40%+ landing page signup rate. Top performers hit 91% opt-in with single-CTA landing pages.

**Type-specific hooks for your drop post:**
- **Type 1 (Reveal a Problem):** Hook with the painful truth. "Most [audience] are making these [N] mistakes and don't even know it. I built a free [audit/checklist/scorecard] that shows you exactly where you're bleeding."
- **Type 2 (Trial of the Solution):** Hook with the result. "I [did the thing] for free for 50 people last week. Here's what happened." Or: "Want to see what [result] looks like for YOUR [business/situation]? I built a free tool that shows you."
- **Type 3 (One Step of a Multi-Step):** Hook with the shortcut. "This one [template/calculator/tool] handles the hardest part of [process]. It normally takes [time]. This does it in [shorter time]."

For the full lead magnet taxonomy, see `free-tool-strategy` SKILL.md.

**Ideation:** Use the 4 pre-validated approaches from `free-tool-strategy` (Expand a proven post, Compress a deep asset, Replicate from adjacent niche, Unbundle your paid offer). Never cold-guess what to build.

### Step 8: Create Awareness Bridge Content (Tier 3)

Maps to 5 awareness levels: Unaware > Problem Aware > Solution Aware > Product Aware > Most Aware.

Never jump levels. Meet the audience where they are and move them forward one level at a time. CTAs progress from no-CTA/story-close (Unaware) to direct CTA (Most Aware).

**Detailed awareness mapping and CTA progression:** See [references/content-frameworks.md](references/content-frameworks.md)

### Step 9: Write CTA Variations

Don't use the same CTA every time. Rotate through these patterns:

**Standard CTAs (Tier 1):**
1. **Direct:** "I teach this in my free [EEC name]. Grab it: [link]"
2. **Soft:** "I write about [topic] weekly in my newsletter. Link in bio."
3. **Social proof:** "Join [X] others who are learning [outcome]. [link]"
4. **Curiosity:** "I break this down step-by-step in my free 5-day email course. [link]"
5. **Value-add:** "Want the complete framework? I put it in a free guide: [link]"

**Soft CTAs (Tier 2 value bombs):**
6. **Story-close:** End with personal reflection, no explicit link
7. **Resource-drop:** "I put the full [template/guide/checklist] in my newsletter. Link in bio."
8. **DM-trigger:** "DM me '[keyword]' and I'll send you the full breakdown."
9. **Application-link:** "If this resonates, I'm taking 5 people through [program]. Apply: [link]"
10. **Wait-list:** "Building something around this. Drop a comment if you want early access."

**Comment-gated demo format:** Personal story hook > introduce the tool as hero (not you) > show the result with numbers > CTA: "Write [keyword] in comments, I'll send you access." This drives comments (algorithm loves it) and captures leads. Don't put links in posts.

#### When to Use Pipeline Mode

Invoke this as a standalone workflow when the user says "repurpose this," provides a transcript/blog/newsletter and asks for social content, or wants to maximize distribution from a single piece.

#### Input

One pillar content piece:
- YouTube video transcript
- Blog post or article
- Newsletter issue
- Podcast episode transcript
- Webinar recording transcript
- Long-form social post that performed well

#### Process

**Step A: Extract Key Insights**
Read the source and pull out 5-7 distinct insights, stories, or interesting points. Each must stand alone -- a reader shouldn't need the source to get value.

For each insight, capture:
- The core idea (1 sentence)
- The best quote or data point
- The emotional trigger (what makes this interesting?)
- The angle (contrarian, proof, framework, story, hot take)

**Step B: Generate Platform-Specific Content**

Each piece must take a DIFFERENT angle from the source. No redundancy across outputs.

**X/Twitter Posts (5-8)**
- Mix of standalone tweets and long-form tweets
- Each stands alone -- no "1/5" threads unless the user requests a thread
- Hook-first: lead with the most interesting point
- Heavy line breaks for readability
- Max 500 words for long-form tweets
- No hashtags unless user's style includes them

**LinkedIn Posts (3)**
- Different angle each (e.g., framework, story, hot take)
- Strong first line (shows before "see more" -- 110 chars max)
- 150-300 words each
- End with question or insight, not hard sell
- No external links in post body

**Email Sections (2)**
- Sections that slot into a newsletter, not full emails
- Problem-solution structure
- Short paragraphs, no fluff
- Natural CTA transition
- 150-250 words each

**Ad Hooks (10)**
- Derived from key insights
- Categorized: curiosity, problem-agitation, result-first, social proof, controversy
- 1-2 sentences each, written to be spoken aloud
- Include recommended format (UGC / static / talking head)

**Short-Form Video Scripts (2)**
- 30-60 seconds, based on two most compelling insights
- Structure: Hook (0-3s) > Context (3-10s) > Insight (10-25s) > CTA (25-30s)
- UGC/talking head format
- Include visual notes for each section

**Step C: Quality Gate**

Run every piece through the Flop Prevention Checklist (Step 11 below) before including in the package. Any piece that fails 2+ checks gets rewritten.

## Engagement Strategy

### Daily Engagement Routine (30 min)
1. Respond to all comments on your posts (5 min)
2. Comment on 5-10 posts from target accounts (15 min)
3. Share/repost with added insight (5 min)
4. Send 2-3 DMs to new connections (5 min)

Quality comments: Add new insight, share a related experience, ask a thoughtful follow-up, or respectfully disagree with nuance. Never "Great post!"

### Brunson Storytelling + Relationship Building

For Attractive Character content categories (backstory, parables, flaws, polarity), HSO post structure scaled by platform, and the Dream 100 warm-up strategy, see [references/brunson-frameworks.md](references/brunson-frameworks.md). Use AC elements to plan WHAT to post, HSO to structure HOW to write it, Dream 100 to grow WITHOUT ads.

### Pop Culture Hooks

Lead with what's trending (new Netflix show, viral moment, cultural event), bridge to your topic. Fresh references beat stale ones. Check what's trending before writing.

### Hero = Tool Pattern

The tool did the transformation, not generic advice. Wrong: "Stop watching Netflix and be productive." Right: "I knew I shouldn't watch Netflix. Knowing didn't help. I set up this bot and it helped. Here's what happened." The founder's personal story is the SETUP. The tool is the HERO.

**Hassid corollary:** Personal achievement content ("I hit 770K followers!") doesn't grow accounts. The tool is the hero, the reader's problem is the stage. Achievement is context, never the main event. If your post is about YOUR milestone, it's content for you, not for them. (Source: Ruben Hassid, 770K LinkedIn followers)

## Analytics & Optimization

### Metrics That Matter

**Awareness:** Impressions, reach, follower growth rate
**Engagement:** Engagement rate, comments (higher value than likes), shares/reposts, saves
**Conversion:** Link clicks, profile visits, DMs received, leads attributed

### Weekly Review

Top 3 performing posts (why did they work?), bottom 3 (what can you learn?), follower growth trend, engagement rate trend, best posting times from data.

**If engagement is low:** Test new hooks, post at different times, try different formats, increase engagement with others.
**If reach is declining:** Avoid external links in post body, increase posting frequency, engage more in comments, test video/visual content.

### Power Law Lens

Social content follows a power law: 1% of posts get 90% of engagement. Most posts will underperform. This is normal, not a sign you're doing it wrong.

**In your weekly review, add this step:** Did any post this week get 2x+ your baseline engagement? If yes, activate the Double-Down Playbook:
- Create a follow-up within 48 hours from a different angle
- Plan 3-5 related pieces exploring adjacent topics within 1 week
- Consider a product or lead magnet around the interest signal within 2 weeks
- Do NOT celebrate and move on. A hit is the most valuable signal you'll get. Compound it.

**If no breakout post this week:** Keep shipping. Volume buys probability. After 500 posts, the math is 91.8% in your favor for at least one hit.

**How this connects to the 3-tier system:** Tier 1 regular content IS the 80% "good enough" content. Ship fast, stay visible, buy lottery tickets. Tier 2 value bombs ARE the 20% big swings. That's where you invest creative energy and where hits come from. The Double-Down Playbook is what you do when a Tier 2 post lands.

Full framework with probability math and expected value calculations: See `content-strategy/references/power-law-framework.md`

### Flop Prevention Checklist (Pre-Publish Gate)

Before posting ANY content, check against these proven flop signals. If the answer to any question is "yes," rewrite before publishing.

- [ ] **Generic motivational?** Does it sound like every other creator in the niche? (avg 4k views vs 340k for specific hooks)
- [ ] **Missing hook in first 3 seconds?** Could someone scroll past without knowing what's in it for them?
- [ ] **Too polished/produced?** Does it feel corporate or overproduced? (scrappy + authentic outperforms polished)
- [ ] **Self-focused?** Are you talking about yourself more than about the viewer's problem?
- [ ] **No specific number?** Can you add a concrete metric, timeframe, or result to the hook?
- [ ] **No pattern interrupt?** Is your take the same as the consensus? Find the contrarian angle.

If 2+ boxes are checked, the content will underperform. Rewrite the hook first. That's where 80% of performance is determined.

Turn normal business operations into endless content without the founder on camera. The 9-node system generates customer-created content at scale. Implementation priority: Competition > Chat scrape > Community scrape > Reviews scrape > Social media scrape > Bonus unlocks > Award unlocks > Events > Lifecycle. See `content-strategy/references/hormozi-marketing-machine.md` for full system.

### Hook Categories for Content
Seven verbal hook types applicable to all social content (from $100M Hooks Playbook):
1. **Labels** -- "[Avatar], I have a [thing] for you"
2. **Questions** -- Yes/Open format
3. **Conditionals** -- "If you're [condition], [implication]"
4. **Commands** -- "[Action] if you [desire]"
5. **Statements** -- "The [superlative] thing you can do..."
6. **Lists/Steps** -- "[Number] ways to [result]"
7. **Narratives** -- Scene-setting story openers

Use the Awareness Pyramid to expand hook reach: Most Aware (offer hooks) -> Problem-Aware (pain hooks) -> Unaware (curiosity hooks). See `video-hook/references/hormozi-hooks.md` for 121 proven hooks by platform.

### Six-Point Testimonial Script
Structured script for capturing social proof content that hits all emotional and logical proof points:
1. **Internal Struggle** -- "What was rock bottom? What did you hate doing?"
2. **External Struggle** -- "What were your objective metrics?"
3. **Skepticism** -- "What were your concerns about buying/joining?"
4. **Overcome** -- "What made you do it anyway?"
5. **External Victory** -- "What are your metrics now?"
6. **Internal Victory** -- "What's the best moment since? What can you do now that you couldn't before?"

Use this script for customer story posts, video testimonials, case study content, and comment-gated demo CTAs. The most important part is the hook -- have them say a few different hooks before the story.
