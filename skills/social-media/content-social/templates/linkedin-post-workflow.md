# LinkedIn Post Workflow (Quick-Start)

Write one LinkedIn post from idea to publish-ready. Fill in the brackets, follow the steps, get a post.

---

## Input

- **Topic:** [What you want to write about]
- **Post type:** [Pick one: teaching | story | opinion | case study | handraiser | belief shift]
- **Key insight:** [The ONE thing you want the reader to take away]
- **Target audience:** [Who specifically should stop scrolling for this]
- **CTA goal:** [What action? DM, comment, click link, reflect, engage?]

---

## Step 1: Write the Hook (First 1-2 Lines)

The hook must stop the scroll. Under 60 characters so it's fully visible on mobile before "see more."

**Pick a hook type:**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Pain mirror | Name their exact frustration | "You're posting daily and getting 4 likes." |
| Pattern interrupt | Challenge a common belief | "Stop writing LinkedIn posts. Seriously." |
| Specific number | Lead with data or result | "47 DMs from one post. Here's what I did." |
| Bold claim | Provoke a reaction | "Cold emails are dead. Here's proof." |
| Question | Ask something they can't scroll past | "What if your next client found YOU?" |
| Confession | Vulnerability that earns attention | "I lost 3 clients last month. My fault." |

**Rules:**
- Hook must connect to the actual content (no clickbait)
- No "In this post I'll share..." -- just start
- First line = the stop. Second line = the pull into "see more."

**Write your hook:** ___

---

## Step 2: Write the Body

Match body structure to your post type:

### Teaching Post
1. Name the problem (1-2 sentences)
2. Give your framework/steps (numbered or bulleted)
3. For each step: what to do + what NOT to do (teach through contrast)
4. End with the insight

### Story Post
1. Set the scene (specific moment, not vague context)
2. Conflict or tension (what went wrong, what was at stake)
3. Turning point (the realization, the action)
4. Lesson (the takeaway that applies to the reader)

### Opinion Post
1. State the contrarian take (bold, clear)
2. Evidence: why you believe this (experience, data, examples)
3. What this means for the reader
4. Acknowledge the counterargument briefly, then reinforce your position

### Case Study Post
1. Client's before state (specific problem, specific numbers)
2. What you did (the method, briefly)
3. Client's after state (specific results, specific numbers)
4. The lesson: what made the difference

### Handraiser Post
1. Name the desire ("If you want X...")
2. Qualify ("And you're currently at Y...")
3. Tease the resource/offer ("I built something for you")
4. CTA: comment a keyword to get it

### Belief Shift Post
1. State the common belief ("Most people think X")
2. Show why it's wrong or incomplete
3. Present the new belief with proof
4. Challenge the reader to adopt it

**Body rules:**
- Short-long-short sentence rhythm. Never uniform.
- Lead with the problem, not the solution.
- Be specific: names, numbers, timeframes, dollar amounts.
- Pair every "do this" with a "don't do this."
- Simple language: "figure out who you help" not "identify your target demographic."
- No jargon unless it's a named framework.

---

## Step 3: Write the Close + CTA

End with one of these (match to your CTA goal):

| CTA Type | When to Use | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Question | Drive comments | "What's the one thing you'd change about your profile?" |
| DM invite | Start conversations | "DM me 'SYSTEM' and I'll send you the framework." |
| Resource tease | Distribute lead magnet | "I turned this into a free PDF. Comment 'GUIDE' and I'll send it." |
| Open loop | Build curiosity for next post | "Tomorrow I'll share the exact script." |
| Reflection | Deep engagement | "Read that again. Then ask yourself: am I doing this?" |
| Repost nudge | Extend reach | "Repost this if someone in your network needs to hear it." |

**Rules:**
- Soft CTA. Never hard-sell.
- CTA should feel like an invitation, not a pitch.
- ONE CTA only. Not three.

---

## Step 4: Format Check

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- [ ] White space between every thought
- [ ] No emojis (unless specifically chosen for effect)
- [ ] No hashtags
- [ ] Hook under 60 characters
- [ ] Total length appropriate (short posts: 150-300 words, long posts: 500-800 words)
- [ ] Reads like talking to one person, not presenting to a crowd
- [ ] Would pass the Coffee Shop Rule (sounds natural spoken aloud)

---

## Step 5: Pre-Flight Check (from content-social)

Before hitting "post":

1. Would I stop scrolling for this hook? (Be honest)
2. Is there ONE clear takeaway?
3. Does the CTA match my goal for this post?
4. Is this specific enough that only MY audience would care?
5. Did I use at least one concrete detail (number, name, timeframe)?

If any answer is "no" -- fix it before posting.

---

## Output

The final post should be:
- Ready to copy-paste directly into LinkedIn
- No explanations, no headers, no commentary around it
- Just the post text, formatted for LinkedIn
