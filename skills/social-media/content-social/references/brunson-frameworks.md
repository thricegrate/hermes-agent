# Brunson Frameworks for Social Content

Russell Brunson's core storytelling and relationship-building frameworks adapted for organic social media. These turn followers into buyers by making your content feel personal, relatable, and strategically warm.

## 1. Attractive Character (AC) for Social

The Attractive Character is the persona your audience connects with. Not a fake persona. The real you, structured so people bond with you fast. Four elements, each maps to a content category.

### Element 1: Backstory

Your origin story. How you got here. What was broken, what changed, what you built.

**Why it works on social:** People don't follow brands. They follow people who went through something they recognize. Your backstory is the fastest shortcut to "this person gets me."

**Content checklist:**
- [ ] Pick ONE specific moment that changed your trajectory (not a vague "journey")
- [ ] Name the emotion you felt at that moment (frustration, fear, excitement, shame)
- [ ] Show where you were vs. where you are now (gap = compelling)
- [ ] Keep it to 3-5 sentences on X, 5-8 on LinkedIn, 15-30 seconds on video
- [ ] End with what you learned, not what you achieved
- [ ] Revisit your backstory from different angles (same origin, different lessons)

**Frequency:** 1-2x/week. Mix into Tier 1 and Tier 2 content.

**Post formula:**
```
[Specific moment from your past]
[What you felt / what was at stake]
[The turning point or realization]
[Where it led you]
[What it taught you -- bridge to audience's situation]
```

**Example:**
"Two years ago I was mass-sending newsletters to 550K subscribers and making less than my friend who had 2K on a Substack. The problem wasn't audience size. It was that I had no product. Just ad revenue and rented attention. That one realization changed everything I built after."

### Element 2: Parables

Short teaching stories. You experienced something, and the lesson applies to your audience. Not hypotheticals. Real moments that carry a transferable insight.

**Why it works on social:** Stories bypass skepticism. A stat says "you should do X." A parable says "I did X, here's what happened." The second one gets saved and shared.

**Content checklist:**
- [ ] Start with the moment, not the lesson (don't telegraph the punchline)
- [ ] Include a specific detail that makes it real (name, place, number, object)
- [ ] The lesson must connect to something your audience is struggling with RIGHT NOW
- [ ] One parable = one lesson. Don't stack multiple takeaways.
- [ ] End with the principle, stated plainly. No clever twist needed.

**Frequency:** 2-3x/week. These are your workhorse Tier 1 posts.

**Post formula:**
```
[What happened -- scene-setting in 1-2 sentences]
[The unexpected detail or twist]
[What you realized]
[The principle your audience can apply]
```

**Example:**
"A coaching client asked me to review her welcome sequence. Seven emails. All beautifully written. Zero personality. I asked her to record a voice note explaining the same thing. It was 10x better. She sounded like a real person. That voice note became her best-performing email. Your writing voice and your real voice are usually two different people. Close the gap."

### Element 3: Character Flaws

Admitting what you're bad at, what you got wrong, where you're still figuring things out. Vulnerability that builds trust, not sympathy.

**Why it works on social:** Perfection repels. Flaws create identification. When you say "I'm bad at X," people think "me too" and that's the beginning of loyalty.

**Content checklist:**
- [ ] Pick a real flaw, not a humble-brag ("my weakness is I work too hard")
- [ ] Show how the flaw affected your results or decisions
- [ ] Don't wallow. State it, own it, move on. 2-3 sentences max on the flaw itself.
- [ ] Connect the flaw to a lesson or workaround you found
- [ ] Never apologize for the flaw. Just name it.

**Frequency:** 1x/week. Powerful in Tier 2 (value bombs) and backstory posts.

**Post formula:**
```
[Name the flaw plainly]
[How it showed up / what it cost you]
[What you did about it (or are still doing)]
[Why this matters for your audience]
```

**Example:**
"English is my third language. I used to avoid video because I thought my accent would kill credibility. So I hid behind text for two years. Built 550K subscribers without showing my face once. Worked great for newsletters. Terrible for building a personal brand. Now I'm doing it anyway. Imperfect beats invisible."

### Element 4: Polarity

Taking a clear stance. Drawing a line. Being FOR something and AGAINST something. Not being edgy for clicks. Having actual convictions and stating them.

**Why it works on social:** Neutral content gets ignored. Strong positions get saved, shared, argued about, and remembered. Polarity creates tribes.

**Content checklist:**
- [ ] State what you believe AND what you're against (both sides of the line)
- [ ] Back it up with experience or evidence (opinion without proof = noise)
- [ ] Accept that some people will disagree. That's the point.
- [ ] Don't attack people. Attack ideas, methods, or conventional wisdom.
- [ ] Pair polarity posts with value posts. All spice, no substance = exhausting.

**Frequency:** 1-2x/week. Works as Tier 1 (engagement driver) or Tier 2 (if backed by deep evidence).

**Post formula:**
```
[Strong position in one sentence]
[Why you believe this -- from experience, not theory]
[What the opposite camp gets wrong]
[What you'd recommend instead]
```

**Example:**
"I think most 'how to grow your newsletter' advice is actively harmful. It optimizes for subscriber count. Not for revenue per subscriber. I've had newsletters with 80K subs making $800/mo and newsletters with 15K subs making $5K/mo. The difference was never growth tactics. It was what you sell to the people who show up."

## 2. Hook, Story, Offer (HSO) for Social Posts

Brunson's core copywriting framework compressed for social media. Every post follows three beats: grab them, hold them, move them.

### The Framework

**Hook** = First line. The thing that stops the scroll. If this fails, nothing else matters.
**Story** = Middle. 2-6 lines depending on platform. The context, setup, or teaching that earns the CTA.
**Offer** = Last line(s). The CTA. What you want them to do next.

### HSO Scaled by Platform

**X/Twitter (single tweet):**
- Hook: 1 sentence (under 15 words, punchy)
- Story: 1-2 sentences (the insight or setup)
- Offer: 1 sentence (follow, reply, link)
- Total: 280 characters or under

**X/Twitter (thread):**
- Hook: Tweet 1 (scroll-stopper + promise)
- Story: Tweets 2-6 (one point per tweet, each tweet re-hooks)
- Offer: Final tweet (CTA + reason to follow)

**LinkedIn:**
- Hook: First line before the fold (12 words max, must earn the "see more" click)
- Story: 3-5 short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each, line breaks between)
- Offer: Final line (question OR CTA, never both)
- Total: 1,200-1,500 characters

**Instagram caption:**
- Hook: First line (shows in preview, must stop the scroll)
- Story: 3-5 paragraphs with line breaks
- Offer: "Link in bio" or "DM me [keyword]"
- Hashtags: 3-5 after the CTA

**Short-form video (Reels/TikTok/Shorts):**
- Hook: 0-3 seconds (pattern interrupt, bold claim, or visual surprise)
- Story: 3-25 seconds (teach, show, demonstrate)
- Offer: Last 5 seconds (follow, comment, link in bio)

### HSO Checklist (Run Before Posting)

- [ ] **Hook test:** Read ONLY the first line. Would you stop scrolling? If no, rewrite.
- [ ] **Story test:** Does the middle section earn the CTA? (Teaching, proof, or emotional connection.) If it's filler, cut it.
- [ ] **Offer test:** Is there a clear next step? Even "save this" counts. No next step = wasted post.
- [ ] **Platform fit:** Is the length right for the platform? A 300-word Instagram caption on X is dead on arrival.
- [ ] **One idea:** Does this post make ONE point? If you're making two, split into two posts.

### HSO Examples by Tier

**Tier 1 (daily, quick value):**
```
Hook: "Your newsletter subject line isn't the problem."
Story: "I tested 200+ subject lines over 18 months. Open rate barely moved. Then I cleaned my list. Open rate jumped 6 points in two weeks. The subject line was never the bottleneck."
Offer: "I write about newsletter growth weekly. Link in bio."
```

**Tier 2 (value bomb):**
```
Hook: "I spent $12K on Meta ads last month for my newsletter. Here's what I'd do differently."
Story: [4-6 paragraphs breaking down what worked, what didn't, actual CPAs, and the one change that cut cost by 40%]
Offer: "I built a calculator that estimates your newsletter ad ROI before you spend a dollar. Comment CALC and I'll send it."
```

**Tier 3 (awareness bridge):**
```
Hook: "3 months ago I was selling ad space in my newsletter for $200/issue."
Story: "Now that same newsletter generates $3K/mo from a single $47 product. Same audience. Same content. Different model. Here's what shifted."
Offer: "I'm taking 10 newsletter operators through this exact pivot. If you have 5K+ subs and want to add product revenue, DM me PIVOT."
```

## 3. Dream 100 Content Strategy

Brunson's Dream 100: identify the 100 people, platforms, and communities that already have your ideal audience. Then earn your way in through genuine engagement before you ever ask for anything.

### The Principle

Your audience already exists. They're following someone else right now. The Dream 100 strategy is about building real relationships with those "someone elses" so their audience discovers you organically.

This is NOT:
- Cold DMing influencers asking for shoutouts
- Commenting "Great post!" on everything
- Tagging people who don't know you exist

This IS:
- Becoming a recognized name in their comments section
- Adding genuine value to their conversations
- Building a relationship where collaboration feels natural, not transactional

### Step 1: Build Your Dream 100 List

Pick 100 accounts across your platforms. Three categories:

**Tier A (30 accounts): Direct competitors or peers**
- Same niche, similar audience size or bigger
- Their followers would follow you if they knew you existed
- You can genuinely add to their conversations

**Tier B (40 accounts): Adjacent niche creators**
- Different topic, overlapping audience
- Their followers need what you offer but aren't looking for it yet
- Cross-pollination opportunities

**Tier C (30 accounts): Aspirational / industry leaders**
- Much bigger audience
- Getting on their radar takes longer but pays more
- Even a reply from them exposes you to thousands

**Selection checklist:**
- [ ] They post at least 3x/week (active accounts only)
- [ ] Their comments section has real engagement (not just bots)
- [ ] Their audience matches your ideal buyer profile
- [ ] You can genuinely engage with their content (no faking interest)
- [ ] Mix of platforms: don't put all 100 on X

### Step 2: The 30-60 Day Warm-Up

**Days 1-14: Consume and engage.**
- Turn on notifications for all 100 accounts
- Read/watch their content daily
- Leave 2-3 thoughtful comments per day across the list
- Comment quality bar: add a new angle, share a relevant experience, ask a question that advances the conversation. "Great point!" does nothing.
- NO DMs. NO asks. NO self-promotion. Just show up.

**Days 15-30: Become a regular.**
- Increase to 5-7 comments per day
- Start sharing their content with your own commentary added
- Reply to their replies (join conversations, not just top-level comments)
- If they reply to your comment, engage back naturally. Don't fanboy.
- Track who's starting to recognize you (replies, likes on your comments)

**Days 31-60: Add value directly.**
- DM with something genuinely useful (NOT a pitch). Examples:
  - "Saw your post about [X]. Reminded me of [relevant resource/data]. Thought you'd find it interesting."
  - "Your take on [topic] made me think about [angle]. Would love to hear your thoughts on [specific question]."
  - Share something you created that's relevant to their recent content
- Offer to help with something specific (no strings attached)
- Start referencing their content in YOUR posts (tag them, give credit)

**Days 60+: Collaborate.**
- Propose low-commitment collaboration: joint thread, content swap, podcast guest
- If they have a product, buy it. Use it. Talk about it publicly.
- Introduction: you're now "that person who always adds great points" not "some random who wants something"

### Dream 100 Weekly Routine

| Activity | Time | Frequency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Comment on Dream 100 posts | 15 min | Daily |
| Share Dream 100 content with commentary | 5 min | 3x/week |
| Send 2-3 value-first DMs | 10 min | Weekly |
| Update Dream 100 list (add/remove) | 15 min | Monthly |
| Propose one collaboration | 10 min | Bi-weekly (after day 60) |

### Dream 100 Tracking

For each person on your list, track:
- [ ] Last engaged (date)
- [ ] Have they replied to you? (Y/N)
- [ ] Have they followed you back? (Y/N)
- [ ] Collaboration attempted? (Y/N)
- [ ] Collaboration completed? (Y/N)
- [ ] Notes (what they respond to, shared interests, mutual connections)

### Rules

1. **Patience is the strategy.** 30-60 days of pure giving before any ask. Most people quit at day 5 because they want immediate ROI. The ROI comes from being a known entity, not a stranger with a pitch.
2. **Quality over quantity.** 3 great comments beat 30 generic ones. One real DM conversation beats 50 cold messages.
3. **Don't be weird.** If you wouldn't say it in person, don't DM it. If it feels like you're stalking, pull back.
4. **Prune the list.** If someone never engages despite 30+ days of genuine effort, replace them. Some people don't engage with their audience. That's fine. Move on.
5. **Play the long game.** Some Dream 100 relationships take 6-12 months to develop. The best collaborations come from real relationships, not transactional exchanges.

### How Dream 100 Connects to the 3-Tier System

- **Tier 1 (daily content):** Your Dream 100 engagement IS part of your daily routine. Comments on their posts drive profile visits to yours.
- **Tier 2 (value bombs):** Tag Dream 100 people when you reference their ideas (with credit). If your value bomb is good, they share it.
- **Tier 3 (awareness bridge):** Dream 100 collaborations (joint content, guest spots) expose you to pre-qualified audiences at scale.

## Integration with Existing Frameworks

- **Kennedy Social Checkpoint** (in `kennedy-social-authority.md`) handles pre-publish quality gates. Brunson frameworks handle content creation and relationship strategy. Use both: Brunson to create, Kennedy to verify before posting.
- **HSO** replaces nothing in post-templates.md. It's the underlying structure. The LinkedIn/X/IG templates are specific formats that already follow HSO implicitly. Use HSO when freestyling a post without a template.
- **Dream 100** overlaps with the Daily Engagement Routine in SKILL.md. Dream 100 is the strategic version: targeted accounts, tracked relationships, deliberate warm-up. The engagement routine is the tactical daily habit. Run both.

## Quick Reference: When to Use Each Framework

| Situation | Framework | What to Do |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Need to build personal connection fast | AC: Backstory | Share a specific origin moment |
| Teaching something, need it to stick | AC: Parables | Wrap the lesson in a real story |
| Audience feels distant or skeptical | AC: Flaws | Admit something real you struggle with |
| Engagement is flat, need to spark debate | AC: Polarity | Take a clear stance, draw a line |
| Writing any social post | HSO | Structure as Hook > Story > Offer |
| Need to grow audience without ads | Dream 100 | Engage 100 key accounts for 30-60 days |
| Launching a product or collaboration | Dream 100 | Warm up relationships BEFORE the ask |
