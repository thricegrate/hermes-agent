## Breaking News Hooks

Create urgency by framing content as time-sensitive news.

**Formula:** `BREAKING: [Tool/Company] can now [impressive capability] (for free)`

**Examples:**
- "BREAKING: AI can now build financial models like Goldman Sachs analysts (for free)"
- "BREAKING: Claude just shipped a feature that changes everything about [workflow]"
- "BREAKING: OpenAI just dropped [feature]. Here's how to use it before everyone else"
- "BREAKING: AI can now do market research like McKinsey"
- "BREAKING: Claude can now do SEO like a $10,000/month agency"
- "OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic just published guides"

**When to use:** Product launches, major updates, new capabilities. Best within 24-48 hours of the event.

**Warning:** Overuse dilutes the effect. Reserve for genuinely new capabilities.

## Shock Number Hooks

Large or specific numbers that create a pattern interrupt.

**Formula:** `[Large number]+ [valuable resources] that [outcome]`

**Examples:**
- "700+ prompts that replace a $5,000 consultant"
- "72+ AI tools to finish months of work in minutes"
- "I found 12 Claude prompts that do what a $5,000 consultant does"
- "135+ AI tools to try in 2026 and save 100s of hours"
- "9 free AI guides that will teach you more than a $2,000 course"
- "Here are 12 insane Claude Opus 4.6 prompts"
- "I collected 700+ mega prompts"
- "120 Must-Use AI Tools" / "1000+ GPT-4 Prompts"

**Variations:**
- **Dollar anchor:** "[N] prompts that replace $[salary]/year [job title] work"
- **Time compression:** "[N] tools to do [timeframe] of work in [shorter timeframe]"
- **Scale signal:** "I tested [N]+ tools. These [smaller N] are the only ones worth using"

## Pain Recognition Hooks

Name the exact frustration your audience feels but hasn't articulated.

**Formula:** `Everyone's hyped about [X]... [but/barely] [real frustration]`

**Examples:**
- "Everyone's hyped about Claude... barely anyone knows how to actually use it"
- "Everyone is hyped about Gemini 3.0 Pro... but barely anyone knows how"
- "Most people will still be 'learning AI' in 2026. A small group will be shipping products"
- "You're not bad at AI. You just don't have the right prompts"
- "Most founders are paying for reports AI can generate in 60 seconds"
- "We're about to create the 1st generation of scientists who can't research without AI"

**Variations:**
- **Gap framing:** "Everyone [does X]... but [almost nobody does Y]"
- **Cost framing:** "You're paying $[X] for something AI does for free"
- **Identity split:** "In [timeframe], there will be two groups: [group A] and [group B]"

## Anti-Overwhelm Hooks

Counter-program against information overload. Position your content as the antidote to complexity.

**Formula:** `[Topic] doesn't need to be [negative emotion]. Just [simple action]`

**Examples:**
- "AI doesn't need to be overwhelming. Just focus on automating ONE workflow per week"
- "Stop trying to learn every AI tool. Master these 5 and you'll outperform 95% of people"
- "You don't need 100 AI tools. You need these 7 and a system"
- "The best AI strategy? Block 30 minutes a day. Test one workflow. That's it"

**When to use:** When your audience is drowning in options and needs permission to simplify.

## Transformation Hooks

Promise a specific before/after outcome with dollar values or status markers.

**Formula:** `These [N] [resources] replaced $[amount]/year of [prestigious job] work`

**Examples:**
- "These 12 Claude prompts replaced $150K/year of investment banking work"
- "I built a $250/day side income using only ChatGPT and these 7 prompts"
- "One AI workflow replaced my $5,000/month marketing agency"
- "This single prompt does what a McKinsey consultant charges $500/hour for"
- "replace $5,000 consultant"
- "ChatGPT + Laptop + Internet + 1 Hour a Day = $250 Daily"
- "can take your biz to $100k/month"

**Variations:**
- **Time transformation:** "What used to take me 4 hours now takes 15 minutes"
- **Status transformation:** "[Tool] gave me McKinsey-level strategy for $0"
- **Career transformation:** "I went from [status A] to [status B] using these [N] tools"
- **Equation format:** "[Tool] + [Simple setup] + [Low effort] = [Dollar outcome]"

**Warning:** The content must genuinely deliver on the promise. Empty transformation claims destroy long-term credibility.

## Contrarian Hooks

Challenge conventional wisdom. Split the audience into camps (which generates debate = replies = distribution).

**Formula:** `[Common belief] is wrong. Here's what actually works`

**Examples:**
- "Prompt engineering is dead. Here's what replaced it"
- "The AI productivity boom? The data says it hasn't happened yet"
- "Stop learning AI tools. Start learning AI workflows"
- "Most AI content is wrong. I reviewed all the studies and here's the truth"
- "AI isn't replacing jobs. It's replacing people who don't use AI"

**When to use:** When you have data, experience, or a framework that contradicts popular opinion. Works best with authority backing (credentials, data, track record).

## Curiosity Gap Hooks

Open an information loop that can only be closed by reading the content.

**Formula:** `I discovered [unexpected thing] about [topic]. [Teaser without answer]`

**Examples:**
- "There's a Claude feature most people don't know exists. It changes everything about how you use AI"
- "I reviewed every AI productivity study. The results surprised me"
- "The most bookmarked AI tweet of 2025 used a trick most people ignore"
- "I found the one prompt pattern that works across Claude, ChatGPT, AND Gemini"

**Rules:**
- The gap must be closed within the thread (don't bait-and-switch)
- The payoff must exceed the promise
- Curiosity gaps work best with authority: "After 3 years of testing..." > "I found..."

## Authority Hooks

Lead with credentials, track record, or institutional backing.

**Formula:** `As a [credentialed role], I [did impressive thing]. Here's what I learned`

**Examples:**
- "I'm a professor who reviewed every AI productivity study published in 2025. Here's the synthesis"
- "After building 47 AI automations for my business, here are the only 5 that matter"
- "I've been doing systematic reviews since before ChatGPT existed. Here's my AI-integrated workflow"
- "I run a 30-employee company. These are the exact AI workflows running our operations"
- "You are a McKinsey-level market analyst"
- "You are a senior strategy consultant at Bain"
- "I learned these from personal experience and hours of experimenting"

**Variations:**
- **Institutional:** "McKinsey just published [X]. Here's what it means for you"
- **Personal track record:** "I've [impressive metric]. Here's the system behind it"
- **Counter-authority:** "I'm a [role] and I'm telling you [surprising thing about your field]"
- **Prompt-as-authority:** "You are a [prestigious role] at [institution]" (authority baked into the prompt itself)

## Question Hooks

Engage the reader by making them think about their own situation.

**Formula:** `[Question that highlights a gap or aspiration]?`

**Examples:**
- "What if your next side hustle wasn't a Shopify store... but an AI agent that sells for you?"
- "What is the actual impact of AI on productivity? (The answer might surprise you)"
- "Are you using AI to save time, or is AI using your time?"
- "How many hours did you waste this week on work AI could have done?"

**When to use:** Works best as tweet 1 when followed by immediate answers. Don't leave questions hanging without payoff.

## Scarcity Hooks

Create time pressure or limited availability.

**Formula:** `[Valuable resource]: free for [limited time] only`

**Examples:**
- "I'm giving away my complete AI prompt library (700+ prompts). Free for 24 hours only"
- "Comment 'GUIDE' before midnight: I'm removing this tomorrow"
- "This took me 6 months to build. Giving it away free this week only"
- "Worth $67, but free today! ... Available free for 24 hours"
- "And for 24 hours, it's 100% FREE!"
- "(Must follow to receive the DM)"

**Rules:**
- Only use if you'll actually follow through on the time limit
- Combine with comment-DM bait for maximum urgency
- Don't use more than once per week: audiences develop immunity

## Direct Accusation Hooks

Call the reader out on behavior they already know is wrong. Creates instant recognition and engagement because they feel seen.

**Formula:** `"You're [doing wrong thing]. [Consequence they know]."`

**Examples:**
- "You're writing LinkedIn posts for your mom, not your audience."
- "You're using AI to sound smart. Your audience wants useful, not impressive."
- "You're posting every day and wondering why nobody cares. It's not the algorithm."
- "You're spending 3 hours on a post that gets 12 likes. Your process is broken."

**Rules:**
- Must be TRUE: the reader self-recognizes immediately. If they don't, it feels like an attack.
- Must be FIXABLE: the post shows how to fix it. Accusation without solution is just mean.
- Not mean-spirited: empathy underneath the bluntness. You've been there too.
- Works best paired with a personal story: "I did this for 6 months before I figured out..."

**Source:** Ruben Hassid (770K LinkedIn followers, 1.56M across 5 accounts)

## Stolen Thought Hooks

Voice what the reader secretly thinks but won't say publicly. Creates a "how did you know?" moment that stops the scroll.

**Formula:** `"You know [uncomfortable truth]. So does [who else knows]."`

**Examples:**
- "You know your LinkedIn posts are boring. So does everyone scrolling past them."
- "You know you're not using AI to its full potential. So does your competitor who is."
- "You secretly think most AI content is garbage. You're right. Here's why yours doesn't have to be."
- "You've thought about quitting LinkedIn at least twice this month. Before you do, read this."

**Rules:**
- Thought must be genuinely private: something they think but wouldn't post about
- Tone is empathetic, not judgmental: you're naming their inner monologue, not mocking it
- Immediately offer the fix: don't leave them sitting in the discomfort
- Most effective when YOU had the same thought: "I used to think this too. Then I..."

**Source:** Ruben Hassid (770K LinkedIn followers, 1.56M across 5 accounts)

## Absurd Reframe Hooks

Take a mundane topic and describe it with life-or-death stakes. The dramatization maps to a real constraint, making it memorable and shareable.

**Formula:** `"Your [mundane thing] has [dramatic constraint]. [Most fail]."`

**Examples:**
- "Your LinkedIn hook has 1.2 seconds to live. Most die instantly."
- "Your first two lines get 110 characters before LinkedIn kills them. You're wasting 40 on filler words."
- "Every post you write enters a gladiator arena of 2 million other posts. Yours brought a butter knife."
- "Your content has a 0.1% chance of being seen. Here's how to 10x those odds."

**Rules:**
- Dramatization must map to a REAL constraint (1.2 seconds of attention, 110-character truncation, etc.)
- Don't cross into parody: the reader should nod, not laugh
- Follow immediately with the practical fix: drama without substance is clickbait
- Works best when the specific number is surprising and verifiable

**Source:** Ruben Hassid (770K LinkedIn followers, 1.56M across 5 accounts)

## Comment-DM Bait CTAs

Not a hook per se, but a CTA format that drives the highest reply counts on the platform. Place after the hook or at thread end.

**Formula:** `Comment "[WORD]" and I'll DM you [the resource]`

**Examples:**
- "Comment "AI" and I'll DM you everything"
- "Like this post and comment Al - I'll send you my ultimate guide"
- "Reply "AI" ... Follow me (so that I can DM)"
- "I'll send you my ultimate guide directly"
- "To get it, just: 1. Like 2. Reply "AI" 3. Follow me"

**Rules:**
- One short word in ALL CAPS: "AI", "GUIDE", "PROMPTS", "SEND"
- Must require follow for DM delivery (grows followers + enables the DM)
- Place after the value is established, never as the opening line
- Actually deliver: broken promises tank your reply rate on future posts

## Save-CTA Hooks

Micro-CTAs woven into the hook tweet or thread body to drive bookmarks. These are short parenthetical nudges, not full sentences.

**Formula:** `([Save/Bookmark] [for later / this])`

**Examples:**
- "(Save for later)"
- "Save this future you will thank you"
- "(you'll want to save this)"
- "(Bookmark this thread)"

**Placement:**
- End of hook tweet (most common): "Here are 12 prompts... (save for later)"
- Mid-thread as a reminder: "Still with me? Save this before you forget"
- Final tweet paired with multi-CTA: "1) Bookmark it 2) Follow me 3) RT tweet 1"

**Why it works:** The explicit save-ask increases bookmark rate 2-3x vs. no ask. Most readers won't think to bookmark unless prompted.

## Multi-CTA Closers

The closing tweet of a thread. Stacks multiple engagement signals in one ask.

**Formula:** `If this [was useful / saved you time / helped]: 1. [Action] 2. [Action] 3. [Action]`

**Examples:**
- "Like this post and comment Al ... (Must follow)"
- "Save this ... Follow @jason_coder0"
- "Save for later ... Follow me @socialwithaayan ... Share it"
- "If this was useful: 1) Bookmark it 2) Follow me for more 3) RT tweet 1 to help others"

**Rules:**
- Lead with a reply prompt first (replies = 10,000 Earlybird pts, 10x more than likes), then bookmark ask
- Include follow ask (grows audience)
- Include RT or share (extends reach)
- Max 3-4 actions: more feels desperate
- Frame as reciprocity ("If this helped you...")

## Combining Hook Types

The strongest hooks stack 2-3 types. Examples:

| Combination | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Breaking + Shock Number + Transformation | "BREAKING: AI can now build financial models like Goldman Sachs analysts (for free). Here are 12 prompts that replace $150K/year work" |
| Pain + Number + Save-Bait | "Everyone's hyped about Claude but barely anyone knows how to use it. Here are 700+ prompts (save for later)" |
| Anti-Overwhelm + Specificity + Authority | "AI doesn't need to be overwhelming. I run a 30-employee company. Just automate ONE workflow per week. Guide below" |
| Contrarian + Data + Curiosity | "The AI productivity boom hasn't happened yet. I reviewed every study. The data says something surprising" |
| Question + Transformation + Scarcity | "What if you could replace your $5K/month agency with one AI workflow? Sharing the exact system (free for 24h)" |
| Direct Accusation + Transformation | "You're writing LinkedIn posts for your mom. Here's the framework that got me from 8K to 770K followers" |
| Stolen Thought + Curiosity Gap | "You secretly think most AI content is garbage. You're right. Here's the one thing the 1% do differently" |
| Absurd Reframe + Shock Number | "Your LinkedIn hook has 1.2 seconds to live. I tested 500+ hooks. These 7 patterns survive" |
| Direct Accusation + Anti-Overwhelm | "You're using 47 AI tools and getting nothing done. Drop 40 of them. Here's which 7 to keep" |
| Stolen Thought + Authority | "You've thought about quitting LinkedIn twice this month. After growing to 770K followers, here's why I almost did too" |

**Rule of thumb:** If your hook only activates one type, add a second. Two-type hooks outperform single-type by ~2x. Three-type hooks are the ceiling: beyond that it feels forced.
