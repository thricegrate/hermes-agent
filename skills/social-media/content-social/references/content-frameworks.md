# Content Marketing Frameworks Reference

## Core Rule

Content without a CTA to your email list is wasted content. Social media = traffic driver. Email = revenue channel.

## Content Multiplication Formula

Each problem from the product's Top 10 generates 4+ pieces of content:
1. **Name it** - "The [problem] that's killing your [outcome]"
2. **Explain it** - "Why [problem] happens (and what to do about it)"
3. **Myth-bust it** - "Stop believing [common myth about problem]"
4. **Quick tip** - "One thing to fix [problem] today"

Each outcome from the Top 10 generates 3+ pieces of content:
1. **Paint it** - "What it looks like when you [outcome]"
2. **Prove it** - "[Person/I] achieved [outcome] by doing [X]"
3. **Path to it** - "The 3-step framework for [outcome]"

**10 problems + 10 outcomes = 70+ content ideas minimum**

## Platform-Specific Formats

### X/Twitter
- **Single tweet:** Hook + insight + CTA (under 280 chars)
- **Thread:** Hook tweet + 3-7 value tweets + CTA tweet
- **Best posting times:** 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-7pm (audience timezone)
- **Frequency:** 1-3 tweets/day minimum

### LinkedIn
- **Hook line:** Scroll-stopping first sentence (shows in preview)
- **Body:** 3-5 short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
- **Lists:** Bullet points or numbered lists for key points
- **CTA:** Comment "LINK" or direct to bio link
- **Frequency:** 1 post/day on weekdays

### Instagram
- **Hook:** First line must grab attention (shows in caption preview)
- **Body:** 3-5 paragraphs of value with line breaks between
- **CTA:** "Link in bio" or "DM me [keyword]"
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant hashtags
- **Frequency:** 3-5 posts/week + daily stories

### YouTube (Long-Form)

YouTube long-form is the highest-leverage content channel. One 10-15 min video generates weeks of repurposable content across every other platform.

**Title principles:**
- Curiosity-driven with extreme wording: "I Survived" > "I Spent," superlatives, specific numbers, contrasts
- Include main keyword for search
- Generate 10+ title options and pick the most clickable

**Thumbnail principles:**
- Bold contrast, readable at mobile phone size
- Expressive face with strong emotion (shock, excitement, joy)
- 3-5 words max text overlay
- Must tell a story the viewer wants to see resolved
- The thumbnail shows WHAT; the title explains WHY you should care

**Structure (retention-optimized):**
- First minute: Match title/thumbnail expectations immediately. Best lighting. Pre-planned dialogue. Front-load the most interesting content.
- Minutes 1-3: Transition from hype to execution. Show, don't tell. Include a 3-minute re-engagement beat (something impressive or unique).
- Minutes 3-6: Most exciting content. Quick scene changes. Highly stimulating. Make viewers fall in love.
- 6-minute re-engagement: Content that pushes the story forward for the back half.
- Back half: Deeper content. Never signal the end.
- Ending: Abrupt cut after the payoff. No summary. No goodbye.

**CTA:** "Link in description" to email opt-in or lead magnet. Mention at the start and end.
**Frequency:** 1-2 videos/week. Consistency matters more than volume.

**Repurposing pipeline (one video fuels everything):**
- 3-5 short-form clips -> TikTok, Reels, Shorts
- 2-3 social media posts -> X, LinkedIn, Instagram
- 1 newsletter issue or segment -> email list
- 1 community engagement post -> YouTube Community, social

For full YouTube production planning, use the `youtube-producer` skill.

## CTA Rotation (5 Patterns)

Don't repeat the same CTA. Rotate through:

1. **Direct:** "I teach this in my free [EEC name]. Grab it: [link]"
2. **Soft:** "I write about [topic] weekly in my newsletter. Link in bio."
3. **Social proof:** "Join [X] others learning [outcome]. [link]"
4. **Curiosity:** "I break this down step-by-step in my free 5-day email course. [link]"
5. **Value-add:** "Want the complete framework? I put it in a free guide: [link]"

## Value Bombs (Tier 2 Content)

Value bombs are breakthrough content pieces designed to prove your methodology and generate organic sharing. They require 3-5x the effort of regular posts but produce 10-50x the impact.

### Definition

A value bomb is a standalone content piece that:
- Delivers a genuine, actionable insight (not a teaser)
- Includes proof: real numbers, screenshots, case studies, or before/after
- Is good enough to be a lead magnet on its own
- Generates saves, shares, and "I need to follow this person" reactions

### 7 Value Bomb Themes

| # | Theme | Structure | Example |
|---|-------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **The Counterintuitive Truth** | Bold claim + evidence + reframe | "Why posting more content is hurting your growth (and what to do instead)" |
| 2 | **The Framework Reveal** | Name + steps + results | "My 3-step system for [outcome]: the exact framework (with real numbers)" |
| 3 | **The Behind-the-Scenes** | Timeline + data + lessons | "I tested [X] for 30 days. Here's what happened (full data inside)." |
| 4 | **The Mistake Autopsy** | What happened + why + fix | "I lost $[X] because of this one mistake. Here's how to avoid it." |
| 5 | **The Speed Run** | Result + compressed timeline + steps | "[Outcome] in [surprisingly short time]: the exact steps I followed" |
| 6 | **The Resource Drop** | Free tool + how to use it + CTA | "Free template: the exact [tool] I use to [outcome] (download link)" |
| 7 | **The Proof Stack** | Multiple results + pattern + methodology | "7 results from people using [method] this month alone" |

### Value Bomb Creation Checklist

For each value bomb, verify:
- [ ] Contains a genuine insight (not repackaged common advice)
- [ ] Includes at least one form of proof (data, screenshot, testimonial, before/after)
- [ ] Is formatted for the platform (thread for X, carousel for IG, article for LinkedIn)
- [ ] Has a soft CTA (not a hard sell; story-close, resource-drop, or DM-trigger)
- [ ] Is 3-5x the depth/effort of a regular post
- [ ] Could stand alone as a lead magnet
- [ ] Connects to a core concept from your product (reinforces your methodology)

### Distribution Strategy

Each value bomb should be distributed across channels:
1. **Primary platform post:** Full-length version on main platform
2. **Email version:** Send to subscriber list as a dedicated value email (feeds `email-sequence` Step 7)
3. **Cross-platform adaptation:** Shorter versions for secondary platforms
4. **Paid boost:** Top-performing value bombs get ad spend behind them (optional)
5. **Evergreen archive:** Compile into a "best of" resource or lead magnet over time

## Awareness Bridge Content (Tier 3)

Content strategically designed to move your audience through the 5 awareness levels toward a buying decision. This is the bridge between "I follow this person" and "I need to work with this person."

### 5 Awareness Levels

| Level | What They Think | Content Goal | Content Types |
|-------|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Unaware** | "I don't have a problem" | Create urgency; make them see the problem | Pattern interrupts, disruptive claims, "what if" scenarios, contrarian takes |
| **Problem Aware** | "I have this problem" | Agitate; show the cost of inaction | Problem-naming posts, pain-point stories, "this is costing you X" content |
| **Solution Aware** | "Solutions exist" | Differentiate; show why your approach is unique | Methodology comparisons, framework reveals, "why most advice is wrong" |
| **Product Aware** | "Your thing exists" | Build trust; handle objections | Testimonials, case studies, FAQ content, "who this is for/not for" |
| **Most Aware** | "I'm almost ready" | Convert; reduce friction | Direct CTAs, guarantee content, "what happens when you start" |

### Soft CTA Progression

As awareness increases, CTAs should progress:

| Awareness Level | CTA Type | Example |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| Unaware/Problem Aware | No CTA or story-close | Just end with a reflection or question |
| Solution Aware | Resource-drop or DM-trigger | "DM me 'FRAMEWORK' for the full breakdown" |
| Product Aware | Application or wait-list | "Taking 5 people through this. Apply: [link]" |
| Most Aware | Direct CTA | "Spots open now. Book your call: [link]" |

### Key Principles
- Never jump awareness levels. Meet the audience where they are.
- Most of your audience is Unaware or Problem Aware. Weight content accordingly.
- Awareness bridge content works best in sequences (content series, multi-part threads).
- The email awareness bridge sequence (from `email-sequence` Step 8) handles the private, 1-to-1 journey. Social bridge content handles the public journey.

## Disruptive Ideas Framework

From AC Ch 19: The most shareable content challenges existing beliefs. Disruptive ideas create pattern interrupts that force attention and reconsideration.

### Creating Disruptive Content

**Formula:** Take a widely-accepted belief in your niche and challenge it with evidence.

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **The Inversion** | "Everyone says [X]. The data says the opposite." | "Everyone says post consistently. I stopped posting for 2 weeks and my engagement doubled. Here's why." |
| **The Hidden Cost** | "You think [X] is helping. It's actually costing you [Y]." | "Your content calendar is costing you $50K/year in missed opportunities." |
| **The Shortcut Exposed** | "[Common shortcut] is actually the longest path to [outcome]." | "Growth hacks are the slowest way to grow. Here's what actually compounds." |
| **The Contrarian Take** | "Unpopular opinion: [widely-held belief] is wrong because [evidence]." | "Unpopular opinion: You don't need a niche. Here's the data from 200 creators." |
| **The Reframe** | "Stop thinking about [X] as [common frame]. Think about it as [new frame]." | "Stop thinking about content as marketing. Think about it as product development." |

### Rules for Disruptive Content
- Must be backed by evidence (data, experience, case study). Contrarian without proof is just clickbait.
- Challenge the idea, not the people who believe it. Avoid arrogance.
- Provide the alternative. Don't just tear down; offer a better path.
- Use sparingly. 1-2 disruptive pieces per week max. Too many and you become the "always disagrees" person.
