# Kennedy Social Authority

Dan Kennedy's direct response frameworks applied to organic social media. Social is not about being social. It's about being strategic.

## Framework 12: Omnipresence and the "WITH" Strategy

### Omnipresence on Social
Kennedy's principle: your audience should feel like you're everywhere. In social media, this means:

**Consistent visibility > viral moments.** One viral post followed by silence is worse than 5 solid posts per week for 6 months. The algorithm rewards consistency. Your audience remembers consistency.

**Multi-channel presence.** Your ideal buyer follows you on X, sees you on LinkedIn, reads your newsletter, and catches your Instagram stories. They can't escape you. Not in an annoying way. In a "this person is clearly the expert" way.

**The Omnipresence Workflow:**
1. Create one pillar piece per week (newsletter issue, long thread, detailed post)
2. Extract 5-7 derivative pieces across platforms
3. Each derivative adapts to the platform, not just copy-pastes
4. Result: your audience encounters you 3-5 times per week across different surfaces

### The "WITH" Strategy for Social
Proximity to credible people transfers credibility. On social, this looks like:

- Quote and tag respected people in your space (with real commentary, not just "Great insight!")
- Share screenshots of DM conversations with their permission
- Co-create content: joint threads, collaborative posts, live conversations
- Comment thoughtfully on their posts. Their audience sees you. Association builds.
- Interview them for your newsletter, then both promote the piece

The goal is not name-dropping. It's genuine co-creation where both audiences benefit.

### Building Celebrity Expert Status Through Social

**The 4-Post Authority Cycle** (rotate weekly):
1. **Diagnosis post**: Name a problem your audience has but hasn't articulated. "Most newsletter operators think low open rates are a subject line problem. It's almost always a list quality problem."
2. **Proof post**: Show real results, screenshots, data. Not theory. Evidence.
3. **Prescription post**: Give the specific steps to fix the problem you diagnosed. Actionable, detailed, generous.
4. **Authority post**: Share a personal story of building, failing, learning. The behind-the-scenes that proves you've done the work.

## Framework 7: Take-Away Selling on Social

Kennedy's take-away selling principle: "This isn't for everyone. Only serious [type] who [qualifier] should apply."

On social, this is one of the most powerful engagement and conversion tools:

### Why It Works
People want what they can't easily have. When you signal exclusivity, demand increases. Generic "everyone can benefit" messaging creates no urgency.

### Take-Away Posts (Templates)

**The Qualifier Post:**
"I'm looking for 5 newsletter operators who already have 5K+ subscribers and want to add $5K/mo in product revenue. If that's you, comment READY. If you're still building your list, this isn't for you yet."

**The Warning Post:**
"Don't try this if you're not willing to publish 3x/week for 90 days straight. Seriously. If consistency isn't your thing, save this for later. For everyone else, here's the system..."

**The Filter Post:**
"I get asked 'how do I start a newsletter?' 50 times a week. I'm not going to answer that here. Instead, here's what to do when you already have one and want to make it profitable."

### Take-Away Rules
1. Never fake it. If your thing truly is for everyone, don't pretend it's exclusive. But if there's a legitimate qualifier, name it.
2. Be specific about who it IS for, not just who it's NOT for.
3. The take-away creates curiosity. Those who "don't qualify" still read the post and remember you.
4. Follow a take-away post with a generous value post. The contrast builds trust.

## Framework 1: Every Post Moves Toward an Offer

Kennedy's #1 rule: always include an offer. On social, this doesn't mean every post is a pitch. It means every post has a strategic purpose that connects to your revenue.

### The Social-to-Revenue Map

**Tier 1 posts (daily):** Build awareness and engagement. CTA = follow, save, comment. These create the audience that eventually buys.

**Tier 2 posts (1-2x/week):** Deliver so much value they create reciprocity. CTA = join newsletter, grab free resource, DM for template. These move people from social follower to email subscriber.

**Tier 3 posts (1-2x/week):** Bridge to buying. CTA = join waitlist, book a call, grab the $27 pack. These convert warm audience to customers.

### The "Offer Gradient" Rule
Not every offer has to be a sale. The gradient:
1. Follow me (free, lowest commitment)
2. Save this post (free, slightly more commitment)
3. Comment a keyword for [resource] (free, but captures intent)
4. Join my newsletter (free, but gives you their email)
5. Grab this free tool/template (free, but requires signup)
6. Get the $27 pack (paid, lowest ticket)
7. Join the membership (paid, recurring)
8. Book a strategy call (paid, high commitment)

Every post should sit somewhere on this gradient. A post without any next step is a missed opportunity.

### The Comment-Gated Offer
Kennedy's "clear instructions" rule meets social media engagement:
1. Create something valuable (audit, template, tool)
2. Post about it with a preview of what's inside
3. CTA: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the link"
4. Reply to every comment with a landing page link (email required)
5. Comments boost the algorithm, email captures the lead, landing page makes the sale

This is direct response marketing adapted for social algorithms. Kennedy would approve.

## Kennedy Social Checkpoint

Before posting any social content, check these 5 things:

1. **Offer gradient**: Where does this post sit on the offer gradient? (If nowhere, add a next step.)
2. **Omnipresence check**: Am I posting across multiple channels this week? (If only one, repurpose.)
3. **Authority signal**: Does this post position me as the expert? (Diagnosis, proof, unique angle.)
4. **Take-away element**: Am I being selective about who this is for? (Specificity creates desire.)
5. **"WITH" check**: Have I connected with a credible voice this week? (Quote, co-create, engage.)
