## LinkedIn Post Templates

### LinkedIn Post Dimensions

Every LinkedIn post follows these constraints:

- **Hook:** 2 sentences, ~55 characters each (~110 chars total). This is the exact space before "...more" on LinkedIn. Not a style choice -- a platform constraint. As a proxy rule: ~12 words max. Test on mobile before publishing.
- **Body:** 80-150 words (Tier 1 daily posts), 150-300 words (Tier 2 value bombs).
- **Close:** 1-2 sentences. Question OR CTA, never both.
- **Total sweet spot:** 1,200-1,500 characters.
- **One insight per post.** One example. One takeaway. That's it.

### LinkedIn Visual Dimensions

- **Optimal single image:** 1080 x 1350px (3:4 portrait). Takes ~80% of mobile screen.
- **Visual hierarchy:** Image = 80% of screen, hook = 20%, everything else = 0%. The image IS the content.
- **Anti-brand rule:** Scrappy, unbranded visuals outperform polished corporate design. No logos, no brand colors, no stock photos. Hand-drawn/sketch aesthetic wins.
- **Videos underperform on LinkedIn.** People scroll at work. They can't turn on sound. Text + image beats video for reach.
- **Best media type:** Useful cheat sheet or infographic worth bookmarking/forwarding. Content people SAVE, not just like.
- **Carousel vs single image:** Single image gets more raw reach. Carousels get more engagement per viewer. Use single image for growth, carousel for depth.

**Source:** Ruben Hassid (770K LinkedIn followers, 1.56M across 5 accounts)

### The Story Post
```
[Hook: Unexpected outcome or lesson (12 words max)]

[Scene + challenge (2-3 sentences)]

[What you tried / turning point (2-3 sentences)]

[Result (1 sentence, include a number)]

[Lesson (1 sentence)]

[Engagement question (1 sentence)]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 120-200 words. Close 1 sentence.

### The Contrarian Take
```
[Bold opinion (12 words max)]

Here's why:

[Reason 1 (1 sentence)]
[Reason 2 (1 sentence)]
[Reason 3 (1 sentence)]

[What you recommend instead (1-2 sentences)]

[Invite discussion (1 sentence)]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 80-120 words. Close 1 sentence.

### The List Post
```
[X things I learned about [topic] after [credibility builder] (12 words max):

1. [Point]: [1-sentence explanation]

2. [Point]: [1-sentence explanation]

3. [Point]: [1-sentence explanation]

[Wrap-up insight (1 sentence)]

[Engagement question (1 sentence)]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 100-150 words. Close 1 sentence.

### The How-To
```
How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe] (12 words max):

Step 1: [Action]
↳ [Why this matters (1 sentence)]

Step 2: [Action]
↳ [Key detail (1 sentence)]

Step 3: [Action]
↳ [Common mistake to avoid (1 sentence)]

[Result you can expect (1 sentence)]

[CTA or question (1 sentence)]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 100-150 words. Close 1 sentence.

### The Cheatsheet Post (Save Magnet)
```
[Action hook: "I [did dramatic thing]." (12 words max)]

[Escalation: "Not just [X]. [Y-ing] it. [Z-ing] it." (1 sentence)]

[Knowledge gap: "If you're doing [thing] without [setup], you're leaving [X]% on the table." (1 sentence)]

[Framework name: "It's a [N]-layer system:"]

[Layer/Section 1]: [One-line explanation]
[Layer/Section 2]: [One-line explanation]
[Layer/Section 3]: [One-line explanation]

[ATTACH: Cheatsheet infographic that delivers the full breakdown]

[Keyword CTA: "Drop [KEYWORD] in the comments and I'll send it to you directly." (1 sentence)]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 120-180 words. Close 1 sentence.

Why this works:
- Cheatsheets get SAVED. Saves are the #1 LinkedIn algorithm signal.
- The post creates desire. The infographic delivers value. Complete loop.
- The keyword CTA ("DROP BUILD") is low-friction and generates engagement signals.
- Framework naming makes it shareable (sharing named frameworks makes the READER look smart).
- The reader gets full value without clicking any link or leaving LinkedIn.

### The Authority Action Hook
```
[Action verb opening: "I spent [time] [doing thing]." (12 words max)]

[Escalation: "Not just [basic]. [Intermediate]. [Advanced]." (1 sentence)]

[Three short sentences. Each one escalates commitment.]

["Using it" -> "Building with it" -> "Breaking it"]
["Reading about it" -> "Testing it" -> "Rebuilding my entire workflow around it"]
```
Target: Hook 12 words. Body 80-120 words. This template IS the hook (use as opening for a longer post).

Why this works:
- Shows depth through ACTIONS, not credentials
- Reader infers expertise without being told
- Three escalating verbs create momentum

## Twitter/X Thread Templates

### The Tutorial Thread
```
Tweet 1: [Hook + promise of value]

"Here's exactly how to [outcome] (step-by-step):"

Tweet 2-7: [One step per tweet with details]

Final tweet: [Summary + CTA]

"If this was helpful, follow me for more on [topic]"
```

### The Story Thread
```
Tweet 1: [Intriguing hook]

"[Time] ago, [unexpected thing happened]. Here's the full story:"

Tweet 2-6: [Story beats, building tension]

Tweet 7: [Resolution and lesson]

Final tweet: [Takeaway + engagement ask]
```

### The Breakdown Thread
```
Tweet 1: [Company/person] just [did thing].

Here's why it's genius (and what you can learn):

Tweet 2-6: [Analysis points]

Tweet 7: [Your key takeaway]

"[Related insight + follow CTA]"
```

## Instagram Templates

### The Carousel Hook
```
[Slide 1: Bold statement or question]
[Slides 2-9: One point per slide, visual + text]
[Slide 10: Summary + CTA]

Caption: [Expand on the topic, add context, include CTA]
```

### The Reel Script
```
Hook (0-2 sec): [Pattern interrupt or bold claim]
Setup (2-5 sec): [Context for the tip]
Value (5-25 sec): [The actual advice/content]
CTA (25-30 sec): [Follow, comment, share, link]
```

## Hook Formulas

The first line determines whether anyone reads the rest.

### Curiosity Hooks
- "I was wrong about [common belief]."
- "The real reason [outcome] happens isn't what you think."
- "[Impressive result]: and it only took [surprisingly short time]."
- "Nobody talks about [insider knowledge]."

### Story Hooks
- "Last week, [unexpected thing] happened."
- "I almost [big mistake/failure]."
- "3 years ago, I [past state]. Today, [current state]."
- "[Person] told me something I'll never forget."

### Value Hooks
- "How to [desirable outcome] (without [common pain]):"
- "[Number] [things] that [outcome]:"
- "The simplest way to [outcome]:"
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead:"

### Contrarian Hooks
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"
- "I stopped [common practice] and [positive result]."
- "Everyone says [X]. The truth is [Y]."

### Social Proof Hooks
- "We [achieved result] in [timeframe]. Here's the full story:"
- "[Number] people asked me about [topic]. Here's my answer:"
- "[Authority figure] taught me [lesson]."
