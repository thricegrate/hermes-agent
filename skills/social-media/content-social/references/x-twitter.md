# X/Twitter Virality Framework

Expert X/Twitter content strategy and copywriting that reverse-engineers virality. Maximize reach, engagement, and bookmarks by combining proven copywriting principles with platform-native mechanics.

## The 9 Virality Levers

Every high-performing tweet activates one or more of these 9 levers. **The best tweets stack 3-5 simultaneously.**

### Lever 1: Perceived Value Density
Pack maximum takeaway value into minimum reading effort. The reader should think "I can't afford to lose this."

- Numbered resource lists (9 guides, 72+ tools, 700+ prompts)
- Copy-paste-ready prompts with `[YOUR X]` placeholders
- Categorized tool roundups organized by use case
- Dollar-value anchoring ("replaces $150K/year work")

### Lever 2: The Hook
The first line determines everything. It must stop the scroll in <1 second.

**Emotional Hook Rule (MANDATORY):** Every hook must trigger a gut-level emotion. Pick your target: WOW, WTF, OMG, LOL, UGH, WHOA, NAH, YEP, OOF, HMM, DAMN, SMH. On X/Twitter, use the abbreviation literally as the opening word. "WTF -- AI just passed the bar exam with a higher score than 90% of lawyers" hits harder than "AI achieved remarkable results on the bar exam." The emotion IS the scroll-stopper. See `skills/humanizer/SKILL.md` for the full list.

**Hook categories:**
- **BREAKING/News**: "BREAKING: AI can now build financial models like Goldman Sachs analysts"
- **Shock number**: "700+ prompts that replace a $5,000 consultant"
- **Anti-overwhelm**: "AI doesn't need to be overwhelming"
- **Pain recognition**: "Everyone's hyped about Claude... barely anyone knows how to use it"
- **Transformation claim**: "These 12 prompts replaced $150K/year of investment banking work"
- **Contrarian**: "Most people are learning AI wrong"

**Complete hook formula library:** See [hook-formulas.md](hook-formulas.md)

### Lever 3: Save-Bait Architecture
Design every tweet as a **reference resource** people save for later, not just read once. Bookmarks are a high-value deep engagement signal on X, tracked separately from likes. But replies carry 10x the Earlybird score weight (10,000 pts vs 1,000 for likes). The best content generates both: utility (bookmarks) + controversy or question (replies). Design for replies first, bookmarks second.

- Structure content as a searchable list, not prose
- Include actionable items (prompts, tools, links)
- Add explicit "Save this" or "Bookmark for later" CTA
- Make the content hard to memorize but easy to reference

### Lever 4: Engagement Mechanics
Build in reasons for people to reply, quote, and retweet.

- **Comment-DM bait**: "Comment 'PROMPTS' and I'll DM you the full list" (drives 2K-4K replies)
- **Completion gaps**: Lists that invite "You missed [X]" replies
- **Multi-CTAs**: Like + Bookmark + Follow woven naturally through threads
- **Provocative questions**: Statements that split the audience into camps

**Why replies dominate everything else:** In X's Earlybird scoring system, each reply is worth 10,000 points vs 1,000 for a like or quote. A tweet with 20 replies and 50 likes scores 250,000. A tweet with 0 replies and 500 likes scores 500,000. The reply-heavy tweet has fewer total engagements but is competitive -- and replies also trigger UTEG social proof (threshold: just 1 connected engagement), which unlocks out-of-network distribution. Small accounts get an additional MTL normalization boost. See [x-algorithm-source.md](x-algorithm-source.md) for the full viral loop with actual thresholds.

### Lever 5: Status Transfer
People share content that makes *them* look smart, informed, or generous.

- Tool lists = "I'm on top of the latest AI"
- Research synthesis = "I engage with serious analysis"
- Free resources = "I'm generous and helpful"
- Contrarian takes = "I think independently"

### Lever 6: Specificity & Numbers
Specific numbers outperform vague claims on every metric. Use exact numbers: "72+" beats "dozens of." Add dollar values. Odd/specific numbers > round numbers for believability.

### Lever 7: Temporal Urgency
Year tags ("AI Tools 2026"), newsjacking, scarcity ("Free for 24 hours only"), FOMO framing.

### Lever 8: Authority & Social Proof
Name-drop prestigious institutions, cite real data, reference your own results, note cross-AI compatibility.

### Lever 9: Visual Scroll-Stop
Screenshots, infographics, before/after comparisons. The feed is text-heavy; visuals are pattern interrupts. First image visible in the card is the hook.

## Tweet Formats

### Single Tweet (280 chars)
Best for: Hot takes, teaser hooks, engagement questions, single-insight bombs.

```
[Hook line, stop the scroll]
[1-2 supporting lines]
[CTA or open loop]
```

### Thread (3-15 tweets)
Best for: Tutorials, tool roundups, prompt collections, deep dives, storytelling.

```
Tweet 1: Hook + promise ("Here are 12 Claude prompts that replace $150K/year work")
Tweet 2: Context or credibility ("I spent 40 hours testing every AI tool...")
Tweets 3-N: The meat (one item per tweet, numbered, scannable)
Final tweet: Recap + multi-CTA ("If this was useful: 1. Bookmark it 2. Follow for more 3. RT the first tweet")
```

**Fill-in-the-blank thread template:** See [../templates/tweet-thread.md](../templates/tweet-thread.md)

### X Article (long-form)
Best for: Deep analysis, original research, comprehensive guides.

```
Title: [Number] + [Topic] + [Value promise]
Opening: Hook paragraph (same rules as tweet hooks)
Body: Structured sections with headers
Closing: Summary + CTA
```

## Writing Workflow

### Step 1: Choose Your Virality Stack
Pick 3-5 levers. The combination determines your approach:

| Stack | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Value Density + Numbers + Save-Bait | "72+ AI tools to finish months of work in minutes" |
| Hook + Authority + Status Transfer | "BREAKING: AI can now build models like Goldman Sachs" |
| Anti-overwhelm + Specificity + Engagement | "Just automate ONE workflow per week. In 3 months = 12 automated." |
| Contrarian + Data + Authority | Academic synthesis post with real study citations |
| Pain Recognition + Resources + Comment-Bait | "Everyone's hyped but barely anyone knows... Comment X for the list" |

### Step 2: Write the Hook First
Write 5+ hook variations before writing the body. The hook carries 80% of a tweet's performance. Test against: scroll-stop test, curiosity gap, specificity test, screenshot test.

### Step 3: Build the Body
One idea per tweet, one tweet per idea. Number every item. Bold the key phrase. Include `[YOUR X]` placeholders. Use line breaks generously.

### Step 4: Engineer the CTAs
Layer them: soft save in tweet 1, engagement bait mid-thread, multi-CTA in final tweet.

### Step 5: Add Visuals
Screenshot prompts in Claude/ChatGPT UI. Create summary infographics. Before/after comparisons.

### Step 6: Timing & Publishing
Best windows: 8-10am and 12-1pm (audience timezone). Tue-Thu outperform weekends. Engage heavily first 30 minutes. Quote-tweet your own thread 2-3 hours later with a different hook angle.

## Storytelling Techniques for Threads

### Question Framework
Frame threads around ONE driving question, not a topic. "Can AI actually replace a $150K analyst?" creates tension.

### Full-Circle Callback
End by returning to the opening line with new meaning. Makes the thread feel complete and shareable.

### Hidden Thesis
Don't state your conclusion in tweet 1. Let the thread prove it. State the thesis only in the last tweet.

### "But/Therefore" Thread Flow
Never connect thread beats with "and then." Use "but" (conflict) and "therefore" (consequence).

### Naming the Abstract
Give tools and concepts a name. "The Algorithm Gods" instead of "the algorithm." Named things are quotable and shareable.

### Vulnerability Break
One raw, honest tweet in an otherwise polished thread builds massive trust. Use once per thread max.

## Quality Standards

### The Slop Test
Before posting, check for: "In today's..." or "In the ever-evolving...", words like "leverage," "utilize," "revolutionize," "game-changer," "cutting-edge", hedges like "might," "could potentially," passive voice, empty enthusiasm.

If you find any, rewrite in direct, opinionated, human voice.

### Lines You Don't Cross
- Never fabricate metrics
- Never promise unrealistic outcomes
- Never use pure engagement bait with no substance
- Credit sources
- Avoid AI slop

## Common Mistakes

- Burying the hook: If the best line is tweet 3, move it to tweet 1
- Thread intros that add nothing without specific credibility
- No CTA: A thread without a CTA is a gift without a card
- Generic lists with no angle, no opinion, no curation thesis
- Over-length threads: If it can be 7 tweets, don't make it 15
- Ignoring the first 30 minutes of comments
- All text, no visuals (screenshots increase engagement 2-3x)
- Writing for likes instead of bookmarks
- Same hook structure every time
