# Velocity Viral Giveaway Framework (LinkedIn Lead Magnet Distribution)

**Source:** Daniel Bustamante (Velocity) -- built 30+ lead magnets, millions of impressions, 15K+ LinkedIn followers, 9K+ newsletter subs from LinkedIn alone.

## The 6-Part Viral Giveaway Post Framework

This is the highest-performing LinkedIn post format for lead magnet distribution. One post generated 3,704 comments and 1,464 likes. Another got 700 email subscribers with 83%+ opt-in rate. 90-95% of conversions happen in first 48 hours.

### 1. The Hook (First 2-3 lines)
- Create a curiosity gap with specific numbers
- End with a colon to encourage "see more" clicks
- Example: "I've landed multiple $5,000-$10,000 clients thanks to my newsletter"

### 2. The Big Promise
- State the transformation or problem solved within 3 seconds of expanded post view
- Be specific about the outcome, not the process

### 3. The Reason Why
- Explain WHY you're giving it away free
- Increases compliance through psychological principle (Cialdini's "reason why")
- Example: "One of my favorite parts of my job? Brainstorming lead magnets for a living."

### 4. The Offer Stack
- List ALL deliverables using bullet points
- Use price anchoring to elevate perceived value
- Example: "2 templates + 2 AI prompts + 1 eight-step guide"
- Never say "free PDF" -- stack the components

### 5. The Crystal Clear CTA
- Direct instructions: like the post, comment specific text, connect for DM
- Use a keyword comment: "Comment '10k' and I'll DM you"
- The keyword creates viral loop (every comment = visibility to commenter's network)

### 6. The Strategic PS
- Handle objections, add social proof, or tease bonuses
- Note: must be connected or send connection request for DM (creates network growth)

## Comment-Gated Distribution Model

This is the growth engine:
1. Post with comment-gated CTA ("comment X to get it")
2. Every comment surfaces post to commenter's network (algorithmic boost)
3. DM the landing page link after comment
4. Landing page captures email
5. Confirmation page upsells (see cro-funnel references)

**Why it works:** LinkedIn algorithm rewards comments more than likes. Each comment = free distribution to a new network. The keyword CTA reduces friction ("just type one word").

## Post Formatting Rules
- 800-1,200 characters optimal
- Short paragraphs (max 3 lines on mobile)
- Bullet points using the dot symbol
- Conversational tone
- Zero emojis
- No scheduling ahead -- stay nimble and reactive

## The Series Format (Viral Content Engine)
Daniel discovered "30 Days of AI Prompts" series format:
- Provides constraints (reduces decision fatigue)
- High virality (doing something unique daily for 30 days)
- "Double dip" monetization (repurpose into newsletters, lead magnets, paid products)
- One post about this format got 222 likes + 355 comments
