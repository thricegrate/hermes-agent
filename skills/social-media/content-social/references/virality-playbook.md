## The 16 Viral Techniques

### 1. Free High-Value Packs
Full copy-paste-ready prompts, PDFs, or tool lists that deliver immediate, tangible value. The content should feel like it could be a paid product given away free.

**Why it works:** Triggers loss aversion ("If I don't save this, I lose $X of value"), reciprocity ("they gave me this for free, I should at least bookmark/follow"), and utility-driven bookmarking.

**Examples with metrics:**
- @heynavtoor: 12 Claude finance prompts: **3.85M views, 42K bookmarks**
- @aiwithjainam: 10 life prompts: **3.5M views**
- @eyishazyer: ChatGPT cheat sheet: **3.1M views**
- @godofprompt: Claude Mastery guide: **177K views**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Here are 12 insane Claude Opus 4.6 prompts that replace $5,000 consultant"
- "9 of the best guides you can't miss"
- "A compilation of FREE Claude resources"

**Implementation:**
- Include actual prompts with `[YOUR X]` placeholder slots
- Make them immediately usable: zero setup required
- Organize by use case (finance, writing, coding, research)
- Anchor dollar value: "These replace $X/year of [expensive profession] work"

### 2. Comment-DM Bait
Ask users to comment a specific word to receive the resource via DM. This drives massive reply counts (2K-4K+), which is the strongest algorithmic signal for distribution.

**Why it works:** Replies are weighted 10x more than likes in X's Earlybird scoring system (10,000 points vs 1,000 per like). A post with 3K replies will be shown to exponentially more people than one with 3K likes. It also builds a DM list for future nurture.

**Examples with metrics:**
- @charliejhills: "Comment REALITY": **4.6K replies**
- @heynavtoor: "Comment 700+": **3.7K replies**
- @Med1_Ai: "Comment $250": **23K replies**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Comment "AI" and I'll DM you everything"
- "Like this post and comment Al - I'll send you my ultimate guide"
- "Reply "AI" ... Follow me (so that I can DM)"

**Implementation:**
- Keep the comment word short and memorable (1 word, ALL CAPS)
- Place the CTA after the hook, not before: "Here are 12 prompts... Comment PROMPTS and I'll DM you the full list"
- Actually deliver via DM (use automation tools like Tweet Hunter or Typefully)
- Follow up DMs with a second resource + newsletter/follow CTA

### 3. Hyped-But-Barely Pain
Open with the exact frustration your audience feels: "Everyone's hyped about [tool]... barely anyone knows how to actually use it." This names the gap between aspiration and reality.

**Why it works:** Hits the *awareness-competence gap*: people know AI matters but feel behind. This hook validates their frustration and positions your content as the bridge.

**Examples:**
- @heyrimsha: Claude "hyped but barely know" opener: **100K+ views**
- @farhana: Same pattern, different tool: **100K+ views**
- @jamescoder12: 700+ Claude prompts with this framing: **100K+ views**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Everyone is hyped about Claude... but barely anyone knows how to actually use it"
- "Everyone is hyped about Gemini 3.0 Pro... but barely anyone knows how"
- "Everyone is hyped about Claude... but barely anyone knows how"

**Implementation:**
- Formula: "Everyone's hyped about [X]... but barely anyone knows [Y]"
- Follow with: "Here are [N] [specific resources] to actually master it"
- Works best for newly launched or recently updated tools

### 4. Huge Numbers in Headlines
Large, specific numbers in the first line (700+, 300+, 72+, 135+) signal massive value and trigger completionist psychology.

**Why it works:** The brain processes numbers faster than words. A large number creates a "wait, what?" pattern interrupt. At a certain scale, quantity itself becomes the value proposition: even if the reader only uses 5 out of 700, the perceived option value is enormous.

**Examples with metrics:**
- @bloggersarvesh: 7 Claude SEO prompts: **3.6M views**
- 120+ AI tools threads: consistently **500K-2M views**
- @Shruti_0810: 72+ AI tools list: **high engagement, massive bookmarks**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Here are 12 insane Claude Opus 4.6 prompts"
- "I collected 700+ mega prompts"
- "120 Must-Use AI Tools" / "1000+ GPT-4 Prompts"

**Implementation:**
- Use exact numbers, not ranges: "72+" not "dozens of"
- Odd/specific numbers outperform round ones (72 > 70, 137 > 100)
- "+" suffix adds open-endedness: "700+" implies "and growing"
- Front-load the number: "700+ prompts that..." not "A collection of prompts (700+)..."

### 5. Timely FOMO / Newsjacking
Tie content to breaking news, product launches, or trending topics. "BREAKING" + new release = urgency + novelty.

**Why it works:** Combines news-alert pattern interruption with fear of missing out. The reader calculates: "If everyone else learns this before me, I'm behind."

**Examples with metrics:**
- @heynavtoor: Claude finance prompts on launch day: **3.85M views**
- @godofprompt: Recursive Meta-Cognition timing: **2.7M views**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "BREAKING: AI can now do market research like McKinsey"
- "BREAKING: Claude can now do SEO like a $10,000/month agency"
- "OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic just published guides"

**Implementation:**
- Monitor product launches (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) and publish within 24 hours
- Use "BREAKING:" prefix for genuinely new capabilities
- Combine with resource content: "BREAKING: [tool] just launched [feature]. Here are [N] prompts to use it"
- Don't fake urgency: audiences learn to ignore false alarms

### 6. Strong Visuals / Scroll-Stoppers
Attach screenshots, preview images, or infographics as the first image. In a text-heavy feed, visuals are pattern interrupts.

**Why it works:** The X feed is 90% text. An image or screenshot stops the scroll physically: your eyes are drawn to visual contrast. Screenshots of tools, prompts in action, or results provide instant credibility.

**Examples:**
- All top-10 performing AI tweets (Med1, heynavtoor, socialwithaayan) include at least one image
- Tool screenshots with UI visible outperform generic stock imagery

**Real tweet texts (proven viral: save-CTA variations that accompany visuals):**
- "(Save for later)"
- "Save this future you will thank you"
- "(you'll want to save this)"

**Implementation:**
- Screenshot the prompt inside Claude/ChatGPT UI (shows it's real)
- Create simple summary infographics (numbered list on a clean background)
- Before/after screenshots for transformation claims
- First image = the hook image. Make it count: it appears in the card preview

### 7. Scannable Numbered Copy-Paste Format
Number every item (1/12, 2/12...), bold the key phrase, include `[YOUR X]` placeholder slots. Zero friction from read to use.

**Why it works:** Reduces cognitive load to near-zero. The reader doesn't have to interpret: they copy, paste, fill in the blanks, and get value immediately. This is the difference between "interesting" and "useful."

**Examples:**
- @socialwithaayan: McKinsey 12 prompts: numbered, bolded, with placeholders
- Every 1M+ view prompt thread uses this format

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "1/ Market Sizing & TAM Analysis You are a McKinsey-level..."
- "1. Claude Cowork can fully analyze..."
- "1. Agent Skills Marketplace..."

**Implementation:**
```
1/ **Sales Coach Prompt**
"You are a world-class sales coach specializing in [YOUR INDUSTRY].
I sell [YOUR PRODUCT] to [YOUR CUSTOMER TYPE] at [YOUR PRICE POINT].
My current challenge is [YOUR BIGGEST SALES OBSTACLE].
Give me a 30-day action plan to close 3x more deals."
```

**Why it works:** Dollar-sign anchoring creates instant perceived value. The reader calculates: "Even if these prompts replace 10% of that work, they're worth $15K to me." The gap between $0 (free content) and $150K (claimed value) is irresistible.

**Examples with metrics:**
- @Med1_Ai: $250/day ChatGPT: **3.4M views, 26K likes**
- @heynavtoor: Replaces $150K IB work: **3.85M views**

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "replace $5,000 consultant"
- "ChatGPT + Laptop + Internet + 1 Hour a Day = $250 Daily"
- "can take your biz to $100k/month"

**Implementation:**
- Anchor to real-world salaries or consulting fees
- Be specific: "$150K/year investment banking work" > "$$$"
- The content must genuinely deliver prompts/tools that do professional-grade work
- Frame as replacement, not earning: "replaces $X work" is more believable than "earn $X"

### 11. Urgency / Artificial Scarcity
"Free for 24 hours only" or "Deleting this in 48h." Creates immediate action pressure.

**Why it works:** Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain motivation. The fear of losing access is more powerful than the excitement of getting it.

**Examples:**
- @Med1_Ai: 24hr free guide: drives instant saves and shares

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Worth $67, but free today! ... Available free for 24 hours"
- "And for 24 hours, it's 100% FREE!"
- "(Must follow to receive the DM)"

**Implementation:**
- Time-box the offer: "Free for 24h" or "available this week only"
- Combine with comment-DM bait for maximum urgency
- Only use when you'll actually follow through (removing the resource)
- Don't overuse: audiences develop immunity to false scarcity

### 12. Multi-CTAs (Stacked Engagement Signals)
Like + Comment + Save + RT + Follow woven through every tweet. Each action feeds a different algorithmic signal.

**Why it works:** X's algorithm weighs different engagement types differently. A post with likes + replies + bookmarks + retweets fires on all cylinders. Most people only do 1 action: if you ask for 4, you get 1-2 on average.

**Examples:**
- 90% of million-view AI posts end with multi-CTA
- Standard format: "If this was useful: 1) Bookmark it 2) Follow me 3) RT tweet 1"

**Real tweet texts (proven viral):**
- "Like this post and comment Al ... (Must follow)"
- "Save this ... Follow @jason_coder0"
- "Save for later ... Follow me @socialwithaayan ... Share it"

**Implementation:**
- Place in the final tweet of a thread
- Frame as reciprocity: "If this saved you time..."
- Limit to 3-4 actions (more feels desperate)
- Ask for replies first, bookmarks second. Replies carry 10x the Earlybird score weight (10,000 pts vs 1,000 for likes). Bookmarks are a separate deep engagement signal. Both matter, but replies drive distribution.

### 13. Thread Structure (Numbered Deep Dive)
Numbered tweets (1/12) with a clear progression. Holds attention across multiple tweets and creates a completionist pull.

**Why it works:** Threading creates a narrative arc. The numbered format ("1/12") signals finite, valuable content. Each tweet should deliver standalone value while pulling the reader to the next.

**Examples:**
- Every 1M+ view AI thread uses numbered format
- 7-12 tweets is the sweet spot (enough depth, not exhausting)

**Real tweet texts (proven viral thread numbering):**
- "1/ Market Sizing ... 2/ Competitive ... 12/ Executive Strategy"
- "1. Claude Cowork ... 2. Claude Cowork to do ... 7. Use Claude Cowork"
- "1. Agent Skills ... 2. MCP Tools ... 5. Awesome Claude Code"

**Implementation:**
- Hook tweet (1/N) is the most important: it carries the thread
- Each tweet should be independently valuable (screenshot-worthy)
- End each tweet with a soft hook to the next
- Final tweet recaps and includes multi-CTA

### 15. High Bookmark / Utility Content
Pure utility content designed to be saved and re-referenced. "Save this" is the explicit or implied CTA.

**Why it works:** Bookmarks are a high-value deep engagement signal tracked separately from likes. They indicate: "This is so useful I need to come back to it." While replies carry 10x the Earlybird score weight (10,000 pts), bookmarks signal utility to the algorithm's quality assessment. Content with high bookmark-to-like ratios gets massively amplified.

**Examples with metrics:**
- @socialwithaayan: McKinsey prompts: **32K bookmarks**
- @Remotion threads: **97K bookmarks**
- Bookmark-to-like ratio of 0.3+ is exceptional (most content is 0.05-0.1)

**Real tweet texts (proven viral save-CTAs):**
- "(Save for later)"
- "you'll want to save this"
- "Save this future you will thank you"

**Implementation:**
- Structure as a reference resource, not a one-time read
- Include actionable items that require saving (prompts, commands, URLs)
- Make content hard to memorize but easy to reference
- Add "(Save for later)" or "(Bookmark this)" as soft CTAs in tweet 1

## The 7 Cross-Cutting Patterns

These are the structural meta-patterns observed across all high-performing AI tweets.

### Pattern 1: The Curation Economy
Every viral tweet is fundamentally about **curation**: filtering, organizing, and presenting existing information. None invented new tools or discovered new research. In an information-overloaded world, curation IS creation.

**Application:** You don't need original research. You need original *organization* of existing resources.

### Pattern 2: Save-Bait Architecture
Optimized for **bookmarks**, not likes. Every tweet is structured as a reference resource, something you return to, not just consume once.

**Metric:** Aim for bookmark-to-impression ratio > 1%. Bookmark-to-like ratio > 0.2.

### Pattern 3: Specificity Over Generality
Numbers dominate: 9, 12, 72+, 700+, $150K, $400K. Specific categories. Named tools. Named institutions. Specificity signals effort, credibility, and usefulness.

**Test:** Replace every vague word with a specific one. If you can add a number, add a number.

### Pattern 4: The Anxiety-to-Empowerment Pipeline
Every tweet addresses an implicit fear and converts it into agency:
- "Am I falling behind on AI?" → Here's what to learn
- "Is AI going to replace me?" → Here's how to use it first
- "Am I wasting money on tools?" → Here are free alternatives
- "Is AI actually useful?" → Here's what the data says
- "Will I get in trouble using AI?" → Here's the ethical framework

**Application:** Identify your audience's fear first, then position your content as the bridge to empowerment.

### Pattern 5: "I Did The Work So You Don't Have To"
Every author positions themselves as having done extensive research, testing, or synthesis. This triggers reciprocity and trust.

**Application:** Quantify your effort: "I tested 50+ tools," "I reviewed every study I could find," "After 40 hours of research..."

### Pattern 6: Status-Transfer Sharing
People share content because sharing it makes *them* look smart, informed, or generous.

**Application:** Before publishing, ask: "What does sharing this say about the sharer?" If the answer is "nothing," rework it.

### Pattern 7: Algorithmic Alignment
The best tweets hit all of X's engagement signals simultaneously:
- High bookmark rate (resource content)
- High reply rate (lists invite additions, comment-bait drives replies)
- High quote-tweet rate (provocative angles generate commentary)
- Long dwell time (threads take time to read)
- Click-through on links (articles, tools)

## Platform Algorithm Mechanics

### What X's Algorithm Rewards (From Earlybird Source Code)

| Signal | Earlybird Weight | How to Trigger |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Replies | 10,000 pts each | Comment-bait, provocative questions, list gaps, "what's yours?" |
| Likes | 1,000 pts each | General engagement (but 10x less valuable than replies) |
| Quote Tweets | 1,000 pts each | Provocative takes, debates |
| Bookmarks | Distinct deep signal | Utility content, prompts, tool lists (tracked separately) |
| Shares | Distinct deep signal | Status-transfer content (tracked separately) |
| Retweets | Separate distribution | Controversy, high-value resources |
| Dwell Time | Ranking factor | Threads, long-form, images that take time to read |
| Video Watch Time | Ranking factor | For video content specifically |
| Link Clicks | Deprioritized | External links hurt native reach |
| Out-of-network eligibility | UTEG threshold: 1 like | Just 1 like from a connected user unlocks OON distribution |

|--------|---------|
| "Not Interested" clicks | Up to -10,000 (wipes out 1 reply or 10 likes) |
| Reports | Up to -20,000 (nuclear) |
| Mutes/blocks | -1,000 each |
| Out-of-network penalty | 0.75x multiplier on all OON tweets |
| Same-author fatigue | Decays to 0.25x after multiple tweets shown |

### Post-Publish Playbook
1. **First 30 minutes:** Reply to every comment. This signals the algorithm the conversation is active.
2. **Hour 1-2:** Quote-tweet your own thread with a different hook angle.
3. **Hour 3-6:** If traction is building, add a "bonus" reply-tweet to the thread.
4. **Next day:** Share a "ICYMI" repost or a remixed version of the best-performing tweet.

## Anti-Patterns That Kill Reach

1. **Thread intros that add nothing**: "I spent 3 months researching this..." without specific credibility is filler
2. **External links in tweet 1**: X deprioritizes tweets with outbound links. Put links in replies or later tweets
3. **Posting and ghosting**: No replies in the first hour = algorithm death
4. **Same hook format every time**: Audiences develop pattern blindness. Rotate between hook types
5. **Engagement pods / like-for-like**: X's algorithm detects and penalizes artificial engagement clusters
6. **Over-frequency**: 10 threads/day dilutes each one. 1-2 high-quality threads/day maximum
7. **Generic AI slop tone**: "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..." is an instant unfollow
8. **Broken promises**: If the hook says "12 prompts" and the thread only has 8, you lose trust permanently
9. **No visuals on prompt content**: Text-only prompt threads get 50-70% less engagement than those with screenshots
10. **Ignoring bookmark optimization**: Designing for likes instead of saves is optimizing for the wrong metric
