## Platform Strategies

### X/Twitter

**Cadence:** 5 posts/day -- 4 regular text tweets + 1 long-form/Atomic Essay/thread with media.

**Post times (ET):** 6:30 AM, 8:30 AM, 2:30 PM, 5:30 PM, 8:30 PM (spaced through the day).

**Auto-retweet:** Every post gets automatically retweeted 9 hours later via Hypefury.

**6 Trending Formats:**

1. **The Paragraph Tweet** -- longer-form single tweet, dense with value
2. **The What / How / Why Tweet** -- structured breakdown of a concept
3. **The Atomic Essay** -- text-based image packed with more than 280 characters of value
4. **The Thread + Media** -- multi-tweet thread with videos/images per lesson
5. **The Long-Form + Media** -- extended single post with supporting media
6. **The Handwritten Infographic** -- hand-drawn visual (hire on Upwork/Fiverr)

**4 Key Trends:**

1. **Algorithm constantly changing.** Frequent updates in 2024 caused dips, but sticking to consistent formats/cadence paid off long-term.
2. **Followers matter less.** ~3/10 tweets in "For You" are from accounts you follow. Good content finds its audience regardless of account size -- idea meritocracy.
3. **Comments matter more.** Strong correlation between engagements and distribution. You can borrow distribution by leaving insightful replies under big creators' posts.
4. **Post + autoplug congruence matters more.** Generic CTAs on unrelated posts no longer convert. The CTA must match the post topic.

**Analytics to track:** Views, Likes, Comments, Reposts, Bookmarks, New Followers. Beginners: skim weekly performance. Advanced: track Impressions (Reach) + New Followers in a spreadsheet.

### LinkedIn

**Cadence:** 1 post/day (possibly 2 -- testing). Every post MUST include media (clear distribution bump for posts with media + high engagement time on media).

**Post times (ET):** 8:30 AM (primary), 8:30 PM (if posting twice).

**5 Trending Formats:**

1. **The Long-Form + Media** -- extended text post with supporting image/video
2. **The Carousel** -- multi-slide visual content (best distribution of all formats)
3. **The Tweet Shot** -- screenshot of a tweet as the media element
4. **The Short-Form Video + Long-Form Text** -- video paired with detailed written post
5. **The Handwritten Infographic** -- hand-drawn visual explanation

**3 Key Trends:**

1. **Comments matter more than ever.** LinkedIn ranks comments by insightfulness. You can jump to top of comment pool by adding genuinely valuable takes.
2. **Carousels get the best distribution.** High engagement from click-through behavior signals the algorithm. Key: unique/branded design, not generic templates.
3. **Video beginning to get additional distribution.** Cole/Bush posting 1 video/week, planning to increase to 2. Had multiple videos go mega-viral (millions of views) in 2024.

**Growth rate benchmark:** ~30-50K views per post, ~1,000 new followers per week (consistent linear growth, fewer viral spikes).

**Analytics tool:** Shield for LinkedIn analytics.

### YouTube

**Cadence:** 2 long-form videos/week + as many Shorts as possible (biggest creators do 8+ Shorts/day, every 3 hours).

**Post times (ET):** 8:30 AM for long-form (post when ready). Every 3 hours for Shorts (volume goal).

**4 Trending Formats:**

1. **Fully Scripted, High-Quality Production** -- multi-environment filming, high-quality graphics
2. **One Take, Direct To Camera** -- unscripted, honest advice, talk like a friend over coffee
3. **Repurposed Video** -- webinars, podcasts uploaded unedited. People appreciate the raw value.
4. **Loom Recording** -- great for beginners and SEO flooding. Low effort, high volume.

**3 Key Trends:**

1. **Zero correlation between production quality and performance.** Low-budget talking heads can outperform 2-week edited productions. Value of content is what matters.
2. **YouTube customers have the highest intent to buy.** Smallest channel by audience size but most profitable. Video creates intimacy and trust that converts.
3. **Thumbnails matter more than production quality.** Upgrading thumbnails drove significantly more growth. If choosing between video editor and thumbnail designer, pick the thumbnail designer.

**Long-form keyword optimization:** Treat YouTube like an SEO platform. Type your niche into YouTube search bar, use the autocomplete suggestions as video titles.

**Analytics to track:** Views, Watch Time, Audience Retention, Average View Duration, Subscriber Growth, Impressions & CTR.

## Social Content Generator Framework (4 Steps)

### Step 1: Choose Your 3 Content Buckets

1. **Futurism** -- the future of your niche and where things are headed
2. **Your Story** -- lessons learned from your experience (relevant to the reader)
3. **Actionable Advice** -- how to do something specific in your niche

(Optional 4th bucket: **News** -- not as timeless, becomes outdated quickly.)

### Step 2: 10 Magical Ways to Expand Any Idea

Take any topic and run it through these 10 lenses:

1. **Tips** -- practical advice
2. **Stats** -- data that proves a point
3. **Steps** -- sequential how-to
4. **Lessons** -- what you learned from experience
5. **Benefits** -- why someone should care/act
6. **Reasons** -- arguments for a position
7. **Mistakes** -- common errors to avoid
8. **Examples** -- real-world case studies
9. **Questions** -- what beginners/experts ask
10. **Personal Stories** -- narrative from your own life

**Bucket mapping (flexible, not rigid):**

- Futurism: Stats, Benefits, Reasons, Questions
- Your Story: Mistakes, Lessons, Personal Stories
- Actionable Advice: Tips, Steps, Examples

Result: 1 topic becomes 10 unique angles.

### Step 3: Change the Audience Archetype (multiply x5)

Take each of the 10 angles and tailor it to different audience segments within your niche. Example for "Tips to buy your first rental property":

- Tips for college students...
- Tips for blue collar workers...
- Tips for Boomers...
- Tips for CEOs...
- Tips for solo founders...

Each "Way" gets 3-5 archetype variations. Result: 10 angles become 50-100 ideas.

### Step 4: Change the Sub-Topic (multiply x5 more)

Keep the topic and audience the same, but swap in a related sub-topic. Example for "Tips for college students to buy their first rental property":

- ...first short-term let
- ...first long-term let
- ...first beach rental property
- ...first city rental property

Result: 50-100 ideas become 250-500 unique content ideas from a single topic.

**The punchline:** You can write about the same overarching topic forever. Saying "I've already written about that" is never true when you apply all 4 steps.

## Autopilot CTA Strategy

### Frequency Rule

**25% of posts get a CTA.** Start there, adjust as you gather data.

### The Congruence Rule

The CTA must match the post content. A gym selfie should not link to a writing newsletter. A ghostwriting post should not plug an AI writing opt-in. Mismatched CTAs kill conversion.

### Timing

Fire the autoplug AFTER the post picks up steam, not immediately. On Hypefury, set a trigger threshold (e.g., after X likes or retweets). Lower tolerance (10 likes) fires early. Higher tolerance (150 likes) fires later but on proven posts. Experiment to find your sweet spot.

### Platform-Specific CTA Methods

**X/Twitter (via Hypefury):**
- Set autoplug to trigger based on likes or retweets threshold
- Save CTA templates in Hypefury's template library
- For threads: add CTA as the final tweet, making it the natural next step
- For surprise viral posts without autoplug set: manually add CTA as a reply

**LinkedIn (4 methods):**
1. **First comment** -- plug opt-in in the first comment (use Taplio to automate/delay)
2. **In the post body** -- include CTA in the post itself (may hurt reach slightly but CTA is visible)
3. **In-post button** -- LinkedIn Premium feature, adds a clickable button below your bio on every post
4. **Edit the post** -- add CTA after post gains traction (use with caution -- can tank reach after edit)

**YouTube:**
- Long-form: make newsletter/opt-in the FIRST line of the video description (above the "more" fold)
- Shorts: link to relevant long-form videos to send viewers down your YouTube rabbit hole

## Click-to-Tweet Viral Loop

A word-of-mouth strategy to get buyers to promote your product on X after purchase.

**How it works:**

1. In your onboarding/welcome email, offer a bonus in exchange for a tweet
2. Use [clicktotweet.com](https://clicktotweet.com/) to create a pre-written tweet with a shareable link
3. Buyer clicks the link, tweet is pre-composed, they just hit "Post"
4. The tweet tags you, so you can retweet it to your audience
5. In ConvertKit/Kit, set up a Rule: when someone clicks the Click-to-Tweet link, add them to a sequence that delivers the bonus

**Win-win:** Buyer gets the promised bonus. You get your product in front of their audience and yours.
