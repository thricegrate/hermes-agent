## LinkedIn

**Best for:** B2B, thought leadership, professional networking, recruiting
**Audience:** Professionals, decision-makers, job seekers
**Posting frequency:** 3-5x per week
**Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-8am, 12pm, 5-6pm

**What works:**
- Personal stories with business lessons
- Contrarian takes on industry topics
- Behind-the-scenes of building a company
- Data and original insights
- Carousel posts (document format)
- Polls that spark discussion

**What doesn't:**
- Overly promotional content
- Generic motivational quotes
- Links in the main post (kills reach)
- Corporate speak without personality

**Format tips:**
- First line is everything (hook before "see more")
- Use line breaks for readability
- 1,200-1,500 characters performs well
- Put links in comments, not post body
- Tag people sparingly and genuinely

**Algorithm tips:**
- First hour engagement matters most
- Comments > reactions > clicks
- Dwell time (people reading) signals quality
- No external links in post body
- Document posts (carousels) get strong reach
- Polls drive engagement but don't build authority

**LinkedIn Hook Physics (Source: Ruben Hassid, 770K followers):**
- You get exactly 2 lines before "...more" truncation. Each line is ~55 characters.
- Total: ~110 characters to earn the click. This is a platform constraint, not a style preference.
- Test every hook on mobile before publishing. Desktop shows more text, mobile is where most people read.
- If your hook doesn't work in 110 characters, it doesn't work. Period.

**LinkedIn Media Strategy:**
- Optimal image: 1080 x 1350px (3:4 portrait). Takes ~80% of mobile screen.
- Single image > carousel for raw reach. Carousel > single image for engagement depth.
- Scrappy, hand-drawn, unbranded visuals outperform polished corporate design.
- Videos underperform on LinkedIn. People scroll at work with sound off. Text + image wins.
- Best-performing media: cheat sheets and infographics worth saving/forwarding.
- NO logos, NO brand colors. The anti-brand aesthetic signals authenticity, not amateurism.

**Reader-Centric Content Rule:**
- "No one cares about you." Personal achievement posts ("I hit 100K followers!") don't grow accounts.
- Test: remove your name from the post. Does it still deliver value to the reader? If not, rewrite.
- Every post must help the reader SOLVE something, SEE something differently, or DO something new.
- The founder's story is the setup, never the main event. The tool/insight/framework is the hero.

**Claude Prompt Templates for LinkedIn (Source: Ruben Hassid):**

Usefulness reframe prompt (paste into Claude to make any draft reader-centric):
```
I want you to evaluate the following LinkedIn post based on ONE criterion: usefulness.
Is this post genuinely useful to someone reading it? Or is it just information dressed as insight?
Be brutally honest. If the reader gains nothing actionable, tell me.
Then rewrite it to be 10x more useful. Keep it concise. No fluff.
Post: [paste your draft]
```

Hook generation prompt (generates 10 hooks across techniques):
```
Write 10 LinkedIn hooks for a post about: [topic].
Each hook must be exactly 2 lines, ~55 characters per line.
Use these techniques (at least 1 of each):
- Direct Accusation (call out wrong behavior)
- Stolen Thought (voice what they secretly think)
- Absurd Reframe (dramatize with real stakes)
- Curiosity Gap
- Contrarian Take
Rank them from most likely to stop the scroll to least.
```

**Fail conditions (kill the post if any are true):**
- Vague inspiration with no concrete takeaway ("believe in yourself" energy)
- Tool praise without showing what it actually does for the reader
- Generic summary of a trend without a stance or personal angle
- Long warm-up that delays the point past the "see more" fold
- Posting just to maintain frequency. Nothing worth saying = skip the day.
- Could've been written by anyone in your niche. No unique POV, no proof, no personal story.

## Twitter/X

**Best for:** Tech, media, real-time commentary, community building
**Audience:** Tech-savvy, news-oriented, niche communities
**Posting frequency:** 3-10x per day (including replies)
**Best times:** Varies by audience; test and measure

**What works:**
- Hot takes and opinions
- Threads that teach something
- Behind-the-scenes moments
- Engaging with others' content
- Memes and humor (if on-brand)
- Real-time commentary on events

**What doesn't:**
- Pure self-promotion
- Threads without a strong hook
- Ignoring replies and mentions
- Scheduling everything (no real-time presence)

**Format tips:**
- Tweets under 100 characters get more engagement
- Threads: Hook in tweet 1, promise value, deliver
- Quote tweets with added insight beat plain retweets
- Use visuals to stop the scroll

**Algorithm tips (from Earlybird source code):**
- Replies = 10,000 pts, likes/quotes = 1,000 pts each. Engineer every post for replies first.
- ONE like from a connected user makes you eligible for out-of-network recommendations (UTEG threshold = 1)
- Small accounts get a normalization boost (MTL): engagement-per-follower ratio > raw follower count
- Clear topic language helps SimClusters match your tweet to interested users (very low threshold: 0.072)
- Same-author fatigue decays to 0.25x after multiple tweets shown -- quality > quantity
- "Not Interested" clicks cost up to -10,000 pts. Don't bore people with generic content.
- Engagement in first 30 min matters (confirmed by algorithm behavior)
- Threads keep people on platform (rewarded with dwell time signal)
- Full algorithm breakdown: see `content-social/references/x-algorithm-source.md`

## Instagram

**Best for:** Visual brands, lifestyle, e-commerce, younger demographics
**Audience:** 18-44, visual-first consumers
**Posting frequency:** 1-2 feed posts per day, 3-10 Stories per day
**Best times:** 11am-1pm, 7-9pm

**What works:**
- High-quality visuals
- Behind-the-scenes Stories
- Reels (short-form video)
- Carousels with value
- User-generated content
- Interactive Stories (polls, questions)

**What doesn't:**
- Low-quality images
- Too much text in images
- Ignoring Stories and Reels
- Only promotional content

**Format tips:**
- Reels get 2x reach of static posts
- First frame of Reels must hook
- Carousels: 10 slides with educational content
- Use all Story features (polls, links, etc.)

**Algorithm tips:**
- Reels heavily prioritized over static posts
- Saves and shares > likes
- Stories keep you top of feed
- Consistency matters more than perfection
- Use all features (polls, questions, etc.)

## TikTok

**Best for:** Brand awareness, younger audiences, viral potential
**Audience:** 16-34, entertainment-focused
**Posting frequency:** 1-4x per day
**Best times:** 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm

**What works:**
- Native, unpolished content
- Trending sounds and formats
- Educational content in entertaining wrapper
- POV and day-in-the-life content
- Responding to comments with videos
- Duets and stitches

**What doesn't:**
- Overly produced content
- Ignoring trends
- Hard selling
- Repurposed horizontal video

**Format tips:**
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds
- Keep it under 30 seconds to start
- Vertical only (9:16)
- Use trending sounds
- Post consistently to train algorithm

## Facebook

**Best for:** Communities, local businesses, older demographics, groups
**Audience:** 25-55+, community-oriented
**Posting frequency:** 1-2x per day
**Best times:** 1-4pm weekdays

**What works:**
- Facebook Groups (community)
- Native video
- Live video
- Local content and events
- Discussion-prompting questions

**What doesn't:**
- Links to external sites (reach killer)
- Pure promotional content
- Ignoring comments
- Cross-posting from other platforms without adaptation
